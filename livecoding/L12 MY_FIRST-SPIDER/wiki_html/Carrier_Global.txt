<p><b>Carrier Global Corporation</b> is an American corporation based in <a href="/wiki/Palm_Beach_Gardens,_Florida" title="Palm Beach Gardens, Florida">Palm Beach Gardens, Florida</a>. Carrier was founded in 1915 as an independent company manufacturing and distributing heating, ventilating and air conditioning (<a href="/wiki/HVAC" class="mw-redirect" title="HVAC">HVAC</a>) systems, and has since expanded to include manufacturing commercial refrigeration and food service equipment, and fire and security technologies.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carrier_Global&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p><a href="/wiki/Willis_Carrier" title="Willis Carrier">Willis Carrier</a> is credited with inventing modern <a href="/wiki/Air_conditioning" title="Air conditioning">air conditioning</a> in 1902. In 1915, Carrier and six other engineers pooled $32,600 to form the Carrier Engineering Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> They purchased their first factory in 1920, in <a href="/wiki/Newark,_New_Jersey" title="Newark, New Jersey">Newark, New Jersey</a>.
</p><p>The corporation bearing his name marketed its air conditioner to the residential market in the 1950s, which led to formerly sparsely populated areas such as the American Southwest becoming home to sprawling suburbs.
</p><p>In 1955, Carrier merged with Affiliated Gas Equipment, Inc., which owned the Bryant Heater Co., Day &amp; Night Water Heater Co., and Payne Furnace &amp; Supply Co.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:GMC_Savana_(Carrier).JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/GMC_Savana_%28Carrier%29.JPG/220px-GMC_Savana_%28Carrier%29.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="176" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1777" data-file-height="1421"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 176px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/GMC_Savana_%28Carrier%29.JPG/220px-GMC_Savana_%28Carrier%29.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="176" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:GMC_Savana_(Carrier).JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A Carrier commercial service van in Montreal, Canada in August 2008</div></div></div>
<p>Carrier Corporation was acquired by <a href="/wiki/United_Technologies_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="United Technologies Corporation">United Technologies Corporation</a> (UTC) in July 1979.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Prior to the acquisition by UTC, Carrier Corporation was known as the Carrier Air Conditioning Company.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/International_Comfort_Products_Corporation" title="International Comfort Products Corporation">International Comfort Products</a> (ICP), headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Lewisburg,_Tennessee" title="Lewisburg, Tennessee">Lewisburg, Tennessee</a>, was acquired by Carrier in 1999. In the 1990s Carrier stopped using the "Day &amp; Night" brand (which was the "D" in the BDP division, or Bryant-Day &amp; Night-Payne) but it was revived in 2006 by ICP.
</p><p>In 2001 Carrier was the "world's largest manufacturer of air-conditioning, heating, and refrigerator equipment" with a "total employment of 42,600" and a revenue of $8.9 billion. Carrier announced that it would be closing its DeWitt (Onondaga Co) plant. This led to the layoff of 1000 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-encyclopedia_newyork_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-encyclopedia_newyork-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>269</span></sup></p><p>In early 2008, Carrier acquired Environmental Market Solutions, Inc. (EMSI), an environmental and green building consulting company based in the United States. The company has received Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED) certification from the US Green Building Council for its factories in Charlotte, NC and Huntington, IN (2009), Shanghai, China (2010), and Monterrey, Mexico (2011).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2013, Carrier, <a href="/wiki/Otis_Elevator_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="Otis Elevator Company">Otis</a>, and <a href="/wiki/UTC_Fire_%26_Security" class="mw-redirect" title="UTC Fire &amp; Security">United Technologies Fire and Security</a> were combined into one subsidiary, UTC Building &amp; Industrial Systems. In 2016, Otis was split off, with the remainder becoming <a href="/wiki/UTC_Climate,_Controls_%26_Security" title="UTC Climate, Controls &amp; Security">UTC Climate, Controls &amp; Security</a>.
</p><p>In January 2016, Carrier announced it would lay off an unspecified number of employees at its research and development division in the town of <a href="/wiki/DeWitt,_New_York" title="DeWitt, New York">DeWitt, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Carrier_Air_Conditioner_move_to_Mexico" title="Carrier Air Conditioner move to Mexico">Carrier Air Conditioner move to Mexico</a></div>
<p>In February 2016, Carrier announced it would close its Indianapolis factory and move production to Monterrey, Mexico. HVAC Systems and Services North America president Chris Nelson cited "ongoing cost and pricing pressures" and Carrier's "existing infrastructure and a strong supplier base" in Mexico, saying that the move would allow the company "to operate more cost effectively."<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The Carrier spokesperson told the crowd that there would be no immediate impact on jobs, that the move would take place over three years, and no jobs would be affected until mid-2017, with the move to be completed by the end of 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>Over the November 2016 Thanksgiving holiday weekend, President Elect <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a> tweeted that he was in talks with Carrier Management to keep the factory in Indiana and not move to Mexico.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>On November 30, 2016, Carrier announced that it had negotiated an agreement<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> with President-elect Trump and Vice President-elect <a href="/wiki/Mike_Pence" title="Mike Pence">Mike Pence</a> to continue manufacturing gas furnaces in Indianapolis, in addition to retaining engineering and headquarters staff, preserving more than 1,000 jobs in Indianapolis.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The agreement included a state incentive package of about $7 million over 10 years.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The number of jobs saved was later revised down to 800.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2017, as part of their previously announced plan, Carrier told the state of Indiana that it will cut 632 jobs from its Indianapolis factory.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Layoffs began at the end of July, 2017 with each worker receiving one week's salary for each year of employment, education and job training, plus 6 months of health insurance as part of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Separation_package&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Separation package (page does not exist)">separation package</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p><i>The New York Times</i> reported on August 10, 2018, that Carrier's Indianapolis furnace plant was plagued by low morale and absenteeism because "employees share a looming sense that a factory shutdown is inevitable — that Carrier has merely postponed the closing until a more politically opportune moment."<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>On November 26, 2018, United Technologies announced that it would spin off UTC Climate, Controls &amp; Security as an independent company known as Carrier Global Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2020, United Technologies announced that the separation and spin off of Carrier had been completed.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Syracuse.2C_New_York_campus"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Syracuse,_New_York_campus">Syracuse, New York campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carrier_Global&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Syracuse, New York campus" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Willis Carrier moved his facilities from New Jersey to <a href="/wiki/Syracuse,_New_York" title="Syracuse, New York">Syracuse, New York</a>, in the 1930s. During the late 20th century, when it was acquired by UTC, it was Central New York State's largest manufacturer. Due to increasing labor and union costs in the Central New York area, Carrier has substantially downsized its presence in Syracuse, with manufacturing work being moved to a variety of domestic and international locations. Meanwhile, managerial employees were moved closer to UTC's Connecticut corporate headquarters which represented a challenge to the local economy.  Over the course of 2011 the majority of the manufacturing buildings of the Syracuse campus were demolished at a cost of nearly $14 million. Despite the loss of manufacturing jobs, the suburban Syracuse Campus, in <a href="/wiki/DeWitt,_New_York" title="DeWitt, New York">DeWitt, New York</a>, remained the primary engineering and design center for Carrier products, with over 1,000 employees and contractors on site. This site also houses the Customer Care Call Center for Carrier branded products.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>In 1980, Carrier was allowed to name the <a href="/wiki/Carrier_Dome" title="Carrier Dome">Carrier Dome</a>, the football and basketball arena at <a href="/wiki/Syracuse_University" title="Syracuse University">Syracuse University</a>, after Mel Holm, the company's then-CEO chair of the university's Board of Trustees,  gave the university $2.75 million toward the facility's construction. Despite being named for an air conditioner manufacturer, the Carrier Dome is not air conditioned, though it will be after renovations are completed in 2022.
</p>
