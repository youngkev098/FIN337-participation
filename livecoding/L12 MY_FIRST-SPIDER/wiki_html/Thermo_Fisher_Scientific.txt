<p>The combined company has added significantly to its technologies, workforce, and revenues through the acquisition of other reagent, consumable, instrumentation, and service providers, including of <a href="/wiki/Life_Technologies_(Thermo_Fisher_Scientific)" title="Life Technologies (Thermo Fisher Scientific)">Life Technologies Corporation</a> in 2013 (for <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a> 13.6 billion, for <a href="/wiki/Molecular_Probes" title="Molecular Probes">probe reagents</a> and advanced DNA sequencing services), Advanced Scientifics (<a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a> 300 million, for bioprocessing technologies) and <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Aesar" title="Alfa Aesar">Alfa Aesar</a> (<a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a> 405 million, for research chemicals), both in 2015; <a href="/wiki/Affymetrix" title="Affymetrix">Affymetrix</a> (<a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a> 1.3 billion, for gene chip technologies) and MTI-GlobalStem (amount unknown, for cell transfection and neurobiology), both in 2016; <a href="/wiki/FEI_Company" title="FEI Company">FEI Company</a> (<a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a> 4.2 billion, an <a href="/wiki/Electron_microscope" title="Electron microscope">electron microscope</a> manufacturer),<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag needs to be fact-checked with the cited source(s). </p><p>As of 2017, the company had revenues of $21 billion and was a <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> company. As of 2018, the company employed on the order of 70,000 workers, and was reporting $US 24 billion in annual revenues.
</p><p>In March 2020, Thermo Fisher received <a href="/wiki/Emergency_use_authorization" class="mw-redirect" title="Emergency use authorization">emergency use authorization</a> from the FDA for a test for <a href="/wiki/2019-nCoV" class="mw-redirect" title="2019-nCoV">2019-nCoV</a> to help address the <a href="/wiki/2019-20_coronavirus_pandemic" class="mw-redirect" title="2019-20 coronavirus pandemic">2019-20 coronavirus pandemic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Predecessors_and_merger">Predecessors and merger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thermo_Fisher_Scientific&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Predecessors and merger" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p><a href="/wiki/Thermo_Electron" title="Thermo Electron">Thermo Electron</a> was co-founded in 1956 by <a href="/wiki/George_N._Hatsopoulos" title="George N. Hatsopoulos">George N. Hatsopoulos</a> and Peter M Nomikos. Hatsopoulos was an <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">MIT</a> PhD in <a href="/wiki/Mechanical_engineering" title="Mechanical engineering">mechanical engineering</a> and Nomikos a <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Business_School" title="Harvard Business School">Harvard Business School</a> graduate.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>  It focused on providing analytical and laboratory products and, and had revenues of over $2 billion in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p><a href="/wiki/Fisher_Scientific" title="Fisher Scientific">Fisher Scientific</a> was founded in 1902 by Chester G. Fisher from <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> It focused on providing laboratory equipment, chemicals, supplies and services used in healthcare, scientific research, safety, and education.<sup id="cite_ref-ir.thermofisher.com_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ir.thermofisher.com-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>On May 14, 2006, Thermo Electron and Fisher Scientific announced that they would <a href="/wiki/Mergers_and_acquisitions" title="Mergers and acquisitions">merge</a> in a tax-free, stock-for-stock exchange; the merged company was named Thermo Fisher Scientific, and had on the order of 30,000 employees, and reported US$ 9 billion in combined revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="This claim needs references to better sources. (March 2020)">better source needed</span></a></i>]</sup> On November 9, 2006, the companies announced that the merger had been completed.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-8">[8]</a></sup> However, the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Trade_Commission" title="Federal Trade Commission">Federal Trade Commission</a> ruled that this acquisition was anticompetitive with regard to <a href="/wiki/Centrifugal_evaporator" title="Centrifugal evaporator">centrifugal evaporators</a>, requiring Fisher to divest <a href="/wiki/Genevac" title="Genevac">Genevac</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>  In April 2007, Genevac was sold to Riverlake Partners LLC<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and the merger closed with FTC approval.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>Currently, the company's products are sold under the brand names of Thermo Scientific, Fisher Scientific, and several other recognized brand names (e.g. Chromacol, <a href="/wiki/Nalgene" title="Nalgene">Nalgene</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cellomics" title="Cellomics">Cellomics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pierce_Biotechnology" title="Pierce Biotechnology">Pierce Protein Research</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fermentas" title="Fermentas">Fermentas</a>). According to company figures, 46% of its sales are in <a href="/wiki/Life_sciences" class="mw-redirect" title="Life sciences">life sciences</a>, 20% in <a href="/wiki/Healthcare" class="mw-redirect" title="Healthcare">healthcare</a>, and 34% in industrial/environmental and safety.<sup id="cite_ref-ir.thermofisher.com_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ir.thermofisher.com-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>Thermo Fisher has offices and operations in many countries, notably the U.S. and in Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p>In September 2017, Thermo Fisher Scientific signed an agreement with the Institute of Pathology Heidelberg (IPH) to establish its Center of Molecular Pathology at <a href="/wiki/Heidelberg_University" title="Heidelberg University">Heidelberg University</a> Hospital as the newest member of the Next Generation Sequencing Companion Dx Center of Excellence Program (COEP). The initiative focuses on establishing strategic collaborations with leading, European-based organizations that can lead studies using Thermo Fisher's Oncomine portfolio of research panels destined for development as companion diagnostics to help drive precision <a href="/wiki/Oncology" title="Oncology">oncology</a> in the region.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
