<p><b>Wabtec Corporation</b> (derived from <b>W</b>estinghouse <b>A</b>ir <b>B</b>rake <b>Tec</b>hnologies Corporation) is an American company formed by the merger of the <a href="/wiki/Westinghouse_Air_Brake_Company" title="Westinghouse Air Brake Company">Westinghouse Air Brake Company</a> (WABCO) and <a href="/wiki/MotivePower" title="MotivePower">MotivePower Industries Corporation</a> in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-Westinghouse-Air-Brake-Co-Jun-1999-8-K_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Westinghouse-Air-Brake-Co-Jun-1999-8-K-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Westinghouse-Air-Brake-Co-Nov-1999-8-K_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Westinghouse-Air-Brake-Co-Nov-1999-8-K-7">[7]</a></sup>  It is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania</a>, US.

title="Locomotive">locomotives</a>, <a href="/wiki/Railway_freight_car" class="mw-redirect" title="Railway freight car">freight cars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Passenger_car_(rail)" title="Passenger car </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wabtec_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company's origins go back to 1869 with the foundation of the Westinghouse Brake Company. That company (also known as WA&amp;B later as WABCO) became independent in 1990 via a management buy-out, and went public in 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-Wabtec-Corp-Feb-2018-10-K_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wabtec-Corp-Feb-2018-10-K-8">[8]</a></sup> Another company, <a href="/wiki/WABCO_Vehicle_Control_Systems" title="WABCO Vehicle Control Systems">WABCO Vehicle Control Systems</a>, also created from the Westinghouse Brake Company, is independent of Wabtec and was spun off by <a href="/wiki/American_Standard_Companies" title="American Standard Companies">American Standard Companies</a> (the ultimate owner) in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-American-Standard-Companies-Inc-Jul-2007-8-K_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-American-Standard-Companies-Inc-Jul-2007-8-K-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>The other company forming Wabtec, <a href="/wiki/MotivePower" title="MotivePower">MotivePower Industries</a>, can be traced back to 1972,<sup id="cite_ref-MotivePower-Industries,-Inc-Mar-1998-10-K_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MotivePower-Industries,-Inc-Mar-1998-10-K-10">[10]</a></sup> with the formation of the <a href="/wiki/MK_Rail" class="mw-redirect" title="MK Rail">MK Rail</a> division by the <a href="/wiki/Morrison_Knudsen" class="mw-redirect" title="Morrison Knudsen">Morrison Knudsen</a> group and the purchase of a manufacturing facility in <a href="/wiki/Boise" class="mw-redirect" title="Boise">Boise</a>. In 1994 Morrison Knudsen created a subsidiary MK Rail Corporation; during the first half of the same decade the MK Rail group expanded with the acquisition of various other locomotive component companies.<sup id="cite_ref-MK-Rail--Corporation-Mar-1994-8-K_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MK-Rail--Corporation-Mar-1994-8-K-11">[11]</a></sup> In 1996, MK Rail group separated from the parent Morrison Knudsen and adopted the name MotivePower Industries Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-MK-Rail--Corporation-Mar-1997-10-K_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MK-Rail--Corporation-Mar-1997-10-K-12">[12]</a></sup> In the later half of the 1990s further companies were acquired – again all in the locomotive components business.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/MotivePower" title="MotivePower">MotivePower</a>, a wholly owned subsidiary of Wabtec, continues to manufacture locomotives.
</p><p>The corporate <a href="/wiki/Logo" title="Logo">logo</a> is supposed to represent an axial view of a mechanical brake valve, where different air ports line up between the 'stator' and 'rotor' depending upon the handle position.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Mergers_and_acquisitions">Mergers and acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wabtec_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mergers and acquisitions" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In March 2010, Wabtec announced that it had purchased Xorail, a railway signaling design and construction company for $40 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Wabtec-Corporation-Apr-2010-8-K_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wabtec-Corporation-Apr-2010-8-K-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2010, Wabtec announced the plan to purchase two manufacturers of rail equipment, Bach-Simpson Corp. and G&amp;B Specialties.<sup id="cite_ref-Wabtec-Corporation-Jul-2010-8-K_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wabtec-Corporation-Jul-2010-8-K-16">[16]</a></sup> The companies produce locomotive components and track products respectively. The acquisition of G&amp;B Specialties was completed on July 28, 2010, for approximately $31.8 million. The acquisition of Bach-Simpson Corporation was completed on August 20, 2010 for approximately $12.0 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Wabtec-Corporation-Nov-2010-10-Q_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wabtec-Corporation-Nov-2010-10-Q-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2010, Wabtec acquired all of the assets of Cleveland, Ohio-based manufacturer of traction motors and electric coils for rail and power generation markets <a href="/w/index.php?title=Swiger_Coil_Systems&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Swiger Coil Systems (page does not exist)">Swiger Coil Systems</a> for approximately $43.0 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Wabtec-Corporation-Feb-2011-10-K_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wabtec-Corporation-Feb-2011-10-K-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>On June 14, 2012, Wabtec acquired Mors Smitt Holding for a purchase price of $88.4 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Wabtec-Corporation-Aug-2012-10-Q_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wabtec-Corporation-Aug-2012-10-Q-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>On June 6, 2014, Wabtec  acquired Fandstan Electric Group, a rail and industrial equipment manufacturer, for a purchase price of $199.4 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Wabtec-Corporation-Feb-2016-10-K_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wabtec-Corporation-Feb-2016-10-K-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>On August 21, 2014, Wabtec acquired Dia-Frag, a manufacturer of friction products including motorcycle braking, for a purchase price of $70.6 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Wabtec-Corporation-Feb-2016-10-K_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wabtec-Corporation-Feb-2016-10-K-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>On June 17, 2015, Wabtec acquired all three units of  Metalocaucho (MTC, in Spain, China and India) who are leaders in the field of suspension and anti-vibration systems, for a purchase price of $23.4 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Wabtec-Corporation-Oct-2015-10-Q_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wabtec-Corporation-Oct-2015-10-Q-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>On July 27, 2015, Wabtec announced that it plans to purchase 51% of <a href="/wiki/Faiveley_Transport" title="Faiveley Transport">Faiveley Transport</a>'s shares in a cash-and-stock deal valued at $1.8 billion, including debt.<sup id="cite_ref-Wabtec-Corporation-Jul-2015-8-K_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wabtec-Corporation-Jul-2015-8-K-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The deal closed on December 1, 2016, for a total of $1.7 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-Wabtec-Corporation-Dec-2016-8-K_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wabtec-Corporation-Dec-2016-8-K-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>On October 12, 2015, Wabtec announced its acquisition of lineside sensor manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Track_IQ" title="Track IQ">Track IQ</a>. <sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>On April 20, 2018, it was reported that <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a> (GE), undergoing a strategic review, was in talks to sell its century-old locomotive business, <a href="/wiki/GE_Transportation" title="GE Transportation">GE Transportation</a>, to Wabtec, according to people familiar with the matter.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>On May 21, 2018, GE and Wabtec confirmed the merger of GE Transportation with Wabtec in an $11 billion deal, completed on February 25, 2019, that saw Wabtec shareholders take a 50.8% shareholding in the merged company, with GE shareholders owning 24.3% and GE itself 24.9%.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wabtec_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: United Kingdom" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Brush_Traction">Brush Traction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wabtec_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Brush Traction" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Wabtec bought <a href="/wiki/Brush_Traction" title="Brush Traction">Brush Traction</a> of <a href="/wiki/Loughborough" title="Loughborough">Loughborough</a>, an English locomotive builder and maintainer, for US$31 million on 25 February 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-Wabtec-Corporation-May-2011-10-Q_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wabtec-Corporation-May-2011-10-Q-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Bearward_Engineering">Bearward Engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wabtec_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Bearward Engineering" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In November 2011, Wabtec acquired Bearward Engineering Ltd, an industrial radiator manufacturer employing some 300 people based in <a href="/wiki/Northampton" title="Northampton">Northampton</a>, England. Bearward Engineering mainly makes cooling systems for power generators. At the time of purchase Bearward had an annual sales of US$70 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Wabtec-Corporation-Nov-2011-8-K_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wabtec-Corporation-Nov-2011-8-K-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Wabtec_Rail_UK">Wabtec Rail UK</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wabtec_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Wabtec Rail UK" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Wabtec Rail Limited is a railway engineering company based in at the <a href="/wiki/Doncaster_Works" title="Doncaster Works">Doncaster Works</a> in <a href="/wiki/Doncaster" title="Doncaster">Doncaster</a>, South Yorkshire, England. Services include the overhaul and repair of railway rolling stock and components. Wabtec Rail occupies part of the former <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Engineering_Limited" title="British Rail Engineering Limited">British Rail Engineering Limited</a> site known locally as the Plant Works. The two main unions on site (<a href="/wiki/Unite_the_Union" title="Unite the Union">Unite</a> and <a href="/wiki/National_Union_of_Rail,_Maritime_and_Transport_Workers" title="National Union of Rail, Maritime and Transport Workers">RMT</a>) gave notice on November 3, 2014, of their intention to ballot their members for industrial action over a pay dispute, for the first time in Wabtec Rail Limited's history.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Wabtec_Rail_Scotland">Wabtec Rail Scotland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wabtec_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Wabtec Rail Scotland" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Wabtec Rail Scotland occupies the <a href="/wiki/Kilmarnock" title="Kilmarnock">Kilmarnock</a> works of the former locomotive builder <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Barclay_Sons_%26_Co." title="Andrew Barclay Sons &amp; Co.">Andrew Barclay Sons &amp; Co.</a>
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="LH_Group_Services">LH Group Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wabtec_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: LH Group Services" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Wabtec announced the $48 million acquisition of diesel engine, transmission and bogie overhaul and industrial locomotive supplier LH Group on October 1, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-Wabtec-Corporation-Nov-2012-10-Q_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wabtec-Corporation-Nov-2012-10-Q-32">[32]</a></sup> Based at Barton under Needwood in Staffordshire, UK. LH has around 400 employees and annual sales of US$65m, about 10% of which are in non-rail markets.
</p>
