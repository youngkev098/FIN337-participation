</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Qorvo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Qorvo was created on January 1, 2015, with the merger of TriQuint Semiconductor and RF Micro Devices (RFMD).<sup id="cite_ref-formed_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formed-2">[2]</a></sup> In June 2015, the new company became a component of the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a>, replacing tobacco company <a href="/wiki/Lorillard" class="mw-redirect" title="Lorillard">Lorillard</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-sp_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sp-6">[6]</a></sup> At the time of joining the S&amp;P 500, Qorvo was <a href="/wiki/Market_capitalization" title="Market capitalization">valued</a> at $12 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-sp_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sp-6">[6]</a></sup> The company employs more than 8,000 people.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> As of mid-2016, the <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a> plant alone was employing almost 1,000 people.<sup id="cite_ref-oreg-2016aug_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oreg-2016aug-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Qorvo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Name" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Pronounced "kor-vo", the name is intended to reflect the "core technologies and innovations" the companies plan to enable following their combination. The name's first syllable is meant to evoke "chorus" – people (TriQuint and RFMD) working in unison, as well as "core" technology.  The second syllable, "vo", is meant to indicate voyage or travel through air or space.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
