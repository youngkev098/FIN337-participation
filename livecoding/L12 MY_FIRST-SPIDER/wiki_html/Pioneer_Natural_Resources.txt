</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pioneer_Natural_Resources&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Pioneer Natural Resources was created in 1997 by the merger of Parker &amp; Parsley Petroleum Company and MESA Inc., owned by <a href="/wiki/T._Boone_Pickens" title="T. Boone Pickens">T. Boone Pickens</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In 2002, Pioneer made discoveries in its offshore Oooguruk field, west of <a href="/wiki/Prudhoe_Bay,_Alaska" title="Prudhoe Bay, Alaska">Prudhoe Bay, Alaska</a>. In 2008, Pioneer became the first independent operator to produce oil on the <a href="/wiki/Alaska_North_Slope" title="Alaska North Slope">Alaska North Slope</a>. These holdings were sold for $550 million in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 2004, Pioneer Natural Resources acquired Evergreen Resources in a $2.1 billion transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2010, the company announced a $1.15 billion midstream joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Reliance_Industries" class="mw-redirect" title="Reliance Industries">Reliance Industries</a> for assets in the <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Ford_Group" title="Eagle Ford Group">Eagle Ford Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The business was sold to <a href="/wiki/Enterprise_Products_Partners" class="mw-redirect" title="Enterprise Products Partners">Enterprise Products Partners</a> for $2.15 billion in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2012, the company acquired Carmeuse Industrial Sands, a silica sand manufacturer, for $297 million.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2013, the company sold a 40% interest in approximately 207,000 net acres (84,000 net hectares) leased in horizontal Wolfcamp Shale to Sinochem Petroleum USA LLC, a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Sinochem_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Sinochem Group">Sinochem Group</a>, for $1.7 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2013, the Company sold its interests in <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> to Caelus Energy Alaska for $550 million.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In 2014, the company sold non-producing assets in the Hugoton Basin to <a href="/wiki/Linn_Energy" title="Linn Energy">Linn Energy</a> for $340 million.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2016, CEO and chairman <a href="/wiki/Scott_D._Sheffield" title="Scott D. Sheffield">Scott D.
