</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jack_Henry_%26_Associates&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Jack Henry &amp; Associates (commonly referred to as JHA) was formed in 1976 by Jack Henry and Jerry Hall in <a href="/wiki/Monett,_Missouri" title="Monett, Missouri">Monett, Missouri</a>. In 1977, Jack Henry &amp; Associates was incorporated and generated $115,222 in revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>On November 20, 1985, an Initial Public Offering made JHA a public company trading 3,125,000 common shares on the <a href="/wiki/Nasdaq" title="Nasdaq">NASDAQ</a> exchange under the symbol JKHY. In 1992, JHA began to aggressively acquire companies that expanded its product offering and its client base. It acquired Symitar in 2000, establishing its second brand which serves the credit union industry. In 2004, JHA's focused diversification acquisition strategy resulted in the acquisition of a number of companies and products that can be sold outside JHA's core client base to all financial services organizations regardless of charter, asset size, core processing platform. In 2006, JHA launched its third primary brand – ProfitStars – to encompass the specialized products and services assembled through its focused diversification acquisition strategy. In 2012, JHA announced $1 billion in annual revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Jack_Henry_Banking">Jack Henry Banking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jack_Henry_%26_Associates&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Jack Henry Banking" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Jack Henry Banking is a provider of integrated computer systems for banks ranging from de novo to institutions. Jack Henry Banking currently serves approximately 1,000 banks.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Symitar">Symitar</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jack_Henry_%26_Associates&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Symitar" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Symitar was founded in 1984 and acquired by JHA in 2000. Symitar is a provider of integrated computer systems for credit unions of all sizes. Symitar's product is Episys, a software application used to manage member base and process transactions.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
