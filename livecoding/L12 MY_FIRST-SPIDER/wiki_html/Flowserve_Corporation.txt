States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> corporation and one of the largest suppliers of industrial and environmental machinery such as <a href="/wiki/Pump" title="Pump">pumps</a>, <a href="/wiki/Valve" title="Valve">valves</a>, <a href="/wiki/End_face_mechanical_seal" class="mw-redirect" title="End face mechanical id="cite_ref-Reidy2006_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reidy2006-5">[5]</a></sup> Headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Irving,_Texas" title="Irving, Texas">Irving, Texas</a>, a suburb of <a href="/wiki/Dallas%E2%80%93Fort_Worth_metroplex" title="Dallas–Fort Worth metroplex">Dallas</a>, Flowserve has over 18,000 employees in more than 55 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Flowserve sells products and offers aftermarket services to engineering and construction firms, <a href="/wiki/Original_equipment_manufacturer" title="Original equipment manufacturer">original equipment manufacturers</a>, distributors and end users. The Flowserve brand name originated in 1997 with a merger of BW/IP and <a href="/wiki/Durco_International" class="mw-redirect" title="Durco International">Durco International</a>.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flowserve&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Flowserve heritage dates back to the 1790 founding of Simpson &amp; Thompson by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Simpson_(engineer)" title="Thomas Simpson (engineer)">Thomas Simpson</a>, later to become <a href="/wiki/Worthington_Simpson_Pumps" class="mw-redirect" title="Worthington Simpson Pumps">Worthington Simpson Pumps</a>, one of the companies that became part of BW/IP.<sup id="cite_ref-Roberts2006_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Roberts2006-8">[8]</a></sup> The company was created in 1997 with the merger of two fluid motion and control companies: BW/IP and Durco International.
</p><p>Acquisitions include HydroTechnik Olomouc in 2006,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Sealing Systems in 2007,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Calder Ag in 2009<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and Valbart Srl in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In October 2011 Flowserve acquired Lawrence Pumps Inc. In March 2013 acquired a manufacturing plant from L&amp;T Valves situated in Maraimalainagar,Tamil Nadu. In 2015 Flowserve acquired SIHI.
</p>

<p>On Feb 3, 2004, Flowserve announced restatement of its financial statements for aggregate estimated pretax adjustments for 2003. The restatement was approximately $11 million.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>On Oct 26, 2004, Flowserve determined to restate its previously announced financial results for the second quarter of 2004, including certain post-closing adjustments and recently identified out-of-period expenses. The restatement had no impact on net cash balance.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>On Feb 7, 2005, Flowserve confirmed that it would restate its 2000 through first quarter 2004 financial statements, resulting from an ongoing comprehensive review of the company's accounts. The restatement reduced net income for the periods restated.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
