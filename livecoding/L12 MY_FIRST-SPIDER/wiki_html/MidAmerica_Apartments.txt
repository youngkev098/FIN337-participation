</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mid-America_Apartment_Communities&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company was founded in 1977 by George E. Cates.<sup id="cite_ref-cates_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cates-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In 1993, the company was organized as a real estate investment trust.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>In February 1994, the company acquired The Cates Company from George E. Cates and became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cates_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cates-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2009, founder George E. Cates retired.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2013, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Colonial_Properties" title="Colonial Properties">Colonial Properties</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2016, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Post_Properties" title="Post Properties">Post Properties</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and was added to the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500_Index" title="S&amp;P 500 Index">S&amp;P 500 Index</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, the company was ranked 142nd on the "Growth Champions" list published by <a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2018, the company paid $11.3 million to settle a complaint that Post Properties violated the design and construction requirements under the <a href="/wiki/Fair_Housing_Act" class="mw-redirect" title="Fair Housing Act">Fair Housing Act</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Americans_with_Disabilities_Act" class="mw-redirect" title="Americans with Disabilities Act">Americans with Disabilities Act</a> at 50 properties.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
