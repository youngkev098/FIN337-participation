</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=L_Brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">

<p>Bella Cabakoff was born in <a href="/wiki/Williamsburg,_Brooklyn" title="Williamsburg, Brooklyn">Williamsburg, Brooklyn</a> and moved to Columbus as a toddler.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> At age 21, she became the youngest buyer for the <a href="/wiki/Lazarus_(department_store)" title="Lazarus (department store)">Lazarus</a> <a href="/wiki/Department_store" title="Department store">department store</a> chain. After spending over 20 years working for Lazarus, in 1951, she and her husband Harry Wexner opened a women's clothing store named Leslie's (after their son) on State Street. This store became the training ground for <a href="/wiki/Les_Wexner" title="Les Wexner">Leslie "Les" Wexner</a>.
</p><p>In 1963, he borrowed $5,000 from his aunt and $5,000 from the bank and opened a store at the <a href="/wiki/Kingsdale_Shopping_Center" title="Kingsdale Shopping Center">Kingsdale Shopping Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Upper_Arlington,_Ohio" title="Upper Arlington, Ohio">Upper Arlington</a>, Ohio. This store was named "<a href="/wiki/The_Limited" title="The Limited">The Limited</a>" because the store focused on clothing for younger women, unlike his parents' general merchandise store.  Later in 1964, Bella and Harry closed their store to join their son in his venture.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>The original board consisted of only the three family members and longtime friend Jim Waldron, who served as Senior <a href="/wiki/Vice_President" class="mw-redirect" title="Vice President">Vice President</a>. Bella Wexner served as secretary until her death in 2001, Harry as chairman (he served until his death in 1975), and Leslie, <a href="/wiki/Chief_Executive_Office" class="mw-redirect" title="Chief Executive Office">CEO</a> from inception, who later succeeded his father as the chairman. As of 2014<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=L_Brands&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, he and his family continue to own 17% of LB. In 1969, Wexner took The Limited Brands public and sold 47,600 shares at $7.25 a share. In 1977, The Limited moved into its main headquarters on Morse Road in Columbus, from which L Brands still operates today. In 1982, The Limited was first listed on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=L_Brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Acquisitions" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The 1980s started a string of acquisitions. In 1982, the <a href="/wiki/Victoria%27s_Secret" title="Victoria's Secret">Victoria's Secret</a> brand, store, and catalogue were purchased from <a href="/wiki/Roy_Raymond_(businessman)" class="mw-redirect" title="Roy Raymond (businessman)">Roy Raymond</a> for $1 million.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>  Also in 1982, 207 <a href="/wiki/Lane_Bryant" title="Lane Bryant">Lane Bryant</a> stores were purchased. In 1985, the exclusive <a href="/wiki/Henri_Bendel" title="Henri Bendel">Henri Bendel</a> store on Fifth Avenue in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> was purchased for $10 million and 798 <a href="/wiki/New_York_%26_Company" title="New York &amp; Company">Lerner</a> stores for $297 million. Finally, in 1988, 25 <a href="/wiki/Abercrombie_%26_Fitch" title="Abercrombie &amp; Fitch">Abercrombie &amp; Fitch</a> stores were purchased for $46 million. In 1996, The Limited ended its ownership of the A&amp;F brand, when it was <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spun off</a> into a publicly traded company.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>The 1990s saw the initial development of the <a href="/wiki/Limited_Too" class="mw-redirect" title="Limited Too">Limited Too</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bath_%26_Body_Works" title="Bath &amp; Body Works">Bath &amp; Body Works</a>, Structure, and <a href="/wiki/Victoria%27s_Secret" title="Victoria's Secret">Victoria's Secret Beauty</a>. Later in 1998, several <a href="/wiki/Bath_%26_Body_Works" title="Bath &amp; Body Works">Bath &amp; Body Works</a> stores were converted to The White Barn Candle Company stores to begin a home fragrance brand.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 2005, Limited Brands purchased home fragrance manufacturer Slatkin &amp; Company.<sup id="cite_ref-Dash2005_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dash2005-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Divestitures">Divestitures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=L_Brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Divestitures" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On August 3, 2007, Limited Brands sold 75% ownership of its flagship Limited chain to buyout firm <a href="/wiki/Sun_Capital_Partners" title="Sun Capital Partners">Sun Capital Partners</a>, Inc. In 2010, the remaining 25% of shares were also purchased by Sun Capital.  <a href="/wiki/Stefan_M._Selig" title="Stefan M. Selig">Stefan M. Selig</a> from <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_America" title="Bank of America">Bank of America</a> was involved in these deals.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-11">[11]</a></sup> Following the sale of their namesake store, Limited Brands changed their company name to L Brands, as per the terms of the sale.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On January 7, 2017, The Limited closed all of its stores in the United States. The web retail operation, thelimited.com, was initially continued to be open for business and ship nationwide.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Despite this, The Limited online soon filed for <a href="/wiki/Bankruptcy" title="Bankruptcy">bankruptcy</a> and discontinued all services.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> However, the <a href="/wiki/Brand" title="Brand">brand</a> was revived in mid-2017 by <a href="/wiki/Sycamore_Partners" title="Sycamore Partners">Sycamore Partners</a> subsidiary <a href="/wiki/Belk" title="Belk">Belk</a> and Limited-branded merchandise is available on its website.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>On February 20, 2020, L Brands announced that it would sell its Victoria Secret division to Sycamore Partners, private equity firm. Under the agreement, Sycamore Partners would gain a 55% controlling sake in Victoria's Secret while L Brands would keep a 45% stake. After the sale closes, Bath &amp; Body Works would become L Brand's sole business. L Brands also announced that CEO Leslie Wexner will step down after the sale completes.<sup id="cite_ref-victoria_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-victoria-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
