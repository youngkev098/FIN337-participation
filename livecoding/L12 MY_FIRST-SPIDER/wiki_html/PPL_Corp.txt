For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/PPL_(disambiguation)" class="mw-disambig" title="PPL (disambiguation)">PPL (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p>The <b>PPL Corporation</b> is an energy company headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Allentown,_Pennsylvania" title="Allentown, Pennsylvania">Allentown, Pennsylvania</a>, United States. It currently controls about 8,000 <a href="/wiki/Megawatt" class="mw-redirect" title="Megawatt">megawatts</a> of regulated electric generating capacity in the United States and delivers electricity to 10.5 million customers in Pennsylvania, <a href="/wiki/Kentucky" title="Kentucky">Kentucky</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>. PPL is the parent to seven regulated utility companies in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup>, and it also provides natural gas delivery service to 321,000 customers in Kentucky. PPL Electric Utilities (formerly known as <b>PP&amp;L</b> and <b>Pennsylvania Power and Light</b>) is the PPL Corporation's primary subsidiary.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=PPL_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Pennsylvania Power &amp; Light was founded in 1920 out of a merger of eight smaller Pennsylvania utilities. It gradually extended its service territory to a crescent-shaped region of central and northeastern Pennsylvania stretching from <a href="/wiki/Lancaster,_Pennsylvania" title="Lancaster, Pennsylvania">Lancaster</a>, through the <a href="/wiki/Lehigh_Valley" title="Lehigh Valley">Lehigh Valley</a> into <a href="/wiki/Scranton" class="mw-redirect" title="Scranton">Scranton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wilkes-Barre" class="mw-redirect" title="Wilkes-Barre">Wilkes-Barre</a>. In 1995, it reorganized as a holding company, PP&amp;L Resources, which changed its name to the current PPL Corporation in 2000.
</p><p>The company limited its activities to Pennsylvania until <a href="/wiki/Deregulation" title="Deregulation">deregulation</a> of electrical utilities in the 1990s encouraged PPL to purchase assets in other states.  The largest of these transactions was PPL's 1998 purchase of 13 plants from <a href="/wiki/Montana_Power_Company" title="Montana Power Company">Montana Power</a> (leaving <a href="/wiki/NorthWestern_Energy" class="mw-redirect" title="NorthWestern Energy">NorthWestern Energy</a> - the buyer of the former Montana Power transmission and distribution systems - vulnerable to high "spot" prices on the energy market). This added over 2,500 MW of capacity and was the largest expansion in PPL's history. In 2014, those hydroelectric facilities were sold to NorthWestern.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2002, PPL announced that Robert G. Byram, PPL's chief nuclear officer since 1997, was retiring from the company. Bryce L. Shriver, who had served as vice president-Nuclear Site Operations at PPL's Susquehanna plant since 2000, became senior vice president and chief nuclear officer.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>PPL's 2008 revenue was $8.2 billion, with a net profit of $930 million, making it number 314 on the 2009 <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> list.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> After acquiring Central Networks and LG&amp;E, PPL's 2011 profit rose to $1.495 billion, on $12.7 billion in revenue, ranking it number 212 on Fortune's 2012 list.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2011, PPL acquired from <a href="/wiki/E.ON" title="E.ON">E.ON</a> the British distribution company Central Networks for £3.5 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-powergen020311_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-powergen020311-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>On June 6, 2014, PPL announced it will be divesting its electrical generation facilities to a newly formed company, <a href="/wiki/Talen_Energy" title="Talen Energy">Talen Energy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-talen_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-talen-8">[8]</a></sup>  On June 1, 2015, the Talen spinoff was completed, allowing PPL to concentrate on the transmission and distribution aspects of the electric utility business.<sup id="cite_ref-Morningcall2_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Morningcall2-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>On July 1, 2016, PPL Solutions, LLC was sold to Hansen Technologies Limited.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> PPL Solutions provides billing, business process outsourcing, call center, and information technology services to regulated utilities in the United States.
</p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Customer_satisfaction">Customer satisfaction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=PPL_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Customer satisfaction" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>PPL Electric Utilities, which serves 1.4 million customers in 29 counties of Pennsylvania in the United States, has received 15 <a href="/wiki/J.D._Power_and_Associates" class="mw-redirect" title="J.D. Power and Associates">J.D. Power and Associates</a> awards for customer satisfaction — more than any other utility in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In June, 2013, J.D. Power announced that PPL EnergyPlus ranked highest in the state for residential customer satisfaction in J.D. Power's first study of competitive electricity suppliers in Pennsylvania.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Headquarters">Headquarters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=PPL_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Headquarters" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2007_-_PPL_Building.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/2007_-_PPL_Building.jpg/150px-2007_-_PPL_Building.jpg" decoding="async" width="150" height="263" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1479" data-file-height="2593"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 150px;height: 263px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/2007_-_PPL_Building.jpg/150px-2007_-_PPL_Building.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="150" data-height="263" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2007_-_PPL_Building.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The PPL Building is the tallest building in <a href="/wiki/Allentown,_Pennsylvania" title="Allentown, Pennsylvania">Allentown, Pennsylvania</a>.</div></div></div>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="PPL_Building">PPL Building</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=PPL_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: PPL Building" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/PPL_Building" title="PPL Building">PPL Building</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/PPL_Building" title="PPL Building">PPL Building</a> is now the tallest building in not just Allentown, but in the greater <a href="/wiki/Lehigh_Valley" title="Lehigh Valley">Lehigh Valley</a>, after the demolition of the <a href="/wiki/Martin_Tower" title="Martin Tower">Martin Tower</a> on May 19, 2019.  The building has 23 stories and is 322 feet (98 m) tall.  It is located at the intersection of Hamilton and Ninth Streets in the downtown area of the city.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Plaza_at_PPL_Center">The Plaza at PPL Center</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=PPL_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: The Plaza at PPL Center" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In June 2003, PPL Corporation dedicated The Plaza at PPL Center, a new office building for continued growth in the company's non-regulated energy businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>The Plaza at PPL Center, PPL's new office building in downtown Allentown, Pennsylvania, boasts a long list of environmental features, including a vegetative roof and innovative energy and water-saving devices.
</p><p>It has been awarded the Gold rating from the U.S. Green Building Council's <a href="/wiki/Leadership_in_Energy_and_Environmental_Design" title="Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design">Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design</a> program for making use of sustainable development principles.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(6)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Investments">Investments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=PPL_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Investments" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-6 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-6">
<p>In a speech before the Edison Electric Institute (EEI) Financial Conference in November 2019, William Spence, PPL’s chairman and chief executive officer, said that the company is focusing on building more advanced cleaner energy technologies and is also increasing its effort on a strategy known as "Energy Forward." The company is investing around $15 billion through 2022 to improve infrastructure and technology in order to "create a smarter, more reliable and resilient energy grid," according to Spence. In the third quarter of 2019, PPL completed a $470 million investment in replacing meters with "advanced" meters in Pennsylvania.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(7)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=PPL_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Marketing" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-7 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-7">
<p>In February 2010, the PPL Corporation purchased the naming rights to the venue originally known as <a href="/wiki/PPL_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="PPL Park">PPL Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Chester,_Pennsylvania" title="Chester, Pennsylvania">Chester, Pennsylvania</a> (which is outside of PPL's service territory), the home stadium of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">Major League Soccer</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Union" title="Philadelphia Union">Philadelphia Union</a>.  As part of the $25 million, 11-year deal, PPL EnergyPlus provides <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_energy" title="Sustainable energy">sustainable energy</a> to PPL Park derived from other sources in <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> After PPL spun off its generation business into the separate <a href="/wiki/Talen_Energy" title="Talen Energy">Talen Energy</a>, the stadium naming rights were assumed by the spinoff company, and the venue is now known as <a href="/wiki/Talen_Energy_Stadium" class="mw-redirect" title="Talen Energy Stadium">Talen Energy Stadium</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>PPL also owns the naming rights to the <a href="/wiki/PPL_Center" title="PPL Center">PPL Center</a> in Allentown, which hosts the <a href="/wiki/Lehigh_Valley_Phantoms" title="Lehigh Valley Phantoms">Lehigh Valley Phantoms</a> of the <a href="/wiki/American_Hockey_League" title="American Hockey League">American Hockey League</a> starting with the 2014 season. PPL paid an undisclosed sum over ten years.<sup id="cite_ref-cost_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cost-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
