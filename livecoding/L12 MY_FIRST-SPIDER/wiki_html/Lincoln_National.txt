</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln_National_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Operations" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p><b>LNC divides operations into four business segments:</b>  
</p><p>1) Annuities,  
</p><p>2) Life Insurance,  
</p><p>3) Retirement Plan Services,  
</p><p>4)  Group Protection.  
</p><p><b>The principal Lincoln subsidiaries are</b> 
</p>
<ul><li>Lincoln National Life Insurance Company</li>
<li>Lincoln Life &amp; Annuity Company of New York</li>
<li>First Penn-Pacific Life Insurance Company</li>
<li>Lincoln Financial Distributors</li>
<li>Lincoln Financial Advisors</li>
<li>Lincoln Financial Securities<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li></ul><p>At December 31, 2016, LNC had consolidated assets under management of $262 billion and consolidated shareholders’ equity of $14.5 billion. They have over 27,000 <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.policypedia.com/agent-directory-by-insurer/LINCOLN%20NATIONAL%20LIFE%20INSURANCE%20COMPANY%20(THE)/0/1000000">insurance agents</a> licensed in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.
</p>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lincoln_National_Corporation_Sign,_Fort_Wayne,_Indiana.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Lincoln_National_Corporation_Sign%2C_Fort_Wayne%2C_Indiana.JPG/220px-Lincoln_National_Corporation_Sign%2C_Fort_Wayne%2C_Indiana.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="136" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3728" data-file-height="2312"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 136px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Lincoln_National_Corporation_Sign%2C_Fort_Wayne%2C_Indiana.JPG/220px-Lincoln_National_Corporation_Sign%2C_Fort_Wayne%2C_Indiana.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="136" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lincoln_National_Corporation_Sign,_Fort_Wayne,_Indiana.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The iconic sign on Lincoln's former corporate headquarters in downtown Fort Wayne, Indiana</div></div></div>
<p>Lincoln traces its origin to June 12, 1905, in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Wayne,_Indiana" title="Fort Wayne, Indiana">Fort Wayne, Indiana</a>, as the Lincoln National Life Insurance Company. Perry Randall, a Fort Wayne attorney and entrepreneur, suggested the name "Lincoln," arguing that the name of <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a> would powerfully convey a spirit of integrity. In August, 1905 <a href="/wiki/Robert_Todd_Lincoln" title="Robert Todd Lincoln">Robert Todd Lincoln</a> provided a photograph of his father, along with a letter authorizing the use of his father's likeness and name for company stationery and advertising.
</p><p>In 1928, LNC president Arthur Hall hired Dr. Louis A. Warren, a Lincoln scholar, and in 1929, LNC acquired one of the largest collections of books about Abraham Lincoln in the United States. The Lincoln Museum in Fort Wayne was the second largest Lincoln museum in the country. The Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library and Museum in <a href="/wiki/Springfield,_Illinois" title="Springfield, Illinois">Springfield, Illinois</a> is now the world's largest museum dedicated to the life and times of Abraham Lincoln, after the closing of the Fort Wayne Lincoln Museum June 30, 2008.
</p><p>Ian Rolland started with Lincoln in 1956, and became president of Lincoln National Life in 1977. When he retired in 1998, new president Jon A. Boscia moved LNC to Philadelphia and started using the Lincoln Financial Group name for marketing. Lincoln National Life, the largest subsidiary, and the Lincoln Museum remained in Fort Wayne.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="1990.E2.80.932007"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1990–2007">1990–2007</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln_National_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1990–2007" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Lincoln Reinsurance was the first US <a href="/wiki/Reinsurance" title="Reinsurance">reinsurance</a> company; it was sold to <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Re" title="Swiss Re">Swiss Re</a> in 2001. K&amp;K Insurance Specialties was the first to insure events like <a href="/wiki/NASCAR" title="NASCAR">NASCAR</a> races; it was sold to <a href="/wiki/Aon_Corp." class="mw-redirect" title="Aon Corp.">AON</a> in 1993. <a href="/wiki/Safeco" title="Safeco">Safeco</a> bought American States, a property/casualty insurance business because Lincoln was primarily in life/health. However, LNC even sold a block of disability income business to <a href="/wiki/MetLife" title="MetLife">MetLife</a> in 1999, as it narrowed its focus.
</p><p>Lincoln moved its headquarters from Indiana to Philadelphia in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-DelInv_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DelInv-6">[6]</a></sup> In Philadelphia Lincoln was headquartered in the West Tower of <a href="/wiki/Centre_Square_(Building)" class="mw-redirect" title="Centre Square (Building)">Centre Square</a> in <a href="/wiki/Center_City,_Philadelphia" title="Center City, Philadelphia">Center City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>Lincoln Financial was naming rights sponsor for the <a href="/wiki/2000_Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="2000 Rugby League World Cup">2000 Rugby League World Cup</a> which was held in England.
</p><p>Lincoln Financial Group purchased the Administrative Management Group, Inc. based in Arlington Heights, Illinois in August 2002. Previously, AMG was a strategic partner of LFG for four years, providing recordkeeping services for the Lincoln Alliance product, a turnkey solution for "employer retirement and employee benefit programs, including investment choices, recordkeeping, plan design, compliance and employee retirement counseling and education."<sup id="cite_ref-AMGAcquisition_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AMGAcquisition-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 2007, the company moved 400 employees, including its top executives, to Radnor Township from <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DelInv_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DelInv-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Jefferson-Pilot_acquisition">Jefferson-Pilot acquisition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln_National_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Jefferson-Pilot acquisition" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Following the acquisition of Jefferson-Pilot Corporation in March 2006, Lincoln Financial acquired group life, disability, and dental insurance divisions. Jefferson-Pilot Corporation was a <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> company based in <a href="/wiki/Greensboro,_North_Carolina" title="Greensboro, North Carolina">Greensboro, North Carolina</a> founded in 1968 from the merger of Jefferson Standard Insurance (founded 1907) and Pilot Life Insurance (founded 1903).  The two companies' association actually dated to 1945, when Jefferson Standard bought majority control of Pilot Life.
</p><p>Lincoln Financial also acquired Jefferson-Pilot's television and radio operations, which were renamed <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Financial_Media" title="Lincoln Financial Media">Lincoln Financial Media</a>. The group owns 18 radio stations in <a href="/wiki/Miami,_Florida" class="mw-redirect" title="Miami, Florida">Miami, Florida</a>; <a href="/wiki/San_Diego,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Diego, California">San Diego, California</a>; <a href="/wiki/Denver,_Colorado" class="mw-redirect" title="Denver, Colorado">Denver, Colorado</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlanta, Georgia">Atlanta, Georgia</a>.  It also owned <a href="/wiki/WBTV" title="WBTV">WBTV</a>, the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> affiliate in Charlotte; <a href="/wiki/WCSC-TV" title="WCSC-TV">WCSC-TV</a>, the CBS affiliate in <a href="/wiki/Charleston,_South_Carolina" title="Charleston, South Carolina">Charleston, South Carolina</a> and <a href="/wiki/WWBT" title="WWBT">WWBT</a>, the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> affiliate in <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">Richmond, Virginia</a>.  In June 2007, the company publicly announced it would explore a sale of this division, and hired Merrill Lynch to assess its strategic options.  It was announced on November 12 that <a href="/wiki/Raycom_Media" title="Raycom Media">Raycom Media</a> purchased the three TV stations,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> including its sports production division, which was the co-holder to football and basketball games in the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Coast_Conference" title="Atlantic Coast Conference">Atlantic Coast Conference</a> with Raycom and sole rightsholders to the <a href="/wiki/Southeastern_Conference" title="Southeastern Conference">Southeastern Conference</a> until 2009, when <a href="/wiki/ESPN_Regional_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="ESPN Regional Television">ESPNPlus</a> and <a href="/wiki/CBS_Sports" title="CBS Sports">CBS Sports</a> acquired the rights.  The Raycom Sports brand was merged with LFS as of January 1, 2008.
</p><p>Though billed as a merger of equals, the merged company carries the LNC name, operates from the LNC offices, with current LNC stockholders holding 61% of the stock, and current LNC directors controlling the new board. The insurance division is based in Greensboro, North Carolina.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_activity">Recent activity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln_National_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Recent activity" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Lincoln purchased Newton County Loan and Savings in order to restructure as a <a href="/wiki/Bank_holding_company" title="Bank holding company">bank holding company</a> and qualify for <a href="/wiki/Troubled_Asset_Relief_Program" title="Troubled Asset Relief Program">Troubled Asset Relief Program</a> (TARP) funding.
</p><p>In January 2009, Lincoln sold its <a href="/wiki/Delaware_Investments" title="Delaware Investments">Delaware Investments</a> subsidiary to <a href="/wiki/Macquarie_Group" title="Macquarie Group">Macquarie Group</a>.  Delaware Investments was integrated into Macquarie's global asset management arm, Macquarie Funds Group effective January 5, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Insurance_patent">Insurance patent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln_National_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Insurance patent" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>Lincoln National is the owner of <span><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//www.google.com/patents/US7089201">U.S. Patent 7,089,201</a></span>, “Method and apparatus for providing retirement income benefits”.  This patent covers methods for administering <a href="/wiki/Annuity_(financial_contracts)" class="mw-redirect" title="Annuity (financial contracts)">variable annuities</a>.  Lincoln's commercial products that are covered by this patent include their i4LIFE  Advantage and 4LaterSM Advantage annuities.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2006, Lincoln filed a <a href="/wiki/Patent_infringement" title="Patent infringement">patent infringement</a> lawsuit against <a href="/wiki/Transamerica_Corporation" title="Transamerica Corporation">Transamerica Life Insurance Company</a> for allegedly infringing its <a href="/wiki/Insurance_patent" title="Insurance patent">insurance patent</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template"><span style="white-space: nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Link_rot" title="Wikipedia:Link rot"><span title=" Dead link since February 2019">dead link</span></a></i>]</span></sup> A similar lawsuit was filed against <a href="/wiki/Jackson_National_Life" title="Jackson National Life">Jackson National Life</a> in October 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>On Feb. 19, 2009, a jury found the Lincoln patent valid and infringed by Transamerica et al.  Damages were assessed at the "reasonable royalty rate" and Transamerica et al. were ordered to pay Lincoln $13 million, or 0.11% of the over $12 billion in assets they had under management by virtue of infringing the patent.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Affiliations">Affiliations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln_National_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Affiliations" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">

</p>
