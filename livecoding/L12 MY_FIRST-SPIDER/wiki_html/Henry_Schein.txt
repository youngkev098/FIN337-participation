</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Henry_Schein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>In 1932, Henry Schein, a graduate of <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a> College of Pharmacy, borrowed $500 and opened a pharmacy in <a href="/wiki/Queens,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Queens, New York">Queens, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-barrons_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-barrons-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ce_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ce-9">[9]</a></sup> The company expanded into dental supplies in the 1960s, and by the late 1980s, Henry Schein held approximately 10 percent of the dental-supply market. Marvin Schein, Henry Schein's son, took over management of daily operations in 1971.<sup id="cite_ref-hbs_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbs-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 1978, founder Henry Schein's son Jay Schein became CEO of the company.<sup id="cite_ref-barrons_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-barrons-8">[8]</a></sup> When Jay Schein died of cancer in 1989, Stanley Bergman took over as CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-barrons_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-barrons-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ce_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ce-9">[9]</a></sup> Bergman was born in South Africa and came to New York in 1976, where he became an accountant. In 1980, he joined Henry Schein and was promoted to <a href="/wiki/Chief_financial_officer" title="Chief financial officer">CFO</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-barrons_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-barrons-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>Henry Schein Inc. filed with the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission">U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission</a> in 1995 to sell 6.2 million shares in an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> with <a href="/wiki/William_Blair_%26_Company" title="William Blair &amp; Company">William Blair &amp; Company</a> as the lead underwriter.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The IPO raised $72.8 million and began trading on the Nasdaq.<sup id="cite_ref-ce_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ce-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hbs_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbs-10">[10]</a></sup> By 1996, the company was up to $1.4 billion in sales and $29 million in profit under Bergman's leadership. That year, the company made seventeen acquisitions.<sup id="cite_ref-hbs_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbs-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In August 1997, Henry Schein Inc. agreed to buy Sullivan Dental Products Inc. for approximately $318 million. The purchase made the company the world's largest distributor of dental equipment and supplies. By that point, Henry Schein had made 16 acquisitions in 1997. Also in 1997, Henry Schein acquired New York-based medical-products distributor Micro BioMedics Inc<sup id="cite_ref-dental_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dental-13">[13]</a></sup> as well as Dentrix Dental Systems.<sup id="cite_ref-hbs_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbs-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>Henry Schein announced the acquisition of demedis GmbH and Euro Dental Holding GmbH in June 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In November 2009, it was announced that Henry Schein Inc. and Butler Animal Health Supply would be launching joint venture Butler Schein Animal Health, the largest veterinary sales and distribution company in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Butler Schein Animal Health was rebranded as Henry Schein Animal Health in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Between 1989 and 2016, Henry Schein purchased approximately 200 companies.<sup id="cite_ref-barrons_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-barrons-8">[8]</a></sup> The company's chief executive officer, Stanley Bergman, was announced as <i><a href="/wiki/Chief_Executive_(magazine)" title="Chief Executive (magazine)">Chief Executive Magazine's</a></i> CEO of the Year in May 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Trade_Commission" title="Federal Trade Commission">FTC</a> accused <a href="/wiki/Benco_Dental" title="Benco Dental">Benco Dental</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patterson_Companies" title="Patterson Companies">Patterson Companies</a>, and Henry Schein of violating antitrust laws. In October 2019, the FTC’s Chief Administrative Law Judge dismissed the claims against Henry Schein while finding that Benco and Patterson violated U.S. antitrust laws.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2019, Henry Schein spun off the company's animal health business and merged it with Vets First Choice to form a new company.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The new company is established as Covetrus.<sup id="cite_ref-December_2018_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-December_2018-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>Henry Schein Inc. was one of the six main defendants in lawsuits with state and local governments of the <a href="/wiki/Opioid_epidemic_in_the_United_States" title="Opioid epidemic in the United States">opioid epidemic in the United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-October_2019_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-October_2019-21">[21]</a></sup> The company was dismissed as a defendant in October 2019 in the bellwether Summit County, Ohio litigation.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Dental">Dental</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Henry_Schein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Dental" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1997, Henry Schein acquired Sullivan Dental Products and Dentrix Dental Systems, making it the world's largest distributor of dental equipment and supplies.<sup id="cite_ref-hbs_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbs-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dental_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dental-13">[13]</a></sup> Dentrix Ascend, Henry Schein's cloud-based software designed for dental offices, is a scalable system. Its interface was built to be intuitive and the company works with users to include features dental offices require.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>Henry Schein established the Henry Schein Dental Business Institute in March 2015 to teach owners and operators of dental practices the fundamentals of business. The first class to complete the program graduated in March 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Henry Schein also launched the Henry Schein Digital Dentistry Program at Temple University's Maurice H. Kornberg School of Dentistry in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2016, Henry Schein's entity Dentrix presented the DEXIS software's ability to integrate X-rays into a patient's chart.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Zahn Dental, Henry Schein's laboratory supply business, acquired Custom Automated Prosthetics in June 2016. The acquisition enabled Henry Schein to expand its prosthetic offerings.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Medical">Medical</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Henry_Schein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Medical" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In November 2014, Henry Schein Medical announced a strategic arrangement with Cardinal Health. Cardinal Health's physician office-sales organization consolidated into Henry Schein Medical.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>

<p>Henry Schein has spun off this division in early 2019 <sup id="cite_ref-December_2018_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-December_2018-20">[20]</a></sup> produces software that is used in veterinary practices designed to improve communication and data management including the online cloud-based application, Rapport.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> In February 2016, Henry Schein released Axis-Q, a software application designed to ease the recording and access of veterinary medical records.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
