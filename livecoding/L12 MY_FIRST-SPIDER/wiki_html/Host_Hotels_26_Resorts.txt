</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Host_Hotels_%26_Resorts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>In 1897, the <a href="/wiki/Van_Noy_Brothers" title="Van Noy Brothers">Van Noy Brothers</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City, Missouri</a>, formed <a href="/wiki/Van_Noy_Railway_News_and_Hotel_Company" title="Van Noy Railway News and Hotel Company">Van Noy Railway News and Hotel Company</a> to operate news stands along the <a href="/wiki/Missouri_Pacific" class="mw-redirect" title="Missouri Pacific">Missouri Pacific</a> and other regional railroads. The company later expanded to various <a href="/wiki/Hospitality" title="Hospitality">hospitality</a> services, including hotels. After several name and business changes it became the Host International Company in 1968. It became part of <a href="/wiki/Marriott_Corporation" title="Marriott Corporation">Marriott Corporation</a> in 1982.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Eleven years later Marriot divided its business into two companies, naming one Host Marriott.<sup id="cite_ref-unit_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unit-5">[5]</a></sup> The new company, much like its predecessor, continued to manage travel concessions at airports and along interstate highways. These concession businesses were further spun off as Host Marriot Services, or <a href="/wiki/HMSHost" title="HMSHost">HMSHost</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-unit_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unit-5">[5]</a></sup> After divesting its non-hotel business and reorganizing as a REIT, it adopted its current name in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
