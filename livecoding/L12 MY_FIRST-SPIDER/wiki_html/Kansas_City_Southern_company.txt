<p>Headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City</a>, Missouri, <b>Kansas City Southern</b> (KCS) (NYSE: KSU) is a transportation holding company with railroad investments in the United States, Mexico and Panama.

<p>KCS' primary U.S. holding, The <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Southern_Railway" title="Kansas City Southern Railway">Kansas City Southern Railway</a> (KCS), a <a href="/wiki/Class_I_railroad" class="mw-redirect" title="Class I railroad">Class I railroad</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> that operates about 3,400 route miles (5,472 km)<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> in 10 states in the midwestern and southeastern United States.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> KCS's major <a href="/wiki/Transport_hub" title="Transport hub">hubs</a> include <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City, Missouri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shreveport,_Louisiana" title="Shreveport, Louisiana">Shreveport, Louisiana</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>. Among the Class I railroads, KCS has the shortest route between Kansas City, the second-largest rail hub in the country, and the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-kcsouthern.com_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kcsouthern.com-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>KCS' primary international holding is <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Southern_de_M%C3%A9xico" title="Kansas City Southern de México">Kansas City Southern de México</a> (KCSM), which  operates about 3,300 route miles (5,311 km) in 15 states in northeastern, central, southeast-central and southwest-central Mexico. KCSM reaches the Gulf of Mexico ports of Tampico, Altamira, and Veracruz, and the Pacific Ocean deepwater <a href="/wiki/List_of_busiest_container_ports" title="List of busiest container ports">container port</a> of <a href="/wiki/L%C3%A1zaro_C%C3%A1rdenas,_Michoac%C3%A1n" title="Lázaro Cárdenas, Michoacán">Lázaro Cárdenas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-forbes_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-6">[6]</a></sup> KCS obtained 100% of ownership of KCSM in 2005, making KCS the only Class I Railroad to own track in Mexico's.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>KCS international holdings also include a 50% interest in Panama Canal Railway Company (PCRC), which operates the <a href="/wiki/Panama_Canal_Railway" title="Panama Canal Railway">Panama Canal Railway</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> providing ocean-to-ocean freight and passenger service along the Panama Canal. PCRC provides ocean-to-ocean <a href="/wiki/Transshipment" title="Transshipment">transshipment</a> service between the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlantic">Atlantic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific oceans</a>. The 47.6-mile (76.6 km) railroad serves as an  <a href="/wiki/Intermodal_freight_transport" title="Intermodal freight transport">intermodal</a> line for world commerce and complements the existing transportation infrastructure provided by the Canal, the <a href="/wiki/Col%C3%B3n_Free_Trade_Zone" title="Colón Free Trade Zone">Colón Free Trade Zone</a> and the Pacific and Atlantic ports. PCRC's wholly owned subsidiary, Panarail Tourism, offers passenger service for business commuters, tourists and private <a href="/wiki/Charters" class="mw-redirect" title="Charters">charters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-kcsouthern.com_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kcsouthern.com-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>Patrick J. Ottensmeyer  was named President in April 2015<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and CEO in June 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> succeeding <a href="/wiki/David_L._Starling" title="David L. Starling">David Starling</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>   Ottensmeyer had served as Executive Vice President of Sales and Marketing and Chief Financial Officer. He was named Railroader of the Year by <a href="/wiki/Railway_Age" title="Railway Age">Railway Age</a> for 2020.  
</p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kansas_City_Southern_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Arthur_Stilwell.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Arthur_Stilwell.jpg/150px-Arthur_Stilwell.jpg" decoding="async" width="150" height="195" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="200" data-file-height="260"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 150px;height: 195px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Arthur_Stilwell.jpg/150px-Arthur_Stilwell.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="150" data-height="195" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Arthur_Stilwell.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Arthur_Edward_Stilwell" class="mw-redirect" title="Arthur Edward Stilwell">Arthur Stilwell</a>, founder of KCS</div></div></div>
<p>In 1887, <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Edward_Stilwell" class="mw-redirect" title="Arthur Edward Stilwell">Arthur Edward Stilwell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Edward_L._Martin" title="Edward L. Martin">Edward L. Martin</a> began construction on and incorporated the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Suburban_Belt_Railway" class="mw-redirect" title="Kansas City Suburban Belt Railway">Kansas City Suburban Belt Railway</a> in suburban <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City, Missouri</a>. Beginning operations in 1890, the railroad served the <a href="/wiki/Argentine,_Kansas" title="Argentine, Kansas">Argentine District</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Kansas" title="Kansas City, Kansas">Kansas City, Kansas</a>; <a href="/wiki/Independence,_Missouri" title="Independence, Missouri">Independence, Missouri</a>; and the riverside commercial and industrial districts of Kansas City.
</p><p>While the Belt Railway was a success, Stilwell had a much bigger dream. Over the ensuing decade, the line grew through construction and acquisition of other roads, such as the <a href="/wiki/Texarkana_and_Fort_Smith_Railway" title="Texarkana and Fort Smith Railway">Texarkana and Fort Smith Railway</a>, to become a through route between Kansas City and <a href="/wiki/Port_Arthur,_Texas" title="Port Arthur, Texas">Port Arthur, Texas</a>. With the final spike being driven north of <a href="/wiki/Beaumont,_Texas" title="Beaumont, Texas">Beaumont, Texas</a>, on September 11, 1897, the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Pittsburg_%26_Gulf" class="mw-redirect" title="Kansas City, Pittsburg &amp; Gulf">Kansas City, Pittsburg and Gulf Railroad Company</a> (KCP&amp;G) was completed. In 1900, KCP&amp;G became The Kansas City Southern Railway Company (KCS).<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In 1939, KCS acquired the <a href="/wiki/Louisiana_and_Arkansas_Railway" title="Louisiana and Arkansas Railway">Louisiana and Arkansas Railway</a> (L&amp;A), providing a route extending from <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a> to <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>, via <a href="/wiki/Shreveport,_Louisiana" title="Shreveport, Louisiana">Shreveport, Louisiana</a>.
</p><p>In 1962, under the name Kansas City Southern Industries, Inc. (KCSI), the company was formally organized as it began to diversify its interests into other industries under the CEO <a href="/wiki/William_N._Deramus_III" title="William N. Deramus III">William Deramus III</a>. The new KCSI focused primarily on the financial industry, along with the rail industry. In 1969, KCSI started the two largest companies that came out of the diversification: DST Systems and <a href="/wiki/Janus_Capital_Group" title="Janus Capital Group">Janus Capital Group</a>, which was known as Stilwell Financial at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> DST Systems is a software development firm that specializes in information processing and management, with the goal of improving efficiency, productivity, and customer service.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Janus Capital Group is a finance firm that provides growth and risk-managed investment strategies.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>The core KCSI rail system changed little until the 1990s, when the purchase of <a href="/wiki/MidSouth_Rail" class="mw-redirect" title="MidSouth Rail">MidSouth Rail</a> extended KCSI's reach east from Shreveport into <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a>. Combined with existing KCSI routes, this created an east-west mainline marketed as the <a href="/wiki/Meridian_Speedway" title="Meridian Speedway">Meridian Speedway</a>. Another acquisition, the <a href="/wiki/Gateway_Western_Railway" title="Gateway Western Railway">Gateway Western Railway</a>, extended KCS's reach from Kansas City to <a href="/wiki/St._Louis,_Missouri" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Louis, Missouri">St. Louis, Missouri</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Springfield,_Illinois" title="Springfield, Illinois">Springfield, Illinois</a>.
</p><p>The 1990s also saw KCSI expand into <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> with the acquisition of partial interests in the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Mexican_Railway" title="Texas Mexican Railway">Texas Mexican Railway</a> (TM) and <a href="/wiki/Grupo_Transportaci%C3%B3n_Ferroviaria_Mexicana" class="mw-redirect" title="Grupo Transportación Ferroviaria Mexicana">Grupo Transportación Ferroviaria Mexicana</a> (TFM).  TFM was created when KCSI and Transportacion Maritima Mexicana (TMM) purchased a <a href="/wiki/Concession_(contract)" title="Concession (contract)">government concession</a> to operate a rail system in Mexico. The concession was also bid on by many other major companies, including the United States' largest railroad, <a href="/wiki/Union_Pacific_Railroad" title="Union Pacific Railroad">Union Pacific Railroad</a>. KCSI and TMM bid on, and won, the concession for $1.4 billion <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>, paying 49% and 51%, respectively. TMM already partially owned the Texas Mexican Railway through a previous concession from the Mexican government. TM was particularly important to KCSI because they held the link from KCSI tracks to TFM tracks via <a href="/wiki/Trackage_rights" class="mw-redirect" title="Trackage rights">trackage rights</a> over the Union Pacific line.
</p><p>Shortly after acquiring the Mexican government's concession, KCSI entered into another joint venture to purchase a government concession.  On June 19, 1998, the government of <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a> turned over control of the <a href="/wiki/Panama_Canal_Railway" title="Panama Canal Railway">Panama Canal Railway</a> to Kansas City Southern Railroad and the privately held Lanigan Holdings, LLC. This created the <a href="/wiki/Panama_Canal_Railway_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="Panama Canal Railway Company">Panama Canal Railway Company</a> (PCRC).
</p><p>After these large <a href="/wiki/Financial_capital" title="Financial capital">capital</a> outputs, KCSI needed new money to improve the Mexican and Panamanian concessions they had purchased, and to continue to make <a href="/wiki/Capital_expenditures" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital expenditures">capital expenditures</a> in the future. To fund these efforts, KCSI <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spun off</a> all assets that were not essential to the rail businesses. Doing this essentially paid off the purchase of their two existing concessions and freed up money to improve them.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The first major improvement that took place was in 2000 and 2001 when the PCRC upgraded the railway to handle large, <a href="/wiki/Intermodal_freight_transport" title="Intermodal freight transport">intermodal shipping containers</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Passenger_car_(rail)" title="Passenger car (rail)">passenger transport</a>.
</p><p>In 2002, the Kansas City Southern Industries formally changed its name to Kansas City Southern (KCS) after spinning off many subsidiary businesses that were not directly related to the railroad business (the largest of which were <a href="/wiki/Janus_Capital_Group" title="Janus Capital Group">Janus Capital Group</a> and DST Systems).<sup id="cite_ref-Kansas_City_Southern_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kansas_City_Southern-20">[20]</a></sup> In 2005, Kansas City Southern purchased TMM's share in TFM and TM, giving them full ownership of the companies. TFM was officially renamed <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Southern_de_M%C3%A9xico" title="Kansas City Southern de México">Kansas City Southern de México, S.A. de C.V.</a><sup id="cite_ref-Kansas_City_Southern_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kansas_City_Southern-20">[20]</a></sup>  The Texas Mexican Railway retained its original name and is a <a href="/wiki/Subsidiary" title="Subsidiary">subsidiary</a> of KCS.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2009, the Kansas City Southern began operating on new trackage between <a href="/wiki/Victoria,_Texas" title="Victoria, Texas">Victoria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rosenberg,_Texas" title="Rosenberg, Texas">Rosenberg</a>, Texas, known as the Macaroni Line
.<sup id="cite_ref-trains_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-trains-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Subsidiaries">Subsidiaries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kansas_City_Southern_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subsidiaries" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Panama_Canal_Railway.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Panama_Canal_Railway.jpg/250px-Panama_Canal_Railway.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="167" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="450" data-file-height="300"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 250px;height: 167px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Panama_Canal_Railway.jpg/250px-Panama_Canal_Railway.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="250" data-height="167" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Panama_Canal_Railway.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A <a href="/wiki/Panama_Canal_Railway" title="Panama Canal Railway">Panama Canal Railway</a> <a href="/wiki/Locomotive" title="Locomotive">locomotive</a> sits in <a href="/wiki/Train_station" title="Train station">a station</a> at <a href="/wiki/Col%C3%B3n,_Panama" title="Colón, Panama">Colón, Panama</a></div></div></div>
<p>In addition to KCS, KCSM and PCRC, Kansas City Southern’s subsidiaries include:
</p>
<ul><li>Gateway Eastern Railway Company (<a href="/wiki/Gateway_Eastern_Railway" title="Gateway Eastern Railway">GWWE</a>) is a wholly owned subsidiary of KCS.  GWWE provides rail service over about 17 miles (27 km) of track in the <a href="/wiki/East_St._Louis,_Illinois" title="East St. Louis, Illinois">East St. Louis, Illinois</a>, area.<sup id="cite_ref-kcsouthern.com_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kcsouthern.com-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>Meridian Speedway, LLC (MSLLC) is a majority-owned, consolidated subsidiary that owns the former KCS rail line between <a href="/wiki/Meridian,_Mississippi" title="Meridian, Mississippi">Meridian, Mississippi</a> and Shreveport, Louisiana, which is a portion of the KCS rail route between Dallas and Meridian, and is known as the "<a href="/wiki/Meridian_Speedway" title="Meridian Speedway">Meridian Speedway</a>." <a href="/wiki/Norfolk_Southern_Railway" title="Norfolk Southern Railway">Norfolk Southern Corporation</a> (NS), through its wholly owned subsidiary, the <a href="/wiki/Alabama_Great_Southern_Railroad" title="Alabama Great Southern Railroad">Alabama Great Southern Railroad</a>, owns a <a href="/wiki/Minority_interest" title="Minority interest">minority interest</a> in MSLLC.<sup id="cite_ref-kcsouthern.com_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kcsouthern.com-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>Southern Capital, Development, and Industrial Services Companies: The Southern Capital company consists of <a href="/wiki/Bulk_material_handling" title="Bulk material handling">bulk storage facilities</a> complete with <a href="/wiki/Port" title="Port">ocean terminals</a> (Pabtex) along with a <a href="/wiki/Railroad_tie" title="Railroad tie">tie</a> and timber plant (Trans-Serve, Inc., also known as Superior Tie and Timber).  The Southern Development Company is a holding company that owns various properties.  The Southern Capital Corporation, LLC is a leasing company owned jointly by KCS and <a href="/wiki/GATX" title="GATX">GATX Capital Corporation</a> of <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco, California</a>, Southern Capital Corporation has the rail assets that once belonged to Carland and KCS, as well as the loan portfolio once owned by Southern Leasing Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-kcsouthern.com_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kcsouthern.com-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>The Texas Mexican Railway Company (<a href="/wiki/Texas_Mexican_Railway" title="Texas Mexican Railway">TexMex</a>) is wholly owned by KCS. TexMex consists of a 157-mile (253 km) railway that connects <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi,_Texas" title="Corpus Christi, Texas">Corpus Christi, Texas</a> to <a href="/wiki/Laredo,_Texas" title="Laredo, Texas">Laredo, Texas</a>. TexMex also owns 400 miles (640 km) of Union Pacific <a href="/wiki/Trackage_rights" class="mw-redirect" title="Trackage rights">trackage rights</a> that spans from <a href="/wiki/Beaumont,_Texas" title="Beaumont, Texas">Beaumont, Texas</a> to <a href="/wiki/Robstown,_Texas" title="Robstown, Texas">Robstown, Texas</a>. With its trackage rights and physical railway, the Texas Mexican Railway connects KCSM and KCS at Laredo and Beaumont. TexMex also owns the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Mexican_Railway_International_Bridge" title="Texas Mexican Railway International Bridge">Texas Mexican Railway International Bridge</a>. It is the only rail bridge that connects the United States with Mexico through Laredo, and 40% of all rail traffic that travels to Mexico crosses over this bridge. Without TexMex, it would be nearly impossible for KCS and KCSM to act as a single company under Kansas City Southern.<sup id="cite_ref-kcsouthern.com_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kcsouthern.com-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>KCS also owns a handful of non-core businesses.  These minor subsidiaries, holding companies or minority investments (investments in which KCS has less than 50 percent ownership), have few employees and serve to support the rail operation. These include Canama Transportation, Caymex Transportation, Inc., Rosenberg Regional LLC, Joplin Union Depot, <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Terminal_Railway" title="Kansas City Terminal Railway">Kansas City Terminal Railway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Port_Arthur,_Texas" title="Port Arthur, Texas">Port Arthur</a> Bulk Marine Terminal Co. and Veals, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-kcsouthern.com_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kcsouthern.com-5">[5]</a></sup></li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Pop_culture">Pop culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kansas_City_Southern_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pop culture" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
