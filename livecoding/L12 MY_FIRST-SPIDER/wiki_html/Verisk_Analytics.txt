</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Verisk_Analytics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company’s <a href="/wiki/Insurance_Services_Office" title="Insurance Services Office">Insurance Services Office</a> (ISO) subsidiary was created in 1971 through the consolidation of various state, regional, and national rating bureaus for various lines of <a href="/wiki/Property_insurance" title="Property insurance">property/casualty insurance</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AnnualReport_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AnnualReport-1">[1]</a></sup> ISO helps insurers with product development, underwriting, and rating. In 2008, Verisk Analytics was established to serve as the parent holding company of ISO. In 2009, Verisk completed its IPO and became a publicly traded company.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>Since 2000, the company has acquired about 30 new businesses, which have helped broaden its product offerings. In 2002, it expanded its decision analytics business with the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/AIR_Worldwide" title="AIR Worldwide">AIR Worldwide</a>, a catastrophe modeling firm.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2004, it entered the healthcare market by acquiring several businesses, through which it offers analytical and reporting systems for health insurers, provider organizations, and self-insured employers. In 2006, the company expanded in the insurance claims sector with the acquisition of Xactware, a provider of estimating software for building repair and construction.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2010, Verisk acquired 3E Company, a provider of services that help customers comply with government-mandated environmental health and safety requirements.<sup id="cite_ref-Reuters2010December7ShankarArchana_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters2010December7ShankarArchana-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012, Verisk acquired Argus  &amp; Advisory Services, a provider of , competitive benchmarking, scoring solutions<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Use_plain_English#Buzzwords" title="Wikipedia:Use plain English"><span title="The material near this tag may use buzzwords designed solely to impress you or obscure meaning. (June 2019)">buzzword</span></a></i>]</sup>, analytics, and customized services to financial institutions and regulators in North America, Latin America, and Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-BloombergBusinessweek2012August8BuhayarNoah_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BloombergBusinessweek2012August8BuhayarNoah-8">[8]</a></sup> Also in 2012, Verisk acquired Aspect Loss Prevention, a provider of loss prevention software and analytic solutions<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Use_plain_English#Buzzwords" title="Wikipedia:Use plain English"><span title="The material near this tag may use buzzwords designed solely to impress you or obscure meaning.
