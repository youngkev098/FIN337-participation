</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_history">Corporate history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=F5_Networks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Corporate history" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>F5 Networks, originally named F5 Labs,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> was established in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-form-10k_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-form-10k-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In 1997, F5 launched its first product<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> a load balancer called BIG-IP. When a server went down or became overloaded, BIG-IP directed traffic away from that server to other servers that could handle the load.
</p><p>In June 1999, the company had its <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> and was listed on the NASDAQ stock exchange with symbol FFIV.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 2010 and 2011, F5 Networks was on <i><a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)">Fortune</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s list of 100 Fastest-Growing Companies.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The company was also rated one of the top ten best-performing stocks by S&amp;P 500 in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
F5 was also named a Best Place to Work by online jobs and recruiting site <a href="/wiki/Glassdoor" title="Glassdoor">Glassdoor</a> in 2015 and 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>Competitors included <a href="/wiki/Cisco_Systems" title="Cisco Systems">Cisco Systems</a> (until 2012),<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Citrix" class="mw-redirect" title="Citrix">Citrix</a> Systems, and <a href="/wiki/Radware" title="Radware">Radware</a>.
</p><p>François Locoh-Donou replaced John McAdam as president and CEO on April 3, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>On May 3, 2017, F5 announced<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> that it would move from its longtime headquarters on the waterfront near Seattle Center to a new downtown Seattle skyscraper that will be called <a href="/wiki/F5_Tower" title="F5 Tower">F5 Tower</a>. The move occurred in early 2019.
</p><p>In 2017 F5 launched a dedicated site and organization focused on gathering global threat intelligence data, analyzing application threats, and publishing related findings, dubbed “<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.f5.com/labs">F5 Labs</a>” in a nod to the company's history. The team continues to research application threats and publish findings every week to benefit the broader security community.
</p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="BIG-IP">BIG-IP</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=F5_Networks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: BIG-IP" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>F5's BIG-IP product family comprises hardware, modularized software, and <a href="/wiki/Virtual_appliance" title="Virtual appliance">virtual appliances</a> that run the F5 TMOS operating system.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-embedded-tmos_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-embedded-tmos-29">[29]</a></sup>  Depending on the appliance selected, one or more BIG-IP product modules can be added.
Offerings include:
</p>
<ul><li><i>Local Traffic Manager (LTM)</i>: Local <a href="/wiki/Load_balancing_(computing)" title="Load balancing (computing)">load balancing</a> based on a full-<a href="/wiki/Proxy_server" title="Proxy server">proxy</a> architecture.</li>
<li><i>Application Security Manager (ASM)</i>: A web <a href="/wiki/Application_firewall" title="Application firewall">application firewall</a>.</li>
<li><i>Access Policy Manager (APM)</i>: Provides access control and authentication for HTTP and HTTPS applications.</li>
<li><i>Advanced Firewall Manager (AFM)</i>: On-premises DDoS protection, data centre firewall.</li>
<li><i>Application Acceleration Manager (AAM)</i>: through technologies such as compression and caching.</li>
<li><i>IP Intelligence (IPI)</i>: Blocking known bad IP addresses, prevention of phishing attacks and botnets.</li>
<li><i>WebSafe</i>: Protects against sophisticated fraud threats, leveraging advanced encryption, client-less malware detection and session behavioral analysis capabilities.</li>
<li><i>BIG-IP DNS</i>: Distributes DNS and application requests based user, network, and cloud performance conditions.</li></ul><h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="BIG-IP_history"><span id="TMM"></span>BIG-IP history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=F5_Networks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: BIG-IP history" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>On September 7, 2004, F5 Networks released version 9.0 of the BIG-IP software in addition to appliances to run the software. Version 9.0 also marked the introduction of the company's TMOS architecture,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> with significant enhancements including:
</p>
<ul><li>Moved from <a href="/wiki/BSD" class="mw-redirect" title="BSD">BSD</a> to <a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a> to handle system management functions (disks, logging, bootup, console access, etc.)</li>
<li>Creation of a Traffic Management Microkernel (TMM) to directly talk to the networking hardware and handle all network activities.<sup id="cite_ref-embedded-tmos_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-embedded-tmos-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></li>
<li>Creation of the standard full-proxy mode, which fully terminates network connections at the BIG-IP and establishes new connections between the BIG-IP and the member servers in a pool. This allows for optimum TCP stacks on both sides as well as the complete ability to modify traffic in either direction.</li></ul><p>Subsequent releases enhanced performance, improves application security, and supported cloud application deployments.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="BIG-IQ">BIG-IQ</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=F5_Networks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: BIG-IQ" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>F5 describes BIG-IQ as a framework for managing BIG-IP devices and application services, irrespective of their form factors (hardware, software or cloud) or deployment model (on-premises, private/public cloud or hybrid). BIG-IQ supports integration with other ecosystem participants such as public cloud providers, and orchestration engines through cloud connectors and through a set of open <a href="/wiki/RESTful" class="mw-redirect" title="RESTful">RESTful</a> <a href="/wiki/API" class="mw-redirect" title="API">APIs</a>. BIG-IQ uses a <a href="/wiki/Multi-tenant" class="mw-redirect" title="Multi-tenant">multi-tenant</a> approach to management. This allows organizations to move closer to IT as a Service without concern that it might affect the stability or security of the services fabric.<a href="/wiki/F5_Networks#cite_note-24" title="F5 Networks"><sup>[24]</sup></a>
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Silverline">Silverline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=F5_Networks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Silverline" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Silverline is a cloud-based application service. Its offerings include security services such as WAF and DDoS protection services.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Cloud.2C_container_and_orchestration_solutions"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Cloud,_container_and_orchestration_solutions">Cloud, container and orchestration solutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=F5_Networks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Cloud, container and orchestration solutions" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2017, the company introduced technologies to make F5 capabilities more portable across a broader range of IT environments, including:<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<ul><li><b>Application Services Proxy</b> is an automated traffic management proxy that provides F5 services (and service portability) with containerized environments.</li>
<li><b>Container Connector</b> combines F5's application services platforms (including BIG-IP and Application Services Proxy) with native container environment management and orchestration systems such as Kubernetes, RedHat OpenShift, Pivotal Cloud Foundry, and Mesos.</li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=F5_Networks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: References" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
