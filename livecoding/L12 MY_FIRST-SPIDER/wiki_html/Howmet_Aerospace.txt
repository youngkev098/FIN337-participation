</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Howmet_History_Before_Alcoa_Acquisition">Howmet History Before Alcoa Acquisition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Howmet_Aerospace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Howmet History Before Alcoa Acquisition" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Howmet's roots can be traced back to 1926 with the founding of Austenal, a company which manufactured raw materials and process for dental appliances. Austenal founders, Reiner Erdle and Charles Prange worked to improve investment chrome base castings utilizing two separate investments: The first coating named 'protective coat" giving a smooth finish was smothered with alcohol binder investment to obtain a correct expansion. This technology replaced gold alloy with <a href="/wiki/Vitallium" title="Vitallium">vitallium</a> and was popular in depression time. During the 1930s, Austenal expanded into aircraft engine <a href="/wiki/Superchargers" class="mw-redirect" title="Superchargers">superchargers</a> with superior castings when <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a> asked for help to improve manufacturing practices for wartime production demands.
</p><p>Howe Sound Company, a metals and mining business, acquired Austenal in 1958, and a year later in 1959, Howe acquired Michigan Steel Casting Co. (MISCO), which provided the monolithic shell process, which uses a ceramic shell with thin, strong walls to increase control of the solidification process to produce a sounder <a href="/wiki/Casting" title="Casting">casting</a>.
</p><p>Howe became Howmet in 1965, marking a transition from a mining company to a manufacturer of precision metal products. Howmet was purchased in 1975 by <a href="/wiki/Pechiney" title="Pechiney">Pechiney</a>, a multinational aluminum company. In 1989, Pechiney purchased the Cercast group of companies, bringing Howmet into the aluminum casting industry.
</p><p>In 1995, Pechiney sold Howmet to a joint venture between <a href="/wiki/Thiokol" title="Thiokol">Thiokol</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Carlyle_Group" title="The Carlyle Group">The Carlyle Group</a>. By late 1997 the ownership structure of Howmet had morphed into a distribution of Thiokol owning 62%, Carlyle 23%, and the public 15%. Thiokol later in 1998 changed its name to Cordant Technologies Inc. By February 1999, Cordant had acquired an 84.7% stake in Howmet.
</p><p>In 2000, Cordant sold its stake in Howmet Corp. to <a href="/wiki/Alcoa" title="Alcoa">Alcoa</a>, which merged Howmet into its Alcoa Industrial Components unit. In 2004, Howmet was merged to form the Alcoa Investment Casting and Forged Products unit. In 2007, Howmet was renamed Alcoa Howmet as a division of the newly formed Alcoa Power and Propulsion unit. 
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History_As_Alcoa_Inc._and_Arconic_Inc.">History As Alcoa Inc. and Arconic Inc.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Howmet_Aerospace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History As Alcoa Inc. and Arconic Inc." data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>On November 1, 2016, Alcoa Inc. spun off its <a href="/wiki/Bauxite" title="Bauxite">bauxite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alumina" class="mw-redirect" title="Alumina">alumina</a>, and aluminum operations to a new company called <a href="/wiki/Alcoa" title="Alcoa">Alcoa Corp.</a><sup id="cite_ref-NBR_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NBR-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CNBC_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNBC-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bloomberg_no_junk_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bloomberg_no_junk-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Alcoa_Inc._Press_Release_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alcoa_Inc._Press_Release-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Dubious_Corporate_Practices_Get_a_Rubber_Stamp_From_Big_Investors_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dubious_Corporate_Practices_Get_a_Rubber_Stamp_From_Big_Investors-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>Alcoa Inc. was renamed Arconic Inc., and retained the operations in aluminum rolling (excluding the Warrick operations), aluminum plate, precision castings, and aerospace and industrial fasteners.<sup id="cite_ref-Alcoa_Inc._Press_Release_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alcoa_Inc._Press_Release-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Arconic_sells_60_percent_stake_in_Alcoa_for_$890_million_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Arconic_sells_60_percent_stake_in_Alcoa_for_%24890_million-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Dubious_Corporate_Practices_Get_a_Rubber_Stamp_From_Big_Investors_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dubious_Corporate_Practices_Get_a_Rubber_Stamp_From_Big_Investors-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Wall_Street_Journal_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wall_Street_Journal-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-The_Motley_Fool_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Motley_Fool-11">[11]</a></sup> It focuses on turning aluminum and other lightweight metals into engineered products such as <a href="/wiki/Turbine" title="Turbine">turbine</a> blades for sectors including aerospace and automotive.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bloomberg_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bloomberg-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bloomberg2_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bloomberg2-14">[14]</a></sup> It trades on the <a href="/wiki/NYSE" class="mw-redirect" title="NYSE">NYSE</a> under the <i>ARNC</i> ticker.<sup id="cite_ref-LancasterOnline_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LancasterOnline-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Pittsburgh_Post-Gazette_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pittsburgh_Post-Gazette-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Fortune_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fortune-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>On January 31, 2017, the hedge fund <a href="/wiki/Elliott_Management_Corporation" title="Elliott Management Corporation">Elliott Management Corporation</a> launched a <a href="/wiki/Proxy_contest" class="mw-redirect" title="Proxy contest">proxy contest</a> against the company. Elliott publicly called for the firing of then CEO, <a href="/wiki/Klaus_Kleinfeld" title="Klaus Kleinfeld">Klaus Kleinfeld</a> citing the company's lackluster stock performance, missed profit forecasts and inefficient spending.<sup id="cite_ref-Wall_Street_Journal,_April_18_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wall_Street_Journal,_April_18-18">[18]</a></sup> On April 17, 2017, Klaus Kleinfeld resigned as chairman and CEO by mutual agreement with the board of Arconic, after sending an unauthorized letter to Elliott.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>On February 8, 2019, the company announced that it would split into two separate businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Arconic Inc. would be renamed Howmet Aerospace Inc. and a new company, Arconic Corp., would be set up and spun out of Arconic Inc. Arconic Corp. will be focused on rolled aluminium products and Howmet Aerospace on engineered products.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The separation was scheduled to become effective on April 1, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>On November 1, 2016, Alcoa Inc. spun off its bauxite, alumina, and aluminum operations to a new company called Alcoa Corp. Alcoa Inc. was renamed Arconic Inc., and retained the operations in aluminum rolling (excluding the Warrick operations), aluminum plate, precision castings, and aerospace and industrial fasteners. Howmet became a part of Arconic Inc. specializing in the <a href="/wiki/Investment_casting" title="Investment casting">investment casting</a> of <a href="/wiki/Superalloys" class="mw-redirect" title="Superalloys">superalloys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aluminum" class="mw-redirect" title="Aluminum">aluminum</a>  and <a href="/wiki/Titanium" title="Titanium">titanium</a> primarily for jet aircraft and industrial <a href="/wiki/Gas_turbine" title="Gas turbine">gas turbine</a> (IGT) engine components. Howmet also provided <a href="/wiki/Hot_isostatic_pressing" title="Hot isostatic pressing">hot isostatic pressing</a>, titanium ingots and protective coating services.
</p><p>Howmet Aerospace Inc. was renamed from Arconic Inc. on April 1, 2020, with a new company <a href="/wiki/Arconic" title="Arconic">Arconic Corporation</a> spun out.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
