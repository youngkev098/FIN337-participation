</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gartner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company was founded in 1979 by <a href="/wiki/Gideon_Gartner" title="Gideon Gartner">Gideon Gartner</a>. Originally a private company, the Gartner Group was launched publicly the first time in the 1980s, then acquired by <a href="/wiki/Saatchi_%26_Saatchi" title="Saatchi &amp; Saatchi">Saatchi &amp; Saatchi</a>, a London-based advertising agency, and then acquired in 1990 by some of its executives, with funding from <a href="/wiki/Bain_Capital" title="Bain Capital">Bain Capital</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dun_%26_Bradstreet" title="Dun &amp; Bradstreet">Dun &amp; Bradstreet</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>  The company went public again in 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2000 the name was simplified to Gartner.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>Gene Hall has been the <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">CEO</a> of the company since August 2004.
</p><p>In the course of its growth, Gartner has acquired numerous companies providing related services, including Real Decisions—which became Gartner Measurement, now part of Gartner's consulting division—and <a href="/wiki/Gartner_Dataquest" class="mw-redirect" title="Gartner Dataquest">Gartner Dataquest</a>, a market research firm. It has also acquired a number of direct competitors, including NewScience in the late 1990s, <a href="/wiki/Meta_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Meta Group">Meta Group</a> in 2005, <a href="/wiki/AMR_Research" title="AMR Research">AMR Research</a> and Burton Group in early 2010, and <a href="/wiki/Ideas_International" title="Ideas International">Ideas International</a> in 2012.
</p><p>In March 2014 Gartner announced that it had acquired the privately held company <a href="/wiki/Software_Advice" title="Software Advice">Software Advice</a> for an undisclosed amount.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2015, Gartner acquired Nubera, the business app discovery network that owns properties like <i>GetApp (a peer review site)</i>, <i>AppStorm</i>, <i>AppAppeal</i>, and <i>CloudWork</i>. Terms of the deal were not disclosed.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In September 2015 it acquired the privately held peer review site (PRS)<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Capterra" title="Capterra">Capterra</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2016 Gartner announced that it had acquired the privately held company <i>SCM World</i>, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">U.K.</a><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>On January 5, 2017 Gartner announced it had reached an agreement to acquire <a href="/wiki/CEB,_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="CEB, Inc.">CEB, Inc.</a> in a cash and stock deal worth about US$2.6 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>On March 7, 2017 Gartner announced that it has agreed to buy<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> New York-based <a href="/wiki/L2_Inc" title="L2 Inc">L2 Inc</a>, which specialises in benchmarking the digital performance of brands. Terms of the deal were not disclosed.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
