Group">Fox Entertainment Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The spin-out was structured so that 21st Century Fox would be the legal successor and continuation of the old News Corporation, with the new News Corp being an entirely new company formed by a <a href="/wiki/Stock_split" title="Stock split">stock split</a>. Following the <a href="/wiki/Acquisition_of_21st_Century_Fox_by_Disney" title="Acquisition of 21st Century Fox by Disney">acquisition of most of 21st Century Fox assets</a> by <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">The Walt Disney Company</a> on March 20, 2019,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Fox_Corporation" title="Fox Corporation">Fox Corporation</a>, which is the News Corp's sister company and the successor of former 21st Century Fox that focused on American broadcasting, news and sports businesses, is also owned and controlled by the <a href="/wiki/Murdoch_family" title="Murdoch family">Murdoch family</a>.
</p><p>Its notable assets include <a href="/wiki/Dow_Jones_%26_Company" title="Dow Jones &amp; Company">Dow Jones &amp; Company</a> (publisher of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a></i>), <a href="/wiki/News_UK" title="News UK">News UK</a> (publisher of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sun_(United_Kingdom)" title="The Sun (United Kingdom)">The Sun</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i>), <a href="/wiki/News_Corp_Australia" title="News Corp Australia">News Corp Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Realtor.com" title="Realtor.com">Realtor.com</a> as well as book publisher <a href="/wiki/HarperCollins" title="HarperCollins">HarperCollins</a>.
</p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=News_Corp_(2013%E2%80%93present)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=News_Corp_(2013%E2%80%93present)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On June 28, 2012, <a href="/wiki/Rupert_Murdoch" title="Rupert Murdoch">Rupert Murdoch</a> announced that <a href="/wiki/News_Corporation_(1980%E2%80%932013)" title="News Corporation (1980–2013)">News Corporation</a>'s publishing operations would be spun off to form a new, publicly traded company.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcn-split_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcn-split-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bb-newscorpbreakup_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bb-newscorpbreakup-5">[5]</a></sup> Murdoch stated that performing this split would "unlock the true value of both companies and their distinct assets, enabling investors to benefit from the separate strategic opportunities resulting from more focused management of each division". The move also came in the wake of a series of <a href="/wiki/2011_News_Corporation_scandals" title="2011 News Corporation scandals">scandals</a> that had damaged the reputation of multiple News Corporation-owned properties.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcn-split_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcn-split-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lat-splitapproved_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lat-splitapproved-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Robert_James_Thomson" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert James Thomson">Robert James Thomson</a>, editor of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a></i>, was announced as the initial chief operating officer for the company; while Murdoch would not serve as CEO, he remained chairman and a shareholder of the new News Corp.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcn-split_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcn-split-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bd-newnews_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bd-newnews-7">[7]</a></sup> Thomson promised that the new company would "cultivate a <a href="/wiki/Startup_company" title="Startup company">start-up</a> sensibility even though we already work for the world's most established and prestigious diversified media and information services company", and would emphasize building new business models around its properties and content.<sup id="cite_ref-newnc-logo_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newnc-logo-8">[8]</a></sup> The logo of the new News Corporation was unveiled at an investor presentation on May 28, 2013; the <a href="/wiki/Handwriting" title="Handwriting">handwritten</a> logo uses script based on Murdoch's own handwriting.<sup id="cite_ref-newnc-logo_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newnc-logo-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>News Corp's board approved the split on May 24, 2013, while shareholders approved the split on June 11;<sup id="cite_ref-lat-splitapproved_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lat-splitapproved-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lat-splitvote_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lat-splitvote-10">[10]</a></sup> Preliminary trading on the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Securities_Exchange" title="Australian Securities Exchange">Australian Securities Exchange</a> of the new News Corp's class B stock began on June 19, 2013, at around $15 per share; a value slightly lower than expected by some analysts. The shares fell in price by 3% to $14.55 per share, valuing the new company at around $7.9 billion <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-theaus-newnewscorp_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theaus-newnewscorp-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ft-newnewscorp_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ft-newnewscorp-12">[12]</a></sup> The corporate split was finalized on June 28, 2013; during the <a href="/wiki/Stock_split" title="Stock split">stock splitting</a> process, one share of the new News Corp was given to shareholders for every four shares they owned in the former News Corp.<sup id="cite_ref-ft-newnewscorp_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ft-newnewscorp-12">[12]</a></sup> The current News Corp began trading on the <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a> stock exchange under the symbol "NWS" on July 1, 2013; at the same time, the former News Corporation (which encompassed purely of media properties, such as <a href="/wiki/Fox_Entertainment_Group" title="Fox Entertainment Group">Fox Entertainment Group</a> and <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox" class="mw-redirect" title="20th Century Fox">20th Century Fox</a>) was renamed <a href="/wiki/21st_Century_Fox" title="21st Century Fox">21st Century Fox</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc-splitofficial_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-splitofficial-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="After_the_split">After the split</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=News_Corp_(2013%E2%80%93present)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: After the split" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On September 4, 2013, News Corp announced that it would sell the <a href="/wiki/Dow_Jones_Local_Media_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Dow Jones Local Media Group">Dow Jones Local Media Group</a>, a group of 33 local newspapers, to Newcastle Investment Corp.—an affiliate of <a href="/wiki/Fortress_Investment_Group" title="Fortress Investment Group">Fortress Investment Group</a>, for $87 million. The newspapers will be operated by <a href="/wiki/GateHouse_Media" title="GateHouse Media">GateHouse Media</a>, a newspaper group owned by Fortress. Robert Thomson indicated that the newspapers "were not strategically consistent with the emerging portfolio" of the company.<sup id="cite_ref-nybj-djlmgsale_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nybj-djlmgsale-15">[15]</a></sup> GateHouse then filed for prepackaged Chapter 11 bankruptcy on September 27, 2013, to restructure its debt obligations to accommodate the acquisition.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> then GateHouse emerged from bankruptcy on November 26, 2013.
</p><p>On December 20, 2013, News Corp announced its acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Mark_Little_(journalist)" title="Mark Little (journalist)">Mark Little</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Storyful_(Company)" class="mw-redirect" title="Storyful (Company)">Storyful</a>, a <a href="/wiki/News_agency" title="News agency">news agency</a> specializing in verifying and distributing user-generated content relating to news events from <a href="/wiki/Social_networking_service" title="Social networking service">social networking services</a>, for $25 million, marking News Corp's first acquisition since the split. Robert Thomson stated that the service had "become the village square for valuable video, using journalistic sensibility, integrity and creativity to find, authenticate and commercialise user-generated content", and that with the purchase, News Corp would "define the opportunities that the digital landscape presents, rather than simply adapt to them".<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-storyful_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-storyful-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-guardian-storyful_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-storyful-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>On May 2, 2014, News Corp acquired romance novel publisher <a href="/wiki/Harlequin_Enterprises" title="Harlequin Enterprises">Harlequin Enterprises</a> from <a href="/wiki/Torstar" title="Torstar">Torstar</a> for $415 million.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The deal closed on August 1; it is now operated as a subsidiary of News Corp's <a href="/wiki/HarperCollins" title="HarperCollins">HarperCollins</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On September 30, 2014, News Corp announced its acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Move_(company)" title="Move (company)">Move, Inc.</a>, a real estate listings company and owner of <a href="/wiki/Realtor.com" title="Realtor.com">Realtor.com</a>. A 20% stake is owned by <a href="/wiki/REA_Group" title="REA Group">REA Group</a>, a publicly traded subsidiary of News Corp Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>News Corp also began making multiple investments in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, such as a $30 million investment in real estate site ProTiger in November 2014,<sup id="cite_ref-tc-protiger_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tc-protiger-22">[22]</a></sup> the December 2014 purchase of BigDecisions.com, a financial planning website,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> and the acquisition of Indian media firm <a href="/wiki/VCCircle" title="VCCircle">VCCircle</a> in March 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2015, News Corp sold its digital education brand <a href="/wiki/Amplify_(company)" title="Amplify (company)">Amplify</a> to a management team supported by a group of private investors for an undisclosed sum.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In June 2016, News Corp acquired <a href="/wiki/Wireless_Group" title="Wireless Group">Wireless Group</a> (formerly <a href="/wiki/UTV_(TV_channel)" title="UTV (TV channel)">UTV</a> Media), a British radio broadcaster, for $296 million<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2020, News Corp sold Unruly, an outstream video ad marketplace, in exchange of 6.91% of Tremor Video stock.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>

<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Dow_Jones_%26_Company" title="Dow Jones &amp; Company">Dow Jones &amp; Company</a>, a New York City-based financial publisher, and owners of the <i><a href="/wiki/Wall_Street_Journal" class="mw-redirect" title="Wall Street Journal">Wall Street Journal</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/MarketWatch" title="MarketWatch">MarketWatch</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Barron%27s_(newspaper)" title="Barron's (newspaper)">Barron's</a></i></li>
