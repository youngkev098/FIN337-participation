</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Align_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Align Technology was founded by Zia Chishti and Kelsey Wirth in 1997 and began working on the development of a <a href="/wiki/Clear_aligners" title="Clear aligners">clear aligner</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Hist_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hist-2">[2]</a></sup>  Align received FDA clearance to market the Invisalign system in 1998 and it was first marketed in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-Hist_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hist-2">[2]</a></sup>  Align Technology went public on January 26, 2001 on the <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">Nasdaq Stock Market</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2011 Align acquired the Israeli company Cadent for $190 million; Cadent manufactured intraoral scanners that included <a href="/wiki/3D_imaging" class="mw-redirect" title="3D imaging">3D imaging</a>, as well as software to display the acquired images and to plan treatment.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>Joseph M. Hogan joined Align in June 2015, and serves as CEO and President.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>The company's sales reached $1 billion for the first time in 2016 and its products were being used in about 8% of people who got braces.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes-7">[7]</a></sup>  In 2017 the company was faced with the challenge of the expiration of its patents on its Invisalign system starting in October 2017 and the beginning of generic competition.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2020, Align Technology announced that it has signed a definitive agreement to acquire Exocad Global Holdings for approximately $418.7 million.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
