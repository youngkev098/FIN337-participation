<p><b>Royal Caribbean Cruises Ltd.</b> (<b>RCCL</b>) is a global <a href="/wiki/Cruising_(maritime)" title="Cruising (maritime)">cruise</a> <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a> incorporated in <a href="/wiki/Liberia" title="Liberia">Liberia</a> and based in <a href="/wiki/Miami,_Florida" class="mw-redirect" title="Miami, Florida">Miami, Florida</a>, US. It is the world's second-largest <a href="/wiki/Cruise_line" title="Cruise line">cruise line</a> operator, after <a href="/wiki/Carnival_Corporation_%26_plc" title="Carnival Corporation &amp; plc">Carnival Corporation &amp; plc</a>. As of March 2009, Royal Caribbean Cruises Ltd. fully owns three cruise lines: <a href="/wiki/Royal_Caribbean_International" title="Royal Caribbean International">Royal Caribbean International</a>, <a href="/wiki/Celebrity_Cruises" title="Celebrity Cruises">Celebrity Cruises</a>, and Azamara Cruises. They also hold a 67% stake in <a href="/wiki/Silversea_Cruises" title="Silversea Cruises">Silversea Cruises</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> a 50% stake in <a href="/wiki/TUI_Cruises" title="TUI Cruises">TUI Cruises</a> and 49% stakes in <a href="/wiki/Pullmantur_Cruises" title="Pullmantur Cruises">Pullmantur Cruises</a> and the now-defunct <a href="/wiki/CDF_Croisi%C3%A8res_de_France" title="CDF Croisières de France">CDF Croisières de France</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Previously Royal Caribbean Cruises also owned 50% of <a href="/wiki/Island_Cruises" title="Island Cruises">Island Cruises</a>, but this was sold to <a href="/wiki/TUI_Travel_PLC" class="mw-redirect" title="TUI Travel PLC">TUI Travel PLC</a> in October 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-RCC_Island_5-0" class="reference"><a </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Caribbean_Cruises&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Royal_Caribbean_Cruises_headquarters.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Royal_Caribbean_Cruises_headquarters.jpg/250px-Royal_Caribbean_Cruises_headquarters.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="142" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1178" data-file-height="667"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 250px;height: 142px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Royal_Caribbean_Cruises_headquarters.jpg/250px-Royal_Caribbean_Cruises_headquarters.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="250" data-height="142" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Royal_Caribbean_Cruises_headquarters.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Royal Caribbean headquarters.</div></div></div>
<p>Royal Caribbean Cruises Ltd. was formed in 1997 when <a href="/wiki/Royal_Caribbean_Cruise_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Caribbean Cruise Line">Royal Caribbean Cruise Line</a> purchased <a href="/wiki/Celebrity_Cruises" title="Celebrity Cruises">Celebrity Cruises</a>. The decision was made to keep the two cruise line brands separate following the merger; as a result Royal Caribbean Cruise Line was re-branded <a href="/wiki/Royal_Caribbean_International" title="Royal Caribbean International">Royal Caribbean International</a> and Royal Caribbean Cruises Ltd. was established as the new parent company of both Royal Caribbean International and Celebrity Cruises.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>A third brand under Royal Caribbean Cruises ownership was formed in 2000 when <a href="/wiki/Island_Cruises" title="Island Cruises">Island Cruises</a> was created as a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/First_Choice_Holidays" class="mw-redirect" title="First Choice Holidays">First Choice Holidays</a>. Island Cruises became an informal cruise line on the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> markets.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2006, Royal Caribbean Cruises purchased Pullmantur Cruises based in Madrid, Spain. From there, the company expanded rapidly with the creation of Azamara Cruises in May 2007 as a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Celebrity_Cruises" title="Celebrity Cruises">Celebrity Cruises</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> It followed this with the formation of <a href="/wiki/CDF_Croisi%C3%A8res_de_France" title="CDF Croisières de France">CDF Croisières de France</a> in May 2008 to serve the French-language market.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>Royal Caribbean also has an interest in <a href="/wiki/TUI_Cruises" title="TUI Cruises">TUI Cruises</a>, a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/TUI_AG" class="mw-redirect" title="TUI AG">TUI AG</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> which began operations in 2009 aimed at a German-speaking market. TUI Cruises's subsidiary, <a href="/wiki/TUI_Travel" title="TUI Travel">TUI Travel</a>, had a 50% interest in Island Cruises following their merger with First Choice Holidays in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In October 2008, Royal Caribbean Cruises rationalized their holdings by selling their share of Island Cruises to TUI.<sup id="cite_ref-RCC_Island_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RCC_Island-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In early 2019, Royal Caribbean announced in a joint venture with ITM Group the formation of Holistica, a company that intends to develop cruise destinations.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The only known destination that the company is to develop is the Grand Lucayan Resort, located in <a href="/wiki/Freeport,_Bahamas" title="Freeport, Bahamas">Freeport</a> after the hotel campus was sold to newly formed subsidiary by the Bahamian Government.
</p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Royal_Caribbean_International">Royal Caribbean International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Caribbean_Cruises&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Royal Caribbean International" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Royal_Caribbean_International" title="Royal Caribbean International">Royal Caribbean International</a></div><p><b>Royal Caribbean International</b> (<b>RCI</b>, formerly <b>Royal Caribbean Cruise Line</b> (<b>RCCL</b>)) is a <a href="/wiki/Cruise_line" title="Cruise line">cruise line</a> brand founded in 1968 in <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> and organised as a wholly owned subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Royal_Caribbean_Cruises_Ltd." class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Caribbean Cruises Ltd.">Royal Caribbean Cruises Ltd.</a> (RCCL) since 1997. Based in <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, Florida, United States, it is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_largest_cruise_lines" title="List of largest cruise lines">largest cruise line by revenue and second largest by passengers counts</a>. In 2018, Royal Caribbean International controlled 19.2% of the worldwide cruise market by passengers and 14.0% by revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> It also operates the four largest passenger ships in the world. As of July 2019, the line operates twenty-six ships and has six additional ships on order.

<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Celebrity_Cruises" title="Celebrity Cruises">Celebrity Cruises</a></div><p><b>Celebrity Cruises</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Cruise_line" title="Cruise line">cruise line</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, Florida, US<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and a wholly owned subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Royal_Caribbean_Cruises_Ltd." class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Caribbean Cruises Ltd.">Royal Caribbean Cruises Ltd.</a> Celebrity Cruises was founded in 1988 by the Greece-based <a href="/wiki/Chandris_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Chandris Group">Chandris Group</a>, and merged with <a href="/wiki/Royal_Caribbean_Cruise_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Caribbean Cruise Line">Royal Caribbean Cruise Line</a> in 1997. Celebrity's signature logo is an "Χ" displayed on the funnel of Celebrity ships, and is the Greek letter <a href="/wiki/Chi_(letter)" title="Chi (letter)">chi</a>, for "Chandris".<sup id="cite_ref-MM_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MM-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Ulrich_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ulrich-16">[16]</a></sup></p><h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Azamara">Azamara</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Caribbean_Cruises&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Azamara" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Pullmantur_Cruises">Pullmantur Cruises</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Caribbean_Cruises&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Pullmantur Cruises" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Pullmantur_Cruises" title="Pullmantur Cruises">Pullmantur Cruises</a></div><p><b>Pullmantur Cruises</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Cruise_line" title="Cruise line">cruise line</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> It began operations in the late 1990s as an offshoot of the <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Travel_agency" title="Travel agency">travel agency</a> Pullmantur. In 2006, Pullmantur Cruises, through its parent company, was purchased by US-based <a href="/wiki/Royal_Caribbean_Cruises_Ltd." class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Caribbean Cruises Ltd.">Royal Caribbean Cruises Ltd.</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-CruiseCritic_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CruiseCritic-19">[19]</a></sup> but Royal Caribbean later sold a 51% stake in the cruise line to Spain-based investment firm Springwater Capital, retaining a 49% stake.<sup id="cite_ref-sprinwater_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sprinwater-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>Pullmantur Cruises is the largest <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Cruise_line" title="Cruise line">cruise line</a>. The company mainly markets to Spanish passengers, although cruises on Pullmantur ships are also sold by some travel operators outside the <a href="/wiki/Spanish-speaking_world" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish-speaking world">Spanish-speaking world</a>. Some of the company's ships operate an "all-inclusive" product, where some extras, such as brand alcoholic beverages, are included in the cruise price.<sup id="cite_ref-CruiseCritic_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CruiseCritic-19">[19]</a></sup> Originally, most Pullmantur ships do not operate cruises for the company during the <a href="/wiki/Northern_hemisphere" class="mw-redirect" title="Northern hemisphere">northern hemisphere</a> winter season. Instead, they are either laid up or under charter to other cruise lines, such as the <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>-based <a href="/w/index.php?title=Viagens_CVC&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Viagens CVC (page does not exist)">Viagens CVC</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CruiseCritic_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CruiseCritic-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="TUI_Cruises">TUI Cruises</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Caribbean_Cruises&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: TUI Cruises" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/TUI_Cruises" title="TUI Cruises">TUI Cruises</a></div><p><b>TUI Cruises</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Cruise_line" title="Cruise line">cruise line</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It was formed in 2007 as a <a href="/wiki/Joint_venture" title="Joint venture">joint venture</a> between the German tourism company <a href="/wiki/TUI_AG" class="mw-redirect" title="TUI AG">TUI AG</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Cruise_line" title="Cruise line">cruise line</a> operator <a href="/wiki/Royal_Caribbean_Cruises_Ltd." class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Caribbean Cruises Ltd.">Royal Caribbean Cruises Ltd.</a>, both of whom hold a 50% stake in the company. The company started operations in 2009 and competes with <a href="/wiki/AIDA_Cruises" title="AIDA Cruises">AIDA Cruises</a> for the German market. It targets German-speaking customers who opt for a premium cruise experience. The onboard product, including food, entertainment and amenities, is custom-tailored for German tastes and German is the main language used onboard its ships.<sup id="cite_ref-Cruise_Industry_News2_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cruise_Industry_News2-21">[21]</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-MM-2002_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MM-2002-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2020 TUI announced the sale of Hapag-Lloyd Kreuzfahrten to the JV <a href="/wiki/TUI_Cruises" title="TUI Cruises">TUI Cruises</a>, on which TUI and <a class="mw-selflink selflink">Royal Caribbean Cruises</a> both hold 50% of the shares.<sup id="cite_ref-MM-2002_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MM-2002-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Holistica">Holistica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Caribbean_Cruises&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Holistica" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Holistica was formed in early 2019 after the purchase of the Grand Lucayan Resort in a joint venture by Royal Caribbean and ITM Group. Since the purchase, Royal Caribbean has announced that it is intended for the resort to be developed into a cruise destination that will benefit the local Freeport economy. It was also announced by the CEO of Royal Caribbean that Freeport Harbor will be developed into a cruise facility of choice.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
