<p>Amphenol was founded in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a> in 1932 by entrepreneur <a href="/wiki/Arthur_J._Schmitt" title="Arthur J. Schmitt">Arthur J. Schmitt</a>, whose first product was a <a href="/wiki/Tube_socket" title="Tube socket">tube socket</a> for <a href="/wiki/Vacuum_tube" title="Vacuum tube">radio tubes</a> (valveholder bases).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>  Amphenol expanded significantly during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, when the company became the primary manufacturer of connectors used in military hardware, including airplanes and radios. From 1967 to 1982 it was part of <a href="/wiki/Bunker_Ramo_Corporation" title="Bunker Ramo Corporation">Bunker Ramo Corporation</a>.
</p><p>The company sells its products into diverse electronics markets, including military-aerospace, industrial, automotive, information technology, mobile phones, wireless infrastructure, broadband, medical, and pro audio. Operations are located in more than 60 locations around the world.
</p><p>Amphenol's world headquarters is located in <a href="/wiki/Wallingford,_Connecticut" title="Wallingford, Connecticut">Wallingford, Connecticut</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>  The largest division of Amphenol is Amphenol Aerospace (formerly <a href="/wiki/Bendix_Corporation" title="Bendix Corporation">Bendix Corporation</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Sidney_(village),_New_York" title="Sidney (village), New York">Sidney</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>. This is the birthplace of the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Military_connector_specifications" title="U.S. Military connector specifications">MIL-DTL-38999</a> cylindrical connector.  Amphenol engineers also invented the commonly used <a href="/wiki/BNC_connector" title="BNC connector">BNC connector</a> ("Bayonet Neill-Concelman")<sup id="cite_ref-SDInd_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SDInd-7">[7]</a></sup>.
</p><p>Amphenol Fiber Systems International is a fiber optic company started in 1993 that specializes in the fabrication and manufacturing of fiber optic connectivity products and systems. AFSI provides solutions for communication systems based on fiber optic interconnect technology. AFSI employs over 100 people at its 50,000 square foot facility in the heart of the telecom corridor in Allen, just north of Dallas, Texas.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>Amphenol Cables on Demand, another division of Amphenol launched in December 2006, specializes in distributing standard cable assemblies via their e-commerce storefront.  They sell more than 2500 audio, video, computer, and networking cables.  Offices are located in New York, California, Florida, Toronto, and China.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amphenol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Acquisitions" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>In May 2005, Amphenol acquired <b>SV Microwave</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-Microwave_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Microwave-9">[9]</a></sup> a manufacturer of <a href="/wiki/Radio_frequency" title="Radio frequency">RF</a> connectors, components and cable assemblies.
</p><p>On October 10, 2005, <a href="/wiki/Teradyne" title="Teradyne">Teradyne</a> and Amphenol announced that Amphenol would acquire Teradyne Connection Systems, for about <a href="/wiki/USD" class="mw-redirect" title="USD">USD</a> $390 million in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-Amphenol-Dec-2005-8-K_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Amphenol-Dec-2005-8-K-10">[10]</a></sup> TCS, based in <a href="/wiki/Nashua,_New_Hampshire" title="Nashua, New Hampshire">Nashua</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a>, manufactures high-density <a href="/wiki/Electronics" title="Electronics">electronic</a> <a href="/wiki/Electrical_connector" title="Electrical connector">connectors</a>, complete <a href="/wiki/Backplane" title="Backplane">backplanes</a>, and systems packaging, a product line that complements Amphenol's existing lines of business.
</p><p>In February 2008, Amphenol acquired <b>SEFEE</b>, a French electronic manufacturer, the next year in 2009 it acquired <b>Jaybeam Wireless</b><sup id="cite_ref-Wireless_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wireless-11">[11]</a></sup>
. Jaybeam Wireless became Amphenol Jaybeam and is now Amphenol Antenna Solutions.
</p><p>On November 15, 2013, Amphenol announced it had entered an agreement to acquire Advanced Sensors Business of <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">GE</a> for approx. $318 million.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2013, Amphenol acquired Tecvox OEM Solutions LLC,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> a business started by Raj Khanijow based in Huntsville AL. Tecvox is a leader in automotive OEM USB connectivity products. Tecvox was the first automotive company to release the OEM USB based media hub and charger.
</p><p>On January 8, 2016, Amphenol finalized its deal to acquire FCI Asia Pte Ltd<sup id="cite_ref-FCI_Asia_Pte_Ltd_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FCI_Asia_Pte_Ltd-15">[15]</a></sup> an interconnect company specializing in the telecom, datacom and wireless communications markets.
</p><p>In July 2016, Amphenol acquired AUXELFTG (AUXEL), a French manufacturer  of power busbars and power interconnect solutions.<sup id="cite_ref-AUXELFTG(Auxel)_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AUXELFTG(Auxel)-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2017, Amphenol acquired Phitek Ltd, a New Zealand-based manufacturer and the world's leading supplier of electronic noise-cancellation, audio enhancement and other electronic touch point devices in the aircraft cabin.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2017, Amphenol acquired Wilcoxon Research (US), Piezo Technologies (US) and Piher Sensors and Controls (Spain), three Industrial Sensing businesses from the  British engineering group Meggitt PLC.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
