</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Equinix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Equinix was founded in 1998 by Al Avery and <a href="/wiki/Jay_Adelson" title="Jay Adelson">Jay Adelson</a>, two facilities managers at <a href="/wiki/Digital_Equipment_Corporation" title="Digital Equipment Corporation">Digital Equipment Corporation</a>. The firm promoted its data center platform as a neutral place where competing networks could connect and share <a href="/wiki/Data_traffic" class="mw-redirect" title="Data traffic">data traffic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-itworld1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itworld1-7">[7]</a></sup>  The firm focused on expanding interconnection from its inception, to capitalize on the so-called “<a href="/wiki/Network_effect" title="Network effect">network effect</a>,” through which each new customer would broaden the appeal of its platform.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>  It expanded to <a href="/wiki/Asia-Pacific" title="Asia-Pacific">Asia-Pacific</a> in 2002<sup id="cite_ref-equinix2_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-equinix2-9">[9]</a></sup> and Europe in 2007,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and then to Latin America in 2011<sup id="cite_ref-datacenterdynamics1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-datacenterdynamics1-11">[11]</a></sup> and  the Middle East in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-datacenterknowledge1_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-datacenterknowledge1-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>Peter Van Camp has led Equinix in a variety of roles for 17 years. He was chief executive officer from May 2000 until 2006, when he was appointed executive chairman of the  board of directors.<sup id="cite_ref-equinix.com_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-equinix.com-13">[13]</a></sup> Steve Smith took over as CEO in 2007<sup id="cite_ref-equinix.com_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-equinix.com-13">[13]</a></sup> and resigned in January 2018. Van Camp was named interim chief executive officer upon Smith's resignation,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> until Charles Meyers was named as the company's president and CEO in September 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions_and_expansion">Acquisitions and expansion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Equinix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acquisitions and expansion" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2002, Equinix merged with i-STT, the Internet infrastructure service subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Technologies_Telemedia" class="mw-redirect" title="Singapore Technologies Telemedia">Singapore Technologies Telemedia</a>, and Pihana Pacific.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> This established the company's presence in China, Singapore, Australia and Japan.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In 2007, the firm announced a $2 billion international expansion plan and entered the European market by acquiring data center operator IXEurope and its facilities in France, Germany, the Netherlands, Switzerland and the United Kingdom. That plan was completed with the 2010 opening of Equinix's 50th global data center, in London.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>  Over the next seven years, the company nearly tripled its data center portfolio, growth the company attributes to increased demand for interconnection services caused by the emergence of cloud computing, along with the expansion of related trends such as the <a href="/wiki/Internet_of_Things" class="mw-redirect" title="Internet of Things">Internet of Things</a> and <a href="/wiki/Big_data" title="Big data">big data</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-datacenterdynamics2_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-datacenterdynamics2-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-equinix4_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-equinix4-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dailycloud1_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dailycloud1-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>The firm's next major acquisition came in 2010, when the company purchased <a href="/wiki/Switch_and_Data" title="Switch and Data">Switch and Data</a> Facilities Company, Inc., a U.S. Internet exchange and colocation services provider with locations in 23 North American markets. The transaction was valued at approximately $683.4 million.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The company extended its operations to the Middle East and in Southeast Asia in 2012, announcing partnerships to enter the Dubai, UAE,<sup id="cite_ref-datacenterknowledge1_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-datacenterknowledge1-12">[12]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Jakarta" title="Jakarta">Jakarta</a>, Indonesia, markets.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Also in 2012, it completed a $230.5 million all-cash transaction for Hong Kong-based data center provider Asia-Tone, adding of six data centers and one disaster recovery center in the Hong Kong, Shanghai and Singapore markets.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>In 2014, Equinix increased its Latin American presence when it paid $225 million to complete the acquisition of ALOG Datacenters of Brazil S.A., the country's leading provider of carrier-neutral data centers. Equinix had acquired a 53% stake in ALOG in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-datacenterdynamics1_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-datacenterdynamics1-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, Equinix converted to a <a href="/wiki/Real_estate_investment_trust" title="Real estate investment trust">real estate investment trust</a> (REIT) in a move to gain tax advantages and enhance shareholder value by offering a regular dividend.<sup id="cite_ref-TMF_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TMF-6">[6]</a></sup> That same year, it acquired the professional services company Nimbo as part of a broader effort  to assist customers executing data center migrations or advancing their network and <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_cloud" class="mw-redirect" title="Hybrid cloud">hybrid cloud</a> strategies.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>Early in 2015 the firm opened five new data centers on four continents, increasing the company's data center footprint to more than 10 million square feet.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>The largest acquisition in company history was announced at the end of May 2015, when Equinix said it would purchase the British company <a href="/wiki/TelecityGroup" title="TelecityGroup">TelecityGroup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-reuters1_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuters1-27">[27]</a></sup> The offer was cleared by the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> in November,<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> but only after Equinix agreed to sell eight of its data centers around Europe to <a href="/wiki/Digital_Realty_Trust" class="mw-redirect" title="Digital Realty Trust">Digital Realty Trust</a> for $874 million.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> In January 2016, Equinix announced that it had completed the Telecity acquisition in a transaction valued at approximately $3.8 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-31">[31]</a></sup> The addition of these  data centers more than doubled Equinix's capacity in Europe, making the company the region's largest retail colocation provider. The deal also added network and cloud density.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2015, the company purchased Japanese provider Bit-Isle for $280 million,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-31">[31]</a></sup> doubling the number of Equinix data centers in Japan to 12 by adding five in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> and one in <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> In 2015, Equinix also acquired Nimbo, a cloud professional services company with expertise in helping enterprises make the transition from legacy data center infrastructure to a more agile hybrid one.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-31">[31]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>In 2016, Equinix opened new data centers in <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a>, Sydney and Tokyo<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> and announced a deal to acquire 29 data centers in 15 markets from <a href="/wiki/Verizon_Communications" title="Verizon Communications">Verizon</a> for $3.6 billion. The purchase, which closed in May 2017, extended Equinix's North American footprint into  <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a> and <a href="/wiki/Culpeper,_Virginia" title="Culpeper, Virginia">Culpeper, Virginia</a>, and  brought the company into <a href="/wiki/Bogota,_Colombia" class="mw-redirect" title="Bogota, Colombia">Bogota, Colombia</a>, for the first time.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> In 2017, the firm also opened a new data center in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, Equinix acquired Itconic which expanded its data center footprint into two new countries, Spain and Portugal. The $66.8M acquisition added five data centers in four metros.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, Equinix purchased the Dallas <a href="/wiki/Infomart" title="Infomart">Infomart</a> building, where it had already been operating four data centers.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> It also acquired Australian data center provider Metronode and its 10 data centers, which expanded the company's operations into four additional cities.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p><p>In 2020, Equinix finalized the purchase of Packet, a startup, for $335 Million USD.<sup id="cite_ref-datacenterknowledge032020_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-datacenterknowledge032020-41">[41]</a></sup> Packet was founded in 2014<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> and evolved to provide <a href="/wiki/Bare-metal_server" title="Bare-metal server">bare-metal servers</a> as a cloud service. With this purchase, Equinix can now offer customers the ability to rapidly deploy physical servers across Equinix's hosting infrastructure.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Data_centers">Data centers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Equinix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Data centers" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Equinix claims to have invested more than $25 billion since its founding to build its data center platform.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p><p>The firm calls its data centers International Business Exchanges (IBXs) to emphasize the collaboration and data exchange it says they enable. Chairman Peter Van Camp has compared them to "international airports where passengers from many different airlines make connections to get to their final destinations."<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> In 2017, the company launched its own <a href="/wiki/Data_center_infrastructure_management" class="mw-redirect" title="Data center infrastructure management">data center infrastructure management</a> platform, called IBX SmartView, for monitoring and maintaining data center operations.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> The company's <a href="/wiki/Internet_Exchange" class="mw-redirect" title="Internet Exchange">Internet Exchange</a> service allows <a href="/wiki/ISPs" class="mw-redirect" title="ISPs">ISPs</a>, content providers, and large enterprises to exchange Internet traffic through a global peering tool.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p><p>The company cites its global average <a href="/wiki/Uptime" title="Uptime">uptime</a> record to demonstrate the reliability of its facilities and services. Equinix received positive press coverage in the aftermath of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Sandy" title="Hurricane Sandy">Superstorm Sandy</a> in 2012 after its customers in the <a href="/wiki/Northeastern_United_States" title="Northeastern United States">Northeast U.S.</a> experienced relatively limited outages, compared to widespread problems at other vendors.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Data_center_.28IBX.29_locations"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Data_center_(IBX)_locations">Data center (IBX) locations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Equinix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Data center (IBX) locations" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<ul><li><b>Americas:</b> United States, Brazil, Canada, Colombia</li>
<li><b>Asia-Pacific:</b> Australia, China, Hong Kong, Indonesia, Japan, South Korea, Singapore</li>
<li><b>Europe and Middle East:</b> Bulgaria, Finland, France, Germany, Ireland, Italy, Netherlands, Poland, Portugal, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, Turkey, United Arab Emirates, United Kingdom<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Cloud">Cloud</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Equinix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Cloud" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>Some analysts have warned that long-term consolidation in the telecommunications industry caused by cloud will decrease demand for data center space and hurt the growth prospects of companies such as Equinix.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> Equinix argues that accelerated cloud proliferation and adoption, along with the emergence of cloud-dependent trends and technologies such as big data, mobile and the Internet of Things, dramatically increase the value of its core interconnection offering.<sup id="cite_ref-datacenterdynamics2_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-datacenterdynamics2-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-equinix4_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-equinix4-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dailycloud1_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dailycloud1-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>Equinix was described by an industry analyst as a "crucial <a href="/wiki/Intermediary" title="Intermediary">middle man</a>," connecting companies that build private clouds on the Equinix platform to the underlying networks and <a href="/wiki/Public_cloud" class="mw-redirect" title="Public cloud">public cloud</a> providers they depend on.<sup id="cite_ref-barrons1_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-barrons1-54">[54]</a></sup></p><p>With the March 2020 acquisition of a startup named Packet, Equinix is expected to offer "bare-metal services" across their existing colocation infrastructure. This will allow customers to deploy and manage dedicated servers in the Equinix locations.<sup id="cite_ref-datacenterknowledge032020_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-datacenterknowledge032020-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Enterprise">Enterprise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Equinix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Enterprise" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>Equinix has emphasized expanding its enterprise business as more companies move aggressively to compete as digital businesses. 
In a 2016 conference, Smith announced a company goal to increase its enterprise customer base exponentially in the next decade. Smith estimated the total addressable enterprise market at 350,000 globally and projected Equinix could land as many as 60,000 enterprise customers by 2026.<sup id="cite_ref-datacenterknowledge2_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-datacenterknowledge2-55">[55]</a></sup> Equinix's total customer base at the end of 2016 was about 8,500, including about 2,250 enterprise customers.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> Industry observers said a challenge for Equinix would be appealing to customers who did not know they needed what Equinix offers: "Courting enterprises … is more difficult than serving Equinix’s traditional customer base, consisting of financial, network, media, and cloud companies. These customers basically knew what they wanted from Equinix, and all Equinix had to do was deliver."<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p><p>Equinix says its appeal to the enterprise market is based on its network density, cloud expertise and what it says is a growing enterprise need to obtain services from multiple cloud providers just to execute routine business. It argues the only way the enterprise can meet customer expectations is with what it called an "interconnection first" mindset<sup id="cite_ref-datacenterknowledge2_55-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-datacenterknowledge2-55">[55]</a></sup> that prioritizes moving closer to end users and enabling faster data processing and analysis at the "edge of the network." The company has devised what it calls an "edge strategy" for the enterprise, named the Interconnection Oriented Architecture. This strategy is designed to allow the enterprise to move closer to end users by capitalizing on Equinix's global footprint and the access it provides to multiple networks and cloud providers.<sup id="cite_ref-computerweekly1_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-computerweekly1-58">[58]</a></sup> The firm also said its interest in cultivating enterprise growth was to ward off the threat presented by the largest public cloud providers, all of whom it hosts in its data centers.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p><p>Some analysts have argued that the continued growth of the public cloud giants – such as <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Web_Services" title="Amazon Web Services">Amazon Web Services</a> and <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Azure" title="Microsoft Azure">Microsoft Azure</a> – would eventually lead them to abandon third-party colocation providers like Equinix, and build their own data centers and take advantage of economies of scale.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> In response, Equinix has positioned its facilities as meeting places where enterprises could directly access as many public cloud services as possible and build customized and business-critical applications. The company believes that attracting more enterprises as they begin relying on multi-cloud strategies will further establish Equinix as an essential interconnection point between the enterprise and the public cloud, including its largest providers.<sup id="cite_ref-computerweekly1_58-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-computerweekly1-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Sustainability">Sustainability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Equinix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sustainability" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<p>In 2015, the company made a long-term pledge to power its entire data center platform with clean and renewable energy.<sup id="cite_ref-enterprisetimes1_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-enterprisetimes1-61">[61]</a></sup> The pledge followed criticism in 2014 from the environmental group <a href="/wiki/Greenpeace" title="Greenpeace">Greenpeace</a>, which said in an annual report on the environmental practices of Internet companies that Equinix had an insufficient commitment to renewable energy and <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">carbon emissions</a> mitigation.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> The company   pledged to use 100% clean and renewable energy globally and signed deals with <a href="/wiki/Wind_farm" title="Wind farm">wind farms</a> in <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a> to buy enough renewable energy to offset the energy consumption of its entire North American data center portfolio.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p><p>Also in 2015, Equinix was also among the signatories of the <a href="/wiki/Presidency_of_Barack_Obama" title="Presidency of Barack Obama">Obama Administration</a>'s American Business Act on Climate Pledge.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, Equinix was honored for its sustainability initiatives at the SEAL Business Sustainability Awards.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
