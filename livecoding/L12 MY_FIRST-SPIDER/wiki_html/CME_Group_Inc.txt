<p><b>CME Group Inc.</b> (Chicago Mercantile Exchange, Chicago Board of Trade, New York Mercantile Exchange, The Commodity Exchange) is a global markets company.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It is the world's largest <a href="/wiki/Financial_derivatives" class="mw-redirect" title="Financial derivatives">financial derivatives</a> exchange,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and trades in <a href="/wiki/Asset_classes" title="Asset classes">asset classes</a> that include agricultural products, currencies, energy, interest rates, metals, and stock indexes.   The company offers <a href="/wiki/Futures_contracts" class="mw-redirect" title="Futures contracts">futures contracts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Option_(finance)" title="Option (finance)">options</a> on futures using its CME Globex <a href="/wiki/Trading_platform" class="mw-redirect" title="Trading platform">trading platforms</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> fixed income trading via BrokerTec and foreign exchange trading on the EBS platform. In addition, it operates a central counterparty clearing provider, CME Clearing. With a range of pre- and post-trade products and services underpinning the entire lifecycle of a trade, CME Group also offers optimization and reconciliation services through TriOptima, and trade processing services through Traiana.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Trading_platforms">Trading platforms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CME_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Trading platforms" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Trading is conducted in two methods: an <a href="/wiki/Open_outcry" title="Open outcry">open outcry</a> format and the CME Globex Trading System which is an <a href="/wiki/Electronic_trading_platform" title="Electronic trading platform">electronic trading platform</a>. 
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Open_outcry">Open outcry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CME_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Open outcry" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Operating during regular trading hours (RTH), the <a href="/wiki/Open_outcry" title="Open outcry">open outcry</a> method consists of floor traders standing in a trading pit to call out orders, prices, and quantities of a particular commodity or its derivatives. Different colored jackets are worn by the traders to indicate what firm they are a part of. In addition, complex hand signals (called <a href="/wiki/Arb_(gesture)" class="mw-redirect" title="Arb (gesture)">Arb</a>) are used. These hand signals were first used in the 1970s. Today, however, headsets are also used by the brokers to communicate with the traders. The pits are areas of the floor that are lowered to facilitate communication, somewhat like a miniature <a href="/wiki/Amphitheater" class="mw-redirect" title="Amphitheater">amphitheater</a>. The pits can be raised and lowered depending on trading volume. To an onlooker, the <a href="/wiki/Open_outcry" title="Open outcry">open outcry</a> system can look chaotic and confusing, but in reality the system is a tried and true method of accurate and efficient trading. An illustrated project to record the hand signal language used in CME's trading pits has been compiled.<sup id="cite_ref-TPH_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TPH-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Electronic_trading">Electronic trading</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CME_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Electronic trading" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>Operating virtually around the clock, today the CME Globex trading system is at the heart of CME Group. Proposed in 1987, it was introduced in 1992 as the first global <a href="/wiki/Electronic_trading_platform" title="Electronic trading platform">electronic trading platform</a> for <a href="/wiki/Futures_contracts" class="mw-redirect" title="Futures contracts">futures contracts</a>. This fully electronic trading system allows market participants to trade from booths at the exchange or while sitting in a home or office thousands of miles away. On October 19, 2004, the one billionth (1,000,000,000) transaction was recorded.  Today, CME Globex operates 24 hours a day, six days a week.  More than 90 percent of total volume at the exchange occurs electronically on CME Globex.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Global_footprint">Global footprint</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CME_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Global footprint" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>In 2019, CME Group’s non-U.S. average daily volume reached a record 4.9 million contracts, up 10% compared with 2018, including 7% growth in Europe, 20% growth in Asia and 47% growth in Latin America.  In addition to maintaining offices across the Americas, Europe and Asia, the company also partners with other global exchanges including Bursa Malaysia Berhad, The Dubai Mercantile Exchange and Singapore Exchange Limited. <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(6)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="CME_Span">CME Span</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CME_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: CME Span" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-6 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-6">
<p>The company pioneered the CME SPAN software that is used around the world as the official performance bond (margin) mechanism of 50 registered exchanges, clearing organizations, service bureaus, and regulatory agencies throughout the world.  In 2019, CME Group announced that it would launch the next generation of CME Span Margin Methodology (CME Span 2) in the first half of 2020, pending regulatory approval. <sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(7)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Mergers_and_acquisitions">Mergers and acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CME_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mergers and acquisitions" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-7 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-7">
<p>On October 17, 2006, The <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Mercantile_Exchange" title="Chicago Mercantile Exchange">Chicago Mercantile Exchange</a> announced a merger with its historic rival the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Board_of_Trade" title="Chicago Board of Trade">Chicago Board of Trade</a> for $8 billion in stock, joining the two financial institutions as <i><b><a class="mw-selflink selflink">CME Group</a> Inc</b></i>. The merger agreement was modified on December 20, 2006 <sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>, May 11, 2007,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> June 14, 2007<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and on July 6, 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The merger was approved by shareholders of both CME and the Chicago Board of Trade on July 9, 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and closed on July 12, 2007, after which the Chicago Board of Trade shares (old symbol: BOT) stopped trading and were converted into CME shares as agreed, and the overarching holding company began life as CME Group, a CME/Chicago Board of Trade Company.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On January 13, 2008 electronic trading at the Chicago Board of Trade shifted to CME Globex.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>On March 17, 2008, <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mercantile_Exchange" title="New York Mercantile Exchange">New York Mercantile Exchange</a> (NYMEX) (owner of both the NYMEX exchange and the COMEX exchange) accepted an offer from CME Group, to purchase NYMEX for $8.9 billion in cash and CME Group Stock. <sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The acquisition was formally completed on August 22, 2008, and the NYMEX systems were fully integrated by September 30, 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>  Also in 2007, NYMEX in a joint venture with partners on the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabian Peninsula</a> opened the <a href="/wiki/Dubai_Mercantile_Exchange" title="Dubai Mercantile Exchange">Dubai Mercantile Exchange</a> (DME).<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>On February 10, 2010, CME announced its purchase of 90% of Dow Jones Indexes, including the <a href="/wiki/Dow_Jones_Industrial_Average" title="Dow Jones Industrial Average">Dow Jones Industrial Average</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>  CME subsequently contributed Dow Jones Indexes to the formation of <a href="/wiki/S%26P_Dow_Jones_Indices" title="S&amp;P Dow Jones Indices">S&amp;P Dow Jones Indices</a> in exchange for a 24.4% ownership interest.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In April 2013, CME purchased the remaining 10% interest in Dow Jones Indexes for $80.0 million. As a result, CME's interest in <a href="/wiki/S%26P_Dow_Jones_Indices" title="S&amp;P Dow Jones Indices">S&amp;P Dow Jones Indices</a> increased from 24.4% to 27.0%.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>On October 17, 2012, CME Group announced it would acquire the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Board_of_Trade" title="Kansas City Board of Trade">Kansas City Board of Trade</a> for $126 Million in cash. KCBOT was the dominant venue for the sale of <a href="/wiki/Hard_red_winter_wheat" class="mw-redirect" title="Hard red winter wheat">hard red winter wheat</a>. The Chicago Board of Trade was the leading trade platform for <a href="/wiki/Soft_red_winter_wheat" class="mw-redirect" title="Soft red winter wheat">soft red winter wheat</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>On March 29, 2018, CME Group announced that it was buying London-based <a href="/wiki/NEX_Group" title="NEX Group">NEX Group</a> for $5.5 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The acquisition was completed on November 2, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
