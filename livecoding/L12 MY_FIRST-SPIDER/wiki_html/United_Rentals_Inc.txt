It offers general, aerial, and specialty rentals to a customer base that includes construction and industrial companies; utilities; municipalities; and homeowners. In addition to rentals, the company offers new and used equipment sales, servicing, and safety training.
</p><p>United Rentals is currently led by Matthew Flannery (CEO), Dale Asplund (Executive Vice President &amp; COO), and Jessica Graziano (Executive Vice President &amp; CFO). It is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Stamford,_Connecticut" title="Stamford, Connecticut">Stamford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2019, United Rentals was announced the 6th best <a href="/wiki/Stock" title="Stock">stock</a> of the 2010s, with a total return of 1,559%.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Rentals&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Operations" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>United Rentals is primarily a provider of construction and industrial equipment: trucks, <a href="/wiki/Aerial_work_platform" title="Aerial work platform">aerial work platforms</a>, counterbalance forklifts, <a href="/wiki/Telescopic_handler" title="Telescopic handler">reach forklifts</a>, earth movers, <a href="/wiki/Compressors" class="mw-redirect" title="Compressors">compressors</a>, homeowner equipment, and similar devices. Together, these are considered general and aerial rentals, and they make up the bulk of URI's rental fleet and customer base.
</p><p>The company also rents equipment in five primary specialty fields:<sup id="cite_ref-:1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<ul><li><b>Trench safety</b> includes shoring equipment for excavation in confined spaces and below-ground construction sites, and the training necessary to safely handle such equipment.</li>
<li><b>Power &amp; HVAC</b> includes temporary power generators and mobile climate control. These devices are often used during commercial renovations, on television and movie sets, or in response to natural disasters.</li>
<li><b>Tool solutions</b> involve renting out trailers stocked with tools and supplies. They are often used at large construction sites or during refinery shutdowns.</li>
<li><b>Fluid solutions</b> include the devices and supplies necessary for transferring, containing or removing liquids. They tend to have applications in mining and agriculture.</li>
<li><b>Reliable Onsite Services</b> involve the renting of portable toilets, restroom and shower trailers.</li></ul><p>Additional specialty fields include:
</p>
<ul><li><b>Radio Services</b> involve the renting of portable radios. United Rentals is an Authorized Dealer for Motorola and can provide a variety of handheld and portable radio solutions.</li>
<li><b>Sports &amp; Entertainment</b> involve the renting of equipment that covers anything from movie production to large events like the Super Bowl.</li>
<li><b>Drone Services</b> involve providing Drone services to scope out your job sites and survey projects. Can be used on anything from large job sites to nuclear silos, etc.</li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Rentals&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:My_Truck_%26_Boom.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/My_Truck_%26_Boom.jpg/220px-My_Truck_%26_Boom.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="4032" data-file-height="3024"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/My_Truck_%26_Boom.jpg/220px-My_Truck_%26_Boom.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:My_Truck_%26_Boom.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A Dodge Ram 1500 truck &amp; Genie SX-180 Boom lift from United Rentals.</div></div></div>
<p>United Rentals was founded in September 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Then-chairman and CEO Bradley Jacobs planned to grow the company through acquisition and consolidation, beginning in October 1997 with six small leasing companies scattered across North America.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In December 1997, three months after forming, United Rentals began trading on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> under the symbol URI.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In June 1998, it acquired U.S. Rentals, Inc., for a sum variously reported as $1.2 billion and $1.31 billion. The acquisition made United Rentals the largest equipment rental company in North America.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>On December 26, 2006, United Rentals announced the sale of their Highway Technology division to HTS Acquisition, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>On July 23, 2007, United Rentals announced a definitive agreement to sell itself to <a href="/wiki/Cerberus_Capital_Management" title="Cerberus Capital Management">Cerberus Capital Management</a> in a transaction valued at approximately $6.6 billion, including the assumption of approximately $2.6 billion in debt obligations. URI's board of directors approved the merger agreement and recommended that stockholders do the same.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>On November 15, 2007, the company announced that Cerberus was not prepared to proceed with the purchase on the terms set forth in its merger agreement. Cerberus confirmed that there had not been a material adverse change at United Rentals, which would have let it bow out of the deal without penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On December 21, 2007, a <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a> Chancery Court ruling denied United Rentals' attempt to force Cerberus to follow through on the takeover bid. However, the court did order Cerberus to pay the $100 million termination fee stipulated in the original agreement.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>On December 16, 2011, United Rentals and <a href="/w/index.php?title=RSC_Holdings,_Inc&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="RSC Holdings, Inc (page does not exist)">RSC Holdings, Inc</a>. announced that they had entered into a definitive merger agreement, under which United Rentals would acquire RSC in a cash-and-stock transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The largest merger in rental equipment history was finalized on April 30, 2012,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> boosting URI to what was at the time a 13 percent market share of the equipment rental industry.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In 2013, United Rentals moved its headquarters from <a href="/wiki/Greenwich,_Connecticut" title="Greenwich, Connecticut">Greenwich, Connecticut</a> to <a href="/wiki/Stamford,_Connecticut" title="Stamford, Connecticut">Stamford, Connecticut</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>On March 9, 2014, the company announced plans to acquire <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Pump&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="National Pump (page does not exist)">National Pump</a> for $780 million. This made United Rentals the second-largest pump rental company in North America.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The transaction was finalized on March 31, 2014.
</p><p>In June 2014, United Rentals was first listed on the <i>Fortune</i> 500. Based on its 2013 revenues of $4.955 billion, the company was ranked exactly 500th.<sup id="cite_ref-UR500_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UR500-23">[23]</a></sup> It had previously made it as high as 517 in 2002, before falling back down.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>On September 19, 2014, URI was added to the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500_Index" title="S&amp;P 500 Index">S&amp;P 500 Index</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>On January 25, 2017, United Rentals announced the purchase of NES Rentals, a competing general equipment rentals company, for approximately $965 million.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> It completed the acquisition on April 3, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>On August 22, 2017, United Rentals announced it had acquired all power generation assets, primarily mobile generator sets, from <a href="/wiki/Cummins" title="Cummins">Cummins Inc.</a> To maintain fleet and customer service continuity, a small number of Cummins employees joined United Rentals.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>On October 2, 2017, United Rentals announced it had completed the acquisition of Neff Corporation, a competing general equipment rentals company, for approximately $1.3 billion. In the transaction, United Rentals acquired approximately $867 million in equipment (based on original equipment cost) and Neff's 69 rental facility locations in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>On October 4, 2017, United Rentals announced it had acquired the assets of Superior Speedie Portable Services, Inc. and Superior Logistics Services, Inc. (together "Superior Speedie"), which includes a fleet of over 8,000 portable toilets and over 130 restroom trailers.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> This new specialty division is named United Rentals Reliable Onsite Services.
</p><p>On July 2, 2018, United Rentals announced it had agreed to purchase BakerCorp International for about $715 million in cash, enhancing its Pump Solutions division. BakerCorp is a leading provider of tank, pump, filtration and trench shoring rentals, with about 950 employees, 46 locations in North America, and another 11 locations in Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Following the transaction, United Rentals changed the name of its Pump Solutions division to United Rentals Fluid Solutions.
</p><p>On September 11, 2018, United Rentals announced an agreement to add <a href="/wiki/BlueLine_Rental" title="BlueLine Rental">BlueLine Rental</a>, one of the top ten rental equipment companies in North America, to its growing list of acquisitions. United Rentals' board of directors unanimously approved the deal to purchase BlueLine from <a href="/wiki/Platinum_Equity" title="Platinum Equity">Platinum Equity</a> for about $2.1 billion in cash. BlueLine has 114 locations in 25 US states, Canada, and Puerto Rico, and approximately 46,000 rental assets marketed primarily to mid-sized and local accounts.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Rentals&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Canada" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>United Rentals of Canada, Inc., a subsidiary of United Rentals, has agreed to acquire WesternOne Rentals &amp; Sales LP with an all cash purchase of $91.8 million. The acquisition will add 330 employees and twelve locations in <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">Manitoba</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
