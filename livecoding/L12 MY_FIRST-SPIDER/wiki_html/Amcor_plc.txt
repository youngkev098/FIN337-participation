</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amcor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Amcor has two reporting segments: Flexibles Packaging and Rigid Plastics.<sup id="cite_ref-AR2017_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AR2017-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>Flexibles Packaging develops and supplies flexible packaging and specialty folding cartons. It has four business units: Flexibles Europe, Middle East and Africa; Flexibles Americas; Flexibles Asia Pacific; and Specialty Cartons.<sup id="cite_ref-AR2017_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AR2017-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>Rigid Plastics is one of the world's largest suppliers of rigid <a href="/wiki/Plastic_packaging" class="mw-redirect" title="Plastic packaging">plastic packaging</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> It has four business units: North America Beverages; North America Specialty Containers; Latin America; and Bericap Closures.<sup id="cite_ref-AR2017_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AR2017-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amcor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Australian Paper Manufacturers was renamed Amcor Limited in 1986. Previously, the company manufactured pulp paper and paperboard products, along with metal can and flexible packaging.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2000, Amcor demerged its business printing papers to focus on global packaging. The spin-off company was named <a href="/wiki/Paperlinx" title="Paperlinx">Paperlinx</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>Amcor's 45 percent interest in <a href="/wiki/Kimberly-Clark" title="Kimberly-Clark">Kimberly-Clark Australia</a>, a tissue and personal-care products company, was divested in June 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Mergers_and_acquisitions">Mergers and acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amcor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mergers and acquisitions" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In July 2002, Amcor acquired the rigid packaging and closures businesses of Schmalbach-Lubeca, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Ratingen" title="Ratingen">Ratingen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> This acquisition, at a cost of around $2.875 billion, made Amcor the largest manufacturer of PET (<a href="/wiki/Polyethylene_terephthalate" title="Polyethylene terephthalate">Polyethylene terephthalate</a>) containers, globally.
</p><p>In 2007, Amcor sold its European PET business to La Seda, a <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>-based manufacturer.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>Amcor announced in February 2008 the construction of a 400,000-tonnes per annum recycled paper mill located at its existing paper mill in <a href="/wiki/Botany,_New_South_Wales" title="Botany, New South Wales">Botany</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a>. The 'B9' paper machine, so named as it was the ninth paper machine to operate at the Botany Mill, was commissioned in 2012 and was officially opened by the <a href="/wiki/Premier_of_New_South_Wales" title="Premier of New South Wales">Premier of New South Wales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barry_O%27Farrell" title="Barry O'Farrell">Barry O'Farrell</a>, on 1 February 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The B9 paper machine produced enough paper to fulfill the company's <a href="/wiki/Australasia" title="Australasia">Australasian</a> paper requirements, and as a result, Amcor's other recycled paper mill in <a href="/wiki/Fairfield,_Victoria" title="Fairfield, Victoria">Fairfield</a>, Victoria, closed in late 2012.
</p><p>In 2010, Amcor completed the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Alcan" title="Alcan">Alcan</a>'s food (<a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>) and global pharmaceutical and tobacco businesses from <a href="/wiki/Rio_Tinto_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Rio Tinto Group">Rio Tinto</a> for US$2.03 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In 2011, Amcor acquired Marfred Industries, one of the largest independently owned packaging manufacturers and distributors in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012, Amcor acquired the business assets of Carter &amp; Associates, a major distributor of Amcor glass wine, champagne and spirit bottles in <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012, Amcor acquired the Aperio Group packaging company, adding further capability in Australia, New Zealand and <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>. This company also acquired Uniglobe Packaging, a business based in <a href="/wiki/Daman,_Daman_and_Diu" class="mw-redirect" title="Daman, Daman and Diu">Daman</a>, north of <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, in the same year.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2013, Amcor demerged its Australasia and Packaging Distribution (AAPD) business into a separate company called Orora, which is listed on the Australian Securities Exchange.<sup id="cite_ref-smh_2013-10-24_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smh_2013-10-24-21">[21]</a></sup> The spin-off allowed Amcor to focus on making flexible- and rigid-plastic packaging and tobacco packaging, mostly for overseas markets. 
</p><p>In 2015, Amcor's chairman, Graeme Liebelt, advised that <a href="/wiki/Ken_MacKenzie_(businessman)" title="Ken MacKenzie (businessman)">Ken MacKenzie</a> would retire as CEO effective 17 April 2015 and would be succeeded by Ron Delia.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2015, Nampak Flexibles, a flexible packaging business in <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> was acquired by Amcor for US$22 million.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2015, the US$30 million acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Souza_Cruz" title="Souza Cruz">Souza Cruz</a>'s internal tobacco packaging operations in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> was completed. This acquisition aligned with Amcor's objective of growing in <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2015, the US$55 million acquisition of Encon, a privately owned preform manufacturing business in the United States, was completed.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2015, the US$45 million acquisition of Deluxe Packages was completed. The business operated one manufacturing plant in <a href="/wiki/Yuba_City,_California" title="Yuba City, California">Yuba City</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2016, Amcor acquired Canadian company Plastics Moulders Limited for US$32 million. The rigid plastics business manufactured containers and closures for the food, home, and personal-care markets in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> from a single plant in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2016, Amcor acquired Alusa, the largest flexible packaging business in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> for a purchase price of US$435 million. It was the largest flexible packaging manufacturer and supplier in <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>, and a participant in <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, with one plant in each of these four countries.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2016, the specialty containers business of Sonoco Products Company, a manufacturer of specialty rigid plastic containers, was acquired by Amcor for US$280 million. The former Sonoco business had six production sites in the United States and one in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2017, Amcor acquired Qite for US$28 million. The <a href="/wiki/North_China" title="North China">North China</a> business produced flexible packaging products to large domestic customers in the dairy and food segments.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2017, Amcor acquired Plasticos, a specialty container business, from a leading food producer in Colombia. The acquisition expanded Amcor’s specialty container product offerings in Latin America, including thin-wall injection moulding and in-mould labelling.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2018, Amcor announced the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Bemis_Company" title="Bemis Company">Bemis Company</a>, a US-based manufacturer, through the acquisition of US$5.25 billion in shares.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Products">Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amcor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Products" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>Amcor develops and produces packaging for use with snacks and confectionery, cheese and yoghurt, fresh produce, beverage and pet food products, and rigid-plastic containers for brands in the food, beverage, pharmaceutical, and personal and home-care segments.<sup id="cite_ref-AGLOBE_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AGLOBE-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p><p>The company's global pharmaceutical packaging addresses requirements for <a href="/wiki/Dosage_form" title="Dosage form">unit doses</a>, safety, patient compliance, <a href="/wiki/Counterfeiting" class="mw-redirect" title="Counterfeiting">anti-counterfeiting</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>Amcor specialty cartons made from plastic materials are used for a variety of end markets, including pharmaceutical, healthcare, food, spirits and wine, personal and home-care products. Amcor also develops and makes wine and spirit closures.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2018, the company commercialised its Liquiform technology, which uses the packaged product instead of compressed air to simultaneously form and fill plastic containers and eliminates costs associated with traditional blow-molding, as well as handling, transporting, and warehousing empty containers.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amcor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Controversies" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>In 2001, Amcor was fined <a href="/wiki/A%24" class="mw-redirect" title="A$">A$</a>10,000 by <a href="/wiki/Environment_Protection_Authority_(Victoria)" title="Environment Protection Authority (Victoria)">EPA Victoria</a> for pollution.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p><p>In 2006, a <a href="/wiki/Four_Corners_(Australian_TV_program)" title="Four Corners (Australian TV program)">Four Corners</a> story, titled The A Team, revealed the controversial methods used by Amcor to influence forestry policies.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p><p>In 2007, following investigations by the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Competition_and_Consumer_Commission" title="Australian Competition and Consumer Commission">Australian Competition and Consumer Commission</a> (ACCC), Amcor and <a href="/wiki/Visy_Industries" title="Visy Industries">Visy</a> were found to have engaged in price fixing and market sharing in the market for the supply of corrugated fibreboard packaging.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p><p>In 2008, Amcor was convicted by the Heidelberg Magistrates' Court for releasing oil to the <a href="/wiki/Yarra_River" title="Yarra River">Yarra River</a> from its <a href="/wiki/Alphington,_Victoria" title="Alphington, Victoria">Alphington</a>, Victoria plant and fined $80,000.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
