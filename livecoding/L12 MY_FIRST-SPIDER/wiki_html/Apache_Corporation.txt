</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_operations">Current operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apache_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Current operations" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>In 2017, the company's total production was 457 thousand <a href="/wiki/Barrel_of_oil_equivalent" title="Barrel of oil equivalent">barrels of oil equivalent</a> (2,800,000 GJ) per day, of which 51% was in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, 36% was in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, and 13% was in the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>As of December 31, 2017, the company had 1.175 <a href="/wiki/1000000000_(number)" class="mw-redirect" title="1000000000 (number)">billion</a> <a href="/wiki/Barrel_of_oil_equivalent" title="Barrel of oil equivalent">barrels of oil equivalent</a> (7.19<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup>9</sup> GJ) of estimated proved reserves, of which 69% was in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, 20% was in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, and 11% was in the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>Almost all of the company's reserves in the United States are in the <a href="/wiki/Permian_Basin_(North_America)" title="Permian Basin (North America)">Permian Basin</a>. The company also has reserves in western <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Panhandle" title="Texas Panhandle">Texas Panhandle</a>, and south <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>The company has been operating in remote areas of the <a href="/wiki/Libyan_Desert" title="Libyan Desert">Libyan Desert</a> in Egypt since 1994 and has not experienced disruptions from political turmoil.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>The company has been operating in the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> since 2003, predominately in the <a href="/wiki/Forties_oilfield" class="mw-redirect" title="Forties oilfield">Forties oilfield</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:PostOakCentralMarsh.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/PostOakCentralMarsh.JPG/220px-PostOakCentralMarsh.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="2272" data-file-height="1704"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/PostOakCentralMarsh.JPG/220px-PostOakCentralMarsh.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:PostOakCentralMarsh.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The headquarters of Apache Corporation in <a href="/wiki/Post_Oak_Central" title="Post Oak Central">Post Oak Central</a>, <a href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Houston, Texas">Houston, Texas</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>In 1954, the Apache Oil Corporation was founded in <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis,_Minnesota" class="mw-redirect" title="Minneapolis, Minnesota">Minneapolis, Minnesota</a>, by Truman Anderson, <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Plank" title="Raymond Plank">Raymond Plank</a> and Charles Arnao with $250,000 in funding.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In 1955, the first wells were drilled in the Cushing field, between <a href="/wiki/Tulsa" class="mw-redirect" title="Tulsa">Tulsa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City" title="Oklahoma City">Oklahoma City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-history1954_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history1954-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In 1960, the company acquired interests in the <a href="/wiki/Foshay_Tower" title="Foshay Tower">Foshay Tower</a>, a Minneapolis landmark. The 32-story imitation of the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Monument" title="Washington Monument">Washington Monument</a>, became  Apache's headquarters from the early 1960s until 1984.<sup id="cite_ref-history1954_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history1954-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In 1967, the oil well Fagerness #1 was drilled, yielding the company's first major discovery.<sup id="cite_ref-history1954_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history1954-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In 1969, the company became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-history1954_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history1954-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In 1970, the company diversified into <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a> with the acquisition of the S&amp;J Ranch in California. The ranch produced <a href="/wiki/Citrus_fruit" class="mw-redirect" title="Citrus fruit">citrus fruit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Common_fig" title="Common fig">figs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pistachio" title="Pistachio">pistachios</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olive" title="Olive">olives</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Almond" title="Almond">almonds</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In 1971, the company formed Apache Exploration Company (subsequently "Apexco") as its oil and gas operating company.<sup id="cite_ref-history1954_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history1954-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In 1977, the company sold Apexco and its non-petroleum holdings, including the ranch, and reinvested into a farm-in agreement with GHK to operate its wells in the <a href="/wiki/Anadarko_Basin" title="Anadarko Basin">Anadarko Basin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-history1954_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history1954-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In 1980, the company acquired a non-operating interest, via a participation in a <a href="/wiki/Royal_Dutch_Shell" title="Royal Dutch Shell">Royal Dutch Shell</a> <a href="/wiki/Joint_venture" title="Joint venture">joint venture</a>, in operations in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Of_Mexico" class="mw-redirect" title="Gulf Of Mexico">Gulf Of Mexico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-history1980s_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history1980s-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 1981, the company created Apache Petroleum Company (APC), the first public <a href="/wiki/Master_limited_partnership" title="Master limited partnership">master limited partnership</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 1985, the company acquired oil and gas wells in 8 states from David Holdings for $200 million. In 1986, the company acquired oil and gas assets in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> from Occidental Petroleum.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 1987, the company moved its headquarters from <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis</a> to <a href="/wiki/Denver" title="Denver">Denver</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-history1980s_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history1980s-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 1991, the company doubled its reserves by acquiring assets from <a href="/wiki/Amoco" title="Amoco">Amoco</a>, including a position in the <a href="/wiki/Permian_Basin_(North_America)" title="Permian Basin (North America)">Permian Basin</a> of West Texas, for $515 million and 2 million shares of Apache.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 1992, the company moved its headquarters to <a href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Houston, Texas">Houston, Texas</a> and signed a lease for 220,000 square feet of office space.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 1993, the company acquired Hadson Energy Resources in a $58 million transaction, expanding its assets to offshore Western Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In 1994, the company began operations in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> by acquiring a 25 percent non-operated interest in the Qarun Concession, operated by the Phoenix Resource Companies. Production began in December 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In 1995, the company acquired Dekalb Energy Canada, marking Apache's return to <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, in a $285 million stock transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In 1995, the company also acquired 315 oil and gas fields in the <a href="/wiki/Permian_Basin_(North_America)" title="Permian Basin (North America)">Permian Basin</a>, the Texas-Louisiana Gulf Coast, western Oklahoma, East Texas, the <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountains" title="Rocky Mountains">Rocky Mountains</a> and the Gulf of Mexico from <a href="/wiki/Texaco" title="Texaco">Texaco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In 1996, the company acquired Phoenix Resources and took over operations of the Qarun Concession in Egypt.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In 1999, the company acquired fields and leases in the Gulf of Mexico from <a href="/wiki/Royal_Dutch_Shell" title="Royal Dutch Shell">Royal Dutch Shell</a> for $715 million in cash, plus 1 million shares of stock.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In 2001, the company acquired operations in the <a href="/wiki/Libyan_Desert" title="Libyan Desert">Libyan Desert</a> of Egypt from <a href="/wiki/Repsol_YPF" class="mw-redirect" title="Repsol YPF">Repsol YPF</a> for $410 million.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In 2002, the company drilled its first deepwater wells in the West Mediterranean Concession of offshore Egypt.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In 2003, the company acquired the <a href="/wiki/Forties_oilfield" class="mw-redirect" title="Forties oilfield">Forties oilfield</a>, the largest field ever discovered in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> as well as assets in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> from <a href="/wiki/BP" title="BP">BP</a> for $1.3 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2005, Apache and <a href="/wiki/ExxonMobil" title="ExxonMobil">ExxonMobil</a> completed a series of agreements that provided for transfers and joint ventures across a broad range of properties in Western Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2005, the company partially sold its stake in the deepwater section of Egypt's concession.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2006, the company acquired Gulf of Mexico Shelf properties from <a href="/wiki/BP" title="BP">BP</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2006, the company sold its oil production interest in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> to Australia-based ROC Oil Company Limited for US$260 million.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>In 2007, A test horizontal well at the Van Gogh project, in <a href="/wiki/Exmouth_Gulf" title="Exmouth Gulf">Exmouth Gulf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia" title="Western Australia">Western Australia</a>, produced 9,694 barrels per day.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2010, the company started production from the Van Gogh development offshore Western Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2010, a Federal judge upheld the company's decision to exclude from its annual meeting ballot a corporate governance proposal from a person who had not proven on a timely basis that he actually was one of the company's shareholders.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2010, the company acquired assets in the Gulf of Mexico from <a href="/wiki/Devon_Energy" title="Devon Energy">Devon Energy</a> for $1.05 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2010, the company acquired assets from <a href="/wiki/BP" title="BP">BP</a> in Texas, southeast New Mexico, western Canada, and Egypt for $7 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2010, the company acquired Mariner Energy for $2.7 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>In 2011, the company discovered eight oil wells in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>'s Faghur Basin.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2012, the company acquired assets in the Beryl field in the North Sea from <a href="/wiki/ExxonMobil" title="ExxonMobil">ExxonMobil</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2012, the company acquired Cordillera Energy Partners for $2.5 billion in cash and 6.3 million shares of common stock. The acquisition added approximately 71 million <a href="/wiki/Barrel_of_oil_equivalent" title="Barrel of oil equivalent">barrels of oil equivalent</a> (430,000,000 GJ) to Apache's reserves and strengthened its position across western Oklahoma and the Texas Panhandle.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2013, the company sold a non-controlling 1/3 share of its Egyptian assets to <a href="/wiki/Sinopec" title="Sinopec">Sinopec</a> <sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, the company sold its assets in <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia" title="Western Australia">Western Australia</a> for $2.1 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p><p>In 2016, the company sold its interest in the Scottish Area Gas Evacuation pipeline.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, the company sold its assets in Canada to <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Resources" title="Paramount Resources">Paramount Resources</a> for C$459.5 million.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p><p>In 2020, Apache admitted its once highly touted 'Alpine High' discovery was determined to be a bust after the company spent $3 Billion on the play.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup>  Steven Keenan, VP of global exploration and Lead Geologist on the play resigned once Alpine High was declared a failure.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2013_toxic_waste_spill">2013 toxic waste spill</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apache_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2013 toxic waste spill" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On June 1, 2013 an Apache pipeline in northern <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>, Canada was noticed to have ruptured, spilling 60,000 barrels (9.5 million litres) of toxic waste in what was cited as one of the largest of such disasters in recent history in North America.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Pipeline_explosion">Pipeline explosion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apache_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Pipeline explosion" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In June 2008, the natural gas pipeline explosion at Apache's processing hub on <a href="/wiki/Varanus_Island" title="Varanus Island">Varanus Island</a> led to the <a href="/wiki/2008_Western_Australian_gas_crisis" title="2008 Western Australian gas crisis">2008 Western Australian gas crisis</a>. The Australian government was forced to drop charges as a result of a technicality.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Attempts_to_limit_shareholder_proposals">Attempts to limit shareholder proposals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apache_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Attempts to limit shareholder proposals" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2007, Apache CEO G. Steven Farris wrote to the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission">U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission</a> in favor of limits on nonbinding shareholder proposals at public company annual meetings.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
