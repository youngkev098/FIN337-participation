<p><b>IQVIA</b>, formerly <b>Quintiles and IMS Health, Inc.</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-bw_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bw-2">[2]</a></sup> is an American multinational company serving the combined industries of <a href="/wiki/Health_information_technology" title="Health information technology">health information technology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clinical_research" title="Clinical research">clinical research</a>. It is a provider of biopharmaceutical development and commercial outsourcing services, focused primarily on Phase I-IV <a href="/wiki/Clinical_trial" title="Clinical trial">clinical trials</a> and associated laboratory and analytical services, including consulting services. It has a network of more than 58,000 employees in more than 100 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-bw_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bw-2">[2]</a></sup> As of 2019, IQVIA was reported to be one </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=IQVIA&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>In 1982, <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Gillings" title="Dennis Gillings">Dennis Gillings</a> founded and incorporated Quintiles Transnational in <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes_2010_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes_2010-4">[4]</a></sup> Quintiles Transnational established Quintiles Pacific Inc. and Quintiles Ireland Ltd. in 1990.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1991 Quintiles GmbH was established in Germany and Quintiles Laboratories Ltd. was established in Atlanta, Georgia.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In September 1996, Quintiles purchased Innovex Ltd. of Britain for $747.5 million in stock.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Quintiles went public in 1997 and completed a successful secondary stock offering.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>Over the course of 2016, concluding on October 3, Quintiles underwent a $17.6 billion merger with <a href="/wiki/IMS_Health" title="IMS Health">IMS Health</a>, becoming QuintilesIMS.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> As a result of the merger, the OneKey worldwide healthcare professionals database developed by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cegedim_Relationship_Management&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cegedim Relationship Management (page does not exist)">Cegedim Relationship Management</a> became part of QuintilesIMS. In November 2017, the company adopted the new name of IQVIA, and changed its ticker symbol on the <a href="/wiki/NYSE" class="mw-redirect" title="NYSE">NYSE</a> from Q to IQV.<sup id="cite_ref-bw_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bw-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
