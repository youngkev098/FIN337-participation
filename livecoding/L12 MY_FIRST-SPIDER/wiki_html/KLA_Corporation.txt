</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=KLA_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>KLA-Tencor was formed in 1997 through the merger of KLA Instruments and Tencor Instruments, two companies in the semiconductor equipment and yield management systems industry. KLA Instruments was founded in 1975 by Ken Levy and Bob Anderson, and focused on defect inspection solutions. Karel Urbanek established Tencor Instruments in 1977 with an emphasis on metrology solutions, later adding John Schwabacher as cofounder.<sup id="cite_ref-xbrlus_5_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xbrlus_5-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>Since the merger, which was valued at $1.3 billion, KLA-Tencor, now KLA Corporation, has acquired 27 additional companies:<sup id="cite_ref-xbrlus_6_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xbrlus_6-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<ul><li>1998: • Amray, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> • Nanopro GmbH<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> • Quantox product line from <a href="/wiki/Keithley_Instruments" title="Keithley Instruments">Keithley Instruments</a>, Inc. • VARS • The Ultrapointe subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Uniphase_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Uniphase Corporation">Uniphase Corporation</a></li>
<li>1999: • ACME Systems, Inc.</li>
<li>2000: • Fab Solutions, from ObjectSpace Inc. • FINLE Technologies, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li>2001: • Phase Metrics, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li>2004: • Candela Instruments, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> • Wafer Inspection Systems business of Inspex, Inc.</li>
<li>2006: • ADE Corporation</li>
<li>2007: • OnWafer Technologies<sup id="cite_ref-IPIRA_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IPIRA-10">[10]</a></sup> • SensArray Corporation<sup id="cite_ref-IPIRA_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IPIRA-10">[10]</a></sup> • Therma-Wave Corporation<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Seeking_Alpha_2019_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Seeking_Alpha_2019-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li>2008: • ICOS Vision Systems Corporation NV<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> • Microelectronic Inspection Equipment (MIE) business unit of Vistec Semiconductor Systems, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-Seeking_Alpha_2019_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Seeking_Alpha_2019-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li>2010: • Ambios Technology, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-Seeking_Alpha_2019_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Seeking_Alpha_2019-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li>2014: • Luminescent Technologies, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-Seeking_Alpha_2019_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Seeking_Alpha_2019-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li>2017: • Zeta Technologies Co. Ltd.<sup id="cite_ref-Seeking_Alpha_2019_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Seeking_Alpha_2019-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li>2018: • Nano Indenter product line from <a href="/wiki/Keysight_Technologies" class="mw-redirect" title="Keysight Technologies">Keysight Technologies</a> • Nanomechanics Inc. • MicroVision<sup id="cite_ref-Seeking_Alpha_2019_12-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Seeking_Alpha_2019-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li>2019: • Orbotech, Ltd.<sup id="cite_ref-Seeking_Alpha_2019_12-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Seeking_Alpha_2019-12">[12]</a></sup> • CAPRES A/S • MicroSense, LLC • Filmetrics, Inc. • Qoniac GmbH<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li></ul><p>On January 10, 2019, KLA-Tencor announced that they will be changing their name to KLA Corporation, and that they will adopt the tagline "Keep Looking Ahead". The name change took effect in July 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-ee_name_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ee_name-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=KLA_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Facilities" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>KLA's facilities include their global headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Milpitas,_California" title="Milpitas, California">Milpitas, California</a>, as well as manufacturing, support and R&amp;D facilities at multiple locations in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Asia-Pacific" title="Asia-Pacific">Asia/Pacific</a>.
</p><p>In June 2019, KLA announced plans to open a second US headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Ann_Arbor,_Michigan" title="Ann Arbor, Michigan">Ann Arbor, Michigan</a>. The facility is scheduled to open in summer 2021, and will host 500-600 new hires, around 50% of whom will be engineers.<sup id="cite_ref-aam_facility_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aam_facility-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="KLA_Foundation">KLA Foundation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=KLA_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: KLA Foundation" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>KLA Foundation (originally KLA-Tencor Foundation) is the company's philanthropic arm, and was founded in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-xbrlus_4_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xbrlus_4-17">[17]</a></sup> KLA Foundation supports and benefits the global communities in which KLA employees live and work.
</p>
