<p>The <b>Phillips 66 Company</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a> company headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Westchase,_Houston" title="Westchase, Houston">Westchase</a>, <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>. It debuted as an independent <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a> company when <a href="/wiki/ConocoPhillips" title="ConocoPhillips">ConocoPhillips</a> executed a <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spin-off</a> of its <a href="/wiki/Downstream_(petroleum_industry)" title="Downstream (petroleum industry)">downstream</a> and <a href="/wiki/Midstream_(petroleum_industry)" class="mw-redirect" title="Midstream (petroleum industry)">midstream</a> assets. Its name dating back to 1927 as a trademark of the <a href="/wiki/Phillips_Petroleum_Company" title="Phillips Petroleum Company">Phillips Petroleum Company</a>, the newly-reconfigured Phillips 66 began trading on the New York Stock Exchange on May 1, 2012, under the ticker PSX. The company is engaged in producing natural gas liquids (NGL) and petrochemicals. The company has approximately 14,000 employees worldwide and is active in more than 65 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-Reuters_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-2">[2]</a></sup> Phillips </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phillips_66&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Beginning">Beginning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phillips_66&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Beginning" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Phillips_Petroleum_Company" title="Phillips Petroleum Company">Phillips Petroleum Co.</a> was founded by Lee Eldas "L.E." Phillips and <a href="/wiki/Frank_Phillips_(oil_industrialist)" title="Frank Phillips (oil industrialist)">Frank Phillips</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bartlesville,_Oklahoma" title="Bartlesville, Oklahoma">Bartlesville, Oklahoma</a>, and incorporated on June 13, 1917. The new company had assets of $3 million, 27 employees and land throughout <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kansas" title="Kansas">Kansas</a>. After discovery of Texas's huge Panhandle gas field in 1918 and the <a href="/wiki/Hugoton_Gas_Field" title="Hugoton Gas Field">Hugoton Field</a> to its north in Kansas, Phillips became increasingly involved in the rapidly developing natural gas industry. In particular, the company specialized in extracting liquids from <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a> and by 1925 was the nation's largest producer of natural gas liquids. In 1927, the company's gasoline was being tested on <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_66" title="U.S. Route 66">U.S. Highway 66</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>.  When the car reached the then-breakneck speed of 66 mph (106 km/h), the company decided to name the new fuel <i>Phillips 66</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>The first Phillips 66 service station opened November 19, 1927, at 805 E. Central Street in <a href="/wiki/Wichita,_Kansas" title="Wichita, Kansas">Wichita, Kansas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> This station still stands, preserved by the local historical society. The first Phillips 66 service station built in Texas opened on July 27, 1928, on the corner of 5th and Main streets in Turkey, Texas.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Turkey_Enterprise_newspaper,_July_26,_1928._7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Turkey_Enterprise_newspaper,_July_26,_1928.-7">[7]</a></sup> In January, 2019, Turkey's Phillips 66 service station was recognized by the Texas Historical Commission as a Recorded Texas Historical Landmark, and a ceremony to unveil the State Historical Marker is planned to take place in April 2020.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Logo">Logo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phillips_66&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Logo" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3><p>
The Phillips 66 shield logo, linking it to <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_66" title="U.S. Route 66">U.S. Route 66</a>, was introduced in 1930 in a black and orange color scheme that would last nearly thirty years. In 1959, Phillips replaced these colors with red, white and black, the one still deployed.</p><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Phillips_66_station,_Bassett,_Nebraska,_USA.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Phillips_66_station%2C_Bassett%2C_Nebraska%2C_USA.jpg/220px-Phillips_66_station%2C_Bassett%2C_Nebraska%2C_USA.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1920"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Phillips_66_station%2C_Bassett%2C_Nebraska%2C_USA.jpg/220px-Phillips_66_station%2C_Bassett%2C_Nebraska%2C_USA.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Phillips_66_station,_Bassett,_Nebraska,_USA.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Old-fashioned Phillips 66 station in <a href="/wiki/Bassett,_Nebraska" title="Bassett, Nebraska">Bassett, Nebraska</a></div></div></div>
<p>From the late 1930s until the 1960s, Phillips employed registered nurses as "highway hostesses" that made random visits to Phillips 66 stations within their districts. The nurses inspected station restroom facilities to ensure they were clean and stocked with supplies. They also served as <a href="/wiki/Concierges" class="mw-redirect" title="Concierges">concierges</a>, spreading goodwill for the company by helping motorists identify suitable dining and lodging facilities. (<a href="/wiki/Union_76" class="mw-redirect" title="Union 76">Union 76</a> employed similar hostesses, called the "Sparkle Corps".)
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Motor_oil">Motor oil</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phillips_66&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Motor oil" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Phillips was among the first oil companies to introduce a <a href="/wiki/Motor_oil#Multi-grade" title="Motor oil">multi-grade motor oil</a>, <b>TropArtic,</b> in 1954. Such motor oils were designed to be used year-round in automobile engines, as opposed to single grades for which different grades of motor oils were recommended to meet weather variances.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Gasland.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Gasland.jpg/220px-Gasland.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="4032" data-file-height="3024"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Gasland.jpg/220px-Gasland.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Gasland.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Phillips66 Shield image by Gas Land Petroleum</div></div></div>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Gas_stations">Gas stations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phillips_66&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Gas stations" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Phillips also had gasoline stations in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>'s western provinces of <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">Manitoba</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a> under the name Pacific 66 until the late 1970s. In 1932, the <a href="/wiki/76_(Phillips_66)" class="mw-redirect" title="76 (Phillips 66)">76 brand</a>, long familiar in the western U.S., was created by Union Oil Company of California (later Unocal). In 1946, Phillips purchased the Utah-based Wasatch Oil Co., bringing the Phillips 66 brand to the northern Rocky Mountain states and the far eastern portions of Oregon and Washington. In 1966, Phillips entered the West Coast market by purchasing <a href="/wiki/Tidewater_Petroleum" title="Tidewater Petroleum">Tidewater Oil Co.</a>'s refining and marketing properties in that region and rebranding all <a href="/wiki/Flying_A" class="mw-redirect" title="Flying A">Flying A</a> distributorships and service stations to Phillips 66.
</p><p>In 1967, Phillips became the nation's second oil company, after <a href="/wiki/Texaco" title="Texaco">Texaco</a>, to sell and market gasoline in all 50 states, by opening a Phillips 66 station in Anchorage, Alaska. However, Phillips' experiment in 50-state marketing was short-lived. 
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:1928_Phillips_66.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/1928_Phillips_66.jpg/220px-1928_Phillips_66.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="2973" data-file-height="1969"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 146px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/1928_Phillips_66.jpg/220px-1928_Phillips_66.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="146" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:1928_Phillips_66.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Restored 1928 Phillips 66 Service Station in Turkey, Texas. The first Phillips 66 Service Station built in Texas</div></div></div>
<p>The company withdrew from gasoline marketing in the northeastern U.S. in 1972, and sold the former Tidewater properties on the West Coast to The Oil &amp; Shale Corporation (<a href="/wiki/Tosco_Corporation" title="Tosco Corporation">Tosco</a>) in 1976. Today, Phillips 66 primarily operates in the Midwest and Southwest. In recent years, the 76, Philips 66 and Conoco brand-names have begun to reappear in Eastern markets, including the New York City metro region, via a licensing deal with <a href="/wiki/Motiva_Enterprises" title="Motiva Enterprises">Motiva Enterprises</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Mergers">Mergers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phillips_66&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mergers" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Phillips Petroleum created a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Chevron_Corporation" title="Chevron Corporation">Chevron Corporation</a>'s chemicals and plastics division in 2000 and also acquired <a href="/wiki/ARCO" title="ARCO">ARCO</a> Alaska Inc. from <a href="/wiki/British_Petroleum" class="mw-redirect" title="British Petroleum">British Petroleum</a>. It purchased <a href="/wiki/Tosco_Corporation" title="Tosco Corporation">Tosco</a>, which included <a href="/wiki/Circle_K" title="Circle K">Circle K</a> convenience stores and <a href="/wiki/Union_76" class="mw-redirect" title="Union 76">Union 76</a> gasoline, in 2001. The 76 brand, long familiar in the western and southern U.S., was created by <a href="/wiki/Union_Oil_Company_of_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Union Oil Company of California">Union Oil Company of California</a> (later <a href="/wiki/Unocal" class="mw-redirect" title="Unocal">Unocal</a>) in 1932. In 1983, Phillips Petroleum purchased the General American Oil Company from owners Algur H. Meadows, Henry W. Peters, and Ralph G. Trippett.
</p><p>In 2002, Phillips Petroleum merged with <a href="/wiki/Conoco_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Conoco Inc.">Conoco</a> to form <a href="/wiki/ConocoPhillips" title="ConocoPhillips">ConocoPhillips</a>. The merged company continued marketing gasoline and other products under the Phillips 66, Conoco, and 76 brands. However, Phillips 66 Company licenses the Phillips 66 brand to <a href="/wiki/Suncor_Energy" title="Suncor Energy">Suncor Energy</a> for its Phillips 66-branded stations in Colorado.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phillips_66&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Marketing" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Phillips_66_Aviation_logo_at_Premier_Jet_Center,_Hillsboro_Airport_(Oregon).jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Phillips_66_Aviation_logo_at_Premier_Jet_Center%2C_Hillsboro_Airport_%28Oregon%29.jpg/220px-Phillips_66_Aviation_logo_at_Premier_Jet_Center%2C_Hillsboro_Airport_%28Oregon%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="857"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 147px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Phillips_66_Aviation_logo_at_Premier_Jet_Center%2C_Hillsboro_Airport_%28Oregon%29.jpg/220px-Phillips_66_Aviation_logo_at_Premier_Jet_Center%2C_Hillsboro_Airport_%28Oregon%29.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="147" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Phillips_66_Aviation_logo_at_Premier_Jet_Center,_Hillsboro_Airport_(Oregon).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Winged version of logo used for domestic airplane fuel stations, seen in Hillsboro, Oregon</div></div></div>
<p>In 1973, Phillips began billing itself as "The Performance Company," promoting innovations with asphaltic materials, fertilizers, and other non-automotive products as well as its traditional automotive productds. Other slogans have included: "Go first-class.... Go Phillips 66"; "The gasoline that won the West"; "Good things for cars and the people who drive them;" "Hard working gas;" and "At Phillips 66, it's performance that counts." Their current slogan (July 2015) is "Proud To Be Here." The 2016 slogan will be "The Power of 2" after reclassification of its brands into either Phillips 66 or Kendall.
</p><p>Phillips 66 conspicuously sponsored <a href="/wiki/PBS" title="PBS">PBS</a> programming during the 1980s. It funded <i><a href="/wiki/A.M._Weather" title="A.M. Weather">A.M. Weather</a></i>; <i>The Search for Solutions</i>; and <i>Onstage with Judith Somogi</i>.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Spin-off">Spin-off</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phillips_66&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Spin-off" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2012, Phillips 66 was <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spun off</a> from <a href="/wiki/ConocoPhillips" title="ConocoPhillips">ConocoPhillips</a>.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Berkshire_Hathaway_trade">Berkshire Hathaway trade</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phillips_66&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Berkshire Hathaway trade" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On December 30, 2013, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Berkshire_Hathaway" title="Berkshire Hathaway">Berkshire Hathaway</a> would trade more than 19 million of its 27.2 million shares in Phillips 66 to acquire a business that makes additives that help crude oil flow through pipelines.  The final number of shares will be determined when the deal actually closes.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Spin-off_of_natural_gas_pipelines">Spin-off of natural gas pipelines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phillips_66&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Spin-off of natural gas pipelines" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On February 17, 2015, Phillips 66 sold two natural gas pipeline systems to its affiliate, Phillips 66 Partners for $1.01 billion in cash and stock.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phillips_66&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Operations" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In the United States, the company operates <a href="/wiki/Conoco" title="Conoco">Conoco</a>, Phillips 66 and <a href="/wiki/76_(Phillips_66)" class="mw-redirect" title="76 (Phillips 66)">76</a> stations.  In Europe, Phillips 66 operates <a href="/wiki/Jet_(brand)" title="Jet (brand)">Jet</a> filling stations in Austria, Denmark, Germany, Sweden and the United Kingdom.  It sold its Jet stations in Belgium, the Czech Republic, Finland, Hungary, Poland and <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a> to its Russian affiliate, <a href="/wiki/Lukoil" title="Lukoil">Lukoil</a>.  It uses the <a href="/wiki/Coop_(Switzerland)" title="Coop (Switzerland)">Coop</a> identity in Switzerland.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The company is the fourth largest finished lubricants supplier in the United States.
</p><p>Phillips 66 owns 13 refineries with a net crude oil capacity of 2.2 million barrels per day (350<span style="margin-left:0.2em">×<span style="margin-left:0.1em">10</span></span><s style="display:none">^</s><sup>3</sup> m<sup>3</sup>/d), 10,000 branded marketing outlets, and 15,000 miles (24,000 km) of pipelines.  It has 50 percent stake in DCP Midstream, LLC, a natural gas gatherers and processors with 7.2 billion cubic feet per day (200<span style="margin-left:0.2em">×<span style="margin-left:0.1em">10</span></span><s style="display:none">^</s><sup>6</sup> m<sup>3</sup>/d) of processing capacity. It also owns 50 percent stake in <a href="/wiki/Chevron_Phillips_Chemical_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="Chevron Phillips Chemical Company">Chevron Phillips Chemical Co</a>.
</p><p>Phillips 66 also owns a one-quarter share in the controversial <a href="/wiki/Dakota_Access_Pipeline" title="Dakota Access Pipeline">Dakota Access Pipeline</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_affairs">Corporate affairs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phillips_66&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Corporate affairs" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>In 2012, after Phillips 66 split from <a href="/wiki/ConocoPhillips" title="ConocoPhillips">ConocoPhillips</a>, it moved its operations from the ConocoPhillips headquarters to the Pinnacle Westchase building,<sup id="cite_ref-NewHQ_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NewHQ-16">[16]</a></sup> a nine-story Class A office building located on 8.4 acres (3.4 ha) of land in <a href="/wiki/Westchase,_Houston" title="Westchase, Houston">Westchase</a>, <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> This was a temporary headquarters location.<sup id="cite_ref-NewHQ_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NewHQ-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2016, Phillips 66 completed its move to a new permanent headquarters on a 14-acre (5.7 ha) plot of land in Westchase. The new headquarters is between Westheimer Road and Briar Forest, in close proximity to the <a href="/wiki/Sam_Houston_Tollway" class="mw-redirect" title="Sam Houston Tollway">Sam Houston Tollway</a>. Phillips 66 purchased the land from a subsidiary of Thomas Properties Group. The architect of record is <a href="/wiki/HOK_(firm)" title="HOK (firm)">HOK</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The official groundbreaking was in November 2013, and the opening was completed on schedule. The 1.1 million square foot headquarters includes conference spaces, medical facilities, food service facilities, outdoor recreational space, a gymnasium with full-size basketball court, and training facilities. Irrigation of the outdoor landscaping is via a <a href="/wiki/Reclaimed_water" title="Reclaimed water">reclaimed water</a> system. The new campus houses 2,200 workers who were spread among six different Houston locations, including the ConocoPhillips headquarters, where several hundred Phillips 66 workers had remained post spin-off.<sup id="cite_ref-NewHQ_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NewHQ-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>On 4 August 2015 it was announced that the company had formed a "long-term partnership" with English <a href="/wiki/Non-League_football" title="Non-League football">non-league</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">association football</a> club <a href="/wiki/Leamington_F.C." title="Leamington F.C.">Leamington F.C.</a>. The deal involved, amongst other things, the renaming of the club's ground from <i>"The New Windmill Ground"</i> to <i>"The Phillips 66 Community Stadium."</i><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
