<p><b>Varian Medical Systems</b> (VAR) of Palo Alto, California, USA, is a radiation <a href="/wiki/Oncology" title="Oncology">oncology</a> treatments and software maker. Their medical devices include <a href="/wiki/Linear_accelerators" class="mw-redirect" title="Linear accelerators">linear accelerators</a> and software for treating cancer and other medical conditions with <a href="/wiki/Radiotherapy" class="mw-redirect" title="Radiotherapy">radiotherapy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radiosurgery" title="Radiosurgery">radiosurgery</a>, <a href="/wiki/Proton_therapy" title="Proton therapy">proton therapy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Brachytherapy" title="Brachytherapy">brachytherapy</a>.  The company supplies software for managing cancer clinics, radiotherapy centers, and medical oncology practices. Varian is a supplier of tubes and digital detectors for <a href="/wiki/X-ray" title="X-ray">X-ray</a> imaging in medical diagnostics, dentistry, veterinary care, scientific, security, industrial inspection including high-energy X-ray technology for cargo screening. Varian Medical Systems employs more than 7,100 people <sup id="cite_ref-xbrlus_1_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xbrlus_1-1">[1]</a></sup> at manufacturing sites in North America, Europe, </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Varian_Medical_Systems&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Varian was founded in 1948 as <a href="/wiki/Varian_Associates" title="Varian Associates">Varian Associates</a> by <a href="/wiki/Russell_H._Varian" class="mw-redirect" title="Russell H. Varian">Russell H. Varian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sigurd_F._Varian" class="mw-redirect" title="Sigurd F. Varian">Sigurd F. Varian</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_Webster_Hansen" class="mw-redirect" title="William Webster Hansen">William Webster Hansen</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Edward_Ginzton" title="Edward Ginzton">Edward Ginzton</a> to sell the <a href="/wiki/Klystron" title="Klystron">Klystron</a>, the first tube which could generate electromagnetic waves at microwave frequencies, and other electromagnetic equipment.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>By 1999, Varian Associates had branched into <a href="/wiki/Semiconductor" title="Semiconductor">semiconductor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vacuum_tube" title="Vacuum tube">vacuum tube</a>, and medical device fields. On April 2, 1999 these divisions split to become <a href="/wiki/Varian_Semiconductor" title="Varian Semiconductor">Varian Semiconductor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Varian,_Inc." title="Varian, Inc.">Varian, Inc.</a> and Varian Medical Systems.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquired_companies">Acquired companies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Varian_Medical_Systems&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acquired companies" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Varian Medical Systems has acquired other companies including Pan-Pacific Enterprises,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> ACCEL Instruments,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Bio-Imaging Research, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Sigma Micro Informatique Conseil,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Argus Software,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Velocity Medical Solutions.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and MeVis Medical Solutions AG <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2018, the company announced the acquisition of Sirtex Medical for $1.3 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 2019, the company acquired CyberHeart, a privately-held company with intellectual property (IP) that covers the use of radiation in the heart (cardiac radioablation) and other forms of radiosurgery for cardiovascular disease.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Spin-off_companies">Spin-off companies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Varian_Medical_Systems&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Spin-off companies" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>30.01.2017 spin-off of Varex Imaging Corporation (manufacturing of X-ray imaging products) from Varian Medical Systems had been successfully completed.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Products">Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Varian_Medical_Systems&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Products" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Linear_accelerators">Linear accelerators</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Varian_Medical_Systems&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Linear accelerators" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Varian manufactures a range of <a href="/wiki/Megavoltage" class="mw-redirect" title="Megavoltage">megavoltage</a> <a href="/wiki/Linear_Accelerator" class="mw-redirect" title="Linear Accelerator">LINACs</a> with varying levels of features and complexity, for example different numbers of <a href="/wiki/Multileaf_collimator" title="Multileaf collimator">multileaf collimators</a> or the ability to perform <a href="/wiki/Radiosurgery" title="Radiosurgery">radiosurgery</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p><b>TrueBeam</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Radiation_therapy" title="Radiation therapy">radiotherapy</a> system.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>The <b>EDGE</b> <a href="/wiki/Radiosurgery" title="Radiosurgery">radiosurgery</a> suite was launched in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The first cancer centers to use the new system were the Champalimaud Foundation in <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Henry_Ford_Health_System" title="Henry Ford Health System">Henry Ford Health System</a> in <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Halcyon">Halcyon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Varian_Medical_Systems&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Halcyon" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2017, Varian launched <b>Halcyon</b>. The system features unique dual-layer <a href="/wiki/Multileaf_collimator" title="Multileaf collimator">MLC</a> that enables high modulation with low leakage for every field or arc.Halcyon is engineered ergonomically to be intuitive, friendly and comfortable for clinical staff and patient alike. This human centered approach means there are fewer parts and protocol for staff to worry about, more time freed up to advance clinical capabilitie, and most importantly, more opportunities to provide the highest level of care for every patient.  <sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Proton_Therapy">Proton Therapy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Varian_Medical_Systems&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Proton Therapy" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Varian manufactures the ProBeam Proton Therapy System, with current and planned installations at several sites globally.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> These are an all <a href="/wiki/Pencil-beam_scanning" title="Pencil-beam scanning">pencil-beam scanning</a> proton therapy system utilizing IMTP (intensity modulated proton therapy), which was developed with <a href="/wiki/Paul_Scherrer_Institute" title="Paul Scherrer Institute">PSI</a> of Switzerland.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Varian also develops <a href="/wiki/Medical_software" title="Medical software">medical software</a> and <a href="/wiki/Radiology_information_system" class="mw-redirect" title="Radiology information system">radiology information system</a> for proton treatment planning system.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Ethos_Therapy">Ethos Therapy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Varian_Medical_Systems&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Ethos Therapy" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>September 16, 2019 — At the 2019 American Society for Radiation Oncology (ASTRO) annual meeting, being held Sept. 15-18 in Chicago, Varian announced Ethos therapy, an artificial intelligence (AI)-driven holistic solution designed to increase the capability, flexibility and efficiency of radiotherapy. This new solution is designed to deliver an entire adaptive treatment in a typical 15-minute timeslot, from patient setup through treatment delivery.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Litigation">Litigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Varian_Medical_Systems&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Litigation" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_speech">Free speech</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Varian_Medical_Systems&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Free speech" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1999 Varian Medical Systems, Inc. sued a former employee for defamation in <a href="/wiki/Varian_v._Delfino" title="Varian v. Delfino">Varian v. Delfino</a>.  The terminated employee Dr. Michelangelo Delfino posted numerous messages criticizing the company on the Internet.  The case finally settled on undisclosed terms.  The lawsuit itself was controversial, heavily publicized, and led to a landmark ruling about California's <a href="/wiki/Anti-SLAPP" class="mw-redirect" title="Anti-SLAPP">anti-SLAPP</a> statute.  Some market-based workers rights activists uphold Delfino as an example because he mortgaged his house to pay his legal bills related to the lawsuit with Varian.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="University_of_Pittsburgh">University of Pittsburgh</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Varian_Medical_Systems&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: University of Pittsburgh" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On April 25, 2012 a <a href="/wiki/United_States_federal_judge" title="United States federal judge">US federal judge</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a> awarded attorney fees, costs, and doubled damages totaling $73.6 million to the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pittsburgh" title="University of Pittsburgh">University of Pittsburgh</a> after the university won a suit on medical patent infringement grounds against Varian.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
