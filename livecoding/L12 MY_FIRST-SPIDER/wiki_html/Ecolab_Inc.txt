</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecolab&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years_:_1923_-_1950s">Early years : 1923 - 1950s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecolab&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years : 1923 - 1950s" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Merritt J. "M.J." Osborn founded Economics Laboratory in 1923 with the tagline "Saving time, lightening labor and reducing costs to those we serve." The company's original product was Absorbit, a product designed to quickly clean carpets in hotel rooms. This was soon followed by Soilax, a dishwasher soap. During the 1930s, the company expanded throughout the United States and sales reached US$5.4 million by the end of 1940s. It acquired the Magnus Company in the early 1950s, which gave the company access to Magnus's industrial specialty businesses - including <a href="/wiki/Pulp_and_paper_industry" title="Pulp and paper industry">pulp and paper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metalworking" title="Metalworking">metalworking</a>, <a href="/wiki/Transportation_industry" class="mw-redirect" title="Transportation industry">transportation</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Petrochemical" title="Petrochemical">petrochemical processing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-EC-C-H-01_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EC-C-H-01-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Transition_to_a_public_corporation_and_expansion:_1950s_-_1986">Transition to a public corporation and expansion: 1950s - 1986</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecolab&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transition to a public corporation and expansion: 1950s - 1986" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In the 1950s, international expansion of <a href="/wiki/Economics_Laboratory" class="mw-redirect" title="Economics Laboratory">Economics Laboratory</a> started, with the establishment of its first overseas subsidiary in <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> in 1956. It became a <a href="/wiki/Publicly_traded_corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Publicly traded corporation">publicly traded corporation</a> in 1957,<sup id="cite_ref-EC-C-H-01_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EC-C-H-01-4">[4]</a></sup> and continued to expand during the 1960s and 1970s. In 1979, it acquired Apollo Technologies, but in 1983 the acquired Apollo subsidiary was shut down. In 1984, Ecolab acquired pest elimination leader Lystads, Inc., of Grand Forks, North Dakota.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> This is the first of many regional pest service firms acquired to create a national pest elimination business.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecolab_Inc.:_1986_-_present">Ecolab Inc.: 1986 - present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecolab&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ecolab Inc.: 1986 - present" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Travelers_Companies-St_Paul-20061008.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Travelers_Companies-St_Paul-20061008.jpg/220px-Travelers_Companies-St_Paul-20061008.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3456" data-file-height="2304"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 147px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Travelers_Companies-St_Paul-20061008.jpg/220px-Travelers_Companies-St_Paul-20061008.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="147" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Travelers_Companies-St_Paul-20061008.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ecolab's headquarters in Saint Paul, Minnesota<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p>In 1986, the company changed its name from Economics Laboratory to Ecolab Inc. and is listed on the New York Stock Exchange.
</p><p>In 1987, the company formed a textile care division to serve commercial laundry customers that process at least 1 million pounds of linen a year.
</p><p>In 1987, Ecolab purchased the lawncare servicer provider ChemLawn for US$376 million. It sold the acquisition in 1992 to <a href="/wiki/ServiceMaster" title="ServiceMaster">ServiceMaster</a> for US$103 million as it couldn't turn ChemLawn into a profitable business.<sup id="cite_ref-EC-C-H-01_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EC-C-H-01-4">[4]</a></sup> Ecolab and the German <a href="/wiki/Fast-moving_consumer_goods" title="Fast-moving consumer goods">fast-moving consumer goods</a> firm <a href="/wiki/Henkel" title="Henkel">Henkel KGaA</a> formed a 50:50 European joint venture called 'Henkel-Ecolab' in mid-1991 to expand into European/Russian markets.<sup id="cite_ref-EC-C-H-10_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EC-C-H-10-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>Ecolab would continue to acquire companies in the 1990s, namely Kay Chemical (1993), Australia-based Gibson Chemical Industries Ltd (1997), and GCS Service Inc (1998). In 2001 it bought out its partner Henkel's 50% stake in the European joint venture, Henkel-Ecolab. Ecolab integrated the former Henkel-Ecolab into its existing global operations. As a legacy of the Henkel-Ecolab cooperation from 1991–2001, Henkel continued to hold a 29.5% stake in Ecolab Inc. after the sale of the joint venture.<sup id="cite_ref-EC-C-H-10_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EC-C-H-10-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In the mid-2000s, strategic acquisitions continued, and Ecolab diversified its portfolio of customer offerings by venturing into the food safety management business as well as a healthcare business unit. In 2005, it opened a new global research, development, and engineering center in <a href="/wiki/Eagan,_Minnesota" title="Eagan, Minnesota">Eagan, Minnesota</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-EC-C-H-14_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EC-C-H-14-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 2006, Ecolab won the Black Pearl Award for Corporate Excellence in Food Safety and Quality, presented by the International Association of Food Protection (IAFP).<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 2007, Ecolab acquired Microtek Medical Holdings, Inc., expanding its infection prevention expertise and offerings to hospitals and other healthcare facilities.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 2008, Henkel sold all of the 73 million shares, i.e., nearly a 29.5% stake it held in Ecolab, ending the two-decade-long partnership.<sup id="cite_ref-EC-C-H-11_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EC-C-H-11-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-EC-C-H-12_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EC-C-H-12-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-EC-C-H-13_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EC-C-H-13-14">[14]</a></sup> The same year, Ecolab established <a href="/wiki/Zurich" class="mw-redirect" title="Zurich">Zurich</a> as its Europe, the Middle East and Africa headquarters.<sup id="cite_ref-EC-C-H-14_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EC-C-H-14-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2011, Ecolab announced a merger with <a href="/wiki/Nalco_Holding_Company" title="Nalco Holding Company">Nalco Holding Company</a>, Inc., and in December 2011 Nalco became a wholly owned subsidiary of Ecolab Inc. after it completed its US$5.4 billion acquisition.<sup id="cite_ref-GT-DEX-2011-H-01_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GT-DEX-2011-H-01-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GT-DEX-2011-H-02_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GT-DEX-2011-H-02-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GT-DEX-2011-H-03_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GT-DEX-2011-H-03-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GT-DEX-2011-H-04_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GT-DEX-2011-H-04-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2012, Microsoft co-founder <a href="/wiki/Bill_Gates" title="Bill Gates">Bill Gates</a>, through his investment vehicles — <a href="/wiki/Cascade_Investment" title="Cascade Investment">Cascade Investment</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Bill_%26_Melinda_Gates_Foundation" title="Bill &amp; Melinda Gates Foundation">Bill &amp; Melinda Gates Foundation</a> — increased his stake of 10.8% in Ecolab to 25%.<sup id="cite_ref-EC-C-H-06_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EC-C-H-06-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-EC-C-H-07_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EC-C-H-07-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-EC-C-H-08_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EC-C-H-08-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2012, Ecolab opened its Taicang, China, manufacturing plant. The facility, one of the company's largest, helped expand Ecolab's presence in Greater China and was the company's first plant to recycle 100% of its wastewater.
</p><p>In October 2012, Ecolab entered an agreement to acquire Champion Technologies.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> That purchase closed in April 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2014, Ecolab, working with <a href="/wiki/Trucost" title="Trucost">TruCost</a>, launched the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.waterriskmonetizer.com">Water Risk Monetizer</a>. It provides industry an online, no-cost financial modeling tool to assess and monetize the risk of <a href="/wiki/Water_scarcity" title="Water scarcity">water scarcity</a> to their operations.
</p><p>In November 2015, for $40.5 million Ecolab acquired the U.S. operations of Charlotte, N.C.-based <a href="/wiki/Swisher_Hygiene" title="Swisher Hygiene">Swisher Hygiene</a> which provides hygiene and sanitizing <a href="/wiki/Solution" title="Solution">solutions</a> for the foodservice, hospitality, retail and healthcare markets.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2016, Ecolab made a minority investment in Aquatech International, LLC of an undisclosed amount.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecolab&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Operations" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Ecolab employs approximately 47,000 people, and operates in more than 170 countries. It is organized in the following geographical divisions:
</p>
<ul><li>North America (based in <a href="/wiki/St._Paul,_Minnesota" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Paul, Minnesota">St. Paul, Minnesota</a>)</li>
<li>Europe (based in <a href="/wiki/Zurich" class="mw-redirect" title="Zurich">Zurich</a>, Switzerland)</li>
<li>Middle East and Africa, including South Africa (based in Dubai, UAE)</li>
<li>Asia Pacific</li>
<li>Greater China</li>
<li>Latin America</li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Products_and_services">Products and services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecolab&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Products and services" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>Ecolab provides water, hygiene and energy technologies and services to the food, energy, healthcare, industrial and hospitality markets.<sup id="cite_ref-EC-C-H-03_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EC-C-H-03-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-EC-C-H-04_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EC-C-H-04-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>The company's food safety services provide consulting to restaurants, hospitals, food retailers and food &amp; beverage manufacturing facilities. It is also a supplier of chemicals used by beef and poultry processors - to reduce pathogens, such as E. coli and salmonella - in uncooked beef and poultry.
</p>
