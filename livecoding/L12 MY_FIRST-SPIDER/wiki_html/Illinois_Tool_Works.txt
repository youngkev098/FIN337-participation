</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="ITW_subsidiaries">ITW subsidiaries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Illinois_Tool_Works&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: ITW subsidiaries" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>ITW comprises a number of subsidiaries. Notable brands include Hobart, <a href="/wiki/Miller_Electric" title="Miller Electric">Miller Electric</a>, DUO-FAST fasteners, Paslode, Foster Refrigerator,<sup id="cite_ref-fosterrefrigerator1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fosterrefrigerator1-6">[6]</a></sup> and the Wolf Range Company. </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Illegal_exports">Illegal exports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Illinois_Tool_Works&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Illegal exports" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Commerce" title="United States Department of Commerce">United States Department of Commerce</a> imposed a $142,000 <a href="/wiki/Civil_penalty" title="Civil penalty">civil penalty</a> on Illinois Tool Works in 2000, to settle allegations that the company illegally exported chemicals to Brazil on seven occasions between March 1994 and October 1997 without the required licenses and making false or misleading statements on Shipper's Export Declarations. Illinois Tool Works agreed to pay the penalty. $37,000 was suspended as part of the settlement. The chemicals exported can be used for commercial purposes and in the making of <a href="/wiki/Chemical_weapons" class="mw-redirect" title="Chemical weapons">chemical weapons</a> as well.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
