(MarketAxess)</b> is an international <a href="/wiki/Financial_technology" title="Financial technology">financial technology</a> company that operates an <a href="/wiki/Electronic_trading_platform" title="Electronic trading platform">electronic trading platform</a> for the institutional <a href="/wiki/Bond_market" title="Bond market">credit markets</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg-1">[1]</a></sup> and also provides <a href="/wiki/Market_data" title="Market data">market data</a> and post-trade services.<sup id="cite_ref-fidesk_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fidesk-2">[2]</a></sup> It enables <a href="/wiki/Institutional_investor" title="Institutional investor">institutional investors</a> and <a href="/wiki/Broker-dealer" title="Broker-dealer">broker-dealers</a> to trade credit instruments, including corporate <a href="/wiki/Bond_(finance)" title="Bond (finance)">bonds</a>, and other types of <a href="/wiki/Fixed_income" title="Fixed income">fixed income</a> products.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ-3">[3]</a></sup></p><table class="infobox vcard" Richard McVey, who is still the company’s Chairman &amp; Chief Executive Officer.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ-3">[3]</a></sup> Headquartered in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>, MarketAxess has 454 employees<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ-3">[3]</a></sup> and serves clients in the Americas, Europe, Latin America and Asia.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg-1">[1]</a></sup> Today, it is the market leader in the electronic trading of US <a href="/wiki/Corporate_bond" title="Corporate bond">corporate bonds</a>, with 85% of investment-grade and 84% of high-yield debt traded electronically on its platform.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg_markets_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg_markets-4">[4]</a></sup> That equates to 20% of all corporate bond trading volume in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-marketsmedia_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-marketsmedia-5">[5]</a></sup>  As of April 2018, the company is valued at $8.5bn.<sup id="cite_ref-fnprofile_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fnprofile-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=MarketAxess&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>McVey originally proposed the model for MarketAxess in 1999 as part of <a href="/wiki/J.P._Morgan_%26_Co." title="J.P. Morgan &amp; Co.">J.P. Morgan</a>'s <i>Lab Morgan</i> program, which was designed to back executives’ ideas for using web-based technology. He launched the company as an independent venture in 2000, with $24 million in capital which he’d raised from other market participants including <a href="/wiki/Bear_Stearns" title="Bear Stearns">Bear Stearns</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fortune_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fortune-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In November that year, MarketAxess began trading investment-grade corporate bonds and gave investors access to new issues and research.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ2_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ2-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 2001, MarketAxess acquired Trading Edge Inc., which owned BondLink, a start-up bond company that had enabled investors to buy and sell bonds online.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ2_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ2-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>The company went public in 2004, with a stock value of $11 a share.<sup id="cite_ref-fortune_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fortune-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012, MarketAxess announced a partnership to link up with <a href="/wiki/BlackRock" title="BlackRock">BlackRock</a>’s <a href="/wiki/Aladdin_(BlackRock)" title="Aladdin (BlackRock)">Aladdin platform</a>, which hosts more than $10 trillion of client assets, as part of its plans to develop an ‘all-to-all’ electronic marketplace.<sup id="cite_ref-fnnews_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fnnews-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>That year it announced the launch of its all-to-all trading marketplace, Open Trading.<sup id="cite_ref-reuters_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 2013, MarketAxess acquired London-based Trax, a provider of market data and post-trade services, including trade matching and regulatory reporting.<sup id="cite_ref-fnnews2_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fnnews2-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, MarketAxess and BlackRock extended their Open Trading partnership, already in place in the US, to the European credit markets. The alliance, which benefits BlackRock clients using its Aladdin platform, aimed to improve liquidity and reduce transaction costs for European market participants.<sup id="cite_ref-waterstech_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-waterstech-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>That year MarketAxess’s Open Trading platform had a record fourth quarter, with approximately 125,000 total transactions and an average daily traded volume of $831 million, up 86% from the previous year.<sup id="cite_ref-reuters2_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuters2-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In the summer of 2017, MarketAxess announced it was establishing an office in Amsterdam.<sup id="cite_ref-waterstechnology_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-waterstechnology-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, MarketAxess reported that January was a record month for electronic daily and monthly trading volumes on its platform, which it attributed in part to new regulatory reporting rules required by <a href="/wiki/Markets_in_Financial_Instruments_Directive_2004#MiFID_II/MiFIR" title="Markets in Financial Instruments Directive 2004">MiFID II</a>, a regulation which came into force on 3 January.<sup id="cite_ref-fidesk_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fidesk-2">[2]</a></sup></p><p>In March the company announced the extension of its all-to-all Open Trading partnership with BlackRock to Asia, focused mainly on US dollar-denominated corporate and sovereign bonds and other securities traded by market participants in the region.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ3_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ3-15">[15]</a></sup> In 2019, MarketAxess Holdings  was announced the second best <a href="/wiki/Stock" title="Stock">stock</a> in the 2010s, with a total return of 3,015%.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2019, the company announced it would enter Treasury trading with the acquisition of the LiquidityEdge platform, a minor player in Treasuries, for $150 million.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
