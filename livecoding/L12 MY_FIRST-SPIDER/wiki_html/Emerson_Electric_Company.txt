</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emerson_Electric&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Emerson was established in 1890 in <a href="/wiki/St._Louis" title="St. Louis">St. Louis, Missouri</a> as <i>Emerson Electric Manufacturing Co.</i> by <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">Civil War</a> Union veteran <a href="/wiki/John_Wesley_Emerson" title="John Wesley Emerson">John Wesley Emerson</a> to manufacture <a href="/wiki/Electric_motor" title="Electric motor">electric motors</a> using a <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patent</a> owned by the Scottish-born brothers Charles and Alexander Meston. In 1892, it became the first to sell <a href="/wiki/Electric_fan" class="mw-redirect" title="Electric fan">electric fans</a> in the United States. It quickly expanded its product line to include electric <a href="/wiki/Sewing_machine" title="Sewing machine">sewing machines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dental_drill" title="Dental drill">electric dental drills</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Power_tool" title="Power tool">power tools</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Emerson_Electric_News_Flashes_-_NARA_-_534557.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Emerson_Electric_News_Flashes_-_NARA_-_534557.jpg/220px-Emerson_Electric_News_Flashes_-_NARA_-_534557.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="278" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="2378" data-file-height="3000"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 278px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Emerson_Electric_News_Flashes_-_NARA_-_534557.jpg/220px-Emerson_Electric_News_Flashes_-_NARA_-_534557.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="278" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Emerson_Electric_News_Flashes_-_NARA_-_534557.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Emerson Electric News Flashes, WWII</div></div></div>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, under the leadership of <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Symington" title="Stuart Symington">Stuart Symington</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Emerson became the world's largest manufacturer of airplane armament. Emerson ranked 52nd among United States corporations in the value of World War II military production contracts.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Symington went on to become the first <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_the_Air_Force" title="United States Secretary of the Air Force">United States Secretary of the Air Force</a> from 1947-1950, a <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic</a> <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">U.S. Senator</a> from Missouri from 1953-1976, and a candidate for the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">Presidency of the United States</a> <a href="/wiki/1960_United_States_presidential_election" title="1960 United States presidential election">in 1960</a>.
</p><p>In 1954, W.R. "Buck" Persons was named company president. Under his leadership, Emerson diversified its business portfolio by acquiring 36 companies. When he retired in 1973, the company had 82 plants, 31,000 employees and $800 million in sales.
</p><p>In 1962, acquired United States Electrical Manufacturing Company as the U.S. Electrical Motors Division, including the brand U.S. MOTORS®.<sup id="cite_ref-USMotorsHistory_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USMotorsHistory-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 1968, acquired <a href="/wiki/InSinkErator" title="InSinkErator">InSinkErator</a> company.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Charles_F._Knight" title="Charles F. Knight">Charles F. Knight</a> served as CEO from 1973 to 2000, and was chairman from 1974 to 2004. His tenure was marked by development of a rigorous planning process, new product and technology development, acquisitions and joint ventures, and international growth.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/David_Farr" title="David Farr">David Farr</a> has served as CEO since 2000 and as chairman since 2004.
</p><p>On December 15, 1999, Emerson Electric Co, moving to boost its growth prospects, agreed to acquire Jordan Industries Inc's telecommunications equipment business for $440 million.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In 2010, U.S. MOTORS® brand sold to <a href="/wiki/Nidec_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Nidec Corporation">Nidec Motor Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-USMotorsHistory_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USMotorsHistory-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>On July 26, 2011, Emerson announced it would locate its Latin America headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Sunrise,_Florida" title="Sunrise, Florida">Sunrise, Florida</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>On December 1, 2016, Platinum Equity acquired Emerson Network Power business unit and rebranded it Vertiv. The acquisition included the brands: Asco, Chloride, Liebert, Netsure, and Trellis.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2018, Emerson completed the purchase of Textron Tools and Test Businesses for $810 million (Including <a href="/wiki/Greenlee" title="Greenlee">Greenlee</a>, Klauke, HD Electric and Sherman + Reilly).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_governance">Corporate governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emerson_Electric&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Corporate governance" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>The company's leaders since the mid-20th century have been, respectively, W. R. Persons (1954-1973; President), <a href="/wiki/Charles_F._Knight" title="Charles F. Knight">Charles Knight</a> (1973-2000, <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">CEO</a>), and <a href="/wiki/David_Farr" title="David Farr">David Farr</a> (2000 forward, CEO).
</p><p>The company's chairmen of the board have been, respectively, Charles Knight (1974-2004) and David Farr (2004 forward).
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_platforms">Business platforms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emerson_Electric&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Business platforms" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>Emerson is structured into 2 business platforms:<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<ul><li>Automation Solutions</li>
<li>Commercial &amp; Residential Solutions</li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_records">Environmental records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emerson_Electric&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Environmental records" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>Researchers at the University of Massachusetts (UMass) Amherst have identified Emerson as the 97th largest corporate producer of <a href="/wiki/Air_pollution_in_the_United_States" title="Air pollution in the United States">air pollution in the United States</a>, down from its previous rank of 56th. Major pollutants indicated by the study include nickel compounds, manganese, diisocyanate, and lead.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="NBC_Heroes_lawsuit">NBC <i>Heroes</i> lawsuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emerson_Electric&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: NBC Heroes lawsuit" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On October 2, 2006, Emerson filed suit in federal court against <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> regarding a scene that appeared in the pilot episode of the network's TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Heroes_(U.S._TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Heroes (U.S. TV series)">Heroes</a></i>. The scene depicted <a href="/wiki/Claire_Bennet" title="Claire Bennet">Claire Bennet</a> reaching into an active garbage disposal, severely injuring her hand. Emerson's suit claims the scene "casts the disposer in an unsavory light, irreparably tarnishing the product" by suggesting that serious injuries will result "in the event consumers were to accidentally insert their hand into one."
</p><p>Emerson asked for a ruling barring future broadcasts of the pilot and to block NBC from using any Emerson trademarks in the future.
</p><p>On February 23, 2007, the case was dropped. NBC Universal and Emerson Electric settled the lawsuit outside of court.<sup id="cite_ref-MediaDailyNews_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MediaDailyNews-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
