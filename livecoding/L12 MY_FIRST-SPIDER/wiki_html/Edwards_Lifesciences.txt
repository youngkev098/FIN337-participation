</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edwards_Lifesciences&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Edwards was originally founded by engineer Miles “Lowell” Edwards in 1958.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Edwards and Dr. Albert Starr, a surgeon at the University of Oregon Medical School, designed, developed, tested and successfully placed in a patient the first Starr-Edwards mitral valve in 1960.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> As a result of the successful heart surgery, Edwards Laboratories was founded in Santa Ana, California that same year.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 1966, Edwards Laboratories was purchased by American Hospital Supply Corporation and became American Edwards Laboratories, which was later acquired by <a href="/wiki/Baxter_International" title="Baxter International">Baxter International</a> in 1985.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On April 3, 2000, Edwards was spun off from Baxter as an independent, publicly held corporation and began trading on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> under the symbol “EW”. Edwards now has a presence in approximately 100 countries with 13,000 employees around the world.<sup id="cite_ref-irvine_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-irvine-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>On January 25, 2017, Edwards completed the acquisition of Valtech Cardio for $340 million. The deal had been first announced the previous November.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>On December 6, 2017, Edwards acquired Harpoon Medical of <a href="/wiki/Baltimore" title="Baltimore">Baltimore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a> for $100 million. Harpoon, founded in 2013, developed a <a href="/wiki/Minimally_invasive_heart_surgery" class="mw-redirect" title="Minimally invasive heart surgery">minimally invasive heart surgery</a> product for <a href="/wiki/Mitral_valve_repair" title="Mitral valve repair">mitral valve repair</a> <sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> to treat degenerative <a href="/wiki/Mitral_regurgitation" class="mw-redirect" title="Mitral regurgitation">mitral regurgitation</a>. At the time of the acquisition, the product was not available on any market.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>On April 18, 2019, Edwards completed the acquisition of CAS Medical Systems of <a href="/wiki/Branford,_Connecticut" title="Branford, Connecticut">Branford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a> for ~$100 million.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>Edwards SAPIEN 3 and SAPIEN 3 Ultra Transcatheter Heart Valve systems were <a href="/wiki/FDA" class="mw-redirect" title="FDA">FDA</a>-approved on August 16, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> These products are used to treat patients with severe <a href="/wiki/Aortic_stenosis" title="Aortic stenosis">aortic stenosis</a> without utilizing <a href="/wiki/Cardiac_surgery#Open-heart_surgery" title="Cardiac surgery">open-heart surgery</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_Segments">Business Segments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edwards_Lifesciences&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Business Segments" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Edwards Lifesciences designs, develops, manufactures, and markets products to treat cardiovascular disease.<sup id="cite_ref-forbes15_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes15-18">[18]</a></sup> Its products are categorized into three areas: Surgical Heart Valve Therapy, Transcatheter Aortic Valve Replacement, and Critical Care.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Edwards serves customers worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Surgical_Heart_Valve_Therapy">Surgical Heart Valve Therapy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edwards_Lifesciences&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Surgical Heart Valve Therapy" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The Surgical Heart Valve Therapy business segment includes tissue heart valves and heart valve repair products for the surgical replacement or repair of a patient's heart valve. The portfolio also includes a diverse line of cardiac surgery systems used during minimally invasive surgical procedures, as well as cannulae, embolic protection devices and other products used during cardiopulmonary bypass.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Transcatheter_Aortic_Valve_Replacement_.28TAVR.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Transcatheter_Aortic_Valve_Replacement_(TAVR)">Transcatheter Aortic Valve Replacement (TAVR)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edwards_Lifesciences&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Transcatheter Aortic Valve Replacement (TAVR)" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The Transcatheter Aortic Valve Replacement business segment includes technologies designed to treat heart valve disease using catheter-based approaches as opposed to open surgical techniques.<sup id="cite_ref-forbes15_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes15-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_Care">Critical Care</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edwards_Lifesciences&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Critical Care" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The Critical Care business segment includes pulmonary artery catheters, disposable pressure transducers and advanced hemodynamic monitoring systems. The portfolio also includes a line of balloon catheter-based vascular products, surgical clips and inserts.<sup id="cite_ref-forbes15_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes15-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
