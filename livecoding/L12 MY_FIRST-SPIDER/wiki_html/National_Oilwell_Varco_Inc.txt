</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_affairs">Corporate affairs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Oilwell_Varco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Corporate affairs" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The headquarters are in <a href="/wiki/Southwest_Management_District" title="Southwest Management District">Southwest Management District</a> (formerly Greater Sharpstown), Houston.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Divisions">Divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Oilwell_Varco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Divisions" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Rig Technologies engineers and manufactures drilling rigs, advanced drilling equipment packages, and the related capital equipment (including <a href="/wiki/Top_drive" title="Top drive">top drives</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iron_roughnecks" class="mw-redirect" title="Iron roughnecks">iron roughnecks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drawworks" class="mw-redirect" title="Drawworks">drawworks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blowout_preventer" title="Blowout preventer">blowout preventers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mud_pump" title="Mud pump">mud pumps</a>, risers, pipe handling, power and control systems) necessary to drill oil and gas wells, as well as marine construction equipment such as heavy-lift cranes, mooring machinery, jacking systems, pipelay and cablelay systems, and <a href="/wiki/Marine_riser_tensioner" title="Marine riser tensioner">marine riser tensioners</a>. In addition, the segment provides aftermarket equipment and services such as spare parts, repair, and equipment rentals as well as remote equipment monitoring, technical support, field service, and customer training.
</p><p>Wellbore Technologies develops the equipment, technologies, and services necessary to maximize the efficiency and economics of oil and gas wells. The segment provides the oil and gas industry with <a href="/wiki/Drill_bit_(well)" title="Drill bit (well)">drill bits</a>, borehole enlargement services, and <a href="/wiki/Directional_drilling" title="Directional drilling">directional drilling</a> tools and optimization/automation software and services; downhole tools for drilling and intervention; premium <a href="/wiki/Drill_pipe" title="Drill pipe">drill pipe</a> and drill stem accessories; solids control and waste management equipment and services; drilling and completion <a href="/wiki/Drilling_fluid" title="Drilling fluid">fluids</a>; data acquisition and analytics technologies; managed-pressure-drilling systems; coating and inspection services and <a href="/wiki/Radio-frequency_identification" title="Radio-frequency identification">RFID</a> technology for drill pipe lifecycle management; and <a href="/wiki/IntelliServ" title="IntelliServ">IntelliServ</a> wired drill pipe.
</p><p>Completion &amp; Production Solutions provides the equipment and technologies necessary to optimize the well completion process and production phase of a well's lifecycle. The segment produces a wide variety of intervention and stimulation equipment for pressure pumping, <a href="/wiki/Coiled_tubing_drilling" title="Coiled tubing drilling">coiled tubing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wireline_(cabling)" title="Wireline (cabling)">wireline</a> operations; composite piping systems, pressure vessels, and structures; integrated processing, production, and pumping equipment (including <a href="/wiki/Artificial_lift" title="Artificial lift">artificial lift</a>) for <a href="/wiki/Upstream_(petroleum_industry)" title="Upstream (petroleum industry)">upstream</a>, <a href="/wiki/Midstream" title="Midstream">midstream</a>, and industrial operations; hydrate inhibition and gas processing technologies; subsea and floating production systems, including flexible pipe and subsea water injection technologies; integral and weld-on connectors for conductor strings, surface casing, and liners; and completion tools—including those involved in multistage <a href="/wiki/Hydraulic_fracturing" title="Hydraulic fracturing">hydraulic fracturing</a>—liner hanger systems, and <a href="/wiki/Downhole_safety_valve" title="Downhole safety valve">subsurface safety valves</a>.
</p><p>In late 2019 NOV bought Ershigs, a fabricator of custom pipes and tanks in Bellingham, WA, USA.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_Accolades">Awards and Accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Oilwell_Varco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards and Accolades" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>In 2007, the <i><a href="/wiki/Houston_Chronicle" title="Houston Chronicle">Houston Chronicle</a></i> ranked NOV the fifteenth best company to work for in Houston. In 2008, the newspaper ranked NOV as the best company in the city.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In the same year, NOV was listed as one of the world's most admired companies by <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> and one of the 100 fastest growing companies by <a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)"><i>Fortune</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In 2009, NOV was again listed as one of the 100 fastest growing companies by <i>Fortune</i> and one of America's top companies by <i>Forbes.</i> In 2015, NOV was listed by the <i>Houston Chronicle</i> as one of Houston's 100 leading companies.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Oilwell_Varco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>National Oilwell's two main predecessors, Oilwell Supply and National Supply, were founded in 1862 and 1893, respectively. These two companies manufactured and distributed pumps and derricks.  In 1930, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Steel" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Steel">United States Steel</a> acquired Oilwell Supply. In 1958, <a href="/wiki/Armco_Steel" class="mw-redirect" title="Armco Steel">Armco Steel</a> merged with National Supply. In 1987, National Supply merged with USS Oilwell to become "National Oilwell".
</p><p>Varco was founded as Abegg and Reinhold Company by Walter Abegg and Baldwin Reinhold in 1908. "VARCO" is an acronym for: <b>V</b>uilleumiere, <b>A</b>begg and <b>R</b>einhold <b>Co</b>mpany after Edgar Vuilleumiere became a partner in 1915, and Varco International in 1973. In 2000, National Oilwell merged with IRI International Corp., founded and managed by <a href="/wiki/Hushang_Ansary" title="Hushang Ansary">Hushang Ansary</a>, which manufactures and sells drilling rigs and specialty steel products.<sup id="cite_ref-BloombergIRI_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BloombergIRI-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In 2005, National Oilwell and Varco merged to become <b>National Oilwell Varco</b>. In 2014, Clay Williams took over as CEO, succeeding former CEO <a href="/wiki/Merrill_A._Miller,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Merrill A. Miller, Jr.">Pete Miller</a>, who served as NOV's CEO for nearly 13 years.<sup id="cite_ref-National_Oilwell_Varco_Moves_Forward_With_CEO_Transition_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-National_Oilwell_Varco_Moves_Forward_With_CEO_Transition-14">[14]</a></sup> Miller will become Executive Chairman of the National Oilwell Varco spinoff company, DistributionNow. The company employs nearly 64,000 people and has over 1,200 facilities across six continents worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-NOV_Annual_Report_2013_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NOV_Annual_Report_2013-15">[15]</a></sup> In February 2012 NOV acquired a Russian coring company Interval Ltd.
</p>
