It offers health care products and insurance services. It is the largest healthcare company in the world by revenue, with 2019 revenue of $242.2 billion. </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Organizational_structure">Organizational structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UnitedHealth_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Organizational structure" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Optum">Optum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UnitedHealth_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Optum" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Optum" title="Optum">Optum</a></div>
<p>The Optum brand was created in 2011 as the company's health services business.<sup id="cite_ref-uhg_makes_optum_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uhg_makes_optum-4">[4]</a></sup> Optum is UnitedHealth's technology-focused arm.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="UnitedHealthcare">UnitedHealthcare</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UnitedHealth_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: UnitedHealthcare" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>UnitedHealthcare includes four divisions:
</p>
<ul><li>UnitedHealthcare Employer and Individual - provides health benefit plans and services for large national employers</li>
<li>UnitedHealthcare Medicare and Retirement - provides health and well-being services to individuals age 65 and older.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>UnitedHealthcare Community and State - serves state programs that care for the economically disadvantaged, the medically underserved and people without the benefit of employer-funded health care coverage, in exchange for a monthly premium per member from the state program.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>UnitedHealthcare Global - serves 6.2 million people with medical benefits, residing principally in Brazil, Chile, Colombia and Peru but also in more than 130 other countries.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_insurance_plans">Health insurance plans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UnitedHealth_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Health insurance plans" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>UnitedHealthcare offers commercial <a href="/wiki/Group_insurance" title="Group insurance">group insurance</a> plans across the United States under several product names with different offerings.<sup id="cite_ref-AdministrativeGuide_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AdministrativeGuide-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<ul><li><b>UnitedHealthcare Select</b> is <a href="/wiki/Exclusive_provider_organization" title="Exclusive provider organization">exclusive provider organization</a> (EPO) with no coverage for out-of-network providers<sup id="cite_ref-Terms_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Terms-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>UnitedHealthcare Select Plus</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Preferred_provider_organization" title="Preferred provider organization">preferred provider organization</a> (PPO).<sup id="cite_ref-Terms_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Terms-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>UnitedHealthcare Choice</b> functions as a HMO plan with access to specialists,<sup id="cite_ref-Terms_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Terms-7">[7]</a></sup> whereas UnitedHealthcare Choice Plus is an HMO which allows for out-of-network coverage.<sup id="cite_ref-Terms_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Terms-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>UnitedHealthcare Navigate, Charter, and Compass</b> require a <a href="/wiki/Primary_care_physician" title="Primary care physician">primary care physician</a> referral to see a specialist<sup id="cite_ref-AdministrativeGuide_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AdministrativeGuide-6">[6]</a></sup> meaning that they are more restrictive <a href="/wiki/Managed_care" title="Managed care">managed care</a> plans, similar to <a href="/wiki/Point_of_service_plan" title="Point of service plan">point of service plans</a>.</li></ul><h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Provider_networks">Provider networks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UnitedHealth_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Provider networks" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>UnitedHealthcare negotiates with providers in periodic contract negotiations; contracts may be discontinued from time to time.<sup id="cite_ref-AdministrativeGuide_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AdministrativeGuide-6">[6]</a></sup> High-profile contract disputes can span provider networks across the nation, as in the case of a 2018 dispute with a major emergency room doctor group, <a href="/wiki/Envision_Healthcare" title="Envision Healthcare">Envision Healthcare</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Provider_directory">Provider directory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UnitedHealth_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Provider directory" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Maintaining up-to-date provider directories is necessary since the <a href="/wiki/Centers_for_Medicare_and_Medicaid_Services" class="mw-redirect" title="Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services">Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services</a> can fine insurers with outdated directories.<sup id="cite_ref-blockchain_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blockchain-9">[9]</a></sup> As a condition of participation, UnitedHealthcare requires that providers notify them of changes, but also has a Professional Verification Outreach program to proactively request information from providers.<sup id="cite_ref-AdministrativeGuide_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AdministrativeGuide-6">[6]</a></sup> However, providers are burdened by having to maintain their information with multiple networks (e.g., competitors to UnitedHealthcare). The total cost of maintaining these directories is estimated at $2.1b annually, and a <a href="/wiki/Blockchain" title="Blockchain">blockchain</a> initiative began in 2018 to share the directory.<sup id="cite_ref-blockchain_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blockchain-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UnitedHealth_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: History" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>In 1974, Richard Taylor Burke founded Charter Med Incorporated, a Minnetonka, Minnesota-based privately held company.
</p><p>In 1977, the United HealthCare Corporation was created to reorganize the company and become the parent company of Charter Med.<sup id="cite_ref-uhgHist_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uhgHist-10">[10]</a></sup> United HealthCare’s charter was to manage the newly created Physicians Health Plan of Minnesota, an early health management organization.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In 1988, United HealthCare started its first <a href="/wiki/Pharmacy_benefit_manager" class="mw-redirect" title="Pharmacy benefit manager">pharmacy benefit management</a>, through its <a href="/wiki/Diversified_Pharmaceutical_Services" title="Diversified Pharmaceutical Services">Diversified Pharmaceutical Services</a> subsidiary. It managed pharmacy benefits delivered both through retail pharmacies and mail.<sup id="cite_ref-uhgHist_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uhgHist-10">[10]</a></sup> The subsidiary was sold to <a href="/wiki/SmithKline_Beecham" class="mw-redirect" title="SmithKline Beecham">SmithKline Beecham</a> in 1994 for $2.3 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-phil1994_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-phil1994-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 1994, United HealthCare acquired Ramsey-HMO, a Florida insurer.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In 1995, the company acquired The MetraHealth Companies Inc. for $1.65 billion. MetraHealth was a privately held company formed by combining the group health care operations of <a href="/wiki/The_Travelers_Companies" title="The Travelers Companies">The Travelers Companies</a> and <a href="/wiki/MetLife" title="MetLife">MetLife</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-uhgHist_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uhgHist-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 1996, United HealthCare acquired HealthWise of America, which operated HMOs in Arkansas, Maryland, Kentucky and Tennessee.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In 1998, the company was reorganized as the <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding</a> of independent companies UnitedHealthcare, Ovations, Uniprise, Specialized Care Services and Ingenix, and rebranded as "UnitedHealth Group".<sup id="cite_ref-uhgHist_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uhgHist-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>Also in 1998, United Health Group acquired HealthPartners of Arizona, operator of Arizona's largest <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Health_Care_Cost_Containment_System" title="Arizona Health Care Cost Containment System">AHCCCS</a> provider.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In 2001, EverCare, a UnitedHealth Group subsidiary merged with LifeMark Health Plans<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In 2002, UnitedHealth Group acquired <a href="/wiki/GeoAccess" title="GeoAccess">GeoAccess</a> and Medicaid insurance company AmeriChoice.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In 2003, UnitedHealth Group acquired Mid Atlantic Medical Services, an insurer serving Maryland, Washington D.C., Virginia, Delaware and West Virginia.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Also in 2003, UnitedHealth Group acquired Golden Rule Financial, a provider of health savings accounts.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>On July 21, 2003, Exante Bank started operating in Salt Lake City, Utah, as a Utah state-chartered industrial loan corporation. It changed its name to OptumHealth Bank in 2008<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and to Optum Bank in 2012.
</p><p>In April 2004, UnitedHealth Group acquired Touchpoint Health Plan, a Wisconsin health plan.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2004, the company acquired Oxford Health Plans.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2005, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/PacifiCare_Health_Systems" title="PacifiCare Health Systems">PacifiCare Health Systems</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> It agreed to divest parts of PacifiCare's commercial health insurance business in <a href="/wiki/Tucson,_Arizona" title="Tucson, Arizona">Tucson, Arizona</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boulder,_Colorado" title="Boulder, Colorado">Boulder, Colorado</a> to satisfy <a href="/wiki/Antitrust" class="mw-redirect" title="Antitrust">antitrust</a> regulator concerns, and also agreed to end its network access agreement with <a href="/wiki/Blue_Shield_of_California" title="Blue Shield of California">Blue Shield of California</a>. The Tucson business was sold to Cigna.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The company acquired Prescription Solutions, another pharmacy benefit manager, as part of its acquisition of <a href="/wiki/PacifiCare_Health_Systems" title="PacifiCare Health Systems">PacifiCare Health Systems</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> This business was later rebranded OptumRx.
</p><p>In February 2006, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/John_Deere" title="John Deere">John Deere</a> Health Care.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2008, the company acquired Sierra Health Services for $2.6 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> As part of the transaction, to obtain regulatory approval, 25,000 customers were sold to <a href="/wiki/Humana" title="Humana">Humana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2009, UnitedHealth Group agreed to acquire <a href="/wiki/Health_Net" title="Health Net">Health Net</a>'s Northeast licensed subsidiaries for up to $570 million in payments spread out over two years.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>Through 2010 and into 2011, senior executives of the company met monthly with executives of other health insurers to limit the effect of the health care reform law.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2010, Ingenix acquired Picis, a health information provider for the high-acuity areas of hospitals.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p><p>In 2011, Logistics Health, Inc. of <a href="/wiki/La_Crosse,_Wisconsin" title="La Crosse, Wisconsin">La Crosse, Wisconsin</a>, was acquired by href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2014, Optum secured a <a href="/wiki/Majority_stake" class="mw-redirect" title="Majority stake">majority stake</a> in the Washington, D.C.-based startup Audax Health.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-5">[5]</a></sup> Audax's CEO, Grant Verstandig, continued running the firm alongside <a href="/wiki/Chief_operating_officer" title="Chief operating <a href="/wiki/Optum" title="Optum">Optum</a> division acquired Davita Medical Group from <a href="/wiki/DaVita_Inc." title="DaVita Inc.">DaVita Inc.</a> for $4.3 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-51" <p>In 2010, UnitedHealth Group spent more than $1.8 million on lobbying activities to work to achieve favorable legislation, and hired seven different lobbying firms to work on its behalf.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">[72]</a></sup> In addition, its corporate political action committee or <a href="/wiki/Political_Action_Committee" class="mw-redirect" title="Political Action Committee">PAC</a>, called United for Health, spent an additional $1 million on lobbying activities in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">[73]</a></sup></p><p><a href="/wiki/QSSI" class="mw-redirect" title="QSSI">QSSI</a>, a subsidiary of UnitedHealth Group, is one of the 55 contractors hired by <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Health_and_Human_Services" title="United States Department of Health and Human Services">United States Department of Health and Human Services</a> to work on the <a href="/wiki/HealthCare.gov" title="HealthCare.gov">HealthCare.gov</a> web site.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">[74]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(6)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UnitedHealth_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Legal issues" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-6 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-6">
<p>In 2006, the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission">U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission</a> (SEC) began investigating the conduct of UnitedHealth Group's management and directors, for <a href="/wiki/Options_backdating" title="Options backdating">backdating of stock options</a>. Investigations were also begun by the <a href="/wiki/Internal_Revenue_Service" title="Internal Revenue Service">Internal Revenue Service</a> and prosecutors in the U.S. attorney's office for the Southern District of New York, who subpoenaed documents from the company. The investigations came to light after a series of probing stories in the <i><a href="/wiki/Wall_Street_Journal" class="mw-redirect" title="Wall Street Journal">Wall Street Journal</a></i> in May 2006, discussing apparent backdating of hundreds of millions of dollars' worth of <a href="/wiki/Stock_options" class="mw-redirect" title="Stock options">stock options</a> by UHC management. The backdating apparently occurred with the knowledge and approval of the directors, according to the <i>Journal</i>. Major shareholders have filed lawsuits accusing former <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> governor <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Kean" title="Thomas Kean">Thomas Kean</a> and UHC's other directors of failing in their fiduciary duty.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">[75]</a></sup> On October 15, 2006, CEO <a href="/wiki/William_W._McGuire" title="William W. McGuire">William W. McGuire</a> was forced to resign, and relinquish hundreds of millions of dollars in stock options. On December 6, 2007, the SEC announced a settlement under which McGuire will repay $468 million, as a partial settlement of the backdating prosecution. <sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">[76]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">[77]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">[78]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2006, the <a href="/wiki/American_Chiropractic_Association" title="American Chiropractic Association">American Chiropractic Association</a> filed a national class action lawsuit against the American Chiropractic Network (ACN), which is owned by UnitedHealth Group and administers chiropractic benefits, and against UnitedHealth Group itself, for alleged practices in violation of the federal <a href="/wiki/Racketeer_Influenced_and_Corrupt_Organizations_Act" title="Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act">Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act</a> (RICO).<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">[79]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="OptumInsight.2C_fka_Ingenix"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="OptumInsight,_fka_Ingenix">OptumInsight, fka Ingenix</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UnitedHealth_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: OptumInsight, fka Ingenix" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In February 2008, New York State Attorney General <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Cuomo" title="Andrew Cuomo">Andrew Cuomo</a> announced an industry-wide investigation into a scheme by health insurers to defraud consumers by manipulating reasonable and customary rates. The announcement included a statement that Cuomo intended "to file suit against Ingenix, Inc., its parent UnitedHealth Group, and three additional subsidiaries." Cuomo asserted that his investigation found that rates found in a database of health care charges maintained by Ingenix were lower than what he determined was the actual cost of certain medical expenses. Cuomo said this inappropriately allowed health insurance companies to deny a portion of provider claims, thereby pushing costs down to members.
<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">[80]</a></sup></p><p>On January 13, 2009, Ingenix announced an agreement with the New York State attorney settling the probe into the independence of the health pricing database. Under the settlement, UnitedHealth Group and Ingenix would pay $50 million to finance a new, non-profit entity that would develop a new health care pricing database. Ingenix would discontinue its medical pricing databases when the new entity makes its product available. The company acknowledged the appearance of a conflict of interest, but admitted no wrongdoing.<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">[81]</a></sup></p><p>On January 15, 2009, UnitedHealth Group announced a $350 million settlement of three class action lawsuits filed in Federal court by the <a href="/wiki/American_Medical_Association" title="American Medical Association">American Medical Association</a>, UnitedHealth Group members, healthcare providers, and state medical societies for not paying out-of-network benefits. This settlement came two days after a similar settlement with Cuomo.<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">[82]</a></sup></p><p>On October 27, 2009, Cuomo announced the creation of FAIR Health, the independent, non-profit organization that will develop a nationwide database for consumer reimbursement, as well as a website where consumers will be able to compare prices before they choose doctors. To fund FAIR Health, the Attorney General's office secured nearly $100 million from insurers such as <a href="/wiki/Aetna" title="Aetna">Aetna</a>, UnitedHealth Group, and <a href="/wiki/Anthem_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Anthem Inc.">Anthem Inc.</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">[83]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Options_backdating_investigations_and_lawsuits">Options backdating investigations and lawsuits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UnitedHealth_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Options backdating investigations and lawsuits" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2006, the SEC began investigating the conduct of UnitedHealth Group's management and directors, including Dr. McGuire, as did the <a href="/wiki/Internal_Revenue_Service" title="Internal Revenue Service">Internal Revenue Service</a> and prosecutors in the U.S. attorney's office for the Southern District of New York, who have subpoenaed documents from the company.
</p><p>The investigations came to light after a series of probing stories in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a></i> in March 2006, discussing the apparent backdating of hundreds of millions of dollars' worth of stock options—in a process called <a href="/wiki/Options_backdating" title="Options backdating">options backdating</a>—by UnitedHealth Group management.<sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">[84]</a></sup>
The backdating apparently occurred with the knowledge and approval of the directors, according to the <i>Journal</i>. Major shareholders have filed lawsuits accusing former <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> governor <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Kean" title="Thomas Kean">Thomas Kean</a> and UnitedHealth Group's other directors of failing in their fiduciary duty.<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">[85]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Resignation_of_McGuire">Resignation of McGuire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UnitedHealth_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Resignation of McGuire" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On October 15, 2006, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/William_W._McGuire" title="William W. McGuire">William W. McGuire</a> would step down immediately as chairman and director of UnitedHealth Group, and step down as CEO on December 1, 2006, due to his involvement in the employee stock options scandal.<sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">[86]</a></sup> Simultaneously, it was announced that he would be replaced as CEO by Stephen Hemsley, who has served as President and COO and is a member of the board of directors. McGuire's exit compensation from UnitedHealth, expected to be around $1.1 billion, would be the largest <a href="/wiki/Golden_parachute" title="Golden parachute">golden parachute</a> in the history of corporate America.<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">[87]</a></sup></p><p>McGuire's compensation became controversial again on May 21, 2009, when <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Edwards" title="Elizabeth Edwards">Elizabeth Edwards</a>, speaking on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Show" title="The Daily Show">The Daily Show</a></i>, used it to support her argument for a public alternative to commercial insurance.<sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89">[88]</a></sup> Edwards stressed the importance of restoring competition in health insurance markets noting that at one point, "the President of UnitedHealth made so much money, that one of every $700 that was spent in this country on health care went to pay him." Since estimates of McGuire's 2005 compensation range from $59,625,444<sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">[89]</a></sup> to $124.8 million,<sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91">[90]</a></sup> and the revenue of UnitedHealth Group was then $71 billion, it is therefore possible that Mrs. Edwards may have meant that one of every $700 that was spent on UnitedHealth Group premiums went to pay McGuire.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="McGuire.27s_settlement_with_SEC"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="McGuire's_settlement_with_SEC">McGuire's settlement with SEC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UnitedHealth_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: McGuire's settlement with SEC" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On December 6, 2007, the SEC announced a settlement under which McGuire was to repay $468 million, including a $7 million civil penalty, as a partial settlement of the backdating prosecution. He was also barred from serving as an officer or director of a public company for ten years.<sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92">[91]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-93" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-93">[92]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94">[93]</a></sup> This was the first time in which the little-used "clawback" provision under the <a href="/wiki/Sarbanes-Oxley_Act" class="mw-redirect" title="Sarbanes-Oxley Act">Sarbanes-Oxley Act</a> was used against an individual by the SEC. The SEC continued its investigations even after it in 2008 settled legal actions against both UnitedHealth Group itself and its former general counsel.<sup id="cite_ref-95" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-95">[94]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Medicare_overbilling_lawsuit">Medicare overbilling lawsuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UnitedHealth_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Medicare overbilling lawsuit" data-section="15" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>A whistleblower lawsuit, filed in 2011, charges UnitedHealth Group's data analytics division with assisting in defrauding <a href="/wiki/Medicare_(United_States)" title="Medicare (United States)">Medicare</a> by boosting risk adjustment scores from <a href="/wiki/Medicare_Advantage" title="Medicare Advantage">Medicare Advantage</a> companies. The suit alleges that UnitedHealth Group subsidiary Ingenix (now OptumInsight) "defrauded the United States of hundreds of millions — and likely billions — of dollars." Former UnitedHealth executive Benjamin Poehling brought the suit under the <a href="/wiki/False_Claims_Act" title="False Claims Act">False Claims Act</a>. The government said it would proceed on claims against two health care companies, UnitedHealth and its Texas subsidiary WellMed Medical Management. In February 2017, a federal judge unsealed the suit after the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Justice" title="United States Department of Justice">Department of Justice</a> announced it would join the case.<sup id="cite_ref-96" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-96">[95]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97">[96]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Richard_Cole.2C_and_others_v._United_Healthcare"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Richard_Cole,_and_others_v._United_Healthcare"><i>Richard Cole, and others v. United Healthcare</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UnitedHealth_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Richard Cole, and others v. United Healthcare" data-section="16" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On April 29, 2019, Judge <a href="/wiki/Robert_N._Scola_Jr." title="Robert N. Scola Jr.">Robert N. Scola Jr.</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_District_Court_for_the_Southern_District_of_Florida" title="United States District Court for the Southern District of Florida">United States District Court for the Southern District of Florida</a>, a cancer survivor, recused himself from a case against United Healthcare, stating that the company's denial of treatment was "immoral and barbaric", and that his opinions regarding the company would prevent him from "deciding this case fairly and impartially."<sup id="cite_ref-98" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-98">[97]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-99" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99">[98]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="PacifiCare_fine_in_California">PacifiCare fine in California</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UnitedHealth_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: PacifiCare fine in California" data-section="17" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2008, the <a href="/wiki/California_Department_of_Insurance" title="California Department of Insurance">California Department of Insurance</a> took action against UnitedHealthcare's subsidiary <a href="/wiki/PacifiCare_Health_Systems" title="PacifiCare Health Systems">PacifiCare Health Systems</a>, acquired in 2005, ultimately fining UnitedHealthcare around $173 million for an estimated over 900,000 violations of the Unfair Insurance Practices Act; by 2019, the case was still being disputed in court, with the possibility of affirming $91 million in penalties.<sup id="cite_ref-100" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-100">[99]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="CMS_fine_over_Medicare_Part_D">CMS fine over Medicare Part D</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UnitedHealth_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: CMS fine over Medicare Part D" data-section="18" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2017, <a href="/wiki/Centers_for_Medicare_and_Medicaid_Services" class="mw-redirect" title="Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services">CMS</a> fined UnitedHealthcare after discovering issues in <a href="/wiki/Medicare_Part_D" title="Medicare Part D">Medicare Part D</a> leading to delays or denials in a 2016 audit.<sup id="cite_ref-101" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-101">[100]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Jersey_fine_in_2018">New Jersey fine in 2018</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UnitedHealth_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: New Jersey fine in 2018" data-section="19" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey_Department_of_Banking_and_Insurance" title="New Jersey Department of Banking and Insurance">New Jersey Department of Banking and Insurance</a> fined UnitedHealthcare $2.5 million due to various compliance issues; this was the largest fine against a licensee in nine years.<sup id="cite_ref-102" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-102">[101]</a></sup></p>

(February 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(8)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Policy_Research_Org._ownership">Policy Research Org. ownership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UnitedHealth_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Policy Research Org. ownership" data-section="24" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-8 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-8">
<p>The Lewin Group is a policy research and consulting firm that provides economic analysis of health care and human services issues and policies.<sup id="cite_ref-Becker_105-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Becker-105">[104]</a></sup>
The organization has existed for about 40 years and has maintained a nonpartisan reputation through its many ownership changes that have occurred over that time.<sup id="cite_ref-Becker_105-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Becker-105">[104]</a></sup>
The Lewin Group was purchased in 2007 by Ingenix, a subsidiary of UnitedHealth Group, but alleges editorial and analytical "independence" from UnitedHealth Group, its parent company.<sup id="cite_ref-Becker_105-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Becker-105">[104]</a></sup>
The Lewin Group discloses its ownership in its reports and on its web site. While the Lewin Group does not advocate for or against any legislation, both <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican</a> politicians frequently cite the firm's studies to argue for and against various <a href="/wiki/U.S._healthcare_reform" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. healthcare reform">U.S. healthcare reform</a> proposals.<sup id="cite_ref-106" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-106">[105]</a></sup> For example, Democratic Senator <a href="/wiki/Ron_Wyden" title="Ron Wyden">Ron Wyden</a> uses Lewin Group estimates to cite the feasibility of his <a href="/wiki/Healthy_Americans_Act" title="Healthy Americans Act">Healthy Americans Act</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-107" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-107">[106]</a></sup> Former U.S. Representative <a href="/wiki/Eric_Cantor" title="Eric Cantor">Eric Cantor</a>, the former House Republican <a href="/wiki/Party_whips_of_the_United_States_House_of_Representatives" class="mw-redirect" title="Party whips of the United States House of Representatives">Whip</a>, has referred to the organization as "the nonpartisan Lewin Group" in arguing against government-funded health insurance proposals.
</p><p>Several Lewin studies have been used to argue both for and against the inclusion of a <a href="/wiki/Public_health_insurance_option" title="Public health insurance option">public option</a> in national health reform. Lewin clients who often cite its findings include <a href="/wiki/The_Commonwealth_Fund" class="mw-redirect" title="The Commonwealth Fund">The Commonwealth Fund</a> which recently held up a <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20090830041528/http://www.commonwealthfund.org/Content/Publications/Fund-Reports/2009/Feb/The-Path-to-a-High-Performance-US-Health-System.aspx">Lewin study it commissioned</a> to advocate for a public plan. The report showed that legislative proposals would achieve nearly universal coverage and "estimated that a public plan could offer small businesses insurance that is at least 9 percent cheaper than current small-business policies."<sup id="cite_ref-108" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-108">[107]</a></sup></p><p>However, The Lewin Group has acknowledged that clients sometimes choose not to publicize the results of studies they have commissioned. Indeed, Lewin Group Vice President John Sheils told <i><a href="/wiki/The_Washington_Post" title="The Washington Post">The Washington Post</a></i> that "sometimes studies come out that don't show exactly what the client wants to see. And in those instances, they have [the] option to bury the study."<sup id="cite_ref-109" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-109">[108]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(9)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Foundations">Foundations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UnitedHealth_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Foundations" data-section="25" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-9 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-9">
<p>UnitedHealth Group has two foundations, the United Health Foundation and UnitedHealthcare Children’s Foundation which were formed in 1999. Since established by UnitedHealth Group in 1999 as a not for profit private foundation, the United Health Foundation has committed more than $170 million to improve health and health care.<sup id="cite_ref-110" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-110">[109]</a></sup></p>
