</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Invesco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Invesco.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Invesco.jpg/220px-Invesco.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1200"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Invesco.jpg/220px-Invesco.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Invesco.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Invesco headquarters in Atlanta, Georgia</div></div></div>
<p>Invesco (then officially spelled with all-capital letters: INVESCO) was created in Atlanta in 1978 when <a href="/wiki/Citizens_%26_Southern_National_Bank" title="Citizens &amp; Southern National Bank">Citizens &amp; Southern National Bank</a> divested its money management operations.<sup id="cite_ref-Karkaria_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Karkaria-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1988, the company was purchased by the British firm <a href="/wiki/Britannia_Arrow" class="mw-redirect" title="Britannia Arrow">Britannia Arrow</a>, based in London, which later took the INVESCO name.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 1997 INVESCO <a href="/wiki/Public_limited_company" title="Public limited company">PLC</a> merged with AIM Investments.<sup id="cite_ref-history_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-8">[8]</a></sup> Upon completion of the merger the company adopted the name Amvescap.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2007 the company reverted to the Invesco name.<sup id="cite_ref-Coleman_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coleman-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>Since 2000 Invesco has grown through acquisitions such as the <a href="/wiki/Exchange-traded_fund" title="Exchange-traded fund">ETF</a> firm PowerShares Capital Management and the <a href="/wiki/Restructuring" title="Restructuring">restructuring</a> of <a href="/wiki/WL_Ross_%26_Co" title="WL Ross &amp; Co">WL Ross &amp; Co</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-history_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 2004, the company agreed to a $450 million settlement with the <a href="/wiki/State_Attorney_General" class="mw-redirect" title="State Attorney General">Attorneys General</a> of New York, <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a> and the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission">SEC</a> after allegations of improper trading practices.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2007 the company changed their name to Invesco (initial cap only) <a href="/wiki/Public_limited_company" title="Public limited company">PLC</a>, moved its primary stock market listing from the <a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange" title="London Stock Exchange">London Stock Exchange</a> to the NYSE and became domiciled in <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>, adopting the name Invesco Ltd.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> As a result, the company's shares were withdrawn from the <a href="/wiki/FTSE_100_Index" title="FTSE 100 Index">FTSE 100 Index</a> and added to the <a href="/wiki/Russell_1000_Index" title="Russell 1000 Index">Russell 1000 Index</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In November 2007, Invesco moved its headquarters from London to Atlanta.<sup id="cite_ref-Karkaria_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Karkaria-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>Invesco planned to list the PowerShares Global Progressive Transportation Portfolio (PTRP on the <a href="/wiki/Nasdaq_Stock_Market" class="mw-redirect" title="Nasdaq Stock Market">Nasdaq Stock Market</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> This fund was closed in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>On October 19, 2009, Invesco bought <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Stanley" title="Morgan Stanley">Morgan Stanley</a>'s Retail Unit, including <a href="/wiki/Van_Kampen_Investments" title="Van Kampen Investments">Van Kampen Investments</a> for $1.5 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-Van_Kampen_Sale_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Van_Kampen_Sale-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2010, Invesco Aim became Invesco. Later that year, it was reported that Invesco had plans to introduce a <a href="/wiki/Risk_parity" title="Risk parity">Risk parity</a> commodity fund according to regulatory filings.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2013, Invesco reported assets under management (AUM) of $778.7 billion<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2017, Invesco agreed to buy Guggenheim Investment's exchange-traded fund business for $1.2 Billion in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>On October 18, 2018, Invesco purchased OppenheimerFunds from <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Mutual_Life_Insurance_Company" title="Massachusetts Mutual Life Insurance Company">Massachusetts Mutual Life Insurance Co.</a> for about $5.7 billion in stock. <sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2020 amid a major <a href="/wiki/Black_Monday_2020" class="mw-redirect" title="Black Monday 2020">stock market crash</a> triggered by the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19" class="mw-redirect" title="COVID-19">COVID-19</a> pandemic, Invesco Mortgage Capital Inc., a branch handling <a href="/wiki/Real_Estate_Investment_Trust" class="mw-redirect" title="Real Estate Investment Trust">Real Estate Investment Trusts</a>, announced they were unable to cover <a href="/wiki/Margin_(finance)#Margin_call" title="Margin (finance)">margin calls</a> due to dramatic devaluations and investors selling shares.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The company also announced they would delay <a href="/wiki/Dividend" title="Dividend">dividend</a> payments.
</p>
