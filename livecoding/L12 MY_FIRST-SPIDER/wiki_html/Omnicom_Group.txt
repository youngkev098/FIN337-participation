</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Omnicom_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>In 1986, Allen Rosenshine, Keith Reinhard and John Bernbach (son of <a href="/wiki/William_Bernbach" title="William Bernbach">William Bernbach</a>) co-created Omnicom in a three-way merger of <a href="/wiki/BBDO" title="BBDO">BBDO Worldwide</a>, <a href="/wiki/Doyle_Dane_Bernbach" class="mw-redirect" title="Doyle Dane Bernbach">Doyle Dane Bernbach</a> and Needham Harper Worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In 1989, Rosenshine stepped down as chairman of Omnicom to return to his role running BBDO Worldwide and Bruce Crawford (who had preceded Rosenshine as CEO of BBDO before leaving to run the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Opera" title="Metropolitan Opera">Metropolitan Opera</a> in 1985)
was named chairman of Omnicom.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 1997, John Wren, the number two executive at Omnicom, became the company's chief executive officer, while Crawford remained as chairman.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2013, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Publicis_Groupe" class="mw-redirect" title="Publicis Groupe">Publicis Groupe</a> and Omnicom Group would merge to form Publicis Omnicom Group, but by May 2014 it was announced that the deal had fallen through and the Publicis-Omnicom  merger would not happen.<sup id="cite_ref-20130728ChicagoTrib_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20130728ChicagoTrib-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20140508NewYorkTimes_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20140508NewYorkTimes-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>By 2014, Omnicom was the second largest agency holding company and had revenue of over $15 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Omnicom launched Omniwomen in April 2014 with the goal of  increasing the number and influence of female leaders within the organization. Omniwomen has more than 10 branches in the US, UK, Canada, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, Germany, China and the UAE.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2017, Omnicom announced that <a href="/wiki/Gracia_Martore" title="Gracia Martore">Gracia Martore</a>, former President and CEO of <a href="/wiki/Tegna,_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Tegna, Inc.">TEGNA Inc.</a>, had joined its Board as an independent director. Her appointment brings the total to 13 directors, 11 of which are independent.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In February 2018, Ronnie S. Hawkins was appointed to the company's board as an independent director, bringing the total to 14 members, 12 of which are independent.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In May 2018, Omnicom Group brought its language strategy agency, maslansky + partners, to <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Omnicom_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Operations" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Omnicom is composed of five major agency networks including Media One Marketing Group that oversee 1500+ agencies as parent companies. The networks are <a href="/wiki/BBDO" title="BBDO">BBDO Worldwide</a>, Diversified Agency Services (DAS), <a href="/wiki/DDB_Worldwide" title="DDB Worldwide">DDB Worldwide</a>, Omnicom Media Group (OMG) and <a href="/wiki/TBWA_Worldwide" title="TBWA Worldwide">TBWA Worldwide</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>BBDO Worldwide, one of the companies present from the initial merger, is a creative agency which has been recognized as one of the most creative networks in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Founded in 2000, <a href="/wiki/Proximity_Worldwide" title="Proximity Worldwide">Proximity Worldwide</a> is BBDO's digitally focused marketing arm. In January 2016, BBDO Worldwide acquired a majority stake in Wednesday Agency Group.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> At the 2017 Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity, BBDO Worldwide was awarded Network of the Year for the sixth time. Clemenger BBDO Melbourne was named Agency of the Year.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> BBDO was the top agency network featured in the 2018 WARC 100, an annual ranking of advertising and media effectiveness.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In 2018, several of the BBDO agencies were ranked in the top 10 awarded individual agencies in the world by The Gunn Report.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>The DAS Group of Companies comprises more than 200 companies focused on public relations, CRM, healthcare, events, promotional marketing, vehicle testing, branding and research.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Diversified_Agency_Services_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Diversified_Agency_Services-31">[31]</a></sup> In 2016 and 2017, Omnicom formed two separate holding groups for healthcare and PR agencies: Omnicom Health Group and Omnicom Precision Marketing Group. Later in 2016, Omnicom Health Group acquired majority stakes in Biopharm Communications and Rabin Martin.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In 2018, Omnicom Health Group acquired Snow Companies a patient engagement communications agency.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Also in 2018, Omnicom Precision Marketing Group acquired a majority stake in Credera.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>DDB Worldwide, one of the three initial companies, is a global advertising network.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-McClellan,_Steve_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McClellan,_Steve-36">[36]</a></sup> In November 2015, DDB Worldwide acquired Grupo ABC, the largest advertising group in Brazil.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> In June 2016, DDB Health was launched as a combination of healthcare agencies, which added medical and healthcare services to DDB's profile.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Some of the businesses included in the DDB Worldwide unit are <a href="/wiki/Tribal_DDB" class="mw-redirect" title="Tribal DDB">Tribal Worldwide</a>, TracyLocke, adam&amp;eveDDB, Roberts + Langer, Spike DDB, Rodgers Townsend, Alma and Uproar!@DDB.<sup id="cite_ref-McClellan,_Steve_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McClellan,_Steve-36">[36]</a></sup></p><p><br>
Omnicom Media Group is the media division of the Omnicom Group Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> In 2017, three Omnicom Media Group agency networks competed at the Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity, including OMD Worldwide, which was named Media Network of the Year.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p><p>TBWA is a global agency network, normally billed as 'The Disruption Company'.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> It was purchased in 1993 and then merged with the Chiat Day firm in 1995. In the United States, some of the company's offices still retain the TBWA Chiat Day name.<sup id="cite_ref-twba_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twba-43">[43]</a></sup> Companies operating under the TBWA network include, Media Arts Lab, Digital Arts Network (DAN), <a href="/wiki/The_Integer_Group" title="The Integer Group">The Integer Group</a>, Lucky Generals,<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Designory, NEBOKO, and other TBWA branded regional agencies.<sup id="cite_ref-twba_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twba-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
