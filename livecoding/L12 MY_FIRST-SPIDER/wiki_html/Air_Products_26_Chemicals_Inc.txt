<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">"Air Products" redirects here. For the former aircraft manufacturer, see <a href="/wiki/ERCO_Ercoupe" title="ERCO Ercoupe">ERCO Ercoupe</a>.</div>
<p><b>Air Products and Chemicals, Inc.</b> is an American international <a href="/wiki/Corporation" title="Corporation">corporation</a> whose principal business is selling <a href="/wiki/Industrial_gas" title="Industrial gas">gases</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chemical" class="mw-redirect" title="Chemical">chemicals</a> for industrial uses. Air Products' headquarters is in <a href="/wiki/Allentown,_Pennsylvania" title="Allentown, Pennsylvania">Allentown</a>, Pennsylvania, in the <a href="/wiki/Lehigh_Valley" title="Lehigh Valley">Lehigh Valley</a> region of <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.  Air Products is the Lehigh Valley's third largest employer, after <a href="/wiki/Lehigh_Valley_Hospital" title="Lehigh Valley Hospital">Lehigh Valley Hospital</a> and St. Luke's Hospital. Leonard P. Pool founded the company in 1940. Its French subsidiary, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Saint-Quentin-Fallavier" title="Saint-Quentin-Fallavier">Saint-Quentin-Fallavier</a>, was the site of a <a href="/wiki/2015_Saint-Quentin-Fallavier_attack" class="mw-redirect" title="2015 Saint-Quentin-Fallavier attack">terrorist attack</a> on June 26, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Air Product's current CEO and Chairman is Sefi Ghasemi.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Products">Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Air_Products_%26_Chemicals&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Products" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Air Products serves customers in technology, energy, healthcare, food and industrial markets worldwide with <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric" class="mw-redirect" title="Atmospheric">atmospheric</a> <a href="/wiki/Industrial_gases" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrial gases">industrial gases</a> (mainly <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nitrogen" title="Nitrogen">nitrogen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Argon" title="Argon">argon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">hydrogen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a>), process and specialty gases, performance materials and chemical intermediates.
</p><p>Air Products produces <a href="/wiki/Semiconductor" title="Semiconductor">semiconductor</a> materials, <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">refinery hydrogen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lng#Liquefaction_technology" class="mw-redirect" title="Lng">natural gas liquefaction (LNG) technology and equipment</a>, <a href="/wiki/Epoxy" title="Epoxy">epoxy</a> additives, <a href="/wiki/Gas_cabinet" title="Gas cabinet">gas cabinets</a>, advanced coatings and <a href="/wiki/Adhesives" class="mw-redirect" title="Adhesives">adhesives</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>  It is also noted<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> as addressing the <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">hydrogen</a> <a href="/wiki/Fuel_cell" title="Fuel cell">fuel cell</a> economy through <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">hydrogen</a> fueling stations as well as its <a href="/wiki/Personal_care" title="Personal care">personal care</a> chemicals business.
</p><p>Air Products also provided the liquid hydrogen and <a href="/wiki/Liquid_oxygen" title="Liquid oxygen">liquid oxygen</a> fuel for the <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_External_Tank" class="mw-redirect" title="Space Shuttle External Tank">Space Shuttle External Tank</a>. Air Products has had a working relationship with <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> for 50 years and has supplied the <a href="/wiki/Liquid_hydrogen" title="Liquid hydrogen">liquid hydrogen</a> used for every <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">Space Shuttle</a> launch and the <a href="/wiki/Project_Mercury" title="Project Mercury">Mercury</a> and <a href="/wiki/Apollo_program" title="Apollo program">Apollo</a> missions.
<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In 2013, Air Products' high purity BIP (built-in purifier) argon was used to determine a more accurate value for the <a href="/wiki/Boltzmann_Constant" class="mw-redirect" title="Boltzmann Constant">Boltzmann Constant</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_record">Environmental record</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Air_Products_%26_Chemicals&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Environmental record" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In 2010, Air Products was awarded the Rushlight Hydrogen and Fuel Cells industry award for the Series 10 hydrogen fuelling station. The Rushlight Awards celebrate the leading environmental technologies and innovations by organizations throughout the UK and Ireland. The purpose is to promote what is happening in the world of hydrogen and fuel cells to the public and enable further development and funding to be granted toward reducing carbon emissions.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Air_Products_van_on_Woodhouse_Lane_in_Leeds_(4th_May_2010).jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Air_Products_van_on_Woodhouse_Lane_in_Leeds_%284th_May_2010%29.jpg/220px-Air_Products_van_on_Woodhouse_Lane_in_Leeds_%284th_May_2010%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3872" data-file-height="2592"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 147px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Air_Products_van_on_Woodhouse_Lane_in_Leeds_%284th_May_2010%29.jpg/220px-Air_Products_van_on_Woodhouse_Lane_in_Leeds_%284th_May_2010%29.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="147" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Air_Products_van_on_Woodhouse_Lane_in_Leeds_(4th_May_2010).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An Air Products van in <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>On January 15, 2004, Air Products (NYSE: APD) was named a Maplecroft Climate Innovation Indexes (CIIs) Leader, ranking 17th out of the 300 largest US companies evaluated for their climate-related innovation and carbon management programs. Air Products is currently working on several preeminent <a href="/wiki/Carbon_capture_and_storage" title="Carbon capture and storage">carbon capture and storage</a> demonstration projects around the world.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>Air Products was included on the <a href="/wiki/Dow_Jones_Sustainability_Index" class="mw-redirect" title="Dow Jones Sustainability Index">Dow Jones Sustainability North America Index</a> as one of the best performing sustainable companies for 2008/11.  Only the top 20 percent of the 600 largest companies largest North American companies are selected for the DJSI North America Index.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 2001, Air Products Received an Award of Merit from the WateReuse Association for Outstanding Water Conservation Efforts for conserving 62 million US gallons (230,000 m<sup>3</sup>) of drinking water annually by converting to recycled water for the cooling process at the Santa Clara, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, manufacturing facility. The award was presented during the 25th Annual WateReuse Symposium. WRA recognizes projects that have advanced the acceptance of water reuse through <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">education</a>, sound science, and technology using reclamation, <a href="/wiki/Recycling" title="Recycling">recycling</a>, reuse, or <a href="/wiki/Desalination" title="Desalination">desalination</a> for the benefit of the public and the environment.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
