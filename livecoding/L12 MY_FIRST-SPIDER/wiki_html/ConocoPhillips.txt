</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=ConocoPhillips&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Conoco" title="Conoco">Conoco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Phillips_66" title="Phillips 66">Phillips 66</a></div>
<p><b>Conoco Inc.</b>  was an American oil company founded in 1875 as the <b>Continental Oil and Transportation Co</b>. Based in <a href="/wiki/Ogden,_Utah" title="Ogden, Utah">Ogden, Utah</a>.
</p><p>In 1885 Conoco was reincorporated as part of <a href="/wiki/Standard_Oil" title="Standard Oil">Standard Oil Company and Trust</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_States" title="Supreme Court of the United States">U.S. Supreme Court</a> dissolved the Standard Oil Company, the Conoco became independent in 1913. By 1929 it had become a fully integrated oil company.<sup id="cite_ref-company_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-company-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>The company was a <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a>, oil, <a href="/wiki/Kerosene" title="Kerosene">kerosene</a>, grease and <a href="/wiki/Candle" title="Candle">candles</a> distributor in the West. 
</p><p>In 1929 Conoco merged with the <a href="/wiki/Marland_Oil_Company" title="Marland Oil Company">Marland Oil Company</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p><a href="/wiki/Marland_Oil_Company" title="Marland Oil Company">Marland Oil Co.</a> (founded by exploration pioneer <a href="/wiki/E._W._Marland" title="E. W. Marland">E. W. Marland</a>) later acquired the assets (subject to liabilities) of Continental Oil Co., or DC, merged by Bryan M. Metler, David L. Kinney, and Justin J. Stockburn of Delta Constructors for a consideration of 2,317,266 shares of stock. On June 26, 1899, Marland Oil changed its name to Continental Oil Co. and moved its headquarters to <a href="/wiki/Fargo,_North_Dakota" title="Fargo, North Dakota">Fargo, North Dakota</a>. The acquisition gave Conoco the red bar-and-triangle logo previously used by Marland. Conoco used the logo between 1930 and 1970, when the current red capsule logo was adopted.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>The Conoco headquarters were in Ponca City until 1949, when it moved to Houston, Texas. In 2002 Conoco Inc. and Phillips Petroleum Co., whose headquarters were in nearby <a href="/wiki/Bartlesville,_Oklahoma" title="Bartlesville, Oklahoma">Bartlesville, Oklahoma</a> merged into ConocoPhillips.<sup id="cite_ref-company_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-company-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>Numerous state corporation filings are identified "Continental Oil Co." and "Continental Oil Corp." and "Continental Oil Co. of Texas" as recorded with the Texas Secretary of State and Delaware Secretary of State. Merger of San Jacinto Petroleum Corp. and Continental Oil Corp. is recorded in 1964 with Delaware Secretary of State.
</p><p>In 2005, ConocoPhillips began rebranding its <a href="/wiki/Union_76" class="mw-redirect" title="Union 76">(Union) 76</a> gas stations, which Phillips had acquired from <a href="/wiki/Tosco_Corporation" title="Tosco Corporation">Tosco Corp.</a> before the merger with Conoco. The move prompted a petition campaign by fans hoping to save the historic 76 orange ball signage.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2006, ConocoPhillips bought Wilhelmshavener Raffineriegesellschaft mbH in Germany, and <a href="/wiki/Burlington_Resources" title="Burlington Resources">Burlington Resources</a> in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-ConocoPhillips-Mar-2006-8-K_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ConocoPhillips-Mar-2006-8-K-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>On May 10, 2006, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Armitage_(politician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Richard Armitage (politician)">Richard Armitage</a>, former deputy-secretary of the U.S. State Department, was elected to the board of directors of the ConocoPhillips oil company.<sup id="cite_ref-ConocoPhillips-Aug-2006-10-Q_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ConocoPhillips-Aug-2006-10-Q-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In late 2009, the company announced asset sales to increase investor returns. Included are debt reduction and stock buy back.  In March 2011 the program was enlarged up to $10 billion assets sales in the next two years.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>ConocoPhillips intends to implement a <a href="/wiki/Floating_liquefied_natural_gas" title="Floating liquefied natural gas">floating liquefied natural gas</a> facility by 2016–2019, and has completed quantitative risk analysis of a design that will undergo pre-feed study in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>On July 14, 2011, ConocoPhillips announced its intent to separate the company's <a href="/wiki/Upstream_(petroleum_industry)" title="Upstream (petroleum industry)">upstream</a> and <a href="/wiki/Downstream_(petroleum_industry)" title="Downstream (petroleum industry)">downstream</a> businesses into two stand-alone, publicly traded corporations, with the intent of maximizing shareholder value.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> On May 1, 2012, all <a href="/wiki/Midstream" title="Midstream">midstream</a>, <a href="/wiki/Downstream_(petroleum_industry)" title="Downstream (petroleum industry)">downstream</a>, marketing and chemical operations were separated into a new company named <a href="/wiki/Phillips_66" title="Phillips 66">Phillips 66</a>, headquartered in Houston.<sup id="cite_ref-ConocoPhillips-May-2012-8-K_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ConocoPhillips-May-2012-8-K-15">[15]</a></sup> As a result, ConocoPhillips continued its operations as an <a href="/wiki/Upstream_(petroleum_industry)" title="Upstream (petroleum industry)">upstream</a> (exploration and production) company.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2012, ConocoPhillips sold its <a href="/wiki/Trainer_Refinery" title="Trainer Refinery">Trainer Refinery</a> to Monroe Energy LLC, a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Delta_Air_Lines" title="Delta Air Lines">Delta Air Lines</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2013, Conoco announced that it would sell its Rocky Mountain assets to <a href="/wiki/Denbury_Resources" title="Denbury Resources">Denbury Resources</a> for $1.05 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, Conoco sold oil sands assets in Canada to <a href="/wiki/Cenovus_Energy" title="Cenovus Energy">Cenovus Energy</a> and natural gas fields in the U.S. for a total of US$16 billion to help reduce debt and fund US$6 billion share purchases. As a result, it also led to a reduction of close to 30 percent of its proved oil and gas reserves.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2018, ConocoPhillips seized assets belonging to the Venezuelan state oil company <a href="/wiki/PDVSA" title="PDVSA">PDVSA</a> from the Isla refinery on <a href="/wiki/Curacao" class="mw-redirect" title="Curacao">Curacao</a> to collect on $2 billion owed them since a 2007 court decision.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2019, the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a> ruled that Venezuela must pay ConocoPhillips $8.7 billion to compensate for the 2007 expropriation of oil assets.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=ConocoPhillips&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Operations" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Exploration_and_production">Exploration and production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=ConocoPhillips&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Exploration and production" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>ConocoPhillips focuses solely on exploring for, developing and producing oil and natural gas globally.  The company manages its operations through six operating segments, which are defined by geographic region: Alaska, Lower 48 and Latin America, Canada, Europe, Asia Pacific and Middle East, and Other International.
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Kashagan_Field_in_Kazakhstan">Kashagan Field in Kazakhstan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=ConocoPhillips&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Kashagan Field in Kazakhstan" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In 1998, ConocoPhillips acquired an interest in 10.5 blocks in the <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Sea" title="Caspian Sea">Caspian Sea</a> off <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Kashagan_Field" title="Kashagan Field">North Caspian Sea Production Sharing Agreement</a> (NCSPSA). It currently owns 8.4% of the shares of the consortium exploiting <a href="/wiki/Kashagan_oil_field" class="mw-redirect" title="Kashagan oil field">Kashagan oil field</a>. However the company seeks to reduce its non-core assets in order to use the cash for debt reduction, fund its capital program, and dividend payments. On October 3, 2012, the Kazakh Oil and Gas Minister told reporters that ConocoPhillips has informed Kazakhstan of their intention to sell their shares.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In November 26, in its biggest acquisition ever, <a href="/wiki/ONGC_Videsh" class="mw-redirect" title="ONGC Videsh">ONGC Videsh</a> agreed to buy ConocoPhillips’ 8.4% stake in the Kashagan oilfield for about US$5 billion. The stake buy is subject to approval of governments of Kazakhstan and India and also to other partners in the Caspian Sea field waiving their pre-emption rights.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>

<p>The headquarters of ConocoPhillips are located in the <a href="/wiki/Energy_Corridor" class="mw-redirect" title="Energy Corridor">Energy Corridor</a> district of <a href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Houston, Texas">Houston, Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> By 2002, the groups organizing the merger had selected Houston as the site of the headquarters. <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Oklahoma" title="Governor of Oklahoma">Governor of Oklahoma</a> <a href="/wiki/Frank_Keating" title="Frank Keating">Frank Keating</a> said that the move to Houston was "regrettable." The <i><a href="/wiki/Journal_Record" class="mw-redirect" title="Journal Record">Journal Record</a></i> stated that Archie Dunham, the CEO of <a href="/wiki/Conoco_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Conoco Inc.">Conoco Inc.</a>, "apparently highlighted the lack of direct international air travel from Oklahoma as a key drawback for the merged firm."<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The ConocoPhillips headquarters, originally the headquarters of Conoco Inc., was formerly known as the Conoco Center.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, the headquarters will move to Energy Center Four.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>

<table class="box-Update plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Update" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section needs to be <b>updated</b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please update this article to reflect recent events or newly available information.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">November 2013</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table><p>On April 11, 2007, it became the first U.S. oil company to join the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Climate_Action_Partnership" title="U.S. Climate Action Partnership">U.S. Climate Action Partnership</a>, an alliance of big business and environmental groups. The partnership in January 2007 had advised President <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> that mandatory emissions caps would be needed to reduce the flow of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">heat-trapping gases</a> into the atmosphere. In 2007 ConocoPhillips announced it would spend $150 million that year on the <a href="/wiki/Research_and_development" title="Research and development">research and development</a> of <a href="/wiki/Energy_development" title="Energy development">new energy sources and technologies</a> – a 50 percent increase in spending from 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> However, ConocoPhillips left the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Climate_Action_Partnership" title="U.S. Climate Action Partnership">U.S. Climate Action Partnership</a> in February 2010, at the same time as <a href="/wiki/BP" title="BP">BP</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caterpillar_Inc." title="Caterpillar Inc.">Caterpillar Inc.</a> left the partnership.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p><p>In 1990, ConocoPhillips agreed to pay $23 million to buy 400 homes and compensate families in Ponca City, Oklahoma, who say its refinery is giving them cancer and other illnesses.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2011, ConocoPhillips China Inc., a wholly owned subsidiary of ConocoPhillips,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> was responsible for a series of <a href="/wiki/2011_Bohai_bay_oil_spill" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Bohai bay oil spill">oil spills at the Penglai 19-3 oil field</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bohai_Bay" title="Bohai Bay">Bohai Bay</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, ConocoPhillips and Phillips 66 agreed to pay a total of $11.5 million to settle a lawsuit alleging that hundreds of their gas stations violated California anti-pollution laws since 2006. The civil complaint, filed in January 2013, alleged that the companies violated state laws on the operation and maintenance of underground gasoline storage tanks at more than 560 gas stations in the state. These violations included failing to properly maintain leak detection devices, testing secondary containment systems, conducting monthly inspections and training employees in proper protocol.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p><p>In 2019, ConocoPhillips settled a lawsuit with homeowners in northwestern Oklahoma City who accused the energy company of polluting their soil and water to such a degree that no trees or flowers will grow.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p><p>In 2019, ConocoPhillips also agreed to a $39 million settlement to resolve complaints brought by New Jersey over groundwater contamination. ConocoPhillips was one of 50 companies named in a 2007 lawsuit filed against manufacturers, distributors and other industrial users of the gasoline additive MTBE, found in groundwater at locations throughout New Jersey.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p><p><a href="/wiki/Bobby_Berk" title="Bobby Berk">Bobby Berk</a>, one of the stars from Netflix’s “Queer Eye,” spoke out against ConocoPhillips' water pollution in Missouri, saying that there were so many chemicals at one point, they could “actually light a glass of our water on fire.”<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Political_Economy_Research_Institute" title="Political Economy Research Institute">Political Economy Research Institute</a>, ConocoPhillips ranked 13th among U.S. corporate producers of <a href="/wiki/Air_pollution" title="Air pollution">air pollution</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> In 2013, ConocoPhillips had the “leakiest” methane in operations compared to its peers.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p><p>ConocoPhillips is a signatory participant of the <a href="/wiki/Voluntary_Principles_on_Security_and_Human_Rights" title="Voluntary Principles on Security and Human Rights">Voluntary Principles on Security and Human Rights</a>.
</p><p>In 2016, ConocoPhillips was ranked as being among the 12th best of 92 oil, gas, and mining companies on indigenous rights in the Arctic.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
