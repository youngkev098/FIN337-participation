<p><b>Yum! Brands, Inc.</b> (or <b>Yum!</b>), formerly <b>Tricon Global Restaurants, Inc.</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Fast_food" title="Fast food">fast food</a> corporation listed on the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a>. Yum! operates the brands <a href="/wiki/KFC" title="KFC">KFC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pizza_Hut" title="Pizza Hut">Pizza Hut</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taco_Bell" title="Taco Bell">Taco Bell</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Habit_Burger_Grill" title="The Habit Burger Grill">The Habit Burger Grill</a>, and <a href="/wiki/WingStreet" title="WingStreet">WingStreet</a> worldwide, except in China, where the brands are operated by a separate company, <a href="/wiki/Yum_China" title="Yum China">Yum China</a>. Prior to 2011, Yum! also owned <a href="/wiki/Long_John_Silver%27s" title="Long John Silver's">Long John Silver's</a> and <a href="/wiki/A%26W_Restaurants" title="A&amp;W Restaurants">A&amp;W Restaurants</a>.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yum!_Brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="PepsiCo_fast_food_division">PepsiCo fast food division</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yum!_Brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: PepsiCo fast food division" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The company's history traces back to 1977, when <a href="/wiki/PepsiCo" title="PepsiCo">PepsiCo</a> entered into the restaurant business by acquiring <a href="/wiki/Pizza_Hut" title="Pizza Hut">Pizza Hut</a>. A year later, PepsiCo purchased Taco Bell.<sup id="cite_ref-ctrib_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ctrib-3">[3]</a></sup> In July 1986, <a href="/wiki/R._J._Reynolds_Tobacco_Company" title="R. J. Reynolds Tobacco Company">R. J. Reynolds</a> sold KFC to PepsiCo<sup id="cite_ref-ctrib_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ctrib-3">[3]</a></sup> in order to pay off debt from its recent purchase of <a href="/wiki/Nabisco" title="Nabisco">Nabisco</a>. In 1990, <a href="/wiki/Hot_%27n_Now" title="Hot 'n Now">Hot 'n Now</a> was acquired via Taco Bell, <sup id="cite_ref-lat1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lat1-4">[4]</a></sup> but the company would later be sold in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1992, PepsiCo acquired <a href="/wiki/California_Pizza_Kitchen" title="California Pizza Kitchen">California Pizza Kitchen</a>. In 1993, it acquired <a href="/wiki/Chevys_Fresh_Mex" title="Chevys Fresh Mex">Chevys Fresh Mex</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/D%27Angelo_Grilled_Sandwiches" title="D'Angelo Grilled Sandwiches">D'Angelo Grilled Sandwiches</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and Canadian chain <a href="/wiki/East_Side_Mario%27s" title="East Side Mario's">East Side Mario's</a>. However these chains would later be sold when PepsiCo exited the restaurant business.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 1997, PepsiCo sold Pepsico Food Systems restaurant-supply unit to Ameriserve Food Distribution Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Tricon_Global_Restaurants">Tricon Global Restaurants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yum!_Brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tricon Global Restaurants" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Yum! was created on May 30, 1997, as Tricon Global Restaurants, Inc. from <a href="/wiki/PepsiCo" title="PepsiCo">PepsiCo</a>'s fast food division as the parent corporation of KFC, Pizza Hut and Taco Bell restaurant companies.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lat2_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lat2-13">[13]</a></sup> Several years before changing its name to "<a href="/wiki/Yum!_Brands#Yum!_Brands" title="Yum! Brands">Yum! Brands</a>", Tricon Global was <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spun off</a> in late 1997. Tricon selected Louisville as its headquarters, near the KFC Restaurant Support Center from the Pepsi HQ in Purchase, New York, by early 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-lat2_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lat2-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In 2000, Tricon Global tested multi-branded locations with <a href="/wiki/A%26W_Restaurants" title="A&amp;W Restaurants">Yorkshire Global Restaurants</a>. By March 2002, the Tricon-Yorkshire multibranding test consisted of 83 KFC/A&amp;Ws, six KFC/Long John Silver's and three Taco Bell/Long John Silver's and was considered successful by the companies.<sup id="cite_ref-pr_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In 2001, KFC started test restaurants in Austin, Texas, called "Wing Works", a chicken wing line sold with one of a few flavored sauces. KFC also hired a consultant to develop a breakfast menu.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Yum.21_Brands"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Yum!_Brands">Yum! Brands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yum!_Brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Yum! Brands" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In March 2002, Tricon announced the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Lexington,_Kentucky" title="Lexington, Kentucky">Lexington, Kentucky</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Global_Restaurants" class="mw-redirect" title="Yorkshire Global Restaurants">Yorkshire Global Restaurants</a>, owner of the <a href="/wiki/Long_John_Silver%27s" title="Long John Silver's">Long John Silver's</a> and <a href="/wiki/A%26W_Restaurants" title="A&amp;W Restaurants">A&amp;W Restaurants</a><sup id="cite_ref-sale_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sale-16">[16]</a></sup> chains and its intention to change the company's name to Yum! Brands, Inc. to match its NYSE ticker symbol. The acquisition was finalized on May 8, 2002, and the name change became effective on May 22, 2002<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On June 6, 2002, Yum! executed a two-for-one <a href="/wiki/Stock_split" title="Stock split">stock split</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Shortly afterwards, due to Yum!'s lifetime contract with Pepsi, Long John Silver's and A&amp;W Restaurants (which previously served Coca-Cola products) began switching to Pepsi products, with A&amp;W Restaurants retaining <a href="/wiki/A%26W_Root_Beer" title="A&amp;W Root Beer">A&amp;W Root Beer</a> from a separate deal with <a href="/wiki/Dr_Pepper/Seven_Up" title="Dr Pepper/Seven Up">Dr Pepper/Seven Up</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Dr_Pepper_Snapple_Group" title="Dr Pepper Snapple Group">Dr Pepper Snapple Group</a>).
</p><p>In 2002, Yum! began testing co-branding locations pairing Pizza Hut with <a href="/wiki/Pasta_Bravo" title="Pasta Bravo">Pasta Bravo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Back_Yard_Burgers" title="Back Yard Burgers">Back Yard Burgers</a>, and A&amp;W.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The Pasta Bravo concept was acquired in 2003 from Pasta Bravo, Inc. of Aliso Viejo, California for $5 million to pair with Pizza Hut.<sup id="cite_ref-nrn_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrn-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In 2003, Yum! launched WingStreet as a hybrid combo unit with an existing Pizza Hut franchise.<sup id="cite_ref-bj_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bj-21">[21]</a></sup> In 2007 and 2008, a thousand WingStreet stores a year were opened. On October 19, 2009, Company president Scott Bergren publicize WingStreet's national launch.<sup id="cite_ref-bj_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bj-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>An <a href="/wiki/East_Dawning" title="East Dawning">East Dawning</a> test <a href="/wiki/Cafeteria-style_restaurant" class="mw-redirect" title="Cafeteria-style restaurant">cafeteria-style restaurant</a> was opened in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> in 2004. After initially failing, Yum! Brands chose the KFC business model (KFC is the most successful Western chain in China) and found greater success.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj_20061020_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj_20061020-22">[22]</a></sup> As of September 30, 2007, eight East Dawning restaurants were in operation.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="International_focus">International focus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yum!_Brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International focus" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tacobellkfcrestaurants.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Tacobellkfcrestaurants.jpg/220px-Tacobellkfcrestaurants.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="155" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="700" data-file-height="493"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 155px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Tacobellkfcrestaurants.jpg/220px-Tacobellkfcrestaurants.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="155" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Tacobellkfcrestaurants.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A single Yum! restaurant facility co-branded as Taco Bell and KFC in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco, California</a></div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kfc_taco_bell.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Kfc_taco_bell.jpg/220px-Kfc_taco_bell.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1216" data-file-height="912"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Kfc_taco_bell.jpg/220px-Kfc_taco_bell.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kfc_taco_bell.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Another cobranded KFC and Taco Bell in <a href="/wiki/Oscoda,_Michigan" title="Oscoda, Michigan">Oscoda, Michigan</a></div></div></div>
<p>In January 2011, Yum! announced its intentions to divest itself of its Long John Silver's and A&amp;W brands to focus on its core brands of KFC, Pizza Hut and Taco Bell. For the decade leading up to the company's announcement, major growth had relied on international expansion. With little presence outside North America, the two chains no longer fit in the company's long-term growth plans.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The foreign expansion—particularly that of Taco Bell, KFC and Pizza Hut—was cited in the firm's January 18, 2011 announcement of its intention to sell the A&amp;W and Long John Silver's chains. Both of those chains also suffered from poor sales, and had fewer locations compared to the other chains in the Yum! Brands portfolio. In September 2011, Yum! announced they had found buyers for the A&amp;W and Long John Silver's chains. A Great American Brand will buy A&amp;W, and Long John Silver's will be acquired by LJS Partners LLC.<sup id="cite_ref-sale_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sale-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2011, Yum! agreed to purchase Chinese <a href="/wiki/Hot_pot" title="Hot pot">hot pot</a> chain <a href="/wiki/Little_Sheep" class="mw-redirect" title="Little Sheep">Little Sheep</a> for <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Dollar" class="mw-redirect" title="Hong Kong Dollar">HK$</a>4.56 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The deal spent more than 4 months in antitrust review by the Chinese Ministry of Commerce, to determine whether or not the transaction would result in a monopolistic positioning of Yum! in the country's restaurant industry. The Ministry approved the deal in November 2011, according to Little Sheep representatives.<sup id="cite_ref-cp_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cp-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012, a KFC opened in <a href="/wiki/Ramallah" title="Ramallah">Ramallah</a> and became the first American fast food restaurant to operate in the <a href="/wiki/West_Bank" title="West Bank">West Bank</a>; a Pizza Hut is also planned.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>In 2013, a few KFC locations in China supplied chicken found to contain "excess levels of chemical residue". Yum! had lost 6% of sales from publicity in China as of January 25th, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>The company opened its first restaurant in <a href="/wiki/Ulaanbaatar,_Mongolia" class="mw-redirect" title="Ulaanbaatar, Mongolia">Ulaanbaatar, Mongolia</a>, in May 2013. For Mongolia, KFC is the first western fast food to open its doors in the country. The company is planning to open 15 more restaurants in Ulaanbaatar in the next 5 years, including the country's first drive-thru service.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Yum! Brands has also opened Taco Bell and Pizza Hut restaurants in newer <a href="/wiki/Target_Corporation" title="Target Corporation">Target</a> stores.
</p><p>Yum! Brands opened its 40,000th store in <a href="/wiki/Calangute" title="Calangute">Calangute</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goa" title="Goa">Goa</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> in October 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>In 2013, its KFC subsidiary opened a fast casual version, KFC eleven, test location in Louisville on Bardstown Road.<sup id="cite_ref-cj_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cj-31">[31]</a></sup> In the third quarter of 2013, Yum! Brands had to book an impairment of the goodwill resulting from the takeover of Little Sheep in 2011 in the amount of $222 million, which reduced profits for 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-Hoang2013_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hoang2013-32">[32]</a></sup></p><p>In 2014, Yum! launched a number of additional restaurant test concepts, Super Chix, U.S. Taco Co. and Banh Shop.<sup id="cite_ref-nrn0_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrn0-33">[33]</a></sup> Yum! opened Super Chix in Central Arlington, Texas, a restaurant similar in format to <a href="/wiki/Chick-fil-A" title="Chick-fil-A">Chick-fil-A</a>, on April 9, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> In the summer of 2014, Yum!'s Taco Bell subsidiary launched its  U.S. Taco Co and Urban Tap Room fast-casual taco concept restaurant in Huntington Beach, California, to take on fast casual restaurants like <a href="/wiki/Chipotle_Mexican_Grill" title="Chipotle Mexican Grill">Chipotle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Panera" class="mw-redirect" title="Panera">Panera</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-time_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-time-35">[35]</a></sup></p><p>In the first quarter of 2015, <a href="/wiki/Third_Point_Management" title="Third Point Management">Third Point Management</a> and Corvex Management separately acquired an unspecified stake in the company.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The sole KFC Eleven was closed in April 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-cj_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cj-31">[31]</a></sup> A second Chix unit opened in May 2015 with additional menu items. Super Chix was sold to founder Nick Ouimet and an investment group in August 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-nrn0_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrn0-33">[33]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, Yum! announced plans to open 10 Pizza Hut restaurants in <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>, after signing a franchise with the country's Belayab Foods and Franchise PLC.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2020, Yum! announced they were acquiring Irvine, California-based <a href="/wiki/The_Habit_Burger_Grill" title="The Habit Burger Grill">The Habit Burger Grill</a>, for $375 million; the transaction has completed on March 18, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj1_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj1-38">[38]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>

<p>The current executive chairman of Yum! Brands is Greg Creed. The previous CEO was <a href="/wiki/David_C._Novak" title="David C. Novak">David C. Novak</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Yum!-Brands-Feb-2000-8-K_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Yum!-Brands-Feb-2000-8-K-40">[40]</a></sup> Novak became CEO of predecessor firm Tricon Global on January 1, 2000, and <a href="/wiki/Board_of_directors" title="Board of directors">chairman of the board</a> on January 1, 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-Yum!-Brands-Mar-2001-10-K_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Yum!-Brands-Mar-2001-10-K-41">[41]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Greg_Creed" title="Greg Creed">Greg Creed</a> replaced Novak in his role as CEO on January 1, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> At the AGM in May 2016, <a href="/wiki/Robert_D._Walter" title="Robert D. Walter">Robert D. Walter</a> became non-executive chairman. At the end of 2019 Greg Creed will retire as CEO and the current COO (David Gibbs) will be Creed's replacement.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p><p>Since 2006, Yum! Brands has served as the corporate sponsor of the <a href="/wiki/Kentucky_Derby" title="Kentucky Derby">Kentucky Derby</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p><p>On October 20, 2015, Yum! Brands, Inc., announced that it intended to separate into two independent, publicly traded companies.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Yum_China" title="Yum China">Yum China</a> was spun off on November 1, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
