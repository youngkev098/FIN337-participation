</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Paper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:View_of_Paper_Mills,_Chisholm,_ME.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/View_of_Paper_Mills%2C_Chisholm%2C_ME.jpg/220px-View_of_Paper_Mills%2C_Chisholm%2C_ME.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="139" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="650" data-file-height="410"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 139px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/View_of_Paper_Mills%2C_Chisholm%2C_ME.jpg/220px-View_of_Paper_Mills%2C_Chisholm%2C_ME.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="139" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:View_of_Paper_Mills,_Chisholm,_ME.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>One of the 17 original founding mills of International Paper c. 1908, built in 1888 as the Otis Falls Pulp &amp; Paper Company in <a href="/wiki/Chisholm,_Maine" title="Chisholm, Maine">Chisholm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The company was incorporated January 31, 1898, upon the merger of 17 pulp and paper mills in the northeastern United States. Its founders and first two presidents were <a href="/wiki/William_A._Russell" class="mw-redirect" title="William A. Russell">William Augustus Russell</a>, who died suddenly in January 1899, and <a href="/wiki/Hugh_J._Chisholm" title="Hugh J. Chisholm">Hugh J. Chisholm</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>  Philip Tell Dodge, president of the <a href="/wiki/Mergenthaler_Linotype_Company" title="Mergenthaler Linotype Company">Mergenthaler Linotype Company</a>, served as its chairman for 11 years.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>  The invention of the <a href="/wiki/Linotype_machine" title="Linotype machine">Linotype</a> dramatically increased the size of newspapers and the need for <a href="/wiki/Newsprint" title="Newsprint">newsprint</a>.  The newly formed company supplied 60 percent of all newsprint in the country.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Hudson_River_Mill">Hudson River Mill</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Paper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Hudson River Mill" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/History_of_papermaking_in_New_York" title="History of papermaking in New York">History of papermaking in New York</a></div>
<p>The Hudson River Mill in <a href="/wiki/Corinth,_New_York" title="Corinth, New York">Corinth</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, where the <a href="/wiki/Sacandaga_River" title="Sacandaga River">Sacandaga River</a> joins the <a href="/wiki/Hudson_River" title="Hudson River">Hudson River</a>, was a pioneer in the development of the modern paper industry in the late 19th century.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The first wood-based <a href="/wiki/Newsprint" title="Newsprint">newsprint</a> paper mill in New York, it was built by <a href="/wiki/Albrecht_Pagenstecher" class="mw-redirect" title="Albrecht Pagenstecher">Albrecht Pagenstecher</a> in 1869.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In the early 20th century, the Hudson River Mill was one of the company's largest plants and served both as its principal office, and a place where paper workers helped shape the direction of the industry's early labor movement.
</p><p>After <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, Hudson River Mill workers developed and perfected the production of <a href="/wiki/Coated_paper" title="Coated paper">coated paper</a> for the company. Shifting economic forces resulted in the mill's closure in November 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The historic mill was slated for partial demolition during 2011. The work including asbestos removal was completed by Northstar Group Services.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>Given the nature of their products, paper plants are highly flammable. Therefore, International Paper Company frequently used <a href="/wiki/Asbestos" title="Asbestos">asbestos</a> insulation in its walls, floors, and roofs as a protective measure.  Asbestos insulation was also used on pipes and <a href="/wiki/Boiler" title="Boiler">boilers</a> throughout International Paper plants. This material intended to protect people turned out to severely damage their health<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The producers did not reveal that their asbestos products were dangerous, even though asbestos was known to cause illnesses as far back as the 1920s. Consequently, many former employees of International Paper have been diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Mesothelioma" title="Mesothelioma">mesothelioma</a> following decades of service.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Mill_workers.27_strike"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Mill_workers'_strike">Mill workers' strike</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Paper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mill workers' strike" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/International_Paper_strike" title="International Paper strike">International Paper strike</a></div>
<p>In 1987, the company's paper mill workers went on strike at a number of its U.S. plants.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Paper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Acquisitions" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<h4 class="in-block"><span id="1986.E2.80.932000"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1986–2000">1986–2000</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Paper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1986–2000" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In 1986, the company acquired the <a href="/wiki/Hammermill_Paper_Company" title="Hammermill Paper Company">Hammermill Paper Company</a>, founded in 1898, which managed eleven papermills nationwide, and had its corporate offices based in Erie, PA; in 1988, the Masonite Corporation; and in 1989, the <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> paper company Zanders Feinpapiere AG and the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> paper manufacturer Aussedat Rey. In 1996, it purchased Federal Paper Board.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <span class="citation-needed-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">In 1999, the company purchased <a href="/wiki/Union_Camp_Corporation" title="Union Camp Corporation">Union Camp Corporation</a>, and in June of 2000 <a href="/wiki/Champion_International" class="mw-redirect" title="Champion International">Champion International</a>. Additionally, it owns shares in the <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chilean</a> company <a href="/wiki/Copec" class="mw-redirect" title="Copec">Copec</a>.</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2011">2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Paper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2011" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Andhra_Pradesh" title="Andhra Pradesh">Andhra Pradesh</a> Paper Mills <a href="/wiki/Rajahmundry" title="Rajahmundry">Rajahmundry</a>, which is now an International Paper company, is one of the biggest integrated paper and pulp manufacturing centers in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. The company produces writing, printing, and copier papers and paper boards for foreign and domestic markets. APPM's production facilities are two mills in Rajahmundry and <a href="/wiki/Kadiam" class="mw-redirect" title="Kadiam">Kadiam</a> with a total production capacity of 240,000 TPD (Tons Per Day).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="tons per acre? (July 2013)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The company is becoming a driving force in sustainability in the paper manufacturing arena through focused social and community programs, including pioneering work in raw material generation through <a href="/wiki/Social_forestry_in_India" title="Social forestry in India">social farm forestry</a>. International Paper owns a majority interest in APPM, and the remaining shares are publicly traded on the <a href="/wiki/Bombay_Stock_Exchange" title="Bombay Stock Exchange">Bombay</a> and <a href="/wiki/National_Stock_Exchange_of_India" title="National Stock Exchange of India">National Stock Exchange</a> of India (APPAPER).
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2012">2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Paper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2012" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In 2012, International Paper, through the merger of its wholly owned subsidiary Metal Acquisition Inc. with and into <a href="/wiki/Temple-Inland" title="Temple-Inland">Temple-Inland</a>, acquired <a href="/wiki/Temple-Inland" title="Temple-Inland">Temple-Inland</a> in a deal valued at $4.5 billion.  Temple-Inland then became a wholly owned subsidiary of International Paper. At the time of sale, Temple-Inland's corrugated packaging operation consisted of 7 mills and 59 converting facilities as well as the building products operation.<sup id="cite_ref-prn_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prn-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Restructuring.2C_2005.E2.80.932006"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Restructuring,_2005–2006">Restructuring, 2005–2006</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Paper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Restructuring, 2005–2006" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2005 and 2006, the company undertook a significant restructuring, selling over 6,000,000 acres (24,000 km<sup>2</sup>) of forestland in the U.S., along with its coated paper, <a href="/wiki/Kraft_paper" title="Kraft paper">kraft paper</a>, wood products, and beverage packaging businesses, as well as subsidiaries Arizona Chemical and New Zealand-based <a href="/wiki/Carter_Holt_Harvey" title="Carter Holt Harvey">Carter Holt Harvey</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Coated_paper" title="Coated paper">coated paper</a> business (four mills in <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>) were sold to <a href="/wiki/Apollo_Management" class="mw-redirect" title="Apollo Management">Apollo Management</a> and now operate as <a href="/wiki/Verso_Paper" class="mw-redirect" title="Verso Paper">Verso Paper</a>. The kraft paper business (composed of a kraft paper mill in <a href="/wiki/Roanoke_Rapids,_North_Carolina" title="Roanoke Rapids, North Carolina">Roanoke Rapids</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> and a dunnage bag plant in <a href="/wiki/Fordyce,_Arkansas" title="Fordyce, Arkansas">Fordyce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arkansas" title="Arkansas">Arkansas</a>) was sold to <a href="/wiki/Kapstone" title="Kapstone">Kapstone</a> Paper and Packaging and operates as Kapstone Kraft Paper.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>The beverage packaging business, now called Evergreen Packaging, was purchased by <a href="/wiki/Carter_Holt_Harvey" title="Carter Holt Harvey">Carter Holt Harvey</a>, following the purchase of CHH by <a href="/wiki/Graeme_Hart" title="Graeme Hart">Graeme Hart</a>. The company sold its wood products division to <a href="/wiki/West_Fraser_Timber" title="West Fraser Timber">West Fraser Timber</a>, based in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>. This included 13 <a href="/wiki/Sawmill" title="Sawmill">sawmills</a>, making West Fraser the second-largest producer of lumber in North America, after <a href="/wiki/Weyerhaeuser_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="Weyerhaeuser Company">Weyerhaeuser Company</a>.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Challenges_of_an_electronic_world">Challenges of an electronic world</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Paper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Challenges of an electronic world" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Under pressure from <a href="/wiki/Budget_sequestration_in_2013" class="mw-redirect" title="Budget sequestration in 2013">budget sequestration in 2013</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Federal_government_of_the_United_States" title="Federal government of the United States">federal government of the United States</a> (IP's largest customer) has moved from physical checks to cheaper electronic transactions.
</p><p>John Runyan, former head of federal government relations for IP, has become executive director of Consumers for Paper Options, a paper industry funded group that advocates for the use of paper documents for clients of the federal government who are Internet challenged.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Company_logo">Company logo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Paper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Company logo" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>The company's <a href="/wiki/Logo" title="Logo">logo</a> was designed by American graphic designers <a href="/wiki/Lester_Beall" title="Lester Beall">Lester Beall</a> and Richard Rogers in 1960. The logo features the letters "I" and "P" which form a stylized arrow also resembling a tree surrounded by a circle. A primary constraint in the design process was the need for a logo simple enough that it could be stenciled onto trees and lumber intended for paper production.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>

Other company related facilities include a recycling center on South Third and two warehouse operations across Memphis.<sup id="cite_ref-MBJ_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MBJ-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Products_and_corporate_structure">Products and corporate structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Paper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Products and corporate structure" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:International_Paper_Co._Mill,_Berlin,_NH.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/International_Paper_Co._Mill%2C_Berlin%2C_NH.jpg/220px-International_Paper_Co._Mill%2C_Berlin%2C_NH.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="142" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="653" data-file-height="421"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 142px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/International_Paper_Co._Mill%2C_Berlin%2C_NH.jpg/220px-International_Paper_Co._Mill%2C_Berlin%2C_NH.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="142" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:International_Paper_Co._Mill,_Berlin,_NH.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>One of the old mills in the former "<a href="/wiki/Mill_town" title="Mill town">mill town</a>" of <a href="/wiki/Berlin,_New_Hampshire" title="Berlin, New Hampshire">Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a>, owned by the International Paper, c. 1912.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:InternationalPaper6413.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/InternationalPaper6413.jpg/220px-InternationalPaper6413.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="300" data-file-height="225"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/InternationalPaper6413.jpg/220px-InternationalPaper6413.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:InternationalPaper6413.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pulp and converting paper mill, located in <a href="/wiki/Georgetown,_South_Carolina" title="Georgetown, South Carolina">Georgetown</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a>.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kwidzyn_by_night.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b4/Kwidzyn_by_night.jpg/220px-Kwidzyn_by_night.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="54" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="651" data-file-height="159"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 54px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b4/Kwidzyn_by_night.jpg/220px-Kwidzyn_by_night.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="54" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kwidzyn_by_night.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Factory in <a href="/wiki/Kwidzyn" title="Kwidzyn">Kwidzyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, at night</div></div></div>
<p>The company used to be the largest producer of <a href="/wiki/Plastic_lid" class="mw-redirect" title="Plastic lid">plastic lids</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paper_cup" title="Paper cup">paper cups</a>, manufacturing for the fast-food giants <a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s" title="McDonald's">McDonald's</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wendy%27s" title="Wendy's">Wendy's</a>,  <a href="/wiki/Subway_(restaurant)" title="Subway (restaurant)">Subway</a>, but its consumer packaging division was sold to <a href="/wiki/Graphic_Packaging" title="Graphic Packaging">Graphic Packaging</a> on January 2, 2018.  Its wood products division was sold in 2007 to West Fraser Timber Co. Ltd., a company headquartered in Vancouver, British Columbia. The company produces <a href="/wiki/Printer_(computing)" title="Printer (computing)">printer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Copier" class="mw-redirect" title="Copier">copier</a> paper, <a href="/wiki/Envelope" title="Envelope">envelopes</a>, corrugated <a href="/wiki/Packaging" class="mw-redirect" title="Packaging">packaging</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pulp_(paper)" title="Pulp (paper)">pulp</a>.
</p><p>The company is a former <a href="/wiki/Dow_Jones_Industrial_Average" title="Dow Jones Industrial Average">Dow Jones Industrial Average</a> component, included in the index from July 3, 1956, to April 7, 2004. It was one of three components to be dropped in the 2004 change, together with <a href="/wiki/AT%26T_Corporation" title="AT&amp;T Corporation">AT&amp;T Corporation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eastman_Kodak" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastman Kodak">Eastman Kodak</a>.
</p><p>Beginning February 1, 2007, the sale of the beverage-packaging division was completed as <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> billionaire <a href="/wiki/Graeme_Hart" title="Graeme Hart">Graeme Hart</a> won the bid with purchase price of nearly $800 million. The division now operates under the Evergreen label.
</p><p>On March 17, 2008, the company announced it was buying the containerboard unit of <a href="/wiki/Weyerhaeuser" title="Weyerhaeuser">Weyerhaeuser</a> for $6 billion in cash.
</p><p>In October 2007, it formed a joint venture with Ilim Holding, Ilim Group, the alliance in the Russian forest sector.
</p><p>On July 1, 2014, the company announced the completion of the xpedx spinoff which merged with Unisource, creating an independent company, <a href="/wiki/Veritiv" title="Veritiv">Veritiv</a> Corporation.
</p><p>On May 2, 2016, it announced it was purchasing the cellulose fiber division of Weyerhaeuser. The acquisition was completed on Dec 1, 2016.
</p>
