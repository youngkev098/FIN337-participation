<p><b>HP Inc.</b> (also known as <b>HP</b> and stylized as <i><b>hp</b></i>) is an American <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">information</a> <a href="/wiki/Technology_company" title="Technology company">technology company</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California" title="Palo Alto, California">Palo Alto, California</a>, United States. It develops <a href="/wiki/Personal_computer" title="Personal computer">personal computers</a> (PCs), <a href="/wiki/Printer_(computing)" title="Printer (computing)">printers</a> and related supplies, as well as <a href="/wiki/3D_printing" title="3D printing">3D printing</a> solutions.

The split was structured so that Hewlett-Packard changed its name to HP Inc. and <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spun off</a> Hewlett Packard Enterprise as a new publicly traded company. HP Inc. retains Hewlett-Packard's pre-2015 stock price history and its former stock ticker symbol, HPQ, while Hewlett Packard Enterprise trades under its own symbol, HPE.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Darrow_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Darrow-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>HP is listed on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> and is a constituent of the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500_Index" title="S&amp;P 500 Index">S&amp;P 500 Index</a>. It is the <a href="/wiki/Market_share_of_personal_computer_vendors" title="Market share of personal computer vendors">world's largest personal computer vendor</a> by unit sales, having regained its position in 2017 since it was overtaken by <a href="/wiki/Lenovo" title="Lenovo">Lenovo</a> in 2013. </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=HP_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard#History" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard § History</a></div>
<p>HP Inc. was formerly known as <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard</a>. Hewlett-Packard was founded in 1939 by <a href="/wiki/Bill_Hewlett" title="Bill Hewlett">Bill Hewlett</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Packard" title="David Packard">David Packard</a>, who both graduated with degrees in <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a> from <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a> in 1935. The company started off in the <a href="/wiki/HP_Garage" title="HP Garage">HP Garage</a> in <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California" title="Palo Alto, California">Palo Alto, California</a>. On November 1, 2015, Hewlett-Packard was renamed to HP Inc. and the company enterprise business were spun off and renamed to <a href="/wiki/Hewlett_Packard_Enterprise" title="Hewlett Packard Enterprise">Hewlett Packard Enterprise</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Darrow_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Darrow-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="As_HP_Inc.">As HP Inc.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=HP_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: As HP Inc." data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In May 2016, HP introduced a new <a href="/wiki/PC_gaming" class="mw-redirect" title="PC gaming">PC gaming</a> sub-brand known as Omen (reusing trademarks associated with <a href="/wiki/VoodooPC" title="VoodooPC">VoodooPC</a>), including gaming laptops and desktops (with the latter offering options such as <a href="/wiki/Water_cooling#Computer_usage" title="Water cooling">water cooling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nvidia" title="Nvidia">Nvidia</a> <a href="/wiki/GeForce_10_series" title="GeForce 10 series">GTX 1080</a> graphics, and HP specifically promoting it as being suitable for <a href="/wiki/Virtual_reality" title="Virtual reality">virtual reality</a> headsets), and other accessories (such as monitors) designed to cater to the market.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2017, HP acquired <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Electronics" title="Samsung Electronics">Samsung Electronics</a>' printer division for $1.05 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Hostile_takeover_bid_by_Xerox">Hostile takeover bid by Xerox</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=HP_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hostile takeover bid by Xerox" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On November 5, 2019, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a></i> reported that print and digital document company <a href="/wiki/Xerox" title="Xerox">Xerox</a> was contemplating acquiring HP.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The company unanimously rejected two unsolicited offers, including a cash-and-stock offer at $22 per-share.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">[10]</a></sup> HP stated that there was "uncertainty regarding Xerox’s ability to raise the cash portion of the proposed consideration" (especially given that Xerox is a smaller company in terms of market cap than HP), and noted the company's aggressiveness.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">[10]</a></sup> On November 26, 2019, Xerox issued a public letter defending allegations by HP that its offer was "uncertain" and "highly conditional", and declared its intent to "engage directly with HP shareholders to solicit their support in urging the HP Board to do the right thing and pursue this compelling opportunity."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>Xerox stated in January 2020 that it would propose the replacement of HP's board of directors during its next shareholder meeting in April 2020. In a statement to <i>TechCrunch</i>, HP disclosed a belief that Xerox's bid was being "driven by" <a href="/wiki/Activist_shareholder" title="Activist shareholder">activist shareholder</a> <a href="/wiki/Carl_Icahn" title="Carl Icahn">Carl Icahn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Xerox raised its bid to $24 per-share in February 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>On February 21, 2020, HP instituted a <a href="/wiki/Shareholder_rights_plan" title="Shareholder rights plan">shareholder rights plan</a> to fend off Xerox's pursuit of a <a href="/wiki/Hostile_takeover" class="mw-redirect" title="Hostile takeover">hostile takeover</a>. Four days later, HP announced that, if shareholders rejected the Xerox purchase, it planned on offering $16 billion in capital return between fiscal 2020 and 2022, including $8 billion in additional share buybacks and raising its "target long-term return of capital to 100% of free cash flow generation". HP criticized Xerox's bid as a "flawed value exchange" based on "overstated synergies".<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On March 5, 2020, HP rejected an offer at $24 per-share.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>On March 31, 2020, Xerox rescinded its bid to buy HP Inc, issuing in a statement, "The current global health crisis and resulting macroeconomic and market turmoil caused by COVID-19 have created an environment that is not conducive to Xerox continuing to pursue an acquisition of HP Inc."<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
