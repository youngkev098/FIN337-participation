<p><b>NVR, Inc.</b> is a company engaged in <a href="/wiki/Home_construction" title="Home construction">home construction</a>. It also operates a mortgage banking and <a href="/wiki/Title_(property)" title="Title (property)">title</a> services business. The company primarily operates on the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">East Coast of the United States</a>. In 2018, 23% of home settlements occurred in the <a href="/wiki/Washington_metropolitan_area" title="Washington metropolitan area">Washington metropolitan area</a> and 8% of home settlements occurred in the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_metropolitan_area" title="Baltimore metropolitan area">Baltimore metropolitan area</a>. The company operates under the Ryan Homes, NVHomes and Heartland Homes brands. The company typically does not engage in <a href="/wiki/Land_development" title="Land development">land development</a>; it acquires finished <a href="/wiki/Land_lot" title="Land lot">land lots</a> that are ready for building, which mitigates risk. </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=NVR,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company was formed in 1980 as NVHomes, Inc. by <a href="/wiki/Dwight_Schar" title="Dwight Schar">Dwight Schar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>In 1986, the company acquired Ryan Homes, which was founded in 1948 in <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh,_Pennsylvania" class="mw-redirect" title="Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania">Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania</a> to provide housing in the expanding post-war economy.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In April 1992, as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Early_1990s_recession" title="Early 1990s recession">early 1990s recession</a>, the company filed <a href="/wiki/Bankruptcy" title="Bankruptcy">bankruptcy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In November 1993, the company became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2012, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Heartland_Homes" title="Heartland Homes">Heartland Homes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2019, the company was added to the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500_Index" title="S&amp;P 500 Index">S&amp;P 500 Index</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Failure_to_provide_certain_amenities_in_Townsend.2C_Delaware"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Failure_to_provide_certain_amenities_in_Townsend,_Delaware">Failure to provide certain amenities in Townsend, Delaware</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=NVR,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Failure to provide certain amenities in Townsend, Delaware" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In February 2010, then <a href="/wiki/Attorney_General_of_Delaware" title="Attorney General of Delaware">Attorney General of Delaware</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beau_Biden" title="Beau Biden">Beau Biden</a>, sued the company for fraud, seeking damages of $10,000 for each of 39 homeowners alleging that the company did not provide promised amenities, primarily a clubhouse with restaurant and fitness center, for homeowners of a development built by the company in <a href="/wiki/Townsend,_Delaware" title="Townsend, Delaware">Townsend, Delaware</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The lawsuit was settled in April 2010, with the company agreeing to complete development of the promised amenities and reimburse homeowners for the period of time when they have been paying for promised amenities that they did not receive.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
