</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Steris was founded in August 1985 in Ohio,<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg-1">[1]</a></sup> under the name "Innovative Medical Technologies".<sup id="cite_ref-overview_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-overview-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012, Steris acquired US Endoscopy for $270 million. Steris also acquired Spectrum Surgical Instruments Corp. and Total Repair Express for $110 million combined.<sup id="cite_ref-dotmed_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dotmed-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>On April 1, 2014, Steris announced its acquisition of Integrated Medical Systems International Inc. for ~$175 million, although it would only cost the company ~$140 million after tax benefits.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>Shortly after, Steris also acquired <a href="/wiki/Chesterfield,_Missouri" title="Chesterfield, Missouri">Chesterfield, Missouri</a>-based Life Systems.<sup id="cite_ref-dotmed_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dotmed-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2014, Steris executed a <a href="/wiki/Tax_inversion" title="Tax inversion">tax inversion</a> from the United States to the United Kingdom, via an offer made to acquire UK-based <a href="/wiki/Synergy_Health" title="Synergy Health">Synergy Health</a> for $1.9 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-massdevice2015_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-massdevice2015-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FT_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FT-5">[5]</a></sup>  The tax inversion was notable as it was the first US inversion post the new rules introduced by the <a href="/wiki/Presidency_of_Barack_Obama" title="Presidency of Barack Obama">Obama administration</a> in 2014 to curb US corporations moving their "legal domicile" to reduce their exposure to US corporate taxation.<sup id="cite_ref-FT_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FT-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GUAR_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GUAR-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NYT_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>On June 24, 2015, Steris announced the acquisitions of General Econopak for $175 million and Black Diamond Video for $51 million.<sup id="cite_ref-massdevice2015_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-massdevice2015-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2018, Steris announced that it would further re-domicile its legal headquarters from the United Kingdom to Ireland; Steris had reduced its corporate tax rate from 31.3 percent to 20 percent as a result of its 2014 tax inversion to the United Kingdom, however, it believed a further reduction could be achieved by moving to Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Profile">Profile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Profile" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In 2018, Steris filed accounts which showed global revenues of over USD 2.6 billion, of which USD 1.8 billion (or 70 percent), came from the US healthcare system; and employed ~12,000 people.<sup id="cite_ref-2018report_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2018report-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-marketwatch_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-marketwatch-2">[2]</a></sup></p><p>Steris' competitors include <a href="/wiki/3M" title="3M">3M</a>, Belimed (<a href="/wiki/Metall_Zug" title="Metall Zug">Metall Zug</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Cantel_Medical_Corporation" title="Cantel Medical Corporation">Cantel Medical Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-owler_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-owler-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>As of 2020, the President and CEO is Walter M. Rosebrough.<sup id="cite_ref-marketwatch_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-marketwatch-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: See also" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
