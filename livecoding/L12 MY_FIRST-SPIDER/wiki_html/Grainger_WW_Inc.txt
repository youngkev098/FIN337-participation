W. (Bill) Grainger. He founded the company in order to provide consumers with access to a consistent supply of motors.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>  The company now serves more than 3 million customers worldwide<sup id="cite_ref-2017_Fact_Book_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2017_Fact_Book-3">[3]</a></sup> with offerings such as motors, lighting, material handling, fasteners, plumbing, tools, and safety supplies, along with inventory management services and technical support.  Revenue is generally from <a href="/wiki/Business-to-business" title="Business-to-business">business-to-business</a> sales rather than <a href="/wiki/Retail" title="Retail">retail</a> sales. </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=W._W._Grainger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company was founded as a supplier for businesses by William Wallace Grainger in 1927 in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, Illinois, and incorporated as W. W. Grainger, Inc. in 1928.<sup id="cite_ref-W.-W.-Grainger-Inc-Aug-1998-10-Q_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W.-W.-Grainger-Inc-Aug-1998-10-Q-6">[6]</a></sup> Sales in the early days were generated primarily through <a href="/wiki/Mail_order" title="Mail order">mail order</a> via <a href="/wiki/Postcard" title="Postcard">post cards</a> and a <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/catalogue" class="extiw" title="wikt:catalogue">catalog</a>. The MotorBook, as the catalog was originally called, was the basis for today's Grainger catalog.  Grainger headquarters are now located in <a href="/wiki/Lake_Forest,_Illinois" title="Lake Forest, Illinois">Lake Forest, Illinois</a>. By 1936, Grainger had established 15 branches to improve customer service.
</p><p>In 1967, Grainger became a <a href="/wiki/Publicly_traded_company" class="mw-redirect" title="Publicly traded company">publicly traded company</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-W.-W.-Grainger-Inc-Mar-2018-DEF-14A_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W.-W.-Grainger-Inc-Mar-2018-DEF-14A-7">[7]</a></sup> Grainger is a profitable corporation and has increased <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/dividend#English" class="extiw" title="wikt:dividend">dividends</a> to its <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/shareholder" class="extiw" title="wikt:shareholder">shareholders</a> for forty five consecutive years.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The company has grown consistently since becoming public and passed <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>10 billion in annual sales in 2016.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Digital">Digital</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=W._W._Grainger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Digital" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In 1996 the grainger.com website was launched with an electronic catalog, later evolving to an eCommerce platform. In 2016, Grainger was named the 11th largest eCommerce retailer in North America by Internet Retailer.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> As businesses continued to shift from ordering through traditional channels, such as using phones or visiting branches, to online and onsite channels including websites, eProcurement platforms and inventory management systems, Grainger evolved. In 2016, more than 65 percent of Grainger orders originated via a digital channel (including Grainger.com, inventory management systems, and eProcurement) and more than 85 percent of orders were shipped directly to the customer or made immediately available through onsite services.<sup id="cite_ref-2017_Fact_Book_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2017_Fact_Book-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2016, more than 50% of total company revenue was from a digital channel.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Subsidiaries">Subsidiaries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=W._W._Grainger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Subsidiaries" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>Grainger's subsidiary <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zoro_Tools&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Zoro Tools (page does not exist)">Zoro Tools, Inc</a>. and website zoro.com was launched in 2011 to serve the US small business market through a web-only transaction-based model,<sup id="cite_ref-2017_Fact_Book_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2017_Fact_Book-3">[3]</a></sup> while grainger.com serves primarily large and mid-sized customers.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Monotaro" title="Monotaro">Monotaro</a> was established in 2000 as a joint venture between Grainger and <a href="/wiki/Sumitomo" class="mw-redirect" title="Sumitomo">Sumitomo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> It has online-only operations serving Japan and other Asian markets.
</p>
