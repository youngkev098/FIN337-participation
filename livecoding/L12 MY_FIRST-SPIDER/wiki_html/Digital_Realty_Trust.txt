</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Digital_Realty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company was formed in 2004 by <a href="/wiki/GI_Partners" title="GI Partners">GI Partners</a>, which contributed 21 data centers that it acquired through bankruptcy auctions and from distressed companies at a 20–40% discount to replacement cost.<sup id="cite_ref-gip_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gip-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>On November 4, 2004, the company became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ipo_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ipo-4">[4]</a></sup> At that time, the company owned 23 properties comprising 5.6 million square feet.<sup id="cite_ref-ipo_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ipo-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2006, the company acquired a property in <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix, Arizona</a> for $175 million.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>By March 2007, GI Partners had sold all of its shares in the company.<sup id="cite_ref-gip_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gip-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2010, the company acquired 3 data centers in Massachusetts and Connecticut for $375 million.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2012, the company acquired a 334,000 square foot data center near <a href="/wiki/Hartsfield-Jackson_Atlanta_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport">Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport</a> for $63 million in a <a href="/wiki/Leaseback" title="Leaseback">leaseback</a> transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The company also acquired a data center in San Francisco for $85 million.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2013, the company acquired the data center of <a href="/wiki/Delta_Air_Lines" title="Delta Air Lines">Delta Air Lines</a> in <a href="/wiki/Eagan,_Minnesota" title="Eagan, Minnesota">Eagan, Minnesota</a> for $37 million in a <a href="/wiki/Leaseback" title="Leaseback">leaseback</a> transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2013, the company doubled capacity at its data center in <a href="/wiki/Chandler,_Arizona" title="Chandler, Arizona">Chandler, Arizona</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2015, the company sold a building in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> for $161 million that it acquired in 2005 for $59 million.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2015, the company acquired Telx for $1.886 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2015, the company acquired 125.9 acres of undeveloped land in <a href="/wiki/Loudoun_County,_Virginia" title="Loudoun County, Virginia">Loudoun County, Virginia</a> for $43 million and announced plans to build a 2 million square foot data center on the property.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2016, the company acquired 8 data centers in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> from <a href="/wiki/Equinix" title="Equinix">Equinix</a> for $874 million.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2017, the company announced a $22 million expansion of its data center in Atlanta.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2017, the company completed the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/DuPont_Fabros_Technology" title="DuPont Fabros Technology">DuPont Fabros Technology</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_States" title="Supreme Court of the United States">Supreme Court</a> ruled in favor of the company in <i><a href="/wiki/Digital_Realty_Trust,_Inc._v._Somers" title="Digital Realty Trust, Inc. v. Somers"> Digital Realty Trust, Inc. v. Somers</a></i>, a case in which Digital Realty fired an employee who had complained internally about the elimination of supervisory controls and the hiding of cost overruns. After he was fired, the employee sued the company, saying he was protected by <a href="/wiki/Whistleblower" title="Whistleblower">whistleblower</a> provisions in <a href="/wiki/Dodd%E2%80%93Frank_Wall_Street_Reform_and_Consumer_Protection_Act" title="Dodd–Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act">Dodd-Frank</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> However the Supreme Court, citing specific language in Dodd-Frank, ruled that these protections only applied to whistleblowers who had first notified the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission"> SEC</a>.
</p><p>In December 2018, the company acquired Ascenty for $1.8 billion. At the time, Ascenty operated eight data centers in Brazil.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2019, Digital Realty announced the acquisition of European data center provider <a href="/wiki/Interxion" title="Interxion">Interxion</a> for $8.4 billion to “create a leading global provider of data centre, colocation and interconnection solutions”.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
