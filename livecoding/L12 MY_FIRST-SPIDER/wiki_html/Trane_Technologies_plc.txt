<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">This article is about the Irish-domiciled company formerly known as Ingersoll-Rand plc. For the American company currently known as Ingersoll Rand Inc., see <a href="/wiki/Ingersoll_Rand" title="Ingersoll Rand">Ingersoll Rand</a>.</div> 
<p><b>Trane Technologies plc</b> (formerly <b>Ingersoll-Rand plc</b>) is an Irish-domiciled diversified industrial manufacturing company formed in 1905 by the merger of Ingersoll-Sergeant Drill Company and Rand Drill Company. It is headquartered near Dublin, Ireland.

<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ingersoll-Sergeant_steam_drill_Fig_7_WBClark_1898.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Ingersoll-Sergeant_steam_drill_Fig_7_WBClark_1898.jpg/220px-Ingersoll-Sergeant_steam_drill_Fig_7_WBClark_1898.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="316" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1089" data-file-height="1563"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 316px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Ingersoll-Sergeant_steam_drill_Fig_7_WBClark_1898.jpg/220px-Ingersoll-Sergeant_steam_drill_Fig_7_WBClark_1898.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="316" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ingersoll-Sergeant_steam_drill_Fig_7_WBClark_1898.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ingersoll-Sergeant steam drill used in mining in the late 1800s</div></div></div>
<p>In 1871, <a href="/wiki/Simon_Ingersoll" title="Simon Ingersoll">Simon Ingersoll</a> (1818–1894) founded Ingersoll Rock Drill Company in New York,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and in 1888 it combined with Sergeant Drill to form Ingersoll Sergeant Drill Company. Also in 1871, brothers Addison Rand (1841–1900) and Jasper Rand, Jr. (1837–1909) established Rand Drill Company with its main manufacturing plant in <a href="/wiki/Tarrytown,_New_York" title="Tarrytown, New York">Tarrytown, New York</a>. Rand drills cleared New York's treacherous <a href="/wiki/Hell_Gate" title="Hell Gate">Hell Gate</a> channel and were used in the construction of water aqueducts for New York City and Washington, D.C., and tunnels in <a href="/wiki/Haverstraw,_New_York" title="Haverstraw, New York">Haverstraw</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Point,_New_York" title="West Point, New York">West Point, New York</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/Weehawken,_New_Jersey" title="Weehawken, New Jersey">Weehawken, New Jersey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1905 Ingersoll-Sergeant Drill Company merged with the Rand Drill Company to form Ingersoll Rand. Ingersoll Rand has grown largely by acquisition since that time.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Diesel_engines">Diesel engines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trane_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Diesel engines" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In the 1920s, Ingersoll Rand supplied <a href="/wiki/Diesel_engine" title="Diesel engine">diesel engines</a> for locomotives built by both General Electric and ALCO.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="21st_century">21st century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trane_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 21st century" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In October 2002, shareholders voted to move the company's <a href="/wiki/Incorporation_(business)" title="Incorporation (business)">incorporation</a> to <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> to capitalize on the savings on U.S. corporate <a href="/wiki/Income_tax" title="Income tax">income taxes</a> on products sold overseas.  Moving the company on paper cost only US$27,000 per year with a <a href="/wiki/Tax_haven" title="Tax haven">tax savings</a> estimated at US$40 million. Ingersoll Rand announced in March 2009 its intention to relocate its offices from Bermuda to Ireland, a decision which shareholders approved in a vote.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2004, the Drilling Solutions business was sold to the Swedish company <a href="/wiki/Atlas_Copco" title="Atlas Copco">Atlas Copco</a>. This included factories in United States, China, Japan making above ground rotary blasthole and deephole drilling machines. Atlas Copco purchased the company for US$225M. Drilling Solutions was a legacy business from the company's founding.
</p><p>In May 2007, the company announced it was looking into a sale or spin-off of its <a href="/wiki/Bobcat_(equipment)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bobcat (equipment)">Bobcat</a>, utility equipment, and attachments divisions.  With this divestiture, Ingersoll Rand was left with the industrial technologies, climate control technologies, and security technologies sectors.  This completed the transformation from the diversified machinery label to a diversified industrial company.
</p><p>In February 2007, <a href="/wiki/Volvo" title="Volvo">Volvo</a>, a Swedish truck and construction equipment manufacturer, announced its agreement to buy the road construction equipment division of Ingersoll-Rand for $1.3 billion in cash to expand its operations in the United States. The road unit manufactures and sells asphalt paving equipment, compaction equipment, milling machines and construction-related material handling equipment and generated net revenues of approximately $850 million for 2006. The sale included manufacturing facilities in Pennsylvania, Germany, China and India, as well as 20 distribution and service facilities in the United States. The business employs approximately 2,000 people worldwide.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>On 30 July 2007, Ingersoll Rand announced that the utility and attachment businesses had been sold to Doosan Infracore, part of the South Korean <a href="/wiki/Chaebol" title="Chaebol">chaebol</a> <a href="/wiki/Doosan" class="mw-redirect" title="Doosan">Doosan</a>, for US$4.9 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>On 17 December 2007, Ingersoll Rand announced an offer to purchase <a href="/wiki/HVAC" class="mw-redirect" title="HVAC">HVAC</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Trane" title="Trane">Trane</a>, in a stock and cash transaction. The purchase was approved by Trane's stockholders, and the unit became a part of the climate control technologies business, which is divided into commercial and residential business units, each reporting directly to the chairman.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The sale was completed on 5 June 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>On 2 December 2013,  Ingersoll Rand completed the spinoff of its security hardware sector into a standalone business named <a href="/wiki/Allegion" title="Allegion">Allegion</a>, which maintains commercial and residential security hardware brands such as Schlage, Von Duprin, LCN, Bricard, Interflex, Normbau and CISA.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2014, it was announced that Ingersoll-Rand would acquire the centrifugal compression unit of <a href="/wiki/Cameron_International" title="Cameron International">Cameron International</a> for $850 million.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2017, the <a href="/wiki/Asthma_and_Allergy_Foundation_of_America" title="Asthma and Allergy Foundation of America">Asthma and Allergy Foundation of America</a> and Allergy Standards Limited<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> announced that the Trane CleanEffects™ whole home air cleaner from Ingersoll Rand has earned the <a href="/wiki/Asthma_and_Allergy_Friendly" title="Asthma and Allergy Friendly">Asthma and Allergy Friendly</a> Certification. This is the first whole home air cleaner to receive this Certification.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2018, Ingersoll Rand received takeover interest in its power tools business, which was slated to be worth up to $750 million in a potential sale. Power Tools is the oldest business with least revenue, and the decision of whether to sell this unit or not has not been announced.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_affairs">Corporate affairs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trane_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Corporate affairs" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Trane Technologies's headquarters are in <a href="/wiki/Swords,_Dublin" title="Swords, Dublin">Swords</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>. Its European headquarters are in <a href="/wiki/Sint-Stevens-Woluwe" title="Sint-Stevens-Woluwe">Sint-Stevens-Woluwe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zaventem" title="Zaventem">Zaventem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. Its Asia headquarters are in Tower B of City Center of Shanghai in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
