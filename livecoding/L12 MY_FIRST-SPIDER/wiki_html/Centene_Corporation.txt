</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_segments">Business segments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Centene_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Business segments" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The main lines of Centene include:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Medicaid" title="Medicaid">Medicaid</a>, where it is the largest provider in the country as of 2018<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Patient_Protection_and_Affordable_Care_Act" title="Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act">ACA</a> <a href="/wiki/Health_insurance_marketplace" title="Health insurance marketplace">health insurance marketplace</a> plans under the Ambetter brand in 15 states as of 2018<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Medicare_(United_States)" title="Medicare (United States)">Medicare</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tricare" title="Tricare">Tricare</a></li>
<li>traditional commercial insurance.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li></ul><p>Centene also contracts with other healthcare and commercial organizations to provide specialty services, including behavioral health care services, case management software, correctional insurance, in-home health services, life and health management, vision, pharmacy benefits management, and telehealth services.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Centene_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Founded as a single health plan by Elizabeth “Betty” Brinn in <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee" title="Milwaukee">Milwaukee, Wisconsin</a> in 1984, Centene has established itself as a national leader in healthcare services.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Chairman, President and CEO Michael F. Neidorff joined the company in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-centene.com_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-centene.com-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>Over the past 35 years, Centene has grown to become a $60 billion member of the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> trading on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>. With over 33,000 employees across the country, Centene operates health plans that serve 12.3 million members in 29 states and 2 international markets and offers health insurance to other healthcare and commercial organizations.<sup id="cite_ref-centene.com_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-centene.com-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>On July 2, 2015, Centene announced it would acquire <a href="/wiki/Health_Net" title="Health Net">Health Net</a>. At announcement, it was estimated Health Net shareholders would own 29% of the combined company.  The merger was finalized in March 2016 and Health Net shareholders received 0.622 shares of Centene common stock and $28.25 in cash for each share at closing, making the total transaction value about $6 billion, including assumption of debt. After its acquisition of Health Net, Centene now provides healthcare to over 11 million people.<sup id="cite_ref-centene.com_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-centene.com-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>Centene currently ranks #51 on the Fortune 500 &amp; #244 on the Fortune Global 500 List."<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On September 12, 2017, it was announced that Centene Corp. would acquire the nonprofit insurer <a href="/wiki/Fidelis_Care" title="Fidelis Care">Fidelis Care</a> for US $3.75 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-centene_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-centene-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>On March 27, 2019, Centene acquired <a href="/wiki/WellCare" title="WellCare">WellCare</a> for US $17 billion. As of 2020, Centene is the largest provider of government-sponsored health plans with roughly 22 million members.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In April of 2019, Centene announced a partnership with the <a href="/wiki/Washington_University_School_of_Medicine" title="Washington University School of Medicine">Washington University School of Medicine</a> designed to accelerate research into treatments for Alzheimer’s disease, breast cancer, diabetes and obesity. As part of the partnership, Centene will fund up to $100 million over 10 years in research at <a href="/wiki/Washington_University_in_St._Louis" title="Washington University in St. Louis">Washington University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Centene_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Controversies" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<p>In a Fox News article dated July 9, 2014, Superior HealthPlan of Texas was alleged to have denied coverage of an infant's surgery for brain cancer at <a href="/wiki/Texas_Children%27s_Hospital" title="Texas Children's Hospital">Texas Children's Hospital</a> where it was alleged to have previously paid claims for the infant.  Part of the source of the controversy lay in reports that that facility had a notably higher survival rate for such operations.  Superior HealthPlan initially claimed that it did not cover Texas Children's Hospital and that it has directed the family to a different facility.  Superior HealthPlan said it was "confident" the family would find capable in-network providers.
</p><p>However, three days later, on July 12, 2014, Fox News reported that Superior HealthPlan had reversed its decision and had consented to cover the needed surgery at Texas Children's Hospital. In that follow-up article, it was reported that the surgery was successful.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, a class action lawsuit was filed against Centene Corp. regarding their Ambetter marketplace healthcare plans, alleging that the company has misled customers with physician coverage networks that are narrower than advertised.<sup id="cite_ref-Healthcare_dive_nes_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Healthcare_dive_nes-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bloomberg_suit_article_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bloomberg_suit_article-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
