Hampshire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup> It is the second-largest full-service bank in <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a>, one of the largest in the <a href="/wiki/Northeastern_United_States" title="Northeastern United States">northeast</a>, and the 46th-largest in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-2" </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=People%27s_United_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company was founded in 1842 as Bridgeport Savings Bank in <a href="/wiki/Bridgeport,_Connecticut" title="Bridgeport, Connecticut">Bridgeport, Connecticut</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-charter_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-charter-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1955, the company acquired Southport Savings Bank.<sup id="cite_ref-change_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-change-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1981, the bank became the first in the country to provide a telephone bill paying service. In 1981, the company acquired the assets of First Stamford Bank and Trust Company.<sup id="cite_ref-change_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-change-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ct_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ct-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1982, the company acquired Guardian Federal Savings and Loan Association of Bridgeport.<sup id="cite_ref-change_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-change-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ct_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ct-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1983, the company acquired People's Bank of Vernon and was renamed People's Bank.<sup id="cite_ref-change_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-change-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ct_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ct-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1985, the company began issuing credit cards.<sup id="cite_ref-change_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-change-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1986, the company acquired First Federal Savings Bank.<sup id="cite_ref-change_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-change-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ct_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ct-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1989, the company moved to a new headquarters building designed by <a href="/wiki/Richard_Meier" title="Richard Meier">Richard Meier</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-change_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-change-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1991, the company acquired the deposits of Hartford-based Landmark Bank.<sup id="cite_ref-change_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-change-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1998, the company acquired Norwich Financial Corporation for $164 million in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2004, the bank sold its credit card division to the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Bank_of_Scotland" title="Royal Bank of Scotland">Royal Bank of Scotland</a> at a premium of $360 million.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On November 22, 2010, the bank opened its first location in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On June 7, 2007, the bank changed its name to People's United Bank.<sup id="cite_ref-charter_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-charter-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-change_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-change-4">[4]</a></sup> On January 1, 2008, the bank acquired <a href="/wiki/Chittenden_Corporation" title="Chittenden Corporation">Chittenden Corporation</a> for $1.9 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Also in 2008, the bank acquired Ocean Bank of New Hampshire, Maine Bank &amp; Trust, Merrill Bank, Flagship Bank, and Bank of Western Massachusetts.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In February 2008, a data storage company, Archive America, lost backup tapes in transit containing the "names, birthdays, Social Security numbers and other information" of customers of the bank and of <a href="/wiki/The_Bank_of_New_York_Mellon" title="The Bank of New York Mellon">The Bank of New York Mellon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In February 2010, the company acquired Financial Federal Corporation, an equipment financing company.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In April 2010, the company acquired Butler Bank, which was seized by the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Deposit_Insurance_Corporation" title="Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation">Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In December 2010, the company acquired LSB Corporation and Smithtown Bancorp.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In July 2011, the company acquired Danvers Bancorp.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In 2012, the company acquired 57 branches in the greater New York metro area from <a href="/wiki/Royal_Bank_of_Scotland" title="Royal Bank of Scotland">Royal Bank of Scotland</a> , including 53 branches in <a href="/wiki/Stop_%26_Shop" title="Stop &amp; Shop">Stop &amp; Shop</a> supermarkets.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In October 2015, the company's insurance agency subsidiary acquired Kesten-Brown Insurance.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In November 2016, the bank was selected to manage the core bank accounts for the commonwealth of <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In April 2017, the company acquired Suffolk Bancorp and in July, it acquired LEAF Commercial Capital.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> In August 2017, the bank was selected to manage the core bank accounts for the state of <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In June 2018, People's United Bank agreed to purchase <a href="/wiki/Farmington_Bank" title="Farmington Bank">Farmington Bank</a> in a deal valued at $544 million.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>

At 16 stories, the People's Bank building (known officially as Bridgeport Center) the tallest building in downtown <a href="/wiki/Bridgeport,_Connecticut" title="Bridgeport, Connecticut">Bridgeport</a>, although that is expected to change due to two new residential highrises planned for 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
