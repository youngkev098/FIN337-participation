</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Business">Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Payments&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Business" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Global Payments provides payment processing services to <a href="/wiki/Merchant" title="Merchant">merchants</a>, allowing them to accept credit and debit cards, along with other payment types (this role is known as a <a href="/wiki/Acquiring_bank" title="Acquiring bank">merchant acquirer</a>).  In return, they receive a percentage of the transaction value (usually about 1-2% for credit cards).  They provide these services both directly to merchants, and indirectly through other financial organizations.  Global Payments also provides other supporting ("vertical") services and analytics ("omnichannel") to customers of its main payment processing services.  
</p><p>As of 2017, 40% of revenue was derived from vertical and omnichannel services.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>  As of 2016, 75% of Global Payments revenue was from <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, 18% was from <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, and 7% was from <a href="/wiki/Asia-Pacific" title="Asia-Pacific">Asia-Pacific</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>  Global Payments processes approximately 6 billion transactions per year for 1 million merchant locations.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>   Global Payments has 8500 employees.
</p><p>It is a <a href="/wiki/Fortune_1000" title="Fortune 1000">Fortune 1000</a> company.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Payments&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Global Payments was founded in 2000 and spun off from National Data Corporation, its former parent company, in 2001.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>  Global Payments has been an independent, publicly-traded company on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> having the ticker symbol “GPN” since its spin off.   
</p><p>In 2009, it paid $75 million for United Card Service, Russia's leading credit card processing company.<sup id="cite_ref-Filkins2018.10.15_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Filkins2018.10.15-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 2011, Global Payments's United Card Service bought <a href="/wiki/Alfa-Bank" title="Alfa-Bank">Alfa-Bank</a>'s credit card processing unit.<sup id="cite_ref-Filkins2018.10.15_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Filkins2018.10.15-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2012, it acquired the smaller Accelerated Payment Technologies for $413 million.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2014, it purchased Australian payment processing company Ezidebit for $305 million.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2015, it bought Payment Processing (also known as PayPros), a <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> company, for $420 million.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>On April 25, 2016, Global Payments completed the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Heartland_Payment_Systems" title="Heartland Payment Systems">Heartland Payment Systems</a><sup id="cite_ref-Filkins2018.10.15_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Filkins2018.10.15-8">[8]</a></sup> for $4.3 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Both Global Payments and its subsidiary Heartland Payment Systems were among the leading credit card processing companies in 2016, according to <a href="/wiki/Business_Insider" title="Business Insider">Business Insider</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>  Its headquarters moved from <a href="/wiki/Sandy_Springs,_Georgia" title="Sandy Springs, Georgia">Sandy Springs</a>, Georgia to <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, Georgia in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, Global Payments initiated acquiring divisions of Active Networks.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, Global Payments completed acquisition of AdvancedMD.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, Global Payments completed acquisition of Sentral Education.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>On May 28, 2019, Global Payments announced a $21.5 billion merger with <a href="/wiki/TSYS" title="TSYS">TSYS</a>. The merger is expected to trigger a Federal Trade Commission investigation.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Payments&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Controversy" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>In 2012, a data breach at Global Payments affected 1.5 million credit and debit card numbers.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>  This breach eventually cost the company around $100 million.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>  In 2015, a technical fault with Global Payments's systems led to thousands of British businesses being unable to accept credit card payments on <a href="/wiki/Valentine%27s_Day" title="Valentine's Day">Valentine's Day</a> weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
