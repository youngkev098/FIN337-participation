</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aptiv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The business was established as the <b>Automotive Components Group</b> in 1994 and changed its name to <b>Delphi Automotive Systems</b> in 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Delphi disclosed some irregular accounting practices in 2005. A number of executives, including CFO Alan Dawes, resigned. Delphi Chairman <a href="/wiki/J.T._Battenberg" title="J.T. Battenberg">J.T. Battenberg</a> retired. Delphi then filed for <a href="/wiki/Chapter_11,_Title_11,_United_States_Code" title="Chapter 11, Title 11, United States Code">Chapter 11 bankruptcy</a> protection to reorganize its struggling U.S. operations.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> As a result of this action, the <a href="/wiki/Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" class="mw-redirect" title="Securities and Exchange Commission">Securities and Exchange Commission</a> granted an application by the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> to <a href="/wiki/Delisting_(stock)" class="mw-redirect" title="Delisting (stock)">delist</a> Delphi's <a href="/wiki/Common_stock" title="Common stock">common stock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bond_(finance)" title="Bond (finance)">bonds</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>Plants in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Real" title="Puerto Real">Puerto Real</a>, <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A1diz_(province)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cádiz (province)">Cádiz</a>, Spain, closed, with a loss of 1,600 direct jobs, and more than 2,500 indirect jobs in February 2007,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> despite having agreed to continue its manufacturing operations until 2010 and receiving more than €25 million from various public administrations in order to guarantee its workers' jobs.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Regional_Government_of_Andalusia" title="Regional Government of Andalusia">Regional Government of Andalusia</a> announced it would begin legal action against the company for breach of local labor laws.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>Delphi sued its investors for US$2.55 billion in securities to aid Delphi as it sought to come out of <a href="/wiki/Bankruptcy" title="Bankruptcy">bankruptcy</a> in May 2008. U.S. Bankruptcy Judge Robert Drain in <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> allowed Delphi to seek payments through a contract against <a href="/wiki/Appaloosa_Management" title="Appaloosa Management">Appaloosa Management LP</a> as well as denying an investors' request for a cap of $250 million for damages.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In April 2009 CoolIT Systems announced the acquisition of the assets of Delphi Thermal Liquid Cooling including intellectual property, machinery, and equipment.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>Delphi's core assets were purchased by a group of private investors to create a new Delphi Corporation in October 2009. Some of its non-core steering operations sold to <a href="/wiki/General_Motors_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="General Motors Company">General Motors Company</a>, the successor to the bankrupt <a href="/wiki/Motors_Liquidation_Company" title="Motors Liquidation Company">Motors Liquidation Company</a> that used to be the old General Motors Corporation. The stock was cancelled. The old Delphi Corporation was renamed DPH Holdings Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The new Delphi incorporated in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>Delphi sold its Thermal Business unit to Mahle-Behr GmbH in July 2015. Together, the Mahle-Behr and Delphi Thermal merger represented the second largest supplier of automotive thermal management systems including interior HVAC components, under-hood powertrain cooling and compressors.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The company announced improvements to self driving technology under development in December 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In the same month Delphi bought <a href="/wiki/HellermannTyton" title="HellermannTyton">HellermannTyton</a> for 1.7 Billion.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>Delphi entered into a partnership agreement with <a href="/wiki/Carbon_(company)" title="Carbon (company)">Carbon</a> in June 2016 to allow use of Carbon's <a href="/wiki/Continuous_Liquid_Interface_Production" title="Continuous Liquid Interface Production">Continuous Liquid Interface Production</a> technology and printers.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The company then bought the self-driving startup <a href="/wiki/NuTonomy" title="NuTonomy">NuTonomy</a> for $450 Million in October 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The company spun off its powertrain division and aftermarket related businesses (now <a href="/wiki/Delphi_Technologies" title="Delphi Technologies">Delphi Technologies</a>) in December 2017 and changed its name to <b>Aptiv PLC</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aptiv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Operations" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>As of December 2017<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Aptiv&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, Aptiv has two diversified business segments:<sup id="cite_ref-10K_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<ul><li>"Signal and Power Solutions (Formerly Electrical/Electronic Architecture)" provides complete vehicle electrical systems, integrating wiring and cable assemblies, electrical centers and connection systems.</li>
<li>"Advanced Safety and User Experience (Formerly Electronics &amp; Safety)" provides advanced software and sensing systems, computing platforms, advanced safety systems and automated driving, user experience and infotainment, as well as other vehicular electronic controls.</li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aptiv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Controversies" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>On 4 March 2005, Delphi said it had fired its CFO and would restate earnings between 1999, when Delphi spun off from General Motors Corp (GM), and 2004 for improper reporting of rebates, credits, or other payments from suppliers.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>  
In June, 2006, Delphi said in a filing that it would restate its 2005 report, which would increase Delphi's reported 2004 net loss by $65 million. In 2013, Delphi became involved in an ongoing lawsuit against GM, because it manufactures ignition switches for the Chevrolet Cobalt, whose original design is alleged to be defective.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In 2009, as a result of its bankruptcy agreement, "Delphi surrendered its pension obligations to the <a href="/wiki/Pension_Benefit_Guaranty_Corporation" title="Pension Benefit Guaranty Corporation">Pension Benefit Guaranty Corp</a>." A group of about 20,000 salaried employees, principally in <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>, have been involved in litigation since then seeking restoration of their full pension rights.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Joint_ventures">Joint ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aptiv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Joint ventures" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<ul><li>EnerDel – started as a joint venture of <a href="/wiki/Ener1" title="Ener1">Ener1</a> and Delphi. In August 2008, Ener1 bought exclusive ownership of EnerDel.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
<li>Condumex – A <a href="/wiki/Grupo_Carso" title="Grupo Carso">Grupo Carso</a> division</li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_record">Environmental record</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aptiv&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Environmental record" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<p>Researchers at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Massachusetts_Amherst" title="University of Massachusetts Amherst">University of Massachusetts Amherst</a> identified Delphi corp. as the 21st-largest corporate producer of <a href="/wiki/Air_pollution" title="Air pollution">air pollution</a> in the United States in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> According to the study, the manufacturer's most toxic emissions included <a href="/wiki/Asbestos" title="Asbestos">asbestos</a> (542 lb/yr), <a href="/wiki/Chromium" title="Chromium">chromium</a> compounds (1,082 lb/yr), <a href="/wiki/Lead" title="Lead">lead</a> compounds (8,466 lb/yr), and <a href="/wiki/Sulfuric_acid" title="Sulfuric acid">sulfuric acid</a> (17,600 lbs/year), while the most massive emissions were <a href="/wiki/Glycol_ethers" title="Glycol ethers">glycol ethers</a> (111,520 lbs/year) and <a href="/wiki/Hydrochloric_acid" title="Hydrochloric acid">hydrochloric acid</a> (80,000 lb/yr).<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
