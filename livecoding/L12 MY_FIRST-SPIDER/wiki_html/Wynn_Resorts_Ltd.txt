</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wynn_Resorts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>In 2000, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Wynn" title="Steve Wynn">Steve Wynn</a> agreed to sell <a href="/wiki/Mirage_Resorts" title="Mirage Resorts">Mirage Resorts</a> to <a href="/wiki/MGM_Grand_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="MGM Grand Inc.">MGM Grand</a>, after having led Mirage and its predecessors since 1973.  Wynn laid the foundation for his next venture that same year, buying the <a href="/wiki/Desert_Inn" title="Desert Inn">Desert Inn</a> for $270 million.<sup id="cite_ref-palmeri_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-palmeri-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>Wynn found an early partner in Japanese billionaire Kazuo Okada of <a href="/wiki/Universal_Entertainment_Corporation" title="Universal Entertainment Corporation">Universal Entertainment Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bradsher_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bradsher-5">[5]</a></sup> Wynn, together with Universal subsidiary Aruze USA, controlled almost half the stock, making it harder for outside investors to exert control, as they had done at Mirage in response to Wynn's prolific spending.<sup id="cite_ref-palmeri_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-palmeri-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sieroty_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sieroty-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>Wynn hired investment banker <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Kramer_(business)" title="Ronald Kramer (business)">Ronald Kramer</a> as president and director in 2002 in order to help take the company public and oversee its expansion.<sup id="cite_ref-kramer_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kramer-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>Wynn Resorts made its <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> on <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a> on October 25, 2002. The company's first project, <a href="/wiki/Wynn_Las_Vegas" title="Wynn Las Vegas">Wynn Las Vegas</a>, opened on April 28, 2005.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Wynn_Macau" title="Wynn Macau">Wynn Macau</a>, the company's second project started construction on June 28, 2004. It opened September 5, 2006 and is now the largest-grossing casino in the region.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Encore_Suites" class="mw-redirect" title="Encore Suites">Encore</a>, an extension to Wynn Las Vegas, broke ground on April 28, 2006, the first anniversary of the opening of Wynn Las Vegas. Encore at <a href="/wiki/Wynn_Macau" title="Wynn Macau">Wynn Macau</a>, the company's second project on the <a href="/wiki/Macau_Peninsula" title="Macau Peninsula">Macau Peninsula</a>, <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a>, <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>, opened on April 21, 2010. A second resort in Macau, <a href="/wiki/Wynn_Palace" title="Wynn Palace">Wynn Palace</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Cotai_Strip" title="Cotai Strip">Cotai Strip</a>, opened August 22, 2016.
</p><p>Wynn bought 52 acres (21 ha) of the Cotai Strip in Macau where he hoped to break ground in 2012 for Wynn Cotai for a 2013 or early 2014 opening.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (November 2019)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>The company considered opening a casino as part of the <a href="/wiki/Entertainment_City" title="Entertainment City">Entertainment City</a> development in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, but decided against it because of corruption in the country's gaming industry.<sup id="cite_ref-sieroty_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sieroty-6">[6]</a></sup> Okada decided to proceed with the project alone, leading to the rupture of his partnership with Wynn. The dispute went public with reciprocal accusations of corrupt practices. Wynn and his allies accused Okada of bribing Philippines gaming regulators with over $110,000 in benefits, including free nights in a luxury suite at Wynn Las Vegas, while Okada claimed that Wynn Resorts' record $135 million donation to the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Macau" title="University of Macau">University of Macau</a> constituted a bribe.<sup id="cite_ref-Bradsher_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bradsher-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Okada resigned as vice chairman in October 2011, and the following February, after a company investigation by the <a href="/wiki/Freeh_Group_International_Solutions" title="Freeh Group International Solutions">Freeh Group</a> confirmed the charges against him, the board of directors forced Aruze USA to sell back its shares at a nearly 30% discount.<sup id="cite_ref-Bradsher_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bradsher-5">[5]</a></sup> Aruze had been the company's largest shareholder, with a 19.7% stake.
</p><p>On September 17, 2014, the Massachusetts Gaming Commission voted to approve Wynn Resorts’ proposed $1.6 billion casino to be located in <a href="/wiki/Everett,_Massachusetts" title="Everett, Massachusetts">Everett, Massachusetts</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Mystic_River" title="Mystic River">Mystic River</a>, just north of <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>. This project will be known as Encore Boston Harbor and was scheduled to open in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>On February 6, 2018, Steve Wynn resigned as CEO of the company amidst sexual allegations and was replaced by Matthew Maddox.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Amid sexual harassment lawsuits involving directors, Maddox announced the planned departure of two board members, Ray Irani and Alvin Shoemaker, on March 7, 2018. The departure of Irani and Shoemaker was to reassure investors that the corporate culture was changing.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In April 2018, the company nominated three women to the board of directors: Dee Dee Myers, Betsy Atkins, and Wendy Webb.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>Talks between Wynn and Crown Resorts of Australia for a $7.1 billion offer collapsed in April 2019; analysts saw the bid as an attempt to diversify away from Macau, where its license may expire in 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>On May 28, 2019 Wynn paid a <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">$</a> 35 million fine to the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> Gaming Commission and the $500,000 fine placed on Matthew Maddox.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Economic_performance">Economic performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wynn_Resorts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Economic performance" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>The Encore in Las Vegas opened shortly after the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Recession" title="Great Recession">Great Recession</a>. Despite the additional casino at the Encore (which added 97 tables and 857 slot machines to the original 190 tables and 2000 slots), net casino revenues for the combined resort were initially lower. Similarly, in the first quarter of 2009, net revenues of the Macau operations declined. Following the global economic recovery, performance of the Wynn properties also recovered. Wynn remained the only gaming operator to not perform mass layoffs during the recession.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In 2017 CNBC reported that Wynn’s stock was up 44% for the year and that Wynn’s Macau operations were the leading US-based resorts there.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
