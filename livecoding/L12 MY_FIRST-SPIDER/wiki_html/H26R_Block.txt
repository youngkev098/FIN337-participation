The company was founded in 1955 by brothers <a href="/wiki/Henry_W._Bloch" title="Henry W. Bloch">Henry W. Bloch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Richard_Bloch" title="Richard Bloch">Richard Bloch</a>.

It also offers consumer tax software as well as online tax preparation and <a href="/wiki/IRS_e-file" title="IRS e-file">electronic filing</a> from their website. The <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City</a>–based company also offers <a href="/wiki/Payroll" title="Payroll">payroll</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Business_consulting" class="mw-redirect" title="Business consulting">business consulting</a> services.
</p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=H%26R_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Founding">Founding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=H%26R_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Founding" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henry_W._Bloch" title="Henry W. Bloch">Henry W. Bloch</a> was a young <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Forces" title="United States Army Air Forces">Army Air Forces</a> navigator who wanted to start a family business with his brothers in Kansas City.<sup id="cite_ref-happy_returns_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-happy_returns-4">[4]</a></sup> Home from the war in 1946, Henry saw a pamphlet suggesting a bright future for companies serving small businesses, and it sparked his imagination.<sup id="cite_ref-happy_returns_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-happy_returns-4">[4]</a></sup> That year, Henry and his older brother, Leon, borrowed $5,000 and opened a small <a href="/wiki/Bookkeeping" title="Bookkeeping">bookkeeping</a> business on Main Street in downtown Kansas City.<sup id="cite_ref-happy_returns_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-happy_returns-4">[4]</a></sup> However, four months later, they had few clients and Leon decided to seek a <a href="/wiki/Law_degree" title="Law degree">law degree</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-happy_returns_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-happy_returns-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>Henry wanted to keep trying with the fledgling business and placed a newspaper ad for help-wanted.<sup id="cite_ref-happy_returns_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-happy_returns-4">[4]</a></sup> He got an unexpected response—from his mother—who proposed that Henry hire his younger brother, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Bloch" title="Richard Bloch">Richard</a>, for the job.<sup id="cite_ref-happy_returns_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-happy_returns-4">[4]</a></sup> Henry and Richard Bloch jointly ran their United Business Company, which focused on accounting, bookkeeping and payroll, but also did some income tax work for clients.<sup id="cite_ref-Dornbrook6Aug14_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dornbrook6Aug14-5">[5]</a></sup> The brothers took out an ad for its $5 tax services in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Kansas_City_Star" title="The Kansas City Star">The Kansas City Star</a></i> newspaper in 1955. The ad was a success and H&amp;R Block was born.<sup id="cite_ref-Tavernise04_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tavernise04-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CNNMoney00_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNNMoney00-7">[7]</a></sup> The Bloch brothers chose to spell the name "Block" with a K to ensure the name is not mispronounced "blotch".<sup id="cite_ref-Tavernise04_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tavernise04-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Expansion_1956.E2.80.931980s"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion_1956–1980s">Expansion 1956–1980s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=H%26R_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Expansion 1956–1980s" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1956, the Blochs decided to expand and picked <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CNNMoney00_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNNMoney00-7">[7]</a></sup> Neither brother wanted to move to New York, so they agreed to sell that regional operation, creating the first H&amp;R Block <a href="/wiki/Chain_store" title="Chain store">franchise</a> tax office.<sup id="cite_ref-Herron17_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Herron17-9">[9]</a></sup> In the following years, H&amp;R Block grew and went public in 1962.<sup id="cite_ref-Tavernise04_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tavernise04-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 1980 H&amp;R Block purchased the <a href="/wiki/Compuserve" class="mw-redirect" title="Compuserve">Compuserve</a> online service.<sup id="cite_ref-GlobeandMail80_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GlobeandMail80-10">[10]</a></sup> By 1986, Block was handling more than 10 million tax returns annually and had opened offices in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Grove86_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grove86-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Brett86_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brett86-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Lampe90_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lampe90-13">[13]</a></sup> That year, the company worked with the <a href="/wiki/Internal_Revenue_Service" title="Internal Revenue Service">Internal Revenue Service</a> to introduce <a href="/wiki/IRS_e-file" title="IRS e-file">electronic filing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Mace85_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mace85-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_history">Recent history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=H%26R_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Recent history" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:H%26R_Block_in_Gillette,_Wyoming.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/H%26R_Block_in_Gillette%2C_Wyoming.jpg/220px-H%26R_Block_in_Gillette%2C_Wyoming.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="5472" data-file-height="3648"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 147px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/H%26R_Block_in_Gillette%2C_Wyoming.jpg/220px-H%26R_Block_in_Gillette%2C_Wyoming.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="147" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:H%26R_Block_in_Gillette,_Wyoming.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An H&amp;R Block in <a href="/wiki/Gillette,_Wyoming" title="Gillette, Wyoming">Gillette, Wyoming</a></div></div></div>
<p>H&amp;R Block acquired MECA Software, a 1980s <a href="/wiki/Personal_financial_management" title="Personal financial management">PFM</a> company that created the TaxCut software, in 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-Schifrin93_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Schifrin93-15">[15]</a></sup> In 1995, H&amp;R Block sold MECA,<sup id="cite_ref-Fisher95_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fisher95-16">[16]</a></sup> but retained TaxCut.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT95_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT95-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>The company began to expand in the 1990s into the financial services arena, offering mortgage loans,<sup id="cite_ref-Hallinan02_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hallinan02-18">[18]</a></sup> banking and business services.<sup id="cite_ref-AP19Aug99_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP19Aug99-19">[19]</a></sup> H&amp;R Block purchased Olde Discount Stockbrokers in 1999<sup id="cite_ref-AP1Dec99_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP1Dec99-20">[20]</a></sup> and operated as a full-service securities broker-dealer under the name H&amp;R Block Financial Advisors. In 2008, the company sold its H&amp;R Block Financial Advisors unit to <a href="/wiki/Ameriprise_Financial" title="Ameriprise Financial">Ameriprise Financial</a> for $315 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Dealbook08_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dealbook08-21">[21]</a></sup> At the time, the unit managed $30 billion in assets and employed 900 financial advisors in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-Dealbook08_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dealbook08-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>RSM McGladrey Business Solutions was created in 1999 when H&amp;R Block acquired the assets of <a href="/wiki/McGladrey" class="mw-redirect" title="McGladrey">McGladrey &amp; Pullen</a>, aside from its auditors and attest services which remained under ownership of McGladrey's partners.<sup id="cite_ref-GlobeandMail30June99_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GlobeandMail30June99-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Following the acquisition H&amp;R Block became the sixth-largest accounting firm in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-GlobeandMail30June99_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GlobeandMail30June99-22">[22]</a></sup> McGladrey had 100 offices in 25 states and offered accounting, consulting, tax services, and international business services to mid-sized companies. Through an alliance with McGladrey &amp; Pullen and other accountancies, the Block subsidiary operated in 70 countries under the RSM International name. In 2011, H&amp;R Block sold the unit back to McGladrey &amp; Pullen.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>H&amp;R Block hired Jeff Jones, a former <a href="/wiki/Target_Corporation" title="Target Corporation">Target Corporation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Uber" title="Uber">Uber</a> executive, as president and CEO on October 9, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-Reuters17_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters17-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Davis17_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Davis17-26">[26]</a></sup> He replaced Tom Gerke, who was interim CEO following the retirement of former CEO Bill Cobb.<sup id="cite_ref-Reuters17_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters17-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Dornbrook17_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dornbrook17-27">[27]</a></sup> Jones has said he sees H&amp;R Block as a <a href="/wiki/Financial_services" title="Financial services">financial services</a> company and a <a href="/wiki/Retail" title="Retail">retail</a> company, and aims to grow the company.<sup id="cite_ref-Dornbrook17_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dornbrook17-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Davis17_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Davis17-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>H&amp;R Block started its first Global Technology Center in India in October 2017. This was created with the vision to grow H&amp;R Block's Product Engineering and IT Solutions Development platforms. It is located in <a href="/wiki/Technopark,_Trivandrum" title="Technopark, Trivandrum">Technopark, Trivandrum</a>, Kerala.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>H&amp;R Block announced in June 2019 that it would acquire <a href="/wiki/Wave_Financial" title="Wave Financial">Wave Financial</a>, a Canadian financial technology company offering bookkeeping, accounting, and payroll services for small businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-Otani19_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Otani19-28">[28]</a></sup> H&amp;B Block acquired Wave for US$405 million (C$537 million).<sup id="cite_ref-Gagliordi19_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gagliordi19-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CP19_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CP19-30">[30]</a></sup> Wave operates as an independent subsidiary by Wave CEO Kirk Simpson from <a href="/wiki/Toronto,_Ontario" class="mw-redirect" title="Toronto, Ontario">Toronto, Ontario</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Gagliordi19_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gagliordi19-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=H%26R_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Controversies" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<h4 class="in-block"><span id="H.26R_Block.27s_own_taxes_2005"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="H&amp;R_Block's_own_taxes_2005">H&amp;R Block's own taxes 2005</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=H%26R_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: H&amp;R Block's own taxes 2005" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In August 2005, H&amp;R Block announced that it had overstated its earnings for 2003 and 2004 by $91.1 million. The company stated that it had "insufficient resources" to identify and report complex transactions in its corporate tax accounting. On February 23, 2006, the company said in its quarterly results that it had miscalculated its own state income taxes for 2005 and 2004, and that it owed an additional $32 million in back taxes.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_Security_numbers_2005">Social Security numbers 2005</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=H%26R_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Social Security numbers 2005" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In December 2005, H&amp;R Block sent its customers free copies of its TaxCut software, and the mailing labels on the packages mistakenly included the recipients' <a href="/wiki/Social_Security_number" title="Social Security number">Social Security numbers</a>. The company said it sent the promotional mailing to former customers and people whose names were taken from purchased lists. It said it is legally required to hold on to customers' tax information, including Social Security numbers, for three years. H&amp;R Block said no customer data has been lost or stolen as a result of the mistake, and that less than 3 percent of the mailings were involved.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Refund_anticipation_loans_2006">Refund anticipation loans 2006</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=H%26R_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Refund anticipation loans 2006" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>California Attorney General <a href="/wiki/Bill_Lockyer" title="Bill Lockyer">Bill Lockyer</a> sued H&amp;R Block in February 2006, alleging the company's <a href="/wiki/Refund_anticipation_loan" title="Refund anticipation loan">refund anticipation loan</a> (RAL) business violated state and federal laws in its marketing and providing of high-cost RALs mainly to low-income clients. The lawsuit also alleged Block received a "substantial portion of the loan fees", in some cases purchasing up to 49.9 percent of the loans, and further alleged that H&amp;R Block at times held onto a customer's tax refund for purposes of paying off RAL-related debt from previous years, including that claimed by other banks or tax preparers.  The complaint stated, "Therefore, Block clients who are claimed to owe debt from a prior year are led to expect a loan, but instead find themselves in a collection proceeding."<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>The company responded that it "believes the refund lending program is both fair and legal, and will vigorously defend against the complaint". On January 2, 2009, California Attorney General <a href="/wiki/Edmund_G._Brown_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Edmund G. Brown Jr.">Edmund G. Brown Jr.</a> reached a $4.85 million settlement with H&amp;R Block, which prohibits the company from deceptively marketing high-cost refund anticipation loans as early "<a href="/wiki/Tax_refund" title="Tax refund">tax refunds</a>." The company set aside $2.45 million in restitution for customers if they purchased a "refund anticipation loan" or a "<a href="/wiki/Refund_anticipation_check" class="mw-redirect" title="Refund anticipation check">refund anticipation check</a>" through H&amp;R Block between January 1, 2001 and December 31, 2008. In addition, H&amp;R Block agreed to pay $500,000 in penalties and $1.9 million in fees and costs.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> In 2011, H&amp;R Block ceased offering RALs altogether, a move praised by consumer rights activists.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Express_IRA_2006">Express IRA 2006</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=H%26R_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Express IRA 2006" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>On March 16, 2006, New York Attorney General <a href="/wiki/Eliot_Spitzer" title="Eliot Spitzer">Eliot Spitzer</a> sued H&amp;R Block, accusing the company of deceptive marketing of its Express IRA retirement accounts. The lawsuit alleged the company assessed fees, including set-up fees, annual fees, and account closing fees that, for 85% of account holders, resulted in the account losing money.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> However, in July 2007, a New York state judge dismissed much of the lawsuit.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Justice Karla Moskowitz of the State Supreme Court excused Block and five of its units from the lawsuit. She let stand the portion of the complaint concerning another unit, H&amp;R Block Financial Advisers, but dismissed allegations of common law fraud. At the time of the ruling, H&amp;R Block said it believed the remaining assertions lacked merit and that it would appeal. On January 6, 2009, a New York state appeals court overruled trial justice Karla Moskowitz's July 2007 ruling and reinstated the lawsuit against H&amp;R Block Inc that accused the company of fraudulently marketing Express IRA retirement accounts to hundreds of thousands of lower-income clients nationwide.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> The matter was settled in 2009, and H&amp;R Block agreed to pay $11.4 million to $19.4 million of fees to customers and $750,000 in fees and other costs.<sup id="cite_ref-Stempel10_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stempel10-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_affairs">Corporate affairs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=H%26R_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Corporate affairs" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>H&amp;R Block is headquartered at 13th and Main streets, Kansas City, Missouri, in a building constructed downtown in the 2000s as part of an effort to revitalize the city's downtown.<sup id="cite_ref-LaMartina04_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LaMartina04-42">[42]</a></sup> The company is governed by an 11-member board of directors.<sup id="cite_ref-Directors_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Directors-43">[43]</a></sup> Jeff Jones is CEO. Tony Bowen is <a href="/wiki/Chief_financial_officer" title="Chief financial officer">CFO</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Sheetz17_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sheetz17-44">[44]</a></sup> As of April 30, 2018<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=H%26R_Block&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, H&amp;R Block employs approximately 2,700 regular full-time employees, and as many as 90,700 including seasonal employees.<sup id="cite_ref-10K2018_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K2018-3">[3]</a></sup> It generates over $3 billion annually in revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-10K2018_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K2018-3">[3]</a></sup> The company also runs The Tax Institute at H&amp;R Block.<sup id="cite_ref-Snider18_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snider18-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=H%26R_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Marketing" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The brand has undertaken several high-profile marketing campaigns, including television commercials featuring actor <a href="/wiki/Jon_Hamm" title="Jon Hamm">Jon Hamm</a> and <a href="/wiki/NBA" class="mw-redirect" title="NBA">NBA</a> player <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davis_(basketball)" class="mw-redirect" title="Anthony Davis (basketball)">Anthony Davis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Birkner17_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Birkner17-46">[46]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Zillgitt15_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zillgitt15-47">[47]</a></sup> H&amp;R Block's "Get Your Billions Back, America" campaign started in 2014. It featured Richard Gartland, a real H&amp;R Block tax professional known for wearing bow ties. The campaign focused on urging taxpayers to seek their full potential refund at tax time.<sup id="cite_ref-Burns15_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Burns15-48">[48]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Rodriguez15_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rodriguez15-49">[49]</a></sup> Anthony Davis, a <a href="/wiki/Power_forward_(basketball)" title="Power forward (basketball)">power forward</a> and <a href="/wiki/Center_(basketball)" title="Center (basketball)">center</a> with the <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans_Pelicans" title="New Orleans Pelicans">New Orleans Pelicans</a>, became a spokesman for H&amp;R Block in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-Zillgitt15_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zillgitt15-47">[47]</a></sup> The company first aired ads featuring <i><a href="/wiki/Mad_Men" title="Mad Men">Mad Men</a></i> actor Jon Hamm in 2017, using the tagline "Get your taxes won", in a 12-spot campaign that was the largest in H&amp;R Block's history.<sup id="cite_ref-Birkner17_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Birkner17-46">[46]</a></sup> Hamm returned as H&amp;R Block's spokesman in 2018 with a new campaign throughout the tax season.<sup id="cite_ref-Gazdik17_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gazdik17-50">[50]</a></sup></p><p>From 2009 to 2016, the company did not advertise during the <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl" title="Super Bowl">Super Bowl</a>. It returned in 2017 with an ad featuring H&amp;R Block's partnership with <a href="/wiki/IBM_Watson" class="mw-redirect" title="IBM Watson">IBM Watson</a> at its retail locations.<sup id="cite_ref-Pasquarelli17_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pasquarelli17-51">[51]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Tompor17_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tompor17-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_social_responsibility">Corporate social responsibility</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=H%26R_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Corporate social responsibility" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The company partners with <a href="/wiki/United_Way" class="mw-redirect" title="United Way">United Way</a> MyFreeTaxes to offer its software for free state and federal tax returns.<sup id="cite_ref-Gallagher16_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gallagher16-53">[53]</a></sup> H&amp;R Block is also a member of the <a href="/wiki/Free_File_Alliance" title="Free File Alliance">Free File Alliance</a>, a consortium of commercial tax preparers and the IRS offering free-tax-prep for low-income and moderate-income taxpayers,<sup id="cite_ref-Snider17_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snider17-54">[54]</a></sup> and Military One Source, which lets members of the military service file for free.<sup id="cite_ref-Ferdinando16_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferdinando16-55">[55]</a></sup></p><p>As part of its corporate social responsibility initiatives, in 2014 H&amp;R Block launched an online game for high school students called the Budget Challenge.<sup id="cite_ref-Kadlec14_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kadlec14-56">[56]</a></sup> The challenge is part of the company's Dollars &amp; Sense financial literacy effort, and offers college scholarships for teenage students who win the game.<sup id="cite_ref-McCabe16_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McCabe16-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_areas">Business areas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=H%26R_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Business areas" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Retail_and_digital_tax_services">Retail and digital tax services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=H%26R_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Retail and digital tax services" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>H&amp;R Block has 70,000 employees<sup id="cite_ref-DePillis17_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DePillis17-58">[58]</a></sup> in its 10,000 U.S. retail tax offices.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> In addition to the company's traditional retail tax offices, it offers <a href="/wiki/Digital_data" title="Digital data">digital</a> tax preparation programs and software. It filed 23 million tax returns worldwide in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-Schaust17_60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Schaust17-60">[60]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Steverman17_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Steverman17-61">[61]</a></sup></p><p>The company's services Tax Pro Review and Tax Pro Go were launched in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-Cohn17_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cohn17-62">[62]</a></sup> Tax Pro Review is an updated version of its former service Best of Both, where clients enter their information and do their returns online and are matched with Block tax professional reviews for help.<sup id="cite_ref-Cohn17_62-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cohn17-62">[62]</a></sup> H&amp;R Block Tax Pro Go is marketed to online filers who do not want to or cannot visit an H&amp;R Block office.<sup id="cite_ref-Davis6Dec17_63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Davis6Dec17-63">[63]</a></sup></p><p>H&amp;R Block's tax software comes in different versions. In addition to the free edition, there are Deluxe, Premium and Self-Employed editions. Users can log on using various devices, import photos of their W-2 forms and use a searchable knowledge base. The Deluxe and Premium editions include free online chat with a tax professional.<sup id="cite_ref-Orem18_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Orem18-64">[64]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Block_Advisors">Block Advisors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=H%26R_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Block Advisors" data-section="15" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In January 2016, H&amp;R Block launched Block Advisors, a service that provides year-round consumer tax preparation.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> The company has opened about 350 Block Advisor offices.<sup id="cite_ref-O'Bannon17_66-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-O'Bannon17-66">[66]</a></sup> Its advisors include <a href="/wiki/Certified_public_accountants" class="mw-redirect" title="Certified public accountants">certified public accountants</a>, enrolled agents, or have been certified by H&amp;R Block as master tax advisors.<sup id="cite_ref-Greenberg16_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Greenberg16-67">[67]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Partnerships">Partnerships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=H%26R_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Partnerships" data-section="16" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2014, the company formed a strategic alliance with <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.xero.com/us/partnerships/hr-block/">Xero</a>.  As part of this alliance, H&amp;R Block will exclusively promote Xero as its preferred small business online accounting solution. H&amp;R Block has 11,000 branded locations nationwide, and will recommend Xero in the locations that sell H&amp;R Block's Small Business Program suite of services.
</p><p>The company signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/Walmart" title="Walmart">Walmart</a> in 2018, to make H&amp;R Block software the only desktop tax preparation software sold at the retailer's stores.<sup id="cite_ref-Franck18_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Franck18-68">[68]</a></sup> H&amp;R Block also created a deal with <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon.com">Amazon.com</a> in 2017 for better product placement of its suite of software products.<sup id="cite_ref-Dornbrook6Dec17_69-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dornbrook6Dec17-69">[69]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, H&amp;R Block partnered with IBM to bring <a href="/wiki/IBM_Watson" class="mw-redirect" title="IBM Watson">IBM Watson</a> technology into its retail offices. Under the partnership, H&amp;R Block tax pros received access to IBM's <a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">artificial intelligence</a> system to help tax pros prepare better tax returns for clients.<sup id="cite_ref-Lohr17_70-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lohr17-70">[70]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Vanian17_71-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vanian17-71">[71]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, H&amp;R Block partnered with <a href="/wiki/LendingTree" title="LendingTree">LendingTree</a> to allow customers credit score access via MyBlock.<sup id="cite_ref-Stimpson18_72-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stimpson18-72">[72]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BankingExchange18_73-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BankingExchange18-73">[73]</a></sup></p>

<p>Following research on the potential expat market in 2013, H&amp;R Block began its Expat Tax Services program to offer tax advice to U.S. citizens living outside the country.<sup id="cite_ref-Burggraf15_74-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Burggraf15-74">[74]</a></sup></p><p>H&amp;R Block maintains financial services products, including the Emerald Card pre-paid debit card<sup id="cite_ref-Dornbrook28Jan14_75-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dornbrook28Jan14-75">[75]</a></sup> and Emerald Advance line of credit.<sup id="cite_ref-Dornbrook16Nov16_76-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dornbrook16Nov16-76">[76]</a></sup> Additionally, the company's Tax Identity Shield offers identity theft protection.<sup id="cite_ref-Tompor11Feb16_77-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tompor11Feb16-77">[77]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_business_areas">Former business areas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=H%26R_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Former business areas" data-section="18" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The H&amp;R Block Bank was chartered in 2006 and offered low-cost services to its low- to moderate-income customers. In September 2015, H&amp;R Block sold H&amp;R Block Bank to <a href="/wiki/BofI_Holding_Inc" class="mw-redirect" title="BofI Holding Inc">BofI Holding Inc</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Davis15_78-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Davis15-78">[78]</a></sup> The two companies reached an agreement for BofI to serve as the bank for H&amp;R Block-branded financial services products including the Emerald MasterCard, Refund Anticipation Checks, and Emerald Advance lines of credit.<sup id="cite_ref-Dornbrook5Aug15_79-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dornbrook5Aug15-79">[79]</a></sup></p><p>Effective 2019, AXOS Bank services the Emerald Card transactions.
