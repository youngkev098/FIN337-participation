</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cboe_Global_Markets&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Founded in 1973 by the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Board_of_Trade" title="Chicago Board of Trade">Chicago Board of Trade</a> and member owned for several decades, on March 11, 2010 the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Board_Options_Exchange" title="Chicago Board Options Exchange">Chicago Board Options Exchange</a> (CBOE) filed paperwork to launch an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and began trading on the <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a> stock exchange on June 15, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2011, CBOE Stock Exchange (CBSX) entered into an agreement to acquire the <a href="/wiki/National_Stock_Exchange_(Jersey_City,_New_Jersey)" title="National Stock Exchange (Jersey City, New Jersey)">National Stock Exchange</a>. The acquisition was completed on December 30, 2011 with both exchanges to operate under separate names. The National Stock Exchange continued to be based in <a href="/wiki/Jersey_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Jersey City">Jersey City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cboe-acquire_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cboe-acquire-4">[4]</a></sup> In May 2014, the <a href="/wiki/National_Stock_Exchange_(Jersey_City,_New_Jersey)" title="National Stock Exchange (Jersey City, New Jersey)">National Stock Exchange</a> ceased trading operations on May 30, 2014. The closure brought the number of active stock exchanges in the United States to 11, as the <a href="/wiki/CBOE_Stock_Exchange" class="mw-redirect" title="CBOE Stock Exchange">CBOE Stock Exchange</a> had closed the month prior. Wrote <i>Bloomberg</i>, that left "just one public exchange, Chicago Stock Exchange Inc., that isn't owned Bats, Nasdaq OMX Group Inc., or IntercontinentalExchange Group Inc."<sup id="cite_ref-bberg-halt_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bberg-halt-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>On June 2, 2015 CBOE announced its acquisition of the LiveVol platform, a market data services provider.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>On January 25, 2016 CBOE announced it had purchased a majority stake in Vest Financial, an investment adviser specializing in "options-centric products".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2016, it was announced that CBOE was purchasing <a href="/wiki/BATS_Global_Markets" title="BATS Global Markets">BATS Global Markets</a>, effective in early 2017, for approximately US$3.2 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>Cboe Global Markets owns and operates four options exchanges: Cboe Options Exchange, Cboe C2 Options Exchange, Cboe BZX Options Exchange, and Cboe EDGX Options Exchange.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
