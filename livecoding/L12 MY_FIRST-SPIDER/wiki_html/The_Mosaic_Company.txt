</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Mosaic_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The Mosaic Company was formed in October 2004 by a merger between <a href="/wiki/IMC_Global" title="IMC Global">IMC Global</a>, a fertilizer company formed in 1909, and <a href="/wiki/Cargill" title="Cargill">Cargill</a>'s crop nutrition division.
It is a combined producer and marketer of concentrated phosphate and potash with a customer base which includes wholesalers, retail dealers and individual growers worldwide. Its headquarters are in Plymouth, Minnesota and it employs approximately 12,000 people in eight countries.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">[3]</a></sup> On October 26, 2018, Mosaic announced that it would be relocating their headquarters to Tampa, Florida.<sup id="cite_ref-:19_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:19-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

Esterhazy K3 is currently in development and has started to supply K1 and K2 surface facilities with ore.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>Product from its Canadian mines is exported through <a href="/wiki/Canpotex" title="Canpotex">Canpotex</a>, an export association of Canadian potash producers.

It has a global distribution network made up of plants, port facilities, warehouses and sales offices.
