<p>M&amp;T Bank has been profitable in every quarter since 1976.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Other than <a href="/wiki/Northern_Trust" title="Northern Trust">Northern Trust</a>, M&amp;T was the only bank in the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500_Index" title="S&amp;P 500 Index">S&amp;P 500 Index</a> not to lower its dividend during the <a href="/wiki/Financial_crisis_of_2007%E2%80%932008" class="mw-redirect" title="Financial crisis of 2007–2008">financial crisis of 2007–2008</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>The bank owns the <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_Savings_Bank" title="Buffalo Savings Bank">Buffalo Savings Bank</a> building in downtown Buffalo. M&amp;T Bank also sponsors <a href="/wiki/M%26T_Bank_Stadium" title="M&amp;T Bank Stadium">M&amp;T Bank Stadium</a>, home of the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Ravens" title="Baltimore Ravens">Baltimore Ravens</a>. M&amp;T Bank is the official bank of the <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_Bills" title="Buffalo Bills">Buffalo Bills</a> in <a href="/wiki/Western_New_York" title="Western New York">Western New York</a> and of their home Stadium <a href="/wiki/New_Era_Field" title="New Era Field">New Era Field</a> in <a href="/wiki/Orchard_Park,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Orchard Park, New York">Orchard Park, New York</a>.  <a href="/wiki/Wilmington_Trust" title="Wilmington Trust">Wilmington Trust</a> is a subsidiary of M&amp;T Bank Corporation, offering global corporate and institutional services, private banking, investment management, and fiduciary services.
</p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%26T_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>M&amp;T was founded in 1856 in <a href="/wiki/Western_New_York" title="Western New York">Western New York</a> state as "Manufacturers and Traders Trust Company". The company opened its first branch on August 29 of that year at 2 East Swan Street in Buffalo.<sup id="cite_ref-history_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 1983, <a href="/wiki/Robert_G._Wilmers" title="Robert G. Wilmers">Robert G. Wilmers</a> was named Chairman and CEO, a position he held until his death in December 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-history_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>Between 1987 and 2009, M&amp;T Bank acquired 20 financial institutions, as follows:<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<ul><li>December 1987: East New York Savings of <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a></li>
<li>January 1989: Monroe Savings Bank of <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_New_York" title="Rochester, New York">Rochester, New York</a></li>
<li>September 1990: Assets of Empire of America Savings Bank of Buffalo (along with <a href="/wiki/KeyBank" title="KeyBank">KeyBank</a> and others)</li>
<li>May 1991: Assets of Goldome Bank of Buffalo (along with <a href="/wiki/KeyBank" title="KeyBank">KeyBank</a> and others)</li>
<li>July 1992: Central Trust and Endicott Trust of <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_New_York" title="Rochester, New York">Rochester, New York</a> and <a href="/wiki/Binghamton,_New_York" title="Binghamton, New York">Binghamton, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li>December 1994: Hudson Valley branches of <a href="/wiki/Chemical_Bank" title="Chemical Bank">Chemical Bank</a>; New York and Ithaca Bancorp of <a href="/wiki/Ithaca,_New_York" title="Ithaca, New York">Ithaca, New York</a>.</li>
<li>July 1995: Hudson Valley branches of <a href="/wiki/Chase_(bank)" class="mw-redirect" title="Chase (bank)">Chase Manhattan</a></li>
<li>January 1997: Green Point Bank branches of <a href="/wiki/Westchester,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Westchester, New York">Westchester, New York</a></li>
<li>June 1999: First National Bank of Rochester</li>
<li>April 1998: OnBank of <a href="/wiki/Syracuse,_New_York" title="Syracuse, New York">Syracuse, New York</a><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li>September 1999: 29 Chase Bank Branches in Buffalo, Jamestown, and Binghamton<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li>October 2000: Keystone Financial of Central Pennsylvania<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li>February 2001: Premier Nation Bancorp of Newburgh<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li>April 2003: Allfirst Bank of <a href="/wiki/Baltimore" title="Baltimore">Baltimore</a>, a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Allied_Irish_Banks" title="Allied Irish Banks">Allied Irish Banks</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, in exchange for 26.7 million shares of M&amp;T and $886 million in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> At the direction of Irish government financial regulators, AIB sold its 22% ownership interest in M&amp;T in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li>July 2006: 21 <a href="/wiki/Citibank" title="Citibank">Citibank</a> branches in Buffalo and Rochester<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li>December 2007: Partners Trust Financial Group, including 33 branches in <a href="/wiki/Upstate_New_York" title="Upstate New York">upstate New York</a>, for $555 million.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></li>
<li>December 2007: 12 <a href="/wiki/First_Horizon_National_Corporation" title="First Horizon National Corporation">First Horizon National Corporation</a> branches in the greater Washington D.C. and Baltimore markets.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></li>
<li>May 2009: <a href="/wiki/Provident_Bank_of_Maryland" title="Provident Bank of Maryland">Provident Bank of Maryland</a> in a stock transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></li>
<li>August 2009: The <a href="/wiki/Federal_Deposit_Insurance_Corporation" title="Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation">Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation</a> (FDIC) seized Bradford Bank and sold all its deposits and most assets to M&amp;T.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> M&amp;T and the FDIC agreed to share future losses on $338 million worth of Bradford's assets.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></li></ul><p>In May 2011, M&amp;T acquired <a href="/wiki/Wilmington_Trust" title="Wilmington Trust">Wilmington Trust</a> for $351 million in stock.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>On August 27, 2012, M&amp;T announced the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Hudson_City_Bancorp" title="Hudson City Bancorp">Hudson City Bancorp</a> for $3.7 billion. The bank had $25 billion in deposits and $28 billion in loans and 135 brick-and-mortar branch locations including 97 in New Jersey.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The acquisition was delayed for 3 years due to a money laundering case involving an M&amp;T branch and the acquisition closed on November 2, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>In 2008, M&amp;T received a $600 million investment by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Treasury" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Treasury">United States Treasury</a> as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Troubled_Asset_Relief_Program" title="Troubled Asset Relief Program">Troubled Asset Relief Program</a> (TARP) and M&amp;T assumed another $482 million in TARP obligations from its acquisitions.<sup id="cite_ref-health_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-health-26">[26]</a></sup> In 2011, the bank repaid $700 million of TARP funds.<sup id="cite_ref-health_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-health-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>On December 16, 2017, Robert Wilmers died and non-Executive Chairman Robert T. Brady became acting Chairman and CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> On December 20, 2017, René F. Jones was appointed Chairman and Chief Executive Officer.
</p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Money_laundering">Money laundering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%26T_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Money laundering" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In June 2014, a U.S. District Judge ordered M&amp;T Bank to forfeit $560,000 in drug proceeds that had been <a href="/wiki/Money_laundering" title="Money laundering">laundered</a> through its <a href="/wiki/Perry_Hall,_Maryland" title="Perry Hall, Maryland">Perry Hall, Maryland</a>, branch. At least 8 times from 2011 to 2013, Deanna Bailey, a drug dealer, went to the branch and had head teller Sabrina Fitts convert cash amounts from $20,000 to $100,000 into larger bills.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Fitts accepted a 1% transaction fee in exchange for not filing a <a href="/wiki/Currency_transaction_report" title="Currency transaction report">currency transaction report</a>. This violated the <a href="/wiki/Bank_Secrecy_Act_of_1970" class="mw-redirect" title="Bank Secrecy Act of 1970">Bank Secrecy Act of 1970</a> which requires all cash transactions of more than $10,000 to be reported to the <a href="/wiki/Internal_Revenue_Service" title="Internal Revenue Service">Internal Revenue Service</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>The acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Hudson_City_Bancorp" title="Hudson City Bancorp">Hudson City Bancorp</a> was delayed for more than 3 years by the Federal Reserve Board, which was unconvinced that the bank's anti-laundering controls were strong enough.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
