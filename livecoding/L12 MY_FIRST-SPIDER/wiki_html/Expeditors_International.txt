</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Financial_information">Financial information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expeditors_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Financial information" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Expeditors became a publicly traded company in 1984 with the listing of its <a href="/wiki/Shares" class="mw-redirect" title="Shares">shares</a> on <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a> under the ticker symbol <b>EXPD</b> (<a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a>: <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.nasdaq.com/symbol/expd">EXPD</a>) and were named to the <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ-100" title="NASDAQ-100">NASDAQ-100</a> in 2002.  During their first year as a public company, Expeditors reported more than $50 million in gross revenues and $2.1 million in net earnings.  Expeditors is currently #374 on the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> ([<a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://fortune.com/fortune500/2019/expeditors-intl-of-washington/">https://fortune.com/fortune500/2019/expeditors-intl-of-washington/</a>).  Total revenues exceeded 8 billion ($8.138 billion) in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>They are known in the financial services community for their unconventional and entertaining <a href="/wiki/United_States_Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Securities and Exchange Commission">SEC</a> filings, which are rumored to be written by former CEO Peter Rose himself<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, as well as its responses to questions submitted to the company, which are placed on Expeditors' Investor website.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>  Rose announced his retirement in March 2014 as CEO, with his retirement as Chairman effective May 2015.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Compensation_structure">Compensation structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expeditors_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Compensation structure" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Expeditors maintains a compensation structure that is unique to the logistics industry.  According to their 2003 annual report,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> "Each of the Company’s branches are independent profit centers and the primary compensation for the branch management group comes in the form of incentive-based compensation calculated directly from the operating income of that branch.  This compensation structure ensures that the allocation of revenue and expense among components of services [...] are done in an objective manner on a fair value basis."
</p>
