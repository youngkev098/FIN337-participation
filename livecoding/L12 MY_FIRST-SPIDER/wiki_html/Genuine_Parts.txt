</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Company_Overview">Company Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Genuine_Parts_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Company Overview" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Founded in 1925,<sup id="cite_ref-official_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-official-2">[2]</a></sup> GPC is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlanta, Georgia">Atlanta, Georgia</a> and consists of multiple subsidiaries that distribute automotive replacement parts, industrial replacement parts, office products and electrical/electronic materials. The Company has paid a cash dividend to shareholders every year since going public in 1948.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCReport2014_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCReport2014-3">[3]</a></sup> The company moved into its new headquarters building in the Wildwood area of <a href="/wiki/Cobb_County" class="mw-redirect" title="Cobb County">Cobb County</a>, Georgia in June 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Automotive_Parts">Automotive Parts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Genuine_Parts_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Automotive Parts" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The Automotive Parts Group, the largest division of GPC, distributes approximately 475,000 automotive replacement parts, accessory items and service items throughout North America, Australia and New Zealand. In North America, parts are sold primarily under the NAPA brand name. The Company’s GPC Asia Pacific business serves the Australasian markets primarily under the brand name Repco.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Industrial_Parts">Industrial Parts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Genuine_Parts_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Industrial Parts" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The Industrial Parts Group, which operates under the name <a href="/wiki/Motion_Industries" title="Motion Industries">Motion Industries</a>, offers access to more than 6.5 million industrial replacement parts and related supplies and serves over 150,000 MRO and OEM customers throughout North America and in all types of industries. These include the food and beverage, forest products, primary metal, pulp and paper, mining, automotive, oil and gas, petrochemical and pharmaceutical industries.<sup id="cite_ref-https://genpt.com/mi.html_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-https://genpt.com/mi.html-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Office_Products">Office Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Genuine_Parts_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Office Products" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The Office Products Group, which operates under the name S. P. Richards Company, distributes more than 62,000 items to over 6,300 resellers and distributors throughout the United States and Canada from a network of 45 distribution centers. Customers include independently owned office product dealers, large contract stationers, national office supply superstores, mail order distributors, internet resellers, college bookstores, office furniture dealers, janitorial and sanitation supply distributors, safety product resellers and food service distributors.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Electrical.2FElectronic_Materials"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Electrical/Electronic_Materials">Electrical/Electronic Materials</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Genuine_Parts_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Electrical/Electronic Materials" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The Electrical/Electronic Materials Group, which operates under the name EIS, Inc., distributes process materials, production supplies, industrial MRO and custom-engineered value added fabricated parts to more than 20,000 customers, including original equipment manufacturers, motor repair shops and a broad variety of industrial assembly and specialty wire and cable markets in North America. Products cover over 100,000 items including wire and cable, insulating and conductive materials, assembly tools and test equipment.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Genuine_Parts_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: History" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In 1925, Carlyle Fraser founded GPC with the purchase of Motor Parts Depot in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlanta, Georgia">Atlanta, Georgia</a> for $40,000. He renamed the parts store Genuine Parts Company. The original Genuine Parts Company store had annual sales of just $75,000, and six employees.
</p><p>For the next 50+ years, GPC, in relationship with NAPA, the <a href="/wiki/National_Automotive_Parts_Association" title="National Automotive Parts Association">National Automotive Parts Association</a>, grew rapidly as independent garages for car repair emerged to meet the needs of the nation's growing number of motor vehicles. In the last 35+ years, GPC has continued to grow through the acquisition of other companies in the automotive industry, as well as in the industrial, office and electrical industries.
</p><p>Today GPC is a leading parts distributor with over 3,100 operations and approximately 48,000 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-genpt.com_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-genpt.com-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Company_Timeline">Company Timeline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Genuine_Parts_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Company Timeline" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">

<p>The Automotive Parts Group  distributes automotive replacement parts, accessory items and service items throughout <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>.  In North America, parts are sold primarily under the NAPA (<a href="/wiki/National_Automotive_Parts_Association" title="National Automotive Parts Association">National Automotive Parts Association</a>) brand name and is widely recognized for quality parts, quality service and knowledgeable people. The Company’s GPC <a href="/wiki/Asia_Pacific" class="mw-redirect" title="Asia Pacific">Asia Pacific</a> business serves the Australasian markets primarily under the brand name <a href="/wiki/Repco" title="Repco">Repco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCReport2014_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCReport2014-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>The Automotive Parts Group supports over 6,000 NAPA AUTO PARTS stores throughout the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, 700 wholesalers in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and 481 automotive locations in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>. These stores sell to both the Retail (DIY) and Commercial (DIFM) automotive aftermarket customer and cover the majority of all domestic and foreign motor vehicle models.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCReport2014_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCReport2014-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>The Industrial Parts Group, operating under the name <a href="/wiki/Motion_Industries" title="Motion Industries">Motion Industries</a>, offers more than 5.9 million industrial replacement parts and related supplies. The Group serves over 150,000 MRO and OEM customers throughout North America and in all types of industries. These include the food and beverage, forest products, primary metal, pulp and paper, mining, automotive, oil and gas, petrochemical and pharmaceutical industries. Strategically targeted specialty industries include power generation, waste-water treatment facilities, wind power generation, solar power, government projects, pipelines, railroads and ports, among others. Motion Industries is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Birmingham,_Alabama" title="Birmingham, Alabama">Birmingham, Alabama</a> and operates 15 distribution centers, 523 branches, and 39 service centers in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, Canada, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCReport2014_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCReport2014-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>The Business Products Group, operating under the name S.P. Richards, distributes more than 61,000 items to over 5,200 resellers and distributors throughout the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> from a network of 60 distribution centers. Customers include independently owned office product dealers, large contract stationers, national office supply superstores, mail order distributors, internet resellers, college bookstores, office furniture dealers, janitorial and sanitation supply distributors, safety product resellers and food service distributors. S.P. Richards is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlanta, Georgia">Atlanta, Georgia</a> and operates 34 full-stocking distribution centers, 2 furniture only distribution centers, 5 S.P. Richards <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> distribution centers, 1 GCN Distribution Center, and 2 impact products distribution centers.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCReport2014_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCReport2014-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>The Electrical/Electronic Materials Group, operating under the name EIS, Inc. is one of North America's leading distributors of process materials, production supplies, industrial MRO and value added fabricated parts. EIS, Inc. is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlanta, Georgia">Atlanta, Georgia</a>, and operates 49 branches and 7 fabrication facilities in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, Canada, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCReport2014_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCReport2014-3">[3]</a></sup> Effective in 2018, EIS was combined into Motion Industries and will be identified as its Electrical Specialties Group.<sup id="cite_ref-https://genpt.com/mi.html_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-https://genpt.com/mi.html-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
