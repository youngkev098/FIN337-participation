</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huntington_Ingalls_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>When it spun off as a new company on March 31, 2011, Huntington Ingalls Industries comprised Northrop Grumman’s shipbuilding businesses in Newport News, Virginia, and Pascagoula, Mississippi.
</p><p>Since its creation, HII has built and expanded its professional and government services through the acquisitions of <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.universalpegasus.com">UniversalPegasus International</a>, the S.M. Stoller Corporation, <a href="/wiki/Camber_Corporation" title="Camber Corporation">Camber Corporation</a>, the Columbia Group’s Engineering Solutions division, G2 Inc. and Fulcrum IT Services.
</p><p>In 2016, HII established a third division, Technical Solutions,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> comprising the company’s services capabilities.
</p><p>Huntington Ingalls Industries is named for the founders of its shipbuilding divisions: <a href="/wiki/Collis_Potter_Huntington" title="Collis Potter Huntington">Collis Potter Huntington</a>, who founded <a href="/wiki/Newport_News_Shipbuilding_and_Drydock_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="Newport News Shipbuilding and Drydock Company">Newport News Shipbuilding and Drydock Company</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Ingersoll_Ingalls_Sr." title="Robert Ingersoll Ingalls Sr.">Robert Ingersoll Ingalls Sr.</a>, who established <a href="/wiki/Ingalls_Shipbuilding" title="Ingalls Shipbuilding">Ingalls Shipbuilding</a>.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Divisions">Divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huntington_Ingalls_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Divisions" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Newport_News_Shipbuilding" title="Newport News Shipbuilding">Newport News Shipbuilding</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newport_News,_Virginia" title="Newport News, Virginia">Newport News, Virginia</a> (U.S. Navy nuclear aircraft carriers, submarines, refueling and complex overhaul, carrier inactivation)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ingalls_Shipbuilding" title="Ingalls Shipbuilding">Ingalls Shipbuilding</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pascagoula,_Mississippi" title="Pascagoula, Mississippi">Pascagoula, Mississippi</a> (U.S. Navy surface combatants, amphibious warships, and U.S. Coast Guard National Security Cutters)</li>
<li>Technical Solutions, (U.S. Navy fleet support, software engineering and IT solutions, cybersecurity, training, unmanned systems, intelligence analysis, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Energy" title="United States Department of Energy">Department of Energy</a> nuclear operations, nuclear fabrication, oil and gas services)</li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huntington_Ingalls_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Facilities" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>HII operates facilities in several key locations across the US:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Newport_News_Shipbuilding" title="Newport News Shipbuilding">Newport News Shipbuilding</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newport_News,_Virginia" title="Newport News, Virginia">Newport News, Virginia</a> (nuclear-powered aircraft carriers and submarines, refueling and complex overhaul, aircraft carrier inactivation)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ingalls_Shipbuilding" title="Ingalls Shipbuilding">Ingalls Shipbuilding</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pascagoula,_Mississippi" title="Pascagoula, Mississippi">Pascagoula, Mississippi</a> (U.S. Navy surface combatants, amphibious assault ships; U.S. Coast Guard national security cutters)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Virginia_Beach,_Virginia" title="Virginia Beach, Virginia">Virginia Beach, Virginia</a> (fleet support, training)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/San_Diego,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Diego, California">San Diego, California</a> (fleet support and repair)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Huntsville,_Alabama" title="Huntsville, Alabama">Huntsville, Alabama</a> (modeling and simulation, training, professional services)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fairfax,_Virginia" title="Fairfax, Virginia">Fairfax, Virginia</a> (IT and cybersecurity services)</li></ul><h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_facilities">Former facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huntington_Ingalls_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Former facilities" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>

<p>HII's 2019 order backlog amounts to $41 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Gerald_R._Ford-class_aircraft_carriers"><i>Gerald R. Ford</i>-class aircraft carriers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huntington_Ingalls_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Gerald R. Ford-class aircraft carriers" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>HII is to build ten <a href="/wiki/Gerald_R._Ford_class_aircraft_carrier" class="mw-redirect" title="Gerald R. Ford class aircraft carrier"><i>Gerald R. Ford</i>-class aircraft carriers</a> for the US Navy. It is scheduled to deliver one carrier every five years starting in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 2019, the U.S. Navy awarded Huntington Ingalls Industries a $15.2 billion block contract for the detail design and construction of <i>Enterprise</i> (CVN 80) and <i>Doris Miller</i> (CVN 81).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Aircraft_carrier_Refueling_and_Complex_Overhaul_.28RCOH.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Aircraft_carrier_Refueling_and_Complex_Overhaul_(RCOH)">Aircraft carrier Refueling and Complex Overhaul (RCOH)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huntington_Ingalls_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Aircraft carrier Refueling and Complex Overhaul (RCOH)" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Newport News Shipbuilding (NNS) is the only shipyard to perform RCOH work on <i>Nimitz</i>-class aircraft carriers. The nearly four-year project is performed once during a carrier’s 50-year life and includes refueling of nuclear reactors, as well as significant repair, upgrade and modernization work.
</p><p>NNS completed RCOH work on the fifth ship, USS <i>Abraham Lincoln</i> (CVN 72), in May 2017, and USS <i>George Washington</i> (CVN 73) arrived in August 2017 to begin its RCOH.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> NNS is currently supporting the pre-advanced planning effort for the USS <i>John C. Stennis</i> (CVN 74) RCOH.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Aircraft_carrier_inactivation">Aircraft carrier inactivation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huntington_Ingalls_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Aircraft carrier inactivation" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Newport News is the only shipyard to provide for the inactivation of nuclear-powered aircraft carriers. Once the service life of a carrier is complete, the ship comes to the shipyard for defueling of its nuclear reactors. The inactivation of the USS <i>Enterprise</i> (CVN 65) is complete and is the first nuclear carrier to undergo the process.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Virginia-class_attack_submarines"><i>Virginia</i>-class attack submarines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huntington_Ingalls_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Virginia-class attack submarines" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Newport News is one of only two shipyards capable of designing and building nuclear-powered submarines. Currently, NNS is building the most advanced attack submarines in the world—the <i>Virginia</i> class. NNS has delivered 17 Virginia-class boats and work continues on 11 boats. <i>Indiana</i> (SSN 789) was delivered to the U.S. Navy in June 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Submarine_design_and_engineering">Submarine design and engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huntington_Ingalls_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Submarine design and engineering" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Newport News Shipbuilding designs and provides on-site installation of state-of-the-art technologies for the <i>Los Angeles</i>-class and <i>Seawolf</i>-class attack submarines. NNS also supports <a href="/wiki/General_Dynamics_Electric_Boat" title="General Dynamics Electric Boat">Electric Boat</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Columbia-class_submarine" title="Columbia-class submarine"><i>Columbia</i>-class</a> ballistic missile submarine program.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="America-class_amphibious_assault_ships"><i>America</i>-class amphibious assault ships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huntington_Ingalls_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: America-class amphibious assault ships" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>USS <i>America</i> (LHA 6) was delivered in April 2014 and commissioned October 11, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> It is first in the new class of amphibious assault ships for the U.S. Navy, replacing USS <i>Tarawa</i> (LHA 1). Ingalls’ next ship in the class, <i>Tripoli</i> (LHA 7), is scheduled to be delivered in 2019. The ship was christened on Sept. 16, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>On June 16, 2017, Ingalls Shipbuilding was awarded $3.1 billion contract to build <i>Bougainville</i> (LHA 8). Construction started on LHA 8 on October 15, 2018, and the ship’s keel was laid on March 13, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Arleigh_Burke-class_destroyers"><i>Arleigh Burke</i>-class destroyers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huntington_Ingalls_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Arleigh Burke-class destroyers" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On September 27, 2018, Ingalls Shipbuilding won a $5.1 billion multi-year contract to build an additional six DDG 51s.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> These destroyers are equipped with the Navy’s Aegis Combat System. The first Flight III ship, <i>Jack. H. Lucas</i> (DDG 125), started fabrication on May 7, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Ingalls has built and delivered 31 ships to the U.S. Navy, with four more under construction. <i>Paul Ignatius</i> (DDG 117), is scheduled for commissioning on July 27 in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Lauderdale,_Florida" title="Fort Lauderdale, Florida">Fort Lauderdale, Florida</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="San_Antonio-class_landing_llatform_docks_.28LPDs.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="San_Antonio-class_landing_llatform_docks_(LPDs)"><i>San Antonio</i>-class landing llatform docks (LPDs)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huntington_Ingalls_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: San Antonio-class landing llatform docks (LPDs)" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Ingalls Shipbuilding is building the entire <i>San Antonio</i> class of ships, the newest addition to the Navy’s 21st century amphibious assault force. LPDs 17 to 27 have been delivered to the U.S. Navy. Ingalls currently has two <i>San Antonio</i>-class ships under construction. The keel of <i>Fort Lauderdale</i> (LPD 28) was authenticated in the fall of 2017,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> and the ship is scheduled to be launched in the first quarter of 2020.The keel of <a href="/wiki/Richard_Miles_McCool" title="Richard Miles McCool"><i>Richard M. McCool</i> <i>Jr.</i></a> (LPD 29)<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> was authenticated on April 12, 2019. On March 26, 2019, Ingalls received a $1.4 billion detailed design and construction contract for the first Flight II ship, LPD 30.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="U.S._Coast_Guard_National_Security_Cutters">U.S. Coast Guard National Security Cutters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huntington_Ingalls_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: U.S. Coast Guard National Security Cutters" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>National Security Cutters, the flagships of the Coast Guard’s cutter fleet, are designed to replace the 378-foot <i>Hamilton</i>-class high-endurance cutters, which entered service during the 1960s. The current program of record is for 11 ships, of which the first eight have been successfully delivered to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">U.S. Coast Guard</a>. Construction is underway on the company’s ninth NSC, Stone (WMSL 758). On December 21, 2018, Ingalls received two contracts from the U.S. Coast Guard to build a 10th and 11th NSC. The contracts are valued at $468.75 million and $462.13 million, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Technical_Solutions_contracts">Technical Solutions contracts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huntington_Ingalls_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Technical Solutions contracts" data-section="15" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<ul><li>Technical Solutions’ Mission Driven Innovative Solutions group (HII-MDIS) is providing the U.S. Army’s Product Manager Force Protection Systems maintenance, repair, supply support, fielding, engineering, configuration management and training.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></li>
<li>Technical Solutions' Fleet Support Group is providing engineering, technical, repair and logistical support to the U.S. Navy’s Coastal Riverine Forces.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></li>
<li>Through a joint venture (as N3B) to manage the Los Alamos Legacy Cleanup Contract at the Department of Energy’s Los Alamos National Laboratory (LANL).<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></li>
<li>In a joint venture with Kellogg Brown and Root’s (KBR) Government Services division, HII's Technical Solutions division to establish and manage Australia’s new Naval Shipbuilding College.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huntington_Ingalls_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: References" data-section="16" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
