</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=O%27Reilly_Auto_Parts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:O%27Reilly_Auto_Parts_Ypsilanti.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/O%27Reilly_Auto_Parts_Ypsilanti.JPG/220px-O%27Reilly_Auto_Parts_Ypsilanti.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="107" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="4308" data-file-height="2099"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 107px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/O%27Reilly_Auto_Parts_Ypsilanti.JPG/220px-O%27Reilly_Auto_Parts_Ypsilanti.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="107" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:O%27Reilly_Auto_Parts_Ypsilanti.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An O'Reilly Auto Parts store in <a href="/wiki/Ypsilanti,_Michigan" title="Ypsilanti, Michigan">Ypsilanti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a></div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:OrEILLYAutoPartsHouston.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/OrEILLYAutoPartsHouston.jpg/220px-OrEILLYAutoPartsHouston.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/OrEILLYAutoPartsHouston.jpg/220px-OrEILLYAutoPartsHouston.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:OrEILLYAutoPartsHouston.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>O'Reilly Auto Parts in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a></div></div></div>
<p>O’Reilly Automotive, Inc. officially started in the auto parts business with one store in <a href="/wiki/Springfield,_Missouri" title="Springfield, Missouri">Springfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a>, in December 1957.<sup id="cite_ref-Company_History_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Company_History-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>Michael Byrne O’Reilly immigrated to America in 1849, settling in <a href="/wiki/St._Louis" title="St. Louis">St. Louis</a>, he worked his way through school to earn a law degree, and then pursued a career as a title examiner.<sup id="cite_ref-Company_History_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Company_History-5">[5]</a></sup>
His son, Charles Francis O’Reilly, attended college in St. Louis and went to work in 1914 as a traveling salesman for Fred Campbell Auto Supply in St. Louis. By 1924, Charles had become familiar with the Springfield area, having traveled by train to sell auto parts throughout the territory. By 1932, he had become manager of Link Motor Supply in Springfield, and one of his sons, Charles H. (Chub) O’Reilly, joined the company. Together they provided the leadership and management that made Link the predominant auto parts store in the area.<sup id="cite_ref-Company_History_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Company_History-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In 1957, Link planned a reorganization, which would have included the retirement of 72-year-old Charles F. O’Reilly, and the transfer of Chub O’Reilly to <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City</a>. Since neither agreed with these plans, they made the decision to form their own company, O’Reilly Automotive, Inc. They opened for business on December 2, 1957, with one store and 13 employees at 403 Sherman in Springfield. Their sales totaled $700,000 in 1958, their first full year of business. By 1961, the company’s volume had reached $1.3 million — the combined volume of O’Reilly Automotive and Ozark Automotive Distributors, a division formed to serve independent automotive jobbers in the area. In March 1975, annual sales volume rose to $7 million and a 52,000 square-foot facility at 233 S. Patterson was built for the O’Reilly/Ozark warehouse operation. By that time, the company had nine stores, all located in southwest Missouri.<sup id="cite_ref-Company_History_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Company_History-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion">Expansion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=O%27Reilly_Auto_Parts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Expansion" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>On April 23, 1993, the company completed an initial public offering, and it now trades on the <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a> market under the symbol ORLY. The company also has a stock purchase plan to enable as many full-time team members as possible to own stock in the company.
</p><p>On January 30, 1998, O'Reilly merged with Hi/LO Auto Supply, adding 182 auto parts stores in <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>, as well as a distribution center in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>. The Hi/LO acquisition made O’Reilly one of the top 10 auto parts chains in the country. On April 25, 2000, the firm announced the purchase of KarPro Auto Parts, which included a 66,000 square foot distribution center in <a href="/wiki/Little_Rock,_Arkansas" title="Little Rock, Arkansas">Little Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arkansas" title="Arkansas">Arkansas</a>, and 14 KarPro stores.
</p><p>On October 1, 2001, O'Reilly purchased Mid-State Automotive Distributors, Inc., adding 82 additional auto parts stores in seven states, as well as two distribution centers. The acquisition made the firm one of the top five auto parts chains in the country. It added its eleventh distribution center in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a> in March 2005.
</p><p>On May 31, 2005, O'Reilly purchased Midwest Automotive Distributors, Inc., adding 72 retail locations in <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montana" title="Montana">Montana</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Dakota" title="North Dakota">North Dakota</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Dakota" title="South Dakota">South Dakota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">Wisconsin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wyoming" title="Wyoming">Wyoming</a>, as well as two distribution centers located in Minnesota and Montana.
</p><p>On July 11, 2008, the company completed the largest acquisition in its history with the purchase of <a href="/wiki/CSK_Auto" title="CSK Auto">CSK Auto</a>, adding 1,273 stores in 12 states.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg-2008apr01_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg-2008apr01-6">[6]</a></sup> This, combined with past acquisitions, made O’Reilly the third largest auto parts chain in the country after <a href="/wiki/Advance_Auto_Parts" title="Advance Auto Parts">Advance Auto Parts</a> and <a href="/wiki/AutoZone" title="AutoZone">AutoZone</a>.
</p><p>In December 2012, O'Reilly announced it was purchasing the auto parts business of VIP Parts, Tires and Service, a <a href="/wiki/Lewiston,_Maine" title="Lewiston, Maine">Lewiston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>-based chain of 56 auto parts stores and service centers with locations in Maine, <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, and one distribution center in Maine. The purchase marked the firm’s expansion into <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>On October 26, 2016, O'Reilly announced that it was acquiring Bond Auto Parts, a 51-store chain headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a> with locations in New Hampshire, Massachusetts, and <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> as well.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>On November 13, 2018, O'Reilly announced that it was acquiring Florida-based Bennett Auto Supply, a 33-store chain headquartered in Pompano Beach.
</p><p>As of July 2018<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=O%27Reilly_Auto_Parts&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> O'Reilly Automotive, Inc. operates more than 5,147 stores in 47 states, including <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>As of February 2019, O'Reilly Automotive, Inc. operates more than 5,400 stores in 47 states, including Alaska and Hawaii.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">[9]</a></sup> The company continues to plan for growth and expansion.
</p><p>On August 20, 2019, O'Reilly Auto announced it would be acquiring Mayasa Auto Parts. This marks O’Reilly Auto Parts first venture into international markets. Mayasa has 5 distribution centers, 20 company stores, and more than 2000 independent jobber locations throughout Mexico.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>As of March 2020 O’Reilly Automotive, Inc. operates more than 5,400 stores in 47 states, including Alaska and Hawaii.[9] The company continues to plan for growth and expansion, projecting the addition of 200-210 net, new stores in 2020.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Executive_management">Executive management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=O%27Reilly_Auto_Parts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Executive management" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>Executive officers of the company include Greg Johnson, Chief Executive Officer and Co-President; Jeff Shaw, Chief Operating Officer and Co-President; Tom McFall, Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer; and Brad Beckham, Executive Vice President of Store Operations and Sales.
