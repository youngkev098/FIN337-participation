<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">This article is about the department store. For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/Kohl_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Kohl (disambiguation)">Kohl</a>.</div>
<p><b>Kohl's</b> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Department_store" title="Department store">department store</a> retail <a href="/wiki/Chain_store" title="Chain store">chain</a>, operated by Kohl's Corporation. With 1,158 locations, it is the largest department store chain in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> as of February 2013<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Kohl%27s&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>. The company was founded by a Polish immigrant Maxwell Kohl, who opened a corner <a href="/wiki/Grocery_store" title="Grocery store">grocery store</a> in <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee" title="Milwaukee">Milwaukee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">Wisconsin</a> in 1927.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Branching out from its successful grocery store chain, the company opened its first department store in 1962.<sup id="cite_ref-kohls-at-50_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kohls-at-50-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/British_American_Tobacco" title="British American Tobacco">British American Tobacco</a> Company took a controlling interest in the company in 1972 while still managed by the Kohl Family, and in 1979, the corporation was sold to <a href="/wiki/BATUS_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="BATUS Inc.">BATUS Inc.</a>  A group of investors purchased the company in 1986 from British American Tobacco and took it <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public</a> in 1992.

Penney</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The company is listed on both the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500_Index" title="S&amp;P 500 Index">S&amp;P 500</a> (since 1998) and the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In terms of revenue, the chain was the 20th-largest <a href="/wiki/Retailer" class="mw-redirect" title="Retailer">retailer</a> in the United States in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> As of 2013, Kohl's was the second-largest U.S. department store company by retail sales.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kohl%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kohl%27s_%E2%80%93_Beaverton,_Oregon.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Kohl%27s_%E2%80%93_Beaverton%2C_Oregon.jpg/250px-Kohl%27s_%E2%80%93_Beaverton%2C_Oregon.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="188" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 250px;height: 188px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Kohl%27s_%E2%80%93_Beaverton%2C_Oregon.jpg/250px-Kohl%27s_%E2%80%93_Beaverton%2C_Oregon.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="250" data-height="188" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kohl%27s_%E2%80%93_Beaverton,_Oregon.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The exterior of a typical Kohl's department store in <a href="/wiki/Beaverton,_Oregon" title="Beaverton, Oregon">Beaverton, Oregon</a></div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bella_Terra_Kohls.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Bella_Terra_Kohls.JPG/260px-Bella_Terra_Kohls.JPG" decoding="async" width="260" height="195" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3456" data-file-height="2592"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 260px;height: 195px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Bella_Terra_Kohls.JPG/260px-Bella_Terra_Kohls.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="260" data-height="195" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bella_Terra_Kohls.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Kohl's in <a href="/wiki/Huntington_Beach,_California" title="Huntington Beach, California">Huntington Beach, California</a></div></div></div>
<p>Maxwell Kohl, who had operated traditional grocery stores since 1927, built his first <a href="/wiki/Supermarket" title="Supermarket">supermarket</a> in 1946, the first in what would become a southeastern Wisconsin chain known as <b>Kohl's Food Stores</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In September 1962, after building Kohl's Food Stores into the largest supermarket chain in the Milwaukee area, Kohl opened his first department store in <a href="/wiki/Brookfield,_Wisconsin" title="Brookfield, Wisconsin">Brookfield, Wisconsin</a>. He positioned Kohl's between the higher-end department stores and the discounters, selling everything from candy to engine oil to sporting equipment.<sup id="cite_ref-kohls-at-50_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kohls-at-50-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In 1972, the <a href="/wiki/British_American_Tobacco" title="British American Tobacco">British American Tobacco</a> Company's U.S. retail division, <a href="/wiki/Batus_Inc." title="Batus Inc.">Batus Inc.</a>, bought a controlling interest in Kohl's Corporation, which at the time operated 50 grocery stores, six department stores, three drug stores, and three liquor stores.<sup id="cite_ref-ms-1972oct28_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ms-1972oct28-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wsj-1972oct30_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-1972oct30-15">[15]</a></sup> The Kohl family, led by Allen Kohl and <a href="/wiki/Herb_Kohl" title="Herb Kohl">Herb Kohl</a>, continued to manage the company. Herb Kohl left the management in 1979,<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-1979may02_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-1979may02-16">[16]</a></sup> and eventually became a United States Senator and owner of the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Bucks" title="Milwaukee Bucks">Milwaukee Bucks</a>. The firm then expanded Kohl's presence from 10 to 39 stores in Wisconsin, <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>. The grocery stores were sold to <a href="/wiki/The_Great_Atlantic_%26_Pacific_Tea_Company" title="The Great Atlantic &amp; Pacific Tea Company">A&amp;P</a> in 1983,<sup id="cite_ref-nyt-1983aug16_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-1983aug16-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wwd-1983sep29_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wwd-1983sep29-18">[18]</a></sup> operating under the name Kohl's Food Store, and later Kohl's Food Emporium. In February 2003, A&amp;P put the Kohl's Food Stores up for sale,<sup id="cite_ref-mbj-2003feb25_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mbj-2003feb25-19">[19]</a></sup> as part of an effort to reduce debt. In 2003, A&amp;P closed all Kohl's Food Stores locations and the Kohl family left remaining management.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-mbj-2003jun13_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mbj-2003jun13-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>A group of investors, led by the senior management, purchased the company in 1986.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt-1986may26_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-1986may26-22">[22]</a></sup> Building on an existing 40 department stores, the company added 27 more stores over the next two years. In 1988, the chain acquired 26 locations from the <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>-based retailer <a href="/wiki/MainStreet" title="MainStreet">MainStreet</a>, gaining several stores in Chicago's suburbs, the <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis%E2%80%93Saint_Paul" title="Minneapolis–Saint Paul">Twin Cities</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt-1988oct29_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-1988oct29-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Kohl's completed its initial public offering on May 19, 1992 and began trading on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> under the symbol KSS.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-prn-1992may19_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prn-1992may19-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>During the 2000s, Kohl's expanded nationwide to 49 states. Building from 76 stores in the Midwest in 1992, Kohl's expanded into the <a href="/wiki/New_York_metropolitan_area" title="New York metropolitan area">New York metropolitan area</a> starting in 2000 after <a href="/wiki/Caldor" title="Caldor">Caldor</a> was liquidated. The chain later expanded into <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> in 2003 with 28 new stores, the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Northwest" title="Pacific Northwest">Pacific Northwest</a> in 2006 with 10 new stores, and the <a href="/wiki/Southeastern_United_States" title="Southeastern United States">Southeast</a> with 43 new stores opening between 2005 and 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> To raise money to repurchase its stock and open new stores, Kohl's sold its credit card division in 2006 to <a href="/wiki/J.P._Morgan_Chase" class="mw-redirect" title="J.P. Morgan Chase">J.P. Morgan Chase</a> for $1.5 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt-2006mar07_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-2006mar07-28">[28]</a></sup> In 2011, Kohl's replaced Chase with <a href="/wiki/Capital_One" title="Capital One">Capital One</a> as their private credit card processing partner for an undisclosed sum.<sup id="cite_ref-wp-2011apr10_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wp-2011apr10-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>Kohl's hired <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> advertising agency <a href="/wiki/DeVito/Verdi" title="DeVito/Verdi">DeVito/Verdi</a> in 2009 to strengthen the Kohl's brand via a series of national television, online, and social media campaigns.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The same year, <i><a href="/wiki/Newsweek" title="Newsweek">Newsweek</a></i> magazine ranked the company 18th overall and first in its industry in its "Green Rankings",<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> an examination of 500 of the largest corporations on their environmental track records. <i>Newsweek</i> remarked that Kohl's had the largest solar power program of any retailer globally, it pursues green building certification, and over 78 locations in six states have solar panels. Kohl's had also begun to sell <a href="/wiki/Reusable_shopping_bag" title="Reusable shopping bag">reusable shopping bags</a> the previous year.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p><p>Kohl's was awarded $62.5 million in <a href="/wiki/Tax_credits" class="mw-redirect" title="Tax credits">tax credits</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin_Economic_Development_Corporation" title="Wisconsin Economic Development Corporation">Wisconsin Economic Development Corporation</a> in 2012. The retailer was to create 3,000 jobs with the funds, but only created 473.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> In the same year, Kohl's requested financing from the village of <a href="/wiki/Menomonee_Falls,_Wisconsin" title="Menomonee Falls, Wisconsin">Menomonee Falls, Wisconsin</a> to finance the building of its new headquarters there. Kohl's received $2 million, the first of five installments that was to equal a total payout of $12 million, only to ultimately back out of the transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, the company opened the first test store of OFF/AISLE, a chain built around selling like-new clothing, home goods, jewelry, and accessories that were purchased and returned at Kohl's stores. The stores sell items at discounted prices, and have a more restrictive return policy than typical Kohl's stores.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p><p>In early January 2017, Kohl's shares fell 19% in value, in what <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a></i> said was "the stock's worst day on record," and noted that it was a noticeable exception to the overall declining volatility of the market.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The company ranked 157th on the 2018 <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a>, the annual list of the largest United States corporations, having earned revenues of $19.095 billion in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, Kohl's entered into a partnership with <a href="/wiki/Amazon_(company)" title="Amazon (company)">Amazon</a>, which included a program where select stores would accept Amazon returns; in 2019, it was expanded nationwide.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018 and 2019, Kohl's announced a pilot program to lease space to grocer <a href="/wiki/Aldi" title="Aldi">Aldi</a> and fitness center <a href="/wiki/Planet_Fitness" title="Planet Fitness">Planet Fitness</a> alongside up to 10 stores each.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p><p>On June 11, 2019, it was announced that Off/Aisle stores would close on August 3, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-40">[40]</a></sup></p><p>On March 30,2020, due to the spread of the <a href="/wiki/2019-20_coronavirus_outbreak" class="mw-redirect" title="2019-20 coronavirus outbreak">2019-20 coronavirus outbreak</a>, it was announced that all Kohl's stores nationwide would be closed indefinitely.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Brands_and_store_layout">Brands and store layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kohl%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Brands and store layout" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Kohl's store brands include <a href="/wiki/Diffusion_line" title="Diffusion line">diffusion lines</a> from high-end designers such as <a href="/wiki/Dana_Buchman" title="Dana Buchman">Dana Buchman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vera_Wang" title="Vera Wang">Vera Wang</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-usa_kohls_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usa_kohls-41">[41]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Narciso_Rodriguez" title="Narciso Rodriguez">Narciso Rodriguez</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-style_kohls_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-style_kohls-42">[42]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Som" title="Peter Som">Peter Som</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HuffPost_kohls_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HuffPost_kohls-43">[43]</a></sup> Celebrities such as <a href="/wiki/Avril_Lavigne" title="Avril Lavigne">Avril Lavigne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lauren_Conrad" title="Lauren Conrad">Lauren Conrad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daisy_Fuentes" title="Daisy Fuentes">Daisy Fuentes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paula_DeAnda" title="Paula DeAnda">Paula DeAnda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marc_Anthony" title="Marc Anthony">Marc Anthony</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tony_Hawk" title="Tony Hawk">Tony Hawk</a> have sold branded clothing exclusively through Kohl's.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p><p>Kohl's private brands generate nearly half of the firm's $19 billion in annual sales. These include in-house clothing brands such as American Beauty, Apt. 9, Croft &amp; Barrow, Jumping Beans, So, Tek Gear, and Urban Pipeline. The Sonoma Goods for Life brand of apparel and home goods alone is worth over a billion dollars, and Kohl's announced relaunch plans for the brand in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Kohl's has also purchased the exclusive retail rights to existing brands such as <a href="/wiki/Candie%27s" title="Candie's">Candie's</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mudd_Jeans" title="Mudd Jeans">Mudd Jeans</a>.
</p><p>Kohl's uses a "racetrack" layout with a single aisle that circles the entire store, a layout borrowed from discount stores.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
