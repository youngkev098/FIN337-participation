</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Motorola_Solutions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Motorola" title="Motorola">Motorola</a></div>
<p>Motorola Solutions began trading as a separate independent company on January 4, 2011, under the NYSE symbol MSI.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-4">[4]</a></sup>  It is the legal successor of the old <a href="/wiki/Motorola" title="Motorola">Motorola</a>, Inc. (founded in 1928); the transaction was structured so the old Motorola changed its name to Motorola Solutions and spun off Motorola Mobility as a separate company. Motorola Solutions retained the old Motorola's pre-2011 stock price history, though the old Motorola's ticker symbol MOT was retired.
</p><p>The company produces and sells communications products and services to public safety and government agencies (law enforcement, fire, emergency medical services, and national government security) as well as private entities including utilities, mining, energy, manufacturing, hospitality, retail, transportation and logistics.
</p><p>After the split, Motorola Solutions comprised the previous Government and Public Safety division of Motorola Inc, the <a href="/wiki/Enterprise_mobility_management" title="Enterprise mobility management">enterprise mobility management</a> division, and the cellular infrastructure group.
</p><p>Chinese telecoms company <a href="/wiki/Huawei" title="Huawei">Huawei</a> and Motorola Solutions have settled their intellectual property disputes in July 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Motorola Solutions sold the cellular infrastructure business to <a href="/wiki/Nokia_Siemens_Networks" class="mw-redirect" title="Nokia Siemens Networks">Nokia Siemens Networks</a>. The acquisition, which was originally announced in July 2010, was completed on April 29, 2011, for $975 million in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> As part of the transaction approximately 6,900 employees transferred to Nokia Siemens Networks. In the former Motorola parent company, it was the second largest division based on revenue.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Post_Split">Post Split</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Motorola_Solutions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Post Split" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>On October 27, 2014, Motorola Solutions sold its Enterprise business to <a href="/wiki/Zebra_Technologies" title="Zebra Technologies">Zebra Technologies</a> for $3.45 billion in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> As part of the sale, approximately 4,500 Motorola Solutions employees from locations throughout the world were transferred to Zebra. A large majority of this business was formerly <a href="/wiki/Symbol_Technologies" title="Symbol Technologies">Symbol Technologies</a>, which Motorola, Inc. acquired in 2007.
</p><p>In August, 2015, the company received a $1 billion investment from the <a href="/wiki/Private_equity" title="Private equity">private equity</a> firm <a href="/wiki/Silver_Lake_Partners" title="Silver Lake Partners">Silver Lake Partners</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> enabling a <a href="/wiki/Share_repurchase" title="Share repurchase">stock buyback</a> and providing Silver Lake with two board seats.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In December, 2015, the company announced that it would acquire <a href="/wiki/Airwave_Solutions" title="Airwave Solutions">Airwave Solutions</a>, the UK based operator of the British public safety radio network servicing the police, fire and ambulance services across England, Scotland and Wales.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The company completed the acquisition on February 19, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>Beginning in March 2017, Motorola Solutions filed a series of lawsuits against China-based two-way radio manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Hytera" title="Hytera">Hytera</a> in the United States, Germany and Australia, as well as with the <a href="/wiki/United_States_International_Trade_Commission" title="United States International Trade Commission">United States International Trade Commission</a> (USITC).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The complaints allege that Hytera is intentionally infringing on patents owned by Motorola Solutions and utilizing trade secrets stolen by three former Motorola Solutions employees who left to join Hytera.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Motorola Solutions is seeking to stop Hytera from selling and importing its devices in these countries. In April 2017, the USITC announced that it had decided to institute an investigation into Hytera’s trade practices.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2017, Motorola Solutions announced it completed the acquisition of Kodiak Networks,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> a privately-held provider of broadband push-to-talk (PTT) for commercial customers.
</p><p>In December 2017, two-way radio manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Hytera" title="Hytera">Hytera</a> filed <a href="/wiki/Antitrust" class="mw-redirect" title="Antitrust">antitrust</a> litigation against Motorola Solutions in alleging that Motorola is engaging in anti-competitive practices that are unlawful under the <a href="/wiki/Sherman_Antitrust_Act" class="mw-redirect" title="Sherman Antitrust Act">Sherman Act</a>  and <a href="/wiki/Clayton_Act" class="mw-redirect" title="Clayton Act">Clayton Act</a>. Hytera's complaint alleges that Motorola Solutions prevents Hytera from competing in the U.S. marketplace by enforcing inflated prices and engaging in a monopolistic scheme that includes forcing LMR dealers to drop Hytera's products.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2018, Motorola Solutions acquired <a href="/wiki/Avigilon" title="Avigilon">Avigilon</a> for about $1 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2019, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Livermore,_California" title="Livermore, California">Livermore, California</a>-based VaaS International Holdings, and its subsidiary, license plate reading technology company Vigilant Solutions, for $445 million.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>

<p>Motorola Solutions manufactures two-way radios and public radio systems for first-responders and law enforcement.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> It also provides software packages for command centers, mapping and drone surveillance.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Apart from radios, it manufactures <a href="/wiki/Body_camera" class="mw-redirect" title="Body camera">body cameras</a> under the Watchguard Video brand and security surveillance under the Avigilon brand.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>Motorola also manufactures <a href="/wiki/Push_to_talk" class="mw-redirect" title="Push to talk">push to talk</a> <a href="/wiki/LTE_(telecommunication)" title="LTE (telecommunication)">LTE</a> devices under the <i>LTE LEX</i> line.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The LEX series runs on <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">Android</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
