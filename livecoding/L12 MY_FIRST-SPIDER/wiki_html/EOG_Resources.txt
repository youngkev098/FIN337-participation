</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_operations">Current operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=EOG_Resources&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Current operations" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>As of December 31, 2019, the company had 3.329 <a href="/wiki/1000000000_(number)" class="mw-redirect" title="1000000000 (number)">billion</a> <a href="/wiki/Barrel_of_oil_equivalent" title="Barrel of oil equivalent">barrels of oil equivalent</a> (2.037<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup>10</sup> GJ) of estimated proved reserves, of which 98% was in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, 2% was in <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="Trinidad and Tobago">Trinidad and Tobago</a>, and a negligible amount was in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. The reserves were 51% <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">petroleum</a>, 22% <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas_liquids" class="mw-redirect" title="Natural gas liquids">natural gas liquids</a>, and 27% <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>In 2019, the company's production averaged 818 thousand <a href="/wiki/Barrel_of_oil_equivalent" title="Barrel of oil equivalent">barrels of oil equivalent</a> (5,000,000 GJ) per day, of which 94% was in the United States, 5% was in Trinidad and Tobago, and 1% was in other areas.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=EOG_Resources&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: United States" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>As of December 31, 2018, of the company's total proved reserves in the United States, 53% was <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">petroleum</a>, 21% was <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas_liquids" class="mw-redirect" title="Natural gas liquids">natural gas liquids</a>, and 26% was <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a>. As of December 31, 2019, the company was the largest petroleum producer in the <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Ford_Group" title="Eagle Ford Group">Eagle Ford Group</a>. The company also owns properties in the <a href="/wiki/Delaware_Basin" title="Delaware Basin">Delaware Basin</a> and other areas of the <a href="/wiki/Permian_Basin_(North_America)" title="Permian Basin (North America)">Permian Basin</a>, including the Leonard, Wolfcamp, and Second Bone Spring Sand shale plays. In the <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountains" title="Rocky Mountains">Rocky Mountains</a>, the company owns properties in the <a href="/wiki/Williston_Basin" title="Williston Basin">Williston Basin</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Bakken_Formation" title="Bakken Formation">Bakken Formation</a> and the Turner, Parkman and <a href="/wiki/Niobrara_Formation" title="Niobrara Formation">Niobrara Formations</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Powder_River_Basin" title="Powder River Basin">Powder River Basin</a>. The company also owns properties in the <a href="/wiki/Austin_Chalk" title="Austin Chalk">Austin Chalk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anadarko_Basin" title="Anadarko Basin">Anadarko Basin</a>, the Fort Worth Basin, and the <a href="/wiki/Marcellus_Shale" class="mw-redirect" title="Marcellus Shale">Marcellus Shale</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=EOG_Resources&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Canada" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The company owns approximately 134,000 net acres with 23 net producing wells in the <a href="/wiki/Horn_River_Formation" title="Horn River Formation">Horn River Formation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Trinidad_and_Tobago">Trinidad and Tobago</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=EOG_Resources&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Trinidad and Tobago" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The company holds several concession contracts for the supply of natural gas in Trinidad and Tobago. As of December 31, 2019, the company held approximately 115,000 net undeveloped acres in Trinidad and Tobago.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="China">China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=EOG_Resources&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: China" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2008, the company acquired assets in the Chuan Zhong Block exploration area in the Sichuan Basin, <a href="/wiki/Sichuan_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Sichuan Province">Sichuan Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> from <a href="/wiki/ConocoPhillips" title="ConocoPhillips">ConocoPhillips</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-china_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-china-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=EOG_Resources&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: History" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In 1998, Mark G. Papa was named Chairman and Chief Executive Officer.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1999, the company became independent from <a href="/wiki/Enron" title="Enron">Enron</a> and changed its name to EOG Resources, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 2000, the company swapped properties with <a href="/wiki/Occidental_Petroleum" title="Occidental Petroleum">Occidental Petroleum</a>. EOG received properties in <a href="/wiki/East_Texas" title="East Texas">East Texas</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_Panhandle" title="Oklahoma Panhandle">Oklahoma Panhandle</a> in exchange for properties in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In February 2000, the company also swapped properties with <a href="/wiki/Burlington_Resources" title="Burlington Resources">Burlington Resources</a>. EOG received properties in <a href="/wiki/West_Texas" title="West Texas">West Texas</a> and the <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a>, specifically in the <a href="/wiki/Permian_Basin_(North_America)" title="Permian Basin (North America)">Permian Basin</a>, in exchange for properties in <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The company was added to the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a> index in October 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>The company acquired properties in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> from <a href="/wiki/Husky_Energy" title="Husky Energy">Husky Energy</a> for $320 million in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In 2006, the company signed a 225,648 square feet (20,963.4 m<sup>2</sup>) lease for office space in the <a href="/wiki/Heritage_Plaza" title="Heritage Plaza">Heritage Plaza</a> building in <a href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Houston, Texas">Houston, Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In 2008, the company acquired assets in the Chuan Zhong Block exploration area in the Sichuan Basin, <a href="/wiki/Sichuan_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Sichuan Province">Sichuan Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> from <a href="/wiki/ConocoPhillips" title="ConocoPhillips">ConocoPhillips</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-china_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-china-3">[3]</a></sup> The company announced major discoveries in 2010 in the <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Ford_Group" title="Eagle Ford Group">Eagle Ford Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2011, the company sold gas-producing properties in South Texas and New Mexico for $637 million.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2014, the company sold its assets in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Also in December 2014, founder <a href="/wiki/Mark_G._Papa" title="Mark G. Papa">Mark G. Papa</a> resigned from the <a href="/wiki/Board_of_directors" title="Board of directors">board of directors</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2015, the company spent $368 million to acquire additional acreage in the <a href="/wiki/Delaware_Basin" title="Delaware Basin">Delaware Basin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2016, the company acquired Yates Petroleum for 26 million shares of common stock valued at $2.3 billion and $37 million in cash. The acquisition increased the company's holdings by 176,000 net acres in the <a href="/wiki/Delaware_Basin" title="Delaware Basin">Delaware Basin</a>, 200,000 net acres in the <a href="/wiki/Powder_River_Basin" title="Powder River Basin">Powder River Basin</a>, and 138,000 net acres on the Northwest Shelf in New Mexico.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, the company formed a <a href="/wiki/Joint_venture" title="Joint venture">joint venture</a> with <a href="/wiki/The_Carlyle_Group" title="The Carlyle Group">The Carlyle Group</a> to develop oil and gas assets in <a href="/wiki/Ellis_County,_Oklahoma" title="Ellis County, Oklahoma">Ellis County, Oklahoma</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2018, the company sold its assets offshore the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
