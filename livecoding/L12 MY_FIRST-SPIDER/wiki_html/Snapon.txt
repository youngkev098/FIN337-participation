</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_operations">Current operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snap-on&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Current operations" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Snap-on Inc. operates plants in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, Elizabethton, Tennessee, and Elkmont, Alabama. Pneumatic and cordless tools are manufactured in <a href="/wiki/Murphy,_North_Carolina" title="Murphy, North Carolina">Murphy, North Carolina</a>. Wheel Balancers and tire changers are produced in Conway, Arkansas.  Torque products are made and assembled in City of Industry, California.  <sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>The company manufactures tool storage cabinets in its <a href="/wiki/Algona,_Iowa" title="Algona, Iowa">Algona, Iowa</a> plant.
</p><p>Snap-on produces hand-held electronic diagnostic tools for the computer systems used in most modern cars and heavy duty vehicles, produced in the US at their <a href="/wiki/Kenosha" class="mw-redirect" title="Kenosha">Kenosha</a> site, along with software development in the US, Ireland and China, as well as automotive emissions control diagnostics equipment in its <a href="/wiki/San_Jose,_California" title="San Jose, California">San Jose, California</a> diagnostic facility. Snap-on diagnostic products are sold in Europe and Brazil under the name <i>Sun</i>.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Sales_approach">Sales approach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snap-on&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sales approach" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Snap-on tools are sold only by dealers and not in retail stores. Snap-on has always maintained the philosophy that the customer's time was too valuable to spend going shopping for tools. Snap-on franchisees visit their customers in their place of work, once weekly, in a van loaded with items for purchase.
</p><p>The Snap-on TechKnow Express is a van that showcases everything Snap-on has to offer in the realm of diagnostic equipment, and the Rock 'n Roll Cab Express is a truck with various types of tool storage showing customization options, including units larger than what can fit on a standard franchisee van. These trucks are typically assigned to a particular region and work within that region with individual franchisees.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snap-on&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Snap-on was founded as the Snap-on Wrench Company in <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee,_Wisconsin" class="mw-redirect" title="Milwaukee, Wisconsin">Milwaukee, Wisconsin</a> in 1920 by Joseph Johnson and William Seidemann.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The business manufactured and marketed ten sockets that would "snap on" to five interchangeable handles. The company's slogan was "5 do the work of 50".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>After <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, Palmer<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (October 2019)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> advertised for a military officer to organize and develop a larger sales force for the expected post war sales boom. Newton Tarble was hired, and came up with the idea of developing routes for company dealers to see mechanics on a weekly basis. Eventually these salesmen became independent businessmen and authorized dealers using larger walk in vans to carry a growing product line.
</p><p>In 1975, Snap-on opened a manufacturing plant in <a href="/wiki/Johnson_City,_Tennessee" title="Johnson City, Tennessee">Johnson City, Tennessee</a> and closed the plant in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 1998, workers at the company's Milwaukee plant voted to join the <a href="/wiki/Teamsters" class="mw-redirect" title="Teamsters">Teamsters</a> <a href="/wiki/Labor_union" class="mw-redirect" title="Labor union">labor union</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 1999, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Bahco" title="Bahco">Bahco</a>, a Swedish hand tool brand.
</p><p>In 2011, the Murphy, North Carolina plant was named one of the top 10 plants in North America by <a href="/wiki/Industry_Week" class="mw-redirect" title="Industry Week">Industry Week</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 2013, the company expanded its hand tool facility in Milwaukee.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 2014, the company acquired Pro-Cut for $42 million.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2016, the company acquired Car-O-Liner Holding AB, a Swedish collision repair tool company, for $155 million.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2016, the company acquired Sturtevant Richmont for $13 million.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2017, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Norbar_Torque" title="Norbar Torque">Norbar Torque Tools</a> Holdings Limited for $72 million.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Racing_sponsorships">Racing sponsorships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snap-on&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Racing sponsorships" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>The company has sponsored <a href="/wiki/Penske_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Penske Racing">Penske Racing</a> teams in the <a href="/wiki/NASCAR" title="NASCAR">NASCAR</a> <a href="/wiki/Monster_Energy_Cup_Series" class="mw-redirect" title="Monster Energy Cup Series">Monster Energy Cup Series</a> and <a href="/wiki/Xfinity_Series" class="mw-redirect" title="Xfinity Series">Xfinity Series</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/IndyCar" title="IndyCar">IndyCar</a>. The first driver Snap-on became associated with was <a href="/wiki/Rick_Mears" title="Rick Mears">Rick Mears</a> in 1979. Since 1992, Snap-on has sponsored <a href="/wiki/Cruz_Pedregon" title="Cruz Pedregon">Cruz Pedregon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In 2004, the company began sponsoring Cruz’s brother <a href="/wiki/Tony_Pedregon" title="Tony Pedregon">Tony Pedregon</a>. Snap-on also sponsors <a href="/wiki/Repsol_Honda" title="Repsol Honda">Repsol Honda Team</a> in <a href="/wiki/MotoGP" class="mw-redirect" title="MotoGP">MotoGP</a> since 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
