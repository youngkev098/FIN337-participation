<p><b>Baker Hughes Company</b> is an international industrial service company and one of the world's largest <a href="/wiki/List_of_oilfield_service_companies" title="List of oilfield service companies">oil field services</a> companies. The company provides the <a href="/wiki/Oil_and_gas_industry" class="mw-redirect" title="Oil and gas industry">oil and gas industry</a> with products and services for <a href="/wiki/Oil_drilling" class="mw-redirect" title="Oil drilling">oil drilling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Formation_evaluation" title="Formation evaluation">formation evaluation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Completion_(oil_and_gas_wells)" title="Completion (oil and gas wells)">completion</a>, production and reservoir consulting. Baker Hughes is organized in <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a> and headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup> The company was originally known as Baker Hughes Incorporated until 2017 when it was merged with <a href="/wiki/GE_Oil_and_Gas" title="GE Oil and Gas">GE Oil and Gas</a> to become Baker Hughes, a GE Company (BHGE), then in 2019 the company divested from <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a> and became Baker Hughes Company. Despite the separation, <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a> still maintains a 38.4% share hold in the company.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Company_Overview">Company Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Company Overview" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Baker Hughes operates in over 120 countries across the globe. Its headquarters are divided between Texas in the United States and the United Kingdom, with research and manufacturing facilities in Australia, Singapore, Malaysia, India, Dubai, Saudi Arabia, Italy, Germany, Norway, Louisiana and Missouri.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The company provide a wide range of products and services, covering all areas of oil and gas exploration and production.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<ul><li><b>Oilfield Services</b> - provides products and services for onshore and offshore operations across the lifecycle of a well, including drilling, evaluation, <a href="/wiki/Completion_(oil_and_gas_wells)" title="Completion (oil and gas wells)">completion</a>, production, and intervention.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li></ul><ul><li><b>Oilfield Equipment</b> - provides products and services required to facilitate the safe and reliable flow of hydrocarbons from the wellhead to the production facility, including deepwater drilling equipment, subsea production systems, onshore wellheads, and related services.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li></ul><ul><li><b>Turbomachinery &amp; Process Solutions</b> - provides equipment and related services for mechanical-drive, compression and power-generation applications across the petroleum industry as well as products and services to serve the downstream segments of the industry.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li></ul><ul><li><b>Digital Solutions</b> - provides operating technologies helping to improve the health, productivity, and safety of asset intensive industries and enable the Industrial <a href="/wiki/Internet_of_things" title="Internet of things">Internet of things</a>, including measurement &amp; controls and software application development platform.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Baker Hughes is the combination of many companies that have developed and introduced technology to serve the petroleum service industry. Their combined history dates back to the early 1900s. During its history, Baker Hughes has acquired and assimilated numerous oilfield pioneers including: Brown Oil Tools, CTC, EDECO, and Elder Oil Tools (completions); <a href="/wiki/Milchem" title="Milchem">Milchem</a> (<a href="/wiki/Drilling_fluid" title="Drilling fluid">drilling fluids</a>); EXLOG (<a href="/wiki/Mud_logging" title="Mud logging">mud logging</a>); Eastman Christensen and Drilex (directional drilling and diamond drill bits); Teleco (measurement while drilling); Tri-State and Wilson (fishing tools and services); Aquaness, <a href="/wiki/Chemlink" title="Chemlink">Chemlink</a> and <a href="/wiki/Petrolite" title="Petrolite">Petrolite</a> (specialty chemicals), <a href="/wiki/Western_Atlas" title="Western Atlas">Western Atlas</a> (seismic exploration, <a href="/wiki/Well_logging" title="Well logging">well logging</a>), <a href="/wiki/BJ_Services" title="BJ Services">BJ Services</a> (pressure pumping).
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Hughes_Tool_Company">Hughes Tool Company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hughes Tool Company" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sharp-Hughes_Tool_Company,_1915.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Sharp-Hughes_Tool_Company%2C_1915.jpg/220px-Sharp-Hughes_Tool_Company%2C_1915.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="159" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1237" data-file-height="895"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 159px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Sharp-Hughes_Tool_Company%2C_1915.jpg/220px-Sharp-Hughes_Tool_Company%2C_1915.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="159" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sharp-Hughes_Tool_Company,_1915.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Hughes_Tool_Company" title="Hughes Tool Company">Hughes Tool Company</a>'s oil drill manufacturing plant in downtown Houston, 1915.</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Hughes_Tool_Company" title="Hughes Tool Company">Hughes Tool Company</a> was founded in 1908 by business partners <a href="/wiki/Walter_Benona_Sharp" title="Walter Benona Sharp">Walter Benona Sharp</a> and <a href="/wiki/Howard_R._Hughes,_Sr." class="mw-redirect" title="Howard R. Hughes, Sr.">Howard R. Hughes, Sr.</a>, father of <a href="/wiki/Howard_Hughes" title="Howard Hughes">Howard Hughes</a>, Jr. That year, Hughes, Sr. and Sharp developed the first <a href="/wiki/Roller_cone_bit" title="Roller cone bit">two-cone drill bit</a>, designed to enable <a href="/wiki/Rotary_drilling" class="mw-redirect" title="Rotary drilling">rotary drilling</a> in harder, deeper formations than was possible with earlier fishtail bits. They conducted two secret tests on a drilling rig in <a href="/wiki/Goose_Creek,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Goose Creek, Texas">Goose Creek, Texas</a>. Each time, Hughes asked the drilling crew to leave the rig floor, pulled the bit from a locked wooden box, and then his associates ran the bit into the hole. The drill pipe twisted off on the first test, but the second was extremely successful. In 1909, the Sharp &amp; Hughes bit was granted a U.S. patent. In the same year, the partners formed the Sharp-Hughes Tool Company in Houston, Texas, to manufacture the bit in a rented space measuring 20 by 40 ft (12 m).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>After Walter Sharp died in 1912, Hughes purchased Sharp's half of the business. The company was renamed Hughes Tool Company in 1915, and Hughes, Jr. inherited it after his father's death in 1924. Through the 1950s and 1960s, Hughes Tool Company remained a private enterprise, owned by Hughes. While Hughes was engaged in his Hollywood and aviation enterprises, managers in Houston, such as Fred Ayers and Maynard Montrose, kept the tool company growing through technical innovation and international expansion. In 1958, the Engineering and Research Laboratory was enlarged to accommodate six laboratory sections that housed specialized instruments, such as a direct reading spectrometer and x-ray diffractometer. In 1959, Hughes introduced self-lubricating, sealed bearing rock bits. After collecting data from thousands of bit runs, Hughes introduced the first comprehensive guides to efficient drilling practices in 1960; 1964 saw the introduction of the X-Line rock bits, combining new cutting structure designs and hydraulic jets.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Baker_Oil_Tool_Company">Baker Oil Tool Company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Baker Oil Tool Company" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Reuben_C._Baker" title="Reuben C. Baker">Reuben C. Baker</a> was a farm boy with a third grade education who followed his brother Aaron Alphonso Baker into the oil business. In 1895 he got a job in a California oilfield with 95 cents in his pocket. His first job was driving a horse team to haul oil for drillers. One year later he became a drilling contractor.
</p><p>In July 1907, the 34-year-old inventor and entrepreneur was working in <a href="/wiki/Coalinga,_California" title="Coalinga, California">Coalinga, California</a> when he was granted a U.S. patent for a <a href="/wiki/Casing_shoe" title="Casing shoe">casing shoe</a> that enabled drillers to efficiently run casing and cement it in oil wells.<sup id="cite_ref-aoghs_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aoghs-8">[8]</a></sup> The casing shoe revolutionized cable tool drilling by ensuring the uninterrupted flow of oil through the bottom of the casing in the well.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>This innovation launched the business that would become Baker Oil Tools. In 1928, Baker Casing Shoe Company changed its name to Baker Oil Tools, Inc., to reflect its product line of completion, cementing and fishing equipment. Baker Oil Tools expanded to Houston in the 1920s, and although buffeted by the <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a>, survived and continued growing into the 1960s. In 1976, it became Baker International Corporation with almost all of its operations headquartered in Houston.
</p><p>In early 1956, during one of the most successful periods in the company's history, Baker retired as President of Baker Oil Tools and was succeeded by his long-time associate Ted Sutter. Baker died only a few weeks later after a brief illness at the age of 85. Although he only had three years of formal education, Baker received more than 150 patents in his lifetime.<sup id="cite_ref-aoghs_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aoghs-8">[8]</a></sup> In 1964, Sutter was succeeded by E.H. "Hubie" Clark, who became the first Baker Hughes chairman of the board in 1987. During its 80-year history prior to its merger with Baker Hughes, Baker Oil Tools had only three chief executives.
</p><p>INTEQ also originally incorporated the drilling fluids division of Baker Hughes which consisted of Milpark and others. This division was called 'INTEQ drilling fluids' which provided the premier brands in oil and gas well drilling muds and wellbore cleaning fluids. In 2003, these product lines were spun off to form the separate entity of Baker Hughes Drilling Fluids (BHDF), with INTEQ continuing as the Drilling and Evaluation (D&amp;E) company. INTEQ provides directional Drilling, MWD/LWD, surface logging (<a href="/wiki/Mud_logging" title="Mud logging">mud logging</a>) and coring services.
</p><p>The company's flagship brand was the AutoTrak rotary steerable drilling system which was a pioneering <a href="/wiki/Directional_drilling" title="Directional drilling">directional drilling</a> tool. Introduced in 1997 with Agip S.p.A., the tool is fundamentally different compared to contemporary rivals such as the PowerDrive and the GeoPilot employing the hybrid technique of "pushing and pointing (vectoring) the bit" rather than only "pointing the bit" or only "pushing the bit".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="1987_merger_to_form_Baker_Hughes">1987 merger to form Baker Hughes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1987 merger to form Baker Hughes" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>As a result of the <a href="/wiki/1980s_oil_glut" title="1980s oil glut">1980s oil glut</a>, in 1987, Baker International acquired Hughes Tool Company to form Baker Hughes Incorporated.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Joint_venture_with_Schlumberger_.282000-2006.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Joint_venture_with_Schlumberger_(2000-2006)">Joint venture with Schlumberger (2000-2006)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Joint venture with Schlumberger (2000-2006)" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1999, the only new seismic technology that was being introduced was the 4-dimensional seismic survey monitoring.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In 2000, during a downturn in the <a href="/wiki/Petroleum_industry" title="Petroleum industry">petroleum industry</a>, the company merged its Western Geophysical division with <a href="/wiki/Schlumberger" title="Schlumberger">Schlumberger</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Geco_(Geophysical_Company_of_Norway)" title="Geco (Geophysical Company of Norway)">Geco (Geophysical Company of Norway)</a> to form <a href="/wiki/WesternGeco" title="WesternGeco">WesternGeco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In 2006, Baker Hughes sold its 30% share of WesternGeco to Schlumberger for $2.4 billion in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Violation_of_Foreign_Corrupt_Practices_Act">Violation of Foreign Corrupt Practices Act</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Violation of Foreign Corrupt Practices Act" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In April 2007, the company pleaded guilty in U.S. federal court to violations of the <a href="/wiki/Foreign_Corrupt_Practices_Act" title="Foreign Corrupt Practices Act">Foreign Corrupt Practices Act</a> (FCPA). The company made $4.1 million in "<a href="/wiki/Commission_(remuneration)" title="Commission (remuneration)">commission</a>" payments between 2001 and 2003, after which the company was awarded an oil-services contract in the <a href="/wiki/Karachaganak_Field" title="Karachaganak Field">Karachaganak Field</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>. The company agreed to pay $44.1 million in fines and penalties.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Threat_of_strike_action">Threat of strike action</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Threat of strike action" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On June 11, 2012, during the annual period of negotiations between trade unions, including SAFE, the Norwegian union for energy workers, and their employer counterparts in <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, 114 Baker Hughes employees were called to a <a href="/wiki/Strike_action" title="Strike action">strike action</a>, which would have affected over a dozen installations.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On June 18, 2012, the strike was called off after the parties agreed to a new collective agreement.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Largest_vessel_in_Gulf_of_Mexico">Largest vessel in Gulf of Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Largest vessel in Gulf of Mexico" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2001, the company introduced the largest <a href="/wiki/Hydraulic_fracturing_proppants" title="Hydraulic fracturing proppants">hydraulic fracturing proppants</a> vessel for deepwater work in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>. It was named after <a href="/wiki/Howard_Hughes" title="Howard Hughes">Howard Hughes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposed_acquisition_by_Halliburton">Proposed acquisition by Halliburton</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Proposed acquisition by Halliburton" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In November 2014, the company entered talks with <a href="/wiki/Halliburton" title="Halliburton">Halliburton</a> over a merger deal valued at $34.6 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> If consummated, it would have been the largest merger in the history of the <a href="/wiki/Petroleum_industry" title="Petroleum industry">petroleum industry</a>. The merger was approved by both companies' stockholders and was waiting on approval from several jurisdictions; however, on April 6, 2016, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Justice" title="United States Department of Justice">United States Department of Justice</a> sued to block the transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>There were concerns that the merger would affect competition and prices in the oilfield service industry. Public officials believed that the asset sale would not help the combined company retain competitiveness, as smaller buyers would not utilize their larger rivals’ assets efficiently. On May 1, 2016, the companies terminated the merger agreement.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Merger_with_General_Electric">Merger with General Electric</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Merger with General Electric" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>At the end of October 2016, the company was in negotiations with <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a> to combine with <a href="/wiki/GE_Oil_and_Gas" title="GE Oil and Gas">GE Oil and Gas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The deal was cleared by the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> in May 2017, and by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Justice" title="United States Department of Justice">United States Department of Justice</a> in June 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2016, it was announced that the company would shed its North America Land Pressure Pumping division to form a new <a href="/wiki/BJ_Services" title="BJ Services">BJ Services</a> as part of its divestment agreement with GE.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The new BJ Services merged with ALLIED Services and ALTCEM. The merger was consummated in July 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2019, GE announced it had sold some of its shares in Baker Hughes, therefore losing its majority control of the company.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Baker Hughes subsequently divested from GE becoming an independent company once again.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Acquisitions" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Brown_Oil_Tools">Brown Oil Tools</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Brown Oil Tools" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In 1929, Cicero C. Brown organized Brown Oil Tools in Houston, and patented the first <a href="/wiki/Completion_(oil_and_gas_wells)" title="Completion (oil and gas wells)">liner hanger</a> in 1937. Liner hangers enable drillers to lengthen their casing strings without having the liner pipe extend all the way to the surface. This saves capital cost and reduces weight borne by offshore platforms. In 1978, Hughes Tool Company acquired Brown Oil Tools. In 1970, Baker Oil Tools acquired Lynes, Inc., which produced liner hangers and other completion equipment. In 1978, Baker Oil Tools introduced the Bakerline casing liner hanger. In 1985, the FlexLock Liner Hanger was introduced, extending the performance range and functionality of liner hanger systems. In 1987, the Brown liner hanger technology was merged into Baker Oil Tools. In 1992, Baker Oil Tools introduced the ZXP Liner Top Packer, with expandable metal seals, which set the stage for development of expandable screens, casing systems and liner hangers. In 1994, Baker Oil Tools introduced multilateral completion systems, which enabled operators to install completion tools and perform selective intervention work in multiple horizontal sections from a common main <a href="/wiki/Borehole" title="Borehole">borehole</a>.
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="BJ_Services_Company">BJ Services Company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: BJ Services Company" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>On April 28, 2010, Baker Hughes acquired <a href="/wiki/BJ_Services" title="BJ Services">BJ Services</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> After the division suffered <a href="/wiki/Market_share" title="Market share">market share</a> losses, the company sold a majority interest in BJ Services to <a href="/wiki/Private_equity_firm" title="Private equity firm">private equity firms</a> to obtain approval for the merger with <a href="/wiki/GE_Oil_and_Gas" title="GE Oil and Gas">GE Oil and Gas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="EXLOG">EXLOG</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: EXLOG" data-section="15" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>Exploration Logging Company (EXLOG) was founded in 1952 in <a href="/wiki/Sacramento,_California" title="Sacramento, California">Sacramento, California</a> by Vern C. Jones, and had 800 employees. It was a leader in <a href="/wiki/Mud_logging" title="Mud logging">mud logging</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> In 1972, Baker Hughes acquired EXLOG. 
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Eastman_Christensen">Eastman Christensen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Eastman Christensen" data-section="16" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In 1929, H. John Eastman introduced "controlled <a href="/wiki/Directional_drilling" title="Directional drilling">directional drilling</a>" in Huntington Beach, California, using whipstocks and magnetic survey instruments to deflect the drill pipe from shore-based rigs to reach oil deposits offshore. In 1934, Eastman Whipstock Inc. was founded by Eastman and Roman W. Hines, the same year the company drilled the world's first <a href="/wiki/Relief_well" title="Relief well">relief well</a> to control a blowout in <a href="/wiki/Conroe,_Texas" title="Conroe, Texas">Conroe, Texas</a>, that had been on fire for more than a year.
</p><p>In 1957, <a href="/wiki/Frank_Christensen" title="Frank Christensen">Frank Christensen</a>'s Christensen Diamond Products opened its manufacturing plant in Celle, Germany. The facility built diamond core heads and drilling bits and soon began producing stabilizers, drilling jars and other equipment. In 1977, the Celle engineering and manufacturing team introduced the Navi-Drill line of downhole drilling motors, which has led the drilling industry in performance and reliability for three decades. Other innovations developed in Celle include the industry's first steerable motor system, and the AutoTrak Rotary Closed Loop System. In 1978, Christensen Diamond Products was acquired by Norton Co. and its name changed to Norton Christensen. In 1986 Eastman Whipstock merged with Norton Christensen to form Eastman Christensen.
</p><p>In 1990, the Baker Hughes acquired Eastman Christensen from <a href="/wiki/Norton_Abrasives" title="Norton Abrasives">Norton Abrasives</a> for $550 million. To receive approval from the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Justice" title="United States Department of Justice">United States Department of Justice</a>, the company sold its Hughes Tool diamond bit business.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup>. Baker Hughes combined Eastman Christensen and the remainder of Hughes Tools to form Hughes Christensen.
</p><p>In 1993, Hughes Christensen introduced the AR-Series PDC bits, anti-whirl bits with increased penetration rates up to 100% in some applications and extended bit life as much as four-fold, compared to previous bit designs.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> By 1995, Hughes Christensen's Gold Series PDC line increased drilling efficiency by reducing the frictional forces that can accumulate in front of the cutting edge, reducing the energy required to remove the rock.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> In 1996, patented ChipMaster PDCs, known for their efficiency and durability, were built on the success of the Eggbeater product line. Hughes Christensen next introduced the Genesis HCM bits for steerable motors with patented EZSteer depth-of-cut control technology. This same technology was adapted to Genesis HCR bits for rotary steerable systems, such as the Baker Hughes AutoTrak rotary closed loop system. Genesis ZX PDCs followed with new Zenith cutters. In 2007, the Celle Technology Center became Baker Hughes' leading research and engineering facility in the Eastern Hemisphere.
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Milchem">Milchem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Milchem" data-section="17" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In 1931, Max B. Miller devised a drilling mud using a white clay as a weighting material. To market the new mud, he formed The Milwhite Company in Texas. In the mid-1930s, the company mined barites in conjunction with the Magnet Cove Barium Corporation (later called Magcobar). After a hiatus during World War II, the company resumed grinding operations using barite from a mine in Missouri and conducted mud sales through independent distributors. After 1956, Milwhite Mud Sales Company built its own sales network. In 1963, the company acquired Aquaness, and in 1964 the combination became <a href="/wiki/Milchem" title="Milchem">Milchem</a>. In 1971, Baker Oil Tools acquired <a href="/wiki/Milchem" title="Milchem">Milchem</a>. In 1985, Baker International acquired the drilling fluids division of <a href="/wiki/Newpark_Resources" title="Newpark Resources">Newpark Resources</a> and merged it with Milchem's mud division to form Milpark.
</p><p>Meanwhile, in 1942, Oil Base Drilling Company was founded by George Miller, and made its first application of oil base mud. The company was acquired by Hughes Tool Company in 1979, and renamed Hughes Drilling Fluids in 1982. In 1987, when Baker Hughes was formed, Hughes Drilling Fluids was merged into Milpark, and in 1993, Milpark became a product line within Baker Hughes INTEQ.
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="OCRE">OCRE</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: OCRE" data-section="18" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In 2001, Baker Hughes acquired OCRE (Scotland) Ltd. from Maritime Well Service, a division of <a href="/wiki/Aker_ASA" title="Aker ASA">Aker ASA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Petrolite">Petrolite</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Petrolite" data-section="19" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Petrolite" title="Petrolite">Petrolite</a> was formed in 1930 from the merger of William Barnickel's Tret-O-Lite with Petroleum Rectifying Company of California (PETRECO). In 1997, Baker Hughes acquired Petrolite for $693 million in stock. At that time, Wm. S. Barnickel &amp; Co. owned 47% of Petrolite.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p><p>In 2003, the division acquired Cornerstone Pipeline Inspection Group.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Teleco_Oilfield_Services">Teleco Oilfield Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Teleco Oilfield Services" data-section="20" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>Teleco Oilfield Services was founded in 1972 and introduced the world's first MWD tool in 1978.
</p><p>April 1992, Baker Hughes acquired Teleco Oilfield Services, a provider of directional measurement-while-drilling technology, from <a href="/wiki/Sonat" title="Sonat">Sonat</a> for $200 million cash, preferred stock and royalty from future sales of Teleco's "triple combo" sensors. In 1993, Baker Hughes INTEQ was formed by combining five of the
Company's oilfield divisions.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Teleco had approximately 1,300 employees, including 430, in <a href="/wiki/Meriden,_Connecticut" title="Meriden, Connecticut">Meriden, Connecticut</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Tri-State_and_Wilson">Tri-State and Wilson</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Tri-State and Wilson" data-section="21" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>Tri-State and Wilson companies were acquired by Baker Oil Tools and merged in 1990. Tri-State provided milling operation, spears, and packer retrieving.
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Western_Atlas">Western Atlas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Western Atlas" data-section="22" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Western_Atlas" title="Western Atlas">Western Atlas</a> was divested from <a href="/wiki/Litton_Industries" title="Litton Industries">Litton Industries</a> in 1994.  Western Atlas specialized in finding oil by mapping geological formations with sound waves and using wires to manipulate tools and gauges in oil and natural-gas wells.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p><p>In 1995, <a href="/wiki/Western_Atlas" title="Western Atlas">Western Atlas</a> acquired 50% of PetroAlliance Services Company, Ltd., which offered seismic, well-logging, and integrated project services in the <a href="/wiki/Post-Soviet_states" title="Post-Soviet states">Post-Soviet states</a>. In May 1997, Western Atlas acquired Sungroup Energy Services, a Canadian <a href="/wiki/Well_logging" title="Well logging">well logging</a>, production testing, and completion services provider. During the fourth quarter of 1997, Western Atlas acquired Geosignal, a seismic data processing company; Seismic Resources, a provider of nonexclusive seismic surveys; and ParaMagnetic Logging, a well-logging research company. In the fourth quarter of 1997, the company acquired Heartland Kingfisher, a Canadian well-logging company.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p><p>On August 10, 1998, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Western_Atlas" title="Western Atlas">Western Atlas</a> for $5.5 billion in stock plus the assumption of $700 million in debt.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> The acquisition was made in part to prevent an acquisition of Western Atlas by <a href="/wiki/Halliburton" title="Halliburton">Halliburton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> The acquisition added strong seismic-data operations as well as downhole wireline and testing services.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="1989_acquisitions">1989 acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baker_Hughes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: 1989 acquisitions" data-section="23" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In June 1989, Baker Hughes acquired <a href="/wiki/Bird_Machine_Company" title="Bird Machine Company">Bird Machine Company</a> for $47.5 million. In Addition, Baker Hughes acquired Vetco Services for $37 million and EDECO Petroleum Services for $12.1 million the same year.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
