</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=A._O._Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The A.O. Smith Corporation was founded in 1874 by Charles Jeremiah Smith as <b>C. J. Smith and Sons</b>, a baby carriage and bicycle parts manufacturer. They began forming steel tubing from sheet metal to make <a href="/wiki/Bicycle_frame" title="Bicycle frame">bicycle frames</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Funding_Universe_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Funding_Universe-7">[7]</a></sup> By 1895, the company was the largest bicycle parts manufacturer. In 1899, Arthur Oliver Smith, a son of the founder, developed the world’s first pressed steel <a href="/wiki/Vehicle_frame" title="Vehicle frame">vehicle frame</a> and they later began making frames for the <a href="/wiki/Peerless" class="mw-redirect" title="Peerless">Peerless Automobile Company</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cadillac" title="Cadillac">Cadillac</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford Motor Company</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 1904, the company incorporated in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, as A. O. Smith Company.
</p><p>In 1913, Lloyd Raymond Smith took over, and in 1914, the company introduced the <i>Smith Motor Wheel</i>, a gasoline-powered device for bicycles and in 1915, they began manufacturing the <i><a href="/wiki/Smith_Flyer" title="Smith Flyer">Smith Flyer</a></i>, which they later sold to <b>Briggs &amp; Stratton Company</b> of Milwaukee. A year later in 1916, A.O. Smith was incorporated in New York, and in 1917, they began manufacturing <a href="/wiki/Bomb" title="Bomb">bomb</a> casings for <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>.
</p><p>In the 1920s, company engineers developed the coated welding rod which they used in manufacturing until 1965, as well as the world’s first fully automated automobile frame <a href="/wiki/Factory" title="Factory">factory</a>, with the capability of making a frame every eight seconds, until 1958, the first arc-welded, high-pressure vessel used to refine oil, which the company produced until 1963, and <a href="/wiki/Pipeline_transport" title="Pipeline transport">oil supply line pipes</a>, until 1972.
</p><p>In 1933, the company used the process of fusing glass to steel to make a large single-piece glass-lined brewery tank. Over the next 32 years, they made more than 11,000 glass-lined tanks. In 1936, the company patented the glass-lined <a href="/wiki/Water_heating" title="Water heating">water heater</a>. Three years later, they began mass-producing residential water heaters, but shifted all production to war-time use during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.
</p><p>In 1940, the company acquired <b>Sawyer Electric</b> of Los Angeles, California, a manufacturer of electric motors. In 1942, it once again began to produce bomb casings, as well as <a href="/wiki/Propeller_(aeronautics)" title="Propeller (aeronautics)">aircraft propellers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Landing_gear" title="Landing gear">undercarriages</a>, <a href="/wiki/Torpedo" title="Torpedo">torpedoes</a>, and air flasks. By 1945, the company had built 4.5 million bombs, 16,750 sets of landing gear, and 46,700 propeller blades. They also built nose frames for the <a href="/wiki/B-25" class="mw-redirect" title="B-25">B-25</a> bomber, water heaters, jeep frames, and components for the atomic bomb project. In 1946, they built a 400,000 square foot residential water heater plant in <a href="/wiki/Kankakee,_Illinois" title="Kankakee, Illinois">Kankakee, Illinois</a>. In 1948, they entered the commercial water heater market after acquiring <b>The Burkay Company</b> of <a href="/wiki/Toledo,_Ohio" title="Toledo, Ohio">Toledo, Ohio</a>. In 1949, the company began producing <i>Harvestore</i>, a <a href="/wiki/Glass_fused_to_steel" class="mw-redirect" title="Glass fused to steel">glass-fused-to-steel</a> silo, targeted at dairy and livestock operations.
</p><p>In the 1950s, A. O. Smith acquired <b>Whirl-A-Way Motors</b> of <a href="/wiki/Dayton,_Ohio" title="Dayton, Ohio">Dayton, Ohio</a>, and consolidated its electric motor manufacturing operations there. The motor division later introduced the hermetic motor, a critical component of air conditioning and refrigeration compressors. Its water heater division introduced the first glass-lined commercial water heater, the <i>A. O. Smith Burkay B-65</i>, and later the company established a glass fiber division to replace steel in many applications. It also expanded into supplying oil field pipe and pipe for service stations, eventually becoming <i>Smith Fiberglass Products</i> in 1986.
</p><p>In the early 1960s, the company opened a commercial water heater and boiler plant in <a href="/wiki/Stratford,_Ontario" title="Stratford, Ontario">Stratford, Ontario</a>, Canada. In 1965, the Motor division invented the enclosed canopy, two-compartment motor, a design that improved the reliability in pool pump motors by separating the switching components from the motor windings. In 1967, fourth generation Smith family member, Lloyd B. Smith, was elected chairman and chief executive officer of the company. By 1969, the company had produced its 10 millionth residential water heater, and by 1972, the company had expanded to Europe. In 1974, the <i>Conservationist</i> line of residential water heaters was introduced. In 1976, the Motor division opened a plant in <a href="/wiki/Bray,_Ireland" class="mw-redirect" title="Bray, Ireland">Bray, Ireland</a>, to supply hermetic motors. In 1978, A.O. Smith began manufacturing storage tanks, beginning with <i>Aquastore</i>, a glass-fused-to-steel tank.
</p><p>By the early 1980s, the Automotive Products unit had manufactured over 100 million passenger car frames, and 50 million truck frames, in Milwaukee, and soon after the company opened its first electric motor assembly operations in <a href="/wiki/Ciudad_Ju%C3%A1rez" title="Ciudad Juárez">Ciudad Juárez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ciudad_Acu%C3%B1a" title="Ciudad Acuña">Ciudad Acuña</a>, Mexico. In 1990, it developed the first hermetic motor insulation system compatible with R-134a non-ozone-depleting refrigerant. In 1995, they acquired <b>Peabody TecTank</b> of <a href="/wiki/Parsons,_Kansas" title="Parsons, Kansas">Parsons, Kansas</a>, allowing A.O. Smith to enter the bulk dry storage market.<sup id="cite_ref-CST_Industries_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CST_Industries-9">[9]</a></sup> That same year the company expanded into Asia by entering two joint-venture agreements: <b>Smith Fiberglass Products</b> and <b>Harbin Composites Corporation</b> to make fiberglass pipes for the China's petroleum industry, and <b>Water Products Company</b> and <b>Yuhan Water Heater</b> to make residential water heaters. In 1996, the Water Products division introduced the <i>Cyclone XHE</i> commercial water heater. In 1997, after 90 years in the automotive industry, A.O. Smith sold its Automotive Products Company to <b><a href="/wiki/Tower_International" title="Tower International">Tower International</a></b>. In 1997, they acquired <b>UPPCO, Inc.</b>, making A.O Smith the world’s leading manufacturer of C-frame sub<a href="/wiki/Fractional_horsepower_motor" class="mw-redirect" title="Fractional horsepower motor">fractional horsepower motors</a>. In 1998, they acquired <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a>’s domestic compressor motor business, as well as the electric motor division of <b><a href="/wiki/Magnetek" title="Magnetek">Magnetek</a></b>. By the end of the decade, Water Products Company had bought out its joint venture partner in Asia, and had opened a plant in <a href="/wiki/Nanjing,_China" class="mw-redirect" title="Nanjing, China">Nanjing, China</a>.
</p><p>In the 2000s, A. O. Smith made a number of acquisitions, by acquiring <b>State Industries, Inc.</b>, and its subsidiary <b>APCOM Inc.</b>, <b>Shenzhen Speeda Industrial Co. Ltd.</b>, and <b>Athens Products</b>, a supplier of hermetic motors for scroll compressor applications, as well as <b>The Changheng Group</b> of <a href="/wiki/Changzhou" title="Changzhou">Changzhou</a>, China, a manufacturer of fractional horsepower motors for HVAC applications, <b>Taicang Special Motor Company, Ltd.</b>, of <a href="/wiki/Suzhou" title="Suzhou">Suzhou</a>, China, <b>Yueyang Zhongmin Special Electrical Machinery Co. Ltd.</b>, <b>Lochinvar Corporation</b>, a manufacturer of high efficiency condensing boilers for hot water and hydronic heating applications, and in 2006, <b>GSW Inc.</b> a water heaters manufacturer for the Canadian market, and <b>The American Water Heater Company</b>, the exclusive supplier of water heaters to <a href="/wiki/Lowe%27s" title="Lowe's">Lowe's</a>, carrying the Whirlpool brand. In 2009, the company entered the water purification industry with a new venture, <b>A. O. Smith (Shanghai) Water Treatment Products Co. Ltd</b>. In 2010, A. O. Smith opened a 76,000 square foot residential water heater manufacturing plant in <a href="/wiki/Bangalore,_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Bangalore, India">Bangalore, India</a>, which later expanded to 297000 sq ft operation with expansion to manufacture of water purification products. <b>Takagi Industrial Company'</b>s North American operations were acquired in 2010.. In 2011, A.O. Smith sold its electric motor business to <a href="/wiki/Regal-Beloit" class="mw-redirect" title="Regal-Beloit">Regal-Beloit</a>.
</p><p>On August 8, 2016, A.O.Smith, acquired Austin-based Water filtration Company Aquasana (previously known as Sun Water Systems) from L' Catterton for $87 Million.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 2016, A.O Smith was successfully sued by a Chicago area family because the company has refused to include a safety feature in their water heaters to prevent scalding deaths.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Immediately after receiving the verdict, A.O. Smith appealed the decision in the Supreme Court and in December 2018 successfully got the verdict reversed.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Brands">Brands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=A._O._Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Brands" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>A.O. Smith operates under the following brand names around the world:  A.O. Smith (United States, Canada, China, Europe, Turkey, India and Vietnam), GSW (Canada), John Wood (Canada), State Water Heaters, American Water Heaters, Reliance Water Heaters, Lochinvar, Takagi and U.S. Craftmaster Water Heaters.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Products">Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=A._O._Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Products" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>A. O. Smith Water Products Company is headquartered in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. The Ashland City, TN location is home of the world’s largest water heater factory.<sup id="cite_ref-Made_In_Tennessee_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Made_In_Tennessee-5">[5]</a></sup> A.O. Smith Corporation manufactures the following types of water heaters for residential uses: gas and propane, hybrid, electric, tankless, and solar. The company manufactures following types of water heaters for commercial uses: oil-fired, gas and electric, as well as <a href="/wiki/Boiler" title="Boiler">boilers</a>, storage tanks, and skid systems. They also provide the following accessories: pump tanks, and expansion tanks.<sup id="cite_ref-Emerson_Swan,_Inc._13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Emerson_Swan,_Inc.-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="A.O._Smith_Foundation">A.O. Smith Foundation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=A._O._Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: A.O. Smith Foundation" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p><b>The A. O. Smith Foundation</b> is a private, nonprofit organization, founded in 1955, that has contributed nearly $35 million to qualifying charitable, educational, scientific, literary, and civic organizations located primarily in communities where A. O. Smith Corporation has facilities. The support for the Foundation comes from the profits of A. O. Smith Corporation.
</p>
