<p><b>Abbott Laboratories</b> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> medical devices and <a href="/wiki/Health_care" title="Health care">health care</a> company with headquarters in the Abbott Park Business Center in <a href="/wiki/Lake_Bluff" class="mw-redirect" title="Lake Bluff">Lake Bluff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>, United States. The company was founded by Chicago <a href="/wiki/Physician" title="Physician">physician</a> Wallace Calvin Abbott in 1888 to formulate known drugs; today, it sells medical devices, diagnostics, branded generic medicines and nutritional products. It split off the research-based pharmaceuticals into <a href="/wiki/AbbVie" class="mw-redirect" title="AbbVie">AbbVie</a> in 2013.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abbott_Laboratories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p><span id="Wallace_Abbott"></span>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dr._Wallace_C._Abbott.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Dr._Wallace_C._Abbott.jpg/220px-Dr._Wallace_C._Abbott.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="132" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="768"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 132px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Dr._Wallace_C._Abbott.jpg/220px-Dr._Wallace_C._Abbott.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="132" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dr._Wallace_C._Abbott.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Dr. Wallace C. Abbott</div></div></div>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Foundation_and_early_history">Foundation and early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abbott_Laboratories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Foundation and early history" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1888 at the age of 30, Wallace Abbott (1857–1921), an 1885 graduate of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">University of Michigan</a>, founded the <b>Abbott Alkaloidal Company</b> in Ravenswood, Chicago. At the time, he was a practicing physician and owned a drug store. His innovation was the use of the active part of a <a href="/wiki/Herbalism" class="mw-redirect" title="Herbalism">medicinal plant</a>, generally an <a href="/wiki/Alkaloid" title="Alkaloid">alkaloid</a> (e.g., <a href="/wiki/Morphine" title="Morphine">morphine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quinine" title="Quinine">quinine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Strychnine" title="Strychnine">strychnine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Codeine" title="Codeine">codeine</a>), which he formed into tiny "dosimetric granules". This approach was successful since it produced more consistent and effective dosages for patients.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1922, the company moved from Ravenswood to North Chicago, Illinois.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="International_expansion">International expansion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abbott_Laboratories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International expansion" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Abbott's first international affiliate was in London in 1907, and the company later added an affiliate in Montreal, Canada (Fact 21). Abbott started operations in <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> as a marketing affiliate in 1948; the company has steadily expanded to comprise a work force of over 1500 employees. Currently, two manufacturing facilities located at Landhi and Korangi in <a href="/wiki/Karachi" title="Karachi">Karachi</a> continue to produce pharmaceutical products.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Expansion continued in 1962 when Abbott entered into a joint venture with Dainippon Pharmaceutical Co., Ltd., of Osaka, Japan, to manufacture radio-pharmaceuticals. In 1964, it merged with Ross Laboratories, making Ross a wholly-owned subsidiary of Abbott, and Richard Ross gained a seat on Abbott's board of directors until his retirement in 1983.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The acquisition of Ross brought Similac under the Abbott umbrella. In the years following the acquisition, Pedialyte and Ensure were introduced as nutritional products by Ross Laboratories while under Abbott's leadership.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 1965, Abbott's expansion in Europe continued with offices in Italy and France. Abbott Laboratories has been present in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> for over 100 years through its subsidiary Abbott India Limited and it is currently <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>'s largest healthcare products company.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>According to Harvard professor <a href="/wiki/Lester_Grinspoon" title="Lester Grinspoon">Lester Grinspoon</a> and Peter Hedblom, "In 1966 Abbott Laboratories sold the equivalent of two million doses of methamphetamine in powder form to a Long Island criminal dealer".<sup id="cite_ref-grinspoon23_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grinspoon23-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>Today, Abbott operates in over 160 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="21st_century">21st century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abbott_Laboratories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 21st century" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2001, the company acquired Knoll, the pharmaceutical division of <a href="/wiki/BASF" title="BASF">BASF</a>. In 2002, it divested the <a href="/wiki/Selsun_Blue" title="Selsun Blue">Selsun Blue</a> brand to <a href="/wiki/Chattem" title="Chattem">Chattem</a>. Later in 2002, the company sold <a href="/wiki/Clear_Eyes" class="mw-redirect" title="Clear Eyes">Clear Eyes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Murine" class="mw-redirect" title="Murine">Murine</a> to <a href="/wiki/Prestige_Brands" title="Prestige Brands">Prestige Brands</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 2004, the company acquired TheraSense, a <a href="/wiki/Diabetes" title="Diabetes">diabetes</a>-care company, which it merged with its MediSense division to become Abbott Diabetes Care. In 2006, Abbott assisted <a href="/wiki/Boston_Scientific" title="Boston Scientific">Boston Scientific</a> in its purchase of <a href="/wiki/Guidant" title="Guidant">Guidant</a> Corporation. As part of the agreement, Abbott purchased the vascular device division of Guidant.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In 2007, Abbott acquired Kos Pharmaceuticals for $3.7 billion in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> At the time of acquisition, Kos marketed <a href="/wiki/Niaspan" class="mw-redirect" title="Niaspan">Niaspan</a>, which raises levels of "good", or HDL, cholesterol and <a href="/wiki/Advicor" class="mw-redirect" title="Advicor">Advicor</a>, a Niaspan combination drug for patients with multiple lipid disorders.
</p><p>In January 2007, the company agreed to sell its <i>in vitro</i> diagnostics and Point-of-Care diagnostics divisions to <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a> for more than $8 billion. These units were slated to be integrated into the <a href="/wiki/GE_Healthcare" title="GE Healthcare">GE Healthcare</a> business unit. The transaction was approved by the boards of directors of Abbott and GE and was targeted to close in the first half of 2007. However, on 11 July 2007, Abbott announced that it had terminated its agreement with GE because the parties could not agree on the terms of the deal.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>On 8 September 2007, the company completed the sale of the UK manufacturing plant at <a href="/wiki/Queenborough" title="Queenborough">Queenborough</a> to Aesica Pharmaceuticals, a private equity-owned UK manufacturer. No announcements have been made restricting the movement of staff to Abbott unlike other sell outs.
</p><p>In November 2007, Abbott announced that Ross Products would be renamed Abbott Nutrition.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>On 26 February 2009, the company completed its acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Medical_Optics" class="mw-redirect" title="Advanced Medical Optics">Advanced Medical Optics</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Ana,_California" title="Santa Ana, California">Santa Ana, California</a>. Abbott sold this business to <a href="/wiki/Johnson_%26_Johnson" title="Johnson &amp; Johnson">Johnson &amp; Johnson</a> in February 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In 2009, Abbott opened a satellite research and development facility at <a href="/wiki/Research_Park,_University_of_Illinois_at_Urbana-Champaign" class="mw-redirect" title="Research Park, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign">Research Park, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2010, Abbott completed its $6.2 billion (EUR 4.5 billion) acquisition of the pharmaceuticals unit of <a href="/wiki/Solvay_S.A." title="Solvay S.A.">Solvay S.A.</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> This provided Abbott with a large and complementary portfolio of pharmaceutical products and also expanded its presence in key emerging markets.<sup id="cite_ref-Abbott-Feb-2010-8-K_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Abbott-Feb-2010-8-K-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>On 22 March 2010, the company completed its acquisition of a <a href="/wiki/Hollywood,_Florida" title="Hollywood, Florida">Hollywood, Florida</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Laboratory_information_management_system" title="Laboratory information management system">LIMS</a> company <a href="/wiki/STARLIMS" class="mw-redirect" title="STARLIMS">STARLIMS</a>. Under the terms of the deal, Abbott Laboratories acquired the company for $14 per share in an all-cash transaction valued at $123 million. On 21 May 2010, Abbott Laboratories said it would buy Piramal Healthcare Ltd.'s Healthcare Solutions unit for $2.2<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> billion to become the biggest drug company in India.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Spin-offs">Spin-offs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abbott_Laboratories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Spin-offs" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, Abbott spun off its hospital products division into a new 14,000 employee company named <a href="/wiki/Hospira" title="Hospira">Hospira</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Hospira was later acquired by <a href="/wiki/Pfizer" title="Pfizer">Pfizer</a> in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2011, Abbott announced that it planned to separate into two companies, one research-based pharmaceuticals and the other in medical devices, generic drugs sold internationally, and diagnostics, with the latter retaining the Abbott name.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Abbott Nutrition, whose products include Similac, Pedialyte, Glucerna, and Ensure, also retained the Abbott name.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-27">[27]</a></sup> The company announced that the other company would be named <a href="/wiki/AbbVie" class="mw-redirect" title="AbbVie">AbbVie</a> in March 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-27">[27]</a></sup> In preparation for the reorganization, Abbott made severe budget cuts and took a $478 million charge in Q3-2012 to pay for the restructuring.<sup id="cite_ref-frost2012_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-frost2012-28">[28]</a></sup> The separation was effective as of 1 January 2013.  AbbVie was officially listed in the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> on 2 January 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Further_developments">Further developments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abbott_Laboratories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Further developments" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:President_Trump_Delivers_Remarks_During_a_Coronavirus_Update_Briefing_(49720569077).jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/President_Trump_Delivers_Remarks_During_a_Coronavirus_Update_Briefing_%2849720569077%29.jpg/220px-President_Trump_Delivers_Remarks_During_a_Coronavirus_Update_Briefing_%2849720569077%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="2000" data-file-height="1333"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 147px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/President_Trump_Delivers_Remarks_During_a_Coronavirus_Update_Briefing_%2849720569077%29.jpg/220px-President_Trump_Delivers_Remarks_During_a_Coronavirus_Update_Briefing_%2849720569077%29.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="147" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:President_Trump_Delivers_Remarks_During_a_Coronavirus_Update_Briefing_(49720569077).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>US President <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a> displays a COVID-19 testing kit from Abbott Laboratories in March 2020</div></div></div>
<p>On 16 May 2014, it was announced that Abbott would acquire the <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a> Kalo Pharma Internacional S.L. for $2.9 billion in order to secure the 73% it held of Chilean pharmaceutical company, <a href="/wiki/CFR_Pharmaceuticals" title="CFR Pharmaceuticals">CFR Pharmaceuticals</a>, which the company said would more than double its branded generic drug portfolio.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2014, the company acquired Russian pharmaceutical manufacturer Veropharm (<a href="/wiki/Voronezh" title="Voronezh">Voronezh</a>) in a deal worth $410 million, which included three manufacturing facilities.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-30">[30]</a></sup> Abbott, which already employs 1,400 people in Russia, said it planned to set up a manufacturing presence in the country when the deal closed.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2015, the company announced it had completed its acquisition of Tendyne Holdings, Inc., a private medical device company focused on developing minimally invasive mitral valve replacement therapies.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-35">[35]</a></sup> Tendyne was acquired for a total transaction value of $250 million. <sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-35">[35]</a></sup> In January 2020, the Tendyne Mitral Valve became the world's first commercially available solution for Mitral Valve Replacement Technology. Abbott obtained CE Mark for the device which now makes it possible to implant it in Europe outside of a clinical setting. The US clinical study for federal approval is still ongoing. <sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2016, the company announced it would acquire <a href="/wiki/Alere" title="Alere">Alere</a> for $5.8 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> In January 2017, Abbott announced it would acquire <a href="/wiki/St._Jude_Medical" title="St. Jude Medical">St. Jude Medical</a> for $25 billion (each share receiving $46.75 in cash &amp; 0.8708 shares of Abbott common stock, equating to an approximate value of $85).<sup id="cite_ref-:0_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, the FDA approved Abbott's FreeStyle Libre glucose monitoring system. The system is designed to read glucose levels through a self-applied sensor and does not require standard finger sticks.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p><p>On 3 October 2017, the company closed the Alere acquisition making the surviving entity the market leader player in the $7 billion point-of-care diagnostic space within the broader $50 billion in-vitro diagnostics market with this takeover.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> With the acquisition of Alere, the company also obtain the subsidiary Arriva Medical, which is the largest mail-order diabetic supplier. Arriva Medical announced business closure after Abbott acquisition effective 31 December 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2018, Reuters reported that "Abbott Laboratories (ABT.N) is among the top five companies for branded generic drugs in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, the company’s chief financial officer, Brian Yoor, said in January."<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2018, Abbott became the first medical device company to introduce a smartphone app glucose reader in the United States when it received FDA clearance to launch FreeStyle LibreLink.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2019, Abbott exercised its option to purchase Cephea Valve Technologies, Inc. who are developing a less-invasive replacement heart valve for people with mitral valve disease.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2020, Abbott received <a href="/wiki/Emergency_use_authorization" class="mw-redirect" title="Emergency use authorization">emergency use authorization</a> (EUA) from the FDA for a <a href="/wiki/2019-nCoV" class="mw-redirect" title="2019-nCoV">2019-nCoV</a> test to help address the <a href="/wiki/2019-20_coronavirus_pandemic" class="mw-redirect" title="2019-20 coronavirus pandemic">2019-20 coronavirus pandemic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Abbott's point-of-care test is regarded as a valuable development due to its small size, which is comparable to a small toaster, and rapid results – 5-minute positive, 13-minute negative.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> Detroit became the first city to receive these tests on April 1, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-49" <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Double_Irish_arrangement" title="Double Irish arrangement">Double Irish arrangement</a></div>
<p>On 19 March 2019, it was reported that Abbott was a long-term user of the <a href="/wiki/Double_Irish" class="mw-redirect" title="Double Irish">Double Irish</a> tax structure, a legal but controversial Irish taxation tool used by U.S. multinationals to reduce U.S. corporate taxes on non-U.S profits.<sup id="cite_ref-di_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-di-52">[52]</a></sup> Abbott's Irish holding company, the Bermuda-resident Abbott Laboratories Vascular Enterprises (ALVE), employed no staff in 2017, but was responsible for distributing Abbot's products and licensing its technology worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-di_52-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-di-52">[52]</a></sup> Newly filed accounts showed that ALVE was incorporated in 2003 and had a pre-tax profit of €2 billion in 2016 and 2017 on revenues of €5.2 billion; no taxation was paid on these profits.<sup id="cite_ref-di_52-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-di-52">[52]</a></sup> ALVE had never filed accounts in Ireland since 2003 as it was structured as an <a href="/wiki/Unlimited_liability_company" class="mw-redirect" title="Unlimited liability company">unlimited liability company</a> (ULL); however, new EU accounts directives required ALVE to file Irish accounts in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-di_52-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-di-52">[52]</a></sup> These accounts listed ALVE's registered office as the address of Ireland's largest tax-law firm, <a href="/wiki/Matheson_(law_firm)" title="Matheson (law firm)">Matheson</a>, who have been identified with Double Irish tax structures for Microsoft and Google.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-53">[53]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-di_52-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-di-52">[52]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Management">Management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abbott_Laboratories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Management" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Miles_D._White" title="Miles D. White">Miles D. White</a> is the executive chairman of Abbott.<sup id="cite_ref-frost2012_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-frost2012-28">[28]</a></sup> He joined Abbott in 1984, serving in management positions including senior vice president of diagnostic operations, executive vice president, and long-time CEO. He was elected to the Board of Directors in April 1998, to Chief Executive Officer in 1998, and to Chairman of the Board in April 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2019, White announced that he was stepping down as CEO after 21 years in charge of Abbott. At the end of March 2020, Robert B. Ford, a long-time Abbott executive, took over as the company's president and chief operating officer.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> White remains the executive chairman.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p><p>Ford joined Abbott in 1996, serving in various management positions including executive vice president of the company’s medical device business, which is now the company’s largest operation.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Humira">Humira</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abbott_Laboratories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Humira" data-section="21" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In March 2003, British company Cambridge Antibody Technology (CAT) stated its wish to "initiate discussions regarding the applicability of the royalty offset provisions for Humira" (<a href="/wiki/Adalimumab" title="Adalimumab">Adalimumab</a>) with Abbott Laboratories in the High Court of London. In December 2004, the judgment ruled for CAT.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup></p><p>Abbott was required to pay CAT US$255 million, some of which was to be passed to its partners in development. Of this sum, the <a href="/wiki/Medical_Research_Council_(United_Kingdom)" title="Medical Research Council (United Kingdom)">Medical Research Council (United Kingdom)</a> (MRC) received US$191M, and in addition, Abbott was asked to pay the MRC a further US$7.5M over five years from 2006, providing that Humira remains on the market.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Depakote">Depakote</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abbott_Laboratories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Depakote" data-section="22" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On 2 October 2012, the company was charged with a $500 million fine and $198.5 million forfeiture for illegal marketing of <a href="/wiki/Depakote" class="mw-redirect" title="Depakote">Depakote</a> for uses not approved by the FDA. The court also ordered Abbott to a five-year term of probation and court supervision.<sup id="cite_ref-doj_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-doj-68">[68]</a></sup>
Shareholders then brought derivative suits against the company directors for breach of fiduciary duty.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup></p><p>Following Abbott's spinoff of its research-based pharmaceuticals business, it no longer owns the commercial rights and associated responsibilities for Humira and Depakote.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup></p>
