</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_Materials&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Founded in 1967 by Michael A. McNeilly and others, Applied Materials <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">went public</a> in 1972.  In subsequent years, the company diversified, until James C. Morgan became CEO in 1976 and returned the company's focus to its core business of semiconductor manufacturing equipment.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> By 1978, sales increased by 17%.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 1984, Applied Materials became the first U.S. semiconductor equipment manufacturer to open its own technology center in Japan<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and the first semiconductor equipment company to operate a service center in China.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In 1987, Applied introduced a CVD machine called the Precision 5000, which differed from existing machines by incorporating diverse processes into a single machine that had multiple process chambers.<sup id="cite_ref-Silicon_Dragon_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Silicon_Dragon-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 1992, the corporation settled a lawsuit with three former employees for an estimated $600,000. The suit complained that the employees were driven out of the company after complaining about the courses <a href="/wiki/Applied_Scholastics" title="Applied Scholastics">Applied Scholastics</a> had been hired to teach there.<sup id="cite_ref-Scientologizing_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Scientologizing-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 1993, the Applied Materials' Precision 5000 was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Smithsonian_Institution" title="Smithsonian Institution">Smithsonian Institution</a>'s permanent collection of <a href="/wiki/Information_Age" title="Information Age">Information Age</a> technology.<sup id="cite_ref-Silicon_Dragon_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Silicon_Dragon-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In November 1996, Applied Materials acquired two <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israeli</a> companies for an aggregate amount of $285 million. Opal Technologies and Orbot Instruments for $175 million and $110 million in cash, respectively. Orbot produces systems for inspecting patterned silicon wafers for yield enhancement during the semiconductor manufacturing process, as well as systems for inspecting masks used during the patterning process. Opal develops and manufactures high-speed <a href="/wiki/Metrology" title="Metrology">metrology</a> systems used by semiconductor manufacturers to verify critical dimensions during the production of integrated circuits.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In 2000, <a href="/wiki/Etec_Systems,_Inc." title="Etec Systems, Inc.">Etec Systems, Inc.</a> was purchased.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>On June 27, 2001, Applied acquired <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israeli</a> company <a href="/wiki/Oramir" title="Oramir">Oramir Semiconductor Equipment Ltd.</a>, a supplier of laser cleaning technologies for semiconductor wafers, in a purchase business combination for $21 million in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-Applied_Materials,_Inc.-10-Q_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Applied_Materials,_Inc.-10-Q-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2008, Applied Materials purchased an Italian company Baccini, a designer of tools used in manufacturing solar cells.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In 2009, Applied Materials opened its Solar Technology Center—the world's largest commercial solar energy research and development facility in Xi'an, China.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>Applied Materials' acquisition of Semitool Inc. was completed in December 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>Applied Materials announced its acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Varian_Semiconductor" title="Varian Semiconductor">Varian Semiconductor</a> in May 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>Applied Materials announced its merger with <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Electron" title="Tokyo Electron">Tokyo Electron</a> on September 24, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> If approved by government regulators, the combined company, to be called Eteris,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> would be the world's largest supplier of semiconductor processing equipment, with a total market value of more than $30 billion.
</p><p>But on April 27, 2015, Applied Materials announced that its merger with Tokyo Electron has been scrapped due to <a href="/wiki/Antitrust" class="mw-redirect" title="Antitrust">antitrust</a> concerns and fears of dominating the semiconductor equipment industry.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>Applied Materials is named among FORTUNE World's Most Admired Companies in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In 2019, Applied Materials bought semiconductor equipment manufacturer (and former <a href="/wiki/Hitachi" title="Hitachi">Hitachi</a> group member) Kokusai Electric Corporation from private equity firm KKR, for $2.2 billion. <sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Finances">Finances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_Materials&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Finances" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">

Applied Materials market capitalization was valued at over US$36.6 billion in November 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>

<p>Applied is organized into three major business sectors: Semiconductor Systems, Applied Global Services, and Display and Adjacent Markets.<sup id="cite_ref-Aug16_PR_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aug16_PR-28">[28]</a></sup> Applied Materials also operates a <a href="/wiki/Venture_Capital" class="mw-redirect" title="Venture Capital">venture</a> investing arm called Applied Ventures.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Semiconductor_Systems">Semiconductor Systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_Materials&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Semiconductor Systems" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The company develops and manufactures equipment used in the wafer fabrication steps of <a href="/wiki/Fabrication_(semiconductor)" class="mw-redirect" title="Fabrication (semiconductor)">creating a semiconductor device</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Atomic_layer_deposition" title="Atomic layer deposition">atomic layer deposition</a> (ALD), <a href="/wiki/Chemical_vapor_deposition" title="Chemical vapor deposition">chemical vapor deposition</a> (CVD), <a href="/wiki/Physical_vapor_deposition" title="Physical vapor deposition">physical vapor deposition</a> (PVD), <a href="/wiki/Rapid_thermal_processing" title="Rapid thermal processing">rapid thermal processing</a> (RTP), <a href="/wiki/Chemical_mechanical_polishing" class="mw-redirect" title="Chemical mechanical polishing">chemical mechanical polishing</a> (CMP), etch, <a href="/wiki/Ion_implantation" title="Ion implantation">ion implantation</a> and wafer inspection.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The company acquired Semitool for this group in late 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> In 2019, Applied Materials agreed to buy semiconductor manufacturer Kokusai for $2.2 Billion.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Applied_Global_Services">Applied Global Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_Materials&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Applied Global Services" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The Applied Global Services (AGS) group offers equipment installation support and warranty extended support, as well as maintenance support. AGS also offers new and refurbished equipment, as well as upgrades and enhancements for installed base equipment. This sector also includes automation software for manufacturing environments.<sup id="cite_ref-Aug16_PR_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aug16_PR-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Display_and_Adjacent_Markets">Display and Adjacent Markets</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_Materials&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Display and Adjacent Markets" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>AGS combined an existing business unit with the display business of <a href="/wiki/Applied_Films_Corporation" title="Applied Films Corporation">Applied Films Corporation</a>, acquired in mid-2006.
</p><p>The manufacturing process for <a href="/wiki/TFT_LCD" class="mw-redirect" title="TFT LCD">TFT LCDs</a> (thin film transistor liquid crystal displays), commonly employed in computer monitors and televisions, is similar to that employed for <a href="/wiki/Integrated_circuit" title="Integrated circuit">integrated circuits</a>. In cleanroom environments both TFT-LCD and integrated circuit production use <a href="/wiki/Photolithography" title="Photolithography">photolithography</a>, chemical and physical vapor deposition, and testing.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Energy_and_Environmental_Solutions_.28former_sector.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Energy_and_Environmental_Solutions_(former_sector)">Energy and Environmental Solutions (former sector)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_Materials&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Energy and Environmental Solutions (former sector)" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2006, the company acquired Applied Films, a glass coating and web coating business. Also in 2006, Applied announced it was entering the solar manufacturing equipment business. The solar, glass and web businesses were organized into the company's Energy and Environmental Solutions (EES) sector.
</p><p>In 2007, Applied announced the Applied SunFab <a href="/wiki/Thin_film" title="Thin film">thin film</a> photovoltaic module production line, with <a href="/wiki/Single-junction_cell" class="mw-redirect" title="Single-junction cell">single</a> or <a href="/wiki/Tandem_cell" class="mw-redirect" title="Tandem cell">tandem junction</a> capability. SunFab applies <a href="/w/index.php?title=Silicon_thin_film&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Silicon thin film (page does not exist)">silicon thin film</a> layers to glass substrate that then produce electricity when exposed to sunlight. In 2009, the company's SunFab line was certified by the <a href="/wiki/International_Electrotechnical_Commission" title="International Electrotechnical Commission">International Electrotechnical Commission</a> (IEC).<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> In 2010, Applied announced that it was abandoning the thin film market and closing down their SunFab division.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Also in 2007, the company acquired privately held, Switzerland-based HCT Shaping Systems SA, a specialist in wafer sawing tools for both solar and semiconductor wafer manufacture, paying approximately $475 million.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p><p>In 2008, Applied acquired privately held, Italy-based Baccini SpA for $330M, company that worked in the metallization steps of solar cell manufacturing.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The company was listed at the top of <a href="/w/index.php?title=VLSI_Research&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="VLSI Research (page does not exist)">VLSI Research</a>'s list of supplier of photovoltaic manufacturing equipment for 2008, with sales of $797M.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p><p>Since July 2016 this sector is no longer reported separately. Remaining solar business activities have been included in "Corporate and Others".<sup id="cite_ref-Aug16_PR_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aug16_PR-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_Materials&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Facilities" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>Applied operates in many locations globally, including in Europe, Japan, North America (principally the United States), Israel, China, Italy, India, Korea, Southeast Asia and Taiwan.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Applied moved into its Bowers Avenue headquarters in Santa Clara, CA, in 1974.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
