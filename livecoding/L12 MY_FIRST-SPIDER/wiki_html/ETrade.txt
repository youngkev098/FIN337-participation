<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">This article is about the financial company. For electronic trading, see <a href="/wiki/Electronic_trading_platform" title="Electronic trading platform">electronic trading platform</a>. For East Trade Street in Charlotte, see <a href="/wiki/Trade_Street" class="mw-redirect" title="Trade Street">Trade Street</a>.</div>
<p><b>E-Trade Financial Corporation</b> (stylized as <b>E*TRADE</b>) offers an <a href="/wiki/Electronic_trading_platform" title="Electronic trading platform">electronic trading platform</a> to <a href="/wiki/Trade" title="Trade">trade</a> <a href="/wiki/Financial_asset" title="Financial asset">financial assets</a> including <a href="/wiki/Common_stock" title="Common stock">common stocks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Preferred_stock" title="Preferred stock">preferred stocks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Futures_contract" title="Futures contract">futures contracts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Exchange-traded_fund" title="Exchange-traded fund">exchange-traded funds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Option_(finance)" title="Option (finance)">options</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mutual_funds" class="mw-redirect" title="Mutual funds">mutual funds</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fixed_income" title="Fixed income">fixed income</a> investments. It also provides services for <a href="/wiki/Employee_stock_ownership_plan" class="mw-redirect" title="Employee stock ownership plan">employee stock ownership plans</a>, student loan benefit administration, advisor services, <a href="/wiki/Margin_(finance)" title="Margin (finance)">margin</a> lending, <a href="/wiki/Online_banking" title="Online banking">online banking</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cash_management" title="Cash management">cash management</a> services.

<p>As of December 31, 2018, the company had 4.9 million brokerage accounts and a total <a href="/wiki/Margin_(finance)" title="Margin (finance)">margin</a> receivables balance of $9.560 billion. In 2018, the company processed 282,243 daily average revenue trades. In 2018, 64% of net revenue was from <a href="/wiki/Interest_income" class="mw-redirect" title="Interest income">interest income</a>, 17% of net revenue was from <a href="/wiki/Commission_(remuneration)" title="Commission (remuneration)">commissions</a> for order execution, 15% was from <a href="/wiki/Payment_for_order_flow" title="Payment for order flow">payment for order flow</a> and management services, and 4% was from other sources. The company has 30 <a href="/wiki/Branch_(banking)" title="Branch (banking)">branches</a>.
</p><p>The company is organized in <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a> and headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Arlington,_VA" class="mw-redirect" title="Arlington, VA">Arlington, VA</a>. The company's primary offices are in <a href="/wiki/Alpharetta,_Georgia" title="Alpharetta, Georgia">Alpharetta, Georgia</a>; <a href="/wiki/Jersey_City,_New_Jersey" title="Jersey City, New Jersey">Jersey City, New Jersey</a>; <a href="/wiki/Arlington,_Virginia" class="mw-redirect" title="Arlington, Virginia">Arlington, Virginia</a>; <a href="/wiki/Sandy,_Utah" title="Sandy, Utah">Sandy, Utah</a>; <a href="/wiki/Menlo_Park,_California" title="Menlo Park, California">Menlo Park, California</a>; <a href="/wiki/Centennial,_Colorado" title="Centennial, Colorado">Centennial, Colorado</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Boston,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston, Massachusetts">Boston, Massachusetts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>On February 20, 2020, <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Stanley" title="Morgan Stanley">Morgan Stanley</a> announced that it would acquire E-Trade for $13 billion in stock.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=E-Trade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>In 1982, <a href="/wiki/William_A._Porter" title="William A. Porter">William A. Porter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bernard_A._Newcomb" title="Bernard A. Newcomb">Bernard A. Newcomb</a> founded TradePlus in <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California" title="Palo Alto, California">Palo Alto, California</a>, with $15,000 in capital. In 1991, Porter and Newcomb founded E-Trade Securities, Inc., with several hundred thousand dollars of startup capital from TradePlus. E*Trade offered its trading services via <a href="/wiki/America_Online" class="mw-redirect" title="America Online">America Online</a> and <a href="/wiki/Compuserve" class="mw-redirect" title="Compuserve">Compuserve</a>. In 1994, its revenues neared $11 million, up from $850,000 in 1992.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>By June 30, 1996, the company had 73,000 accounts and processed 8,000 trades per day, with quarterly revenue of $15 million.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>On August 16, 1996, the company became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2001, the company acquired Web Street Securities for $45 million in stock.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2004, <a href="/wiki/Toronto-Dominion_Bank" title="Toronto-Dominion Bank">Toronto-Dominion Bank</a> held talks to merge its <a href="/wiki/TD_Waterhouse" title="TD Waterhouse">TD Waterhouse</a> discount brokerage with E*Trade, but the two sides could not come to an agreement over control of the merged entity.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> TD Waterhouse instead merged with Ameritrade to form <a href="/wiki/TD_Ameritrade" title="TD Ameritrade">TD Ameritrade</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> After the merger, E*Trade continued talks to merge with TD Ameritrade but the two sides could not agree on price and governance rights.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:DLJ_direct_metrocard_ad_2001.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/DLJ_direct_metrocard_ad_2001.jpg/220px-DLJ_direct_metrocard_ad_2001.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="350" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1536" data-file-height="2442"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 350px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/DLJ_direct_metrocard_ad_2001.jpg/220px-DLJ_direct_metrocard_ad_2001.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="350" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:DLJ_direct_metrocard_ad_2001.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>2001 MetroCard advertisement for DLJ direct</div></div></div>
<p>In August 2005, the company acquired Harrisdirect from <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_Montreal" title="Bank of Montreal">Bank of Montreal</a>. Two months later, E*Trade acquired Brown &amp; Company (aka BrownCo) from <a href="/wiki/JPMorgan_Chase" title="JPMorgan Chase">JPMorgan Chase</a> for $1.6 billion in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> As a result, E*Trade closed its office in Rancho Cordova, California, on August 31, 2006 and terminated 500 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2007, Etrade Australia, which was a separately operated company owned 6% by the company, was purchased by <a href="/wiki/ANZ_Bank" class="mw-redirect" title="ANZ Bank">ANZ Bank</a> for $432 million.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>On November 29, 2007, E-Trade announced a transaction in which <a href="/wiki/Citadel_LLC" title="Citadel LLC">Citadel LLC</a> invested $2.5 billion in cash in exchange for the company's securitized <a href="/wiki/Subprime_mortgage" class="mw-redirect" title="Subprime mortgage">subprime mortgages</a>, <a href="/wiki/Collateralized_debt_obligation" title="Collateralized debt obligation">collateralized debt obligations</a> (CDOs) and <a href="/wiki/Second_lien_loan" title="Second lien loan">second lien loans</a>, as well as 12.5% senior unsecured notes, and 84,687,686 shares of <a href="/wiki/Common_stock" title="Common stock">common stock</a> (equal to 19.99% of the then currently outstanding shares). This resulted in a net $2.2 billion reduction in assets on the company's <a href="/wiki/Balance_sheet" title="Balance sheet">balance sheet</a>. Citadel received a seat on the <a href="/wiki/Board_of_directors" title="Board of directors">board of directors</a> of the company and <a href="/wiki/Mitch_Caplan" title="Mitch Caplan">Mitch Caplan</a> resigned as CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2007, the company revoked the brand name license from SBI E*Trade Securities in Japan.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2008, E*Trade named Donald Layton, formerly <a href="/wiki/JPMorgan_Chase" title="JPMorgan Chase">JPMorgan Chase</a> vice chairman, as its new CEO. Layton had joined E*Trade's board of directors in November 2007, at the same time as the <a href="/wiki/Citadel_LLC" title="Citadel LLC">Citadel LLC</a> deal.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2008, the company sold its Canadian division to <a href="/wiki/Scotiabank" title="Scotiabank">Scotiabank</a> for CAD$444 million.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2009, Robert Druskin, a former chief operating officer of <a href="/wiki/Citigroup" title="Citigroup">Citigroup</a> was named interim CEO and chairman.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>On March 22, 2010, Steven Freiberg was named CEO. Freiberg was the former co-CEO of Citigroup's global consumer group and former head of its credit card unit.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>On January 17, 2013, Paul T. Idzik was appointed CEO. Idzik had previously been Group Chief Executive of <a href="/wiki/DTZ" title="DTZ">DTZ</a> and also served ten years at <a href="/wiki/Barclays" title="Barclays">Barclays</a> bank.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>On September 12, 2016, E*Trade acquired OptionsHouse for $725 million and Karl A. Roessner was appointed CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>On October 19, 2017, E*Trade acquired RIA custodial services company Trust Company of America.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>On August 14, 2019, Mike Pizzi was appointed CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>
red
On December 9, 2019, E*Trade acquired student loan benefit provider Gradifi.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>On February 20th 2020 Morgan Stanley announced their acquisition of E-Trade in an all stock deal valued at approximately $13bn. Acquiring all outstanding shares of E-Trade at a ratio of 1.0432 Morgan Stanley shares per E-Trade share.  The ratio equates to $58.74 per E-Trade share based on the closing price of E-Trade shares on February 19th 2020.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span id=".22E.2ATrade_Baby.22_advertising_campaign_.282008-2014.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id='"E*Trade_Baby"_advertising_campaign_(2008-2014)'>"E*Trade Baby" advertising campaign (2008-2014)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=E-Trade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title='Edit section: "E*Trade Baby" advertising campaign (2008-2014)' data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>E*Trade Baby was an integrated advertising campaign that featured a baby that talked about finance. The goal was to make the audience believe that E*Trade was easy to use. The campaign, which appeared online, on television, and in print, was created by Tor Myhren, then of <a href="/wiki/Grey_Global_Group" title="Grey Global Group">Grey Global Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> At first, Myhren had doubts if the ad campaign would work.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2008, during <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLII" title="Super Bowl XLII">Super Bowl XLII</a>, the company debuted advertisements featuring the baby, voiced by comedian <a href="/wiki/Pete_Holmes" title="Pete Holmes">Pete Holmes</a>, discussing investing in an adult voice in front of a <a href="/wiki/Webcam" title="Webcam">webcam</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2009, during <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLIII" title="Super Bowl XLIII">Super Bowl XLIII</a>, the company once again aired a 30-second advertisement featuring the baby, along with a <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> page, updates on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>, and videos on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> As a result of the advertisement, the company realized a 19% increase in online applications and an 86% increase in website traffic in the week after the game.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p><p>In 2011, the baby was featured in an advertisement called “Enzo the Tailor”, in which the baby was fitted for a custom-made suit and talked about how his tailor could retire in <a href="/wiki/Tuscany" title="Tuscany">Tuscany</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2013, the company aired an advertisement during <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLVII" title="Super Bowl XLVII">Super Bowl XLVII</a> featuring the baby. In the advertisement, the baby focuses on how much money is at stake in <a href="/wiki/401(k)" title="401(k)">401(k)</a> account fees and offers a better approach: "come to E*TRADE, and Save It".<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p><p>On March 21, 2014, the company announced the end of the E*Trade baby via a commercial which aired during the <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_I_Men%27s_Basketball_Tournament" title="NCAA Division I Men's Basketball Tournament">NCAA Division I Men's Basketball Tournament</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
