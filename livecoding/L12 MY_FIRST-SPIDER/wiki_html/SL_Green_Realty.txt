</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=SL_Green_Realty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company's predecessor, S.L. Green Properties, Inc., was formed in 1980 by <a href="/wiki/Stephen_L._Green" title="Stephen L. Green">Stephen L. Green</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>In 1997, the company was formed as a successor.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>In 2000, the company sold the <a href="/wiki/Whitehall_Building" title="Whitehall Building">Whitehall Building</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p><p>In 2002, in partnership with SITQ, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/One_Astor_Plaza" title="One Astor Plaza">One Astor Plaza</a> for $483.5 million.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In 2003, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/461_Fifth_Avenue" title="461 Fifth Avenue">461 Fifth Avenue</a> for $62.3 million.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2004, the company sold <a href="/wiki/The_Knickerbocker_Hotel_(Manhattan)" title="The Knickerbocker Hotel (Manhattan)">The Knickerbocker Hotel (Manhattan)</a> for $160 million.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In 2005, the company acquired the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Life_Insurance_Company_Tower" title="Metropolitan Life Insurance Company Tower">Metropolitan Life Insurance Company Tower</a> for $916 million and converted the building into condominiums.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 2006, the company acquired Reckson Associates in a $4 billion transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2007, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/388_Greenwich_Street" title="388 Greenwich Street">388 Greenwich Street</a> from <a href="/wiki/Citigroup" title="Citigroup">Citigroup</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Leaseback" title="Leaseback">leaseback</a> transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Citigroup reacquired the building in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 2010, the company acquired the <a href="/wiki/Pershing_Square_Building" title="Pershing Square Building">Pershing Square Building</a> from <a href="/wiki/Shorenstein_Properties" title="Shorenstein Properties">Shorenstein Properties</a> for $330 million.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 2011, the company sold <a href="/wiki/One_Court_Square" title="One Court Square">One Court Square</a> in <a href="/wiki/Long_Island_City" title="Long Island City">Long Island City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a> to David Werner and <a href="/wiki/Joel_Schreiber" title="Joel Schreiber">Joel Schreiber</a> for $481 million<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2017, the company sold the <a href="/wiki/Montague-Court_Building" class="mw-redirect" title="Montague-Court Building">Montague-Court Building</a> for $171.0 million. The company had bought the property for $107.5 million in 2007 and made significant upgrades.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2018, the company purchased a $148 million stake in <a href="/wiki/245_Park_Avenue" title="245 Park Avenue">245 Park Avenue</a> from Chinese conglomerate <a href="/wiki/HNA_Group" title="HNA Group">HNA Group</a>. The company was also appointed as property manager and leasing manager.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
