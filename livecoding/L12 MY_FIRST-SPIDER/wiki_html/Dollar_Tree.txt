<p><b>Dollar Tree Stores, Inc.</b>, formerly known as Only $1.00, is an American <a href="/wiki/Chain_store" title="Chain store">chain</a> of <a href="/wiki/Discount_store" title="Discount store">discount</a> <a href="/wiki/Variety_store" title="Variety store">variety stores</a> that sells items for $1 or less. Headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake,_Virginia" title="Chesapeake, Virginia">Chesapeake, Virginia</a>, it is a <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> company and operates 15,115 stores throughout the 48 contiguous <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">U.S. states</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10-K_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10-K-2">[2]</a></sup> Its stores are supported by a nationwide logistics network of eleven distribution centers. The company operates one-<a href="/wiki/Dollar_store" class="mw-redirect" title="Dollar store">dollar stores</a> under the names of Dollar Tree and Dollar Bills. The company also operates a multi-price-point variety chain under the <a href="/wiki/Family_Dollar" title="Family Dollar">Family Dollar</a> banner.

Each Dollar Tree stocks a variety of products including national, regional, and private-label brands. Departments found in a Dollar Tree store include health and beauty, food and snacks, party, seasonal décor, housewares, glassware, dinnerware, household cleaning supplies, candy, toys, gifts, gift bags and wrap, stationery, craft supplies, teaching supplies, automotive, electronics, pet supplies, and books. Most Dollar Tree stores also sell frozen foods and dairy items such as milk, eggs, pizza, ice cream, frozen dinners, and pre-made baked goods. In August 2012, the company began accepting manufacturer's coupons at all of its store locations.
</p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dollar_Tree&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dollar_Tree&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1953, K. R. Perry<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> opened a <a href="/wiki/Ben_Franklin_Stores" class="mw-redirect" title="Ben Franklin Stores">Ben Franklin</a> variety store in <a href="/wiki/Norfolk,_Virginia" title="Norfolk, Virginia">Norfolk, Virginia</a>, which later became known as <i>K&amp;K 5&amp;10</i>. In 1970, K.R. Perry, Doug Perry, and Macon Brock started <i>K&amp;K Toys</i> in Norfolk, Virginia. This mall concept grew to over 130 stores on the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">East Coast</a>. In 1986, Doug Perry, Macon Brock, and Ray Compton started another <a href="/wiki/Chain_store" title="Chain store">chain store</a> called <i>Only $1.00</i> with five stores, one in Georgia, one in Tennessee, and three in Virginia. The expansion of dollar stores was continued alongside K&amp;K Toys stores, mostly in enclosed malls. In 1991, the corporation made a decision to focus exclusively on the expansion of dollar stores after selling K&amp;K stores to <a href="/wiki/KB_Toys" title="KB Toys">KB Toys</a>, owned by <a href="/wiki/Melville_Corporation" title="Melville Corporation">Melville Corporation</a>.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="1990s">1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dollar_Tree&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1990s" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1993, the name <i>Only $1.00</i> was changed to <i>Dollar Tree Stores</i> to address what could be a multi-price-point strategy in the future, and part <a href="/wiki/Equity_(finance)" title="Equity (finance)">equity</a> interest was sold to SKM partners, a <a href="/wiki/Private_equity_firm" title="Private equity firm">private equity firm</a>.
</p><p>Brock and the co-founders of Dollar Tree got the idea for the company from another retailer known as Everything's A Dollar, which went bankrupt in the 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>On March 6, 1995, Dollar Tree, Inc. went public on the <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a> exchange at $15 a share, with a <a href="/wiki/Market_cap" class="mw-redirect" title="Market cap">market cap</a> then calculated at $225 million.
</p><p>In 1996, Dollar Tree acquired <i>Dollar Bill$, Inc.</i>, a <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>-based chain of 136 stores.
</p><p>In 1997, the company opened its first <a href="/wiki/Distribution_center" title="Distribution center">distribution center</a> and its new store support center, both located in <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake,_Virginia" title="Chesapeake, Virginia">Chesapeake, Virginia</a>.
</p><p>In 1998, Dollar Tree acquired <i>98-Cent Clearance Centers</i> in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.
</p><p>In 1999, Dollar Tree acquired <i>Only $One</i> stores based in <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> state. That same year, the company opened its second distribution center in <a href="/wiki/Olive_Branch,_Mississippi" title="Olive Branch, Mississippi">Olive Branch, Mississippi</a>.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s">2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dollar_Tree&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2000s" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Super_Dollar_Tree_Northwood.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Super_Dollar_Tree_Northwood.JPG/220px-Super_Dollar_Tree_Northwood.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="4320" data-file-height="3240"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Super_Dollar_Tree_Northwood.JPG/220px-Super_Dollar_Tree_Northwood.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Super_Dollar_Tree_Northwood.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>This Dollar Tree store in <a href="/wiki/Northwood,_Ohio" title="Northwood, Ohio">Northwood, Ohio</a>, is one of the few stores that continues to use the defunct Super Dollar Tree banner.</div></div></div>
<p>In 2000, Dollar Tree acquired <i>Dollar Express</i>, a <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>-based company, and also built a new distribution center in <a href="/wiki/Stockton,_California" title="Stockton, California">Stockton, California</a>. In 2001, the company opened two additional distribution centers, in <a href="/wiki/Savannah,_Georgia" title="Savannah, Georgia">Savannah, Georgia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Briar_Creek,_Pennsylvania" title="Briar Creek, Pennsylvania">Briar Creek, Pennsylvania</a>. In 2003, Dollar Tree acquired <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_City" title="Salt Lake City">Salt Lake City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Utah" title="Utah">Utah</a>-based <i>Greenbacks, Inc.</i>, and opened a new distribution center in <a href="/wiki/Marietta,_Oklahoma" title="Marietta, Oklahoma">Marietta, Oklahoma</a>.
</p><p>In 2004, Dollar Tree opened its first store in North Dakota which marked its operation of stores in all 48 contiguous states. The company also opened new distribution centers in <a href="/wiki/Joliet,_Illinois" title="Joliet, Illinois">Joliet, Illinois</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ridgefield,_Washington" title="Ridgefield, Washington">Ridgefield, Washington</a>.
</p><p>In 2006, Dollar Tree celebrated its 20th year of retailing at a $1.00 price point, opened its 3,000th store, and acquired 138 <i><a href="/wiki/Deals" title="Deals">DEAL$</a></i> stores, previously owned by SUPERVALU INC.
</p><p>In 2007, Dollar Tree expanded its Briar Creek Distribution Center, crossed the $4 billion sales threshold, and had a <a href="/wiki/Market_cap" class="mw-redirect" title="Market cap">market capitalization</a> of $3.29 billion. In 2008, Dollar Tree earned a place in the Fortune 500. By the close of 2009, the company opened a store in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a>, and purchased a new distribution center in <a href="/wiki/San_Bernardino,_California" title="San Bernardino, California">San Bernardino, California</a>.
</p><p>In 2009, Dollar Tree redesigned its website with a new e-commerce platform. DollarTree.com sells Dollar Tree merchandise in larger quantities to individuals, small businesses, and organizations. The company also advertises in-store events, specials, seasonal promotions, and featured products through the site and users can locate a retail store, research information about Dollar Tree, and view product recalls. Dollar Tree also recently added customer ratings and reviews and customer stories to the site.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s">2010s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dollar_Tree&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010s" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dollar_Tree_interior_in_Gillette,_Wyoming.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Dollar_Tree_interior_in_Gillette%2C_Wyoming.jpg/220px-Dollar_Tree_interior_in_Gillette%2C_Wyoming.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="5472" data-file-height="3648"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 147px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Dollar_Tree_interior_in_Gillette%2C_Wyoming.jpg/220px-Dollar_Tree_interior_in_Gillette%2C_Wyoming.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="147" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dollar_Tree_interior_in_Gillette,_Wyoming.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Interior of a Dollar Tree in <a href="/wiki/Gillette,_Wyoming" title="Gillette, Wyoming">Gillette, Wyoming</a></div></div></div>
<p>In 2010, the corporation opened its 4,000th <a href="/wiki/Chain_store" title="Chain store">chain store</a> and acquired 86 Canadian <a href="/wiki/Dollar_Giant" class="mw-redirect" title="Dollar Giant">Dollar Giant</a> stores which are based in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver, British Columbia</a>. The stores are operated in <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>. These are the first <a href="/wiki/Retail" title="Retail">retail</a> locations outside of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> operated by Dollar Tree.
</p><p>In 2011, Dollar Tree achieved total sales of $6.63 billion, opened 278 new stores, and completed a 400,000 square-foot expansion of its distribution center in Savannah, Georgia.
</p><p>In 2012, Dollar Tree opened another 345 new stores and exceeded $7 billion in sales, with an end-of-the-year <a href="/wiki/Market_cap" class="mw-redirect" title="Market cap">market cap</a> at $9.13 billion.
</p><p>On July 28, 2014, Dollar Tree announced that it was offering $9.2 billion for the purchase of competitor chain store <a href="/wiki/Family_Dollar" title="Family Dollar">Family Dollar</a>. On August 18, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Dollar_General" title="Dollar General">Dollar General</a> lodged a competing bid of $9.7 billion for Family Dollar.<sup id="cite_ref-DollarBid_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DollarBid-5">[5]</a></sup> The bid was rejected on August 20, 2014, by the Family Dollar board, which said it would proceed with the deal with Dollar Tree.<sup id="cite_ref-BidRebuffed_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BidRebuffed-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2015, Dollar Tree announced plans to divest 300 stores in order to appease US regulators scrutinizing its proposed takeover of Family Dollar stores.<sup id="cite_ref-Reuters_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2015, the firm agreed to sell 330 stores to <a href="/wiki/Private_equity" title="Private equity">private equity</a> company Sycamore Partners as part of the approval process for its $8.5 billion takeover of Family Dollar.<sup id="cite_ref-reuters.com_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuters.com-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>The company was ranked 134th on the 2018 <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> list of the United States corporations by revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2019, as part of its reposition plan, Dollar Tree announced that it will close up to 390 Family Dollar stores along with renovating 1,000 other locations.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_strategy">Business strategy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dollar_Tree&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Business strategy" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dollar_Tree_at_night_-_Hillsboro,_Oregon.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Dollar_Tree_at_night_-_Hillsboro%2C_Oregon.JPG/220px-Dollar_Tree_at_night_-_Hillsboro%2C_Oregon.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="177" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1904" data-file-height="1533"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 177px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Dollar_Tree_at_night_-_Hillsboro%2C_Oregon.JPG/220px-Dollar_Tree_at_night_-_Hillsboro%2C_Oregon.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="177" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dollar_Tree_at_night_-_Hillsboro,_Oregon.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Exterior of a store in <a href="/wiki/Hillsboro,_Oregon" title="Hillsboro, Oregon">Hillsboro, Oregon</a></div></div></div>
<p>Dollar Tree is classified as an extreme discount store.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-11">[11]</a></sup> It claims to be able to achieve this because their buyers "work extremely hard to find the best bargains out there", and it has "great control over the tremendous <a href="/wiki/Buying_power" class="mw-redirect" title="Buying power">buying power</a> at the dollar price-point".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Its prices are primarily designed to attract those with financial difficulties, but it has also become popular with more affluent customers.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_Dollar_bidding">Family Dollar bidding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dollar_Tree&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Family Dollar bidding" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On July 28, 2014, Dollar Tree announced that a deal had been reached and approved by both parties to purchase <a href="/wiki/Family_Dollar" title="Family Dollar">Family Dollar</a> for $8.5 billion plus acquisition of the $1 billion in debt currently held by Family Dollar.<sup id="cite_ref-reuters-buyout_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-buyout-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cltob-buyout_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cltob-buyout-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wsj-buyout_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-buyout-15">[15]</a></sup> The deal came in the month following <a href="/wiki/Activist_shareholder" title="Activist shareholder">activist investor</a> and major shareholder<sup id="cite_ref-icahn-sec-purchase_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icahn-sec-purchase-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Carl_Icahn" title="Carl Icahn">Carl Icahn</a>'s demand that Family Dollar be promptly put up for sale.<sup id="cite_ref-icahn-sec-demand_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icahn-sec-demand-17">[17]</a></sup>  After their reported deal had been struck, <a href="/wiki/Dollar_General" title="Dollar General">Dollar General</a> entered the bidding, surpassing Dollar Tree's offer, with a $9.7 billion bid on August 18, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On August 20, 2014 Family Dollar rejected the Dollar General bid, saying it was not a matter of price, but concerns over antitrust issues that had convinced the company and its advisers that the deal could not be concluded on the terms proposed. The Family Dollar board had been analyzing potential antitrust issues that could arise from doing a deal with Dollar General, since the start of the year a statement from CEO <a href="/wiki/Howard_R._Levine" title="Howard R. Levine">Howard Levine</a> outlined.<sup id="cite_ref-BidRebuffed_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BidRebuffed-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

dollar">C$</a>1.25 or less.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On October 11, 2010, Dollar Tree announced its acquisition of <b>Dollar Giant</b>, originally incorporated in 2001 in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, Canada, for $52 million.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> At the time of the acquisition, Dollar Giant had about 85 <a href="/wiki/Retail_outlet" class="mw-redirect" title="Retail outlet">retail outlets</a> in the provinces of <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Approximately 30 of its retail locations are in British Columbia, making it the largest dollar store chain in that province. It was Canada's fourth largest operator of <a href="/wiki/Dollar_store" class="mw-redirect" title="Dollar store">dollar stores</a>. Dollar Tree has since rebranded all of its Dollar Giant stores to Dollar Tree; these were the first retail locations outside of the United States operated by Dollar Tree.  The company now operates 227 stores across Canada, concentrated in Western Canada and Ontario.  Dollar Tree Canada's merchandising team is located in <a href="/wiki/Mississauga" title="Mississauga">Mississauga, Ontario</a>, while its corporate office remains in <a href="/wiki/Burnaby" title="Burnaby">Burnaby</a> in <a href="/wiki/Greater_Vancouver" title="Greater Vancouver">Greater Vancouver</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Consumer_Product_Safety_Commission" class="mw-redirect" title="Consumer Product Safety Commission">Consumer Product Safety Commission</a> lists several recalls for products sold at Dollar Tree stores. The recalled products include salsa jars with broken glass inside them, hot-melt mini glue guns (recalled in January 2008) which could short circuit and cause burns,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and candle sets (recalled in February 2004) which could produce excessive flame.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
