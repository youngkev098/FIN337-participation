</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=DXC_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Creation">Creation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=DXC_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Creation" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>DXC Technology was founded in 2017<sup id="cite_ref-forbes_profile_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes_profile-3">[3]</a></sup> as the result of the spin-off of <a href="/wiki/Hewlett_Packard_Enterprise" title="Hewlett Packard Enterprise">Hewlett Packard Enterprise</a>'s Enterprise Service segment<sup id="cite_ref-forbes_spinoff_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes_spinoff-4">[4]</a></sup> and its merger with <a href="/wiki/Computer_Sciences_Corporation" title="Computer Sciences Corporation">Computer Sciences Corporation</a> (CSC). At the time of its creation, DXC Technology had revenues of $25 billion, employed 170,000 people and operated in 70 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>The spinoff from <a href="/wiki/Hewlett_Packard_Enterprise" title="Hewlett Packard Enterprise">HPE</a> did not include two parts of the Enterprise Service segment: the <a href="/wiki/Mphasis" title="Mphasis">Mphasis Limited</a> reporting unit and the <i>Communications and Media Solutions</i> product group.<sup id="cite_ref-forbes_spinoff_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes_spinoff-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In India, the company started a three-year plan to reduce the number of offices in the country from 50 to 26, and reduce headcount by 5.9% (around 10,000) employees.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> With about 43,000 employees (more than 1/3rd of its workforce) in India, the company is restructuring its workforce to meet its new revenue profile.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, DXC split off its public sector segment to create a new company, Perspecta Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2019, American businessman <a href="/wiki/Mike_Salvino" title="Mike Salvino">Mike Salvino</a> was appointed president and CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=DXC_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Acquisitions" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2017, the company completed its first acquisition, buying Tribridge, a provider of Microsoft Dynamics 365 solutions.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, it announced several additional acquisitions, including Molina Medicaid Solutions (previously part of <a href="/wiki/Molina_Healthcare" title="Molina Healthcare">Molina Healthcare</a>), Argodesign and two <a href="/wiki/ServiceNow" title="ServiceNow">ServiceNow</a> partners, BusinessNow and TESM.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2019, DXC Technology acquired Luxoft . According to the information from SEC database, DXC Technology now owns 83% of Luxoft shares. The eventual close of the deal was in June 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=DXC_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Sport" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
