<p><b>Best Buy Co., Inc.</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> <a href="/wiki/Consumer_electronics" title="Consumer electronics">consumer electronics</a> <a href="/wiki/Retailer" class="mw-redirect" title="Retailer">retailer</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Richfield,_Minnesota" title="Richfield, Minnesota">Richfield, Minnesota</a>. It was originally founded by <a href="/wiki/Richard_M._Schulze" title="Richard M. Schulze">Richard M. Schulze</a> and James Wheeler in 1966<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> as an audio specialty store called <b>Sound of Music</b>. In 1983, it was re-branded under its current name with an emphasis placed on consumer electronics.

Best Buy also operates the Best Buy Mobile and Insignia brands in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, plus Five Star in China.<sup id="cite_ref-SEC_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEC-3">[3]</a></sup> Best Buy sells cellular phones from <a href="/wiki/Verizon_Wireless" title="Verizon Wireless">Verizon Wireless</a>, <a href="/wiki/AT%26T_Mobility" title="AT&amp;T Mobility">AT&amp;T Mobility</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sprint_Corporation" title="Sprint Corporation">Sprint Corporation</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. In Canada, carriers include <a href="/wiki/Bell_Mobility" title="Bell Mobility">Bell Mobility</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rogers_Wireless" title="Rogers Wireless">Rogers Wireless</a>, <a href="/wiki/Telus_Mobility" title="Telus Mobility">Telus Mobility</a>, their <a href="/wiki/Fighter_brand" title="Fighter brand">fighter brands</a>, and competing smaller carriers, such as <a href="/wiki/SaskTel" title="SaskTel">SaskTel</a>.
</p><p>Best Buy was named "Company of the Year" by <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Forbes (magazine)">Forbes</a></i> magazine in 2004,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> "Specialty Retailer of the Decade" by Discount Store News in 2001,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> ranked in the Top 10 of "America's Most Generous Corporations" by <i>Forbes</i> in 2005 (based on 2004 giving),<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> made <i><a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)">Fortune</a></i> magazine's list of "Most Admired Companies" in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and "The Most Sustainable Company in the United States" by <i>Barron's</i> in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hubert_Joly" title="Hubert Joly">Hubert Joly</a> is executive chairman of Best Buy, having been succeeded as CEO by Corie Barry in June 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-startribune_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startribune-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ascends_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ascends-12">[12]</a></sup> According to <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!_Finance" title="Yahoo! Finance">Yahoo! Finance</a>, Best Buy is the largest specialty retailer in the United States <a href="/wiki/Consumer_electronics" title="Consumer electronics">consumer electronics</a> retail industry.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The company ranked No. 72 in the 2018 <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> list of the largest United States corporations by total revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Best_Buy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Best_Buy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early history" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sound_of_Music_logo.png" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Sound_of_Music_logo.png/100px-Sound_of_Music_logo.png" decoding="async" width="100" height="101" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="162" data-file-height="163"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 100px;height: 101px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Sound_of_Music_logo.png/100px-Sound_of_Music_logo.png" data-alt="" data-width="100" data-height="101" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sound_of_Music_logo.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Sound of Music logo from 1966 until 1983</div></div></div>
<p>On August 22, 1966, Richard M. Schulze and a business partner opened Sound of Music, an electronics store specializing in <a href="/wiki/High_fidelity" title="High fidelity">high fidelity</a> stereos in <a href="/wiki/St._Paul,_Minnesota" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Paul, Minnesota">St. Paul, Minnesota</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-businessweek_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessweek-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-startribuneone_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startribuneone-24">[24]</a></sup> Schulze financed the opening of his first store with his personal savings and a second mortgage he took out on his family's home.<sup id="cite_ref-businessweek_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessweek-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-discount_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-discount-25">[25]</a></sup> In 1967, Sound of Music acquired Kencraft Hi-Fi Company and Bergo Company.<sup id="cite_ref-discount_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-discount-25">[25]</a></sup> Sound of Music earned $1 million in revenue and made about $58,000 in profits in its first year.<sup id="cite_ref-businessweek_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessweek-23">[23]</a></sup> In 1969, Sound of Music had three stores and Schulze bought out his business partner.
</p><p>Sound of Music operated nine stores throughout <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a> by 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-startribuneyears_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startribuneyears-26">[26]</a></sup> In 1981, the <a href="/wiki/Roseville,_Minnesota" title="Roseville, Minnesota">Roseville, Minnesota</a> Sound of Music location, at the time the largest and most profitable Sound of Music store, was hit by a tornado.<sup id="cite_ref-businessweek_23-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessweek-23">[23]</a></sup> The store's roof was sheared off and showroom destroyed, but the storeroom was left intact.<sup id="cite_ref-businessweek_23-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessweek-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-businessweekbg_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessweekbg-27">[27]</a></sup> In response, Schulze decided to have a "Tornado Sale" of damaged and excess stock in the damaged store's parking lot.<sup id="cite_ref-businessweek_23-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessweek-23">[23]</a></sup> He poured the remainder of his marketing budget into advertising the sale, promising "best buys" on everything.<sup id="cite_ref-businessweekbg_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessweekbg-27">[27]</a></sup> Sound of Music made more money during the four-day sale than it did in a typical month.<sup id="cite_ref-startribuneone_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startribuneone-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:122px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Best_Buy_Co._Superstores_logo.png" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Best_Buy_Co._Superstores_logo.png/120px-Best_Buy_Co._Superstores_logo.png" decoding="async" width="120" height="120" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="131" data-file-height="131"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 120px;height: 120px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Best_Buy_Co._Superstores_logo.png/120px-Best_Buy_Co._Superstores_logo.png" data-alt="" data-width="120" data-height="120" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Best_Buy_Co._Superstores_logo.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Best Buy Co. Superstores logo from 1983 until 1984</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:122px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Best_Buy_Superstores_logo.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Best_Buy_Superstores_logo.jpg/120px-Best_Buy_Superstores_logo.jpg" decoding="async" width="120" height="82" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="265" data-file-height="181"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 120px;height: 82px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Best_Buy_Superstores_logo.jpg/120px-Best_Buy_Superstores_logo.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="120" data-height="82" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Best_Buy_Superstores_logo.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Best Buy Superstores logo from 1984 until 1989</div></div></div>
<p>In 1983, with seven stores and $10 million in annual sales, Sound of Music was renamed Best Buy Company, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-startribuneyears_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startribuneyears-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-businessweekbg_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessweekbg-27">[27]</a></sup> The company also expanded its product offerings to include home appliances and <a href="/wiki/Videocassette_recorder" title="Videocassette recorder">VCRs</a>, in an attempt to expand beyond its then-core customer base of 15-to-18-year-old males. Later that year, Best Buy opened its first superstore in <a href="/wiki/Burnsville,_Minnesota" title="Burnsville, Minnesota">Burnsville, Minnesota</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-businessweekbg_27-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessweekbg-27">[27]</a></sup> The Burnsville location featured a high-volume, low price business model, which was borrowed partially from Schulze's successful Tornado Sale in 1981.<sup id="cite_ref-businessweek_23-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessweek-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-businessweekbg_27-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessweekbg-27">[27]</a></sup> In its first year, the Burnsville store out-performed all other Best Buy stores combined.<sup id="cite_ref-startribuneone_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startribuneone-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-discount_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-discount-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Best_Buy_Logo.svg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Best_Buy_Logo.svg/220px-Best_Buy_Logo.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="152" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="807" data-file-height="556"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 152px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Best_Buy_Logo.svg/220px-Best_Buy_Logo.svg.png" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="152" data-srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Best_Buy_Logo.svg/330px-Best_Buy_Logo.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Best_Buy_Logo.svg/440px-Best_Buy_Logo.svg.png 2x" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Best_Buy_Logo.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Best Buy logo from 1989 until May 9, 2018, used in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> until July 26, 2019</div></div></div>
<p>Best Buy debuted on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> in 1987.<sup id="cite_ref-chainstore_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chainstore-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-internationaldirectory_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-internationaldirectory-29">[29]</a></sup> In 1989, the company introduced a new store concept dubbed "Concept II".<sup id="cite_ref-discount_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-discount-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-chainstore_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chainstore-28">[28]</a></sup> Concept II replaced dimly lit industrial-style stores with brighter and more fashionably fixtured stores.<sup id="cite_ref-discount_25-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-discount-25">[25]</a></sup> Stores also began placing all stock on the sales floor rather than in a stock room, had fewer salespersons and provided more self-help product information for its customers.<sup id="cite_ref-chainstore_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chainstore-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-internationaldirectory_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-internationaldirectory-29">[29]</a></sup> Best Buy also did away with commissioned salespeople.<sup id="cite_ref-businessweek_23-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessweek-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-chainstore_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chainstore-28">[28]</a></sup> The commission-free sales environment "created a more relaxed shopping environment free of the high-pressure sales tactics used in other stores," but was unpopular with salespersons and suppliers.<sup id="cite_ref-chainstore_28-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chainstore-28">[28]</a></sup> Some suppliers, such as <a href="/wiki/Maytag" title="Maytag">Maytag</a>, <a href="/wiki/Whirlpool_Corporation" title="Whirlpool Corporation">Whirlpool</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a>, were upset that salespeople would no longer be pushing their products and stopped selling in Best Buy stores altogether.<sup id="cite_ref-businessweek_23-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessweek-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-startribuneone_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startribuneone-24">[24]</a></sup> The suppliers returned after Best Buy's sales and revenue grew following the roll-out of Concept II.<sup id="cite_ref-businessweekbg_27-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessweekbg-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>In 1992, the company achieved $1 billion in annual revenues.<sup id="cite_ref-internationaldirectory_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-internationaldirectory-29">[29]</a></sup> In 1995, Best Buy debuted "Concept III" stores, which were larger than its previous stores.<sup id="cite_ref-internationaldirectory_29-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-internationaldirectory-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-discountstorenews11_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-discountstorenews11-30">[30]</a></sup> The Concept III stores included expanded product offerings, interactive <a href="/wiki/Touchscreen" title="Touchscreen">touchscreen</a> kiosks that displayed product information for both customers and employees, and demonstration areas for products such as <a href="/wiki/Surround_sound" title="Surround sound">surround sound</a> stereo systems and videogames.<sup id="cite_ref-discountstorenews11_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-discountstorenews11-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-customerelectronics_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-customerelectronics-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>Best Buy launched its "Concept IV" stores with its expansion into <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a> in 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-internationaldirectory_29-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-internationaldirectory-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-discountstorenews26_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-discountstorenews26-32">[32]</a></sup> Concept IV stores included an open layout with products organized by category, cash registers located throughout the store, and slightly smaller stores than Concept III stores.<sup id="cite_ref-startribunemoore_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startribunemoore-33">[33]</a></sup> The stores also had large areas for demonstrating <a href="/wiki/Home_cinema" title="Home cinema">home theater systems</a> and computer software.<sup id="cite_ref-internationaldirectory_29-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-internationaldirectory-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-providencenew_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-providencenew-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>In 1999, Best Buy was added to <a href="/wiki/Standard_%26_Poor%27s" title="Standard &amp; Poor's">Standard &amp; Poor's</a> <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-internationaldirectory_29-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-internationaldirectory-29">[29]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 2000, Best Buy formed Redline Entertainment, an independent music label and action-sports video distributor.<sup id="cite_ref-newswire_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newswire-36">[36]</a></sup> The company acquired Magnolia Hi-Fi, Inc., an audio-video retailer located in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a>, in December 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-internationaldirectory_29-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-internationaldirectory-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2001, Best Buy acquired <a href="/wiki/Musicland" title="Musicland">Musicland</a> Stores Corporation, a <a href="/wiki/Minnetonka,_Minnesota" title="Minnetonka, Minnesota">Minnetonka, Minnesota</a>-based retailer that sold home entertainment products under the Sam Goody, Suncoast Motion Picture Company, Media Play and OnCue brands. Best Buy purchased the company for $425 million in cash and the assumption of $271 million of Musicland debt.<sup id="cite_ref-internationaldirectory_29-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-internationaldirectory-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-audioweek_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-audioweek-37">[37]</a></sup> Later that year, Best Buy acquired the <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>-based electronics-chain <a href="/wiki/Future_Shop" title="Future Shop">Future Shop Ltd.</a>, marking its entrance to the international marketplace.<sup id="cite_ref-internationaldirectory_29-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-internationaldirectory-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-discountstorenewscn_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-discountstorenewscn-38">[38]</a></sup> Under the deal, Future Shop was purchased for approximately $377 million and continued to operate as subsidiary independent from Best Buy Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-saint_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-saint-39">[39]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-twice_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twice-40">[40]</a></sup></p><p><a href="/wiki/Brad_Anderson_(executive)" title="Brad Anderson (executive)">Brad Anderson</a> succeeded Richard Schulze as Best Buy <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">CEO</a> in July 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-startribunegeek_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startribunegeek-41">[41]</a></sup> Anderson had begun working at Best Buy in 1973 while attending seminary school.<sup id="cite_ref-startribunegeek_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startribunegeek-41">[41]</a></sup> He was promoted to vice president in 1981 and executive vice president in 1986. Anderson had most recently served as president and <a href="/wiki/Chief_operating_officer" title="Chief operating officer">COO</a> of Best Buy, a position he had held since 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-startribunegeek_41-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startribunegeek-41">[41]</a></sup> In September of that year, Best Buy opened the first Canadian Best Buy-branded store in <a href="/wiki/Mississauga" title="Mississauga">Mississauga, Ontario</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-discountstorenewson_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-discountstorenewson-42">[42]</a></sup> In October, Best Buy acquired <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis</a>-based Geek Squad, then a 24-hour residential computer repair business with offices in Minneapolis, <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> and <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-startribunegeek_41-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startribunegeek-41">[41]</a></sup></p><p>Best Buy stores in the U.S. surpassed the 600-store mark and the company opened its first global-sourcing office in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard600_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard600-43">[43]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-shanghaidaily_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shanghaidaily-44">[44]</a></sup> In June, Best Buy divested itself of Musicland in a deal with Sun Capital Partners under which Sun Capital received all of Musicland's stock and debt.<sup id="cite_ref-mergersacquis_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mergersacquis-45">[45]</a></sup> Best Buy launched its "Reward Zone" loyalty program in July following an 8-month test of the program in <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-warrens_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-warrens-46">[46]</a></sup> Also in 2003, Best Buy's corporate offices were consolidated into a single campus in <a href="/wiki/Richfield,_Minnesota" title="Richfield, Minnesota">Richfield, Minnesota</a>.
</p><p>In January 2004, Best Buy hired Virtucom Group to revamp Best Buy's website and handle all of the company's online content.<sup id="cite_ref-virtucom_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-virtucom-47">[47]</a></sup> In May, the company launched its "customer centricty" program, which segmented its stores according to customer profiles. The program also called for employees to focus on specific customer groups rather than product categories.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> In October, Best Buy completed rolling out Geek Squad "precincts" in every American Best Buy store.<sup id="cite_ref-financialtimes_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-financialtimes-49">[49]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2005, Best Buy began eliminating <a href="/wiki/Rebate_(marketing)" title="Rebate (marketing)">mail-in rebates</a> in response to negative customer reaction against them, and instead started giving out instant rebates via notebook computers.<sup id="cite_ref-twincityjournal_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twincityjournal-50">[50]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2006, Best Buy acquired a majority interest in Chinese appliance retailer Jiangsu Five Star Appliance for $180 million. At the time of the deal, Jiangsu was the fourth-largest appliance chain in China with 193 stores across eight Chinese provinces.<sup id="cite_ref-xinhua_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xinhua-51">[51]</a></sup> In June, the company opened Geek Squad precincts at <a href="/wiki/Office_Depot" title="Office Depot">Office Depot</a> in <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-warrens_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-warrens-46">[46]</a></sup> The market test was later expanded to <a href="/wiki/Denver" title="Denver">Denver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-odearnings_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odearnings-52">[52]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2007, the first Best Buy-branded store in China officially opened in Shanghai, China.<sup id="cite_ref-sinocast_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sinocast-53">[53]</a></sup> In March 2007, Best Buy acquired Speakeasy, a <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle, Washington</a>-based broadband <a href="/wiki/Voice_over_IP" title="Voice over IP">VOIP</a>, data and IT services provider. The acquisition was worth $80 million and, under terms of the deal, Speakeasy began operating as a wholly owned subsidiary of Best Buy. The company's products also became part of Best Buy's For Business program.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> Best Buy also expanded its Geek Squad market tests in March, opening Geek Squad precincts in <a href="/wiki/FedEx_Office" title="FedEx Office">FedEx Kinkos</a> stores located in <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis" title="Indianapolis">Indianapolis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charlotte,_North_Carolina" title="Charlotte, North Carolina">Charlotte, North Carolina</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dowjones_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dowjones-55">[55]</a></sup> In October 2007, Best Buy became the first consumer-electronics retailer to exit the analog television market, carrying only digital products that became mandatory in June 2009 by the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Communications_Commission" title="Federal Communications Commission">FCC</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nbc_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nbc-56">[56]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2008, Best Buy opened its first store in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nuevodia_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nuevodia-57">[57]</a></sup> Best Buy's Geek Squad market tests in Office Depot and FedEx Kinkos stores ended by March.<sup id="cite_ref-reuters4_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuters4-58">[58]</a></sup> Also in March, the company began promoting the <a href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" class="mw-redirect" title="Blu-ray Disc">Blu-ray</a> optical-disc format over the <a href="/wiki/HD_DVD" title="HD DVD">HD DVD</a> format, a move which ultimately contributed to Toshiba's decision to drop HD DVD.<sup id="cite_ref-informationweek_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-informationweek-59">[59]</a></sup> In May, the company agreed to buy 50% of the retail division of The Carphone Warehouse, a <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London, England</a>-based mobile phone retailer.<sup id="cite_ref-retailweekcw_60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-retailweekcw-60">[60]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-startribunecw_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startribunecw-61">[61]</a></sup> The deal was worth $2.1 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-startribunecw_61-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startribunecw-61">[61]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2008, Best Buy announced that it would start selling musical instruments and related gear in over 80 of its retail stores, making the company the second-largest musical-instrument distributor in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-associatedpress27_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-associatedpress27-62">[62]</a></sup> Best Buy became the first third-party retail seller of <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple's</a> <a href="/wiki/IPhone" title="IPhone">iPhone</a> in September.<sup id="cite_ref-crnnews_63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-crnnews-63">[63]</a></sup> Later that month, the company agreed to acquire Napster for $121 million.<sup id="cite_ref-reutersnapster_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reutersnapster-64">[64]</a></sup> In December, Best Buy opened its first store in Mexico.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2009, Best Buy leveraged its partnership with The Carphone Warehouse to launch Best Buy Mobile, a Best Buy-branded mobile retailer. Best Buy Mobile standalone stores were opened in shopping malls and urban downtowns. These Best Buy Mobile outlets were also added in all Best Buy-branded stores.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2009, Brian Dunn became Best Buy CEO. Dunn replaced Brad Anderson, who was retiring.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> Dunn had joined Best Buy in 1985 as a sales associate. In 2000, Dunn became senior vice president of East Coast operations and president of North American retail operations in 2004. He had most recently served as president of Best Buy, a position he had held since 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p><p>Best Buy partnered with Roxio's CinemaNow to launch an on-demand movie download service that would allow customers to stream content to any device sold by Best Buy that connected to the Internet in November 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> In December, the first Turkish Best Buy opened in <a href="/wiki/%C4%B0zmir" title="İzmir">İzmir</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s">2010s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Best_Buy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010s" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Best_Buy,_Germantown,_Maryland,_October_7,_2014.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Best_Buy%2C_Germantown%2C_Maryland%2C_October_7%2C_2014.JPG/170px-Best_Buy%2C_Germantown%2C_Maryland%2C_October_7%2C_2014.JPG" decoding="async" width="170" height="140" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3350" data-file-height="2760"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 170px;height: 140px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Best_Buy%2C_Germantown%2C_Maryland%2C_October_7%2C_2014.JPG/170px-Best_Buy%2C_Germantown%2C_Maryland%2C_October_7%2C_2014.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="170" data-height="140" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Best_Buy,_Germantown,_Maryland,_October_7,_2014.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A Best Buy store in <a href="/wiki/Germantown,_Maryland" title="Germantown, Maryland">Germantown, Maryland</a>, U.S., that opened in 2002.</div></div></div>
<p>In April 2010, Best Buy opened its first <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>-based Best Buy-branded store in <a href="/wiki/Thurrock" title="Thurrock">Thurrock</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-billboardbiz_70-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboardbiz-70">[70]</a></sup> The company eventually opened 11 Best Buy stores in the United Kingdom. The company closed all UK-based Best Buy stores in early 2012. In November 2011, Best Buy purchased The Carphone Warehouse's share of Best Buy Mobile for $1.3 billion. Best Buy and The Carphone Warehouse maintained their Best Buy Europe joint venture, which at the time operated 2,500 mobile phone stores throughout Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup></p><p>The company closed all of its Best Buy-branded stores in China by February 2011, when it merged Best Buy China's operations with Jiangsu Five Star, which had become a wholly owned subsidiary of Best Buy in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-SEC_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEC-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-xinhua28_72-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xinhua28-72">[72]</a></sup> In December 2011, Best Buy purchased mindSHIFT Technologies, a company that provided IT support for small and medium-sized businesses, for $167 million.<sup id="cite_ref-zdnet_73-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zdnet-73">[73]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012, in response to overall revenue decline, Best Buy announced plans to undergo a "transformation strategy". Stores began to adopt a redesigned "Connected Store" format, providing the Geek Squad with a centralized service desk and implementing a "store-within-a-store" concept for Pacific Kitchen &amp; Bath and Magnolia Design Center.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2012, Brian Dunn resigned as Best Buy's CEO during an internal company investigation into allegations of personal misconduct stemming from an inappropriate relationship with a female Best Buy employee.<sup id="cite_ref-journaldunn_75-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-journaldunn-75">[75]</a></sup> Best Buy named Director <a href="/wiki/George_L._Mikan_III" title="George L. Mikan III">George L. Mikan III</a> interim CEO following Dunn's resignation.<sup id="cite_ref-journaldunn_75-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-journaldunn-75">[75]</a></sup> The internal investigation was released in May 2012 and alleged that Best Buy founder and chairman Richard Schulze knew of Dunn's inappropriate relationship and failed to notify the Best Buy board.<sup id="cite_ref-journaldunn_75-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-journaldunn-75">[75]</a></sup> Schulze subsequently resigned his chairmanship of the company.<sup id="cite_ref-journaldunn_75-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-journaldunn-75">[75]</a></sup> Best Buy Director Hatim Tyabji replaced Schulze as Best Buy chairman.<sup id="cite_ref-journaldunn_75-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-journaldunn-75">[75]</a></sup></p><p><a href="/wiki/Hubert_Joly" title="Hubert Joly">Hubert Joly</a> replaced Mikan as Best Buy CEO in September 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-businessinsider_76-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessinsider-76">[76]</a></sup> Joly had previously served as CEO of <a href="/wiki/Carlson_Companies" class="mw-redirect" title="Carlson Companies">Carlson</a>, a hospitality conglomerate, since 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-businessinsider_76-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessinsider-76">[76]</a></sup> He led initiatives such as price matching, speeding up delivery times for online purchases, establishing "<a href="/wiki/Store_within_a_store" class="mw-redirect" title="Store within a store">store within a store</a>" sections for major brands such as <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple</a>, <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>, <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Electronics" title="Samsung Electronics">Samsung</a>, and giving more product training to employees.<sup id="cite_ref-success_77-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-success-77">[77]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2013, Best Buy exited the European consumer electronics market when it sold its 50% stake in Carphone Warehouse back to the UK-based mobile phone retailer.<sup id="cite_ref-wsjeu_78-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsjeu-78">[78]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bloombergeu_79-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloombergeu-79">[79]</a></sup> The sale was worth approximately $775 million.<sup id="cite_ref-wsjeu_78-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsjeu-78">[78]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bloombergeu_79-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloombergeu-79">[79]</a></sup></p><p>An increasing trend towards online shopping began to erode revenues and profits in the 2010s. A 4% dip in sales for the June 30, 2014 quarter, marked the tenth quarter in a row where Best Buy's sales had declined. The company, in announcing the result, said it was focusing more on digital-media in its marketing, moving away from newspaper, magazine, and television advertising.<sup id="cite_ref-BestBuyDip_80-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BestBuyDip-80">[80]</a></sup></p><p>On March 28, 2015, Best Buy announced the shutdown of the Future Shop chain in Canada; 65 of its 131 former locations were converted into Best Buy locations, while the rest (primarily those in close proximity to an existing Best Buy) were closed permanently.<sup id="cite_ref-gandm-fsclosed_81-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gandm-fsclosed-81">[81]</a></sup></p><p>On March 1, 2018, the company announced that it would shut down its 250 standalone Best Buy Mobile stores in the United States by the end of May, due to low revenue and high costs. The Best Buy Mobile stores were reported to account for 1 percent of the company's revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup></p><p>On May 9, 2018, the company unveiled a new logo for the first time in nearly three decades.<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup></p><p>On July 2, 2018, Best Buy announced it was cutting the amount of store space devoted to selling physical music, citing the popularity of streaming services as having reduced sales.<sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup></p><p>On April 15, 2019, Best Buy announced its current CFO, Corie Barry, would be its next CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-11">[11]</a></sup> She replaced Hubert Joly, who served as CEO since August 2012, in June 2019. Joly subsequently became executive chairman of the company.<sup id="cite_ref-startribune_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startribune-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ascends_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ascends-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p>Best Buy sells <a href="/wiki/Consumer_electronics" title="Consumer electronics">consumer electronics</a> and a variety of related merchandise, including software, video games, music, mobile phones, digital cameras, car stereos and video cameras, in addition to home appliances (washing machines, dryers, and refrigerators), in a <a href="/wiki/Commission_(remuneration)" title="Commission (remuneration)">non-commissioned</a> sales environment.<sup id="cite_ref-SEC_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEC-3">[3]</a></sup> Under the <a href="/wiki/Geek_Squad" title="Geek Squad">Geek Squad</a> brand, Best Buy offers computer repair, warranty service, and accidental service plans.<sup id="cite_ref-SEC_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEC-3">[3]</a></sup> Best Buy provides an online community forum for members, where consumers can discuss product experiences, ask questions and get answers from other members or retail product experts.<sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup></p><p>The building exteriors of Best Buy-branded stores are typically light brown, with the entrance designed to look like a blue box emerging from the structure.<sup id="cite_ref-todaysworld_86-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-todaysworld-86">[86]</a></sup> Corporate employees operated under a <a href="/wiki/ROWE" title="ROWE">results only work environment</a> from 2005 until March 2013, when the management style was abandoned by Best Buy CEO Hubert Joly.<sup id="cite_ref-startribunerowe1_87-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startribunerowe1-87">[87]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-startribunerowe2_88-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startribunerowe2-88">[88]</a></sup></p><p>As of October 29, 2016, Best Buy operated 1,026 Best Buy, 331 Best Buy Mobile stand-alone stores and 28 stand-alone Pacific Sales stores in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-SEC_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEC-3">[3]</a></sup> Best Buy also operated: 135 Best Buy and 53 Best Buy Mobile stand-alone stores in Canada; and 18 Best Buy stores and 5 Best Buy Express stores in Mexico.<sup id="cite_ref-SEC_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEC-3">[3]</a></sup> Best Buy exited the European market in April 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-wsjeu_78-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsjeu-78">[78]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bloombergeu_79-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloombergeu-79">[79]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="House_brands">House brands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Best_Buy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: House brands" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Best Buy also produces products under eight <a href="/wiki/House_brand" class="mw-redirect" title="House brand">house brands</a>:<sup id="cite_ref-SEC_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEC-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<ul><li>Dynex – Discount electronic and computer equipment such as Blu-ray players, data and power cables, HDTVs, office supplies, storage media, and webcams</li>
<li>Init – Storage products such as media storage, equipment bags, totes, and furniture for home theaters</li>
<li>Insignia – Electronic equipment, small appliances, and accessories including adapters, cables, HDTVs, mini refrigerators, and tablets</li>
<li>Magnolia Design Center (in select Best Buy stores) - design and installation of custom home theater setups</li>
<li>Magnolia Home Theater (in select Best Buy stores) – High-end home theater selections for both audio and visual equipment, including 4K, 3D, and large televisions, projectors, receivers, and speakers</li>
<li>Modal – style-oriented mobile accessories, including Bluetooth speakers, cables, and cases</li>
<li>Pacific Sales (in select Best Buy stores) – High-end appliances</li>
<li>Platinum – Highest-quality in-house brand for the company, producing many products, such as cables, cell phone accessories, tablet accessories, and digital imaging equipment</li>
<li>RocketFish – Cables primarily used with home-theater installation and setup as well as on computer and gaming accessories</li>
<li>RocketFish Mobile – Phone cases, gel skins, clips, and chargers for cell phones, GPS, and other exclusive high-end products</li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Best_Buy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Controversies" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Warranty">Warranty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Best_Buy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Warranty" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2000, two Florida consumers brought a lawsuit against the company, alleging that it engaged in fraudulent business practices related to the sale of <a href="/wiki/Extended_warranty" title="Extended warranty">extended warranties</a> (or, more accurately, <a href="/wiki/Service_plan" title="Service plan">service plans</a>). The suit claimed that store employees had misrepresented the manufacturer's <a href="/wiki/Warranty" title="Warranty">warranty</a> in order to sell its own Product Service/Replacement Plan and that Best Buy had "entered into a corporate-wide scheme to institute high-pressure sales techniques involving the extended warranties" and that the company used "artificial barriers to discourage consumers who purchased the 'complete extended warranties' from making legitimate claims."<sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup> The company ultimately settled for $200,000 but admitted no wrongdoing.<sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup></p><p>In 2014, Best Buy settled for $4.55 million in a class action lawsuit filed against them in April 2010 by consumers who claimed Best Buy was making unsolicited phone calls in contravention of the <a href="/wiki/Telephone_Consumer_Protection_Act_of_1991" title="Telephone Consumer Protection Act of 1991">Telephone Consumer Protection Act</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Pricing">Pricing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Best_Buy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Pricing" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In the second quarter of 2007, <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_Attorney_General" title="Connecticut Attorney General">Connecticut Attorney General</a> <a href="/wiki/Richard_Blumenthal" title="Richard Blumenthal">Richard Blumenthal</a> ordered an investigation into the company's use of an in-store website alleged to have misled customers on item sales prices.<sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup> In December 2007, the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> reported on the same issue, in which some customers claimed they thought they were surfing the Internet version of bestbuy.com at an in-store kiosk only to learn that the site reflected in-store prices only. In response, company spokesperson Sue Busch indicated the in-store kiosks were not intended for price-match purposes and rather were a means to navigate in-store availability. Since the initial investigation, a banner was placed on the in-store site to make its customers more aware of the difference.<sup id="cite_ref-93" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-93">[93]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Analog_televisions">Analog televisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Best_Buy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Analog televisions" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On April 26, 2008, the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Communications_Commission" title="Federal Communications Commission">U.S. Federal Communications Commission</a> (FCC) fined the company $280,000 for not alerting customers that the <a href="/wiki/Analog_television" title="Analog television">analog televisions</a> it sold would not receive over-the-air stations after the <a href="/wiki/Digital_television_transition" title="Digital television transition">digital transition</a> on June 12, 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup> The company challenged this ruling in May 2008 by the FCC saying it was and is in compliance with current FCC regulations pertaining to the digital transition.<sup id="cite_ref-95" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_issues">Environmental issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Best_Buy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Environmental issues" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Best Buy was one of several companies named in a 2007 report by <a href="/wiki/Greenpeace" title="Greenpeace">Greenpeace</a> for purchasing raw materials or manufactured products derived thereof from logging companies that, in the opinion of Greenpeace, contribute to unethical <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">deforestation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Taiga" title="Taiga">taiga</a> in Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-96" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-96">[96]</a></sup></p><p>Since that time, however, the company launched what it calls Greener Together to increase the energy efficiency of its products as well as reduce consumer waste through more recyclable packaging and proper disposal of certain electronic components such as rechargeable batteries, and empty ink cartridges.<sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97">[97]</a></sup></p><p>As a way to improve its image and past environmental issues, the company introduced a recycling program in 2009 that has since collected nearly half-a-billion pounds of consumer electronics and e-waste and is available at all their stores for a nominal fee. These items are then handed over to certified recyclers in the U.S. for proper recycling. The company's goal is to collect one billion pounds of recycling.<sup id="cite_ref-98" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-98">[98]</a></sup></p><p>It also has been named to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="United States Environmental Protection Agency">U.S. Environmental Protection Agency</a> top-50 list of the largest green-power purchasers. In 2011, the company purchased nearly 119 million kilowatt-hours (kWh) of green power –  electricity generated from renewable resources, such as wind, solar, geothermal, biogas, biomass and low-impact hydropower.<sup id="cite_ref-99" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99">[99]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="FBI_collaboration">FBI collaboration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Best_Buy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: FBI collaboration" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In their attempt to combat child pornography, the FBI hired several Best Buy employees from the Geek Squad division to covertly work for them flagging potential targets. In one incident, a customer brought in his computer for troubleshooting, which a Best Buy employee flagged as containing images of child pornography and reported to the FBI. The customer was indicted for possession of child pornography, although the judge in the case later threw out nearly all the evidence against the defendant due to "false and misleading statements" made by an FBI agent while trying to secure a search warrant for the customer's house, and the government ultimately dropped the case.<sup id="cite_ref-100" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-100">[100]</a></sup></p>
