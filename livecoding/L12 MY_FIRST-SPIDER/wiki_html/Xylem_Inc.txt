</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xylem_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>On January 12, 2011, Xylem's parent, ITT Corporation announced its plan to separate the company into three, stand-alone publicly traded, independent companies".<sup id="cite_ref-ITT-Corporation-Jul-2011-10-12B_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITT-Corporation-Jul-2011-10-12B-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-reuters.com_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuters.com-6">[6]</a></sup> The future water company was named Xylem, pronounced zi-lem. (The name "<a href="/wiki/Xylem" title="Xylem">Xylem</a>", derived from classical Greek, is the tissue that transports water in plants.) It comprises three business units – Water Solutions, Analytics, and Applied Water Systems.<sup id="cite_ref-about_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-about-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>ITT Corporation spun off its defense businesses into a company named <a href="/wiki/ITT_Exelis" class="mw-redirect" title="ITT Exelis">ITT Exelis</a>, and retained its corporate name, logo and “Engineered for life” company tagline for its other activities.<sup id="cite_ref-reuters_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2014, Patrick Decker succeeded Steven R. Loranger as the CEO and president of Xylem.<sup id="cite_ref-Xylem-Inc-Mar-2014-8-K_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Xylem-Inc-Mar-2014-8-K-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Loranger was Chairman, President and CEO of ITT Corporation when it spun its water businesses off as Xylem in October 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-Xylem-Inc-Sep-2013-8-K_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Xylem-Inc-Sep-2013-8-K-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Brands">Brands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xylem_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Brands" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Flygt">Flygt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xylem_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Flygt" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Flygt_propellerpumpar.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Flygt_propellerpumpar.jpg/220px-Flygt_propellerpumpar.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3840" data-file-height="2160"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 124px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Flygt_propellerpumpar.jpg/220px-Flygt_propellerpumpar.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="124" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Flygt_propellerpumpar.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A selection of Flygt submersible <a href="/wiki/Propeller_pump" class="mw-redirect" title="Propeller pump">propeller pumps</a> in Xylems factory in Emmaboda</div></div></div>
<p><b>Flygt</b> is a manufacturer of dry and <a href="/wiki/Submersible_pump" title="Submersible pump">submersible pumps</a>, <a href="/wiki/Submersible_mixer" title="Submersible mixer">submersible mixers</a> and related intelligent control systems. Product development and manufacturing of Flygt products are based in Sweden.
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="History_2">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xylem_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In 1901, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Per_Alfred_Stenberg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Per Alfred Stenberg (page does not exist)">Per Alfred Stenberg</a> established a foundry in the small town of <a href="/wiki/Emmaboda" title="Emmaboda">Emmaboda</a> in southern Sweden. One of the main products was <a href="/wiki/Molding_(process)" title="Molding (process)">molds</a> for the glass industry. Per Alfred transfer ownership of the company to his sons. In 1922, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hilding_Flygt&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hilding Flygt (page does not exist)">Hilding Flygt</a> set up a pump and fan sales company in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>. Through a newspaper advertisement the two companies are brought into contact with one another in 1929, and led to the Stenberg brothers beginning to produce Flygt pumps in Emmaboda. 
Sixten Englesson, chief engineer, invented the first <a href="/wiki/Submersible_pump" title="Submersible pump">submersible drainage pump</a> in 1947 and the first submersible sewage pump in 1956. In 1968, the Stenberg family sold the company to <a href="/wiki/ITT_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="ITT Corporation">ITT Corporation</a>. During the 1970s, the product range was expanded with mixers and other products.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Goulds_Water_Technology">Goulds Water Technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xylem_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Goulds Water Technology" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><b>Goulds Water Technology</b> is an American manufacturer of water pumps. Incorporated in the 19th century, Goulds is now a brand of Xylem Inc.
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="History_3">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xylem_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: History" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>The corporate history of Goulds Pumps began in <a href="/wiki/Seneca_Falls,_New_York" title="Seneca Falls, New York">Seneca Falls, New York</a> in 1848, when Seabury S. Gould purchased the interests of Edward Mynderse and H.C. Silsby in Downs, Mynderse &amp; Co., a pump making business which had started up in 1840. The company, known as <b>Downs &amp; Company</b> until 1869, cast and assembled the world's first all-metal pump in 1849.<sup id="cite_ref-pump-zone.com_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pump-zone.com-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In 1869, the Goulds name was added, and the company became known as <b>Goulds Manufacturing Company</b>. The Gould family ran the operation from 1872 until 1964, renaming the company <b>Goulds Pumps Incorporated</b> in 1926.
</p><p>In the 1960s, Goulds expanded by adding acquisitions of companies in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>. The company expanded under a joint operational agreement into <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>. By the 1990s, Goulds expanded into <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> and <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>.
</p><p>In 1997, Goulds Pumps was purchased by ITT Industries. Goulds and ITT served 130 nations combined. Goulds Pumps was a subsidiary with its own name under ITT. Goulds Pumps management moved back to Seneca Falls in 1998.
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Branding_under_Xylem">Branding under Xylem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xylem_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Branding under Xylem" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>On October 31, 2011, Xylem Inc. completed its spinoff from <a href="/wiki/ITT_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="ITT Corporation">ITT Corporation</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Xylem-Inc-Nov-2011-10-Q_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Xylem-Inc-Nov-2011-10-Q-15">[15]</a></sup> and became a leading brand of water solution products, with 2011 revenues of $3.8 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-Xylem-Inc-Feb-2012-10-K_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Xylem-Inc-Feb-2012-10-K-16">[16]</a></sup> Goulds Water Technology is a brand of Xylem Inc. providing pumps for residential, commercial and agricultural applications and <a href="/wiki/Goulds_Pumps" class="mw-redirect" title="Goulds Pumps">Goulds Pumps</a> remains a part of ITT Corporation providing pump products for the industrial market.
</p><p>Xylem comprises three business units – Water Solutions, Analytics, and Applied Water Systems. These three units are interconnected, anticipating and reflecting evolving needs and sharing their applications expertise to cover every stage of the water cycle. The Goulds Water technology brand<sup id="cite_ref-unitedstates.xylemappliedwater.com_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unitedstates.xylemappliedwater.com-17">[17]</a></sup> is part of the Applied Water Systems business unit that manufacturers energy efficient centrifugal and turbine pumps, controllers, variable frequency drives, and accessories for agricultural, building trades, commercial and light industrial water and products for wastewater applications.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Hypack">Hypack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xylem_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Hypack" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><b>Hypack</b>, a leading producer of hydrographic data acquisition and processing software, was acquired by Xylem in November, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-Xylem_Acquires_Hypack_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Xylem_Acquires_Hypack-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="YSI">YSI</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xylem_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: YSI" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><b>YSI, a Xylem brand</b> is a developer and manufacturer of sensors, instruments, software, and data collection platforms for environmental water quality monitoring and testing.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_history">Corporate history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xylem_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Corporate history" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>The company reaches back to 1948 when a three-man partnership was forged at Antioch College in Yellow Springs, Ohio, USA. This village, between Dayton and Columbus, still serves as the headquarters for what started as the "Yellow Springs Instrument Company" — now known as YSI Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In the 1980s, Malte von Matthiessen succeeded Hardy Trolander as president. In <code>200z</code>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Ron Geis appointed new General Manager of YSI; former CEO Rick Omlor continues as Executive Advisor.
In <code>200x</code>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> YSI merged with Analytics division of ITT Corp. to form new water company Xylem.
</p>

<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xylem_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Acquisitions" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>The firm acquired various companies: in the 1980s Design Analysis Associates, Inc., a firm founded by William I. Fletcher; and acquired Endeco, a Marion, MA, oceanographic data collection and monitoring company. In the 1990s they acquired Victory Engineering Company, Springfield, NJ to complement YSI temperature product line, and SonTek, a precision water velocity measurement company, founded in California; they also acquired controlling interests in Clandon Scientific in England In the 2000s, they acquired Hydrodta UK, and acquired environmental business from AMJ Equipment Corp and renamed the new environmental monitoring and services company AMJ Environmental in St. Petersburg, Florida. In <code>200a</code>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Design Analysis Associates in Logan, UT, a manufacturer of data loggers and radar level sensors
</p><p>Xylem announced that it would buy Sensus USA Inc., an advanced metering technology manufacturer, in August 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-Xylem-Inc-Aug-2016-8-K_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Xylem-Inc-Aug-2016-8-K-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The acquisition was completed on October 31,2016 for $1.7 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-Xylem-Inc-Nov-2016-8-K_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Xylem-Inc-Nov-2016-8-K-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Products">Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xylem_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Products" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>The company from the start introduced the first commercial product in many categories: In 1952, they introduced the First quartz crystal electronic stopwatch, and the first practical electronic thermometer. In 1956, first commercial <a href="/wiki/Heart-lung_machine" class="mw-redirect" title="Heart-lung machine">heart-lung machine</a>, in 1957, the first instantaneous blood-gas analyzer.<sup id="cite_ref-history_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>They continued development in their key area of temperature measurement. They produced the first interchangeable thermistor temperature probes in 1955, and developed it into a complete line of precision interchangeable thermistors in 1961, and in 1965 developed the first linear thermistor temperature sensor, which <i>Industrial Research</i> magazine recognized as one of the 100 most significant new technological products of the year. In other analytical instruments, they produced the first practical <a href="/wiki/Dissolved_oxygen" class="mw-redirect" title="Dissolved oxygen">dissolved oxygen</a> meter for field and laboratory use in 1965, which the same magazine recognized as yet another of the most significant technological products of the year.<sup id="cite_ref-history_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>Their oxygen meter had used the technique of <a href="/wiki/Polarography" title="Polarography">polarography</a>, and they developed further products on the same principle, including t the first immobilized-enzyme-activated polarographic sensor in 1972. They then developed further biomedical instrumentation: the first commercially successful whole blood glucose analyzer in 1975, the first commercial alcohol measurement instrument using immobilized enzyme electrode technology in 1982. They continued using <a href="/wiki/Immobilized_enzyme" title="Immobilized enzyme">immobilized enzyme</a> techniques, for the first direct whole-blood L-lactate assays using immobilized enzyme electrode also in 1982. They also continued developing thermal technology, with the first commercial <a href="/wiki/Gallium" title="Gallium">gallium</a> melting-point temperature standard (29.7715 °C) in 1977 and the first robotic-manufactured glass-encapsulated precision thermistors in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-history_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>They produced their first <a href="/wiki/Environmental_monitoring" title="Environmental monitoring">environmental monitoring</a> instrument, a dissolved oxygen sensor, in 1992. They then developed instrumentation for conductivity measurement of <a href="/wiki/Ultrapure_water" title="Ultrapure water">ultrapure water</a> in 1996, and the first accurate commercial fiber-optic based CO2 measurement system, in 1998.
</p><p>Beginning in the late 1990s, they began producing analytical software and integrated analytical instrumentation: BOD Analyst(TM), software that automates BOD testing.They also began developing integrated analytical systems: the YSI 600 QS Quick Sample multiparameter spot sampling system, the YSI 5200 Recirculating System Monitor, and the YSI 7100 Multiparameter Bioanalytical System (MBS).
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Charitable">Charitable</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xylem_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Charitable" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In the spring of 2015, <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Specimen_Marine_Laboratory" title="Gulf Specimen Marine Laboratory">Gulf Specimen Marine Laboratory</a>, a small nonprofit educational and scientific marine supply company, raised over $72,000 in a <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a> campaign to replace their ancient and failing Wedeco ozonator system for water purification required in their aquarium. Xylem Inc provided their new ozonator system "at cost", with full engineering support and installation allowing GSML to have the new system with the funds they had raised.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
