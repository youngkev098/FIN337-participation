</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Synchrony_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Synchrony traces its roots to 1932.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> GE Capital Retail Bank was started during the <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a>, providing customers a line of credit to purchase GE appliances. The lending arm remained GE Capital Retail Bank until being spun off from its parent company in June 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-10">[10]</a></sup> GE filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission on March 13, 2014 announcing its intention to spin-off the portion of its GE Capital business dedicated to retail credit cards. GE’s SEC filing indicated that the new company would be called Synchrony Financial.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">[9]</a></sup>  On July 31, 2014 Synchrony Financial raised $2.88 billion in its <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_corporate_history">Recent corporate history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Synchrony_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Recent corporate history" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In 2017, Synchrony acquired GP Shopper.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, Synchrony acquired Loop Commerce, which provides a patented digital gifting platform called GiftNow.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In 2018, Synchrony’s largest program was the acquisition of PayPal’s $7.6 billion credit receivables portfolio.<sup id="cite_ref-2018-10K_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2018-10K-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>  PayPal also chose Synchrony Bank to be its exclusive issuer for the PayPal Credit point of sale financing program in the United States through 2028. Additionally, PayPal agreed to extend its existing co-brand credit card relationship with Synchrony through the same 10-year term. These transactions (collectively, the “PayPal transaction”) officially closed in July 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In 2019, Synchrony announced the acquisition of Pets Best, which offers pet insurance and wellness plans for dogs and cats. Pets Best will operate under Synchrony's CareCredit platform.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p><a href="/wiki/GIC_Private_Limited" title="GIC Private Limited">GIC Private Limited</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_wealth_fund" title="Sovereign wealth fund">sovereign wealth fund</a> of <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, owns 7.72% of Synchrony's stock.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Products_and_technology">Products and technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Synchrony_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Products and technology" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>Synchrony is the largest provider of private label credit cards in the U.S. The company provides private label credit cards for such brands as <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon.com">Amazon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cathay_Pacific" title="Cathay Pacific">Cathay Pacific</a>, CheapOAir, OneTravel,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lowe%27s" title="Lowe's">Lowe's</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Johnson_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Johnson-19">[19]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Guitar_Center" title="Guitar Center">Guitar Center</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gap_Inc." title="Gap Inc.">Gap</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/BP" title="BP">BP</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Furniture_HomeStores" class="mw-redirect" title="Ashley Furniture HomeStores">Ashley HomeStores</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-retail_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-retail-23">[23]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Discount_Tire" title="Discount Tire">Discount Tire</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-retail_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-retail-23">[23]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/J.C._Penney" class="mw-redirect" title="J.C. Penney">J.C. Penney</a>, and <a href="/wiki/P._C._Richard_%26_Son" title="P. C. Richard &amp; Son">P. C. Richard &amp; Son</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-retail_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-retail-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>In 2019, Synchrony expanded its auto and home acceptance locations and value propositions.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Working with the Discover Network, the Synchrony HOME Credit Card can be used by consumers to finance their home needs at more than a million large, medium, and small retail locations nationwide. <sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Synchrony also increased its Car Care acceptance network to more than 500,000 locations across 25 categories including gas, auto parts and service, car washes, parking, and ride sharing. <sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>Synchrony has four Innovation Stations, which are located in Stamford, Connecticut; Chicago, Illinois; Kettering, Ohio;<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> and Hyderabad, India. <sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup>The in-house, cross-functional teams use agile methodology to test new ideas/technologies, solve client problems, and improve speed to market.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>Synchrony’s corporate ventures arm, Synchrony Ventures, invests in early-stage companies that provide emerging technology and products.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>In 2016, Synchrony Financial launched Digital Apply, a credit application site, and SyPI (Synchrony plug-in), an integrated plug-in credit solution for retailers’ mobile apps, through which customers can access their credit card information.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> 
This plug-in helps credit card customers to access their credit information and manages bill payments and other card related activities.
As of 2019, more than 20 retailers had adopted SyPI, and nearly $2 billion in credit card payments had been made through the technology. <sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, Synchrony created the Alexa Store Card skill for customers to manage their Amazon Store Cards via voice technology.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p><p>A majority of Synchrony’s retail partners have deployed Synchrony’s AI-enabled virtual assistant Sydney for cardholders. <sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Public-private_partnerships">Public-private partnerships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Synchrony_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Public-private partnerships" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>In 2016, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Connecticut" title="University of Connecticut">University of Connecticut</a>’s School of Engineering announced the launch of Synchrony’s Center of Excellence in Cybersecurity. <sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, Synchrony opened an emerging tech center at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Illinois" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Illinois">University of Illinois</a>. <sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Settlements">Settlements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Synchrony_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Settlements" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<p>In June 2014, shortly before Synchrony’s <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> debut, the <a href="/wiki/Consumer_Financial_Protection_Bureau" title="Consumer Financial Protection Bureau">U.S. Consumer Financial Protection Bureau</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Justice" class="mw-redirect" title="Department of Justice">Department of Justice</a> reached a $225 million settlement with the company after it entered into a consent decree with the  Consumer Financial Protection Bureau. The settlement stated that while operating as <a href="/wiki/GE_Capital_Bank" class="mw-redirect" title="GE Capital Bank">GE Capital Bank</a>, the company had engaged in deceptive and discriminatory credit card practices.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup>  According to <i>USA Today</i>, “The bank said it discontinued such sales practices in 2012 and has already refunded more than $11 million in fees as a result of its own review.” <sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
