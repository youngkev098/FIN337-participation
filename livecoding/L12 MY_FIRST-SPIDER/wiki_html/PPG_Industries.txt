<p><b>PPG Industries, Inc.</b> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> company and global supplier of <a href="/wiki/Paint" title="Paint">paints</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coating" title="Coating">coatings</a>, and specialty materials. With headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, PPG operates in more than 70 countries around the globe. By revenue it is the largest coatings company in the world followed by <a href="/wiki/AkzoNobel" title="AkzoNobel">AkzoNobel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/PPG_Place" title="PPG Place">PPG Place</a>, an office and retail complex in downtown Pittsburgh, and is known for its glass facade designed by <a href="/wiki/Philip_Johnson" title="Philip Johnson">Philip Johnson</a>.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Founding_and_20th_century">Founding and 20th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=PPG_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Founding and 20th century" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<table class="box-Expand_section plainlinks metadata ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs expansion</b>. <small>You can help by <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=PPG_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=">adding to it</a>.</small>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">September 2015</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table><div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Plaques_in_Pittsburgh_-_IMG_7580.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Plaques_in_Pittsburgh_-_IMG_7580.JPG/240px-Plaques_in_Pittsburgh_-_IMG_7580.JPG" decoding="async" width="240" height="180" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 240px;height: 180px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Plaques_in_Pittsburgh_-_IMG_7580.JPG/240px-Plaques_in_Pittsburgh_-_IMG_7580.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="240" data-height="180" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Plaques_in_Pittsburgh_-_IMG_7580.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pittsburgh Plate Glass Company plaque in the plaza at <a href="/wiki/PPG_Place" title="PPG Place">PPG Place</a></div></div></div>
<p><b>Pittsburgh Plate Glass Company</b>  was founded in 1883 by Captain <a href="/wiki/John_Baptiste_Ford" title="John Baptiste Ford">John Baptiste Ford</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Pitcairn,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="John Pitcairn, Jr.">John Pitcairn, Jr.</a>, at <a href="/wiki/Creighton,_Pennsylvania" title="Creighton, Pennsylvania">Creighton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>.
</p><p>Based in <a href="/wiki/Creighton,_Pennsylvania" title="Creighton, Pennsylvania">Creighton, Pennsylvania</a> (about 20 miles north of Pittsburgh along the <a href="/wiki/Allegheny_River" title="Allegheny River">Allegheny River</a>), PPG soon became the United States' first commercially successful producer of high-quality, thick <a href="/wiki/Flat_glass" class="mw-redirect" title="Flat glass">flat glass</a> using the plate process. PPG was also the world's first plate glass plant to power its furnaces with locally produced <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a>, an innovation which rapidly stimulated widespread industrial use of the cleaner-burning fuel.<sup id="cite_ref-PPG_Industries_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PPG_Industries-2">[2]</a></sup></p><p>PPG expanded quickly. By 1900, known as the "Glass Trust", it included 10 plants, had a 65 percent share of the U.S. plate glass market, and had become the nation's second largest producer of paint.<sup id="cite_ref-Local_Heritage_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Local_Heritage-3">[3]</a></sup> Today, known as PPG Industries, the company is a multibillion-dollar, <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> corporation with 150 manufacturing locations around the world. It now produces <a href="/wiki/Coatings" class="mw-redirect" title="Coatings">coatings</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glass" title="Glass">glass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fiberglass" title="Fiberglass">fiberglass</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Chemicals" class="mw-redirect" title="Chemicals">chemicals</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PPG_Industries_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PPG_Industries-2">[2]</a></sup></p><p>Pitcairn served as a director of PPG from its start, its president from 1897 to 1905, and  chairman of the board from 1894 until his death.<sup id="cite_ref-biodict_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-biodict-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="20th_century">20th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=PPG_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 20th century" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>On 19 December 1968 the company changed its name to PPG Industries, Inc., to show its diverse offerings. Ditzler Color Company, established in 1902 in Detroit as an automotive color concern, was purchased by Pittsburgh Plate Glass Company (now PPG) in 1928. 
In the mid-1980s, Cipisa, a Spanish paint company was acquired and renamed PPG Ibérica. The CEO of Cipisa, Pere Nadal Carres became CEO of PPG Ibérica.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 1990 PPG founded <a href="/wiki/Transitions_Optical" title="Transitions Optical">Transitions Optical</a> as a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Essilor" title="Essilor">Essilor</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="21st_century">21st century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=PPG_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 21st century" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>In October 2000, PPG Industries announced it had agreed to buy Courtaulds Aerospace for $512.5 million.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Based in Glendale, California, the aerospace business has annual sales of approximately $240 million, employs 1,200 people. It manufactures sealants in Glendale, California, US and Shildon, England; coatings and sealants in Mojave, California, US; glazing sealants at Gloucester City, New Jersey, US; and coatings at Gonfreville, France. The business also operates 14 application-support centers in North America, Europe, Africa, Asia, and Australia.
</p><p>In 2006, the company rolled out a two-tiered pay system which resulted in massive strikes at union locations across the country. The system, now in place company-wide for non-salaried employees, provides lower rates of pay and fewer benefits to those employed after the roll-out than those employed before the roll-out. In West Virginia, US after a bitter five-month strike, the company still refused to eliminate the two-tiered system. In Lake Charles, Louisiana, US, the employment contracts of union employees had to be extended during a strike regarding the two-tiered system.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 2007, the company was involved in a lawsuit on failing to disclose a purchase reduction of its two major auto glass customers.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On 2 January 2008, PPG acquired the SigmaKalon Group of companies for $3.2 billion from private investment firm <a href="/wiki/Bain_Capital" title="Bain Capital">Bain Capital</a>, strongly increasing its paint and specialty coatings business.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 2008, PPG renamed their automotive glass division PGW (Pittsburgh Glass Works) and sold a majority of it to <a href="/wiki/Kohlberg_%26_Company" title="Kohlberg &amp; Company">Kohlberg &amp; Company</a>. PPG retained 40% ownership of PGW  until March 2016 when they sold their stake to automotive parts salvage company <a href="/wiki/LKQ_Corporation" title="LKQ Corporation">LKQ Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012, PPG acquired <a href="https://da.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dyrup" class="extiw" title="da:Dyrup">Dyrup A/S</a>, founded in <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> in 1928 as <i>S. Dyrup &amp; Co A/S</i> by Sigurd Dyrup and Axel Monberg and Ejnar Thorsen of <a href="/wiki/MT_H%C3%B8jgaard" title="MT Højgaard">MT Højgaard</a>, some of whose brands are Dyrup, GORI (outdoor woodcare paint), and Bondex.
</p><p>In April 2013, PPG completed the acquisition of AkzoNobel North American <a href="/wiki/Architectural_coatings" title="Architectural coatings">architectural coatings</a> business including <a href="/wiki/Glidden_(paints)" title="Glidden (paints)">Glidden</a>, Liquid Nails, and Flood brands.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>  2013 revenue was <a href="/wiki/US%24" class="mw-redirect" title="US$">US$</a>15.1 billion, while assets were US$15.9 billion.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>On April 1, 2014, PPG finalized the sale of Transitions Optical to its joint venture partner, Essilor International of France, however, PPG’s technical center in Monroeville will continue to provide research and development services for Transitions.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>  On November 5, 2014 PPG closed a deal, to purchase Mexican Consorcio Comex, S.A. de C.V. (“<a href="/wiki/Comex_Group" title="Comex Group">Comex</a>”) for $2.3 billion <sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2015, PPG Industries completed the acquisition of REVOCOAT, a global supplier of sealants.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/Charles_E._Bunch" class="mw-redirect" title="Charles E. Bunch">Chuck Bunch</a> remains <a href="/wiki/Executive_Chairman" class="mw-redirect" title="Executive Chairman">Executive Chairman</a>, while Michael McGarry serves as <a href="/wiki/President_(corporate_title)" title="President (corporate title)">President</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">CEO</a>.
</p><p>In July 2016, PPG announced its sale of the flat glass business to <a href="/wiki/Vitro" title="Vitro">Vitro</a>, a glass manufacturer based in Mexico, for $750 million.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2016, <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Post-Gazette" title="Pittsburgh Post-Gazette">Pittsburgh Post-Gazette</a> announced the acquisition of the naming rights to <a href="/wiki/Consol_Energy_Center" class="mw-redirect" title="Consol Energy Center">Consol Energy Center</a> by PPG; the facility will be known as <a href="/wiki/PPG_Paints_Arena" title="PPG Paints Arena">PPG Paints Arena</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2017, PPG announced the sale of its remaining fiberglass operations to <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Electric_Glass" title="Nippon Electric Glass">Nippon Electric Glass</a> for $541 million.  This was following PPG's 2016 sale of its European fiberglass operations to NEG, and divesting its ownership in two other Asian fiberglass joint ventures.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Attempted_acquisition_of_AkzoNobel">Attempted acquisition of AkzoNobel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=PPG_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Attempted acquisition of AkzoNobel" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/AkzoNobel" title="AkzoNobel">AkzoNobel</a></div>
<p>In March 2017, the company launched an unsolicited takeover bid of €20.9 billion, which was promptly rejected by AkzoNobel’s management.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Days later, the company launched an increased bid of €24.5 billion ($26.3 billion), which was again rejected by AkzoNobel’s management.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> A number of shareholders urged AkzoNobel to explore the offer and subsequent negotiations.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In April, activist investor <a href="/w/index.php?title=Elliot_Investors&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Elliot Investors (page does not exist)">Elliot Investors</a> called for the removal of the Chairman of Akzo, <a href="/wiki/Antony_Burgmans" title="Antony Burgmans">Antony Burgmans</a>, following Akzo's refusal to enter talks with PPG. Elliott, which has a 3.25% stake in the company, claimed it was one of a group of investors that met the Dutch legal threshold of 10% voting-share support, which is needed to call an extraordinary meeting to vote on a proposal to remove Burgmans.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On April 13, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Templeton_Global_Equity&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Templeton Global Equity (page does not exist)">Templeton Global Equity</a> said it was among another group of investors calling for an extraordinary meeting of AkzoNobel shareholders to discuss Burgmans' continued tenure as Chairman.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Later in the same month Akzo outlined its plan to separate its chemicals division and pay shareholders €1.6 billion in extra dividends, in order to attempt to hold off PPG.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The new Akzo strategy was dismissed by PPG, which claimed that their offer represented better value for shareholders,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> supported by activist Akzo shareholder Elliot Advisors.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> On April 24, a day before Akzo's annual meeting of shareholders, PPG increased its final offer by approximately 8% to $28.8 billion (€26.9 billion, €96.75 per share), with Akzo's share price rising 6% to a record of €82.95 per share.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Akzo shareholder Columbia Threadneedle Investments urged the company to open dialogue with PPG,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> whilst PPG claimed that the deal would add to earning within its first year.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Great Britain's largest pension scheme investors, Universities Superannuation Scheme (USS), urged Akzo to engage with PPG.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> On 2 May, <i><a href="/wiki/Reuters" title="Reuters">Reuters</a></i> revealed that the supervisory board of Akzo was meeting to discuss how to deal with PPG's third offer, still maintaining it did not value the company highly enough.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> In early May, Akzo again rejected PPG's bid, citing the deal still undervalues the company, as well as potentially facing antitrust risks, and not addressing other concerns such as "cultural differences". Under Dutch company law, PPG must now decide to either make a formal bid or walk away.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> In early June PPG chose to abandon pursuit.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_record">Environmental record</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=PPG_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Environmental record" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">

<p>In November 2010, PPG agreed to remove 700,000 tons of toxic waste from <a href="/wiki/Canal_Crossing,_Jersey_City" title="Canal Crossing, Jersey City">Canal Crossing</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Brownfield_land" title="Brownfield land">brownfield site</a> in <a href="/wiki/Jersey_City,_New_Jersey" title="Jersey City, New Jersey">Jersey City, New Jersey</a> where the company operated a <a href="/wiki/Chromium" title="Chromium">chromium</a> processing plant between 1954 and 1963.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-recipient_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-recipient-41">[41]</a></sup> Stringent standards were agreed to in a federal court settlement.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p><p>Lime Lake Reclamation Project of PPG Industries in Barberton, Ohio, received special awards in the National Beneficial Use of Biosolids Program from <a href="/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="United States Environmental Protection Agency">Environmental Protection Agency</a>’s (EPA) region 5 in 1998.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="In_sports">In sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=PPG_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: In sports" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<p>PPG has been involved in sports for numerous years, including being the primary sponsor for the <a href="/wiki/Champ_Car" class="mw-redirect" title="Champ Car">CART</a> Indy car series in the 1980s-1990s. It has also been an associate sponsor with the present day <a href="/wiki/IndyCar_Series" title="IndyCar Series">IndyCar Series</a>, and in the <a href="/wiki/Monster_Energy_NASCAR_Cup_Series" class="mw-redirect" title="Monster Energy NASCAR Cup Series">Monster Energy NASCAR Cup Series</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Since the race's inception in 1994, PPG has sponsored the trophy for the <a href="/wiki/Brickyard_400" title="Brickyard 400">Brickyard 400</a>.
</p><p>On October 4, 2016, it was announced that The <a href="/wiki/Consol_Energy_Center" class="mw-redirect" title="Consol Energy Center">Consol Energy Center</a> in Pittsburgh would be giving up its naming rights, and transferred to PPG, making the arena named the <a href="/wiki/PPG_Paints_Arena" title="PPG Paints Arena">PPG Paints Arena</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
