</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_operations">Current operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silicon_Valley_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Current operations" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company focuses on lending to technology companies, providing multiple services to <a href="/wiki/Venture_capital" title="Venture capital">venture capital</a>, <a href="/wiki/Revenue-based_financing" title="Revenue-based financing">revenue-based financing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Private_equity" title="Private equity">private equity</a> firms that invest in technology and biotechnology, and also on <a href="/wiki/Private_banking" title="Private banking">private banking</a> services for <a href="/wiki/High-net-worth_individual" title="High-net-worth individual">high-net-worth individuals</a>, in its home market in Silicon Valley.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup> In addition to taking deposits and making loans, the bank operates venture capital and private equity divisions that sometimes invest in the firm's commercial banking clients.<sup id="cite_ref-start_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-start-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>The bank is the largest bank in <a href="/wiki/Silicon_Valley" title="Silicon Valley">Silicon Valley</a> in terms of deposits, with a 25.9% market share as of June 30, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>The bank operates from 29 offices in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and has banking operations in: 
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Herzliya_Pituah" title="Herzliya Pituah">Herzliya Pituah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> <sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li></ul><p>In addition, SVB Global Services India LLP is a non-banking subsidiary of SVB Financial Group in <a href="/wiki/Bangalore" title="Bangalore">Bangalore</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> providing IT and back office support.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silicon_Valley_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Silicon Valley Bank (SVB) was founded in 1982; its first office opened in 1983.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The bank’s main strategy was collecting deposits from businesses financed through venture capital. It then expanded into banking and financing venture capitalists themselves, and added services aimed at allowing the bank to keep clients as they matured from their startup phase.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 1993, the bank's founding CEO, Roger V. Smith, was replaced by John C. Dean; Smith became Vice Chairman of the bank.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Smith left in 1994 to launch the Smith Venture Group.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In 2002, the bank began expanding its private banking business, which up to that point had been done primarily as a favor to wealthy venture capitalists and entrepreneurs<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In 2004, the bank opened international subsidiaries in <a href="/wiki/Bangalore" title="Bangalore">Bangalore</a>, India, and <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>; in 2005 it opened offices in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In 2006, the bank began operations in the UK and opened its first branch there in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-start_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-start-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2008, SVB Financial received a $235 million investment from the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Treasury" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Treasury">U.S. Treasury</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Troubled_Asset_Relief_Program" title="Troubled Asset Relief Program">Troubled Asset Relief Program</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The U.S. Treasury received $10 million in dividends from Silicon Valley bank and, in December 2009, the bank repurchased the outstanding stock and warrants held by the government, funding this through a stock sale of $300 million.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2011, the bank was named "Bank Of The Year" by the <a href="/wiki/Export%E2%80%93Import_Bank_of_the_United_States" title="Export–Import Bank of the United States">Export–Import Bank of the United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In April, Ken Wilcox, who had been CEO since 2000, left the CEO position, while remaining Chairman of the Board; he was replaced by Greg Becker.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2012, the bank announced a 50-50 joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Shanghai_Pudong_Development_Bank" title="Shanghai Pudong Development Bank">Shanghai Pudong Development Bank</a> (SPDB) to provide capital to start-up technology entrepreneurs.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In July 2015, the joint venture was granted approval by the China Bank Regulatory Commission (CBRC) to operate in <a href="/wiki/Renminbi" title="Renminbi">renminbi</a> (RMB), the official currency of the People’s Republic of China. This license allows the joint venture to provide banking products and services to its clients in local Chinese currency.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2017, Michael R. Descheneaux was named <a href="/wiki/President_(corporate_title)" title="President (corporate title)">president</a> of the company.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
