<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">This article is about the television broadcasting company. For the company prior to the spin off and acquisition by <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">The Walt Disney Company</a>, see <a href="/wiki/21st_Century_Fox" title="21st Century Fox">21st Century Fox</a>.</div>
<p><b>Fox Corporation</b> (abbreviated <b>Fox Corp.</b> and informally "<b>New Fox</b>"; simply called as <b>Fox</b>) is an American media company headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Midtown_Manhattan" title="Midtown Manhattan">Midtown Manhattan</a>, New York City. It was formed from the 2019 <a href="/wiki/Acquisition_of_21st_Century_Fox_by_Disney" title="Acquisition of 21st Century Fox by Disney">acquisition</a> of <a href="/wiki/21st_Century_Fox" title="21st Century Fox">21st Century Fox</a> (itself formed from the 2013 split of <a href="/wiki/News_Corporation_(1980%E2%80%932013)" title="News Corporation (1980–2013)">News Corporation</a>) by <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">The Walt Disney Company</a>. Fox Corp. was <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spun off</a> from 21st Century Fox and its stock began trading on March 19, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-FSN_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FSN-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Fox_Notes_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fox_Notes-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Chang_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chang-8">[8]</a></sup> It is owned by the <a href="/wiki/Murdoch_family" title="Murdoch family">Murdoch family</a> via a family trust with 39% interest; <a href="/wiki/Rupert_Murdoch" title="Rupert Murdoch">Rupert Murdoch</a> is co-executive chairman, while his son <a href="/wiki/Lachlan_Murdoch" title="Lachlan Murdoch">Lachlan Murdoch</a> is chairman and CEO.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fox_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company name traces back through a series of mergers and demergers to the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Film" title="Fox Film">Fox Film Corporation</a> founded by <a href="/wiki/William_Fox_(producer)" title="William Fox (producer)">William Fox</a> on February 1, 1915.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fox_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On December 14, 2017, <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">The Walt Disney Company</a> announced its <a href="/wiki/Acquisition_of_21st_Century_Fox_by_Disney" title="Acquisition of 21st Century Fox by Disney">intent to acquire</a> the motion picture, cable entertainment, and direct broadcast satellite divisions of <a href="/wiki/21st_Century_Fox" title="21st Century Fox">21st Century Fox</a>. The remainder of the company would form a so-called "New Fox", maintaining control of assets such as Fox's <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">television network</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fox_Television_Stations" title="Fox Television Stations">broadcast stations</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fox_News" title="Fox News">Fox News</a>, the national operations of <a href="/wiki/Fox_Sports_(United_States)" title="Fox Sports (United States)">Fox Sports</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox" class="mw-redirect" title="20th Century Fox">20th Century Fox</a> studio lot, which would be leased to Disney for seven years. Fox's <a href="/wiki/Fox_Sports_Networks" title="Fox Sports Networks">regional sports networks</a> were to be included in the sale to Disney.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In May 2018, it was confirmed that <a href="/wiki/Lachlan_Murdoch" title="Lachlan Murdoch">Lachlan Murdoch</a>, rather than <a href="/wiki/James_Murdoch" title="James Murdoch">James Murdoch</a>, would take charge of the New Fox company.<sup id="cite_ref-Lachlan_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lachlan-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>The merger faced antitrust scrutiny; Disney would not legally be able to own both the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> and Fox networks due to an <a href="/wiki/Federal_Communications_Commission" title="Federal Communications Commission">FCC</a> policy known as the "dual network rule", which prohibits mergers between the top broadcast networks.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Justice" title="United States Department of Justice">Department of Justice</a> also ordered that Fox's regional sports networks be divested by Disney within 90 days of closing. Disney owns 80 percent of <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a>, and the Justice Department felt that the Fox regional networks' combination with ESPN would make Disney too dominant in the cable sports market.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cnbc_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnbc-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In mid-2018, <a href="/wiki/NBCUniversal" title="NBCUniversal">NBCUniversal</a>'s parent company <a href="/wiki/Comcast" title="Comcast">Comcast</a> instigated bidding wars over both the Fox assets Disney planned to purchase, and the British broadcaster <a href="/wiki/Sky_(company)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sky (company)">Sky plc</a> (a company which 21st Century Fox held a stake in, and was planning to acquire the remainder). In July 2018, Fox agreed to an increase of Disney's offer to $71.3 billion to fend off Comcast's counter-bid.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> British regulators ordered that a blind auction be held for Sky's assets, which was won by Comcast.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>On October 10, 2018, it was reported that in preparation for the impending completion of the sale, the new, post-merger organizational structure of "New Fox" would be implemented by January 1, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-New_Fox_Date_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-New_Fox_Date-4">[4]</a></sup> On November 14, 2018, it was revealed that the new independent company will maintain the original Fox name.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On January 7, 2019, Fox Corporation's <a href="/wiki/Registration_statement" title="Registration statement">registration statement</a> was filed by the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission">U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>On January 11, 2019, Fox stated in a securities filing that it had no plans to bid on its former regional sports networks;<sup id="cite_ref-FSN_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FSN-6">[6]</a></sup> they would instead go to a consortium led by <a href="/wiki/Sinclair_Broadcast_Group" title="Sinclair Broadcast Group">Sinclair Broadcast Group</a>, with Fox Corporation continuing to license the FSN name to those stations until Sinclair can develop a new brand.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On March 12, 2019, Disney announced that the sale would be completed by March 20, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-spinoffdate-variety_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-spinoffdate-variety-21">[21]</a></sup> On March 19, 2019, Fox Corporation officially began trading on the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a>, replacing 21st Century Fox on the index.<sup id="cite_ref-Chang_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chang-8">[8]</a></sup> Republican politician and former <a href="/wiki/Speaker_of_the_United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="Speaker of the United States House of Representatives">Speaker of the House</a> <a href="/wiki/Paul_Ryan" title="Paul Ryan">Paul Ryan</a> also joined Fox Corporation's board at this time.<sup id="cite_ref-vty_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vty-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Beginning_of_operations">Beginning of operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fox_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Beginning of operations" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Fox Corporation began operating separately on March 18, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-vty_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vty-2">[2]</a></sup> Chairman and CEO Lachlan Murdoch led a town hall meeting three days later, indicating that stock would be issued to the corporation's employees based on longevity.<sup id="cite_ref-Otterson_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Otterson-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2019, via the Fox Sports division, Fox Corporation acquired a 4.99% stake in Canadian <a href="/wiki/Online_gambling" title="Online gambling">online gambling</a> operator <a href="/wiki/The_Stars_Group" title="The Stars Group">The Stars Group</a> for $236 million. As a result, it was also announced that the companies would co-develop <a href="/wiki/Sports_betting" title="Sports betting">sports betting</a> products for the U.S. market under the branding Fox Bet.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>In early July 2019, Fox Entertainment announced the formation of SideCar, with executives led by <a href="/wiki/Gail_Berman" title="Gail Berman">Gail Berman</a>. SideCar is a content development unit for Fox and other outlets.<sup id="cite_ref-vty1_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vty1-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2019, Fox Corporation announced it would acquire <a href="/wiki/Credible_Labs" title="Credible Labs">Credible Labs</a> for $397 million and animation studio <a href="/wiki/Bento_Box_Entertainment" title="Bento Box Entertainment">Bento Box Entertainment</a> for $50 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Duran_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Duran-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Andreeva_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Andreeva-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>On March 17, 2020, Fox announced they would be acquiring streaming service <a href="/wiki/Tubi" title="Tubi">Tubi</a> for $440 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Baysinger_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Baysinger-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
