</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Schlumberger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:SchlumbergerSolutionsCenterHoustonTX.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/SchlumbergerSolutionsCenterHoustonTX.JPG/220px-SchlumbergerSolutionsCenterHoustonTX.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1704" data-file-height="2272"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 293px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/SchlumbergerSolutionsCenterHoustonTX.JPG/220px-SchlumbergerSolutionsCenterHoustonTX.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="293" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:SchlumbergerSolutionsCenterHoustonTX.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>This <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a> building, as of 2015, houses the Schlumberger U.S. executive offices</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:SchlumbergerComplexSugarLAndTX.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/SchlumbergerComplexSugarLAndTX.JPG/220px-SchlumbergerComplexSugarLAndTX.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="2272" data-file-height="1704"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/SchlumbergerComplexSugarLAndTX.JPG/220px-SchlumbergerComplexSugarLAndTX.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:SchlumbergerComplexSugarLAndTX.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Schlumberger complex in <a href="/wiki/Sugar_Land,_Texas" title="Sugar Land, Texas">Sugar Land, Texas</a></div></div></div>
<p>Schlumberger was founded in 1926 by two brothers <a href="/wiki/Schlumberger_brothers" title="Schlumberger brothers">Conrad and Marcel Schlumberger</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Alsace" title="Alsace">Alsace</a> region in France as the Electric Prospecting Company  (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr">Société de prospection électrique</i>). The company recorded the first-ever <a href="/wiki/Resistivity_logging" title="Resistivity logging">electrical resistivity</a> <a href="/wiki/Well_logging" title="Well logging">well log</a> in <a href="/wiki/Merkwiller-Pechelbronn" title="Merkwiller-Pechelbronn">Merkwiller-Pechelbronn</a>, France in 1927. Today, Schlumberger  supplies the <a href="/wiki/Petroleum_industry" title="Petroleum industry">petroleum industry</a> with services such as seismic acquisition and processing, <a href="/wiki/Formation_evaluation" title="Formation evaluation">formation evaluation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Well_test_(oil_and_gas)" title="Well test (oil and gas)">well testing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Directional_drilling" title="Directional drilling">directional drilling</a>, well cementing and <a href="/wiki/Hydraulic_fracturing" title="Hydraulic fracturing">stimulation</a>, artificial lift, well completions, <a href="/wiki/Flow_assurance" title="Flow assurance">flow assurance</a> and consulting, and software and information management. The company is also involved in the <a href="/wiki/Groundwater" title="Groundwater">groundwater</a> extraction<sup id="cite_ref-Carbon_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carbon-11">[11]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Carbon_capture_and_storage" title="Carbon capture and storage">carbon capture and storage</a> industries.<sup id="cite_ref-Water_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Water-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>The Schlumberger brothers had experience conducting geophysical surveys in countries such as <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The new company sold electrical-measurement mapping services, and recorded the first-ever <a href="/wiki/Resistivity_logging" title="Resistivity logging">electrical resistivity</a> <a href="/wiki/Well_logging" title="Well logging">well log</a> in <a href="/wiki/Merkwiller-Pechelbronn" title="Merkwiller-Pechelbronn">Merkwiller-Pechelbronn</a>, France in 1927. The company quickly expanded, logging its first well in the U.S. in 1929, in <a href="/wiki/Kern_County" class="mw-redirect" title="Kern County">Kern County</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. In 1935, the Schlumberger Well Surveying Corporation was founded in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>, later evolving into Schlumberger Well Services, and finally Schlumberger Wireline &amp; Testing. Schlumberger invested heavily in research, inaugurating the Schlumberger-Doll Research Center in <a href="/wiki/Ridgefield,_Connecticut" title="Ridgefield, Connecticut">Ridgefield, Connecticut</a> in 1948, contributing to the development of a number of new logging tools. In 1956, Schlumberger Limited was incorporated as a <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a> for all Schlumberger businesses, which by now included American testing and production company Johnston Testers.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>Over the years, Schlumberger continued to expand its operations and acquisitions. In 1960, Dowell Schlumberger (50% Schlumberger, 50% <a href="/wiki/Dow_Chemical" class="mw-redirect" title="Dow Chemical">Dow Chemical</a>), which specialized in pumping services for the oil industry, was formed. In 1962, Schlumberger Limited became listed on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Listings_Directory_Schlumberger_Limited_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Listings_Directory_Schlumberger_Limited-13">[13]</a></sup> That same year, Schlumberger purchased Daystrom, an electronic instruments manufacturer in <a href="/wiki/South_Boston,_Virginia" title="South Boston, Virginia">South Boston, Virginia</a> which was making furniture by the time the division was sold to <a href="/wiki/S%26H_Green_Stamps" title="S&amp;H Green Stamps">Sperry &amp; Hutchinson</a> in 1971.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Schlumberger purchased 50% of Forex in 1964 and merged it with 50% of Languedocienne to create the Neptune Drilling Company. The first computerized reservoir analysis, SARABAND, was introduced in 1970. The remaining 50% of Forex was acquired the following year; Neptune was renamed Forex Neptune Drilling Company. In 1979, <a href="/wiki/Fairchild_Camera_and_Instrument" title="Fairchild Camera and Instrument">Fairchild Camera and Instrument</a> (including <a href="/wiki/Fairchild_Semiconductor" title="Fairchild Semiconductor">Fairchild Semiconductor</a>) became a subsidiary of Schlumberger Limited.
</p><p>In 1981, Schlumberger established the first international data links with e-mail. In 1983, Schlumberger opened their Cambridge Research Center in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Cambridgeshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Cambridge, Cambridgeshire">Cambridge, England</a> and in 2012 it was renamed the Schlumberger Gould Research Center after the company's former CEO <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Gould" title="Andrew Gould">Andrew Gould</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>The SEDCO drilling rig company and half of Dowell of North America were acquired in 1984, resulting in the creation of the Anadrill drilling segment, a combination of Dowell and The Analysts' drilling segments. Forex Neptune was merged with SEDCO to create the <a href="/wiki/Transocean" title="Transocean">Sedco Forex Drilling Company</a> the following year, when Schlumberger purchased Merlin and 50% of <a href="/wiki/Geophysical_Company_of_Norway" class="mw-redirect" title="Geophysical Company of Norway">GECO</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In 1987, Schlumberger completed their purchases of Neptune (North America), Bosco and Cori (Italy), and Allmess (Germany). That same year, <a href="/wiki/National_Semiconductor" title="National Semiconductor">National Semiconductor</a> acquired Fairchild Semiconductor from Schlumberger for <a href="/wiki/USD" class="mw-redirect" title="USD">$</a>122 million.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In 1991, Schlumberger acquired PRAKLA-SEISMOS, and pioneered the use of <a href="/wiki/Geosteering" title="Geosteering">geosteering</a> to plan the drill path in horizontal wells.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In 1992, Schlumberger acquired software company <a href="/wiki/GeoQuest_Systems" title="GeoQuest Systems">GeoQuest Systems</a>. With the purchase came the conversion of SINet to <a href="/wiki/TCP/IP" class="mw-redirect" title="TCP/IP">TCP/IP</a> and thus internet capable. In the 1990s Schlumberger bought out the petroleum division, AEG meter, and ECLIPSE reservoir study team Intera Technologies Corp. A joint venture between Schlumberger and <a href="/wiki/Cable_%26_Wireless_plc" title="Cable &amp; Wireless plc">Cable &amp; Wireless</a> resulted with the creation of Omnes, which then handled all of Schlumberger's internal IT business. Oilphase and Camco International were also purchased.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In 1999, Schlumberger and <a href="/wiki/Smith_International" title="Smith International">Smith International</a> created a joint venture, M-I L.L.C., the world's largest drilling fluids (mud) company. The company consists of 60% Smith International, and 40% Schlumberger. Since the joint venture was prohibited by a 1994 antitrust consent decree barring Smith from selling or combining their fluids business with certain other companies, including Schlumberger, the U.S. District Court in Washington, D.C. found Smith International Inc. and Schlumberger Ltd. guilty of criminal contempt and fined each company $750,000 and placed each company on five years probation. Both companies also agreed to pay a total of $13.1 million, representing a full disgorgement of all of the joint venture's profits during the time the companies were in contempt.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In 2000, the <a href="/wiki/Geco-Prakla" class="mw-redirect" title="Geco-Prakla">Geco-Prakla</a> division was merged with <a href="/wiki/Western_Geophysical" title="Western Geophysical">Western Geophysical</a> to create the seismic contracting company <a href="/wiki/WesternGeco" title="WesternGeco">WesternGeco</a>, of which Schlumberger held a 70% stake, the remaining 30% belonging to competitor <a href="/wiki/Baker_Hughes" title="Baker Hughes">Baker Hughes</a>. Sedco Forex was spun off, and merged with Transocean Drilling company in 2000.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In 2001, Schlumberger acquired the IT consultancy company <a href="/wiki/Sema_Group" title="Sema Group">Sema plc</a> for $5.2 billion. The company was an Athens 2004 Summer Olympics partner, but Schlumberger's venture into IT consultancy did not pay off, and divestiture of Sema to <a href="/wiki/Atos_Origin" class="mw-redirect" title="Atos Origin">Atos Origin</a> was completed that year for $1.5 billion. The cards division was divested through an IPO to form <a href="/wiki/Axalto" title="Axalto">Axalto</a>, which later merged with <a href="/wiki/Gemplus_International" class="mw-redirect" title="Gemplus International">Gemplus</a> to form <a href="/wiki/Gemalto" title="Gemalto">Gemalto</a>, and the Messaging Solutions unit was spun off and merged with Taral Networks to form <a href="/wiki/Airwide_Solutions" title="Airwide Solutions">Airwide Solutions</a>. In 2003, the Automated Test Equipment group, part of the 1979 <a href="/wiki/Fairchild_Semiconductor" title="Fairchild Semiconductor">Fairchild Semiconductor</a> acquisition, was spun off to NPTest Holding, which later sold it to <a href="/wiki/Credence_Systems_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Credence Systems Corporation">Credence</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In 2004, Schlumberger Business Consulting was launched and a decade later was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Accenture" title="Accenture">Accenture</a>. <sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In 2005, Schlumberger purchased <a href="/wiki/Waterloo_Hydrogeologic" title="Waterloo Hydrogeologic">Waterloo Hydrogeologic</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reliable_sources" title="Wikipedia:Reliable sources"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on an unreliable source. (January 2011)">unreliable source?</span></a></i>]</sup> which was followed by several other <a href="/wiki/Groundwater" title="Groundwater">groundwater</a> industry related companies, such as Westbay Instruments, and Van Essen Instruments. Also that year, Schlumberger relocated its U.S. corporate offices from New York to Houston.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In 2006, Schlumberger purchased the remaining 30% of WesternGeco from <a href="/wiki/Baker_Hughes" title="Baker Hughes">Baker Hughes</a> for US$2.4 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Also that year, the Schlumberger-Doll Research Center was relocated to a newly built research facility in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge, Massachusetts</a> to replace the <a href="/wiki/Ridgefield,_Connecticut" title="Ridgefield, Connecticut">Ridgefield, Connecticut</a> research center. The facility joins the other research centers operated by the company in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Cambridgeshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Cambridge, Cambridgeshire">Cambridge, England</a>; <a href="/wiki/Moscow,_Russia" class="mw-redirect" title="Moscow, Russia">Moscow, Russia</a>; <a href="/wiki/Stavanger,_Norway" class="mw-redirect" title="Stavanger, Norway">Stavanger, Norway</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Dhahran,_Saudi_Arabia" class="mw-redirect" title="Dhahran, Saudi Arabia">Dhahran, Saudi Arabia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ResearchCenters_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ResearchCenters-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In 2010, the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Smith_International" title="Smith International">Smith International</a> in an all-stock deal valued at $11.3 billion was announced. The sale price is 45.84-a-share price was 37.5 percent higher than Smith closing price on 18 February 2010.  The deal is the biggest acquisition in Schlumberger history.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The merger was completed on August 27, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>  Also announced in 2010 were Schlumberger plans to acquire Geoservices, a French-based company specializing in energy services, in a deal valued at $1.1 billion, including debt.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>In 2014, Schlumberger announced the purchase of the remaining shares of SES Holdings Limited (“Saxon”), a Calgary-based provider of international land drilling services, from First Reserve and certain members of Saxon management. The transaction is subject to customary closing conditions, including the receipt of regulatory approvals. Schlumberger had a minority share in Saxon previously.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, Schlumberger was indicted by the US Department of Justice for sanction violations of conducting business in Iran and Sudan; the company was fined $233 million, amounting to the largest fine for sanctions to date.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, due to a downturn in the global oil and gas industry, Schlumberger announced 21,000 layoffs accounting for 15% of the company's total workforce.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2015, Schlumberger agreed to acquire oilfield equipment manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Cameron_International" title="Cameron International">Cameron International</a> for $14.8 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2018, Schlumberger announced that WesternGeco would be exiting the seismic acquisition business, both onshore and offshore, while retaining its multiclient data processing and interpretation segments. This decision followed the bankruptcy filings of several competitors in the seismic services sector.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="France">France</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Schlumberger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: France" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The company offices in the <a href="/wiki/La_D%C3%A9fense" title="La Défense">La Défense</a> business district of <a href="/wiki/Puteaux" title="Puteaux">Puteaux</a> and in Paris.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Schlumberger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: United States" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Schlumberger maintains a 33-acre (13 ha) campus at the northeast corner of <a href="/wiki/U.S._Highway_90A" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Highway 90A">U.S. Highway 90A</a> and Gillingham Lane in <a href="/wiki/Sugar_Land,_Texas" title="Sugar Land, Texas">Sugar Land, Texas</a>; as of 2017 Schlumberger is the third largest employer in the city. In 2015 Schlumberger announced that it was moving its U.S. corporate headquarters to the Sugar Land facility from its Houston office building. The company plans to build new buildings with a scheduled completion time of late 2017. They include a total of 250,000 square feet (23,000 m<sup>2</sup>) of Class A office space and an "amenities" building with 100,000 square feet (9,300 m<sup>2</sup>) of space.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p><p>In the 1930s the North American headquarters were established in Houston. In the 1970s, the company's top executives in North America were relocated to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. By 2006, the head office had 50 executives and support staff. By that year the company was moving that office to a building near the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Galleria" class="mw-redirect" title="Houston Galleria">Houston Galleria</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Clantongrowth_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clantongrowth-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>Schlumberger moved its Houston-area offices from 5000 <a href="/wiki/Interstate_45" title="Interstate 45">Gulf Freeway</a> in Houston to the Sugar Land campus in 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Schlumhq_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Schlumhq-36">[36]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_record">Environmental record</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Schlumberger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Environmental record" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>In 2009, <i><a href="/wiki/Newsweek" title="Newsweek">Newsweek</a></i> released their "Green Rankings" <sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup>  a ranking of the 500 largest corporations on  track records on a number of environmental issues. Schlumberger was ranked 118th out of 500 overall, and 3rd  out of 31  in their industry. <i>Newsweek</i> remarked that to mitigate global warming Schlumberger has invested in carbon sequestration which involves long-term storage of CO2 and that the company's seismic survey ships are 20% to 25% more fuel-efficient than those of other seismic contractors  from using fuels that emit less pollution and towing equipment that creates less drag on the vessels.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Radioactive_sources">Radioactive sources</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Schlumberger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Radioactive sources" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2006, a radioactive canister imported by Schlumberger was recovered in the <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia" title="Western Australia">Western Australian</a> outback desert.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup>  The canister had been lost 
by the company's transport partner, when the improperly secured container fell off the trailer on which it was being transported.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p><p>In 2010, the <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen" title="Aberdeen">Aberdeen</a> Sheriff court fined Schlumberger Oilfield UK £300,000 for losing a radioactive source on the rig floor on the Ensco 101 mobile drilling rig in the North Sea for 4 hours.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Spills">Spills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Schlumberger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Spills" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, the Pennsylvania Department of Environmental Protection fined Chesapeake Appalachia LLC and Schlumberger Technology Corp. more than $15,500 each for a hydrochloric acid spill in February 2009 at Chesapeake's Chancellor natural gas well site in Asylum Township, <a href="/wiki/Bradford_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Bradford County, Pennsylvania">Bradford County, Pennsylvania</a>. Officials said the leak did not contaminate groundwater.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Brownfields">Brownfields</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Schlumberger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Brownfields" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2006, as the current owner of a facility in <a href="/wiki/Pickens,_South_Carolina" title="Pickens, South Carolina">Pickens, South Carolina</a>, Schlumberger agreed to pay $11.8 million to federal and state agencies for a problem caused by the previous owner, Sangamo-Weston, a capacitor manufacturing plant. The cause of the problem was from <a href="/wiki/Polychlorinated_biphenyls" class="mw-redirect" title="Polychlorinated biphenyls">polychlorinated biphenyls</a> (PCB) released into the environment by Sangamo-Weston from 1955 to 1987.  According to the Justice Department's Environment and Natural Resources Division, an additional agreement by Schlumberger to purchase and remove dams will directly improve the Twelvemile Creek, South Carolina <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystem</a> and provide significant environmental benefits for the affected communities.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Deepwater_Horizon">Deepwater Horizon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Schlumberger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Deepwater Horizon" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Schlumberger was contracted to perform <a href="/wiki/Well_logging" title="Well logging">wireline logging</a> on the <i><a href="/wiki/Deepwater_Horizon" title="Deepwater Horizon">Deepwater Horizon</a></i> oil rig in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> in 2010. However, the wireline log was cancelled and the Schlumberger standby crew was released by <a href="/wiki/BP" title="BP">BP</a> and left the rig earlier on the same day of the <a href="/wiki/Deepwater_Horizon_explosion" title="Deepwater Horizon explosion">Deepwater Horizon explosion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
