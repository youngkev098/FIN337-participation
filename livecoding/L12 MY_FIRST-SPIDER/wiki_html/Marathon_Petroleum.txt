<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Marathon_Petroleum_Co._HQ.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Marathon_Petroleum_Co._HQ.JPG/220px-Marathon_Petroleum_Co._HQ.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="2160" data-file-height="1440"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 147px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Marathon_Petroleum_Co._HQ.JPG/220px-Marathon_Petroleum_Co._HQ.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="147" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Marathon_Petroleum_Co._HQ.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Marathon Petroleum Headquarters in Findlay, Ohio</div></div></div>
<p>Marathon Petroleum Corporation was formed on November 9, 1998, as a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Marathon_Oil" title="Marathon Oil">Marathon Oil</a>.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_parent_company">Former parent company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marathon_Petroleum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Former parent company" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Marathon_Oil" title="Marathon Oil">Marathon Oil</a>, the company's former parent, dates back to 1887 when several small oil companies in Ohio banded together to form The Ohio Oil Company.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 1889, it was purchased by John D. Rockefeller's Standard Oil. It remained a part of <a href="/wiki/Standard_Oil" title="Standard Oil">Standard Oil</a> until the Standard Oil Trust was broken in 1911. In 1930, The Ohio Oil Company bought the Transcontinental Oil Company and established the "Marathon" brand name. In 1962, the company changed its name to "Marathon Oil Company". From 1982 until 2002, Marathon Oil was a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/U.S._Steel" title="U.S. Steel">U.S. Steel</a>.
</p><p>In 2011, Marathon Oil announced the spin-off of its downstream refining and marketing assets to a separate company called Marathon Petroleum Corporation.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Predecessor_company">Predecessor company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marathon_Petroleum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Predecessor company" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The predecessor company of Marathon Petroleum Corporation, Marathon Petroleum Company LLC, formerly known as Marathon Ashland Petroleum LLC, was formed by the merger of the refining operations of <a href="/wiki/Marathon_Oil" title="Marathon Oil">Marathon Oil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ashland_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Ashland Inc.">Ashland Inc.</a> in 1998. In 2005, the company became a 100% owned subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Marathon_Oil" title="Marathon Oil">Marathon Oil</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-history_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 2006, Marathon began using <a href="/wiki/STP_(motor_oil_company)" title="STP (motor oil company)">STP</a>-branded additives in its gasoline.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 2009, the company completed a $3.9 billion expansion of its refinery in <a href="/wiki/Garyville,_Louisiana" title="Garyville, Louisiana">Garyville, Louisiana</a>, that increased the plant’s capacity by 180,000 barrels per day.<sup id="cite_ref-history_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 2010, the company sold its 74,000 barrel-per-day refinery in <a href="/wiki/St._Paul_Park,_Minnesota" title="St. Paul Park, Minnesota">St. Paul Park, Minnesota</a>, along with associated terminals, pipelines, and inventory as well as 166 <a href="/wiki/SuperAmerica" title="SuperAmerica">SuperAmerica</a> convenience stores to Northern Tier Energy for $900 million.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-corporate_spin-off_from_Marathon_Oil">Post-corporate spin-off from Marathon Oil</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marathon_Petroleum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-corporate spin-off from Marathon Oil" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Marathon_Petroleum_Complex.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Marathon_Petroleum_Complex.jpg/220px-Marathon_Petroleum_Complex.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="4032" data-file-height="3024"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Marathon_Petroleum_Complex.jpg/220px-Marathon_Petroleum_Complex.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Marathon_Petroleum_Complex.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Marathon Petroleum campus.</div></div></div>
<p>On June 30, 2011, Marathon Oil distributed all of its shares in the company to its shareholders via a <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">corporate spin-off</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2012, <a href="/wiki/Wheeling,_West_Virginia" title="Wheeling, West Virginia">Wheeling, West Virginia</a>-based Tri-State Petroleum signed a contract to switch 50 stations in <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, and <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia" title="West Virginia">West Virginia</a> to the Marathon brand. Most of Tri-State's stations before the deal were <a href="/wiki/ExxonMobil" title="ExxonMobil">ExxonMobil</a>-branded stations, the majority <a href="/wiki/Exxon" title="Exxon">Exxon</a> as well as a few scattered <a href="/wiki/Mobil" title="Mobil">Mobil</a> stations in the immediate Wheeling area. Included in the deal were 18 Exxon stations in the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_metropolitan_area" class="mw-redirect" title="Pittsburgh metropolitan area">Pittsburgh metropolitan area</a>, significantly boosting Marathon's presence in the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a> market, where former parent company <a href="/wiki/U.S._Steel" title="U.S. Steel">U.S. Steel</a> is based. (Exxon would offset its Pittsburgh losses by taking over the retail contracts of several <a href="/wiki/Shell_Oil_Company" title="Shell Oil Company">Shell</a> stations in the area, leaving Shell with a significantly reduced presence, while the Mobil brand was withdrawn from the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Panhandle_of_West_Virginia" title="Northern Panhandle of West Virginia">Northern Panhandle of West Virginia</a> altogether.) Before the deal, Marathon had a much smaller presence in <a href="/wiki/Western_Pennsylvania" title="Western Pennsylvania">Western Pennsylvania</a>, while having a somewhat larger presence in West Virginia and an almost <a href="/wiki/Omnipresence" title="Omnipresence">ubiquitous</a> presence in <a href="/wiki/Appalachian_Ohio" title="Appalachian Ohio">Southern Ohio</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 2013, Marathon purchased numerous assets from <a href="/wiki/BP" title="BP">BP</a> including a 451,000 barrel per day refinery in Texas City, Texas, four light product distribution terminals, and retail marketing contracts for 1,200 retail stations throughout the southeastern United States.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In 2014, <a href="/wiki/Speedway_LLC" title="Speedway LLC">Speedway LLC</a>, a subsidiary of the company, purchased the retail operations of <a href="/wiki/Hess_Corporation" title="Hess Corporation">Hess Corporation</a> for $2.82 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Refinery_fire">Refinery fire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marathon_Petroleum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Refinery fire" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2016, a fire at the Galveston Bay refinery in <a href="/wiki/Texas_City,_Texas" title="Texas City, Texas">Texas City, Texas</a>, injured three contract workers, resulting in a lawsuit seeking $1 million in damages.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Multiple lawsuits were filed resulting in Marathon paying $86 million to settle.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="2018_acquisition_of_Andeavor.2C_planned_spin-off_of_Speedway_LLC"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018_acquisition_of_Andeavor,_planned_spin-off_of_Speedway_LLC">2018 acquisition of Andeavor, planned spin-off of Speedway LLC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marathon_Petroleum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2018 acquisition of Andeavor, planned spin-off of Speedway LLC" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On April 30, 2018, Marathon agreed to buy <a href="/wiki/Andeavor" title="Andeavor">Andeavor</a>, an independent refinery and oil company based in the Western United States, for $23 billion. Marathon will acquire all of Andeavor's outstanding shares.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On October 1, 2018, the merger was completed. This merger brings the SuperAmerica convenience stores back to Speedway.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On October 31, 2019, Marathon announced plans to spin off their Speedway convenience stores. Gary Heminger will also retire from his role as  Marathon chairman and CEO. <sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
