<p><b>S&amp;P Global Inc.</b> (prior to April 2016 <b>McGraw Hill Financial, Inc.</b>, and prior to 2013 <b>McGraw-Hill Companies</b>) is an American <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">publicly traded corporation</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. Its primary areas of business are financial information and analytics. It is the <a href="/wiki/Parent_company" title="Parent company">parent company</a> of S&amp;P Global Ratings, S&amp;P Global Market Intelligence, and <a href="/wiki/S%26P_Global_Platts" title="S&amp;P Global Platts">S&amp;P Global Platts</a>, and is the majority owner of the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_Dow_Jones_Indices" title="S&amp;P Dow Jones Indices">S&amp;P Dow Jones Indices</a> joint venture.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_history">Corporate history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%26P_Global&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Corporate history" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The predecessor companies of S&amp;P Global have history dating to 1888, when <a href="/wiki/James_H._McGraw" title="James H. McGraw">James H. McGraw</a> purchased the <i>American Journal of Railway Appliances</i>. He continued to add further publications, eventually establishing The <i>McGraw Publishing Company</i> in 1899. <a href="/wiki/John_A._Hill" title="John A. Hill">John A. Hill</a> had also produced several technical and trade publications and in 1902 formed his own business, The <i>Hill Publishing Company</i>. In 1909 both men, having known each other's interests, agreed upon an alliance and combined the book departments of their publishing companies into The <i>McGraw-Hill Book Company</i>. John Hill served as President, with James McGraw as Vice-President. In 1917, the remaining parts of each business were merged into The <i>McGraw-Hill Publishing Company</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-mcgraw-history0_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mcgraw-history0-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1986, McGraw-Hill bought out competitor The Economy Company, then the United States' largest publisher of educational material. The buyout made McGraw-Hill the largest educational publisher in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-TJR_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TJR-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 1964, after Hill died, both <i>McGraw-Hill Publishing Company</i> and <i>McGraw-Hill Book Company</i> merged into <i>McGraw-Hill, Inc.</i>
</p><p>In 1966, McGraw-Hill purchased <a href="/wiki/Credit_rating_agency" title="Credit rating agency">credit rating agency</a> <a href="/wiki/Standard_%26_Poor%27s" title="Standard &amp; Poor's">Standard &amp; Poor's</a> from Paul Talbot Babson.
</p><p>In 1979, McGraw-Hill purchased <a href="/wiki/Byte_(magazine)" title="Byte (magazine)"><i>Byte</i> magazine</a> from its owner/publisher <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Williamson" title="Virginia Williamson">Virginia Williamson</a> who then became a vice-president of McGraw-Hill.
</p><p>In 1995, McGraw-Hill, Inc. became The <i>McGraw-Hill Companies, Inc.</i> as part of a corporate <a href="/wiki/Rebranding" title="Rebranding">rebranding</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mcgraw-history3_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mcgraw-history3-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (December 2011)">failed verification</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In 2007, McGraw-Hill launched an online study network, <a href="/wiki/GradeGuru" class="mw-redirect" title="GradeGuru">GradeGuru</a>.com, which gave McGraw-Hill an opportunity to connect directly with its end users, the students. The site closed on April 29, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:133px;"><a href="/wiki/File:McGraw-Hill_Broadcasting.png" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/c/c7/McGraw-Hill_Broadcasting.png" decoding="async" width="131" height="43" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="131" data-file-height="43"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 131px;height: 43px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/c/c7/McGraw-Hill_Broadcasting.png" data-alt="" data-width="131" data-height="43" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:McGraw-Hill_Broadcasting.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Former logo of McGraw-Hill Broadcasting</div></div></div>
<p>On October 3, 2011, McGraw-Hill announced it was selling its entire television station group to the <a href="/wiki/E._W._Scripps_Company" title="E. W. Scripps Company">E. W. Scripps Company</a> for $212 million.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The sale was completed on December 30, 2011. It had been involved in broadcasting since 1972, when it purchased four television stations from a division of <a href="/wiki/Time_Inc." title="Time Inc.">Time Inc.</a>.  The sale included McGraw-Hill Broadcasting's stations <a href="/wiki/KERO-TV" title="KERO-TV">KERO-TV</a> and <a href="/wiki/KZKC-LP" title="KZKC-LP">KZKC-LP</a> <a href="/wiki/Bakersfield,_California" title="Bakersfield, California">Bakersfield</a>; <a href="/wiki/KGTV" title="KGTV">KGTV</a> and <a href="/wiki/KZSD-LP" class="mw-redirect" title="KZSD-LP">KZSD-LP</a> <a href="/wiki/San_Diego,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Diego, California">San Diego</a>; <a href="/wiki/KZCS-LP" class="mw-redirect" title="KZCS-LP">KZCS-LP</a> <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Springs,_Colorado" title="Colorado Springs, Colorado">Colorado Springs</a>; flagship station <a href="/wiki/KMGH-TV" title="KMGH-TV">KMGH-TV</a> and <a href="/wiki/KZCO-LD" title="KZCO-LD">KZCO-LD</a> <a href="/wiki/Denver,_Colorado" class="mw-redirect" title="Denver, Colorado">Denver</a>; <a href="/wiki/KZFC-LP" class="mw-redirect" title="KZFC-LP">KZFC-LP</a> <a href="/wiki/Fort_Collins,_Colorado" title="Fort Collins, Colorado">Fort Collins</a>; and <a href="/wiki/WRTV" title="WRTV">WRTV</a> <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis" title="Indianapolis">Indianapolis</a>.
</p><p>McGraw Hill has produced the "Glencoe" series of books for decades.
</p><p>On November 26, 2012, McGraw-Hill announced it was selling its entire education division, known as <i><a href="/wiki/McGraw-Hill_Education" title="McGraw-Hill Education">McGraw-Hill Education</a></i> to <i><a href="/wiki/Apollo_Global_Management" title="Apollo Global Management">Apollo Global Management</a></i> for $2.5 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On March 22, 2013, it announced it had completed the sale for $2.4 billion cash.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>On May 1, 2013, shareholders of McGraw-Hill voted to change the company's name to <i>McGraw Hill Financial</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-investor.mhfi.com_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-investor.mhfi.com-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>McGraw-Hill divested the subsidiary McGraw-Hill Construction to <a href="/wiki/Symphony_Technology_Group" title="Symphony Technology Group">Symphony Technology Group</a> for US$320 million on September 22, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The sale included <a href="/wiki/Engineering_News-Record" title="Engineering News-Record">Engineering News-Record</a>, <a href="/wiki/Architectural_Record" title="Architectural Record">Architectural Record</a>, Dodge and Sweet's.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> McGraw-Hill Construction has been renamed Dodge Data &amp; Analytics.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2016, it was announced that McGraw-Hill Financial would change its name to <i>S&amp;P Global Inc.</i> by the end of April 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The company officially changed its name following a shareholder vote on April 27, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2016, the company announced it was selling <a href="/wiki/J.D._Power_and_Associates" class="mw-redirect" title="J.D. Power and Associates">J.D. Power and Associates</a> to investment firm XIO Group for $1.1 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_organization">Corporate organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%26P_Global&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Corporate organization" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>S&amp;P Global now organizes its businesses in four units based on the market in which they are involved.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="S.26P_Global_Ratings"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="S&amp;P_Global_Ratings">S&amp;P Global Ratings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%26P_Global&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: S&amp;P Global Ratings" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>S&amp;P Global Ratings provides independent investment research including ratings on various investment instruments.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="S.26P_Global_Market_Intelligence"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="S&amp;P_Global_Market_Intelligence">S&amp;P Global Market Intelligence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%26P_Global&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: S&amp;P Global Market Intelligence" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>S&amp;P Global Market Intelligence is a provider of multi-asset class and real-time data, research, news and analytics to <a href="/wiki/Institutional_investors" class="mw-redirect" title="Institutional investors">institutional investors</a>, investment and commercial banks, investment advisors and wealth managers, corporations, and universities. Subsidiaries include <a href="/wiki/Leveraged_Commentary_%26_Data" title="Leveraged Commentary &amp; Data">Leveraged Commentary &amp; Data</a>.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="S.26P_Dow_Jones_Indices"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="S&amp;P_Dow_Jones_Indices">S&amp;P Dow Jones Indices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%26P_Global&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: S&amp;P Dow Jones Indices" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Launched on July 2, 2012, <a href="/wiki/S%26P_Dow_Jones_Indices" title="S&amp;P Dow Jones Indices">S&amp;P Dow Jones Indices</a> is the world's largest global resource for index-based concepts, data, and research. It produces the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Dow_Jones_Industrial_Average" title="Dow Jones Industrial Average">Dow Jones Industrial Average</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>S&amp;P Dow Jones Indices calculates over 830,000 indices, publishes benchmarks that provide the basis for 575 ETFs globally with $387 billion in assets invested, and serves as the DNA for $1.5 trillion of the world's indexed assets.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="S.26P_Global_Platts"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="S&amp;P_Global_Platts">S&amp;P Global Platts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%26P_Global&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: S&amp;P Global Platts" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Headquartered in London, <a href="/wiki/Platts" class="mw-redirect" title="Platts">S&amp;P Global Platts</a> is a provider of information and a source of benchmark price assessments for the commodities, energy, <a href="/wiki/Petrochemicals" class="mw-redirect" title="Petrochemicals">petrochemicals</a>, metals, and agriculture markets. It has offices in more than 15 cities, including major energy centres such as <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">Dubai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>, and international business centres such as <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, and <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.
</p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(7)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="McGraw-Hill_Building">McGraw-Hill Building</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%26P_Global&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: McGraw-Hill Building" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-7 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-7">

The predecessor company McGraw Hill Inc. had been based at <a href="/wiki/330_West_42nd_Street" title="330 West 42nd Street">330 West 42nd Street</a> and both have been known as <i>The <a href="/wiki/McGraw-Hill_Building_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="McGraw-Hill Building (disambiguation)">McGraw Hill Building</a></i>,
a name originally used for a prior headquarters at 469 Tenth Avenue.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(8)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Connection_to_the_family_of_George_W._Bush">Connection to the family of George W. Bush</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%26P_Global&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Connection to the family of George W. Bush" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-8 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-8">
<p>The McGraws and the <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> family have close ties dating back several generations. Harold McGraw Jr. (deceased) was a member of the national grant advisory and founding board of the Barbara Bush Foundation for Family Literacy.<sup id="cite_ref-Bushes_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bushes-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(9)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="McGraw-Hill_Federal_Credit_Union">McGraw-Hill Federal Credit Union</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=S%26P_Global&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: McGraw-Hill Federal Credit Union" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-9 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-9">
<p>Established in 1935, the McGraw-Hill Federal Credit Union originally served employees of the McGraw-Hill companies in New York City only.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> The credit union moved from its location inside the McGraw-Hill building to <a href="/wiki/East_Windsor,_New_Jersey" class="mw-redirect" title="East Windsor, New Jersey">East Windsor</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>, in 2005. Its accounts are insured by the <a href="/wiki/National_Credit_Union_Administration" title="National Credit Union Administration">National Credit Union Administration</a>. It provides savings, checking accounts, <a href="/wiki/Certificate_of_deposit" title="Certificate of deposit">CDs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Money_market_account" title="Money market account">money-market accounts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Individual_retirement_account" title="Individual retirement account">IRAs</a>, credit cards, auto loans, and home mortgages. In February 2019, the credit union announced plans to merge with <a href="/wiki/Pentagon_Federal_Credit_Union" title="Pentagon Federal Credit Union">Pentagon Federal Credit Union</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> completing to convert customers to those of PenFed on May 1, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> The East Windsor branch currently operates as a PenFed branch as of September 2019.
</p>

<p>In 1999, the <a href="/wiki/National_Building_Museum" title="National Building Museum">National Building Museum</a> presented the McGraw-Hill Companies with its annual <a href="/wiki/Honor_Award" title="Honor Award">Honor Award</a> for the corporation's contributions to the <a href="/wiki/Built_environment" title="Built environment">built environment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
