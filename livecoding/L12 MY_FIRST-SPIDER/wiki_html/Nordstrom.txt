<p><b>Nordstrom, Inc.</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'n' in 'nigh'">n</span><span title="/ɔːr/: 'ar' in 'war'">ɔːr</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span></span>/</a></span></span>) is an American <a href="/wiki/Luxury_goods" title="Luxury goods">luxury</a> <a href="/wiki/Department_store" title="Department store">department store</a> chain founded in 1901 by <a href="/wiki/John_W._Nordstrom" title="John W. Nordstrom">John W. Nordstrom</a> and Carl F. Wallin. It originated as a shoe store, and evolved into a full-line retailer with departments for clothing, footwear, handbags, jewelry, accessories, cosmetics, and fragrances. Some stores feature home furnishings and wedding departments, and several have in-house cafes, restaurants, and espresso bars.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nordstrom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nordstrom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early history" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>John W. Nordstrom was born on February 15, 1871, in the town of <a href="/wiki/Lule%C3%A5" title="Luleå">Luleå</a> in Northern Sweden.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In 1887, Nordstrom <a href="/wiki/Immigration" title="Immigration">immigrated</a> to the United States at the age of 16.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4">[4]</a></sup> His name at birth was Johan Nordström, which he later anglicized to John Nordstrom.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> After landing in New York, he began working in Michigan and was able to save enough money to purchase a 20-acre (81,000 m<sup>2</sup>) potato farm in <a href="/wiki/Arlington,_Washington" title="Arlington, Washington">Arlington, Washington</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In 1897, he joined the <a href="/wiki/Klondike_Gold_Rush" title="Klondike Gold Rush">Klondike Gold Rush</a> in Canada's <a href="/wiki/Yukon_Territory" class="mw-redirect" title="Yukon Territory">Yukon Territory</a>. After two years of prospecting, he finally struck gold, but sold his disputed claim for $13,000.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> Returning to Seattle with his newfound wealth, he married Hilda Carlson in 1900 and searched for a business venture.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> He eventually settled on a shoe store that opened in 1901, called Wallin &amp; Nordstrom.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4">[4]</a></sup> Carl F. Wallin, the co-founder of the store, was the owner of the adjacent shoe repair shop.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> John and Hilda had five children, three of whom would follow him into the family business, <a href="/wiki/Everett_Nordstrom" title="Everett Nordstrom">Everett W.</a> (1903), <a href="/wiki/Elmer_Nordstrom" title="Elmer Nordstrom">Elmer J.</a> (1904) and Lloyd N. Nordstrom.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 1928, John W. Nordstrom retired and sold his shares to two of his sons, Everett and Elmer. In 1929, Wallin also retired and sold his shares to them. The 1930 grand opening of the remodeled Second Avenue store marked the change of name to Nordstrom's. Lloyd Nordstrom subsequently joined the company in 1933, and the three brothers ran the business together for almost forty years.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>By 1958, Nordstrom's had expanded to eight stores in two states but still sold only shoes. Their expansion was based on customer service, deep product offerings and full size ranges. Apparel came with its purchase of Best Apparel of Seattle in 1963, and the company's name was changed to Nordstrom Best.<sup id="cite_ref-Nordstrom_History_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nordstrom_History-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="1970s_and_1980s">1970s and 1980s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nordstrom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1970s and 1980s" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1971, the company was taken public on <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It was moved to the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> in 1999 under the ticker symbol JWN after John W. Nordstrom, its founder.<sup id="cite_ref-Nordstrom_History_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nordstrom_History-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>By 1975, Nordstrom expanded into <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> (the only time by acquisition) by purchasing <a href="/wiki/Alaska_Commercial_Company" title="Alaska Commercial Company">Northern Commercial Company</a> and opened its first Nordstrom Rack clearance store in Seattle. A strong northwest regional retailer with sales already approaching $250 million making it the third-largest specialty retailer in the United States, the company opened its first Southern California store at <a href="/wiki/South_Coast_Plaza" title="South Coast Plaza">South Coast Plaza</a> in <a href="/wiki/Costa_Mesa,_California" title="Costa Mesa, California">Costa Mesa</a> in 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> By the early 1990s, it had opened 26 stores plus Racks in California. Subsequent expansion relied on creating a strongly decentralized regional structure, beginning with the Northeast in the <a href="/wiki/Tysons_Corner_Center" title="Tysons Corner Center">Tysons Corner Center</a> in Virginia (1988), the Midwest  in the <a href="/wiki/Oakbrook_Center" title="Oakbrook Center">Oakbrook Center</a> in Illinois (1991), the Southeast in Atlanta (1998), and the Southwest in Dallas (1996). In a new region, the initial store was used as a base for training and recruitment for subsequent expansion, and was usually backed by its own distribution center. From 1978 to 1995, Nordstrom opened a total of 46 full-line department stores.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 1976, Nordstrom opened a series of stores called Place Two to sell a more limited selection of apparel in smaller markets. By 1983, there were ten Place Two stores, but the cost of upgrading the smaller stores, especially from a systems perspective, outweighed the benefit, and the division was discontinued.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The company also expanded into direct sales in 1993, beginning with a catalog division<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> led by John N.'s son Dan that was followed by an e-commerce business. Nordstrom.com's fulfillment and contact centers are located in <a href="/wiki/Cedar_Rapids,_Iowa" title="Cedar Rapids, Iowa">Cedar Rapids, Iowa</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Seattle,_Washington" class="mw-redirect" title="Seattle, Washington">Seattle, Washington</a>. Currently, it has distribution centers in <a href="/wiki/Ontario,_California" title="Ontario, California">Ontario, California</a>; <a href="/wiki/Newark,_California" title="Newark, California">Newark, California</a>; <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland, Oregon</a>; <a href="/wiki/Dubuque,_Iowa" title="Dubuque, Iowa">Dubuque, Iowa</a>; <a href="/wiki/Upper_Marlboro,_Maryland" title="Upper Marlboro, Maryland">Upper Marlboro, Maryland</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Gainesville,_Florida" title="Gainesville, Florida">Gainesville, Florida</a>.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="1990s_and_2000s">1990s and 2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nordstrom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1990s and 2000s" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Nordstrom FSB, a wholly owned subsidiary of Nordstrom, Inc., is a federally chartered savings bank doing business as Nordstrom Bank. It was formed in 1991 in <a href="/wiki/Scottsdale,_Arizona" title="Scottsdale, Arizona">Scottsdale, Arizona</a>, with its customer contact center in <a href="/wiki/Centennial,_Colorado" title="Centennial, Colorado">Centennial, Colorado</a>. Nordstrom FSB was formerly known as Nordstrom National Credit Bank and changed its name to Nordstrom FSB in March 2000. The bank offers various banking and credit products, such as Nordstrom Signature VISA, Nordstrom retail credit and debit cards, interest-bearing checking accounts, check cards, and certificates of deposits. It offers Nordstrom customers cards under Nordstrom Rewards – its customer loyalty program – where customers earn points when making purchases with the card at Nordstrom and other retailers. Other rewards include Nordstrom Notes which are redeemed or used like cash in stores for new purchases and the Nordstrom Signature VISA card also has an optional travel/leisure rewards feature. The Nordstrom Rewards program features 4 levels of status depending on annual spending and offers various promotional times throughout the year to earn double, triple and even ten-times points.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In 1995, Nordstrom opened their first store location in New York State. It is located at The Westchester, an upscale shopping mall in White Plains, New York, a suburb of New York City. Nordstrom has since opened stores in Garden City, New York, and Manhattan. 
</p><p>Beginning in 1995, the fourth generation of brothers and cousins had been co-presidents for a time. After John Whitacre was appointed as the first non-Nordstrom CEO in 1997,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In 1998, Nordstrom replaced its downtown Seattle store with a new flagship location in the former <a href="/wiki/Frederick_%26_Nelson" title="Frederick &amp; Nelson">Frederick &amp; Nelson</a> building across the street. At 383,000 square feet (35,600 m<sup>2</sup>), the downtown Seattle location is the chain's largest store. By contrast, the smallest Nordstrom store, as of September 2008, opened in 1980 in <a href="/wiki/Salem,_Oregon" title="Salem, Oregon">Salem, Oregon</a>, with a total area of just under 72,000 square feet (6,700 m<sup>2</sup>). This location has been since closed.
</p><p>In 2000 the Nordstrom family reasserted its control, with the sons of Bruce A. (<a href="/wiki/Blake_Nordstrom" title="Blake Nordstrom">Blake</a>, Erik and Peter) assuming senior roles in the company which they continue to hold.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Blake Nordstrom served as sole President of the company from 2000–2014 with Erik and Peter joining him as co-Presidents in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>Nordstrom sold its stand-alone boutique chain <a href="/wiki/Fa%C3%A7onnable" title="Façonnable">Façonnable</a> in 2007, though it still offers that label in its lineup.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s">2010s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nordstrom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010s" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3><p>
In February 2011, Nordstrom announced the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/HauteLook" title="HauteLook">HauteLook</a>, an LA-based online retailer that offers flash sales on designer goods. The deal included Nordstrom paying $180 million in stock and a three-year "earn-out" payment based on <a href="/wiki/HauteLook" title="HauteLook">HauteLook</a>'s financial performance.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> </p><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nordstrom_Rack_store,_Tanasbourne_-_Hillsboro,_Oregon.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Nordstrom_Rack_store%2C_Tanasbourne_-_Hillsboro%2C_Oregon.jpg/220px-Nordstrom_Rack_store%2C_Tanasbourne_-_Hillsboro%2C_Oregon.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="118" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3689" data-file-height="1977"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 118px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Nordstrom_Rack_store%2C_Tanasbourne_-_Hillsboro%2C_Oregon.jpg/220px-Nordstrom_Rack_store%2C_Tanasbourne_-_Hillsboro%2C_Oregon.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="118" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nordstrom_Rack_store,_Tanasbourne_-_Hillsboro,_Oregon.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A Nordstrom Rack store in <a href="/wiki/Hillsboro,_Oregon" title="Hillsboro, Oregon">Hillsboro, Oregon</a></div></div></div><p>On May 26, 2011, Nordstrom announced that they would be closing the downtown <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis,_Indiana" class="mw-redirect" title="Indianapolis, Indiana">Indianapolis, Indiana</a> <a href="/wiki/Circle_Center" class="mw-redirect" title="Circle Center">Circle Center</a> Mall location due to declining business at the site. The remaining location on the north side of the city at the Fashion Mall at Keystone as well as the future Nordstrom Rack store in the Rivers Edge Shopping Center, also on the north side, remained as the only two locations in the state. Erik Nordstrom, president of stores at Nordstrom stated, "We've enjoyed serving our customers in downtown Indianapolis, but unfortunately our business has declined over the long term for some time and despite our efforts to turn things around we don't see the outlook significantly changing."<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2011, Nordstrom opened the store Treasure &amp; Bond in <a href="/wiki/SoHo,_Manhattan" title="SoHo, Manhattan">SoHo</a>, New York. Intended to test the waters of the tumultuous high-end department store scene in New York, all profits of Treasure &amp; Bond are donated to charity. The customer base of the smaller store is thought to be the younger sisters of the typical Nordstrom customer.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>Nordstrom announced plans to open a Nordstrom Rack in <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee" title="Milwaukee">Milwaukee</a> in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In late November 2012, Nordstrom announced that they would also be opening a full-line Nordstrom Department Store in 2015 in Milwaukee at <a href="/wiki/Mayfair_Mall" title="Mayfair Mall">Mayfair Mall</a>, a half-mile away from the Nordstrom Rack location. Milwaukee was the largest metropolitan area in the country that did not have a Nordstrom until 2015. Such unmet demand was highlighted in Nordstrom's decision to open in the Milwaukee area.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>On January 4, 2013, Nordstrom announced plans for a new location in the <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis-Saint_Paul" class="mw-redirect" title="Minneapolis-Saint Paul">Minneapolis-Saint Paul</a> metropolitan region. The store, to be located at the <a href="/wiki/Ridgedale_Center" title="Ridgedale Center">Ridgedale Center</a>, would be the second in the area. Following the success of its <a href="/wiki/Mall_of_America" title="Mall of America">Mall of America</a> opening in 1991, Nordstrom sought to expand in the Twin Cities market.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Plans for a Ridgedale Center store were first released in 2007, only to be canceled in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The location eventually opened in September 2015.
</p><p>In May 2015, Pete and <a href="/wiki/Erik_Nordstrom_(businessman)" class="mw-redirect" title="Erik Nordstrom (businessman)">Erik Nordstrom</a> joined their brother <a href="/wiki/Blake_Nordstrom" title="Blake Nordstrom">Blake Nordstrom</a> as co-presidents of Nordstrom. This arrangement lasted till Blake Nordstrom's death in January 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-SeattleTimes_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SeattleTimes-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>Before this appointment, Pete Nordstrom was executive vice president and president of merchandising while Erik Nordstrom was executive vice president and president of Nordstrom.com. <sup id="cite_ref-SeattleTimes_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SeattleTimes-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>Nordstrom opened its first Rack location in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> in the fall of 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In the fall of 2015, Nordstrom opened their first Nordstrom Rack store in Delaware.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup>  The company is opening its first full-line store in New York City on April 12, 2018, a three-floor store at 57th Street and Broadway, in the Columbus Circle neighborhood.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>In 2016, Nordstrom entered into a partnership with <a href="/wiki/Tesla_Motors" class="mw-redirect" title="Tesla Motors">Tesla Motors</a> under which the automaker opened retail galleries inside the Nordstrom locations in <a href="/wiki/The_Grove_at_Farmers_Market" title="The Grove at Farmers Market">The Grove</a>, Los Angeles, in <a href="/wiki/SouthPark_Mall_(Charlotte,_North_Carolina)" title="SouthPark Mall (Charlotte, North Carolina)">SouthPark Mall</a> in Charlotte, North Carolina and in <a href="/wiki/Somerset_Collection" title="Somerset Collection">Somerset Collection</a> in Troy, Michigan.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>In 2016, Nordstrom began selling products from <a href="/wiki/J.Crew" title="J.Crew">J. Crew</a>, Olivia Palermo + Chelsea28, <a href="/wiki/Adore_Me" title="Adore Me">Adore Me</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Eloquii" class="mw-redirect" title="Eloquii">Eloquii</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> In 2017, Nordstrom announced they would be expanding their natural beauty-focused stations.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p><p>On February 2, 2017, <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_News" title="Bloomberg News">Bloomberg News</a> reported that Nordstrom would cease to carry <a href="/wiki/Ivanka_Trump" title="Ivanka Trump">Ivanka Trump</a>'s branded fashion line, citing weak sales. The retailer cuts 10 percent of its most poorly performing brands each year. Nordstrom's action came after a boycott campaign against retailers carrying products having Trump family labels.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup>  President <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a> responded with a <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">tweet</a> directed at Nordstrom, condemning the brand for its treatment of his daughter. The company's stock dropped by 0.65% in the aftermath of the tweet before ending the day with a gain of more than 4%. <i><a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)">Fortune</a></i> declared this quick recovery to have broken Trump's market-moving "tweet curse".<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2017, Nordstrom launched its first "Nordstrom Local" in West Hollywood, which is set to focus on personal shopping and styling, rather than carrying merchandise. In June 2017, Nordstrom Inc.'s founding family, which owned 31.2 percent of the company, said it was looking to take Nordstrom private.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> In October, the family suspended its efforts, because of trouble completing a financing package.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Lenders were asking for 13 percent interest, about twice the typical rate for retailers.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p><p>On January 2, 2019 <a href="/wiki/Blake_Nordstrom" title="Blake Nordstrom">Blake Nordstrom</a>, co-president of Nordstrom, died suddenly at the age of 58. He was a lifetime employee of his family's company, rising through the ranks until he became a vice-president and then co-president.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2020s">2020s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nordstrom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2020s" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On March 3, 2020, <a href="/wiki/Erik_Nordstrom_(businessman)" class="mw-redirect" title="Erik Nordstrom (businessman)">Erik Nordstrom</a> became the sole CEO of Nordstrom.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p><p>Nordstrom announced in March 2020 that they would temporarily close all locations to help contain the spread of <a href="/wiki/COVID-19" class="mw-redirect" title="COVID-19">COVID-19</a>.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="International_expansion">International expansion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nordstrom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International expansion" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>

Later in 2016, a second Toronto location was opened at <a href="/wiki/Yorkdale_Shopping_Centre" title="Yorkdale Shopping Centre">Yorkdale Shopping Centre</a> on October 21, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> Additionally, a third Toronto store in <a href="/wiki/Sherway_Gardens" title="Sherway Gardens">Sherway Gardens</a> opened on September 15, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> The first Nordstrom Rack opened at Vaughan Mills on March 21, 2018; and then a second location in Deerfoot Meadows in Calgary and a flagship location at One Bloor in Toronto opened on May 3, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> Nordstrom Rack opened three other additional stores in Fall 2018; one at Heartland Town Centre in Mississauga, one at Ottawa Trainyards, and finally one at South Edmonton Commons.
</p><p>Nordstrom opened a 138,000-square-foot, two-level, full-line store March 2015 in <a href="/wiki/The_Mall_of_San_Juan" title="The Mall of San Juan">The Mall of San Juan</a> in San Juan, Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> After <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> in 2017, the store suffered major water damage and it closed temporarily after the hurricane.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <p>Nordstrom holds six annual sales. Two of these are Half-Yearly sales, which take place semi-annually in May and late December, as well as three additional seasonal sales.
</p><p>Nordstrom's largest sale event is the Nordstrom Anniversary Sale, which is held each July.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> Limited amounts of merchandise set to be released for the following fall are sent to Nordstrom stores early, where they are sold at discount. For ten days prior to Anniversary Sale, Nordstrom gives early access to members who make appointments.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 2009, Nordstrom was listed at No. 72 in <i><a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)">Fortune</a></i> magazine's 100 Best Companies to Work For 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup>  Nordstrom is a Hall of Fame member of <i>Fortune</i> magazine's "<a href="/wiki/100_Best_Companies_to_Work_For" title="100 Best Companies to Work For">100 Best Companies to Work For</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup>  Nordstrom was No. 36 on the same list in 2008, No. 24 in 2007, No. 46  in 2006 and No. 88 in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> For the 20th year in a row, they made the list coming in at No. 94 in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p><p>In 2010, it ranked number 53, and dropped to 61 in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, the company was ranked 183rd on the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> list of the largest United States corporations by revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2013, the fashion trade magazine, <i><a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Wear_Daily" title="Women's Wear Daily">Women's Wear Daily</a></i> reported Nordstrom as the top fashion retailer.<sup id="cite_ref-Study_Finds_Nordstrom_Again_Top_Fashion_Retailer_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Study_Finds_Nordstrom_Again_Top_Fashion_Retailer-64">[64]</a></sup></p><p>2019 was the 25th year that Nordstrom was listed on the Fortune 500.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
