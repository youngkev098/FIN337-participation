</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lennar_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lennar_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early history" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The company dates back to F&amp;R Builders, a company founded in 1954 by Gene Fisher and real estate developer Arnold P. Rosen.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1956, Lenard Miller (who would later become the namesake of the University of Miami Miller School of Medicine), a 23-year-old entrepreneur that owned 42 lots in <a href="/wiki/Miami-Dade_County,_Florida" title="Miami-Dade County, Florida">Miami-Dade County, Florida</a>, invested $10,000 and partnered with the company.<sup id="cite_ref-Carsrud_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carsrud-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 1969 the company reached an equity base of $1 million, and by 1971 Miller and Rosen changed the name to Lennar Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-Carsrud_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carsrud-7">[7]</a></sup> That year the firm became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a><sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup> of $8.7 million. It became listed on the New York Stock Exchange in 1972.<sup id="cite_ref-Carsrud_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carsrud-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 1977, the company acquired Mastercraft Homes, based in <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix, Arizona</a>, for approximately $2 million, as well as the Womack Development Company. That year Rosen also retired, staying on with the company as a director. In 1989, Lennar originated its “Everything’s Included” program, which placed extra options on built homes as a part of the lump sum charged for the property in its entirety.<sup id="cite_ref-Carsrud_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carsrud-7">[7]</a></sup> In 1992, following <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Andrew" title="Hurricane Andrew">Hurricane Andrew</a>, the company faced several lawsuits from homeowners alleging careless building quality.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In 1997, Stuart Miller, the son of co-founder Leonard Miller, also took over the company. Leonard Miller died in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In 2005 the company was added to the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Carsrud_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carsrud-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s">2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lennar_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2000s" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In November 2006, chairman Robert J. Strudler, who previously led U.S. Home Corp until it was acquired by Lennar in 2000, died.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In December 2007, during the <a href="/wiki/Subprime_mortgage_crisis" title="Subprime mortgage crisis">subprime mortgage crisis</a>, the company sold an 80% interest in 11,000 properties for 40% of their previously stated book value to <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Stanley" title="Morgan Stanley">Morgan Stanley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In early 2008, the company reported a 60% year-over-year drop in new home deliveries as it suffered from the <a href="/wiki/Financial_crisis_of_2007-2008" class="mw-redirect" title="Financial crisis of 2007-2008">financial crisis of 2007-2008</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In 2007 Lennar also founded Rialto Capital Management, which was originated to acquire distressed real estate and mortgage debt.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In 2011, former businessman and convicted felon <a href="/wiki/Barry_Minkow" title="Barry Minkow">Barry Minkow</a> engaged in an extortion scheme to manipulate the company's stock, which caused the shares to lose 26% of their value in one day. Minkow was sentenced to 5 years in prison, and was ordered to pay $584 million in restitution. San Diego real estate developer Nicolas Marsch III hired Minkow to back his claims that Lennar cheated Marsch out of millions of dollars on a private golf community.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012 the company's home orders returned to its pre-2008 levels.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> That year the company began a new program called “Lennar Next Gen”, which focused on building homes for multi-generation households.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The following year, in 2013, Lennar Commercial Investors was founded in order for Lennar to enter the commercial real estate market.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> In 2015, the average Lennar house was estimated to cost $358,000.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In February 2018, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/CalAtlantic_Homes" title="CalAtlantic Homes">CalAtlantic Homes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The $9.3 billion merger made the company become the largest home builder in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Through the acquisition, Lennar Corporation will be controlling over 250,000 homesites nationwide.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2018, Lennar announced that <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Alexa" title="Amazon Alexa">Amazon's Alexa</a> will be included into all of the 35,000 new homes built this year.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lennar_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Acquisitions" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="1980s_and_1990s">1980s and 1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lennar_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1980s and 1990s" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Soon afterward its founding, the company acquired both Bert L. Smokler &amp; Company and Dreyfus Interstate Development Corp., entering the house building industry directly in the Midwestern US.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In 1984, the company acquired H. Miller &amp; Sons for $24 million.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> In 1989, the company acquired Richmond American Homes of Florida for $18 million.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> In 1992 they also acquired Amerifirst's $1 billion real estate portfolio in a joint venture with Morgan Stanley. Then in 1993, they acquired the $2 billion Westinghouse loan portfolio in a joint venture with Lehman Brothers.<sup id="cite_ref-Carsrud_7-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carsrud-7">[7]</a></sup> In 1995, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Friendswood_Development_Company" title="Friendswood Development Company">Friendswood Development Company</a> from <a href="/wiki/Exxon" title="Exxon">Exxon</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> and acquired California company Bramalea.<sup id="cite_ref-Carsrud_7-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carsrud-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 1996, the company acquired Winncrest Homes. The company also acquired 2,200 acres (8.9 km<sup>2</sup>) acres in and took over management of <a href="/wiki/Coto_de_Caza,_California" title="Coto de Caza, California">Coto de Caza, California</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Census-designated_place" title="Census-designated place">census-designated place</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Gated_community" title="Gated community">gated community</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Chevron_Corporation" title="Chevron Corporation">Chevron Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> In 1997, the company acquired West Venture Homes.<sup id="cite_ref-wj_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wj-37">[37]</a></sup> In 2002 the company merged with Pacific Greystone, and acquired Theyst Venture Homes, 2700 homes in the North Natomas Community, and 800 San Diego homesites. In 1998, the company acquired North American Title Company, Winncrest Homes, Polygon, and ColRich Communities.<sup id="cite_ref-Carsrud_7-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carsrud-7">[7]</a></sup> The company also acquired 3 closely held home construction companies operating in California for $370 million.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> The following year the company acquired Eagle Home Mortgage and Souththeyst Land Title.<sup id="cite_ref-Carsrud_7-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carsrud-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s_2">2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lennar_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2000s" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2000, the company acquired U.S. Home Corporation for $476 million in cash and stock,<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> which resulted in the company doubling in size.<sup id="cite_ref-Carsrud_7-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carsrud-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2001, the company acquired home building operations from <a href="/wiki/Fortress_Investment_Group" title="Fortress Investment Group">Fortress Investment Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> In 2002, the company acquired Patriot Homes based in <a href="/wiki/Columbia,_Maryland" title="Columbia, Maryland">Columbia, Maryland</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Barry Andrews Homes in Baltimore, Maryland, as well as Don Galloway Homes, The Genesee Company, Cambridge Homes, and Sunstar Communities.<sup id="cite_ref-Carsrud_7-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carsrud-7">[7]</a></sup> It also acquired Concord Homes and Summit Homes, both based in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> The company then acquired 650 acres (2.6 km<sup>2</sup>) on <a href="/wiki/Mare_Island" title="Mare Island">Mare Island</a>, in a closed Navy base, for redevelopment.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> In 2003 Lennar then acquired Coleman Homes.<sup id="cite_ref-Carsrud_7-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carsrud-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 2004, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Newhall_Land_and_Farming_Company" title="Newhall Land and Farming Company">Newhall Land and Farming Company</a> for $990 million.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> The company also acquired the assets of Queens Properties for $33.8 million,<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> in addition to Connel-Barron Homes and Classic American Homes.<sup id="cite_ref-Carsrud_7-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carsrud-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2005, Lennar acquired Barker Coleman Homes,<sup id="cite_ref-Carsrud_7-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carsrud-7">[7]</a></sup> and the company acquired the 3,718-acre <a href="/wiki/Marine_Corps_Air_Station_El_Toro" title="Marine Corps Air Station El Toro">Marine Corps Air Station El Toro</a> for redevelopment.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> In 2006 twenty of the company's prior acquisitions changed their name to Lennar,<sup id="cite_ref-Carsrud_7-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carsrud-7">[7]</a></sup> a year in which Lennar also acquired 13 acres in <a href="/wiki/Anaheim,_California" title="Anaheim, California">Anaheim, California</a>, for $56 million.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It also spun off its commercial servicing division, LNR Property Corporation, which was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Starwood_Capital_Group" title="Starwood Capital Group">Starwood Capital Group</a> in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-wj_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wj-37">[37]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2013, the company acquired 20.6 acres in <a href="/wiki/Norcross,_Georgia" title="Norcross, Georgia">Norcross, Georgia</a>, for $7.59 million.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In April 2014, the company proposed building 572 single family homes and 124 townhouses in <a href="/wiki/Forsyth_County,_Georgia" title="Forsyth County, Georgia">Forsyth County, Georgia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> In June 2014, the company began selling houses at the former navy shipyard at <a href="/wiki/Bayview-Hunters_Point,_San_Francisco" class="mw-redirect" title="Bayview-Hunters Point, San Francisco">Bayview-Hunters Point, San Francisco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> In February 2016, the company acquired 143 acres in Miami Lakes for $74.4 million and announced plans to build 267 single-family homes and 238 townhouses on the site.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> The company also sold a new 167-unit apartment complex in Chicago.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> In November 2016, the company sold a 252-unit apartment complex in <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando, Florida</a>, for $42.83 million.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2017, the company acquired a parcel in <a href="/wiki/Charlotte,_North_Carolina" title="Charlotte, North Carolina">Charlotte, North Carolina</a>, for $23 million an announced plans to construct a mixed-use property on the site.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> In February 2017, the company acquired WCI Communities, which operated in Florida, for $643 million.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> In March 2017, the company acquired Westchester Pavilion in <a href="/wiki/White_Plains,_New_York" title="White Plains, New York">White Plains, New York</a>, for $56.6 million and announced plans to redevelop the site into 700 apartments and 90,000 square feet of retail space.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> In April 2017, the company acquired a 225-acre farm in <a href="/wiki/Wake_County,_North_Carolina" title="Wake County, North Carolina">Wake County, North Carolina</a>, for $25.7 million.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> In October 2017, the company acquired land in <a href="/wiki/Plantation,_Florida" title="Plantation, Florida">Plantation, Florida</a>, and announced plans to build a 271-unit luxury apartment complex on the site.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
