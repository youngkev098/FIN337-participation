With regards to consumable products, it specializes in <a href="/wiki/Anesthetics" class="mw-redirect" title="Anesthetics">anesthetics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dental_caries" class="mw-redirect" title="Dental caries">plaque and gum disease</a> prevention (<a href="/wiki/Prophylaxis" class="mw-redirect" title="Prophylaxis">prophylaxis</a>) and tooth polishers. Casey Jr., formerly CEO of the Medical Segment of <a href="/wiki/Cardinal_Health" title="Cardinal Health">Cardinal Health</a>, as CEO and a member of the board.<sup id="cite_ref-Casey_CEO_PR_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Casey_CEO_PR-8">[8]</a></sup>
In 2018 a Netherlands-based certification company awarded Dentsly the “Top Employer” certification.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 2019 the company joined forces with the American Association for Dental Research (AADR) to co-sponsor the Student Competition for Advancing Dental Research and its Application (SCADA).  The SCADA aims to bolster students research.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<p>The three products that were tested are
</p>
<ul><li>Palodent Sectional Matrix System adapts to the natural contour of the tooth</li>
<li>SDR Smart Dentine Replacement adapts to the shape of the cavity floor and matrix band.</li>
<li>ceram.x universal is a complete nano-ceramic composite that adapts to the cavity walls, SDR material and the practitioner's sculpting techniques.</li></ul><p>Class II restorations involves corrections to fractures, cavities and aesthetics.  The restoration can fail due to fracture, food impaction, and inflammation.  
It was determined that when used together the products are more effective and thus provide a complete class II solution.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dentsply_Sirona&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="1899.E2.80.932000"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1899–2000">1899–2000</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dentsply_Sirona&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1899–2000" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Dentsply was founded by four <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a> businessmen in 1899 as Dentists' Supply Company, about the same time it made its first acquisition, a <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a> <a href="/wiki/Porcelain" title="Porcelain">porcelain</a> teeth manufacturer.  One of the four founders, George H. Whiteley became the main operator of the company as a result of his experience as a ceramicist.  Whiteley's contributions to the company were invaluable as he was responsible for many innovations such as a patented process involving platinum rings that reduced tooth breakage.  Other innovations (credited to doctors like James Williams who were hired by the company for research purposes) included better fitting <a href="/wiki/Dentures" title="Dentures">dentures</a>, tooth size for people of different face shapes and age specific colored teeth.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In the early 1920s, the company selected European distributor E. de Trey &amp; Sons as its primary marketer to Europe, however it quickly became the company's main distributor in the US also.  A rivalry between De Trey and the main other denture equipment producer at the time, Ash Company, nearly eliminated cash flows due to extreme price cutting. The problem was resolved when the two decided to merge their distribution businesses into one named the Amalgamated Dental Company Limited.  In 1925, 45% of Zahnfabrik (artificial teeth producer) was bought by Dentists' Supply and its distributor Amalgamated Dental bought a 30% interest in the manufacturer.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>High American tariffs and increased efforts to gain market share abroad, especially in Europe and Australia, led the company to set up more research and manufacturing centers abroad.  Its first foreign subsidiary was established in Australia as a means to acquire Natudryl Manufacturing.  In the 1950s and 1960s the company developed many of the ideas used by its equipment today like tooth cleaning machine Dentsply Cavitron and Neolux which improved the finish of plastic teeth. The company renamed itself Dentsply International in 1969 due to its products' brand name being more widely recognized.<sup id="cite_ref-sec_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sec-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>Key acquisitions by Dentists' Supply Company: LD Caulk Company in 1963, Ransom and Randolph Company in 1964, F&amp;F Koenigkramer Company (chairs and stools). Largest was Amalgamated Dental Industrial (former distributor that played a major role in helping the company grow early on that also controlled Australia's largest dental supply chain) in 1976.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In 1993, after it acquired Gendex Corporation in a reverse takeover, the company went public on the <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a>.  The new company then purchased medical <a href="/wiki/X-ray_tube" title="X-ray tube">X-ray tubes</a> supplier Eureka X-Ray Inc., which was key since Gendex was a major manufacturer of X-ray systems (Eureka X-Ray was later sold in 1994).<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Healthco, its main distributor in the US, went bankrupt in 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="2000.E2.80.93present"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2000–present">2000–present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dentsply_Sirona&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2000–present" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, the most important markets for sales were Europe &amp; CIS (40%), USA (37%), Latin America (5%), Asia (excl CIS and Japan, 5%), Canada (4%), Japan (4%), Middle East &amp; Africa (3%) and Australia (2%).<sup id="cite_ref-tenreport_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tenreport-7">[7]</a></sup> Dentsply holds a leading market share in the dental restorative sector ($4.0 billion according to the company).<sup id="cite_ref-elevenfin_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elevenfin-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tenfin_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tenfin-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2011, Dentsply acquired Astra Tech, the world's third largest maker of dental implants from the Anglo-Swedish pharmaceutical company <a href="/wiki/AstraZeneca" title="AstraZeneca">AstraZeneca</a> for $1.8 billion. The deal raises revenue by 25% ($535M) and was completed on August 31, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Acquisitions in 2011 caused the value of long term debt to increase by 147% while total assets grew by 146% (current assets however decreased in value).<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>  Acquisition/restructuring charges were one of the reasons net income decreased 7.4%.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>Markets outside the United States are becoming increasingly important: they accounted for 67% of Dentsply revenue in 2013 steady with 2012 but up from 66% and 63% the previous two years.<sup id="cite_ref-thirteenfin_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thirteenfin-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-twelvefin_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twelvefin-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012, acquisitions contributed all of the growth in sales (16.2%, currency effects were -3.8%).  2012 sales growth by region: Europe 27.5% (vs 17.9% in 2011), USA 13.8% (vs 4.9%), elsewhere 15.9% (vs 9.4%).<sup id="cite_ref-twelvefin_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twelvefin-24">[24]</a></sup>  88% of net sales were in dental products, 12% from consumable medical device products (casting industry).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>On February 29, 2016 Dentsply combined with Long Island-based Sirona Dental Systems in a $14.5 billion merger of equals.  While both supply dental products, Sirona's business centers on dental equipment in contrast with Dentsply's consumables.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In 2017, CEO Jeffrey Slovin, executive chairman Bret Wise, and president Christopher Clark all resigned.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-taps_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-taps-25">[25]</a></sup> On January 17, 2018, Dentsply Sirona announced the appointment of Donald Casey Jr., formerly CEO of the Medical Segment of <a href="/wiki/Cardinal_Health" title="Cardinal Health">Cardinal Health</a>, as CEO and a member of the board,<sup id="cite_ref-Casey_CEO_PR_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Casey_CEO_PR-8">[8]</a></sup> with Casey to take the role in February and succeed interim CEO Mark Thierer.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-taps_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-taps-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
