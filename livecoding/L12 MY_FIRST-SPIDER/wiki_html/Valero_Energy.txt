<p><b>Valero Energy Corporation</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> international manufacturer and marketer of transportation fuels, other <a href="/wiki/Petrochemical" title="Petrochemical">petrochemical</a> products, and power. It is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio" title="San Antonio">San Antonio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Throughout the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, the company owns and operates 15 <a href="/wiki/Oil_refinery" title="Oil refinery">refineries</a>, and one in <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, with a combined throughput capacity of approximately 3 million barrels (480,000 m<sup>3</sup>) per day, 11 <a href="/wiki/Ethanol" title="Ethanol">ethanol</a> plants with a combined production capacity of 1.2 billion US gallons (4,500,000 m<sup>3</sup>) per year, and a 50-megawatt <a href="/wiki/Wind_farm" title="Wind farm">wind farm</a>. Before the 2013 spinoff of <a href="/wiki/CST_Brands" title="CST Brands">CST Brands</a>, Valero was one of the United States' largest retail operators with approximately 6,800 retail and branded wholesale outlets in the United States, Canada, United Kingdom, and the Caribbean under the Valero, Diamond Shamrock, Shamrock, <a href="/wiki/Ultramar" title="Ultramar">Ultramar</a>, Beacon, and <a href="/wiki/Texaco" title="Texaco">Texaco</a> brands.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valero_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2>
<p>Valero was created on January 1, 1980,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> as a <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spin-off</a> of <a href="/wiki/Coastal_States_Gas_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Coastal States Gas Corporation">Coastal States Gas Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">[5]</a></sup> Valero took over the <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a> operations of the defunct subsidiary of Coastal States Gas, LoVaca Gathering Company.  The name Valero comes from <i><a href="/wiki/Alamo_Mission_in_San_Antonio" title="Alamo Mission in San Antonio">Misión San Antonio de Valero</a>,</i> the original name of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Alamo" title="Battle of the Alamo">Alamo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">[5]</a></sup> Valero acquired a small state-of-the-art oil refinery in <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi,_Texas" title="Corpus Christi, Texas">Corpus Christi, Texas</a> in April 1981, two years before the gasoline refinery was finished, when it purchased a stake in Saber Energy Inc. of Houston.  In October 1984 Valero purchased the remaining 50% of the refinery, and soon after began refining operations in 1984.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 1997, Valero spun off its refinery and retail divisions into a separate company, which kept the Valero name. At the same time, the remaining divisions, which consisted primarily of natural gas operations, were acquired by the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Gas_and_Electric_Company" title="Pacific Gas and Electric Company">Pacific Gas and Electric Company</a>. Later that year, the firm acquired <a href="/w/index.php?title=Basis_Petroleum&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Basis Petroleum (page does not exist)">Basis Petroleum</a>, which left it with four refineries in <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>. In 1998, it then acquired a <a href="/wiki/Paulsboro,_New_Jersey" title="Paulsboro, New Jersey">Paulsboro, New Jersey</a>, refinery, the company's first outside of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Coast" class="mw-redirect" title="Gulf Coast">Gulf Coast</a> area.
</p><p>In 2000, Valero purchased <a href="/wiki/ExxonMobil" title="ExxonMobil">ExxonMobil</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Benicia,_California" title="Benicia, California">Benicia, California</a>, refinery and interest in 350 Exxon-branded service stations in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, mainly in the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay_Area" title="San Francisco Bay Area">San Francisco Bay Area</a>. The company also began retailing gasoline under the Valero brand. In June 2001, Valero acquired the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Huntway_Refining_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Huntway Refining Company (page does not exist)">Huntway Refining Company</a>, along with two asphalt plants on the <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="West Coast of the United States">West Coast</a>.
</p><p>On December 31, 2001, Valero completed its acquisition of Ultramar <a href="/wiki/Diamond_Shamrock" title="Diamond Shamrock">Diamond Shamrock</a>.  The merger left Valero with over 4,700 <a href="/wiki/Ultramar" title="Ultramar">Ultramar</a>, Diamond Shamrock, and Beacon retail sites in the United States, Canada, and the Caribbean. With this acquisition, Valero also received ownership of Shamrock Logistics L.P., which was renamed Valero L.P.  In 2006, the division was spun off as <a href="/wiki/NuStar_Energy" title="NuStar Energy">NuStar Energy</a>.  Starting in 2002, Valero has expanded its marketing to the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">East Coast</a>, specifically the <a href="/wiki/Northeastern_United_States" title="Northeastern United States">Northeast</a> and <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, using the Valero brand. The acquisition also includes all past Diamond Shamrock assets, including the former Sigmor Petroleum assets founded by Thomas E. Turner, founder of TETCO Inc. (the Sigmor brand name was the initials of Sigfried Moore, Turner's former employer) which merged with Shamrock Oil and Gas in 1960 forming Sigmor Shamrock - which was merged into Diamond Shamrock in 1982. Turner later repurchased Mission Petroleum Carriers when it was acquired by Diamond Shamrock which was the foundation of TETCO,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and the business portfolio of the former <a href="/wiki/National_Convenience_Stores" title="National Convenience Stores">National Convenience Stores</a>, which was acquired by Diamond Shamrock in November 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>On April 25, 2005, Valero agreed to buy <a href="/wiki/Premcor" title="Premcor">Premcor</a>, Inc., for $8 billion in cash and stock to become the largest U.S. refiner, as record prices for gasoline and other fuel boosted profits. On June 30, 2005, Valero announced that it was beginning a two-year process of converting Diamond Shamrock stations to the Valero brand.  In the next year, on May 5, 2008, Valero agreed to buy 72 <a href="/wiki/Albertsons_LLC" class="mw-redirect" title="Albertsons LLC">Albertsons</a> gas stations.
</p><p>Valero laid off 500 employees at its <a href="/wiki/Delaware_City_Refinery" title="Delaware City Refinery">refinery</a> in <a href="/wiki/Delaware_City,_Delaware" title="Delaware City, Delaware">Delaware City, Delaware</a> on November 20, 2009, due to profitability concerns.  It was reported the refinery had lost $1 million per day since the beginning of the year.<sup id="cite_ref-OGJ091120_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OGJ091120-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:PreviousValeroCompanyLogos.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/43/PreviousValeroCompanyLogos.png/150px-PreviousValeroCompanyLogos.png" decoding="async" width="150" height="203" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="271" data-file-height="367"></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:PreviousValeroCompanyLogos.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Previous logos used by Valero.  <b>Top</b>: original company logo from 1980.  <b>Below</b>: Logo used until 2018.</div></div></div>On March 11, 2011, Valero announced that it had agreed to a major European purchase from <a href="/wiki/Chevron_Corporation" title="Chevron Corporation">Chevron Corp.</a> for $730 million in cash, not including working capital that will be determined at closing.  Estimates on the value of the working capital would place the total price at $1.73 billion.  The acquisition included Chevron's <a href="/wiki/Pembroke_Refinery" title="Pembroke Refinery">Pembroke Refinery</a> in <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> — one of Europe's largest and most complex — together with marketing and logistical assets throughout the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, which include 4 pipelines, 11 terminals, an aviation fuel business, about 1,000 retail outlets, inventory and other items.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><p>In 2013, Valero spun off its retail operations into a new publicly traded company, <a href="/wiki/CST_Brands" title="CST Brands">CST Brands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ip_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ip-13">[13]</a></sup> Under long-term supply agreements, Valero continues to supply fuel to over 7,400 retail locations, many of which use brand names formerly owned by Valero.<sup id="cite_ref-valerohist_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-valerohist-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>A change to the logo, store canopy and facade was announced in April 2018. Known as "Vanguard", with various hues of blue, white, and yellow, Valero explained that applying the new design to all its stores would take several months to complete.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

</td></tr></tbody></table><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_record">Environmental record</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valero_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Environmental record" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Political_Economy_Research_Institute" title="Political Economy Research Institute">Political Economy Research Institute</a> ranks Valero 28th among corporations that emit airborne pollutants in the United States. The ranking is based on both the quantity (3.4 million pounds in 2005) and the toxicity of the emissions.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>Valero was the biggest<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> financial backer of the failed 2010 <a href="/wiki/California_Proposition_23_(2010)" class="mw-redirect" title="California Proposition 23 (2010)">California Proposition 23</a> and contributed more than $4 million by August 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>  Had it passed, Proposition 23 would have delayed action on greenhouse-gas emissions in the state of California by delaying current implementation of the California's <a href="/wiki/Global_Warming_Solutions_Act_of_2006" title="Global Warming Solutions Act of 2006">Global Warming Solutions Act of 2006</a> until the state attained an unemployment rate of 5.5% or less for one full year.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Critics argued that because that had happened only three times over the last 40 years, the proposition would have had the practical effect of repealing the law.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>Valero owns two oil refineries in California.  The <a href="/wiki/Benicia_Refinery" title="Benicia Refinery">Benicia Refinery</a> is located on the <a href="/wiki/Carquinez_Strait" title="Carquinez Strait">Carquinez Strait</a>, a tributary of the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay" title="San Francisco Bay">San Francisco Bay</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The Wilmington Refinery<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> is located 23 miles (37 km) south from downtown Los Angeles.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>Valero's Wilmington (CA), Benicia (CA), and Port Arthur (TX) refineries are known to carry and process crude oil from the Amazon region of South America, a large concern for environmentalists trying to protect the Amazon rainforest. In 2015, Wilmington and Benicia processed about 13,000 and 7,200 barrels of Amazonian crude oil per day, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Defense_contracts">Defense contracts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valero_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Defense contracts" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2>
<p>For many years, Valero Energy Corp. has been awarded contracts worth several hundred millions of dollars by the United States Defense Logistics Agency Energy (dla Energy) through the U.S. Defense Energy Support Center (DESC) to provide fuel, often <a href="/wiki/JP-8" title="JP-8">JP-8</a>, to Israel.<sup id="cite_ref-Bryce090116_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bryce090116-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Amnesty140804_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Amnesty140804-31">[31]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ValeroInsidegov_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ValeroInsidegov-32">[32]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-UPI171016_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UPI171016-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Divestitures">Divestitures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valero_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Divestitures" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2>
<p>During 2010, Valero sold its operations on the United States Atlantic coast.
</p><p>In November 2009, Valero Energy closed its operations at Delaware City.<sup id="cite_ref-OGJ091120_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OGJ091120-9">[9]</a></sup> Later, Valero Energy reached an agreement to sell the assets of its Delaware City Refining and Delaware Pipeline to a <a href="/wiki/Petroplus" title="Petroplus">Petroplus Holdings</a> subsidiary, <a href="/wiki/PBF_Energy" title="PBF Energy">PBF Energy Partners LP</a>, for approximately $220 million in September 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-bbpbf_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbpbf-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>Shortly after the divestiture of <a href="/wiki/Delaware_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Delaware City">Delaware City</a>, the company sold its refinery at the <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Paulsboro" title="Port of Paulsboro">Port of Paulsboro</a> to PBF Holdings, a wholly owned subsidiary of PBF Energy, as well. The sale concluded Valero's refinery ownership on the East Coast.<sup id="cite_ref-bbpbf_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbpbf-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OGJ100927_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OGJ100927-35">[35]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OGJ110311_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OGJ110311-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valero_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Acquisitions" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Chevron_pembroke_ref.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/Chevron_pembroke_ref.jpg/220px-Chevron_pembroke_ref.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="427"></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Chevron_pembroke_ref.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Pembroke_Refinery" title="Pembroke Refinery">Pembroke Refinery</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a></div></div></div>
<p>Through acquisitions in 2011, Valero entered the European market while strengthening its position in the United States, by reducing costs in supplying the Atlantic coast.
</p><p>On August 1, 2011, Valero acquired the Pembroke Refinery from Chevron, as well as the marketing and logistics assets, for $730 million, excluding working capital, which was valued at approximately $1 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-OGJ110311_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OGJ110311-36">[36]</a></sup> The Pembroke plant is one of the largest and most complex refineries in <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">Western Europe</a> with a total throughput capacity of 270,000 barrels (43,000 m<sup>3</sup>) per day and a <a href="/wiki/Nelson_complexity_index" title="Nelson complexity index">Nelson complexity index</a> rating of 11.8.<sup id="cite_ref-ValeroPembroke_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ValeroPembroke-37">[37]</a></sup> This puts Valero at a total of 15 refineries and 2.9 million barrels (460,000 m<sup>3</sup>) per day of throughput capacity overall, solidifying the company’s standing as the world’s largest independent refinery.<sup id="cite_ref-ValeroPembroke_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ValeroPembroke-37">[37]</a></sup></p><p>Valero also purchased ownership interest in four major pipelines and eleven fuel terminals, a 14,000-barrel (2,200 m<sup>3</sup>)-per-day aviation fuel business, and a network of more than 1,000 Texaco-branded wholesale sites, which is the largest branded dealer network in the United Kingdom and the second-largest in <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>. Valero has continued with the Texaco brand in these markets.<sup id="cite_ref-OGJ110311_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OGJ110311-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Retail">Retail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valero_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Retail" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:VALERO_CORNER_STORE.JPG" class="image"><img alt="This is the flagship Valero fueling station located in San Antonio, Texas." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/VALERO_CORNER_STORE.JPG/220px-VALERO_CORNER_STORE.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="2400" data-file-height="1800"></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:VALERO_CORNER_STORE.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>This is the flagship Valero fueling station located at UTSA Blvd. and I-10 West in <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio" title="San Antonio">San Antonio, TX</a>, prior to its 2018 re-branding to <a href="/wiki/Circle_K" title="Circle K">Circle K</a>.<br>It is currently operated by <a href="/wiki/Alimentation_Couche-Tard" title="Alimentation Couche-Tard">Alimentation Couche-Tard</a>, which owns the Circle K brand.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Valero-Cornerstore_HCTX.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Valero-Cornerstore_HCTX.jpg/220px-Valero-Cornerstore_HCTX.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="122" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1082" data-file-height="599"></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Valero-Cornerstore_HCTX.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Valero Gas Station in <a href="/wiki/Haltom_City,_Texas" title="Haltom City, Texas">Haltom City, Texas</a> on Western Center Blvd. that opened in 1986 as Diamond Shamrock, fully converted to Valero in 2005.</div></div></div>
<p>Valero retails <a href="/wiki/Gasoline" title="Gasoline">gasoline</a> branded as Valero, Shamrock, Diamond Shamrock,<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ultramar" title="Ultramar">Ultramar</a>, Beacon, and Total, the last under license from <a href="/wiki/Total_S.A." title="Total S.A.">Total S.A.</a>. While this arm of the company was the most visible to the public, it was, according to CEO Bill Greehey, "a very small part of [Valero's] operations".<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p><p>Valero attempted to shift its focus from being a <a href="/wiki/Filling_station#Types_of_filling_stations_in_the_US" title="Filling station">discount gasoline brand</a> to becoming a premium brand.  As part of the shift, Valero began to <a href="/wiki/Rebrand" class="mw-redirect" title="Rebrand">rebrand</a> its Ultramar, Beacon, Total, and Diamond Shamrock stations to the Valero brand.  The Beacon and Shamrock brands are used by retailers as a low-cost alternative to the premium Valero brand. The Shamrock brand is based on the former <a href="/wiki/Shamrock_Oil" class="mw-redirect" title="Shamrock Oil">Shamrock Oil</a> and Gas Company, which merged with <a href="/wiki/Diamond_Alkali" title="Diamond Alkali">Diamond Alkali</a> in 1967 to form Diamond Shamrock, thus declaring the trademark from official abandonment. The name Ultramar, while being eliminated in the United States, continued as Valero's brand name in Canada. Valero introduced its updated "Corner Store" retail concept on December 28, 2007, opening the company's first 5,500-square-foot (510 m<sup>2</sup>) prototype in western San Antonio. The Corner Store retail division, originally part of Diamond Shamrock, was absorbed into Valero's business portfolio in 2001. Not all Valero gas stations included a Corner Store - one Valero gas station in Euless, Texas east of Fort Worth is co-branded with a <a href="/wiki/7-Eleven" title="7-Eleven">7-Eleven</a> convenience store.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Creation_of_CST_Brands">Creation of CST Brands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valero_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Creation of CST Brands" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On July 31, 2012, during the 2nd Quarter Earnings Conference Call, Valero announced intentions to separate the retail business from the remainder of the company.  CFO Mike Ciskowski stated "We believe the separation of our retail business by way of a tax-efficient distribution to our shareholders will create operational flexibility within the business and unlock value for our shareholders."<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> In 2013, Valero completed the spinoff of the retail operations as <a href="/wiki/CST_Brands" title="CST Brands">CST Brands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ip_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ip-13">[13]</a></sup> Valero no longer owns retail operations using the Valero, Diamond Shamrock, Shamrock, Beacon, Ultramar, or Texaco names, but Valero continues to supply fuel.<sup id="cite_ref-valerohist_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-valerohist-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Credit_cards">Credit cards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valero_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Credit cards" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Valero issues its own private label <a href="/wiki/Credit_card" title="Credit card">credit cards</a> for its stations through its credit card-only subsidiary, DSRM National Bank.  The initials stand for "Diamond Shamrock Refining &amp; Marketing", the unit of Diamond Shamrock which created it before being purchased by Valero.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup>  The credit card operations are based in <a href="/wiki/Amarillo,_Texas" title="Amarillo, Texas">Amarillo, Texas</a>, a city where Diamond Shamrock was once previously based.
</p>
