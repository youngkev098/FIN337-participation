<p><b>L3Harris Technologies</b> (<b>L3Harris</b>) is an American technology company, <a href="/wiki/Defense_contractor" class="mw-redirect" title="Defense contractor">defense contractor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">information technology</a> <a href="/wiki/Service_(economics)" title="Service (economics)">services</a> provider that produces <a href="/wiki/Command_and_control" title="Command and control">C6ISR</a> systems and products, <a href="/wiki/Wireless" title="Wireless">wireless</a> equipment, tactical radios, <a href="/wiki/Avionics" title="Avionics">avionics</a> and electronic systems, <a href="/wiki/Night_vision_device" class="mw-redirect" title="Night vision device">night vision equipment</a>, and both terrestrial and spaceborne <a href="/wiki/Antenna_(radio)" title="Antenna (radio)">antennas</a> for use in the government, defense, and commercial sectors. They specialize in surveillance solutions,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> microwave weaponry,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and electronic warfare.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It was formed from the merger of <a href="/wiki/L3_Technologies" title="L3 Technologies">L3 Technologies</a> (formerly L-3 Communications) and <a href="/wiki/Harris_Corporation" title="Harris Corporation">Harris Corporation</a> on June 29, 2019,<sup id="cite_ref-close_date_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-close_date-6">[6]</a></sup> and is expected to be the sixth-largest defense contractor in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-thompson_7-0" </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=L3Harris_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/L3_Technologies" title="L3 Technologies">L3 Technologies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harris_Corporation" title="Harris Corporation">Harris Corporation</a></div>
<p>The "Harris Automatic Press Company" was founded by Alfred S. Harris in <a href="/wiki/Niles,_Ohio" title="Niles, Ohio">Niles, Ohio</a>, in 1895. The company spent the next 60 years developing <a href="/wiki/Lithograph" class="mw-redirect" title="Lithograph">lithographic</a> processes and <a href="/wiki/Printing_press" title="Printing press">printing presses</a> before acquiring <a href="/wiki/Typesetting" title="Typesetting">typesetting</a> company <a href="/wiki/Intertype_Corporation" title="Intertype Corporation">Intertype Corporation</a>. In 1967, they merged with Radiation, Inc. of <a href="/wiki/Melbourne,_Florida" title="Melbourne, Florida">Melbourne, Florida</a>, a developer of antenna, <a href="/wiki/Integrated_circuit" title="Integrated circuit">integrated circuit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Modem" title="Modem">modem</a> technology used in the <a href="/wiki/Space_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Space race">space race</a>. The company headquarters was moved from Cleveland to Melbourne in 1978.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> On May 29, 2015, Harris finalized the purchase of competitor <a href="/wiki/Exelis_Inc." title="Exelis Inc.">Exelis Inc.</a>, almost doubling the size of the original company.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>L-3 Communications was formed in 1997 to acquire certain business units from <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_Martin" title="Lockheed Martin">Lockheed Martin</a> that had previously been part of <a href="/wiki/Loral_Corporation" title="Loral Corporation">Loral Corporation</a>. These units had belonged to <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_Corporation" title="Lockheed Corporation">Lockheed Corporation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Martin_Marietta" title="Martin Marietta">Martin Marietta</a>, which had merged three years before in 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>  The company was founded by (and named for) Frank Lanza and Robert LaPenta in partnership with <a href="/wiki/Lehman_Brothers" title="Lehman Brothers">Lehman Brothers</a>. Lanza and LaPenta had both served as executives at Loral and Lockheed.<sup id="cite_ref-funding_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-funding-12">[12]</a></sup> The company continued to expand through <a href="/wiki/Mergers_and_acquisitions" title="Mergers and acquisitions">mergers and acquisitions</a> to become one of the top ten U.S. government contractors.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> At the end of 2016, the company changed its name from L-3 Communications Holdings, Inc. to L3 Technologies, Inc. to better reflect the company's wider focus since its founding in 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2018, Harris and L3 announced an all-stock "merger of equals". The merger was completed on June 29, 2019, and the new company, L3Harris Technologies, Inc., is based in Melbourne, Florida, where Harris was headquartered.<sup id="cite_ref-close_date_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-close_date-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_organization">Business organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=L3Harris_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Business organization" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>As of 2019<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=L3Harris_Technologies&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> L3Harris is organized under four business segments: Integrated Mission Systems, Space &amp; Airborne Systems, Communication Systems, and Aviation Systems.<sup id="cite_ref-overview_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-overview-1">[1]</a></sup> It is led by a 12-member board of directors, including <a href="/wiki/Chairperson" title="Chairperson">Chairman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">CEO</a> <a href="/wiki/William_M._Brown_(businessman)" title="William M. Brown (businessman)">William M. Brown</a> (former Harris CEO) and <a href="/wiki/President_(corporate_title)" title="President (corporate title)">President</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chief_operating_officer" title="Chief operating officer">COO</a> <a href="/wiki/Chris_Kubasik" title="Chris Kubasik">Chris Kubasik</a> (former L3 CEO).<sup id="cite_ref-close_date_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-close_date-6">[6]</a></sup> According to merger documents Brown is planning to transition to Executive Chairman in 2021, with Kubasik taking over the CEO role, and Kubasik will become both Chairman and CEO the following year.<sup id="cite_ref-agree_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-agree-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Integrated_Mission_Systems">Integrated Mission Systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=L3Harris_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Integrated Mission Systems" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Palm_Bay,_Florida" title="Palm Bay, Florida">Palm Bay, Florida</a>, Integrated Missions Systems specializes in intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance systems, electrical and electronic systems for maritime use, and electro-optical systems including infrared, laser imaging, and targeting systems.<sup id="cite_ref-overview_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-overview-1">[1]</a></sup> It comprises divisions, including <a href="/wiki/Wescam" title="Wescam">Wescam</a>, that had a combined revenue of $4.9 billion in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-overview_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-overview-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Space_.26_Airborne_Systems"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Space_&amp;_Airborne_Systems">Space &amp; Airborne Systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=L3Harris_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Space &amp; Airborne Systems" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Palm_Bay,_Florida" title="Palm Bay, Florida">Palm Bay, Florida</a>, Space &amp; Airborne Systems specializes in space payloads and sensors, optical and wireless networking, avionics, and situational awareness solutions.<sup id="cite_ref-overview_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-overview-1">[1]</a></sup> It comprises divisions that had a combined revenue of $4.0 billion in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-overview_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-overview-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Communications_Systems">Communications Systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=L3Harris_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Communications Systems" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_New_York" title="Rochester, New York">Rochester, New York</a>, Communications Systems specializes in tactical communications, broadband communications, night vision, and public safety.<sup id="cite_ref-overview_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-overview-1">[1]</a></sup> It comprises divisions, including <a href="/wiki/EOTech" title="EOTech">EOTech</a>, that had a combined revenue of $3.8 billion in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-overview_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-overview-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Aviation_Systems">Aviation Systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=L3Harris_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Aviation Systems" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Arlington,_Texas" title="Arlington, Texas">Arlington, Texas</a>, Aviation Systems defense aviation products such as <a href="/wiki/UAV" class="mw-redirect" title="UAV">UAVs</a> and associated RF equipment; commercial aviation products including airport security, data link communication, collision avoidance systems, and <a href="/wiki/Flight_recorder" title="Flight recorder">flight recorders</a>; commercial and military training systems, including flight and maintenance simulation; and mission networks.<sup id="cite_ref-overview_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-overview-1">[1]</a></sup> It comprises divisions, such as <a href="/wiki/AVCATT" title="AVCATT">Link Training &amp; Simulation</a> (which traces its history to the original company founded by <a href="/wiki/Ed_Link" class="mw-redirect" title="Ed Link">Ed Link</a>, inventor of the flight simulator), that had a combined revenue of $3.8 billion in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-overview_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-overview-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
