<p><b>Corteva, Inc.</b> (also known as <b>Corteva Agriscience</b>) is a major American <a href="/wiki/Agricultural_chemical" class="mw-redirect" title="Agricultural chemical">agricultural chemical</a> and seed company that was the agricultural unit of <a href="/wiki/DowDuPont" class="mw-redirect" title="DowDuPont">DowDuPont</a> prior to being spun off as an independent <a href="/wiki/Publicly_traded" class="mw-redirect" title="Publicly traded">public</a> company. It is the biggest pure play (stand alone) agricultural organization in the world.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Size">Size</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Corteva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Size" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The DowDuPont agricultural businesses that became Corteva had revenue of over $14 billion in 2017,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> which would have placed the company in the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> for that year. A major part of the company is <a href="/wiki/Pioneer_Hi-Bred_International" class="mw-redirect" title="Pioneer Hi-Bred International">Pioneer Hi-Bred International</a>, which DuPont purchased in 1999.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Corteva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In February 2020, Corteva, then the largest US-based manufacturer of the pesticide <a href="/wiki/Chlorpyrifos" title="Chlorpyrifos">chlorpyrifos</a>, announced that it would cease chlorpyrifos production by the end of 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Corteva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Criticism" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>In March 2019 Corteva was criticised by the <a href="/wiki/Humane_Society_of_the_United_States" title="Humane Society of the United States">Humane Society of the United States</a> for testing a new fungicide on <a href="/wiki/Beagle" title="Beagle">beagle dogs</a> as such testing is required by regulatory authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It has said the company will "proactively engage with government officials to minimize or cease animal studies, where possible, and once the industry receives confirmation that animal testing is no longer required, it will cease testing immediately and make every effort to re-home the animals" after it was reported that researchers intended to put the animals to death.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
