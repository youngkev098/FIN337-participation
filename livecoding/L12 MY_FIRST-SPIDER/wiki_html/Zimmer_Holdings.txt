</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Products">Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zimmer_Biomet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Products" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Zimmer designs, develops, manufactures and markets <a href="/wiki/Orthopaedics" class="mw-redirect" title="Orthopaedics">orthopaedics</a> products, including knee, hip, shoulder, elbow, foot and ankle artificial joints and dental prostheses. Zimmer has operations in more than 40 countries around the world (US, Australia, Austria, Belgium, Canada, China, Czech Republic, Finland, France, Germany, India, Ireland, Israel, Italy, Japan, Latin America, Netherlands, Brasil, Poland, Portugal, Russia, South Africa, Spain etc.) and sells products in more than 100 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p>On April 24, 2014, it was announced that Zimmer had agreed to purchase <a href="/wiki/Biomet_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Biomet Inc.">Biomet Inc.</a> for $13.4 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-Zimmer-Biomet-Inc-Apr-2014-8-K_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zimmer-Biomet-Inc-Apr-2014-8-K-7">[7]</a></sup> In October 2014 however, it was announced that the EU’s antitrust regulators had opened an extensive investigation into Zimmer’s bid for Biomet due to concern that it would substantially lessen competition in certain markets.<sup id="cite_ref-Zimmer-Biomet-Inc-Oct-2014-8-K_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zimmer-Biomet-Inc-Oct-2014-8-K-8">[8]</a></sup> In March 2016, European Commission (“EC”) conditionally cleared Zimmer’s proposed acquisition of Biomet, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-Zimmer-Biomet-Inc-Mar-2015-425_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zimmer-Biomet-Inc-Mar-2015-425-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>and on June 24, 2015, Zimmer completed its acquisition of Biomet forming Zimmer Biomet Holdings.<sup id="cite_ref-Zimmer-Biomet-Inc-Jun-2015-8-K_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zimmer-Biomet-Inc-Jun-2015-8-K-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2014, the company announced it would acquire <a href="/w/index.php?title=ETEX_Holdings,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="ETEX Holdings, Inc. (page does not exist)">ETEX Holdings, Inc.</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Zimmer-Biomet-Inc-Feb-2015-10-K_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zimmer-Biomet-Inc-Feb-2015-10-K-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2016, Zimmer Biomet announced it would acquire <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ortho_Transmission,_LLC&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ortho Transmission, LLC (page does not exist)">Ortho Transmission, LLC</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The company also announced it would acquire sports medicine company, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cayenne_Medical,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cayenne Medical, Inc. (page does not exist)">Cayenne Medical, Inc.</a><sup id="cite_ref-Zimmer-Biomet-Inc-Apr-2016-8-K_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zimmer-Biomet-Inc-Apr-2016-8-K-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Later in the same year the company acquired medical device manufacturer, <a href="/w/index.php?title=LDR_Holding_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="LDR Holding Corporation (page does not exist)">LDR Holding Corporation</a> for $37 per share ($1 billion in total),<sup id="cite_ref-Zimmer-Biomet-Inc-Jun-2016-8-K_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zimmer-Biomet-Inc-Jun-2016-8-K-16">[16]</a></sup> merging it into their Spine division.<sup id="cite_ref-Zimmer-Biomet-Inc-Jul-2016-8-K_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zimmer-Biomet-Inc-Jul-2016-8-K-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In July the company acquired French surgical robotics company Medtech. <sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In August, the company announced it was acquiring Delaware-based <a href="/w/index.php?title=CD_Diagnostics&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="CD Diagnostics (page does not exist)">CD Diagnostics</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Zimmer-Biomet-Inc-Aug-2016-8-K_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zimmer-Biomet-Inc-Aug-2016-8-K-20">[20]</a></sup> a diagnostics company developing immunoassays and biomarker testing.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In September, Zimmer announced the acquisition of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Clinical_Graphics,_B.V.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Clinical Graphics, B.V. (page does not exist)">Clinical Graphics, B.V.</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>. 
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Justin_Zimmer_House" title="Justin Zimmer House">Justin Zimmer House</a> in Warsaw is listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>.
</p>
