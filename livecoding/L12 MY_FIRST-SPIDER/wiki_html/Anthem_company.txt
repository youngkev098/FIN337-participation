<p>Anthem is ranked 33rd on the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>Prior to 2014, it was named WellPoint, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-changes_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-changes-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The company was formed by the 2004 merger of WellPoint, based in California, and Anthem, based in Indianapolis, after both companies acquired several health insurance companies.
</p><p>The company operates as <b>Anthem Blue Cross</b> in California, where it has about 800,000 customers and is the largest health insurer. It operates as <b>Empire BlueCross BlueShield</b> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_State" class="mw-redirect" title="New York State">New York State</a> and as <b>Anthem Blue Cross and Blue Shield</b> in 10 states.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthem_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Anthem">Anthem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthem_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Anthem" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1946, <b>Anthem</b> began in Indianapolis, Indiana, as Mutual Hospital Insurance Inc. and Mutual Medical Insurance Inc. The companies grew significantly, controlling 80% of the medical insurance market in Indiana by the 1970s.<sup id="cite_ref-directory_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-directory-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 1972, The two firms, then known as Blue Cross of Indiana and Blue Shield of Indiana, entered into a joint operating agreement.
</p><p>In 1985, The two companies merged into <b>Associated Insurance Companies, Inc,</b>, later called, <b>The Associated Group</b>, a <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a>, but usage of the name "Anthem" persisted.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 1989, the company purchased American General Insurance Co. for $150 million and in 1991, it acquired The Shelby Insurance Co., based in <a href="/wiki/Shelby,_Ohio" title="Shelby, Ohio">Shelby, Ohio</a>, for $125 million.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 1989, The Associated Group founded Acordia, a brokerage that sold and serviced insurance and employee benefit programs.<sup id="cite_ref-directory_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-directory-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 1993, Acordia acquired American Business Insurance for $130 million<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and the Federal Kemper Insurance Company for $100 million.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The Associated Group bought Southeastern Mutual Insurance Company, the operator of Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Kentucky.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In 1994, it sold Raffensperger, Hughes &amp; Co., Inc., Indiana's largest investment bank, to <a href="/wiki/National_City_Corp." title="National City Corp.">National City Corp.</a><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In 1995, The Associated Group acquired Community Mutual Insurance, a provider of Blue Cross and Blue Shield insurance plans in Ohio with over 1.9 million policy holders), then set up <b>Anthem Blue Cross and Blue Shield</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-strategy_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-strategy-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In 1996, The Associated Group changed its name to <b>Anthem Insurance Company</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-strategy_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-strategy-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In August 1997, Anthem acquired Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Connecticut.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> It also sold Acordia to management.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In 1999, Anthem acquired Blue Cross and Blue Shield of New Hampshire and Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Colorado and Nevada. The acquisitions made since 1996 added 850,000 policy holders. Among its customer base were 2.4 million PPO and 964,000 HMO enrollees.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In 2000, Anthem acquired Blue Cross Blue Shield of Maine.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In 2001, In October, Anthem underwent <a href="/wiki/Demutualization" title="Demutualization">demutualization</a> and became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>, which made it the 4th largest public <a href="/wiki/Managed_health_care" class="mw-redirect" title="Managed health care">managed health care</a> company in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In 2002, Anthem acquired Trigon Healthcare of Virginia, a Blue Cross and Blue Shield plan, the largest insurer in Virginia, for $4.04 billion. Anthem Insurance Company reached 11.9 million members.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Blue_Cross_of_California">Blue Cross of California</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthem_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Blue Cross of California" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><b>Blue Cross of California</b> was the predecessor of <b>WellPoint Health Network Inc</b>.
</p><p>In 1982, Blue Cross of California was founded with the consolidation of Blue Cross of Northern California (established in 1936) and Blue Cross of Southern California (established in 1937).<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In 1992, <b>WellPoint</b> was formed to operate Blue Cross of California's managed care business.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>In January 1993, Blue Cross of California spun off its managed care business into a publicly traded entity, <b>WellPoint Health Networks Inc.</b> Blue Cross of California retained an 80% interest and voting control.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>In 1996, Blue Cross of California restructured to a <a href="/wiki/For-profit_corporation" title="For-profit corporation">for-profit corporation</a>, designating WellPoint Health Networks Inc. as the parent organization.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>In April 1996, WellPoint completed its acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Mutual_Life_Insurance_Company" title="Massachusetts Mutual Life Insurance Company">Massachusetts Mutual Life Insurance Company</a>'s group life and health insurance subsidiaries for approximately $380 million, making it the second largest publicly held managed health company in the U.S. with 4 million policy holders.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>In March 1997, WellPoint acquired the group health and life businesses of <a href="/wiki/John_Hancock_Financial" title="John Hancock Financial">John Hancock Financial</a> for $86.7 million. With this acquisition, WellPoint expanded its presence into Michigan, Texas, and the mid-Atlantic, and gained a unit that concentrated on serving the needs of large employers.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>In 2000, WellPoint acquired PrecisionRx, a mail service pharmacy fulfillment center in Texas.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>In 2001, Wellpoint offered to acquire <a href="/wiki/CareFirst_BlueCross_BlueShield" title="CareFirst BlueCross BlueShield">CareFirst BlueCross BlueShield</a> for $1.37 billion, including $119 million in bonuses to Carefirst executives.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> In 2003, the offer was rejected by the Maryland insurance commissioner.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2001, WellPoint acquired Rush Prudential Health Plans, a Chicago provider, for $204 million.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In March 2001, WellPoint acquired Cerulean Companies, the parent company of Blue Cross Blue Shield of Georgia.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p><p>In 2002, WellPoint acquired RightChoice Managed Care, a Missouri-based company, for $1.5 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> WellPoint also acquired MethodistCare of <a href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Houston, Texas">Houston, Texas</a><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> and HealthLink.
</p><p>In 2003, WellPoint acquired Golden West Dental and Vision of <a href="/wiki/Camarillo,_California" title="Camarillo, California">Camarillo, California</a>, and Cobalt, including Blue Cross and Blue Shield United of Wisconsin.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2004, <b>Wellpoint, Inc.</b> was formed by the merger of Anthem Insurance Company and WellPoint Health Networks Inc. The merger was structured as Anthem acquiring WellPoint Health Networks and renaming itself WellPoint, Inc. WellPoint continued to use 'Anthem' as the brand name under which it operated. It sold its Blue Cross and Blue Shield products in 11 states.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p><p>In 2005, WellPoint acquired Alexandria, Va.–based Lumenos, a provider of <a href="/wiki/Consumer-driven_health_care" class="mw-redirect" title="Consumer-driven health care">consumer-driven health care</a>, for $185 million.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Lumenos was the pioneer and market leader in consumer-driven health plans. In December, WellPoint acquired WellChoice, a <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>-based Blue Cross Blue Shield provider, for approximately $6.5 billion,<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> making New York the 14th state in which WellPoint is a Blue Cross Blue Shield licensee.
</p><p>In 2007, WellPoint acquired Chicago-based American Imaging Management, a radiology benefit management company that creates software to help physicians choose cost-effective locations for their patients to receive medical imaging tests.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> WellPoint also acquired Chicago based American Imaging Management (AIM), the leading radiology benefit management company.
</p><p>In January 2008, Leslie Margolin became the president of California operations. She resigned in July 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p><p>In 2008, WellPoint acquired Resolution Health, a firm that analyzes patient history for potential medical problems such as adverse drug interactions.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p><p>In 2009, WellPoint acquired DeCare Dental, a dental insurance firm.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p><p>In 2011, WellPoint acquired CareMore, a <a href="/wiki/Cerritos,_California" title="Cerritos, California">Cerritos, California</a>-based provider of insurance and care centers for elderly patients.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012, WellPoint acquired <a href="/wiki/Amerigroup" title="Amerigroup">Amerigroup</a> for $4.9 billion, anticipating significant growth due to Medicaid expansion under the <a href="/wiki/Patient_Protection_and_Affordable_Care_Act" title="Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act">Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2012, CEO <a href="/wiki/Angela_Braly" title="Angela Braly">Angela Braly</a> resigned due to pressure from investors.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p><p>On August 13, 2014, WellPoint announced it intended to change its name to <b>Anthem, Inc.</b>, effective in December.<sup id="cite_ref-changes_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-changes-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2015, the company acquired Simply Healthcare Holdings, a Medicaid and Medicare managed care company based in Florida.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2015, Anthem offered to acquire <a href="/wiki/Cigna" title="Cigna">Cigna</a> for more than $54 billion in cash and stock.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2017, a <a href="/wiki/United_States_district_court" title="United States district court">United States district court</a> ruling blocked the Cigna merger on grounds of <a href="/wiki/Anti-competitive_practices" title="Anti-competitive practices">anti-competitive practices</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> On February 14, <a href="/wiki/Cigna" title="Cigna">Cigna</a> called off its merger agreement with Anthem.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2017, Anthem announced that it would not renew its <a href="/wiki/Pharmacy_benefit_management" title="Pharmacy benefit management">pharmacy benefit management</a> relationship with <a href="/wiki/Express_Scripts" title="Express Scripts">Express Scripts</a> saying it had been overcharged $3 billion and that instead, Anthem would eventually handle the PBM process itself through its new IngenioRx unit. Anthem announced that it would enter a 5-year contract with <a href="/wiki/CVS_Health" title="CVS Health">CVS Health</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> Cigna then announced plans in March 2018 to acquire Express Scripts for $58 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p><p>On November 6, 2017, <a href="/wiki/Gail_Koziara_Boudreaux" title="Gail Koziara Boudreaux">Gail Koziara Boudreaux</a> was named CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, the company announced a $20 million expansion of its headquarters and the signing of a lease in Atlanta for its technology center.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2020, Anthem announced the acquisition of Beacon Health Options, and independently held behavioral health organization.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Quality_of_care">Quality of care</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthem_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Quality of care" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In 2011, in the category of "Meeting National Standards of Care," California's state <a href="/wiki/Patient_advocacy" title="Patient advocacy">patient advocacy</a> office gave Anthem a rating of 2 out of 4 stars.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> In 2014, it received 3 out of 4 stars in the same category.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthem_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Controversies" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Giving_for_uninsured">Giving for uninsured</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthem_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Giving for uninsured" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2007, WellPoint pledged to spend $30 million over three years, through the company's charitable foundation, to help the uninsured. In March 2010, the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> reported that WellPoint's tax records and website showed that the company gave only $6.2 million by 2009. The company disputed that, saying that the foundation did fulfill its $30-million commitment by mid-2009, but the company declined to provide any financial details to support its claim.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Policy_cancellations">Policy cancellations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthem_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Policy cancellations" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2007, the California Department of Managed Health Care (DMHC) investigated Anthem's policies for revoking (rescinding) health care insurance policies. The DMHC randomly selected 90 instances where Anthem canceled the insurance of policy holders who had been diagnosed with costly or life-threatening illnesses, to find how many of these cancellations were legal. The agency concluded that all these cancellations were illegal.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2008, Anthem Blue Cross agreed to a settlement with the <a href="/wiki/California_Department_of_Managed_Health_Care" title="California Department of Managed Health Care">California Department of Managed Health Care</a>; however in doing so, WellPoint did not officially admit liability. To resolve allegations of improper policy rescissions (cancellations), WellPoint paid $10 million and reinstated plans for 1,770 policy-holders who were affected by cancelled policies. The company also agreed to provide compensation for any <a href="/wiki/Medical_debt" title="Medical debt">medical debts</a> incurred by these policy-holders.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2010, Reuters alleged that Wellpoint "using a <a href="/wiki/Computer_algorithm" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer algorithm">computer algorithm</a>, identified women recently diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Breast_cancer" title="Breast cancer">breast cancer</a> and then singled them out for cancellation of their policies."<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> The story not only caused considerable public outrage, but it also led Secretary of Health and Human Services, <a href="/wiki/Kathleen_Sebelius" title="Kathleen Sebelius">Kathleen Sebelius</a>, and President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>, to call on WellPoint to end the practice.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p><p>In 2011, Anthem began cancelling policies of members who had been paying premiums with <a href="/wiki/Credit_card" title="Credit card">credit cards</a>, sometimes without calling or emailing the member ahead of time.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Opposition_to_health_care_reform">Opposition to health care reform</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthem_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Opposition to health care reform" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The former Vice President for Public Policy and External Affairs at WellPoint, Elizabeth Fowler, is the Senior Counsel to <a href="/wiki/Max_Baucus" title="Max Baucus">Max Baucus</a>, the chairman of the Senate Finance Committee and a leading opponent of the public option in health care reform.
</p><p>In August 2009, Anthem, the largest for-profit insurer in California, contacted its employees and urged them to get involved to oppose <a href="/wiki/Health_care_reforms_proposed_during_the_Obama_administration" title="Health care reforms proposed during the Obama administration">Health care reforms proposed during the Obama administration</a>. Consumer Watchdog, a nonprofit watchdog organization in Santa Monica, asked California Atty. Gen. <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Brown" title="Jerry Brown">Jerry Brown</a> to investigate its claim that WellPoint had illegally pushed workers to write to their elected officials, attend town hall meetings and enlist family and friends to ensure an overhaul that would match the firm’s interests. According to Consumer Watchdog, California's labor code directly prohibits coercive communications, including forbidding employers from controlling, coercing or influencing employees' political activities or affiliations. WellPoint had not been contacted by the California attorney general and had not seen any complaint.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup></p><p>Through 2010 and into 2011, WellPoint senior executives met monthly with executives of other major health insurers to blunt the effect of the <a href="/wiki/Patient_Protection_and_Affordable_Care_Act" title="Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act">Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2009_premium_increase_in_Maine">2009 premium increase in Maine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthem_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2009 premium increase in Maine" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, Anthem Health Plans of Maine, a WellPoint subsidiary, sued the state of <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a> for the right to increase premiums further.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> Since Maine licenses insurance companies through its Department of Insurance, Anthem needed the state's permission to raise rates.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> The Court disagreed with Anthem and found that, unlike with other forms of insurance, the Maine Insurance Code does not require the Superintendent to consider profits.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_premium_increase_in_California">2010 premium increase in California</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthem_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2010 premium increase in California" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In February 2010, WellPoint announced that rates would increase on some Anthem Blue Cross individual policies in California by as high as 39%. The announcement resulted in an investigation by regulators from the Federal and California governments.<sup id="cite_ref-raises_75-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-raises-75">[75]</a></sup> Anthem Blue Cross gained worldwide media attention and became a <a href="/wiki/Poster_child" title="Poster child">poster child</a> for the problem of rising cost of health care in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-raises_75-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-raises-75">[75]</a></sup> The rate increase came one year after Anthem had raised rates 68% on individual policy holders.<sup id="cite_ref-raises_75-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-raises-75">[75]</a></sup></p><p>To explain the rate increases, some which were four times the rate of medical inflation, Anthem said the company had experienced a death spiral: the company claimed that with increased <a href="/wiki/Unemployment" title="Unemployment">unemployment</a> and declining <a href="/wiki/Wages" class="mw-redirect" title="Wages">wages</a>, healthy customers dropped their insurance policies. Consequently, the remaining risk pool became sicker and thus more expensive to insure; and, in turn, prices were forced up and pushed more people out of the market.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup></p><p>In response to the outrage from politicians and consumers, Anthem postponed the rate increase until May 1, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup></p><p>Senator <a href="/wiki/Dianne_Feinstein" title="Dianne Feinstein">Dianne Feinstein</a> of California proposed giving the <a href="/wiki/Federal_government_of_the_United_States" title="Federal government of the United States">Federal government of the United States</a> authority to block insurance premium hikes that it considers to be "unjustified".<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Reclassifying_expenses">Reclassifying expenses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthem_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Reclassifying expenses" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On 17 March 2010, WellPoint announced it was reclassifying some of its administrative costs as medical care costs in order to meet <a href="/wiki/Loss_ratio" title="Loss ratio">loss ratio</a> requirements under the <a href="/wiki/Patient_Protection_and_Affordable_Care_Act" title="Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act">Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act</a>, which requires insurers to spend at least 80% or 85% of customer premiums on health care services, depending on the type of plan.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2009-10_security_breach">2009-10 security breach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthem_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2009-10 security breach" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In June 2010, Anthem sent letters to 230,000 customers in California warning them that their personal data might have been accessed online via a <a href="/wiki/Data_breach" title="Data breach">data breach</a>. After a routine upgrade in October 2009, a third-party vendor stated that all security measures had been properly reinstated, when in fact they had not. As a result, personal information of thousands of coverage applicants who were under the age of 65 was exposed in the open. After a Los Angeles-area woman found that her application for coverage was publicly available, she filed a <a href="/wiki/Class_action" title="Class action">class action</a> lawsuit against Anthem. While gathering evidence for the proceeding, the woman's lawyers downloaded some confidential customer information from Anthem's website and alerted Anthem about the breach. According to the lawyers, confidential information had remained exposed for five months.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Denial_of_benefits">Denial of benefits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthem_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Denial of benefits" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In May 2014, Anthem Blue Cross refused to pay for the hospitalization of a <a href="/wiki/Sonoma_County,_California" title="Sonoma County, California">Sonoma County, California</a> man for <a href="/wiki/Stage_four_cancer" class="mw-redirect" title="Stage four cancer">stage four cancers</a>, although he had paid Anthem over $100,000 in premiums.<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup> Anthem ended up paying for coverage following public outcry.<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_medical_data_breach">2015 medical data breach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthem_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2015 medical data breach" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Anthem_medical_data_breach" title="Anthem medical data breach">Anthem medical data breach</a></div>
<p>On February 4, 2015, Anthem, Inc. disclosed that criminal hackers had broken into its servers and potentially stolen over 37.5 million records that contain <a href="/wiki/Personally_identifiable_information" class="mw-redirect" title="Personally identifiable information">personally identifiable information</a> from its servers. According to Anthem, Inc., the data breach extended into multiple <a href="/wiki/Blue_Cross_Blue_Shield_Association#Member_companies" title="Blue Cross Blue Shield Association">brands Anthem, Inc. uses</a> to market its healthcare plans, including, Anthem Blue Cross, Anthem Blue Cross and Blue Shield, Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Georgia, Empire Blue Cross and Blue Shield, <a href="/wiki/Amerigroup" title="Amerigroup">Amerigroup</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caremore" class="mw-redirect" title="Caremore">Caremore</a>, and <a href="/wiki/UniCare" title="UniCare">UniCare</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup> Healthlink was also victimized. Anthem says the medical information and financial data was not compromised. Anthem has offered free credit monitoring in the wake of the breach.<sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup> According to <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_News" title="Bloomberg News">Bloomberg News</a>, China may be responsible for this data breach. Michael Daniel, chief adviser on <a href="/wiki/Cybersecurity" class="mw-redirect" title="Cybersecurity">cybersecurity</a> for President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>, said he would be changing his own password.<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup> About 80 million company records were hacked, stoking fears that the stolen data could be used for <a href="/wiki/Identity_theft_in_the_United_States" title="Identity theft in the United States">identity theft</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup> The compromised information contained names, birthdays, medical IDs, <a href="/wiki/Social_security_number" class="mw-redirect" title="Social security number">social security numbers</a>, street addresses, e-mail addresses, employment information and income data.<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup> In 2019, two Chinese nationals were indicted for the breach.<sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Regulatory_fines">Regulatory fines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthem_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Regulatory fines" data-section="15" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2017, the <a href="/wiki/California_Department_of_Managed_Health_Care" title="California Department of Managed Health Care">California Department of Managed Health Care</a> fined the company $5 million for untimely response to consumer complaints.<sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup> In 2019, this was settled at $2.8 million.<sup id="cite_ref-93" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-93">[93]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Finances">Finances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthem_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Finances" data-section="16" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">

Anthem's shares traded at over $183 per share, and its market capitalization was valued at over US$69.1 billion in October 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup></p>
