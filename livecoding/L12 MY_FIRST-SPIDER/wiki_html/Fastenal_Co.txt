</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fastenal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Founded in 1967 by <a href="/wiki/Bob_Kierlin" title="Bob Kierlin">Bob Kierlin</a>, it was <a href="/wiki/Incorporation_(business)" title="Incorporation (business)">incorporated</a> December 24, 1968.<sup id="cite_ref-reuterstop_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuterstop-8">[8]</a></sup> Fastenal's offerings are purchased, not made. But as of 2009, the company has at least one <a href="/wiki/Heading_(metalworking)" title="Heading (metalworking)">cold heading</a> manufacturing line. By 2004, 50% of product was processed steel.
</p><p>It entered the Canadian market in 1994,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> as of 2009 Fastenal had retail sales outlets in every province and two Canadian distribution centers. As of 2009, Fastenal had established retail outlets in 14 of Mexico's states as well as a distribution center.<sup id="cite_ref-fourtmex_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fourtmex-10">[10]</a></sup> It commenced its Mexico activities in 2001. Fastenal's <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asian</a> trading company, Fastenal Asian Sourcing and Trading Co, is a <a href="/wiki/Wholly_Foreign-Owned_Enterprise" class="mw-redirect" title="Wholly Foreign-Owned Enterprise">Wholly Foreign-Owned Enterprise</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, where it directs sourcing<sup id="cite_ref-pres4_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pres4-11">[11]</a></sup> and import purchasing activities.  Opening in 2001, Fastenal's <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> location was its first site outside <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pres8_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pres8-12">[12]</a></sup> By 2009, sales operations in this small city state<sup id="cite_ref-pres8_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pres8-12">[12]</a></sup> were complemented by those in its larger neighbor <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pres9_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pres9-13">[13]</a></sup> Locally incorporated Fastenal Malaysia Sdn Bhd is associated with Fastenal's Malaysian activities. The company runs an A2LA accredited testing laboratory in this nation as of 2014.
</p><p>In 2009, Fastenal acquired parts of Holo-Krome, a <a href="/wiki/Socket_head_screw" class="mw-redirect" title="Socket head screw">socket head screw</a>-making company.<sup id="cite_ref-pres9_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pres9-13">[13]</a></sup> Fastenal and was added to the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a> index in late 2008, replacing removed corporations.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012, Lee Hein took over as President of Fastenal. Hein started out in the company as a store employee.
</p><p>In January 2016, Daniel L. Florness became the President and CEO of Fastenal. Florness started with Fastenal in 1996 as the company's chief financial officer.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>Fastenal.ca was launched in January 2017.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Products_and_services">Products and services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fastenal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Products and services" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">

construction and manufacturing.<sup id="cite_ref-reuterstop_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuterstop-8">[8]</a></sup> Today's product range is more diverse, and the company had a total of 690,000 individual products as of 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-reuterstotal_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuterstotal-7">[7]</a></sup> In addition to a plethora of <a href="/wiki/Stock_keeping_unit" title="Stock keeping unit">SKUs</a>, the company offers a variety of services, including inventory management, small <a href="/wiki/Fastener" title="Fastener">fastener</a> manufacture, vending, and <a href="/wiki/Machining" title="Machining">machining</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-introvid_9-2" class="reference"><a province of Canada,<sup id="cite_ref-fourtmex_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fourtmex-10">[10]</a></sup> 14 Mexican states,<sup id="cite_ref-fourtmex_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dist-17">[17]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dist_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dist-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p><a href="/wiki/Machining" title="Machining">Machining</a> may be a better term for what Fastenal calls its "manufacturing" operations. As of 2000, the company employs 400-plus people at six manufacturing locations,<sup id="cite_ref-pres9_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pres9-13">[13]</a></sup> including one manufacturing bolts made using a newer method, <a href="/wiki/Cold_heading" class="mw-redirect" title="Cold heading">cold heading</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Rockford,_Illinois" title="Rockford, Illinois">Rockford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-pres9_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pres9-13">[13]</a></sup> an operation in <a href="/wiki/Wallingford,_Connecticut" title="Wallingford, Connecticut">Wallingford</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-pres9_13-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pres9-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a>, and another in Malaysia, etc.<sup id="cite_ref-pres9_13-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pres9-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>Internationally, Fastenal has sites in China, <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>, Malaysia, the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, Singapore, and the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dist_17-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dist-17">[17]</a></sup> In 2006, the company had two sourcing offices in Asia, China and Taiwan, both have an accredited testing laboratory,<sup id="cite_ref-pres6_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pres6-18">[18]</a></sup> which, as of 2007, was A2LA certified.
</p>
