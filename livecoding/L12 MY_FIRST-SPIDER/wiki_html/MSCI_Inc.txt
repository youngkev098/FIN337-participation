</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=MSCI&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>In 1968, <a href="/wiki/Capital_Group_Companies" title="Capital Group Companies">Capital International</a> published indexes covering the global stock market for non-U.S. markets. In 1986, Morgan Stanley licensed the rights to the indexes from Capital International and branded the indexes as the Morgan Stanley Capital International (MSCI) indexes.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> By the 1980s, the MSCI indexes were the primary benchmark indexes outside of the US before being joined by <a href="/wiki/FTSE_Group" title="FTSE Group">FTSE</a>, <a href="/wiki/Citibank" title="Citibank">Citibank</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Standard_%26_Poor%27s" title="Standard &amp; Poor's">Standard &amp; Poor's</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Gastineau2002_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gastineau2002-3">[3]</a></sup> After <a href="/wiki/Dow_Jones_%26_Company" title="Dow Jones &amp; Company">Dow Jones</a> started float weighting its index funds, MSCI followed.<sup id="cite_ref-Gastineau2002_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gastineau2002-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2004, MSCI acquired <a href="/wiki/Barra,_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Barra, Inc.">Barra, Inc.</a>, to form MSCI Barra.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In mid-2007, parent company Morgan Stanley decided to <a href="/wiki/Divestment" title="Divestment">divest</a> from, and perhaps <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spin off</a>, MSCI.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> This was followed by an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> of a minority of stock in November 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The divestment was completed in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The company is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>Some companies in MSCI's peer group includes <a href="/wiki/Glass_Lewis" title="Glass Lewis">Glass Lewis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Factset" class="mw-redirect" title="Factset">Factset</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_Wealth_Fund_Institute" title="Sovereign Wealth Fund Institute">Sovereign Wealth Fund Institute</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Standard_%26_Poor%27s" title="Standard &amp; Poor's">Standard &amp; Poor's</a>.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=MSCI&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acquisitions" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In 2010 MSCI <a href="/w/index.php?title=Formally&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Formally (page does not exist)">formally</a> acquired <a href="/wiki/RiskMetrics" title="RiskMetrics">RiskMetrics</a> Group, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Measurisk&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Measurisk (page does not exist)">Measurisk</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012 MSCI acquired <a href="/w/index.php?title=Investment_Property_Databank&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Investment Property Databank (page does not exist)">Investment Property Databank</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 2013, MSCI acquired <a href="/w/index.php?title=Investor_Force&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Investor Force (page does not exist)">Investor Force</a> from ICG Group (formerly <a href="/wiki/Internet_Capital_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Internet Capital Group">Internet Capital Group</a>) in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2014, MSCI acquired <a href="/w/index.php?title=GMI_Ratings&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="GMI Ratings (page does not exist)">GMI Ratings</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2019, MSCI formally acquired <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Delta&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Carbon Delta (page does not exist)">Carbon Delta</a>, a Zurich based climate change analytics company.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Indexes">Indexes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=MSCI&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Indexes" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>The MSCI global equity indexes have been calculated since 1969 and include MSCI World<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and MSCI EAFE.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Initially, the company used eight factors in developing its indexes: momentum, volatility, value, size, growth, size nonlinearity, liquidity, and financial leverage.<sup id="cite_ref-DrakeFabozzi2012_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DrakeFabozzi2012-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018 MSCI announced it would begin including mainland Chinese "A" shares in its Emerging Markets Index.  Initially the domestic Chinese companies received a 5% weighting in the index even though the stated methodology called for a 40% weighting.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>  While MSCI is the last major index provider to include the companies, the decision has proved controversial due to the fact that many Chinese listed companies refuse to permit the <a href="/wiki/Public_Company_Accounting_Oversight_Board" title="Public Company Accounting Oversight Board">Public Company Accounting Oversight Board</a> to inspect their financial records, thereby sparking criticism and questions from Senator <a href="/wiki/Marco_Rubio" title="Marco Rubio">Marco Rubio</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>  In February 2019, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a></i> reported the decision was the result of pressure from the Chinese government.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In March 2019, <a href="/wiki/CNBC" title="CNBC">CNBC</a> reported that MSCI would quadruple the weightings of mainland Chinese shares in its global benchmarks.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
