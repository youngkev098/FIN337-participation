</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_Service_Enterprise_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:200px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Public_Service_Logo.gif" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/9c/Public_Service_Logo.gif" decoding="async" width="198" height="197" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="198" data-file-height="197"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 198px;height: 197px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/9c/Public_Service_Logo.gif" data-alt="" data-width="198" data-height="197" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Public_Service_Logo.gif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Public Service Logo</div></div></div>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_Service_Enterprise_Group_history">Public Service Enterprise Group history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_Service_Enterprise_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Public Service Enterprise Group history" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The Public Service Enterprise Group (PSEG) was established in 1985<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> to take control of the Public Service Electric and Gas Company (PSE&amp;G).
</p><p>In 1989, Public Service Enterprise Group establishes the Enterprise Diversified Holdings Inc.(now PSEG Energy Holdings) to begin consolidation of unregulated businesses. In 2000, Public Service Enterprise Group split PSE&amp;G subsidiary's  unregulated national power generation assets to form PSEG Power, while the PSE&amp;G subsidiary continued operating in New Jersey as a regulated gas and electric delivery company.<sup id="cite_ref-PSEG_History_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSEG_History-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2005, the acquisition of PSEG by <a href="/wiki/Exelon" title="Exelon">Exelon</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> based utility conglomerate, was approved by the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Energy_Regulatory_Commission" title="Federal Energy Regulatory Commission">Federal Energy Regulatory Commission</a>; however, the deal was never consummated and eventually dissolved after it became clear that it would not win state regulatory approval from the <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey_Board_of_Public_Utilities" title="New Jersey Board of Public Utilities">New Jersey Board of Public Utilities</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 2009, PSEG began installing <a href="/wiki/Solar_panel" title="Solar panel">solar panels</a> on 200,000 utility poles in its service area in a project costing $773 million, the largest such project in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-tsl_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tsl-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-magnolia_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-magnolia-8">[8]</a></sup> The Solar 4 All project increased the capacity for renewable energy in New Jersey and was completed in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-cn_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cn-9">[9]</a></sup> In addition, PSEG is building four solar farms in <a href="/wiki/Edison,_New_Jersey" title="Edison, New Jersey">Edison</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hamilton_Township,_Mercer_County,_New_Jersey" title="Hamilton Township, Mercer County, New Jersey">Hamilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Linden,_New_Jersey" title="Linden, New Jersey">Linden</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Trenton,_New_Jersey" title="Trenton, New Jersey">Trenton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pseg_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pseg-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_Service_Electric_and_Gas_Company_history">Public Service Electric and Gas Company history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_Service_Enterprise_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Public Service Electric and Gas Company history" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alternate_logo_for_Public_Service_Electric_and_Gas_Company.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a2/Alternate_logo_for_Public_Service_Electric_and_Gas_Company.jpg/220px-Alternate_logo_for_Public_Service_Electric_and_Gas_Company.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="29" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="334" data-file-height="44"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 29px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a2/Alternate_logo_for_Public_Service_Electric_and_Gas_Company.jpg/220px-Alternate_logo_for_Public_Service_Electric_and_Gas_Company.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="29" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alternate_logo_for_Public_Service_Electric_and_Gas_Company.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Logo for <b>Public Service Electric and Gas Company</b> (<b>PSE&amp;G</b>) subsidiary, displayed on some pages on the PSEG website as of 2012</div></div></div>
<p>The Public Service Electric and Gas Company, commonly referred to as PSE&amp;G, is the primary subsidiary of the Public Service Enterprise Group (PSEG).
</p><p>Public Service Corporation was formed in 1903 by combining more than 400 gas, electric and transportation companies in New Jersey. In 1928, the corporation merged its electric and gas utilities into a single company, PSE&amp;G. Also in 1928, Public Service Coordinated Transport was formed as an umbrella for the transit businesses. The parent Public Service Corporation was dissolved in 1948 and PSE&amp;G became an independent company, with Public Service Coordinated Transport as a subsidiary.
</p><p>PSCT was renamed <a href="/wiki/Transport_of_New_Jersey" title="Transport of New Jersey">Transport of New Jersey</a> in 1971, and sold to <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey_Transit" class="mw-redirect" title="New Jersey Transit">New Jersey Transit</a> in 1980, leaving PSE&amp;G exclusively in the utility business.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 1985, PSE&amp;G reorganized as a holding company, Public Service Enterprise Group.
</p>

<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:PSEG_Kearny_peak_jeh.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/PSEG_Kearny_peak_jeh.JPG/220px-PSEG_Kearny_peak_jeh.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="166" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="2345" data-file-height="1772"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 166px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/PSEG_Kearny_peak_jeh.JPG/220px-PSEG_Kearny_peak_jeh.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="166" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:PSEG_Kearny_peak_jeh.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Kearney,_New_Jersey" class="mw-redirect" title="Kearney, New Jersey">Kearny</a> plant</div></div></div>
<p>PSE&amp;G serves the population in an area consisting of a 2,600-square-mile (6,700 km<sup>2</sup>) diagonal corridor across the state from <a href="/wiki/Bergen_County,_New_Jersey" title="Bergen County, New Jersey">Bergen</a> to <a href="/wiki/Gloucester_County,_New_Jersey" title="Gloucester County, New Jersey">Gloucester</a> Counties.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> PSE&amp;G is the largest provider of gas and electric service, servicing 1.8 million gas customers and 2.2 million electric customers in more than 300 urban, suburban and rural communities, including New Jersey's six largest cities.
</p><p>PSEG Nuclear operates three <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_reactor" title="Nuclear reactor">nuclear reactors</a> at two facilities in <a href="/wiki/Lower_Alloways_Creek_Township,_New_Jersey" title="Lower Alloways Creek Township, New Jersey">Lower Alloways Creek Township</a>. PSEG owns one reactor at <a href="/wiki/Hope_Creek_Nuclear_Generating_Station" title="Hope Creek Nuclear Generating Station">Hope Creek Nuclear Generating Station</a> and operates two reactors at <a href="/wiki/Salem_Nuclear_Power_Plant" title="Salem Nuclear Power Plant">Salem Nuclear Power Plant</a> where PSEG Nuclear holds a 57 percent stake (in partnership with <a href="/wiki/Exelon" title="Exelon">Exelon Corporation</a>). Exelon also operates two reactors at  <a href="/wiki/Peach_Bottom_Nuclear_Generating_Station" title="Peach Bottom Nuclear Generating Station">Peach Bottom Nuclear Generating Station</a> in a 50/50 joint venture with PSEG.<sup id="cite_ref-PSEG_Nuclear_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSEG_Nuclear-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>PSEG Long Island provides electricity to 1.1 million customers in Nassau and Suffolk counties, and the Rockaway Peninsula of <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a>, part of <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> This system operates under an agreement with the <a href="/wiki/Long_Island_Power_Authority" title="Long Island Power Authority">Long Island Power Authority</a>, the state agency that owns the system, that went into effect January 1, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> PSEG was selected to essentially privatize LIPA, taking over near complete control of the system including its brand name, whereas before this agreement only a number of functions were performed by the private sector and the system was operated under the LIPA name.
</p>

<p>PSEG's transmission line voltages are 500,000 volts, 345,000 volts, 230,000 volts and 138,000 volts with interconnections to utilities in <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a>, and <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>. The company's subtransmission voltages are 69,000 volts and 26,000 volts. PSEG's distribution voltages are 13,000 volts and 4,160 volts.
</p>

<p>In 2001, PSEG received The Walter B. Jones Memorial and NOAA Excellence Awards in Coastal and Ocean Resource Management<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> in the category of Excellence in Business Leadership for its Estuary Enhancement Program.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>Researchers at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Massachusetts_Amherst" title="University of Massachusetts Amherst">University of Massachusetts Amherst</a> have identified PSEG as the 48th-largest corporate producer of <a href="/wiki/Air_pollution_in_the_United_States" title="Air pollution in the United States">Air pollution in the United States</a>, with roughly five million pounds of toxic chemicals released annually into the air.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Major pollutants indicated by the study include <a href="/wiki/Manganese" title="Manganese">manganese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chromium" title="Chromium">chromium</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nickel" title="Nickel">nickel</a> salts; <a href="/wiki/Sulfuric_acid" title="Sulfuric acid">sulfuric</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hydrochloric_acid" title="Hydrochloric acid">hydrochloric acid</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
