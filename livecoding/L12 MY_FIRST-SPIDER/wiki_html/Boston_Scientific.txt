</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boston_Scientific&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Boston Scientific was formed June 29, 1979, in Watertown, Massachusetts, as a holding company for a medical products company called Medi-Tech, and to position the company for growth in interventional medicine.<sup id="cite_ref-ourHist_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ourHist-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-IPO">Post-IPO</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boston_Scientific&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Post-IPO" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The company went public through an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> on May 19, 1992.<sup id="cite_ref-ourHist_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ourHist-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>The Taxus Stent was approved in 2003 in Europe and other countries and approved in the United States by the FDA in March 2004. It was the second drug-eluting stent approved in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-taxusAp_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-taxusAp-2">[2]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2004 the company announced that it had exercised an exclusive option to acquire Precision Vascular Systems, Inc., as part of a series of agreements between Boston Scientific and Precision Vascular in 2002 - for an undisclosed sum.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In June Boston acquired Advanced Bionics Corporation for $740 million in cash, plus earn out payments.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In December Boston completed its acquisition of Advanced Stent Technologies, Inc. for $120 million payable in Boston Scientific stock, plus the possibility of future contingent payments. AST had been developing stent and stent delivery systems specifically designed to address the anatomical needs of <a href="/wiki/Coronary_artery_disease" title="Coronary artery disease">coronary artery disease</a> in bifurcated vessels.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2005, Boston exercised an exclusive option to acquire TriVascular, Inc. for an undisclosed sum. TriVascular was founded in 1998 to develop less-invasive medical devices and procedures for treating <a href="/wiki/Abdominal_aortic_aneurysms" class="mw-redirect" title="Abdominal aortic aneurysms">abdominal aortic aneurysms</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In the same month the company also announced it had exercised its option to acquire CryoVascular Systems, Inc. and its proprietary <a href="/wiki/Angioplasty" title="Angioplasty">angioplasty</a> device to treat atherosclerotic disease of the legs and other peripheral arteries.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In June Boston Scientific announced that its wholly owned subsidiary, <i>Nemo I Acquisition, Inc.</i>, had successfully acquired Rubicon Medical Corporation, with Rubicon became a wholly owned subsidiary of Boston Scientific.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Guidant_merger">Guidant merger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boston_Scientific&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Guidant merger" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In January 2006, the company announced an offer for <a href="/wiki/Guidant" title="Guidant">Guidant</a> of $72 per share or $25 billion,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> this offer was however, rejected. On April 21, the company acquired longtime competitor <a href="/wiki/Guidant" title="Guidant">Guidant</a> for $27.2 billion. Guidant was split between Boston Scientific and <a href="/wiki/Abbott_Laboratories" title="Abbott Laboratories">Abbott Laboratories</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-merger">Post-merger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boston_Scientific&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-merger" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>

Atritech developed a novel device designed to close the left atrial appendage in patients with atrial fibrillation who are at risk for ischemic stroke.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In the same month, Boston Scientific acquired Intelect Medical, Inc. for $78 million<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> and the remaining 86 percent of Sadra Medical, Inc. not already owned for $193 million plus contingent payments.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> At the same time, the business divested its neurovascular business to <a href="/wiki/Stryker_Corporation" title="Stryker Corporation">Stryker Corporation</a> for $1.5 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2012, Boston Scientific officially acquired Cameron Health for a total sum of $1.3 billion, paid out incrementally as various revenue milestones were achieved.<sup id="cite_ref-PRNewsBoston12_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PRNewsBoston12-3">[3]</a></sup> In September the company announced it would acquire BridgePoint Medical, Inc., developer of a catheter-based system to treat coronary chronic total occlusions.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> In October, the company acquired Rhythmia Medical, Inc., developer of mapping and navigation methods for use in cardiac catheter ablations and other electrophysiology procedures.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> A month later the business acquired catheter-based renal denervation system developer, Vessix Vascular, Inc..<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2013 Boston announced it would acquire Bard EP, the <a href="/wiki/Electrophysiology" title="Electrophysiology">electrophysiology</a> business of C.R. <p>The company is assessed quite positively by its own employees. In the 2019 <i>Best Places to Work - Employee's Choice Survey</i>, conducted by the <a href="/wiki/Glassdoor" title="Glassdoor">Glassdoor</a> website, Boston Scientific ranked 43rd out of 100 companies listed, with an average score of 4.3 out of 5 possible stars.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> The feedback for this survey is given by current and former employees. Boston Scientific has 43% women among its employees, 38% of management positions and 23% of corporate top management positions are held by women (data for US workforce, as of 2018).<sup id="cite_ref-WorkingMother_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WorkingMother-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_commitment">Social commitment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boston_Scientific&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Social commitment" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Boston Scientific has the self-imposed standard of implementing an integrative organizational culture and actively strives to further improve this by increasing diversity at the level of management and executives. To this end, the company has set itself the following goals in 2018:<sup id="cite_ref-Diversity_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Diversity-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<ul><li>Leadership role by belonging to the top 10 companies in the integration of employees of the following groups: <a href="/wiki/Women" class="mw-redirect" title="Women">women</a>, <a href="/wiki/People_of_color" class="mw-redirect" title="People of color">people of color</a>, disabled persons and members of the <a href="/wiki/LGBTQ" class="mw-redirect" title="LGBTQ">LGBTQ</a> community.</li>
<li>Increase the proportion of people of color with supervisor or managerial functions to 20% in the USA including Puerto Rico (end of 2018: 19.6%, +1.8% compared to 2017).</li>
<li>Increase the proportion of women in supervisor or managerial positions to 40% worldwide (end of 2018: 38.4%, +1.0% against 2017).</li></ul><p>The success of these efforts is confirmed by the following independent assessment results:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a> added the company in 2019 to its list of <i>Best Employers For Diversity</i> for its efforts to foster a corporate culture that welcomes and supports all employees. Boston Scientific ranked 85th out of 500 companies.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_News" title="Bloomberg News">Bloomberg</a> recognized Boston Scientific for its inclusion in the <i>Gender Equality Index</i> in 2019, which includes companies that publicly demonstrate their commitment to equality and the advancement of women in the workplace. The index lists a total of 230 companies from ten economic sectors and 36 countries and regions.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></li>
<li>The US magazine <i>Working Mother</i> voted the company into the list of the <i>100 Best Companies for Working Mothers</i> twice, most recently in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-WorkingMother_53-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WorkingMother-53">[53]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Human_Rights_Campaign" title="Human Rights Campaign">Human Rights Campaign</a> has elected Boston Scientific as a <i>Best Place to Work for LGBTQ Equality</i> in its 2019 edition of the eponymous list.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Lawsuits">Lawsuits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boston_Scientific&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Lawsuits" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Johnson_.26_Johnson_lawsuits"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Johnson_&amp;_Johnson_lawsuits">Johnson &amp; Johnson lawsuits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boston_Scientific&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Johnson &amp; Johnson lawsuits" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Beginning in 2003, Boston Scientific and <a href="/wiki/Johnson_%26_Johnson" title="Johnson &amp; Johnson">Johnson &amp; Johnson</a> were involved in a series of litigations involving patents covering <a href="/wiki/Heart_stent" class="mw-redirect" title="Heart stent">heart stent</a> medical devices. Both parties claimed that the other had infringed upon their patents. The litigation was settled once Boston Scientific agreed to pay $716 million to Johnson &amp; Johnson in September 2009 and an additional $1.73 billion in February 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p><p>It was announced in November 2014 that <a href="/wiki/Johnson_%26_Johnson" title="Johnson &amp; Johnson">Johnson &amp; Johnson</a> would have another chance for payback after a multibillion-dollar trial was set for 20 November 2014. A <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> federal court judge would hear the case without a jury to decide whether Boston Scientific should be held liable for the contract breach.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Transvaginal_mesh">Transvaginal mesh</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boston_Scientific&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Transvaginal mesh" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Boston Scientific is one of several manufacturers of a medical device called transvaginal mesh, a type of <a href="/wiki/Surgical_mesh" title="Surgical mesh">surgical mesh</a> used to treat <a href="/wiki/Pelvic_organ_prolapse" title="Pelvic organ prolapse">pelvic organ prolapse</a>.  In 2015, Boston Scientific announced it would pay $119 million to 2,970 lawsuit plaintiffs, who had been injured by the mesh.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p><p>On Sunday, May 13, 2018, <a href="/wiki/60_Minutes" title="60 Minutes">60 Minutes</a> broadcast a story suggesting that Boston Scientific used counterfeit <a href="/wiki/Marlex" title="Marlex">Marlex polypropylene</a> resin to manufacture their mesh product.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup>  Boston Scientific responded by saying the broadcast was "irresponsible and misleading,"<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> citing a 2017 <a href="/wiki/Food_and_Drug_Administration" title="Food and Drug Administration">Food and Drug Administration</a> (FDA) report stating that although they found variability in the polypropylene resin, "these differences do not present new safety or effectiveness concerns."<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p><p>The Mayo Clinic has described the advantages and risks associated with the use of transvaginal mesh.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
