<p>Devon was founded in 1971 by John Nichols and his son, <a href="/wiki/J._Larry_Nichols" title="J. Larry Nichols">J. Larry Nichols</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-acct_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acct-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In 1988, the company became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-acct_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acct-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2000, the company was added to the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500_Index" title="S&amp;P 500 Index">S&amp;P 500 Index</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-santafe_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-santafe-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In 2004, Devon was one of several companies in the <a href="/wiki/Petroleum_industry" title="Petroleum industry">petroleum industry</a> for which <a href="/wiki/Shareholder_resolution" title="Shareholder resolution">shareholder resolutions</a> were introduced that would have required the companies to monitor their effects on <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" class="mw-redirect" title="Climate change">climate change</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2008, co-founder John Nichols died.<sup id="cite_ref-acct_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acct-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2010, the company sold assets in Brazil, Azerbaijan, and the Gulf of Mexico to <a href="/wiki/BP" title="BP">BP</a> for $7 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2012, the company completed construction of its current headquarters, the 50-story <a href="/wiki/Devon_Energy_Center_(Oklahoma_City)" class="mw-redirect" title="Devon Energy Center (Oklahoma City)">Devon Energy Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City" title="Oklahoma City">Oklahoma City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a> and closed its office in the <a href="/wiki/Allen_Center" title="Allen Center">Allen Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Houston" title="Downtown Houston">Downtown Houston</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2014, the company sold its conventional assets in Canada to <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Natural_Resources" title="Canadian Natural Resources">Canadian Natural Resources</a> for C$3.125 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2014, the company sold assets to <a href="/wiki/Linn_Energy" title="Linn Energy">Linn Energy</a> for $2.3 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2015, Dave Hager was named <a href="/wiki/President_(corporate_title)" title="President (corporate title)">president</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">chief executive officer</a> of the company.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2016, Devon announced plans to lay off 1,000 employees, including 700 in Oklahoma City, and cut its <a href="/wiki/Dividend" title="Dividend">dividend</a> as part of a cost-cutting effort due to low prices of its products.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, the company sold its Lavaca County assets in the Eagle Ford.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2019, the company sold its assets in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> to <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Natural_Resources" title="Canadian Natural Resources">Canadian Natural Resources</a> for CAD $3.8 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2019, the company almost capped a blowout at a natural gas well, which prompted authorities to seal off thousands of acres of land near the Eagle Ford Shale towns of Yorktown and Nordheim. Crews were able to install a capping stack on the well to reduce natural gas flowing from the well.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>

<p>Devon contributed over $1 million in each of the last 3 U.S. election cycles, almost entirely to organizations and individuals affiliated with the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican Party</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In 2016, the company contributed $750,000 to the Senate Leadership Fund, whose goal is to protect the Republican majority in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a>. It also gave $500,000 to the Congressional Leadership Fund, whose goal is to protect the Republican majority in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">United States House of Representatives</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>Devon and its lobbyists have been noted to have close ties to government officials. In 2014, an investigation by <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> uncovered that a three-page letter signed by <a href="/wiki/Scott_Pruitt" title="Scott Pruitt">Scott Pruitt</a>, then the <a href="/wiki/Attorney_General_of_Oklahoma" title="Attorney General of Oklahoma">Attorney General of Oklahoma</a>, to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="United States Environmental Protection Agency">United States Environmental Protection Agency</a> advocating for a relaxing of laws related to <a href="/wiki/Hydraulic_fracturing" title="Hydraulic fracturing">hydraulic fracturing</a> was actually written by lobbyists for Devon Energy and not by Pruitt.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, a <a href="/wiki/Shareholder_resolution" title="Shareholder resolution">shareholder resolution</a> was introduced that would have required the company to disclose its <a href="/wiki/Lobbying" title="Lobbying">lobbying</a> activity against regulations to prevent <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" class="mw-redirect" title="Climate change">climate change</a>. The resolution received votes of support by approximately 20% of shareholders.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
