<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">This article is about the American company formerly known as Gardner Denver. For the Irish-domiciled company formerly known as Ingersoll Rand plc, see <a href="/wiki/Trane_Technologies" title="Trane Technologies">Trane Technologies</a>.</div> 
<p><b>Ingersoll Rand Inc.</b> (formerly <b>Gardner Denver Inc.</b>), founded in 1859, is an American worldwide provider of industrial equipment, technologies and related parts and services to a broad and diverse customer base through a family of brands. The company has over 30 manufacturing facilities located in the Americas, Europe, the Middle East, and Asia Pacific with offices in 35 different countries. Based in <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee,_Wisconsin,_USA" class="mw-redirect" title="Milwaukee, Wisconsin, USA">Milwaukee, Wisconsin, USA</a>, it operates in three groups: the Industrials Group, Energy Group, and Medical Group.

Unsourced material may be challenged and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Burden_of_evidence" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">removed</a>.<br><small><span class="plainlinks"><i>Find sources:</i> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//www.google.com/search?as_eq=wikipedia&amp;q=%22Ingersoll+Rand%22">"Ingersoll Rand"</a> – <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//www.google.com/search?tbm=nws&amp;q=%22Ingersoll+Rand%22+-wikipedia">news</a> <b>·</b> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//www.google.com/search?&amp;q=%22Ingersoll+Rand%22+site:news.google.com/newspapers&amp;source=newspapers">newspapers</a> <b>·</b> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//www.google.com/search?tbs=bks:1&amp;q=%22Ingersoll+Rand%22+-wikipedia">books</a> <b>·</b> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//scholar.google.com/scholar?q=%22Ingersoll+Rand%22">scholar</a> <b>·</b> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.jstor.org/action/doBasicSearch?Query=%22Ingersoll+Rand%22&amp;acc=on&amp;wc=on">JSTOR</a></span></small></span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">March 2019</span>)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table><p>The <b>Industrials Group</b> designs, manufactures, markets and services a wide range of products including rotary screw, reciprocating and sliding vane compressors, multistage and positive displacement, centrifugal and side-channel blowers, vacuum technology as well as mobile transport products. The end market sectors served by the Industrials Group are primarily industrial manufacturing, transportation, energy, mining and construction, environmental, and food and beverage.
</p><p>The <b>Energy Group</b> manufactures, markets and services liquid ring pumps and engineered systems for power generation, environmental, petrochemical, pulp and paper and industrial applications; pumps and fluid transfer equipment used primarily in oil and natural gas well drilling, servicing and production, and petrochemical and industrial applications; and water jetting pumps. The Energy Group includes three divisions: Petroleum Pump, Nash, and <a href="/wiki/EMCO_Wheaton" title="EMCO Wheaton">EMCO Wheaton</a>. The Petroleum Pump Division designs, manufactures, tests and markets a diverse range of pumps for the oil &amp; gas industry. Nash is the leader in liquid ring vacuum pumps. The Emco Wheaton Division designs, manufactures and installs a wide range of solutions for the loading and unloading of almost any liquid and compressed gas products from river barges, ships and oceangoing supertankers. In addition, Emco Wheaton and Todo swivel joints, Dry-Break couplers and adapters, off-highway fueling systems and grounding equipment are all used within bunkering applications; the transfer of oil via hoses, pipelines, or loading arms for the purpose of providing fuel or lubricants to a tank vessel or nontank.
</p><p>The <b>Medical Group</b> manufactures single-piece piston reciprocating, diaphragm and linear compressor and vacuum pumps, which are used in medical, environmental and laboratory applications.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ingersoll_Rand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<table class="box-Prose plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-style ambox-Prose" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>is in <a href="/wiki/MOS:LIST" class="mw-redirect" title="MOS:LIST">list</a> format, but may read better as <a href="/wiki/MOS:PROSE" class="mw-redirect" title="MOS:PROSE">prose</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> You can help by <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Ingersoll_Rand&amp;action=edit">converting this section</a>, if appropriate. <a href="/wiki/Help:Editing" title="Help:Editing">Editing help</a> is available.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">March 2019</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table><ul><li>1859 The Gardner Governor Company, founded by Robert Gardner, introduces a speed control for <a href="/wiki/Steam_engine" title="Steam engine">steam engines</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> This innovation, known as the <a href="/wiki/Flyball_governor" class="mw-redirect" title="Flyball governor">flyball governor</a>, helped pave the way to later production of other industrial products such as <a href="/wiki/Air_compressor" title="Air compressor">air compressors</a>. By the turn of the century, the company sold more than 150,000 governors across the United States and Canada.</li>
<li>1927 The Gardner Governor Company merges with the Denver Rock Drill Company and becomes Gardner-Denver.</li>
<li>1943 Gardner Denver is listed on the New York Stock Exchange</li>
<li>1950s During the post-war growth period, Gardner-Denver makes a number of acquisitions, including Keller Tool Company and the CycloBlower Company, a manufacturer of helical screw blowers.</li>
<li>1960s Gardner Denver adds the Apex Machine and Tool Company and the Martin Decker Corporation.</li>
<li>1979 The company is acquired by <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Industries" title="Cooper Industries">Cooper Industries</a>, Inc. (Cooper).</li>
<li>1985-1988 Cooper purchases Sutorbilt and DuroFlow blowers, OPI well servicing pumps, and Joy compressors.</li>
<li>1994 As Cooper Industries’ strategy changes, the Gardner Denver Industrial Machinery Division is spun off as an independent company. Gardner Denver, Inc. then becomes a publicly held company trading on the New York Stock Exchange.</li>
<li>2004 Drilling Solutions business was sold to the Swedish company <a href="/wiki/Atlas_Copco" title="Atlas Copco">Atlas Copco</a>.</li></ul><p>Gardner Denver, Inc. continues to acquire complementary companies. Twenty-five companies have been acquired since the spin-off from Cooper Industries, including: 
</p>
<ul><li>1996 Lamson Corp., Syracuse, New York, USA and Twentieth Century Manufacturing (TCM), Tulsa, Oklahoma, USA</li>
<li>1997 Oy Tamrotor AB, Tampere, Finland and Champion Pneumatic, Princeton, Illinois, USA</li>
<li>1998 Fort Worth, Texas-based Geological Equipment and Wittig, Schopfheim, Germany</li>
<li>1999 US-based Allen-Stuart Equipment Co and Butterworth Jetting Systems, both Houston, Texas, as well as Air Relief, Mayfield, Kentucky</li>
<li>2000 Invincible Airflow Systems, Baltic, Ohio, Jetting Systems &amp; Accessories, Inc. and CRS Power Flow, Inc, both Houston, Texas</li>
<li>2001 Gloucester, UK compressor manufacturer Belliss &amp; Morcom and Syracuse, New York based •	Hoffman Air &amp; Filtration System</li>
<li>2003 Chaparral, Odessa, Texas, USA</li>
<li>2004 Syltone / Emco Wheaton, Margate, UK and Kirchhain, Germany as well as Nash Elmo</li>
<li>2005 Bottarini, Lonate, Italy as well as US and Europe based Thomas Rietschle</li>
<li>2006 Todo, Toreboda, Sweden</li>
<li>2008 CompAir, Redditch, UK and Simmern, Germany</li>
<li>2009 Ilmvac, Ilmenau, Germany; Gardner Denver, Inc. marks its sesquicentennial (150 years) as a company</li>
<li>2011 Robuschi, Parma, Italy</li></ul><p>In 2013, Gardner Denver goes from a publicly traded to a private company when its outstanding shares are purchased by US multi-national private equity firm Kohlberg Kravis Roberts &amp; Co. L.P.
</p>
<ul><li>2014 Acquisition of Texas-based Conroe Plastics Molding, Inc.</li>
<li>2016 - sold Air Relief to Epic</li>
<li>2017 Conducted IPO<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>2020 The Gardner-Denver Company merges with the industrial businesses of Ingersoll Rand plc (now known as <a href="/wiki/Trane_Technologies" title="Trane Technologies">Trane Technologies</a>) and becomes Ingersoll Rand Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ingersoll_Rand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: References" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
