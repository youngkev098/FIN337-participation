</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kraft_Heinz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The merger of Kraft Foods and H.J. Heinz was agreed by the boards of both companies, with approval by shareholders and regulatory authorities in early 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC32050266_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC32050266-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ift_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ift-8">[8]</a></sup> The new Kraft Heinz Company became the world's fifth-largest food and beverage company<sup id="cite_ref-time3757678_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-time3757678-9">[9]</a></sup> and the third-largest in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC32050266_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC32050266-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The Kraft Heinz co-headquarters are in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Aon_Center_(Chicago)" title="Aon Center (Chicago)">Aon Center</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a> at <a href="/wiki/PPG_Place" title="PPG Place">PPG Place</a>, with other offices across the United States, Canada, South America, Europe, Asia, and Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-kraftheinzcompany.com_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kraftheinzcompany.com-11">[11]</a></sup> The companies completed the merger on July 2, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-Merger_Press_Release_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Merger_Press_Release-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>The merger did not affect the <a href="/wiki/Naming_rights" title="Naming rights">naming rights</a> to <a href="/wiki/Heinz_Field" title="Heinz Field">Heinz Field</a>, home of the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Steelers" title="Pittsburgh Steelers">Pittsburgh Steelers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Heinz_Field_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heinz_Field-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>On February 17, 2017, it was reported that Kraft Heinz Co. had made a $143 billion approach to take over the British-Dutch multinational <a href="/wiki/Unilever" title="Unilever">Unilever</a>, a significantly larger competitor with 126,000 more employees and £24bn larger revenue than Kraft Heinz.<sup id="cite_ref-telegraph.co.uk_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph.co.uk-14">[14]</a></sup> Unilever declined the initial proposal.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The takeover was subsequently abandoned on 19 February soon after UK Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Theresa_May" title="Theresa May">Theresa May</a> had ordered a scrutiny of the deal.<sup id="cite_ref-telegraph.co.uk_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph.co.uk-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2018, Kraft Heinz launched Springboard Brands, a business focused on growing organic, natural, and "super-premium" food brands.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Later that year, it was announced Kraft Heinz would acquire the Primal Kitchen brand as part of the company's Springboard Incubator.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The $200 million deal was completed in early 2019 and was expected to generate $50 million in new annual revenue.
</p><p>In July 2018, Kraft Heinz announced it was committing to make all of its global packaging recyclable, reusable or compostable by 2025.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2019, it was announced <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Patricio" title="Miguel Patricio">Miguel Patricio</a>, former Chief Marketing Officer of <a href="/wiki/InBev" title="InBev">InBev</a> would replace Bernardo Hees as CEO of Kraft Heinz.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Patricio took position of CEO in late June 2019 and <a href="/wiki/Alex_Behring" title="Alex Behring">Alex Behring</a> remained chairman of the company.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Later that year, <a href="/wiki/Paulo_Basilio" title="Paulo Basilio">Paulo Basilio</a> returned to his role at Chief Financial Officer and Corrado Azzarita assumed the position of Chief Information Officer.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>

<p>For the fiscal year 2017, Kraft Heinz reported earnings of <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$</span>11.0 billion, with an annual revenue of <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$</span>26.2 billion, a decline of 0.6% over the previous fiscal cycle. Kraft Heinz's shares traded at over $61 per share, and its market capitalization was valued at over <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$</span>136 billion in September 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2019, shares in Kraft Heinz fell to a record low of under $35, after the company reported a $10.2bn loss for the previous year as the company announced that it would take a $15.4 billion write down of its Kraft and Oscar Meyer brands, slashed its dividend, and acknowledged that the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission">U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission</a> had opened a probe into its accounting practices.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup>  In August 2019 Kraft Heinz announced a further $1.22 billion in write downs.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>  In August 2019, Kraft Heinz announced it was bringing back its former CFO, Paulo Basilio, who had served in the position until 2017, to replace David Knopf, saying it wanted a "seasoned veteran" in the job following a series of accounting errors.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
