</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Molson_Coors_Beverage_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>On 9 October 2007, SABMiller and Molson Coors Brewing Company announced a joint venture to be known as <a href="/wiki/MillerCoors" title="MillerCoors">MillerCoors</a> for their U.S. operations that will wholesale all of their products.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> MillerCoors is headquartered in Chicago.
</p><p>On 2 February 2011, the company purchased <a href="/wiki/Sharp%27s_Brewery" title="Sharp's Brewery">Sharp's Brewery</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a> in England for £20 million.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>On 26 May 2011, seventh-generation family member Andrew Molson succeeded Pete Coors as acting Chairman of the company.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In early 2012 the company expanded into the Central and Eastern Europe markets by acquiring the region's market-leading brewery StarBev from <a href="/wiki/CVC_Capital_Partners" title="CVC Capital Partners">CVC</a> Capital Partners.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2014, it was revealed in the media that the firm was in talks to acquire <a href="/wiki/Heineken" title="Heineken">Heineken</a>’s operations in the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>The company launched two e-commerce platforms for <a href="/wiki/Blue_Moon_(beer)" title="Blue Moon (beer)">Blue Moon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coors_Light" title="Coors Light">Coors Light</a> in 2019. The brand pages were the company's first purchased-enabled platforms.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>On 30 October 2019, the company changed its name slightly to the Molson Coors Beverage Company and announced that the company's headquarters will move to Chicago.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Molson_Coors_2016_expansion">Molson Coors 2016 expansion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Molson_Coors_Beverage_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Molson Coors 2016 expansion" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>During the merger discussions between Anheuser-Busch InBev and SABMiller in 2015, the U.S. Department of Justice (DOJ) had agreed to proposed deal only on the basis that SABMiller "spins off all its MillerCoors holdings in the U.S.—which include both Miller- and Coors-held brands—along with its Miller brands outside the U.S." The entire ownership situation was complicated: "In the United States, Coors is majority owned by MillerCoors (a subsidiary of SABMiller) and minority owned by Molson Coors, though internationally it's entirely owned by Molson Coors, and Miller is owned by SABMiller."<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>SABMiller agreed to divest itself of the Miller brands by selling its stake in MillerCoors to Molson Coors. The merger between the two companies closed on October 10, 2016. The spinoff deal was completed on October 11, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-9">[9]</a></sup> As per the agreement with the regulators, SABMiller sold to <a href="/wiki/Molson_Coors" class="mw-redirect" title="Molson Coors">Molson Coors</a> full ownership of the Miller brand portfolio outside of the U.S. and Puerto Rico for US$12 billion. Molson Coors also retained "the rights to all of the brands currently in the MillerCoors portfolio for the U.S. and Puerto Rico, including Redd’s and import brands such as Peroni, Grolsch and Pilsner Urquell." The agreement made Molson Coors the world's third largest brewer.<sup id="cite_ref-acq_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acq-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In Canada, Molson Coors regained the right to make and market Miller Genuine Draft and Miller Lite.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>After SABMiller divested itself of all interests in MillerCoors, then Molson Coors became the largest brewer in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2020_Milwaukee_campus_shooting">2020 Milwaukee campus shooting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Molson_Coors_Beverage_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2020 Milwaukee campus shooting" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_brewery_shooting" title="Milwaukee brewery shooting">Milwaukee brewery shooting</a></div>
<p>On February 26, 2020, six people, including the shooter, were killed at a shooting near the company's Milwaukee brewing campus.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The Milwaukee complex serves as a site for Molson Coors' corporate offices and brewing facilities and was in the "Miller Valley" area, which served as the headquarters for the Miller Brewing Company before it was acquired by Molson Coors.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_structure">Corporate structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Molson_Coors_Beverage_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Corporate structure" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Molson Coors has a number of divisions and subdivisions.
</p><p>The <b>Molson Coors Canada</b> division entered the craft beer market with the acquisitions of Creemore Springs Brewery and <a href="/wiki/Granville_Island_Brewing" title="Granville Island Brewing">Granville Island Brewing</a>, to create the <i>Six Pints Specialty Beer Company</i>, a subdivision that first was an umbrella for those brands and aiming to include other Molson-owned and affiliated brands.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>From October 11, 2016, through December 31, 2019, MillerCoors was the US business division of Molson Coors.<sup id="cite_ref-Brown_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brown-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>Currently, Molson Coors operates through Molson Coors North America (formerly MillerCoors) and Molson Coors Europe.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Management_team">Management team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Molson_Coors_Beverage_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Management team" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">

<p>The Molson Coors Brewing Company conducted a comprehensive, and voluntary investigation of its pollution and environmental emissions.  Coors was not violating the <a href="/wiki/Clean_Air_Act_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="Clean Air Act (United States)">Clean Air Act</a> but was encouraged by the Environmental Audit Privilege and Voluntary Disclosure Act which immunizes and credits organizations for conducting environmental self-audits, which can grant immunity from environmental regulation fines.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>The United States government had thought that Coors was a minor violator of emissions such as <a href="/wiki/Volatile_organic_compounds" class="mw-redirect" title="Volatile organic compounds">volatile organic compounds</a> (VOCs), but the investigating showed otherwise, revealing that Coors was 17 times over the estimated value of emissions.  Molson Coors then provided the audit results to the Colorado Department of Health which culminated in a $1.05 million fine for the 189 violations of state pollution laws.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>Although Molson Coors said they did not know about the volatile organic compounds they were emitting, they do claim to be environmentally aware. Coors invented a new printing technology technique which uses ultra-violet light to cure the print, a technique which the company claims is more environmentally sound than the traditional gas firing technique.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>In an in-depth analysis of the climate change "countermovement," the Coors Affiliated Foundation was listed among the top donors, having funded roughly 1% (US$6.2 million) of all <a href="/wiki/Climate_change_denial" title="Climate change denial">climate denial</a> research conducted between 2003–2010.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
