<p><b>Alliant Energy Corporation</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Public_utility" title="Public utility">public utility</a> holding company that incorporated in <a href="/wiki/Madison,_Wisconsin" title="Madison, Wisconsin">Madison, Wisconsin</a> in 1981.<sup id="cite_ref-Alliant-Energy-Feb-2010-10-K_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alliant-Energy-Feb-2010-10-K-4">[4]</a></sup>  It consists of four subsidiaries:<sup id="cite_ref-Alliant-Energy-Feb-2010-10-K_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alliant-Energy-Feb-2010-10-K-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Alliant-Energy-Nov-2012-10-Q_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alliant-Energy-Nov-2012-10-Q-5">[5]</a></sup></p><ul><li><b>Interstate Power and Light Company (IPL)</b> is a public utility that generates and distributes <a href="/wiki/Electricity" title="Electricity">electricity</a> and distributes and transports <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a> in Iowa.</li>
<li><b>Wisconsin Power and Light Company (WPL)</b> provides similar services as IPL in southern and central Wisconsin.</li>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alliant_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>IPC expanded greatly in the late 1920s to include operations in Iowa, Wisconsin, Minnesota, North and South Dakota, Nebraska, Oklahoma, and Manitoba (Canada). One of the largest purchases in the late 1920s was the purchase of what became its northern Minnesota territory from the <a href="/wiki/Wilbur_Foshay" class="mw-redirect" title="Wilbur Foshay">Wilbur Foshay</a> interests.  Also was widely carried along CSX Rail lines.
</p><p>In the 1930s and 1940s, the effects of the Depression and passage of laws to regulate utility operations at the state and federal level had an effect on IPC as well as its neighbors. In Iowa, the legislature passed laws making it easier for cities to establish municipal utilities (and indeed, there are over 100 municipal utilities in Iowa today). By 1940, the state of Nebraska had passed laws that forced all investor-owned utilities in that state to sell their operations to one of several "public power districts" operating in that state.
</p><p>During the 1940s, IPC divested itself of all its operations that were not part of its main territory in southern MN / northern Iowa. The largest chunk of territory was that operating in northeast ND / northern MN / Emerson, Manitoba which was sold in summer 1943 and fall 1944. The SD properties (around Winner) were sold in 1946. It would not be until 1956 when the last isolated district — the area around Waconia, MN — was sold to <a href="/wiki/Northern_States_Power" class="mw-redirect" title="Northern States Power">Northern States Power</a>.
</p><p>As IPC sold its properties that were isolated from the main system, it picked up others that were adjacent to its system and more easily interconnected: the Eastern Iowa Power system, and the Iowa / Minnesota properties of the Central States Power and Light Corporation.
</p><p>The Wisconsin operations were sold to Wisconsin Power and Light (now the eastern half of Alliant's operations).
</p><p>The other part of IPL — IES Utilities — incorporated in Iowa in 1925 as the Iowa Railway and Light Corporation and was later known as Iowa Electric Light and Power Company. IES expanded across that part of Iowa south of IPC's territory, eventually acquiring the Iowa operations of the <a href="/wiki/Ameren" title="Ameren">Union Electric Company</a> of St. Louis, MO.
</p><p>IES and IPC merged in the mid-1990s to form IPL.<sup id="cite_ref-Alliant-Energy-Jun-2002-U-1/A_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alliant-Energy-Jun-2002-U-1/A-6">[6]</a></sup> Today, IPL provides electricity and gas to communities in Iowa, while WPL serves southern and central Wisconsin.<sup id="cite_ref-Alliant-Energy-Nov-2012-10-Q_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alliant-Energy-Nov-2012-10-Q-5">[5]</a></sup>
In late 2007, Alliant Energy received final approval to sell their utility services in Illinois to Jo-Carroll Energy.
</p><p>In 2000, the utility purchased the <a href="/wiki/Naming_rights" title="Naming rights">naming rights</a> to the campus of the <a href="/wiki/Dane_County,_Wisconsin" title="Dane County, Wisconsin">Dane County</a> Expo Center just southwest of downtown <a href="/wiki/Madison,_Wisconsin" title="Madison, Wisconsin">Madison</a>, including the county-owned arena, which were all renamed the <a href="/wiki/Alliant_Energy_Center" title="Alliant Energy Center">Alliant Energy Center</a>.
</p><p>In the 1970s IES constructed the <a href="/wiki/Duane_Arnold_Energy_Center" title="Duane Arnold Energy Center">Duane Arnold Energy Center</a>, a 615 <a href="/wiki/Watt#Multiples" title="Watt">MW</a> nuclear plant in <a href="/wiki/Palo,_Iowa" title="Palo, Iowa">Palo, Iowa</a>.  In 2006, Alliant closed a deal with <a href="/wiki/Florida_Power_%26_Light" title="Florida Power &amp; Light">FPL Energy</a> to sell their stake in the plant.<sup id="cite_ref-Alliant-Energy-Mar-2006-10-K_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alliant-Energy-Mar-2006-10-K-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>Alliant Energy also provides several non-regulated services, including ground transportation and energy engineering (such as wind and geothermal energy).
</p><p>In 2007 it acquired 200 MW Buffalo Creek <a href="/wiki/Wind_Farm" class="mw-redirect" title="Wind Farm">Wind Farm</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hampton,_Iowa" title="Hampton, Iowa">Hampton, Iowa</a> from <a href="/wiki/Wind_Capital_Group" title="Wind Capital Group">Wind Capital Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>Alliant Energy sold to ITC Holdings their transmission system (34.5 kV, 69 kV, 115 kV, 161 kV and 345 kV) in Iowa and Minnesota in 2007.
</p><p>In 2015, the company finalized the sale of its Minnesota territory to a consortium of 12 electric cooperatives operating in that state.
</p><p>In 2016, Alliant began offering a $500 rebate for its customers who purchase a home charging station for <a href="/wiki/Electric_vehicle" title="Electric vehicle">electric vehicles</a>. To encourage the use of electric vehicles in Madison, Wisconsin, the company also offered free public charging at its Madison office.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

Grassley is often referred to as the "father" of American wind energy because of his work on the federal renewable energy production tax credit, according to Kampling.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
