class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10-K-1">[1]</a></sup></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" style="padding-right: 0.5em;">Website</th><td style="line-height: 1.35em;"><span class="url"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://hilton.com/">hilton<wbr></wbr>.com</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table><p>Hilton is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Tysons,_Virginia" title="Tysons, Virginia">Tysons Corner, Virginia</a>. As of March 31, 2019, its portfolio includes 5,757 properties (including timeshare properties) with 923,110 rooms in 113 countries and territories, including 689 that are managed and 4,947 that are franchised, with the combined managed and franchised properties having a total of 893,494 rooms, in addition to 68 that are owned or leased including 59 that are wholly owned or leased, one owned by a consolidated non-wholly owned entity, two that are leased by consolidated <a href="/wiki/Variable_interest_entity" title="Variable interest entity">variable interest entities</a> (VIEs) and six that are owned or leased by unconsolidated affiliates.<sup id="cite_ref-Q1-2019_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Q1-2019-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Fact_sheet_December_2017_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fact_sheet_December_2017-3">[3]</a></sup> Prior to their December 2013 <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">IPO</a>, Hilton was ranked as the 36th largest <a href="/wiki/Privately_held_company" title="Privately held company">privately held company</a> in the United States by <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>Hilton has 15 brands across different market segments, including <a href="/wiki/Conrad_Hotels" title="Conrad Hotels">Conrad Hotels &amp; Resorts</a>, Canopy by Hilton, <a href="/wiki/Curio,_A_Collection_by_Hilton" class="mw-redirect" title="Curio, A Collection by Hilton">Curio - A Collection by Hilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hilton_Hotels_%26_Resorts" title="Hilton Hotels &amp; Resorts">Hilton Hotels &amp; Resorts</a>, <a href="/wiki/DoubleTree" title="DoubleTree">DoubleTree by Hilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Embassy_Suites_Hotels" class="mw-redirect" title="Embassy Suites Hotels">Embassy Suites Hotels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hilton_Garden_Inn" title="Hilton Garden Inn">Hilton Garden Inn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hampton_by_Hilton" title="Hampton by Hilton">Hampton by Hilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Homewood_Suites_by_Hilton" title="Homewood Suites by Hilton">Homewood Suites by Hilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Home2_Suites_by_Hilton" title="Home2 Suites by Hilton">Home2 Suites by Hilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hilton_Grand_Vacations" title="Hilton Grand Vacations">Hilton Grand Vacations</a>, <a href="/wiki/Waldorf_Astoria_Hotels_%26_Resorts" title="Waldorf Astoria Hotels &amp; Resorts">Waldorf Astoria Hotels &amp; Resorts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tru_by_Hilton" title="Tru by Hilton">Tru by Hilton</a>, Tapestry Collection by Hilton, and Motto by Hilton.
</p><p>On December 12, 2013, Hilton again became a public company, raising an estimated $2.35 billion in its second IPO.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> At the time, <a href="/wiki/The_Blackstone_Group" title="The Blackstone Group">The Blackstone Group</a> held a 45.8 percent stake in the company.<sup id="cite_ref-Securities_and_Exchange_Commission_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Securities_and_Exchange_Commission-6">[6]</a></sup> In October 2016, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>'s <a href="/wiki/HNA_Group" title="HNA Group">HNA Group</a> agreed to acquire a 25 percent equity interest in Hilton from Blackstone. The transaction was expected to close in the first quarter of 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Hilton's largest stockholders were until mid 2018 HNA Group, Blackstone, and <a href="/wiki/Wellington_Management_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Wellington Management Group">Wellington Management Group</a>, which own 25%, 15.2%, and 6.7% of Hilton common stock respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-DEF14A_2017_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DEF14A_2017-10">[10]</a></sup>Hilton Hotels are now a stand-alone company.
</p><p>Hilton was founded by <a href="/wiki/Conrad_Hilton" title="Conrad Hilton">Conrad Hilton</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cisco,_Texas" title="Cisco, Texas">Cisco, Texas</a>, in 1919 and had its headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills,_California" title="Beverly Hills, California">Beverly Hills, California</a>, from 1969 until 2009. In August 2009, the company moved to Tysons Corner, unincorporated <a href="/wiki/Fairfax_County,_Virginia" title="Fairfax County, Virginia">Fairfax County, Virginia</a>, near <a href="/wiki/McLean,_Virginia" title="McLean, Virginia">McLean</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TysonsMap_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TysonsMap-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hilton_Worldwide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>In 1919, <a href="/wiki/Conrad_Hilton" title="Conrad Hilton">Conrad Hilton</a> purchased his first hotel, the 40-room Mobley Hotel in <a href="/wiki/Cisco,_Texas" title="Cisco, Texas">Cisco, Texas</a>, and bought additional Texas hotels as years passed.<sup id="cite_ref-hiltonworldwide.com_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiltonworldwide.com-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In 1925, the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Hilton" title="Dallas Hilton">Dallas Hilton</a> became the first hotel to use the Hilton name.<sup id="cite_ref-hiltonworldwide.com_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hiltonworldwide.com-14">[14]</a></sup> In 1927, Hilton expanded to <a href="/wiki/Waco,_Texas" title="Waco, Texas">Waco, Texas</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Sawyer_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sawyer-15">[15]</a></sup> where he opened the first hotel with air-conditioning in public areas and cold running water.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In 1943, Hilton purchased the <a href="/wiki/The_Roosevelt_Hotel_(Manhattan)" title="The Roosevelt Hotel (Manhattan)">Roosevelt Hotel</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Hotel" title="Plaza Hotel">Plaza Hotel</a>, both well-established high-end luxury hotels less than a mile apart in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Midtown_Manhattan" title="Midtown Manhattan">Midtown Manhattan</a> neighborhood. With this pair of acquisitions Hilton established the first hospitality company to span the contiguous United States.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The company incorporated in 1946 as the Hilton Hotels Corporation, and subsequently began public trading of shares on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-22">[22]</a></sup> In 1947, the Roosevelt Hotel became the first hotel in the world to have <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">televisions</a> in its rooms.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p><br></p><p>Hilton International was founded a few years later, in 1949, with the opening of the <a href="/wiki/Caribe_Hilton_Hotel" title="Caribe Hilton Hotel">Caribe Hilton Hotel</a> in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Barman Ramon "Monchito" Marreno claimed he created the <a href="/wiki/Pi%C3%B1a_colada" title="Piña colada">piña colada cocktail</a> at this resort.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-22">[22]</a></sup>  Hilton purchased the <a href="/wiki/Waldorf_Astoria_New_York" title="Waldorf Astoria New York">Waldorf Astoria New York</a> in the same year.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Statler_Hotel" class="mw-redirect" title="Statler Hotel">Hotels Statler Company</a> was acquired in 1954 for $111 million in what was then the world's most expensive real estate transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> One year later, Hilton created the world's first central reservations office, titled "HILCRON". The reservations team in 1955 consisted of eight members on staff booking reservations for any of Hilton's then 28 hotels. Reservations agents used the "availability board" to track records. The chalk board measured 30 feet (9.1 m) by 6 feet (1.8 m) and allowed HILCRON to make over 6,000 reservations in 1955.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Bookings could be made for any Hilton via telephone, <a href="/wiki/Telegram" class="mw-redirect" title="Telegram">telegram</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Teleprinter" title="Teleprinter">Teletype</a>.
</p><p>Later in 1955, Hilton launched a program to ensure every hotel room would include <a href="/wiki/Air_conditioning" title="Air conditioning">air conditioning</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Taraborrelli_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Taraborrelli-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>In late 1955, Hilton opened the first post–World War II property in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul, Turkey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Taraborrelli_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Taraborrelli-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hamblin_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hamblin-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>Hilton is credited with pioneering the <a href="/wiki/Airport_hotel" class="mw-redirect" title="Airport hotel">airport hotel</a> concept with the opening of the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a> Airport Hilton in 1959.<sup id="cite_ref-King_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-King-32">[32]</a></sup></p><p>In 1965, Hilton launched Lady Hilton, the first hotel concept created specifically for women guests.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> To appeal to female travelers, a number of properties offered floors occupied by only women along with distinct amenities for their usage.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p><p>In 1969, the first DoubleTree Hotel opened. However, Hilton was not affiliated with the brand until its acquisition of the parent company in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p><p>Hilton purchased the <a href="/wiki/Flamingo_Las_Vegas" title="Flamingo Las Vegas">Flamingo Las Vegas</a> in 1970,<sup id="cite_ref-Rothman_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rothman-38">[38]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Al_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Al-39">[39]</a></sup> which would become the first in the domestic gaming business to be listed on the NYSE.<sup id="cite_ref-Guest_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guest-40">[40]</a></sup></p><p>In 1979, founder Conrad Hilton died at the age of 91.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> Hilton Hotels Corporation later created the Conrad Hotels brand in honor of Hilton.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p><p>Hilton Honors (formerly Hilton HHonors), the company's guest loyalty program, was initiated in 1987.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> In 1994, the Honors surpassed competing hotel <a href="/wiki/Loyalty_program" title="Loyalty program">loyalty programs</a> by offering members both hotel credit points and <a href="/wiki/Frequent-flyer_program" title="Frequent-flyer program">airline credit miles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Collis_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Collis-44">[44]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Reynolds_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reynolds-45">[45]</a></sup></p><p>The company has been a sponsor of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Olympic_%26_Paralympic_Committee" title="United States Olympic &amp; Paralympic Committee">United States Olympic Team</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Two_chains_with_one_name">Two chains with one name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hilton_Worldwide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Two chains with one name" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The company spun off its international operations into a separately traded company on December 1, 1964, known as "Hilton International Co." It was acquired in 1967 by <a href="/wiki/Trans_World_Corporation" title="Trans World Corporation">Trans World Corp.</a>, the holding company for <a href="/wiki/Trans_World_Airlines" title="Trans World Airlines">Trans World Airlines</a>. In 1986, it was sold to <a href="/wiki/UAL_Corporation" title="UAL Corporation">UAL Corp.</a>, the holding company for <a href="/wiki/United_Airlines" title="United Airlines">United Airlines</a>, which became Allegis Corp. in an attempt to re-incarnate itself as a full-service travel company, encompassing <a href="/wiki/Westin_Hotels" class="mw-redirect" title="Westin Hotels">Westin Hotels</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Hertz_Corporation" title="The Hertz Corporation">Hertz rental cars</a> in addition to Hilton International and United Airlines. In 1987, after a corporate <a href="/wiki/Putsch" class="mw-redirect" title="Putsch">putsch</a>, the renamed UAL Corp. sold Hilton International to <a href="/wiki/Ladbrokes" class="mw-redirect" title="Ladbrokes">Ladbroke Group plc</a>, a British leisure and gambling company, which, in May 1999, adopted the name "Hilton Group plc."<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hiltonhotelsheadquarters.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Hiltonhotelsheadquarters.jpg/220px-Hiltonhotelsheadquarters.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="168" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1265" data-file-height="966"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 168px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Hiltonhotelsheadquarters.jpg/220px-Hiltonhotelsheadquarters.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="168" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hiltonhotelsheadquarters.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The former Hilton Hotels Corporation headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills,_California" title="Beverly Hills, California">Beverly Hills, California</a></div></div></div>
<p>As a result, there were two separate, fully independent companies operating hotels under the Hilton name. Those Hilton Hotels outside the U.S. were, until 2006, styled as "Hilton International" hotels. Because the two chains were contractually forbidden to operate hotels in the other's territory under the Hilton name, for many years hotels run by Hilton International in the U.S. were called Vista International Hotels, while hotels operated by the American arm of Hilton outside the U.S. were named Conrad Hotels.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p><p>In 1997, to minimize longtime <a href="/wiki/Consumer_confusion" title="Consumer confusion">consumer confusion</a>, the American and British Hilton companies adopted a joint marketing agreement under which they shared the same logos, promoted each other's brands and maintained joint reservation systems. At that point, the Vista chain was phased out, while Conrad has been restyled as one of the luxury brands of Hilton (along with the <a href="/wiki/Waldorf_Astoria_Hotels_%26_Resorts" title="Waldorf Astoria Hotels &amp; Resorts">Waldorf-Astoria Collection</a>) and operates hotels within the U.S. as well as abroad.
</p><p>In 1971, Hilton acquired International Leisure Company, including the <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Hilton" class="mw-redirect" title="Las Vegas Hilton">Las Vegas Hilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flamingo_Hilton" class="mw-redirect" title="Flamingo Hilton">Flamingo Hilton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p><p>In 1998, Hilton spun off its gaming operations into a separate, publicly held company called Park Place Entertainment (later <a href="/wiki/Caesars_Entertainment,_Inc." title="Caesars Entertainment, Inc.">Caesars Entertainment, Inc.</a>)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In 1999, Hilton acquired <a href="/wiki/Promus_Hotel_Corporation" title="Promus Hotel Corporation">Promus Hotel Corporation</a>, which included the DoubleTree, <a href="/wiki/Red_Lion_Hotels" title="Red Lion Hotels">Red Lion</a>, Embassy Suites, Hampton Inn, and Homewood Suites brands.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="21st_century">21st century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hilton_Worldwide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 21st century" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:HiltonHotelsLogo.svg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/39/HiltonHotelsLogo.svg/220px-HiltonHotelsLogo.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="168" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="391"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 168px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/39/HiltonHotelsLogo.svg/220px-HiltonHotelsLogo.svg.png" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="168" data-srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/39/HiltonHotelsLogo.svg/330px-HiltonHotelsLogo.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/39/HiltonHotelsLogo.svg/440px-HiltonHotelsLogo.svg.png 2x" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:HiltonHotelsLogo.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hilton Hotels &amp; Resorts, Hilton's flagship brand</div></div></div>
<p>In 2001, Hilton agreed to sell Red Lion to WestCoast Hospitality.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p><p>On December 29, 2005, Hilton Hotels Corporation agreed to re-acquire the Hilton International chain from its British owner, Hilton Group plc, for £3.3 billion ($5.71 billion). As well as bringing the two Hilton companies back together as a single entity, this deal also included Hilton plc properties operating as Conrad Hotels, <a href="/wiki/Scandic_Hotels" title="Scandic Hotels">Scandic Hotels</a> and LivingWell Health Clubs.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> On February 23, 2006, the deal closed, making Hilton Hotels the world's fifth <a href="/wiki/List_of_chained-brand_hotels" title="List of chained-brand hotels">largest hotel operator</a> in number of rooms.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup>  Scandic Hotels was sold the next year on March 1 to <a href="/wiki/EQT_Partners" title="EQT Partners">EQT Partners</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Christopher_J_Nassetta,_President_%26_CEO,_Hilton_Worldwide_(14004654374).jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Christopher_J_Nassetta%2C_President_%26_CEO%2C_Hilton_Worldwide_%2814004654374%29.jpg/220px-Christopher_J_Nassetta%2C_President_%26_CEO%2C_Hilton_Worldwide_%2814004654374%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="5616" data-file-height="3744"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 147px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Christopher_J_Nassetta%2C_President_%26_CEO%2C_Hilton_Worldwide_%2814004654374%29.jpg/220px-Christopher_J_Nassetta%2C_President_%26_CEO%2C_Hilton_Worldwide_%2814004654374%29.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="147" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Christopher_J_Nassetta,_President_%26_CEO,_Hilton_Worldwide_(14004654374).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Christopher J. Nassetta in 2004</div></div></div>
<p>On July 3, 2007, Hilton Hotels Corp. agreed to an all-cash buyout from the <a href="/wiki/The_Blackstone_Group" title="The Blackstone Group">Blackstone Group LP</a> in a $26 billion (including debt) deal that would make Blackstone the world's largest hotel owner.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> At $47.50 per share, the buyout price was 32 percent higher than the closing value of a share of Hilton stock on July 3.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> The deal was the culmination of a year of on and off discussions with Blackstone.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> In October 2007, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Christopher_J._Nassetta&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Christopher J. Nassetta (page does not exist)">Christopher J. Nassetta</a> was appointed President and Chief Executive Officer of Hilton.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> In February 2009, Hilton Hotels Corp. announced that its headquarters were moving from Beverly Hills, California to Fairfax County, Virginia.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p><p>While Blackstone saw an opportunity to streamline the company and push Hilton's expansion overseas when Blackstone pursued Hilton in 2006 and 2007, the buyout saddled the company with $20 billion of debt just as the economy was turning down. The debt had very liberal terms, so there was no danger of default, but when travel slowed, the company suffered. In April 2010, Hilton and Blackstone restructured the debt. Blackstone invested a further $800 million of equity and the debt was reduced to $16 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p><p>Hilton returned to being a public company on December 12, 2013. This second IPO in the company's history raised an estimated $2.35 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> The Blackstone Group retained a 45.8% stake in the company.<sup id="cite_ref-Securities_and_Exchange_Commission_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Securities_and_Exchange_Commission-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>The company announced in February 2016 that Hilton would turn its hotel holdings into a <a href="/wiki/Real_estate_investment_trust" title="Real estate investment trust">real estate investment trust</a>. Prior to making the announcement, the company went to the IRS for approval.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2016, Hilton announced its intention to spin off its timeshare and real estate businesses, creating three independent public companies.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> The spin-offs of <a href="/wiki/Park_Hotels_%26_Resorts" title="Park Hotels &amp; Resorts">Park Hotels &amp; Resorts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hilton_Grand_Vacations" title="Hilton Grand Vacations">Hilton Grand Vacations</a> were completed in January 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> As of 2018, the company is a fully independent publicly traded company (just like in the pre-buyout days)  after the exits of Blackstone and HNA.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup></p>

<p>As of 2019, about 97% of the rooms branded under Hilton were managed or franchised to independent operators and companies. During its 2007–2013 ownership of Hilton, Blackstone Group pursued a strategy of predominantly expanding Hilton's reach through franchise agreements, while relatively few new properties were actually operated by Hilton. Hence, the proportion of franchised rooms grew significantly during this period.<sup id="cite_ref-Karmin_69-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Karmin-69">[69]</a></sup>  The practice of franchising is popular within the <a href="/wiki/Hospitality_industry" title="Hospitality industry">hospitality industry</a> among most major hotel chains, as the parent company does not have to pay for the maintenance and overhead costs of franchised properties. Franchisees must follow strict brand standards in order to maintain a licensing agreement with Hilton.<sup id="cite_ref-Karmin_69-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Karmin-69">[69]</a></sup> Many of Hilton's flagship properties, airport properties, and largest resorts, however, are corporately managed.

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Hilton_Honors">Hilton Honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hilton_Worldwide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Hilton Honors" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Hilton Honors (formerly Hilton HHonors) is Hilton's guest <a href="/wiki/Loyalty_program" title="Loyalty program">loyalty program</a>, through which frequent guests can accumulate points and airline miles by staying within the Hilton portfolio. The program is one of the largest of its type, with approximately 71 million members.<sup id="cite_ref-Fact_sheet_December_2017_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fact_sheet_December_2017-3">[3]</a></sup>  There are four levels of elite status within the Hilton Honors program including Member, Silver, Gold and Diamond.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup></p><p>Hilton renamed the Hilton HHonors program to Hilton Honors in February 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup></p>

<p>According to Careerbliss.com, Hilton ranked first in the list of "2012 Happiest Companies in America", with a score of 4.36 out of 5.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> The survey looked at job reviews from more than 100,000 employees, with such characteristics life as work-life balance, company culture and reputation, and the relationships employees have with their bosses.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup>  Hilton has scored 100% on the <a href="/wiki/Corporate_Equality_Index" title="Corporate Equality Index">Corporate Equality Index</a> each year from 2014 to 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup></p><p>In 2016, Hilton was named one of the "World's 25 Best Multinational Workplaces" by <i><a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)">Fortune</a></i> and Great Place to Work.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, <i>Fortune</i> ranked Hilton number 26 in their list of "The 100 Best Companies to Work For".<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup></p><p>In 2019, <i>Fortune</i> ranked Hilton number 1 in their list of "The 100 Best Companies to Work For".<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup></p>

<li>In 1971, <i><a href="/wiki/Diamonds_Are_Forever_(film)" title="Diamonds Are Forever (film)">Diamonds Are Forever</a></i> was filmed at the <a href="/wiki/Westgate_Las_Vegas_Resort_%26_Casino" class="mw-redirect" title="Westgate Las Vegas Resort &amp; Casino">Las Vegas Hilton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup></li>
<li>On April 3, 1973, Dr. <a href="/wiki/Martin_Cooper_(inventor)" title="Martin Cooper (inventor)">Martin Cooper</a> made the world's first cell phone call in front of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Hilton_Midtown" title="New York Hilton Midtown">New York Hilton Midtown</a>. A press conference was held at the hotel later that day to mark the milestone.<sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup></li>
<li>In 1976, during a music tour, <a href="/wiki/Ike_Turner" title="Ike Turner">Ike</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tina_Turner" title="Tina Turner">Tina Turner</a> were staying at the Statler Hilton (now the <a href="/wiki/The_Statler_Hotel_%26_Residences" title="The Statler Hotel &amp; Residences">Statler Hotel &amp; Residences</a>) in downtown <a href="/wiki/Dallas,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Dallas, Texas">Dallas</a> when Tina decided to leave Ike. Hotel management has since renovated the room.<sup id="cite_ref-Kennedy_93-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kennedy-93">[93]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup></li>
<li>In 1995, the <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">James Bond</a> movie <i><a href="/wiki/GoldenEye" title="GoldenEye">GoldenEye</a></i> was filmed at the <a href="/wiki/Langham_Hilton" class="mw-redirect" title="Langham Hilton">Langham Hilton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-95" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-96" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-96">[96]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/The_Insider_(film)" title="The Insider (film)">The Insider</a></i> was filmed in 1999 at the <a href="/wiki/Seelbach_Hilton" class="mw-redirect" title="Seelbach Hilton">Seelbach Hilton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97">[97]</a></sup></li>
