Established in 1985, the company primarily operates media properties serving members of "passionate" audiences.

<li><a href="/wiki/Lionsgate" title="Lionsgate">Lionsgate</a> (3.05%)</li></ul></div></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" style="padding-right: 0.5em;">Website</th><td style="line-height: 1.35em;"><span class="url"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://corporate.discovery.com">corporate<wbr></wbr>.discovery<wbr></wbr>.com</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table><p>The company's core operations consist of a group of non-scripted and <a href="/wiki/Factual_television" title="Factual television">factual television</a> brands, such as the namesake <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Channel" title="Discovery Channel">Discovery Channel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Animal_Planet" title="Animal Planet">Animal Planet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Science_Channel" title="Science Channel">Science Channel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/TLC_(TV_network)" title="TLC (TV network)">TLC</a>. In 2018, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Scripps_Networks_Interactive" title="Scripps Networks Interactive">Scripps Networks Interactive</a>, adding networks such as <a href="/wiki/Food_Network" title="Food Network">Food Network</a>, <a href="/wiki/HGTV" title="HGTV">HGTV</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Travel_Channel" title="Travel Channel">Travel Channel</a> to its portfolio.
</p><p>Discovery also owns or has interests in local versions of its channel brands in international markets, in addition to its other major regional operations such as <a href="/wiki/Eurosport" title="Eurosport">Eurosport</a> (a pan-European group of sports channels), <a href="/wiki/GolfTV" title="GolfTV">GolfTV</a> (an international <a href="/wiki/Golf" title="Golf">golf</a>-focused streaming service, which is the international digital rightsholder of the <a href="/wiki/PGA_Tour" title="PGA Tour">PGA Tour</a>), <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Communications_Nordic" title="Discovery Communications Nordic">Discovery Communications Nordic</a> (which operates general-interest channels in <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> and <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>), <a href="/wiki/TVN_Group" title="TVN Group">TVN Group</a> in <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, and a portfolio of various free-to-air channels in Germany, Italy, and the United Kingdom.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Discovery,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Discovery_Communications">Discovery Communications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Discovery,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Discovery Communications" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Discovery_Communications.svg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/c7/Discovery_Communications.svg/200px-Discovery_Communications.svg.png" decoding="async" width="200" height="44" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="180" data-file-height="40"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 200px;height: 44px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/c7/Discovery_Communications.svg/200px-Discovery_Communications.svg.png" data-alt="" data-width="200" data-height="44" data-srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/c7/Discovery_Communications.svg/300px-Discovery_Communications.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/c7/Discovery_Communications.svg/400px-Discovery_Communications.svg.png 2x" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Discovery_Communications.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Logo as Discovery Communications used from 2007 until the acquisition of Scripps Networks in 2018. <p>The company's namesake and flagship brand, <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Channel" title="Discovery Channel">Discovery Channel</a>, first launched on June 17, 1985.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1991, Discovery Channel's owners acquired <a href="/wiki/TLC_(TV_network)" title="TLC (TV network)">The Learning Channel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In October 1996, Discovery launched several new spin-off networks, including <a href="/wiki/Animal_Planet" title="Animal Planet">Animal Planet</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Digital_cable" title="Digital cable">digital cable</a> channels <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Family" title="Discovery Family">Discovery Kids</a>, <a href="/wiki/Destination_America" title="Destination America">Discovery Travel &amp; Living</a>, <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Civilization" class="mw-redirect" title="Discovery Civilization">Discovery Civilization</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Science_Channel" title="Science Channel">Science Channel</a>. This was followed by the 1997 purchase of a 70% stake in <a href="/wiki/Travel_Channel" title="Travel Channel">Travel Channel</a>, and the 1998 launches of <a href="/wiki/Discovery_en_Espa%C3%B1ol" title="Discovery en Español">Discovery en Español</a>, <a href="/wiki/Military_Channel" class="mw-redirect" title="Military Channel">Discovery Wings</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Health_Channel" title="Discovery Health Channel">Discovery Health Channel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-blueskies_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blueskies-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Also in 1998, Discovery acquired a stake in the struggling CBS Eye on People channel; Discovery eventually acquired the remainder of CBS's stake, leading to its January 1999 re-launch as <a href="/wiki/Discovery_People" title="Discovery People">Discovery People</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The network quietly folded in 2000, being replaced by other Discovery channels on providers.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>On September 1, 2001, Discovery Communications bought The Health Channel, and announced that it would be re-branded as <a href="/wiki/FitTV" title="FitTV">FitTV</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 2002, Discovery re-launched Discovery Civilization as Discovery Times, as part of a joint venture with <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In June 2002, coinciding with Discovery's 17th anniversary, the company launched a 24/7 <a href="/wiki/High-definition_television" title="High-definition television">high definition</a> channel known as <a href="/wiki/Discovery_HD_Theater" class="mw-redirect" title="Discovery HD Theater">Discovery HD Theater</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In 2003, Discovery Communications moved its headquarters from <a href="/wiki/Bethesda,_Maryland" title="Bethesda, Maryland">Bethesda</a> to <a href="/wiki/Silver_Spring,_Maryland" title="Silver Spring, Maryland">Silver Spring</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In 2003 and 2004, Discovery acquired academic film companies such as AGC, AIMS Multimedia, and Clearvue &amp; SVE to form Discovery Education, which is now led by Bill Goodwyn.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In 2019, Discovery Communications moved its headquarters to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="New York City, New York">New York City</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> keeping 300 employees in a smaller hub in Silver Spring.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Zaslav_era">Zaslav era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Discovery,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Zaslav era" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Former <a href="/wiki/NBC_Universal" class="mw-redirect" title="NBC Universal">NBC Universal</a> executive <a href="/wiki/David_Zaslav" title="David Zaslav">David Zaslav</a> was named president and CEO of Discovery Communications on November 16, 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In the years that followed, Zaslav placed a major focus on bolstering Discovery's core networks, programming, and expanding the company's reach into <a href="/wiki/Digital_media" title="Digital media">digital media</a>. He described these goals as reflecting an expansion into becoming a "content company" rather than just a "cable company".<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2007,  Discovery sold its stake in Travel Channel back to <a href="/wiki/Cox_Communications" title="Cox Communications">Cox Communications</a>, in exchange for the stake in Discovery that Cox owned.<sup id="cite_ref-bandc-dissellstc_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bandc-dissellstc-23">[23]</a></sup> Cox would later sell the controlling interest in the channel to <a href="/wiki/Scripps_Networks_Interactive" title="Scripps Networks Interactive">Scripps Networks Interactive</a> in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-variety-scrippsacquirestc_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-variety-scrippsacquirestc-24">[24]</a></sup> In June 2008, Discovery Home was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Planet_Green" class="mw-redirect" title="Planet Green">Planet Green</a>, a network devoted primarily to environmentalism and <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_living" title="Sustainable living">sustainable living</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt-planetgreen_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-planetgreen-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>In 2007, according to <a href="/wiki/Crunchbase" title="Crunchbase">Crunchbase</a>, <a href="/wiki/TreeHugger" title="TreeHugger">TreeHugger</a> was acquired by Discovery Communications on August 1, 2007, for $10 million.<sup id="cite_ref-crunchbase_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-crunchbase-26">[26]</a></sup> In 2012, <a href="/wiki/Mother_Nature_Network" title="Mother Nature Network">Mother Nature Network</a>, founded by Joel Babbit and Chuck Leavell (now Narrative Content Group) acquired TreeHugger.
</p><p>On January 15, 2008, Discovery announced that it had entered into a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey" title="Oprah Winfrey">Oprah Winfrey</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Harpo_Productions" title="Harpo Productions">Harpo Productions</a> to re-launch Discovery Health as a new service, <a href="/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey_Network_(U.S._TV_channel)" class="mw-redirect" title="Oprah Winfrey Network (U.S. TV channel)">OWN: The Oprah Winfrey Network</a>, in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-wp-ownannounce_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wp-ownannounce-27">[27]</a></sup> In 2008, Discovery Times was re-launched as <a href="/wiki/Investigation_Discovery" title="Investigation Discovery">Investigation Discovery</a>, a new brand that would be dedicated to <a href="/wiki/True_crime" title="True crime">true crime</a> programs and documentaries.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> On April 30, 2009, Discovery announced a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Hasbro" title="Hasbro">Hasbro</a> to re-launch Discovery Kids as a new youth- and family-oriented entertainment channel.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The channel, ultimately named <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Family" title="Discovery Family">The Hub</a>, launched on October 10, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-owens1_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-owens1-31">[31]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nyt-hubreboot_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-hubreboot-32">[32]</a></sup> After multiple delays, OWN officially launched on January 1, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-nyp-owndelay_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyp-owndelay-33">[33]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-np-ownlaunch_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-np-ownlaunch-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>On March 17, 2009, Discovery Communications sued <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon.com">Amazon.com</a> for <a href="/wiki/Patent_infringement" title="Patent infringement">patent infringement</a> by its <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Kindle" title="Amazon Kindle">Kindle</a> <a href="/wiki/E-reader" title="E-reader">e-reader</a> line, regarding "secure distribution of electronic text and graphics to subscribers and secure storage". The patents were originally developed by Discovery founder <a href="/wiki/John_Hendricks" title="John Hendricks">John Hendricks</a>, who had been developing technologies related to <a href="/wiki/E-book" title="E-book">e-books</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Digitization" title="Digitization">digitization</a> of television programs. While Discovery had divested the television-related patents, it retained the e-book patents.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Amazon subsequently accused Discovery of violating a patent for an "Internet-based customer referral system";<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The two parties settled in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p><p>On October 4 of 2011, due to the wider implementation of high-definition feeds for mainstream cable channels, HD Theater was re-launched as <a href="/wiki/Velocity_(TV_network)" class="mw-redirect" title="Velocity (TV network)">Velocity</a>, a new "upscale male" network focusing on automotive programming.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> On May 28, 2012, Planet Green (which had begun to abandon its original concept in 2010 due to poor viewership)<sup id="cite_ref-nyt-planetgreen_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-planetgreen-25">[25]</a></sup> was re-launched as <a href="/wiki/Destination_America" title="Destination America">Destination America</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2014, Discovery launched <a href="/wiki/Curiosity_(website)" title="Curiosity (website)">Curiosity</a>, a website that aggregates <a href="/wiki/Online_education" class="mw-redirect" title="Online education">online education</a> content.<sup id="cite_ref-VBJan14_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VBJan14-42">[42]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2014, Discovery and its shareholder <a href="/wiki/Liberty_Media" title="Liberty Media">Liberty Media</a> acquired British television studio <a href="/wiki/All3Media" title="All3Media">All3Media</a> for $930 million in a 50/50 joint venture. The new ownership stated that All3Media would be operated as an independent company.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> In October 2014, Discovery acquired controlling interest in Hub Network from Hasbro and re-branded it as <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Family" title="Discovery Family">Discovery Family</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Hub_to_Discovery_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hub_to_Discovery-44">[44]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Discovery_Family_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Discovery_Family-45">[45]</a></sup>  In November 2014, Curiosity was spun out as a venture-funded <a href="/wiki/Startup_company" title="Startup company">startup</a>, receiving $6 million in funding.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2015, Discovery launched Discovery Go, a <a href="/wiki/TV_Everywhere" title="TV Everywhere">TV Everywhere</a> service offering access to live streaming and on-demand content from Discovery Communications' cable networks.<sup id="cite_ref-mcn-discoverygo_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mcn-discoverygo-47">[47]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2016, Discovery initiated a restructuring plan aiming to save $40 to $60 million by the third quarter of 2016, including a shift in strategy to "maximize" its linear television business whilst plotting larger investments in content, digital media, sports, and international markets.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> In August 2016, Discovery purchased a minority stake in the Hong Kong-based digital talent and content company VS Media; Discovery intended to have VS distribute <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Digital_Networks" title="Discovery Digital Networks">Discovery Digital Networks</a> content in China, and to offer their own resources to VS.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2016, Discovery purchased a minority stake in <a href="/wiki/Group_Nine_Media" title="Group Nine Media">Group Nine Media</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Digital_media" title="Digital media">digital media</a> holding company consisting of <a href="/wiki/Thrillist_Media_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Thrillist Media Group">Thrillist Media Group</a>, <a href="/wiki/NowThis" class="mw-redirect" title="NowThis">NowThis</a>, The Dodo and Discovery's digital network Seeker—for $100 million. The transaction gave Discovery an option to acquire a majority stake at a later date.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Discovery.2C_Inc."></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Discovery,_Inc.">Discovery, Inc.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Discovery,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Discovery, Inc." data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On July 31, 2017, Discovery announced it would acquire <a href="/wiki/Scripps_Networks_Interactive" title="Scripps Networks Interactive">Scripps Networks Interactive</a>, owner of networks such as <a href="/wiki/Food_Network" title="Food Network">Food Network</a> and <a href="/wiki/HGTV" title="HGTV">HGTV</a>, for $14.6 billion, pending regulatory approval.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> On March 6, 2018, the acquisition was completed with the company renamed <b>Discovery, Inc.</b> afterwards. At this time, SNI shareholders own 20% of Discovery's stock.<sup id="cite_ref-thr-snicompleted_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thr-snicompleted-55">[55]</a></sup> Discovery will retain an operational hub in SNI's home city of <a href="/wiki/Knoxville" class="mw-redirect" title="Knoxville">Knoxville</a>, but planned to move its corporate headquarters from Silver Spring, Maryland, where it has operated since 2003, to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> in late-2019.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> Following the acquisition, Discovery began to integrate personalities from the two companies into each other's programming, and began to produce new programs (such as a revival of TLC's <i><a href="/wiki/While_You_Were_Out" title="While You Were Out">While You Were Out</a></i> in conjunction with HGTV, and a competition series between the stars of Food Network's <i><a href="/wiki/Ace_of_Cakes" title="Ace of Cakes">Ace of Cakes</a></i> and TLC's <i><a href="/wiki/Cake_Boss" title="Cake Boss">Cake Boss</a></i>) to serve as vehicles for <a href="/wiki/Crossover_(fiction)" title="Crossover (fiction)">crossovers</a> between personalities from Discovery and SNI's networks.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p><p>Following the completion of the purchase, Discovery's brands were divided into two groups under different <a href="/wiki/Chief_brand_officer" title="Chief brand officer">chief brand officers</a>: SNI's chief programmer Kathleen Finch became chief brand officer of Lifestyle, overseeing the six former Scripps channels, in addition to Discovery's lifestyle networks such as TLC. <a href="/wiki/Rich_Ross" title="Rich Ross">Rich Ross</a>, who formerly served as group president of Discovery and Science, stepped down, and would be replaced by TLC president Nancy Daniels as chief brand officer of Discovery &amp; Factual.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p><p>On August 3, 2017, Discovery announced that it would contribute Velocity into a joint venture with the digital, live events, and direct-to-consumer businesses of automotive publisher TEN: The Enthusiast Network. Discovery will hold a majority stake in the venture; it will not include TEN's print brands, but there will be opportunities for cross-promotion.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> In April 2018, it was announced that TEN had been renamed <a href="/wiki/Motor_Trend_Group" title="Motor Trend Group">Motor Trend Group</a>, and that Velocity would be re-branded as an extension of its namesake magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Motor_Trend" title="Motor Trend">Motor Trend</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2017, Discovery announced that it would acquire an additional 24.5% stake in OWN that it did not already own, for $70 million. Oprah Winfrey will remain as CEO of OWN, and extended her exclusivity agreement with the network and Discovery through 2025. Harpo Productions retains a "significant minority stake" in the venture.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> In February 2018, Discovery sold a majority stake in Discovery Education to the private equity firm Francisco Partners.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p><p>During its 2018 upfronts, Zaslav stated that the company was now strongly focused on serving "passionate fans and passionate audiences", and was preparing to increase its focus on direct-to-consumer offerings targeting such audiences. The SNI purchase, Eurosport's Olympics rights, and the aforementioned <i>Motor Trend</i>-branded network were described as being examples of this strategy.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-64">[64]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Food_Network" title="Food Network">Food Network</a>-branded DTC service would also launch in 2019, with a focus on live and on-demand cooking classes.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2018, Discovery announced a 12-year agreement to acquire the international media rights to the <a href="/wiki/PGA_Tour" title="PGA Tour">PGA Tour</a>, with plans to develop an international streaming service to house these rights.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> The service was officially announced in October 2018 as <a href="/wiki/GolfTV" title="GolfTV">GolfTV</a>, with Eurosport executive vice president Alex Kaplan named the president and general manager of Discovery Golf.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> Former Amazon executive Peter Faricy, who was named Discovery's chief executive of global direct-to-consumer operations in August 2018, considered GolfTV consistent with the aforementioned strategy, as golf was one of several sports "where people's passions and their interest in participating and learning more becomes a core part of their life."<sup id="cite_ref-spm_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-spm-68">[68]</a></sup></p><p>With Discovery choosing 230 Park Avenue South as its new New York City headquarters, Discovery sold its former Silver Spring headquarters to Foulger-Pratt and <a href="/wiki/Cerberus_Capital_Management" title="Cerberus Capital Management">Cerberus Capital Management</a> in September 2018, and leased a smaller space at nearby 8403 Colesville Road.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2018, Chip and Joanna Gaines of the former HGTV series <i><a href="/wiki/Fixer_Upper_(TV_series)" title="Fixer Upper (TV series)">Fixer Upper</a></i> announced on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_Starring_Jimmy_Fallon" title="The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon">The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon</a></i>, that they were in early talks to form a "lifestyle focused media network" with Discovery named after their personal company Magnolia. It was reported that Discovery had considered rebranding either <a href="/wiki/DIY_Network" title="DIY Network">DIY Network</a> or <a href="/wiki/Great_American_Country" title="Great American Country">Great American Country</a> as this new channel.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> Discovery in April 2019 confirmed its Magnolia joint venture, which is scheduled to launch in 2020 and replace DIY Network, with an over-the-top subscription component to launch later.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> The channel's launch date of October 4, 2020 was announced by Discovery CEO David Zaslav at the Television Critics Association presentation on January 1, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2019, Discovery increased its ownership of Play Sports Group, operator of the YouTube channel <a href="/wiki/Global_Cycling_Network" title="Global Cycling Network">Global Cycling Network</a>, to 71%. Discovery previously acquired a 20% share in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> The group is the foundation for an in development global cycling OTT service.<sup id="cite_ref-spm_68-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-spm-68">[68]</a></sup></p><p>On April 1, 2019, after a previous agreement with the broadcaster lapsed six years prior, Discovery announced a 10-year global content partnership with <a href="/wiki/BBC_Studios" title="BBC Studios">BBC Studios</a>, under which it will become the exclusive subscription video-on-demand rightsholder of programming from the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Natural_History_Unit" title="BBC Natural History Unit">BBC Natural History Unit</a> (which will become the basis of an international subscription service by 2020) outside of China, Ireland, and the UK. In addition, Discovery and the BBC reached an agreement to break up their <a href="/wiki/UKTV" title="UKTV">UKTV</a> joint venture (which was inherited from Scripps), with Discovery acquiring the BBC's shares in the <a href="/wiki/Good_Food" title="Good Food">Good Food</a>, <a href="/wiki/Home_(TV_channel)" class="mw-redirect" title="Home (TV channel)">Home</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Really_(TV_channel)" title="Really (TV channel)">Really</a> channels, and BBC Studios acquiring Discovery's shares in the seven remaining channels.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup></p><p>On May 13, 2019, Discovery announced that it would acquire <i><a href="/wiki/Golf_Digest" title="Golf Digest">Golf Digest</a></i> from <a href="/wiki/Cond%C3%A9_Nast" title="Condé Nast">Condé Nast</a> to integrate into its Discovery Golf division.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup></p>

As a result of the dividend, each holder of a share of the company's Series A common stock, Series B common stock or Series C common stock will receive one additional share of the company's Series C common stock on or about August 6, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup></p><p>Liberty Global and Discovery Communications paid approximately $195 million for a 3.4% stake in <a href="/wiki/Lionsgate" title="Lionsgate">Lionsgate Entertainment</a> in November 2015. <p>On September 1, 2010, the Discovery headquarters were the site of a hostage taking; a lone gunman identified as James J. Lee, armed with two <a href="/wiki/Starting_pistol" title="Starting pistol">starter pistols</a><sup id="cite_ref-CNNconvict_107-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNNconvict-107">[107]</a></sup> and an <a href="/wiki/Improvised_explosive_device" title="Improvised explosive device">explosive device</a>, took three people <a href="/wiki/Hostage_crisis" class="mw-redirect" title="Hostage crisis">hostage</a> inside of the Discovery Communications headquarters in Silver Spring, Maryland, prompting an evacuation of the building.
Lee's motive was believed to have been grounded in <a href="/wiki/Environmental_activism" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental activism">environmental activism</a>. Lee had previously been arrested in 2008 while protesting in front of the same site.
The National Consortium for the Study of Terrorism and Responses to Terrorism (START) at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Maryland" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Maryland">University of Maryland</a> has since labeled the crime a <a href="/wiki/Terrorism_in_the_United_States" title="Terrorism in the United States">terrorist attack</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Background_Report_108-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Background_Report-108">[108]</a></sup></p><p>The incident began at 1:00 p.m. <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Daylight_Time" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastern Daylight Time">ET</a>, when 43-year-old James Jay Lee entered the building with two starter pistols and fired a single round at the ceiling of the lobby.<sup id="cite_ref-Fox_109-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fox-109">[109]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CNN_110-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-110">[110]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Montgomery_County_Police_Department" title="Montgomery County Police Department">Montgomery County Police Department</a> (MCPD) confirmed that Lee had an explosive device and was holding three people<sup id="cite_ref-111" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-111">[111]</a></sup> hostage in the lobby.<sup id="cite_ref-Fox_109-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fox-109">[109]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NewYork_112-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NewYork-112">[112]</a></sup> The building was placed on lockdown and most of the 1,500 employees were evacuated.<sup id="cite_ref-NewYork_112-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NewYork-112">[112]</a></sup> Children from a day care center inside were safely removed to a nearby McDonald's restaurant on Colesville Rd.<sup id="cite_ref-Fox_109-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fox-109">[109]</a></sup>  Lee was shot dead by an MCPD SWAT team at 4:48 p.m. ET after the hostages made a run to escape.<sup id="cite_ref-Dan_Morse_113-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dan_Morse-113">[113]</a></sup> The remaining hostages were immediately freed.<sup id="cite_ref-114" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-114">[114]</a></sup> The incident was described by the FBI as the first instance of a would-be suicide bomber taking hostages in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-115" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-115">[115]</a></sup></p><p>James Jay Lee (c. 1967 – September 1, 2010) was an environmental protester who, in 2008, was given six months of <a href="/wiki/United_States_federal_probation_and_supervised_release" title="United States federal probation and supervised release">supervised probation</a> and fined $500 after he was arrested during a protest outside the Discovery Communications headquarters. 
Lee had published criticisms of the network in an online manifesto at Savetheplanetprotest.com,<sup id="cite_ref-CNN_110-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-110">[110]</a></sup> among which was a demand for the company to cease the broadcasting of television series displaying or encouraging the birth of "<a href="/wiki/Antinatalism" title="Antinatalism">parasitic human infants and the false heroics behind those actions</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-116" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-116">[116]</a></sup> His manifesto also railed against "immigration pollution and <a href="/wiki/Anchor_baby" title="Anchor baby">anchor baby</a> filth", leading commentators such as Mark Potok of the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Poverty_Law_Center" title="Southern Poverty Law Center">Southern Poverty Law Center</a> to decry Lee as an "<a href="/wiki/Eco-fascist" class="mw-redirect" title="Eco-fascist">eco-fascist</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-117" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-117">[117]</a></sup> Lee's opinions were dominated by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Robert_Malthus" title="Thomas Robert Malthus">Malthusian</a> analysis,<sup id="cite_ref-Andrew_Leonard_118-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Andrew_Leonard-118">[118]</a></sup> though he also cited works ranging from <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Quinn" title="Daniel Quinn">Daniel Quinn</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/My_Ishmael" title="My Ishmael">My Ishmael</a></i> to former U.S. Vice President <a href="/wiki/Al_Gore" title="Al Gore">Al Gore</a>'s documentary <i><a href="/wiki/An_Inconvenient_Truth" title="An Inconvenient Truth">An Inconvenient Truth</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Police_kill_119-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Police_kill-119">[119]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Washington_Post" title="The Washington Post">The Washington Post</a></i> credited the <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> community for initially breaking the story.<sup id="cite_ref-120" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-120">[120]</a></sup></p>
