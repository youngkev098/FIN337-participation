<p><b>Walgreens Boots Alliance, Inc.</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Chicago,_Illinois" class="mw-redirect" title="Chicago, Illinois">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>, that owns <a href="/wiki/Walgreens" title="Walgreens">Walgreens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boots_UK" class="mw-redirect" title="Boots UK">Boots</a>, and a number of pharmaceutical manufacturing, wholesale, and distribution companies. The company was formed on December 31, 2014, after Walgreens purchased the 55% stake in UK and Switzerland-based <a href="/wiki/Alliance_Boots" title="Alliance Boots">Alliance Boots</a> that it did not already own. The total price of the acquisition was $4.9 billion in cash and 144.3 million common shares with fair value of $10.7 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-Walgreens-Boots-Alliance-Dec-2014-8-K12B_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walgreens-Boots-Alliance-Dec-2014-8-K12B-2">[2]</a></sup> Walgreens had previously purchased 45% of the company for $4.0 billion and 83.4 million common shares in August 2012 with an option to purchase the remaining shares within three years.<sup id="cite_ref-Walgreens-Boots-Alliance-Dec-2014-10-Q_3-0" class="reference"><a <h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Retail_Pharmacy_USA">Retail Pharmacy USA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Walgreens_Boots_Alliance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Retail Pharmacy USA" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Walgreens" title="Walgreens">Walgreens</a> and <a href="/wiki/Duane_Reade" title="Duane Reade">Duane Reade</a></div>
<p>Walgreens and Duane Reade operate within the Retail Pharmacy USA division of Walgreens Boots Alliance. Both businesses sell prescription and non-prescription drugs, and a range of household items, including personal care and beauty products. Walgreens provides access to consumer goods and services, plus pharmacy, photo department, health and wellness services in the United States through its retail drugstores. The division has 9,277 drugstores as of August 31, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Walgreens runs several online stores, such as: Beauty.com, <a href="/wiki/Drugstore.com" title="Drugstore.com">Drugstore.com</a> and VisionDirect.com.
</p><p>Walgreens has stores in all 50 US states, the District of Columbia, Puerto Rico, and the U.S. Virgin Islands.
</p><p>On October 27, 2015, Walgreens announced the purchase of competitor <a href="/wiki/Rite_Aid" title="Rite Aid">Rite Aid</a> for $17.2 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> However, that deal was later scrapped due to antitrust concerns in favor of a $5.18 billion deal, in which Walgreens only acquired half of Rite Aid locations.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>On September 19, 2017, the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Trade_Commission" title="Federal Trade Commission">Federal Trade Commission</a> (FTC) approved a fourth deal agreement to purchase 1,932 Rite Aid stores for $4.38 billion total, a transaction which was completed in January 2018. <sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Retail_Pharmacy_International">Retail Pharmacy International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Walgreens_Boots_Alliance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Retail Pharmacy International" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Boots_UK" class="mw-redirect" title="Boots UK">Boots UK</a> and <a href="/wiki/Farmacias_Benavides" title="Farmacias Benavides">Farmacias Benavides</a></div>

The Boots brand has a history stretching back over 170 years<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and is a familiar sight on Britain's high streets. Stores are located in prominent high street and city center locations as well as in local communities. Most branches include a pharmacy and focus on healthcare, personal care, and cosmetic products, with most stores selling over-the-counter medicines. Larger stores typically offer a variety of healthcare services in addition to dispensing prescriptions, and <a href="/wiki/Chlamydia_infection" class="mw-redirect" title="Chlamydia infection">chlamydia</a> testing and treatment (private service). Optician services are also offered in many larger stores, with <a href="/wiki/Boots_Opticians" title="Boots Opticians">Boots Opticians</a> providing eye tests along with the sale of spectacles and contact lenses.
</p><p>Many stores also feature traditional <a href="/wiki/Photo_processing" class="mw-redirect" title="Photo processing">photo processing</a> and/or a HP picture kiosk where users of digital cameras and camera phones can create prints via Bluetooth, USB, or CD.  Larger stores usually offer a range of electrical equipment such as hairdryers, <a href="/wiki/Curlers" class="mw-redirect" title="Curlers">curlers</a>, and foot massagers, while selected stores offer a range of sandwiches, baguettes, wraps, salads, and beverages.
</p><p>Since 1936, there have been Boots stores outside the UK. Stores in countries as widely spread as New Zealand, Canada (see <a href="/wiki/Pharma_Plus" class="mw-redirect" title="Pharma Plus">Pharma Plus</a>), and France were all closed in the 1980s. Today, there are Boots branded stores outside the UK and Ireland and other countries including United Arab Emirates, Bahrain, Norway, Lithuania, The Netherlands, Thailand.
</p><p>The remainder of the division is made up of the pharmacies <a href="/wiki/Farmacias_Benavides" title="Farmacias Benavides">Benavides</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> and Farmacias Ahumada (FASA) in <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Pharmaceutical_Wholesale">Pharmaceutical Wholesale</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Walgreens_Boots_Alliance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Pharmaceutical Wholesale" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Alliance_Healthcare" title="Alliance Healthcare">Alliance Healthcare</a></div>
<p>Alliance Healthcare forms the main part of Walgreens Boots Alliance's Pharmaceutical Wholesale division. It operates twice daily deliveries to more than 16,500 delivery points in the UK alone.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Internationally, the Pharmaceutical Wholesale Division, which mainly operates under the Alliance Healthcare brand, supplies medicines, other healthcare products and related services to more than 115,000 pharmacies, doctors, health centers and hospitals each year from 300 distribution centers in 11 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The division's Alphega Pharmacy network provides a range of services for independent pharmacies, including branding, professional training and patient care, retail support services and supply benefits together with pharmacy and IT support.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>

<p>Boots produces a large number of brands, including No7, Soltan and Botanics, Boots Pharmaceuticals, and Boots Laboratories, that Alliance Boots and Walgreens sought to launch internationally following the first share purchase in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-Walgreen-Co-Oct-2012-10-K_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walgreen-Co-Oct-2012-10-K-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>Launched in 1935, No7 is best known for its anti-aging beauty serums, developed in Nottingham, that first appeared in 2007. The range comprises products designed to target the aging concerns of specific age groups. No7 became available in Walgreens and Duane Reade stores in the US from November 2012, beginning in Los Angeles.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>Launched in 1939, Soltan markets its UVA 5-star protection, a standard of protection developed by Boots and now adopted as the benchmark for suncare products in the UK.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Although in both 2004 and 2015, Watchdog, a BBC consumer investigative TV program and cited on BBC News, plus the consumer WHICH? Magazine, each did an investigation finding the 5-star rating was unsubstantiated, and skincare experts declared it to be far less safe than claimed.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>First launched in 1995, the Botanics range, developed in partnership with the Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew, uses plant extracts in a variety of products and includes a range of organic products. The Botanics range is also available through third party retailers.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>The Boots own brand range of products includes skincare, medicines, healthcare products, and many more. Boots Laboratories skincare range for independent pharmacy customers was launched in France and Portugal in 2008/09 and is also sold in Spain, Italy, and Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>Boots now owns Almus Pharmaceuticals, a brand of generic prescription drugs, launched in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> It is now sold in five countries and is an umbrella brand for a wide range of lower cost generic medicines. Almus placed considerable emphasis on the design of the packaging in an attempt to reduce the number of errors by the dispensing chemist and by the patient relating to incorrect dosage which can result in either a dangerous <a href="/wiki/Accidental_overdose" class="mw-redirect" title="Accidental overdose">accidental overdose</a> or an equally dangerous under dose.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>Walgreens has a self-branded line of products, "Well at Walgreens".<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, Walgreens Boots Alliance paid £140 million (about $250 million) for UK skincare brand Liz Earle Naturally Active, an Avon subsidiary since 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Liz Earle Beauty Co co-founder Liz Earle, 'one of the biggest names in the beauty industry'<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> stayed on as an 'ambassador'<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> after selling the company for an undisclosed sum<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and told her own <i>Liz Earle Wellbeing</i> magazine's website that '...alongside my new digital and print publishing venture...at the moment I’m still connected to Liz Earle Beauty Co and continue to work as a consultant to the brand that carries my name. I’m involved in new product development...'<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup>  In 2012, Liz Earle announced that she had left the company.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
