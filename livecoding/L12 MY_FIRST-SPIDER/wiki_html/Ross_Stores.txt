<p><b>Ross Stores, Inc.</b>, operating under the brand name <b>Ross Dress for Less</b>, is an American chain of <a href="/wiki/Discount_store" title="Discount store">discount</a> department stores headquartered in Dublin, California.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It is the largest <a href="/wiki/Off-price" title="Off-price">off-price</a> retailer in the U.S., as of 2018, Ross operates 1,483 stores in 37 <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. states</a>, the <a href="/wiki/District_of_Columbia" class="mw-redirect" title="District of Columbia">District of Columbia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> covering much of the country, but with no presence in  <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, northern <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> and areas of the <a href="/wiki/Midwestern_United_States" </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ross_Stores&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Ross Department Store was first opened in <a href="/wiki/San_Bruno,_California" title="San Bruno, California">San Bruno, California</a>, in 1950 by Morris "Morrie" Ross. Morris would work 85 hours a week doing all of the buying and bookkeeping for his department store. In 1958 Ross sold his store to William Isackson to become a residential and commercial real estate developer.<sup id="cite_ref-Morrie_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Morrie-5">[5]</a></sup> Isackson built the company to six stores, located in San Bruno, Pacifica, Novato, Vacaville, Redwood City, and Castro Valley. In 1982 a group of investors, including Mervin Morris, founder of the <a href="/wiki/Mervyns" class="mw-redirect" title="Mervyns">Mervyns</a> chain of department stores, purchased the six Ross Department Stores in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco</a>, changed the format to <a href="/wiki/Off-price" title="Off-price">off-price</a> retail units, and within three years rapidly expanded the chain to 107 stores under Stuart Moldaw and Don Rowlett.<sup id="cite_ref-ross_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ross-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> By the end of 1995 the chain reached an annual sales of $1.4 billion with 292 stores in 18 states. By 2012 Ross reached $9.7 billion for the fiscal year with 1,091 stores in 33 states with an additional 108 for <a href="/wiki/Dd%27s_Discounts" class="mw-redirect" title="Dd's Discounts">Dd's Discounts</a> in 8 states.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Ross moved its headquarters from <a href="/wiki/Newark,_California" title="Newark, California">Newark</a> to <a href="/wiki/Pleasanton,_California" title="Pleasanton, California">Pleasanton, California</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Tri-Valley" title="Tri-Valley">Tri-Valley</a> area, in 2003.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Barbara_Rentler" title="Barbara Rentler">Barbara Rentler</a> took the place of CEO Michael Balmuth on June 1, 2014; she was the 25th female CEO of a <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> company.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>  Ross  moved its headquarters from Pleasanton to neighboring <a href="/wiki/Dublin,_California" title="Dublin, California">Dublin, California</a>, in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
