The Company operates through three segments: temporary and consultant staffing, permanent placement staffing, and risk consulting and internal audit services. Through its Accountemps, Robert Half Finance &amp; Accounting, Robert Half Management Resources divisions, it provides temporary, full-time and project professionals in the fields of accounting and finance. Other divisions include OfficeTeam, which specializes in administrative and customer service staffing, The Creative Group, that focuses on design, artistic, and creative talent, and Robert Half Legal that offer flexible and direct-hire staffing for legal professionals.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2002, <a href="/wiki/Protiviti" title="Protiviti">Protiviti</a> Inc. was founded as a subsidiary of Robert Half, which provides <a href="/wiki/Internal_audit" title="Internal audit">internal audit</a>, financial, operations, technology, governance, and risk consulting services.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>Robert Half is listed by <a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)"><i>Fortune</i> magazine</a> as the "World's Most Admired Companies" in the temporary-help industry for 21 years consecutively (as of 2019) since it first made the list in 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Half_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<table class="box-Expand_section plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs expansion</b> with:  information on the company's products, performance, leadership, and status, in particular, for years other than 1948 and 1986-2003. <small>You can help by <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Robert_Half_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=">adding to it</a>.</small>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">February 2017</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table><p>The company's founding was in 1948,<sup id="cite_ref-fundinguniverse_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fundinguniverse-14">[14]</a></sup> in New York City,<sup id="cite_ref-RHHist_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RHHist-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Self-published_sources" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on a self-published source. (February 2017)">self-published source</span></a></i>]</sup> with its original name being variously reported as being Robert Half Inc., Robert Half Finance &amp; Accounting,<sup id="cite_ref-fundinguniverse_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fundinguniverse-14">[14]</a></sup> and Robert Half Personnel Agency.<sup id="cite_ref-RHHist_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RHHist-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Self-published_sources" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on a self-published source. (February 2017)">self-published source</span></a></i>]</sup> The founding, in any case, is reported to have been of two separate business entities, the Robert Half (RH) portion as "an employment agency for accountants," and the Accountemps portion "to supply accountants and other financial professionals… on a temporary basis."<sup id="cite_ref-fundinguniverse_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fundinguniverse-14">[14]</a></sup> Founder Robert Half had previously managed the hiring of accounting staff for a sizeable textile manufacturing concern, and saw an opportunity as no placement agency had been focusing on this sector of the market.<sup id="cite_ref-RHHist_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RHHist-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Self-published_sources" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on a self-published source. (February 2017)">self-published source</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In the mid- to late-1980s, Robert Half (RH) altered its prior focus—which had been like that of the field in general—from relatively undifferentiated temporary clerical and light industry staffing, to providing temporary workers at higher skill levels via new "professional staffing divisions".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Self-published_sources" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on a self-published source. (February 2017)">self-published source</span></a></i>]</sup> Having grown to over 150 locations in the United States and launched an aggressive campaign to re-purchase many of the separate franchises, the firm went public in 1987, then expanded its operations into Europe in 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-fundinguniverse_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fundinguniverse-14">[14]</a></sup> In addition to their Accountemps brand, the firm expanded to include specialty groups focusing on technology, legal and creative services personnel.<sup id="cite_ref-fundinguniverse_14-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fundinguniverse-14">[14]</a></sup> Inroads into the legal sector began with the company's 1991 acquisition of "The Affiliates, a firm in Southern California that placed temporary and permanent paralegal, legal administrative, and other legal support personnel," with it being renamed as Robert Half Legal.<sup id="cite_ref-fundinguniverse_14-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fundinguniverse-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>Robert Half went on to establish itself as a key information provider for government agencies and others seeking statistics and trends regarding employment,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (February 2017)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> with its market-specific salary guides being used as a resource by the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Dept_of_Labor" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Dept of Labor">U.S Department of Labor</a> in the preparation of their own forecasts.<sup id="cite_ref-fundinguniverse_14-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fundinguniverse-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In addition to their main staffing line of business, RH also provides independent risk consulting, internal audit and <a href="/wiki/Information_technology_consulting" title="Information technology consulting">information technology consulting</a> services via its Protiviti subsidiary, which was founded in 2002 with the acquisition of former employees of <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Andersen" title="Arthur Andersen">Arthur Andersen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fundinguniverse_14-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fundinguniverse-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Since then, the group has grown from 700 to over 2,900 staff,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Self-published_sources" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on a self-published source. (February 2017)">self-published source</span></a></i>]</sup> and continues to be led by Andersen alumni.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In 2003, the company was finally able to buy back the last of the few remaining independent franchise operations.<sup id="cite_ref-fundinguniverse_14-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fundinguniverse-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In 2006, RH acquired the assets of PG Lewis &amp; Associates, a leading national provider of Data Forensics and Cyber Security services founded in 2003 by serial technology entrepreneur, Paul G. Lewis.  Financial terms were not disclosed.
</p>
