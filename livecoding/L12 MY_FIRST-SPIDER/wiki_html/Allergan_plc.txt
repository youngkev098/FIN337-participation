</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allergan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Allergan plc was created from the 2015 merger and Irish <a href="/wiki/Corporation_tax_in_the_Republic_of_Ireland" title="Corporation tax in the Republic of Ireland">corporate tax inversion</a> of two companies, Irish-based <a href="/wiki/Actavis" title="Actavis">Actavis plc</a> and U.S.-based Allergan, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span id="Allergan.2C_Inc."></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Allergan,_Inc.">Allergan, Inc.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allergan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Allergan, Inc." data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Allergan,_inc_logo.png" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/3d/Allergan%2C_inc_logo.png/220px-Allergan%2C_inc_logo.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="44" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="707" data-file-height="141"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 44px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/3d/Allergan%2C_inc_logo.png/220px-Allergan%2C_inc_logo.png" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="44" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Allergan,_inc_logo.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Logo used for the original Allergan until the merger with Actavis in 2015</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Allergan_headquarters.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Allergan_headquarters.jpg/220px-Allergan_headquarters.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="155" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="2776" data-file-height="1956"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 155px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Allergan_headquarters.jpg/220px-Allergan_headquarters.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="155" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Allergan_headquarters.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Allergan, Inc. headquarters in Irvine</div></div></div>
<p>In 1948, pharmacist Gavin S. Herbert founded <a href="/wiki/Allergan,_Inc." title="Allergan, Inc.">Allergan Pharmaceuticals, Inc</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">[9]</a></sup> In 1970, Allergan, Inc. became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">[9]</a></sup> In 1977, Allergan announced plans to build a factory in Westport, Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 1980, it was acquired by <a href="/wiki/SmithKline_Beckman" class="mw-redirect" title="SmithKline Beckman">SmithKline Corporation</a> for $259 million<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and was later spun-off by SmithKline Beecham in 1989.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 1989, Allergan opened a manufacturing plant in Waco, Texas to manufacture eye care products.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In 1991, Allergan acquired the company Oculinum which manufactured products for eye muscle disorders.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Following the acquisition, the product was renamed Botox.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In 1996, Allergan received FDA approval for <a href="/wiki/Brimonidine" title="Brimonidine">Alphagan</a> ophthalmic solution for open-angle glaucoma and ocular hypertension.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-17">[17]</a></sup> In 1998, Allergan established The Allergan Foundation, a private US-based charitable foundation.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In 2005, Allergan acquired Inamed which had a licensing agreement with Corneal Group Laboratoires for Juvederm products.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In 2006, Allergan gained rights to Juvederm products.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Actavis_plc">Actavis plc</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allergan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Actavis plc" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In the spring of 1983, colleagues <a href="/wiki/Allen_Chao" title="Allen Chao">Allen Chao</a> and David Hsia formed Watson Pharmaceuticals, a drug development in <a href="/wiki/Libertyville,_Illinois" title="Libertyville, Illinois">Libertyville, Illinois</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In late fall of 1983, the company moved to <a href="/wiki/Corona,_California" title="Corona, California">Corona, California</a> and began to develop and manufacture generic pharmaceuticals.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-25">[25]</a></sup> In February 1993, the company became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>In 2011, the company moved its corporate headquarters from <a href="/wiki/Corona,_California" title="Corona, California">Corona, California</a> to <a href="/wiki/Parsippany-Troy_Hills,_New_Jersey" title="Parsippany-Troy Hills, New Jersey">Parsippany-Troy Hills, New Jersey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> On October 31, 2012, Watson acquired Actavis for <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>4.25 billion and took the Actavis name.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In 2013, Actavis acquired Irish–registered <a href="/wiki/Warner_Chilcott" title="Warner Chilcott">Warner Chilcott</a> for $5 billion and used the transaction to execute a <a href="/wiki/Corporation_tax_in_the_Republic_of_Ireland#List_of_inversions" title="Corporation tax in the Republic of Ireland">corporate tax inversion</a> to Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> In 2014, Actavis acquired Forest Laboratories for $25 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Acquisition_by_Actavis_plc_.282015.E2.80.932019.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisition_by_Actavis_plc_(2015–2019)">Acquisition by Actavis plc (2015–2019)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allergan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Acquisition by Actavis plc (2015–2019)" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Corporation_tax_in_the_Republic_of_Ireland#Corporate_tax_inversions" title="Corporation tax in the Republic of Ireland">Corporation tax in the Republic of Ireland § Corporate tax inversions</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Allergan_Headquarters_in_Ireland_(Clonsaugh_Park,_Coolock,_Dublin).jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Allergan_Headquarters_in_Ireland_%28Clonsaugh_Park%2C_Coolock%2C_Dublin%29.jpg/220px-Allergan_Headquarters_in_Ireland_%28Clonsaugh_Park%2C_Coolock%2C_Dublin%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="164" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="260" data-file-height="194"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 164px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Allergan_Headquarters_in_Ireland_%28Clonsaugh_Park%2C_Coolock%2C_Dublin%29.jpg/220px-Allergan_Headquarters_in_Ireland_%28Clonsaugh_Park%2C_Coolock%2C_Dublin%29.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="164" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Allergan_Headquarters_in_Ireland_(Clonsaugh_Park,_Coolock,_Dublin).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Allergan plc headquarters in Ireland</div></div></div>
<p>In 2015, Irish–based Actavis plc acquired U.S.–based <a href="/wiki/Allergan,_Inc." title="Allergan, Inc.">Allergan, Inc.</a> for $70 billion, and in effect redomiciled Allergan to Ireland in a corporate tax inversion.<sup id="cite_ref-activis_deals_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-activis_deals-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Actavis CEO Brent Saunders became the CEO of the combined company.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> In June 2015, the company took the Allergan name.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> At the time, over 85% of the combined sales of the post-merger Allergan plc were from the U.S. healthcare system.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>As of 2020, the company operated four facilities in Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2015">2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allergan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2015" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>On July 7, 2015, the company acquired the rights to the late stage <a href="/wiki/Calcitonin_gene-related_peptide" title="Calcitonin gene-related peptide">CGRP</a> <a href="/wiki/Migraine" title="Migraine">migraine</a> portfolio of <a href="/wiki/Merck_%26_Co" class="mw-redirect" title="Merck &amp; Co">Merck &amp; Co</a>, as well as two experimental drugs for an upfront payment of $250 million.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Later that summer, the company acquired Oculeve for $125 million<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and Naurex for an upfront payment of $560 million.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> In October 2015, the company acquired AqueSys, developer of ocular implants for an initial payment of $300 million,<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> along with Kythera Biopharmaceuticals, a company focused on the medical aesthetics market, for $2.1 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p><p>In late 2015, the company announced the acquisition of Northwood Medical Innovation, the developer of <a href="/wiki/EarFold" title="EarFold">earFold</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> On November 25, 2015, the company announced it would partner with Rugen Therapeutic to develop new therapies for <a href="/wiki/Autism_spectrum_disorder" class="mw-redirect" title="Autism spectrum disorder">autism spectrum disorder</a> and <a href="/wiki/Obsessive_compulsive_disorder" class="mw-redirect" title="Obsessive compulsive disorder">obsessive compulsive disorder</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Abandon_Merger_with_Pfizer">Abandon Merger with Pfizer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allergan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Abandon Merger with Pfizer" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>On November 23, 2015, Allergan and <a href="/wiki/Pfizer" title="Pfizer">Pfizer</a> announced their intention to merge in a $160 billion transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> On April 5, 2016, after the Obama administration announced its plan to ban <a href="/wiki/Tax_inversion" title="Tax inversion">tax inversions</a>, Pfizer terminated the acquisition and paid Allergan a $150 million <a href="/wiki/Breakup_fee" title="Breakup fee">breakup fee</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2016">2016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allergan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2016" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In April 2016, the company announced it would partner with Heptares Therapeutics to develop a subtype-selective <a href="/wiki/Muscarinic_agonist" title="Muscarinic agonist">muscarinic agonists</a> for <a href="/wiki/Alzheimer%27s_disease" title="Alzheimer's disease">Alzheimer's disease</a><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> and announced the acquisition of Topokine Therapeutics, gaining the phase IIb/III compound XAF5 - a treatment for <a href="/wiki/Dermatochalasis" title="Dermatochalasis">dermatochalasis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2016, Allergan plc sold its <a href="/wiki/Generic_drug" title="Generic drug">generic drugs</a> business to <a href="/wiki/Teva_Pharmaceutical_Industries" class="mw-redirect" title="Teva Pharmaceutical Industries">Teva Pharmaceutical Industries</a> in a $40.5 billion deal<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> and announced the acquisition of ForSight VISION5 for more than $95 million.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2016, CEO Saunders announced what he called a social contract to limit prices on the company's medications.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> That same month, the company acquired RetroSense Therapeutics for more than $60 million,<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup>  announced the acquisition of Tobira Therapeutics for approximately $1.7 billion<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> and, a day later, the acquisition of Akarna Therapeutics for $50 million.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2016, the company sold Anda, its generic drug distribution business, to Teva for $500 million<sup id="cite_ref-:6_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-58">[58]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> and acquired Vitae Pharmaceuticals, focused on dermatology treatments, for $639 million.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_58-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-58">[58]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> That same month, the company announced it would acquire Motus Therapeutics, a developer of treatments for gastrointestinal disorders, for $200 million.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> On November 22, 2016, the company acquired Chase Pharmaceuticals for $125 million.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2017-2020">2017-2020</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allergan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2017-2020" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In 2017, the company acquired LifeCell, a specialist in regenerative medicine, for $2.9 billion and Zeltique Aesthetics, maker of CoolSculpting, for $2.4 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_65-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-65">[65]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> On June 7, the company announced the acquisition of Keller Medical, a company that manufactures devices for use during <a href="/wiki/Breast_augmentation" title="Breast augmentation">breast augmentation</a> surgery.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> On December 12, the company announced the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Repros_Therapeutics" title="Repros Therapeutics">Repros Therapeutics</a>, a developer of drugs for <a href="/wiki/Reproductive_system_disease" title="Reproductive system disease">reproductive system diseases</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2018, Allergan acquired the aesthetic company, Bonti, for $195 million.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2019, the company acquired Envy Medical, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> As of 2019, Allergan's Botox product had annual sales of nearly $4 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, along with several other drug manufacturers and distributors, the company was sued by several municipalities and states in the U.S. due to the manufacture of <a href="/wiki/Opioid" title="Opioid">opioids</a>, which have been abused in what has been referred to as the <a href="/wiki/Opioid_epidemic" title="Opioid epidemic">opioid <h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisition_by_AbbVie_Inc.">Acquisition by AbbVie Inc.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Allergan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Acquisition by AbbVie Inc." data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In June 2019, U.S. pharmaceutical company <a href="/wiki/AbbVie" class="mw-redirect" title="AbbVie">AbbVie</a> announced it had reached an agreement to acquire Allergan for $63 billion,<sup id="cite_ref-abbvieguardian_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abbvieguardian-7">[7]</a></sup> which would return Allergan to the U.S. for tax purposes.<sup id="cite_ref-abbviebloomberg_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abbviebloomberg-8">[8]</a></sup> On the announcement of the transaction, Abbvie disclosed that its 2019 net effective tax rate was 9%, but that post the acquisition, the Group's effective tax rate would rise to 13%. The merger is expected to close in 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-abbviebloomberg_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abbviebloomberg-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

The company manufactures several pharmaceutical products including Botox (<a href="/wiki/Botulinum_toxin" title="Botulinum toxin">botulinum toxin</a>), Juvederm (<a href="/wiki/Injectable_filler" title="Injectable filler">injectable filler</a>), CoolSculpting, Alphagan (brimonidine), Vraylar (cariprazine HCI), Linzess (<a href="/wiki/Linaclotide" title="Linaclotide">linaclotide</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup> and Ubrelvy (<a href="/wiki/Ubrogepant" title="Ubrogepant">ubrogepant</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-:2_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:5_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:7_65-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-65">[65]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:4_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup></p>
