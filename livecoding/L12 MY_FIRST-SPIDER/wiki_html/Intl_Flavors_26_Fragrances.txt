</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Flavors_%26_Fragrances&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>International Flavors &amp; Fragrances was formed in 1958 by the merger between Polak &amp; Schwarz (P&amp;S) and van Ameringen-Haebler. Polak &amp; Schwarz was founded in 1889 by Leopold Schwarz, who had an interest in spices, flavors, and fragrances, and his brother-in-law, Joseph Polak in the small <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> town of <a href="/wiki/Zutphen" title="Zutphen">Zutphen</a>.  P&amp;S quickly grew to 36 sites globally.  Arnold Louis (A.L.) van Ameringen was hired by Polak &amp; Schwarz to open P&amp;S's US office in 1917 before losing his job with them.  Relieved of his duties, he opened van Ameringen &amp; Co. at 13 Gold Street in Manhattan, took over Morana, Inc. in 1920, and became van Ameringen-Haebler (VAH) in 1929.
</p><p>Constant Cornelis (Cook) Brummer was a director at Polak &amp; Schwarz during World War II. Because he saved tens of thousands of Jewish workers lives, he is compared to <a href="/wiki/Oskar_Schindler" title="Oskar Schindler">Oskar Schindler</a>. He was recognized by Israel as <a href="/wiki/Righteous_Among_the_Nations" title="Righteous Among the Nations">Righteous Among the Nations</a> in January 2017, for risking his own life to save others from the Nazis.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>The two companies did well through the bulk of the 20th century, despite difficulties for P&amp;S during World War II.  While both companies managed flavors and fragrances, Polak &amp; Schwarz had particular strengths in flavors and van Ameringen-Haebler built a powerful reputation in fragrances.  In 1958, the two companies announced their merger to become International Flavors &amp; Fragrances Inc. IFF was listed on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> in 1964. It was cross listed to the <a href="/wiki/Euronext_Paris" title="Euronext Paris">Euronext Paris Market</a> in 2015, becoming the first US-listed company to make use of Euronext Paris Fast Path process.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 1968, in conjunction with the Monell Foundation, IFF established the <a href="/wiki/Monell_Chemical_Senses_Center" title="Monell Chemical Senses Center">Monell Chemical Senses Center</a>, dedicated to the research of taste and smell.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 1985, Dr. Braja D. Mookherjee, a scientist at IFF, pioneered Living Flower technology, which changed the way perfumes are created. In 1990, IFF broke ground in their R&amp;D facility located in <a href="/wiki/Union_Beach,_NJ" class="mw-redirect" title="Union Beach, NJ">Union Beach, NJ</a>, to develop its 5,000-square-foot greenhouse and botanical garden, which can maintain tropical and subtropical temperature zones for more than 1,300 varieties of exotic plants.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>It is home to a miniature rose called "Overnight Scentsation" (also known as the "Space Rose") which was cultivated for experiments in space and left the Earth during a 10-day flight on board the shuttle <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Discovery" title="Space Shuttle Discovery">Discovery</a> (STS-95) in 1998. Low gravity critically altered the fragrance of the rose, which was later commercialized by IFF and Shiseido.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>Henry G. Walter, Jr., former IFF Chairman, 1962–1985,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> was a noted philanthropist and prolific writer, who pioneered the field of <a href="/wiki/Aromatherapy" title="Aromatherapy">aromatherapy</a>. He is considered one of the most influential people in the Industry.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> It has been speculated that he inspired some of the storylines and characters in the book <a href="/wiki/Masters_of_Sex_(book)" title="Masters of Sex (book)">Masters of Sex</a> by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Maier" title="Thomas Maier">Thomas Maier</a>, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Masters_of_Sex" title="Masters of Sex">Showtime series</a> by the same the name.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> During World War II, his wife, <a href="/wiki/Rosalind_P._Walter" title="Rosalind P. Walter">Rosalind (Palmer) Walter</a> worked on the night shift as a riveter on the Corsair, the F4U Marine gull-winged, fighter airplane, inspiring the song "Rosie the Riveter."<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In 2000, IFF acquired <a href="/w/index.php?title=Laboratoire_Monique_R%C3%A9my&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Laboratoire Monique Rémy (page does not exist)">Laboratoire Monique Rémy</a> based in Grasse, France and forms IFF LMR Naturals, strengthening its position in the development and use of raw materials, natural ingredients for fragrances and flavors.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In 2000, IFF acquired Bush Boake Allen with its complementary products, technologies,and presence in key markets, making IFF the largest flavor and fragrance house in the world for several years.
</p><p>In 2014, IFF acquired Aromor Flavors and Fragrances Ltd., a privately held manufacturer and marketer of complex specialty ingredients that are used in fragrances and flavors based in Israel in Kibbutz Givat Oz.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, IFF announced that it completed the acquisition of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ottens_Flavors&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ottens Flavors (page does not exist)">Ottens Flavors</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> This was followed in 2016 with the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/David_Michael_%26_Co." title="David Michael &amp; Co.">David Michael &amp; Company</a>. Using this merger as a catalyst, they have since launched Tastepoint by IFF to serve dynamic middle-tier customers.
</p><p>In 2015, IFF acquired <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lucas_Meyer_Cosmetics&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lucas Meyer Cosmetics (page does not exist)">Lucas Meyer Cosmetics</a>, a cosmetic actives firm based in Canada that develops, manufactures and markets innovative ingredients for the cosmetic and personal care industry.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>IFF has creative, sales, and manufacturing facilities in 35 different countries and is a member of the S&amp;P 500 Index. IFF is a $10.8 billion market capital company<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> with a global footprint that generated over 75 percent of its 2016 sales outside the United States and 51 percent in the emerging markets.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 2014, the Company celebrated its 125th anniversary year, from the founding of Polak &amp; Schwarz on January 27, 1889 in Zutphen, Netherlands, to the 1958 merger with van Ameringen-Haebler to form IFF. IFF is ranked among Forbes Best Employers.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>On May 8, 2018, IFF acquired <a href="/wiki/Frutarom" title="Frutarom">Frutarom</a> for $7.1 Billion.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>Between 2016 and 2018, <a href="/wiki/Kirsten_Rausing" title="Kirsten Rausing">Kirsten</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finn_Rausing" title="Finn Rausing">Finn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jorn_Rausing" class="mw-redirect" title="Jorn Rausing">Jorn Rausing</a> became the top shareholders of IFF, having bought nearly 20% of the shares via entities in <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liechtenstein" title="Liechtenstein">Liechtenstein</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_Record">Environmental Record</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Flavors_%26_Fragrances&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Environmental Record" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>IFF published its first external sustainability report: Sustainability: The Essence of IFF in 2011. In 2012, IFF adopted the 12 Principles of Green Chemistry as an important differentiator in product design and development.
</p><p>In 2014, IFF opened a 4,000 kilowatt Photovoltaic Solar Installation in New Jersey, which supplies approximately 40 percent of the electricity needs of its <a href="/wiki/Hazlet,_New_Jersey" title="Hazlet, New Jersey">Hazlet</a>, NJ fragrances manufacturing and creative center facilities. It has more than 16,000 modules and approximately 1 million solar cells covering 20 acres of IFF property, making IFF a leader in renewable energy in its sector.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, IFF achieved LEED Silver Certification for its Flavors Creative Center in Indonesia and achieved Roundtable for Sustainable Palm Oil (RSPO) Supply Chain Certification for its facilities in the United States, the United Kingdom and the Netherlands.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, Coca-Cola, FUJIFILM, International Flavors and Fragrances, Agristo, Cofely and Water Board De Dommel opened a collective waste water treatment plant on the premises of FUJIFILM in Tilburg, Netherlands. The waste water treatment plant can process about 10,000,000 litre per day or more than 3,500,000,000 litre waste water per year. The plant has a low level of energy consumption and implements the latest technology in the field of water treatment. After treatment, the waste water is discharged as clean water into the Wilhelmina Canal, making it available for reuse by the four companies, or for another sustainable use in the Tilburg region.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>In 2016, IFF was awarded a place on the CDP Climate "A" List for the 2nd year in a row and achieved a 7% year-over-year reduction in GHG emissions intensity.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> IFF also became the first flavors and fragrances company to join Together for Sustainability (TfS), driving responsible sourcing throughout their supply chain.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>In 2016, IFF deployed the industry's first-ever on-site wind turbine at its Tilburg, Netherlands manufacturing facility.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> It has an output of 2.4 megawatts, producing approximately 6.5 million kWh of renewable electricity per year, which provides up to 30 percent of the site's electricity needs, and when combined with purchased green electricity the facility is powered by 100 percent renewable electricity.
</p><p>In 2016, IFF released the First-Ever Cradle to Cradle Certified™ Fragrance: PuraVita™. Designed through a mindset of sustainability the fragrance earned IFF a Platinum level Material Health Certificate.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p><p>In 2016, IFF and Unilever launched the Vetiver Together Partnership to Improve the Lives of Vetiver Farming Communities in Haiti and strengthen the supply chain. The project is supported by the Enhancing Livelihoods Fund – a partnership between <a href="/wiki/Unilever" title="Unilever">Unilever</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oxfam_Great_Britain" class="mw-redirect" title="Oxfam Great Britain">Oxfam Great Britain</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Ford_Foundation" title="Ford Foundation">Ford Foundation</a> and is being implemented by <a href="/wiki/Heifer_International" title="Heifer International">Heifer International</a> in Haiti.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p><p>IFF is rated gold by EcoVadis for sustainability, which ranks it as a top supplier and partner.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, IFF, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan,_New_York_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Manhattan, New York City">Manhattan, New York City</a>, joined the We Are Still In<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> network demonstrating America's enduring commitment to tackling climate change, ensuring a clean energy future, and upholding the <a href="/wiki/Paris_Agreement" title="Paris Agreement">Paris Agreement</a>. IFF announced that it joined the <a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Business_Act_on_Climate_Pledge&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="American Business Act on Climate Pledge (page does not exist)">American Business Act on Climate Pledge</a> in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, International Flavors &amp; Fragrances’ <a href="/wiki/South_Brunswick,_New_Jersey" title="South Brunswick, New Jersey">South Brunswick, New Jersey</a> facility earned <a href="/w/index.php?title=GreenCircle_Certified_LLC_Zero_Waste_to_Landfill&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="GreenCircle Certified LLC Zero Waste to Landfill (page does not exist)">GreenCircle Certified LLC Zero Waste to Landfill</a> facility status, making it the only facility of its kind in the flavor and fragrance industry to achieve GreenCircle's zero waste status.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p><p>IFF was recognized as one of the top green companies in the U.S. in the 2016 Newsweek Green Rankings <sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> and named on Barron's 100 Most Sustainable Companies List <sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> in 2018.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Partnerships">Partnerships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Flavors_%26_Fragrances&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Partnerships" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>In 2001, IFF collaborated with the Cosmetic Executive Women (CEW) at the Raymond-Poincaré hospital in Garches, France to develop <a href="/w/index.php?title=Olfactotherapy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Olfactotherapy (page does not exist)">Olfactotherapy</a>, based on the link between smells and memories, which is now used in several hospitals to help patients with brain injuries, stroke victims and Alzheimer's patients with memory impairment or of language.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p><p>In 2016, IFF joined the <a href="/wiki/Well_Living_Lab" title="Well Living Lab">Well Living Lab</a> as a Sustaining Alliance Member. The Well Living Lab is a collaboration of the <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic" title="Mayo Clinic">Mayo Clinic</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Delos_Innovate_Well&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Delos Innovate Well (page does not exist)">Delos Innovate Well</a> that is seeking to, among other things, study indoor environments and create healthier indoor spaces.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, IFF Lucas Meyer Cosmetics invested in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bio_ForeXtra&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bio ForeXtra (page does not exist)">Bio ForeXtra</a> to expand its raw material access.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p><p>IFF is a member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Flavour_Association" title="European Flavour Association">European Flavour Association</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-EFFA_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EFFA-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Flavors_%26_Fragrances&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Controversy" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>IFF has been involved in several lawsuits relating to cases of <a href="/wiki/Bronchiolitis_obliterans" class="mw-redirect" title="Bronchiolitis obliterans">bronchiolitis obliterans</a> allegedly resulting from exposure to <a href="/wiki/Diacetyl" title="Diacetyl">diacetyl</a> in <a href="/wiki/Artificial_butter_flavoring" title="Artificial butter flavoring">butter flavorings</a> by workers at the Gilster-Mary Lee <a href="/wiki/Popcorn" title="Popcorn">popcorn</a> plant in <a href="/wiki/Jasper,_Missouri" title="Jasper, Missouri">Jasper, Missouri</a>. The plaintiffs claim the manufacturers knew about the dangers of the butter flavoring used in the popcorn factory but failed to give a warning. The defendants said they were unaware of the risk and suggested there was not enough evidence to prove their product had caused the disease. Several suits have already been settled amounting to almost $53 million in damages.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
