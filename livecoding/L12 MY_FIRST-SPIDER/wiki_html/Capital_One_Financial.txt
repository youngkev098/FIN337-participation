href="/wiki/JPMorgan_Chase" title="JPMorgan Chase">JPMorgan Chase</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_America" title="Bank of America">Bank of America</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Citigroup" title="Citigroup">Citigroup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>With a market share of 5%, Capital One is also the 2nd largest <a href="/wiki/Car_finance" title="Car finance">auto finance</a> company in the United States, following <a href="/wiki/Ally_Financial" title="Ally Financial">Ally Financial</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In the fourth quarter of 2018, 75% of the company's revenues were from credit cards, 14% were from consumer banking, and 11% were from commercial banking.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup> Capital One has consistently ranked as one of the best places to work for, appearing in multiple Glassdoor's Best Places To Work reports.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>. In 2020, <i><a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)">Fortune</a></i> magazine ranked Capital One at number 24 on their Fortune List of the Top 100 Companies to Work For in 2020 based on an employee survey of satisfaction.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Divisions">Divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capital_One&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Divisions" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Capital One operates 3 divisions as follows:<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<ul><li><b>Credit Cards</b> – Capital One issues credit cards in the United States, Canada, and the United Kingdom and is the 3rd largest credit card issuer, after <a href="/wiki/JPMorgan_Chase" title="JPMorgan Chase">JPMorgan Chase</a> and <a href="/wiki/Citigroup" title="Citigroup">Citigroup</a>. As of December 31, 2018, Capital One had $107.350 billion in credit card loans outstanding in the United States and $9.011 billion of credit card loans outstanding in Canada and the United Kingdom, with credit cards in total representing 47.3% of total loans outstanding.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Consumer Banking</b> – offers banking services, including <a href="/wiki/Checking_account" class="mw-redirect" title="Checking account">checking accounts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saving_account" class="mw-redirect" title="Saving account">saving accounts</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Money_market_account" title="Money market account">money market accounts</a> via its branches and <a href="/wiki/Direct_bank" title="Direct bank">direct bank</a> as well as retail and auto loans. As of December 31, 2018, the company had $2.864 billion in retail loans outstanding and $56.341 billion in <a href="/wiki/Car_finance" title="Car finance">car finance</a> loans outstanding, representing 22.9% of total loans outstanding.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Commercial banking</b> – As of December 31, 2018, Capital One had $70.333 billion in loans outstanding secured by commercial, multifamily, and industrial properties, representing 28.6% of total loans outstanding.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capital_One&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Monoline_credit_card_company_.281994.E2.80.932004.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Monoline_credit_card_company_(1994–2004)">Monoline credit card company (1994–2004)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capital_One&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Monoline credit card company (1994–2004)" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Capital_One_footprint_2010-01.png" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Capital_One_footprint_2010-01.png/150px-Capital_One_footprint_2010-01.png" decoding="async" width="150" height="252" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="945" data-file-height="1590"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 150px;height: 252px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Capital_One_footprint_2010-01.png/150px-Capital_One_footprint_2010-01.png" data-alt="" data-width="150" data-height="252" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Capital_One_footprint_2010-01.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Capital One retail footprint</div></div></div>
<p>On July 27, 1994, <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">Richmond, Virginia</a>-based Signet Financial Corp (now part of Wells Fargo) announced the <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">corporate spin-off</a> of its credit card division, OakStone Financial, naming <a href="/wiki/Richard_Fairbank" title="Richard Fairbank">Richard Fairbank</a> as CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Signet renamed the subsidiary Capital One in October 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>At that time, Capital One was a <i>monoline</i> bank, meaning that all of its revenue came from a single product, in this case, credit cards.<sup id="cite_ref-realist_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-realist-13">[13]</a></sup> This strategy is risky in that it can lead to losses during bad times.<sup id="cite_ref-realist_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-realist-13">[13]</a></sup> Capital One attributed its relative success as a monoline to its use of data collection to build <a href="/wiki/Demographic_profile" title="Demographic profile">demographic profiles</a>, allowing it to target personalized offers of credit directly to consumers.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>Capital One began operations in Canada in 1996.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Expansion_into_auto_loans_.281998.E2.80.93present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion_into_auto_loans_(1998–present)">Expansion into auto loans (1998–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capital_One&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Expansion into auto loans (1998–present)" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:CapitalOne_360_Booth_Boston.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/CapitalOne_360_Booth_Boston.jpg/200px-CapitalOne_360_Booth_Boston.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="113" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="4128" data-file-height="2322"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 200px;height: 113px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/CapitalOne_360_Booth_Boston.jpg/200px-CapitalOne_360_Booth_Boston.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="200" data-height="113" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:CapitalOne_360_Booth_Boston.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Activities announcing the opening of the CapitalOne 360 Café in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a></div></div></div>
<p>In July 1998, Capital One acquired auto financing company Summit Acceptance Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In 1999, Capital One was looking to expand beyond credit cards. CEO Richard Fairbank announced moves to use Capital One's experience with collecting consumer data to offer loans, insurance, and phone service.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2001, PeopleFirst Finance LLC was acquired by Capital One.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>The companies were combined and re-branded as Capital One Auto Finance Corporation in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In late 2002, Capital One and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Postal_Service" title="United States Postal Service">United States Postal Service</a> proposed a negotiated services agreement for bulk discount in mailing services.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The resulting three-year agreement<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> was extended in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In June 2008, however, Capital One had filed a complaint<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> with the USPS regarding the terms of the next agreement,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> citing the terms of the NSA of Capital One's competitor, <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_America" title="Bank of America">Bank of America</a>. Capital One subsequently withdrew its complaint to the <a href="/wiki/Postal_Regulatory_Commission" title="Postal Regulatory Commission">Postal Regulatory Commission</a> following a settlement with the USPS.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>Onyx Acceptance Corporation was acquired by Capital One in January 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Expansion_into_retail_banking_.282005.E2.80.93present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion_into_retail_banking_(2005–present)">Expansion into retail banking (2005–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capital_One&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Expansion into retail banking (2005–present)" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>While many other monolines were acquired by larger, diverse banks, Capital One expanded into <a href="/wiki/Retail_banking" title="Retail banking">retail banking</a> with a focus on subprime customers.
</p><p>Capital One acquired <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans,_Louisiana" class="mw-redirect" title="New Orleans, Louisiana">New Orleans, Louisiana</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Hibernia_National_Bank" title="Hibernia National Bank">Hibernia National Bank</a> for $4.9 billion in cash and stock in 2005<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> and acquired <a href="/wiki/Melville,_New_York" title="Melville, New York">Melville, New York</a>-based <a href="/wiki/North_Fork_Bank" title="North Fork Bank">North Fork Bank</a> for $13.2 billion in cash and stock in 2006,<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> which reduced its dependency on credit cards from 90% to 55%.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>In 2007, Capital One acquired NetSpend, a marketer of prepaid debit cards, for $700 million.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/2007_subprime_mortgage_financial_crisis" class="mw-redirect" title="2007 subprime mortgage financial crisis">2007 subprime mortgage financial crisis</a>, Capital One closed its mortgage platform, GreenPoint Mortgage, due in part to investor pressures.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p><p>In 2008, Capital One received an investment of US$3.56 billion from the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Treasury" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Treasury">United States Treasury</a> as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Troubled_Asset_Relief_Program" title="Troubled Asset Relief Program">Troubled Asset Relief Program</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> On June 17, 2009, Capital One completed the repurchase of the stock the company issued to the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Treasury" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Treasury">U.S. Treasury</a> paying a total of US$3.67 billion, resulting in a profit of over $100 million to the U.S. Treasury.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p><p>The <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission">U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission</a> criticized Capital One's conduct during the crisis, claiming that they understated auto loan losses during the <a href="/wiki/Financial_crisis_of_2007%E2%80%932008" class="mw-redirect" title="Financial crisis of 2007–2008">financial crisis of 2007–2008</a>. In 2013, Capital One paid $3.5 million to settle the case, but was not required to directly address the allegations of wrongdoing.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2009, Capital One acquired <a href="/wiki/Chevy_Chase_Bank" title="Chevy Chase Bank">Chevy Chase Bank</a> for $520 million in cash and stock.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2011, Capital One acquired Canada-based <a href="/wiki/Hudson%27s_Bay_Company" title="Hudson's Bay Company">Hudson's Bay Company</a>'s private credit card portfolio from <a href="/wiki/Synchrony_Financial" title="Synchrony Financial">Synchrony Financial</a>, then known as GE Financial.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2011, <a href="/wiki/ING_Group" title="ING Group">ING Group</a> announced the sale of its ING Direct division to Capital One for US$9 billion in cash and stock.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> On August 26, 2011, the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Reserve_Board_of_Governors" title="Federal Reserve Board of Governors">Federal Reserve Board of Governors</a> announced it would hold public hearings on the Capital One acquisition of ING Direct, and extend to October 12, 2011, the public comment period that had been scheduled to end August 22.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> The move came amidst rising scrutiny of the deal on systemic risk, or "Too-Big-to-Fail," performance under the Community Reinvestment Act, and pending legal challenges. A coalition of national civil rights and consumer groups, led by the National Community Reinvestment Coalition, were joined by Rep. <a href="/wiki/Barney_Frank" title="Barney Frank">Barney Frank</a> to challenge immediate approval of the deal. The groups argued that the acquisition was a test of the <a href="/wiki/Dodd-Frank_Wall_Street_Reform_and_Consumer_Protection_Act" class="mw-redirect" title="Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act">Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act</a>, under which systemically risky firms must demonstrate a public benefit that outweighs new risk before they are allowed to grow. Kansas City Federal Reserve Bank head <a href="/wiki/Thomas_M._Hoenig" title="Thomas M. Hoenig">Thomas M. Hoenig</a> was also skeptical of the deal.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> In February 2012, the acquisition was approved by regulators and Capital One completed its acquisition of ING Direct.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Capital One received permission to merge ING into its business in October 2012,<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> and rebranded ING Direct as Capital One 360 in November 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2011, Capital One signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/Kohl%27s" title="Kohl's">Kohl's</a> to handle Kohl's private label credit card program that was previous serviced by <a href="/wiki/Chase_Bank" title="Chase Bank">Chase Bank</a> for a seven-year period for an undisclosed amount.<sup id="cite_ref-wp-2011apr10_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wp-2011apr10-50">[50]</a></sup> The contract between the two company companies was extended in May 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2011, Capital One reached a deal with <a href="/wiki/HSBC" title="HSBC">HSBC</a> to acquire its U.S. credit card operations.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> Capital One paid US$31.3 billion in exchange for US$28.2 billion in loans and $600 million in other assets. The acquisition was completed in May 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p><p>On February 26, 2012, along with several other banks, Capital One announced support for the <a href="/wiki/Isis_Mobile_Wallet" class="mw-redirect" title="Isis Mobile Wallet">Isis Mobile Wallet</a> payment system.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> However, in September 2013, Capital One dropped support for the venture.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012, Capital One closed 41 branch locations.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, Capital One closed several branch locations to leave 174 operating branches in the D.C. metro area.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p><p>On February 19, 2014, Capital One became a 25% owner in <a href="/wiki/ClearXchange" class="mw-redirect" title="ClearXchange">ClearXchange</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Peer-to-peer_transaction" title="Peer-to-peer transaction">Peer-to-peer transaction</a> money transfer service designed to make <a href="/wiki/Electronic_funds_transfer" title="Electronic funds transfer">electronic funds transfers</a> to customers within the same bank and other financial institutions via mobile phone number or email address.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> ClearXchange was sold to Early Warning in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2015, Capital One acquired Level Money, a budgeting app for consumers.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p><p>On July 8, 2015, the company acquired Monsoon, a design studio, development shop, marketing house and strategic consultancy.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, Capital One acquired <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a>'s Healthcare Financial Services unit, which included $8.5 billion in loans made to businesses in the <a href="/wiki/Healthcare" class="mw-redirect" title="Healthcare">healthcare</a> industry, for $9 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2016, Capital One acquired <a href="/wiki/Paribus" title="Paribus">Paribus</a>, a price tracking service, for an undisclosed amount.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2019, Capital One signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/Walmart" title="Walmart">Walmart</a> to handle Walmart's private label and co-branded credit card programs that was previously serviced by <a href="/wiki/Synchrony_Financial" title="Synchrony Financial">Synchrony Financial</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Exit_from_mortgage_banking_.282006.E2.80.932007_and_2011.E2.80.932017.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Exit_from_mortgage_banking_(2006–2007_and_2011–2017)">Exit from mortgage banking (2006–2007 and 2011–2017)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capital_One&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Exit from mortgage banking (2006–2007 and 2011–2017)" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In November 2017, Sanjiv Yajnik announced that the mortgage market is too competitive in the low rate environment to make money in the business.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> The company exited the mortgage origination business on November 7, 2017, laying off 1,100 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> This was the second closure; the first occurred on August 20, 2007, when GreenPoint Mortgage unit was closed.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> GreenPoint had been acquired December 2006 when Capital One paid $13.2 billion to North Fork Bancorp Inc. The re-emergence into the mortgage industry came in 2011 with the purchase of online bank ING Direct USA.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_acquisitions">Other acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capital_One&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Other acquisitions" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In May 2018, the company acquired Confyrm, a digital identity and fraud alert service.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports_marketing">Sports marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capital_One&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sports marketing" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>Since 2001, Capital One has been the principal sponsor of the <a href="/wiki/College_football" title="College football">college football</a> Florida Citrus Bowl, which has been called the <a href="/wiki/Capital_One_Bowl" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital One Bowl">Capital One Bowl</a> since 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> It sponsors a mascot challenge every year, announcing the winner on the day of the Capital One Bowl. The name of the stadium was changed in 2014 to the Orlando Citrus Bowl and was then changed again to <a href="/wiki/Camping_World_Stadium" title="Camping World Stadium">Camping World Stadium</a> in 2016, following a multi-year naming rights sponsorship with <a href="/wiki/Camping_World" title="Camping World">Camping World</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p><p>Capital One is one of the top three sponsors of the <a href="/wiki/NCAA" class="mw-redirect" title="NCAA">NCAA</a>, paying an estimated $35 million annually in exchange for advertising and access to consumer data.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> Capital One also sponsored the <a href="/wiki/EFL_Cup" title="EFL Cup">EFL Cup</a>, an English Soccer Competition, from 2012 to 2016. The company sponsored <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_United_F.C." title="Sheffield United F.C.">Sheffield United F.C.</a> from 2006 to 2008. In 2017, the company became the sponsor of the <a href="/wiki/Capital_One_Arena" title="Capital One Arena">Capital One Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Washington_D.C." class="mw-redirect" title="Washington D.C.">Washington D.C.</a><sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, to celebrate the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Capitals" title="Washington Capitals">Washington Capitals</a>' second-ever <a href="/wiki/2018_Stanley_Cup_Finals" title="2018 Stanley Cup Finals">Stanley Cup Finals</a> appearance, the firm temporarily changed its logo by replacing the word "Capital" with the Capitals' <a href="/wiki/Washington_Capitals#Logos_and_jerseys" title="Washington Capitals">titular logo</a>, without the "s" plural.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_citizenship">Corporate citizenship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capital_One&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Corporate citizenship" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>Capital One operates some charitable programs, such as the "No Hassle Giving" <a href="/wiki/Web_portal" title="Web portal">web portal</a>, in which Capital One covers the transaction fees on customer and non-customer donations made through the site.<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> The accountability organization <a href="/wiki/National_Committee_for_Responsive_Philanthropy" title="National Committee for Responsive Philanthropy">National Committee for Responsive Philanthropy</a> has been highly critical of Capital One's relatively low rate of giving, stating that "Capital One's philanthropic track record is dismal".<sup id="cite_ref-NCRP_82-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NCRP-82">[82]</a></sup> The organization pointed out that Capital One's donations of 0.024% of revenue were much less than the industry median of 0.11% of revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-NCRP_82-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NCRP-82">[82]</a></sup> Capital One has disputed the groups figures, saying that "... In 2011 alone, our giving totals are more than 6 times greater ($30 million) than the number given by the NCRP".<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism_and_legal_actions">Criticism and legal actions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capital_One&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Criticism and legal actions" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Fines_for_misleading_customers_to_pay_extra_for_services">Fines for misleading customers to pay extra for services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capital_One&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Fines for misleading customers to pay extra for services" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In July 2012, Capital One was fined by the <a href="/wiki/Office_of_the_Comptroller_of_the_Currency" title="Office of the Comptroller of the Currency">Office of the Comptroller of the Currency</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Consumer_Financial_Protection_Bureau" title="Consumer Financial Protection Bureau">Consumer Financial Protection Bureau</a> for misleading millions of its customers, such as paying extra for payment protection or credit monitoring when they took out a card.<sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup> The company agreed to pay $210 million to settle the legal action and to refund two million customers.<sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup> This was the CFPB's first public enforcement action.<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Automated_dialing_to_customers.27_phones"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Automated_dialing_to_customers'_phones">Automated dialing to customers' phones</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capital_One&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Automated dialing to customers' phones" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In August 2014, Capital One and three collection agencies entered into an agreement to pay $75.5 million to end a consolidated class action lawsuit pending in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_District_Court_for_the_Northern_District_of_Illinois" title="United States District Court for the Northern District of Illinois">United States District Court for the Northern District of Illinois</a> alleging that the companies used an automated dialer to call customers’ cellphones without consent, which is a violation of the <a href="/wiki/Telephone_Consumer_Protection_Act_of_1991" title="Telephone Consumer Protection Act of 1991">Telephone Consumer Protection Act of 1991</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup> It is notable that this legal action involved informational telephone calls, which are not subject to the "prior express written consent" requirements which have been in place for telemarketing calls since October 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_amendment_to_terms_of_use_to_allow_personal_visits">2014 amendment to terms of use to allow personal visits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capital_One&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2014 amendment to terms of use to allow personal visits" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2014, Capital One amended its <a href="/wiki/Terms_of_use" class="mw-redirect" title="Terms of use">terms of use</a> to allow it to "contact you in any manner we choose", including a "personal visit . . . at your home and at your place of employment." It also asserted its right to "modify or suppress caller ID and similar services and identify ourselves on these services in any manner we choose."<sup id="cite_ref-latimes_89-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-89">[89]</a></sup> The company stated that it would not actually make personal visits to customers except "As a last resort, . . . if it becomes necessary to repossess [a] sports vehicle".<sup id="cite_ref-latimes_89-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-89">[89]</a></sup> Capital One also attributed its assertion of a right to "<a href="/wiki/Caller_ID_spoofing" title="Caller ID spoofing">spoof</a>" as necessary because "sometimes the number is 'displayed differently' by 'some local phone exchanges,' something that is 'beyond our control'".<sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="July_2019_security_breach">July 2019 security breach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capital_One&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: July 2019 security breach" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Capital One publicly acknowledged on 29 July 2019 that they had found unauthorized access had occurred ten days earlier by an individual who had breached the account and identity security of 106 million people in the United States and Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Federal_Bureau_of_Investigation" title="Federal Bureau of Investigation">FBI</a> arrested Paige Thompson, who had previously worked as a <a href="/wiki/Software" title="Software">software</a> <a href="/wiki/Engineer" title="Engineer">engineer</a> for <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Web_Services" title="Amazon Web Services">Amazon Web Services</a>, Capital One's <a href="/wiki/Cloud_computing" title="Cloud computing">cloud hosting</a> company. Capital One declared that Thompson had accessed about 140,000 <a href="/wiki/Social_Security_numbers" class="mw-redirect" title="Social Security numbers">Social Security numbers</a>, a million Canadian <a href="/wiki/Social_Insurance_Number" title="Social Insurance Number">social insurance numbers</a>; 80,000 bank account numbers, and an unknown number of names and addresses of customers. Capital One began offering free <a href="/wiki/Credit_report_monitoring" title="Credit report monitoring">credit monitoring services</a> to those affected by the breach.<sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-93" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-93">[93]</a></sup></p><p>Thompson's employment at Amazon appears to have ended in September 2016. Amazon stated that the security vulnerability she used to access Capital One could have been discovered by anyone, the information that facilitated her activity was not gained from work at Amazon, and that she gained access via "a misconfiguration of the (Capital One-designed) web application and not the underlying (Amazon-designed) cloud-based infrastructure".<sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup></p>

<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Capital_One_Response">Capital One Response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capital_One&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Capital One Response" data-section="16" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>Critics lambasted the bank's effort to downplay the hack while investigations were ongoing, and described the bank as more concerned about its image than the needs of its clients. Several Capital One customers stated that the first time they heard about the hack was through the media and the bank did not disclose the breach or explain its implications to affected customers.<sup id="cite_ref-96" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-96">[96]</a></sup>. On <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a> and in the mainstream press, Capital One's contradictory July 2019 press statement was mocked<sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97">[97]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-98" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-98">[98]</a></sup> for saying "No bank account numbers or Social Security numbers were compromised," but then listing hundreds of thousands of bank account numbers and social security numbers that were compromised. 
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Lawsuits">Lawsuits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capital_One&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Lawsuits" data-section="17" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>Lawsuits were filed against Capital One and its employees in federal<sup id="cite_ref-99" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99">[99]</a></sup> and circuit courts.<sup id="cite_ref-100" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-100">[100]</a></sup></p><p>Additional Lawsuits were filed against both Amazon and Github, alleging they were aware of the exploit but did not act to fix or patch the vulnerability<sup id="cite_ref-101" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-101">[101]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Federal_Government_Violations">Federal Government Violations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capital_One&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Federal Government Violations" data-section="18" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Relative to other large banks, Capital one has received fewer sanctions or default judgments against it.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> But some<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (February 2020)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> allude this is a result of its closer proximity to DC and possible relations to federal regulators in Washington, DC.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 2015 the bank disclosed that it was under federal investigations for bank fraud, money laundering, and possible racketeering charges. No further information was given and  government investigators would only confirm that it was under scrutiny for "unspecified charges".<sup id="cite_ref-102" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-102">[102]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, Capital one was fined $100 million for failure to monitor, detect, and prevent money laundering.<sup id="cite_ref-103" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-103">[103]</a></sup> Charging documents <sup id="cite_ref-104" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-104">[104]</a></sup>specified Capital One failed to file suspicious activity reports, had deficiencies in its risk assessment, remote deposit capture and generally had weaknesses that compromised  national banking security controls. The bank was the subject of a larger investigation that feds alleged siphoned funds out of US jurisdiction to safe havens.
