<p><b>Intuit Inc.</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Business_software" title="Business software">business</a> and <a href="/wiki/Financial_software" title="Financial software">financial software</a> company that develops and sells financial, accounting, and <a href="/wiki/Tax_preparation" class="mw-redirect" title="Tax preparation">tax preparation</a> software and related services for <a href="/wiki/Small_business" title="Small business">small businesses</a>, <a href="/wiki/Accountants" class="mw-redirect" title="Accountants">accountants</a>, and individuals. The company is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Mountain_View,_California" title="Mountain View, California">Mountain View, California</a>. As of 2019,<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Intuit&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> more than 95% of its revenues and earnings come from its activities within the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Intuit's products include the tax preparation application <a href="/wiki/TurboTax" title="TurboTax">TurboTax</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Small_business" title="Small business">small business</a> accounting program <a href="/wiki/QuickBooks" title="QuickBooks">QuickBooks</a>.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intuit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company was founded in 1983 by <a href="/wiki/Scott_Cook" title="Scott Cook">Scott Cook</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tom_Proulx" title="Tom Proulx">Tom Proulx</a> in <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California" title="Palo Alto, California">Palo Alto, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>Intuit was conceived by Scott Cook, whose prior work at Procter &amp; Gamble helped him realize that <a href="/wiki/Personal_computer" title="Personal computer">personal computers</a> would lend themselves towards replacements for paper-and-pencil based personal accounting.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On his quest to find a programmer he ended up running into <a href="/wiki/Tom_Proulx" title="Tom Proulx">Tom Proulx</a> at <a href="/wiki/Stanford" class="mw-redirect" title="Stanford">Stanford</a>. The two started Intuit, which initially operated out of a modest room on University Avenue in <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto" class="mw-redirect" title="Palo Alto">Palo Alto</a>. The first version of Quicken was coded in Microsoft's <a href="/wiki/BASIC_programming_language" class="mw-redirect" title="BASIC programming language">BASIC programming language</a> for the IBM PC and <a href="/wiki/UCSD_Pascal" title="UCSD Pascal">UCSD Pascal</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Apple_II" title="Apple II">Apple II</a> by Tom Proulx and had to contend with a dozen serious competitors.
</p><p>In 1991  Microsoft decided to produce a competitor to Quicken called <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Money" title="Microsoft Money">Microsoft Money</a>. To win retailers' loyalty, Intuit included a US$15 rebate coupon, redeemable on software customers purchased in their stores. This was the first time a software company offered a rebate.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>Roughly around the same time the company engaged <a href="/wiki/John_Doerr" title="John Doerr">John Doerr</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kleiner_Perkins_Caufield_%26_Byers" class="mw-redirect" title="Kleiner Perkins Caufield &amp; Byers">Kleiner Perkins Caufield &amp; Byers</a> and diversified its product lineup. In 1993 Intuit went public and used the proceeds to make a key acquisition: the tax-preparation software company Chipsoft based in <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a>. The time after the <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">IPO</a> was marked by rapid growth and culminated with a buyout offer from <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> in 1994; at this time Intuit's <a href="/wiki/Market_capitalization" title="Market capitalization">market capitalization</a> reached US$2 <a href="/wiki/1000000000_(number)" class="mw-redirect" title="1000000000 (number)">billion</a>.
</p><p>When the buyout fell through because of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Justice" title="United States Department of Justice">United States Department of Justice</a>'s disapproval, the company came under intense pressure in the late 1990s when Microsoft started to compete vigorously with its core Quicken business.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In response, Intuit launched new web-based products and solutions and put more emphasis on QuickBooks and on TurboTax. The company made a number of investments around this time. Among others, it purchased a large stake in <a href="/wiki/Excite" title="Excite">Excite</a> and acquired Lacerte Software, a <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a>-based developer of tax preparation software used by tax professionals. It also divested itself of its online bill payment service unit and extended and strengthened its partnership with <a href="/wiki/CheckFree" class="mw-redirect" title="CheckFree">CheckFree</a>.
</p><p>In June 2013, Intuit announced it would sell its financial services unit to private equity firm <a href="/wiki/Thoma_Bravo" title="Thoma Bravo">Thoma Bravo</a> for $1.03 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2015, Intuit laid off approximately 5% of its workforce as part of a company reorganization.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>As of May 2018, Intuit had more than US$5 billion in annual <a href="/wiki/Revenue" title="Revenue">revenue</a> and a market capitalization of about US$50 billion.
</p><p>In August 2018, CEO <a href="/wiki/Brad_D._Smith" title="Brad D. Smith">Brad D. Smith</a> announced that he would step down as Intuit's CEO at the end of 2018. The company announced that Sasan Goodarzi would become Intuit's CEO at the beginning of 2019. Smith will remain chairman of Intuit's board of directors.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intuit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Canada" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><b>Intuit Canada ULC</b>, an indirect <a href="/wiki/Subsidiary" title="Subsidiary">wholly owned subsidiary</a> of Intuit, is a developer of financial management and tax preparation software for <a href="/wiki/Personal_finance" title="Personal finance">personal finance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Small_business" title="Small business">small business</a> <a href="/wiki/Accounting_software" title="Accounting software">accounting</a>. Services are delivered on a variety of platforms including <a href="/wiki/Desktop_software" class="mw-redirect" title="Desktop software">application software</a>, <a href="/wiki/Software_plus_services" class="mw-redirect" title="Software plus services">software connected to services</a>, <a href="/wiki/SaaS" class="mw-redirect" title="SaaS">software as a service</a>, <a href="/wiki/Platform_as_a_service" title="Platform as a service">platform as a service</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mobile_application" class="mw-redirect" title="Mobile application">mobile applications</a>. Intuit Canada has employees located all across Canada, with offices in <a href="/wiki/Edmonton" title="Edmonton">Edmonton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mississauga" title="Mississauga">Mississauga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>.
</p><p><b>Intuit Canada</b> traces its origins to the 1993 acquisition by Intuit of a Canadian <a href="/wiki/Tax_preparation_software" class="mw-redirect" title="Tax preparation software">tax preparation software</a> developer. In 1992, Edmontonians and <a href="/wiki/University_of_Alberta" title="University of Alberta">University of Alberta</a> graduates <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Johnson_(Canadian_executive)" title="Bruce Johnson (Canadian executive)">Bruce Johnson</a> and Chad Frederick had built a <a href="/wiki/Tax_preparation_software" class="mw-redirect" title="Tax preparation software">tax preparation</a> product called WINTAX – Canada's first<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Microsoft Windows</a>-based personal tax preparation software. In 1993, they agreed to be acquired by Chipsoft, manufacturer of the U.S. personal income tax software <a href="/wiki/TurboTax" title="TurboTax">TurboTax</a>. Shortly after the WINTAX acquisition, Chipsoft agreed to merge with Intuit, the developer of the <a href="/wiki/Quicken" title="Quicken">Quicken</a> financial software.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Intuit Canada continued to update and support the WINTAX software, which was renamed QuickTax in 1995 and then renamed TurboTax in 2010. Intuit Canada quickly became the hub for international development at Intuit, producing localized versions of Quicken and <a href="/wiki/QuickBooks" title="QuickBooks">QuickBooks</a> for Canada (in French and English) and the United Kingdom. The U.K. version of Quicken was discontinued in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Online_communities">Online communities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intuit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Online communities" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>Intuit has several online communities, some of which offer integration or cross-sells into other Intuit products. QuickBooks online community for QuickBooks users and small business owners, Quicken Online Community for Quicken users and those who need help with the personal finances, and the Accountant Online Community and Jump Up. Each consists of blogs, an expert locator map and event calendar, forums and discussion groups, podcasts, videocasts and webinars, and other user-created content.
</p><p>JumpUp (formerly JackRabbit Beta) is a free social networking and resources site for small business owners and/or start-ups. Free tools and services include an interactive business planner, online training for developing a successful business plan, starting costs calculator, cash flow calculator, break-even calculator, templates for business planning and sample business plans.
</p><p>Intuitlabs.com is a website Intuit created to get new solutions into people's hands quickly. The early versions of these products and services are called rough cuts, and they're offered for free so people try them and give feedback to enable rapid improvement and make sure they solve real problems well.
</p><p>TaxAlmanac is a free online tax research resource. Content on TaxAlmanac is written by tax professionals from across the country and takes advantage of the knowledge of academia as well as practitioners. The site includes key information including the Internal Revenue Code, Treasury Regulations, Tax Court Cases, and a variety of articles.
</p><p>Modeled after <a href="/wiki/English_Wikipedia" title="English Wikipedia">English Wikipedia</a>, TaxAlmanac was launched in May 2005. The June 6, 2005 edition of Time magazine featured an article entitled "It's a Wiki, Wiki World"<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> on English Wikipedia in which TaxAlmanac was highlighted as "A Community of Customers". The November 21, 2005 edition of <i><a href="/wiki/Business_Week" class="mw-redirect" title="Business Week">Business Week</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> featured an article titled "50 Smart Ways to Use the Web" in which TaxAlmanac was selected as one of the 50. TaxAlmanac made the short list as one of the 7 in the collaboration category.
</p><p>Intuit shut down TaxAlmanac effective June 1, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Many of the users have migrated to a new site called TaxProTalk.com.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>Zipingo was a free <a href="/wiki/Website" title="Website">website</a> where users could rate services such as <a href="/wiki/Independent_contractor" title="Independent contractor">contractors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Restaurants" class="mw-redirect" title="Restaurants">restaurants</a>, and other businesses. Ratings and comments were either entered from the website or through Quicken and QuickBooks. The site was closed by Intuit on August 23, 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>

<p>For the fiscal year 2018, Intuit reported earnings of US$1.211 billion, with an annual revenue of US$5.964 billion, an increase of 15.2% over the previous fiscal cycle. Intuit's shares traded at over $194 per share, and its market capitalization was valued at over US$55 billion in November 2018.

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="1990s">1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intuit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 1990s" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1993, Intuit acquired Chipsoft, a tax preparation software company based in San Diego.
</p><p>In 1994, Intuit acquired the tax preparation software division of Best Programs of Reston, VA.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> In the same year, Intuit acquired Parsons Technology from <a href="/wiki/Bob_Parsons" title="Bob Parsons">Bob Parsons</a> for $64 million.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p><p>In 1996, Intuit acquired GALT Technologies, Inc of Pittsburgh, PA.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p><p>In 1998, Intuit acquired <a href="/wiki/Lacerte" class="mw-redirect" title="Lacerte">Lacerte</a> Software Corp., which now operates as an Intuit subsidiary.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup>  The Lacerte subsidiary focuses on tax software used by professional accountants who prepare taxes for a living. It is generally used by larger firms with more complex workflows and clients.
</p><p>On March 2, 1999, Intuit acquired Computing Resources Inc. for approximately $200 million. This acquisition allowed Intuit to offer a payroll processing platform through its QuickBooks software program.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> In December 1999, Intuit purchased Rock Financial for a sum of $532M. The company was renamed <a href="/wiki/Quicken_Loans" title="Quicken Loans">Quicken Loans</a>. In June 2002, Rock Financial founder <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gilbert_(businessman)" class="mw-redirect" title="Dan Gilbert (businessman)">Dan Gilbert</a> led a small group of private investors in purchasing the Quicken Loans subsidiary back from Intuit.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s">2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intuit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2000s" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>2001, Intuit invests in UK market hiring local management team led by Stephen Lee, Managing Director &amp; Neil Atkins, Marketing Director with an aim to become Europe's leading B2B &amp; B2C packaged accounts solution.
</p><p>In 2002, Intuit acquired <a href="/wiki/Management_Reports_International" class="mw-redirect" title="Management Reports International">Management Reports International</a>, a Cleveland-based real estate management software firm. The firm was renamed Intuit Real Estate Solutions (IRES) and offers real estate management solutions for Windows and the web.
</p><p>In 2003, Intuit Inc. acquired 'Innovative Merchant Solutions' (IMS). IMS provided merchant services to all types of businesses nationwide. The acquisition gave Intuit the ability to process credit cards through its core product, QuickBooks, without the need for hardware leasing. They can also provide traditional terminal-based credit card processing and downloading transactions directly into the QuickBooks software.
</p><p>In November 2005, Intuit acquired MyCorporation.com, an online business document filing service, for $20 million from original founders Philip and Nellie Akalp.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2006, Intuit acquired StepUp Commerce, an online localized product listing syndicator, for $60 million in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2006, Intuit acquired <a href="/wiki/Digital_Insight" title="Digital Insight">Digital Insight</a>, a provider of online banking services.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2007, Intuit acquired Electronic Clearing House<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> to add check processing power.
</p><p>In December 2007, Intuit acquired <a href="/wiki/Homestead_Technologies" title="Homestead Technologies">Homestead Technologies</a>  <sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> which offers web site creation and e-commerce tools targeted at the small business market, for $170 million.
</p><p>In April 2009, Intuit acquired Boorah, a restaurant review site.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p><p>On June 2, 2009, Intuit Inc. announced the signing of a definitive agreement to purchase PayCycle Inc., an online payroll services, in an all-cash transaction for approximately $170 million.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p><p>On September 14, 2009, Intuit Inc. agreed to acquire Mint.com, a free online personal finance service, for $170 million.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s">2010s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intuit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2010s" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On January 15, 2010, Intuit Inc. spun off Intuit Real Estate Solutions (which Intuit acquired in 2002) as a stand-alone company.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> The new company took on its previous moniker, and is now known as <a href="/wiki/MRI_Software" title="MRI Software">MRI Software</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p><p>On May 21, 2010, Intuit Inc. acquired MedFusion, a Cary, NC leader of Patient to Provider communications for approximately $91 million.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p><p>On August 10, 2010, Intuit Inc. acquired the personal finance management app Cha-Ching.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p><p>On June 28, 2011, Intuit Inc. acquired the Web banking technology assets of Mobile Money Ventures, a mobile financial solutions provider, for an undisclosed amount. This acquisition is expected to position Intuit as the largest online and mobile technology provider to financial institutions.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p><p>On May 18, 2012, Intuit Inc. acquired Demandforce, an automated small business marketing, and customer communications SaaS provider for approximately $423.5 million.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup></p><p>On August 15, 2012, Intuit, Inc, announced an agreement to sell their 'Grow Your Business' business unit to <a href="/wiki/Endurance_International_Group" title="Endurance International Group">Endurance International</a>. The carve-out included the Intuit Websites and Weblistings products which were formed from the Homestead Technologies and StepUp Commerce acquisitions.<sup id="cite_ref-thewhir1_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thewhir1-67">[67]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p><p>On July 1, 2013, Intuit announced an agreement to sell their Intuit Financial Services (IFS) business unit (formerly known as Digital Insight) to <a href="/wiki/Thoma_Bravo" title="Thoma Bravo">Thoma Bravo</a> for more than $1.03 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p><p>On August 19, 2013, Intuit announced that they had sold their Intuit Health business unit (formerly known as MedFusion) back to MedFusion's founder, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Malik" title="Steve Malik">Steve Malik</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2013, Intuit Inc. acquired <a href="/wiki/Tax_planning" class="mw-redirect" title="Tax planning">tax planning</a> software Good April for an undisclosed amount.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup></p><p>On October 23, 2013, Intuit acquired Level Up Analytics, a data consulting firm.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p><p>On October 30, 2013, Intuit Inc. acquired Full Slate, a developer of appointment scheduling software for small businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2014, Intuit Inc. bought Invitco to help bookkeepers put bill processing in the cloud.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2014, Intuit Inc. acquired Check for approximately $360 million to offer bill pay across small business and personal finance products.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2014, Intuit Inc. acquired Acrede, UK-based provider of global, cross-border and cloud-based payroll services.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2015, Intuit Inc. acquired Playbook HR.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2016, Intuit Inc. announced an agreement to sell Demandforce to <a href="/wiki/Internet_Brands" title="Internet Brands">Internet Brands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></p><p>On March 3, 2016, Intuit announced plans to sell Quicken to <a href="/wiki/H.I.G._Capital" title="H.I.G. Capital">H.I.G. Capital</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup></p><p>On March 8, 2016, Intuit announced plans to sell Quickbase to private equity firm <a href="/wiki/Welsh,_Carson,_Anderson_%26_Stowe" title="Welsh, Carson, Anderson &amp; Stowe">Welsh, Carson, Anderson &amp; Stowe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup></p><p>On May 1, 2017, Intuit announced to sell TruPay.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>Intuit acquired Bankstream in 2017.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>On December 5, 2017 Intuit announced its acquisition of TSheets for $340 million.<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2020s">2020s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intuit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2020s" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On February 24, 2020, Intuit announced that it planned to acquire <a href="/wiki/Credit_Karma" title="Credit Karma">Credit Karma</a> for $7.1 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(7)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Lobbying">Lobbying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intuit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Lobbying" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-7 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-7">
<p>In 2007, Intuit <a href="/wiki/Lobbying" title="Lobbying">lobbied</a> to make sure taxpayers cannot electronically file their <a href="/wiki/Tax_return_(United_States)" title="Tax return (United States)">tax returns</a> directly to the <a href="/wiki/IRS" class="mw-redirect" title="IRS">IRS</a> by negotiating a deal preventing the IRS from setting up its own web portal for e-filing.<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup></p><p>In 2009, the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> reported that Intuit spent nearly $2 million in political contributions to eliminate free online state tax filing for low-income residents in California.<sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup> According to the <i>New York Times</i>, from 2009–2014, Intuit spent nearly $13 million lobbying, as reported by <a href="/wiki/Center_for_Responsive_Politics" title="Center for Responsive Politics">Open Secrets</a>, as much as <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple</a>. Intuit spent $1 million on the race for the state comptroller to support Tony Strickland, a Republican who opposed <a href="/wiki/ReadyReturn" title="ReadyReturn">ReadyReturn</a>, against John Chang, a Democrat who supported ReadyRun (and won). Joseph Bankman, professor of tax law, Stanford Law School, and advocate of simplified filing, believes that the campaign warned politicians that if they supported free filing, Intuit would help their opponents.<sup id="cite_ref-Manjoo_85-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Manjoo-85">[85]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup></p><p>On March 26, 2013, <a href="/wiki/ProPublica" title="ProPublica">ProPublica</a> reported that the company lobbied against return-free filing as recently as 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup> One year later, ProPublica reported that the company appeared to be linked to a number of <a href="/wiki/Op-ed" title="Op-ed">op-eds</a> and letters to <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">Congress</a> in a campaign advocating against direct tax filing backed by the <a href="/wiki/Computer_%26_Communications_Industry_Association" title="Computer &amp; Communications Industry Association">Computer &amp; Communications Industry Association</a>, an advocacy organization of which Intuit is a member.<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup></p>

<p>In October 2008, Intuit's Canadian <a href="/wiki/Subsidiary" title="Subsidiary">subsidiary</a> Intuit Canada was named one of <a href="/wiki/Alberta%27s_Top_Employers" class="mw-redirect" title="Alberta's Top Employers">Alberta's Top Employers</a> by Mediacorp Canada Inc., which was announced by the <i><a href="/wiki/Calgary_Herald" title="Calgary Herald">Calgary Herald</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Edmonton_Journal" title="Edmonton Journal">Edmonton Journal</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup> In 2020, <i><a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)">Fortune</a></i> magazine ranked Intuit at number 11 on their Fortune List of the Top 100 Companies to Work For in 2020 based on an employee survey of satisfaction.<sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup></p>
