</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Analog_Devices&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company was founded by two <a href="/wiki/MIT" class="mw-redirect" title="MIT">MIT</a> graduates, <a href="/wiki/Ray_Stata" title="Ray Stata">Ray Stata</a> and Matthew Lorber in 1965.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The same year, the company released its first product, the model 101 op amp,<sup id="cite_ref-electronicsweekly.com_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-electronicsweekly.com-11">[11]</a></sup> which was a hockey-puck sized module used in test and measurement equipment.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 1967, the company published the first issue of its technical magazine, Analog Dialogue.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In 1969, Analog Devices filed an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and became a <a href="/wiki/Publicly_traded_company" class="mw-redirect" title="Publicly traded company">publicly traded company</a>. Ten years later, the company was listed on the New York Stock Exchange.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In 1973, the company was the first to launch laser trim wafers and the first CMOS digital-to-analog converter.<sup id="cite_ref-electronicsweekly.com_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-electronicsweekly.com-11">[11]</a></sup> By 1996, the company reported over $1 billion in company revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-timeline_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeline-16">[16]</a></sup> That same year, Jerald Fishman was named president and CEO, a position he held until his death in 2013 (see below).<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In 2000, Analog Devices's sales grew by over 75% to $2.578 Billion and the company acquired five companies including BCO Technologies PLC, a manufacturer of thick film semiconductors, for $150 million.<sup id="cite_ref-timeline_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeline-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2008, <a href="/wiki/ON_Semiconductor" title="ON Semiconductor">ON Semiconductor</a> completed the acquisition of the CPU Voltage and PC Thermal Monitoring Business from Analog Devices., for $184 million.
</p><p>By 2004, Analog Devices had a customer base of 60,000 and its portfolio included over 10,000 products.<sup id="cite_ref-timeline_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeline-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2016, Analog and <a href="/wiki/Linear_Technology" title="Linear Technology">Linear Technology</a> agreed that Analog would acquire Linear in an approximately $14.8 billion cash and stock deal.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Locations">Locations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Analog_Devices&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Locations" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Analog Devices is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Norwood,_Massachusetts" title="Norwood, Massachusetts">Norwood, Massachusetts</a>, with regional headquarters
located in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>; <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>; <a href="/wiki/Limerick" title="Limerick">Limerick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical">Historical</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Analog_Devices&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Historical" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jerald_G._Fishman" title="Jerald G. Fishman">Jerald G. Fishman</a> was the CEO and president of Analog Devices from 1996 until his death in March 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> In 2004, Fishman was named CEO of the Year by Electronic Business. He was a 35-year veteran of Analog Devices and also served on the board of directors of Analog Devices, <a href="/wiki/Cognex_Corporation" title="Cognex Corporation">Cognex Corporation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Xilinx" title="Xilinx">Xilinx</a>.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Products_and_technologies">Products and technologies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Analog_Devices&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Products and technologies" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>Analog Devices products include analog signal processing and digital signal processing technologies.<sup id="cite_ref-reuters_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-41">[41]</a></sup> These technologies include data converters, amplifiers, <a href="/wiki/Radio_frequency" title="Radio frequency">radio frequency (RF)</a> technologies, <a href="/wiki/Embedded_system" title="Embedded system">embedded processors</a> or digital signal processing (DSP) ICs, power management, and interface products.<sup id="cite_ref-reuters_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-41">[41]</a></sup></p><p><b>Data converters</b> include <a href="/wiki/Analog-to-digital_converter" title="Analog-to-digital converter">analog-to-digital converters</a> (ADCs) and <a href="/wiki/Digital-to-analog_converter" title="Digital-to-analog converter">digital-to-analog converters</a> (DACs)<sup id="cite_ref-reuters_41-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-41">[41]</a></sup> that convert electrical signal representations of real-world analog phenomena, such as light, sound, waveforms, temperature, motion, and pressure into digital signals or data, and back again.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Analog Devices ADC and DAC ICs are used in <a href="/wiki/Medical_system" class="mw-redirect" title="Medical system">medical systems</a>, scientific instrumentation, wireless and wired communications, <a href="/wiki/Radar" title="Radar">radar</a>, industrial <a href="/wiki/Process_control" title="Process control">process control</a>, audio and video equipment, and other digital-processing-based systems, where an accurate signal conversion is critical. Data converters account for more than 50% of ADI's revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> ADI's companion amplifier ICs provide accurate, high-speed and precise signals for driving data converters and are key for applications such as digital audio, current sensing, and precision instrumentation.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p><p>The company's data converter chips are used by <a href="/wiki/National_Instruments" title="National Instruments">National Instruments</a> in high-precision measurement instrumentation systems.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Its data converters and amplifiers are also used by scientists and researchers in project "IceCube" – an underground telescope that uses digital optical modules (DOMS) to detect subatomic particles in the South Pole.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p><p><b>Power management products</b> for customers in the industrial, wireless infrastructure and digital camera markets support signal chain design requirements, such as dynamic range, transient performance, and reliability.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p><p><b>Interface products</b> include a broad range of interface IC products offered by the company in product categories such as CAN (controller area network),<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> digital isolators,<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> level translators, LVDS, mobile I/O expander and keyboard controller, USB, and RS-232.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p><p><b>Amplifiers</b> includes precision and operational amplifiers,<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> instrumentation,<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> current sense, differential amplifiers,<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> audio amplifiers, video amplifiers/buffers/filters, variable gain amplifiers, comparators, voltage, other specialty amplifiers and products for special linear functions.
</p><p><b>Radio frequency integrated circuits (RFICs)</b><sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> address the RF signal chain and simplify RF system development.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> The company's RF portfolio includes TruPwr<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> RMS power detectors and logarithmic amplifiers; <a href="/wiki/Phase-locked_loop" title="Phase-locked loop">PLL</a> and <a href="/wiki/Direct_digital_synthesizer" class="mw-redirect" title="Direct digital synthesizer">DDS</a> synthesizers; RF prescalers; variable gain amplifiers;<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> ADC drivers, gain blocks, LNAs and other RF amplifiers.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p><p><b>Processors and DSP</b> are programmable signal processing integrated circuits that execute specialized software programs, or algorithms, associated with processing digitized real-time data. Analog Devices Processors and DSPs are the <a href="/wiki/Blackfin" title="Blackfin">Blackfin</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Super_Harvard_Architecture_Single-Chip_Computer" title="Super Harvard Architecture Single-Chip Computer">SHARC</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> SigmaDSP,<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/TigerSHARC" title="TigerSHARC">TigerSHARC</a>, ADSP-21xx and Precision Analog Microcontrollers. These make up the company's embedded processing and DSP portfolio, that are multi-DSP signal processing,<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_2">Historical</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Analog_Devices&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Historical" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Analog Devices had a line of <a href="/wiki/Microtechnology#Micro_electromechanical_systems" title="Microtechnology">micro-electromechanical systems</a> (MEMS) microphones until it sold that business to <a href="/wiki/InvenSense" title="InvenSense">InvenSense</a> in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> Analog Devices MEMS microphones were found in smart phones, tablet PCs, security systems, and medical applications.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> ADI's MEMS <a href="/wiki/Accelerometers" class="mw-redirect" title="Accelerometers">accelerometers</a> were designed into game pad controllers by Microsoft, Logitech and Pellican.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Markets">Markets</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Analog_Devices&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Markets" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Healthcare">Healthcare</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Analog_Devices&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Healthcare" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Analog Devices sells linear, mixed-signal, MEMS and digital signal processing technologies for medical imaging, patient monitoring, medical instrumentation and home healthcare.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> The company's precision signal-processing components and Blackfin digital signal processors are included in Karmelsonix's Wholter, an overnight pulmonary monitor, and the Wheezometer, a personal asthmatic assessment device.<sup id="cite_ref-paul_74-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paul-74">[74]</a></sup> Accelerometers produced by Analog Devices are included in <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.pocketcpr.com">ZOLL Medical's PocketCPR</a>, which measures the depth of chest compressions and provides audible and visual feedback to a rescuer to allow adjustment to proper depth and to the correct rate of compression.<sup id="cite_ref-paul_74-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paul-74">[74]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Automotive">Automotive</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Analog_Devices&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Automotive" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Analog Devices develops components for safety systems, such as stability control systems and driver assistance systems, infotainment and interior applications.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> Powertrain systems in hybrid and electric vehicles use high-precision data conversion products in battery monitoring and control systems.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Industrial">Industrial</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Analog_Devices&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Industrial" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Analog Devices industrial market<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> includes process control systems that help drive productivity, energy efficiency and reliability.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BusinessWeek_78-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BusinessWeek-78">[78]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Consumer">Consumer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Analog_Devices&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Consumer" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Analog Devices has technology for consumer electronics, which includes signal processing circuits for image processing, auto focus, and image stabilization for digital still cameras and camcorders, audio and video processors for home theater systems,<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> DVD recorders, and high-definition televisions and advanced touch screen controllers for portable media devices.<sup id="cite_ref-BusinessWeek_78-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BusinessWeek-78">[78]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(6)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Competitors">Competitors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Analog_Devices&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Competitors" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-6 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-6">
<p>In 2009, Databeans published its report on the top semiconductor analog suppliers. Analog Devices was named number two with other suppliers including: <a href="/wiki/Texas_Instruments" title="Texas Instruments">Texas Instruments</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Semiconductor" title="National Semiconductor">National Semiconductor</a> (acquired by Texas Instruments in 2011),<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Maxim_Integrated_Products" class="mw-redirect" title="Maxim Integrated Products">Maxim Integrated Products</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Linear_Technology" title="Linear Technology">Linear Technology</a> (collaborating with Analog Devices since March 2017).<sup id="cite_ref-dylan_81-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dylan-81">[81]</a></sup> Other competitors include: <a href="/wiki/Infineon_Technologies" title="Infineon Technologies">Infineon Technologies</a>, <a href="/wiki/STMicroelectronics" title="STMicroelectronics">STMicroelectronics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Intersil" title="Intersil">Intersil Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dylan_81-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dylan-81">[81]</a></sup>
In 2011, Analog Devices was the third ranked analog semiconductor supplier.<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(7)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Analog_Dialogue"><i>Analog Dialogue</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Analog_Devices&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Analog Dialogue" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-7 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-7">
<p>In 1967, Analog Devices first published <i><a href="/wiki/Analog_Dialogue" title="Analog Dialogue">Analog Dialogue</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup> Dan Sheingold took the position of editor two years later, which he held for over four decades.<sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sheingold_85-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sheingold-85">[85]</a></sup> The current editor is Scott Wayne.<sup id="cite_ref-Sheingold_85-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sheingold-85">[85]</a></sup> It is currently the longest-running in-house publication in the electronics industry.<sup id="cite_ref-free_86-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-free-86">[86]</a></sup></p><p><i>Analog Dialogue</i> is a forum for the exchange of circuits, systems, and software for real-world signal processing and is the technical <a href="/wiki/Magazine" title="Magazine">magazine</a> published by Analog Devices.<sup id="cite_ref-free_86-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-free-86">[86]</a></sup> It discusses products, applications, technology, and techniques for analog, digital, and mixed-signal processing. <i>Analog Dialogue</i> is published monthly on the Web. The featured technical articles are also compiled in quarterly print editions.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(8)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Communities">Communities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Analog_Devices&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Communities" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-8 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-8">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Community">Community</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Analog_Devices&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Community" data-section="15" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, Analog Devices announced EngineerZone, an online technical support community.<sup id="cite_ref-brian_87-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brian-87">[87]</a></sup> EngineerZone was launched so the design engineering community (customers, prospects, partners, employees and students) can ask questions, share knowledge and search for answers to their questions in an open forum.<sup id="cite_ref-brian_87-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brian-87">[87]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Resources">Resources</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Analog_Devices&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Resources" data-section="16" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Analog Devices <b>circuits from the lab</b> <b>reference circuits</b> are engineered and tested for quick system integration to help solve design challenges ranging from common to complex. Reference circuits are smaller, modular designs that are more broadly applicable than application-specific reference designs.
</p><p>Each reference circuit is documented with test data, theory of operation, and component selection decision criteria. In addition, <a href="/wiki/Reference_circuit" title="Reference circuit">reference circuits</a> are tailored to meet real-world system integration needs and may also include board layout schematics, CAD tools models, device drivers, and evaluation hardware.<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup></p>
