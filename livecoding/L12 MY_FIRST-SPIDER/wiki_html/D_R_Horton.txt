</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=D._R._Horton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<table class="box-Prose plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-style ambox-Prose" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>is in <a href="/wiki/MOS:LIST" class="mw-redirect" title="MOS:LIST">list</a> format, but may read better as <a href="/wiki/MOS:PROSE" class="mw-redirect" title="MOS:PROSE">prose</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> You can help by <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=D._R._Horton&amp;action=edit">converting this section</a>, if appropriate. <a href="/wiki/Help:Editing" title="Help:Editing">Editing help</a> is available.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">February 2020</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table><p>The company was founded in 1978 by Donald R. Horton.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In 1997, the company acquired Continental Homes for $305 million and the assumption of $278 million in debt.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The company also entered the <a href="/wiki/Tucson,_Arizona" title="Tucson, Arizona">Tucson, Arizona</a> market.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 1998, the company promoted Donald J. Tomnitz to vice chairman and chief executive and promoted Richard Beckwitt to president.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The company also acquired Cambridge Homes.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 1999, the company acquired Century Title Agency.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 2001, the company acquired Emerald Builders.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The company also acquired Fortress Homes and Communities of Florida.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In 2002, the company acquired Schuler Homes.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The company also acquired 300 acres in Arlington, Texas.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In 2013, the company re-entered the <a href="/wiki/Nashville" class="mw-redirect" title="Nashville">Nashville</a> market.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2015, the company acquired Pacific Ridge Homes, based in <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>, for $72 million.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The acquisition included 350 lots, 90 homes in inventory and 40 homes in sales order backlog. Horton also acquired control of about 400 lots through option contracts.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2015, the company received approval from the <a href="/wiki/Honolulu_City_Council" title="Honolulu City Council">Honolulu City Council</a> to begin construction on an 11,750-home <a href="/wiki/Planned_community" title="Planned community">planned community</a> in West <a href="/wiki/Oahu" title="Oahu">Oahu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In 2016, the company acquired Wilson Parker Homes for $90 million.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, the company moved its headquarters from <a href="/wiki/Fort_Worth,_Texas" title="Fort Worth, Texas">Fort Worth, Texas</a> to <a href="/wiki/Arlington,_Texas" title="Arlington, Texas">Arlington, Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, the company acquired Terramor Homes, Classic Builders and Westport Homes.
</p>
