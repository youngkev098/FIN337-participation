</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jacobs_Engineering_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Jacobs Engineering was founded in 1947 by <a href="/wiki/Joseph_J._Jacobs" title="Joseph J. Jacobs">Joseph J. Jacobs</a>. The company's current Chief Executive Officer is Steven J. Demetriou. He has been the Chairman of the Board since July 28, 2016 and has been its CEO and President since 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The previous President and CEO was Craig L. Martin from 2006 until 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-Star_Tribune_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Star_Tribune-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>The company is publicly traded as a <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> company.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-11">[11]</a></sup> As of September 2018, Jacobs had more than 80,800 employees globally, and more than 400 offices in North America, South America, Europe, the Middle East, Australia, Africa, and Asia.<sup id="cite_ref-jacobs1_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jacobs1-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2016, the company moved its headquarters from <a href="/wiki/Pasadena" class="mw-redirect" title="Pasadena">Pasadena</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> to <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bizjournals1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bizjournals1-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>On August 9, 2017, the Pentagon awarded a $4.6 billion Integrated Research &amp; Development for Enterprise Solutions (IRES) follow-on contract to Jacobs Technology Inc, a unit of Jacobs Engineering Group Inc. to provide products and services for the Missile Defense Agency and its Missile Defense Integration and Operations Center.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2018, Jacobs agreed to sell its Energy, Chemicals and Resources (ECR) segment to <a href="/wiki/WorleyParsons" class="mw-redirect" title="WorleyParsons">WorleyParsons</a>. After the completion of the transaction, Jacobs would focus on its two higher growth and higher margin lines of business – Aerospace, Technology, Environmental and Nuclear (ATEN) and Buildings, Infrastructure &amp; Advanced Facilities (BIAF).<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jacobs_Engineering_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acquisitions" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In FY 2008, Jacobs spent $264 million to acquire Carter and Burgess, Lindsey Engineering and a 60% stake in Zamel and Turbag Consulting Engineers.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>. In FY 2010, Jacobs acquired TechTeam, Tybrin and Jordan, Jones and Goulding for $259.5 million total.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In FY 2014, Jacobs announced it completed a merger transaction with <a href="/wiki/Sinclair_Knight_Merz" title="Sinclair Knight Merz">Sinclair Knight Merz (SKM)</a>, a 6,900-person professional services firm headquartered in Australia. The purchase price reflects an enterprise value of AUS$1.2 billion (US$1.1 billion) plus adjustments for cash, debt and other items. <sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>On August 2, 2017, Jacobs acquired <a href="/wiki/CH2M_Hill" title="CH2M Hill">CH2M Hill</a> – a global engineering firm in key infrastructure and government service sectors, including water, transportation, environmental and nuclear in a $3.27 billion cash-and-stock deal.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2020, Jacobs acquired Wood Nuclear, the nuclear services arm of UK-based <a href="/wiki/John_Wood_Group" title="John Wood Group">John Wood Group</a>, for £250m, adding 2000 staff and bringing Jacobs' total UK workforce to almost 11,000.<sup id="cite_ref-Lowe-09Mar2020_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lowe-09Mar2020-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
