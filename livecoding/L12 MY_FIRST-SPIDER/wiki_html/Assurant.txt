</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Assurant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Assurant can trace its roots back to the founding of the La Crosse Mutual Aid Association, which was established to sell disability insurance in Wisconsin in the early 1890s. La Crosse Mutual Aid Association would later become the Time Insurance Company.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>  In 1978, N.V. AMEV of the Netherlands acquired the Time Insurance Company via its U.S. holding company AMEV Holdings, Inc. During the next 12 years, AMEV Holdings, Inc. would expand through acquisition, buying American Security Insurance (credit-related insurance); United Family (funeral insurance); Western Insurance Company (mutual funds); and Superior Insurance (auto insurance).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In 1990, N.V. AMEV of the Netherlands acquired VSB Groep NV bank to become the Netherlands first financial conglomerate combining an insurer and a bank, creating Fortis.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 1991, AMEV acquired the group life, accident and health insurance of <a href="/wiki/Mutual_Benefit_Life_Insurance_Company" title="Mutual Benefit Life Insurance Company">Mutual Benefit Life Insurance Company</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> AMEV Holdings, Inc. was rebranded Fortis, Inc. in 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Superior Insurance was sold in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Fortis acquired John Alden in 1998 and American Bankers Insurance in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>Fortis Inc. sold its life insurance to <a href="/wiki/The_Hartford" title="The Hartford">The Hartford</a> in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Fortis' American business was subsequently renamed as Assurant and spun off from the parent company in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>On June 10, 2015 Assurant announced an exit from the health insurance marketplace to focus on housing and lifestyle specialty protection products and services, and would be winding down its Assurant Health business.<sup id="cite_ref-assuranthealth.com_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-assuranthealth.com-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Beckers_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beckers-20">[20]</a></sup> In October 2015, Assurant announced it had completed the sale of Assurant Health’s existing supplemental and self-funded business lines to National General Holdings Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-Beckers_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beckers-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Blumenthal_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Blumenthal-22">[22]</a></sup> Assurant began to wind down its major medical operations and did not participate in the next Affordable Care Act open enrollment period beginning in November 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-assuranthealth.com_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-assuranthealth.com-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Beckers_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beckers-20">[20]</a></sup> Assurant sold its HSA assets and other medical-account assets to SelectAccount in March 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-Steyer_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Steyer-23">[23]</a></sup> In 2015 Assurant also announced it was exiting the employee benefits marketplace.<sup id="cite_ref-Scism_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Scism-24">[24]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sun_Life_Financial" title="Sun Life Financial">Sun Life Financial</a> agreed to acquire Assurant Employee Benefits for $940 million in September 2015,<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Scism_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Scism-24">[24]</a></sup> and closed the sale in March 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Assurant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Operations" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Assurant operates three main segments:
</p>
<ul><li>Global Housing, which offers lender-placed insurance, multi-family housing, and mortgage solutions.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Use_plain_English#Buzzwords" title="Wikipedia:Use plain English"><span title="The material near this tag may use buzzwords designed solely to impress you or obscure meaning. (October 2019)">buzzword</span></a></i>]</sup><sup id="cite_ref-2017_10-K_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2017_10-K-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>Global Lifestyle, which offers mobile device protection services and extended service contracts for consumer electronics and appliances, vehicle protection services, and credit and other insurance.<sup id="cite_ref-2017_10-K_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2017_10-K-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>Global Preneed, which offers pre-funded funeral insurance and annuity products.<sup id="cite_ref-2017_10-K_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2017_10-K-4">[4]</a></sup></li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Assurant_Health_policy_claim_denials_and_cancellations">Assurant Health policy claim denials and cancellations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Assurant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Assurant Health policy claim denials and cancellations" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>Assurant Health (now divested from Assurant)<sup id="cite_ref-Steyer_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Steyer-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Beckers_20-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beckers-20">[20]</a></sup> was repeatedly found to cancel health policies for some customers that had serious medical conditions, and 12 states criticized the firm for denying claims, with most of the states levying fines against Assurant.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> A number of individual cases have been reported.
</p><p>In September 2009, the <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina_Supreme_Court" title="South Carolina Supreme Court">South Carolina Supreme Court</a> upheld a lower court’s verdict that the firm (then known as "Fortis") wrongly revoked the health insurance policy of a holder who had contracted <a href="/wiki/HIV" title="HIV">HIV</a> subsequent to getting an insurance policy, and ordered the firm to pay $10 million to the plaintiff. The court found that:
</p>
<dl><dd>Pursuant to company policy in cases involving long-term disease, Fortis launched an investigation to determine whether [the plaintiff] had failed to disclose a pre-existing condition on his policy application...A Fortis investigator reviewed the records and discovered [an] erroneously-dated intake note in [the doctor’s] files. That information was then forwarded to [a] Fortis Senior Underwriter...for review. [The underwriter]...recommended that [the plaintiff’s] policy be rescinded on the grounds that he had misrepresented his HIV positive status.</dd></dl><p>A <a href="/wiki/Reuters" title="Reuters">Reuters</a> report on the ruling stated that Fortis had a company policy to target every recently diagnosed HIV-positive policyholder for an automatic fraud investigation as a pretext to rescind their policy, according to undisclosed records. As in that case, their insurance policies often were canceled on incorrect information, flimsy evidence, or for no reason at all.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> In March 2010, the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_States" title="Supreme Court of the United States">U.S. Supreme Court</a> rejected the firm's appeal of the ruling.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2010, a Boulder, Colorado jury found that Assurant Health had breached its contract with a woman who was severely injured in a hit-and-run accident, and awarded her $183,551 for medical bills and approximately $37.1 million in punitive damages. This was described as "one of the largest bad-faith judgments in Colorado history".<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
