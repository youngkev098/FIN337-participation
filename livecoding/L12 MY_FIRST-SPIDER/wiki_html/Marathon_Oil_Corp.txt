</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_operations">Current operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marathon_Oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Current operations" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>As of December 31, 2015, the company had 2.163 <a href="/wiki/1000000000_(number)" class="mw-redirect" title="1000000000 (number)">billion</a> <a href="/wiki/Barrel_of_oil_equivalent" title="Barrel of oil equivalent">barrels of oil equivalent</a> (1.323<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup>10</sup> GJ) of estimated proven reserves, of which 44% was in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, 32% was in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, 12% was in <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a>, and 11% was in other countries in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, primarily <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a>.
</p><p>The company has concessions with the <a href="/wiki/Waha_Oil_Company" title="Waha Oil Company">Waha Oil Company</a> in <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a>. Libya accounts for 235 million <a href="/wiki/Barrel_of_oil_equivalent" title="Barrel of oil equivalent">barrels of oil equivalent</a> (1.44<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup>9</sup> GJ) of estimated proved reserves, although the company did not sell any product from these operations in 2015 since operations were interrupted by civil and political unrest.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>In Canada, the company was focused on the <a href="/wiki/Athabasca_oil_sands" title="Athabasca oil sands">Athabasca oil sands</a> project, in which the company owned a 20% interest.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>The company's proved reserves consisted 40% of <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">petroleum</a>, 32% <a href="/wiki/Synthetic_crude" title="Synthetic crude">synthetic crude</a>, 19% <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a> and 9% <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas_liquids" class="mw-redirect" title="Natural gas liquids">natural gas liquids</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, the company sold 438 thousand <a href="/wiki/Barrel_of_oil_equivalent" title="Barrel of oil equivalent">barrels of oil equivalent</a> (2,680,000 GJ) per day.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, the company derived 13% of its revenues from sales to <a href="/wiki/Irving_Oil" title="Irving Oil">Irving Oil</a> and 11% of its revenues from sales to <a href="/wiki/Shell_Oil" class="mw-redirect" title="Shell Oil">Shell Oil</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>In 2016, the company plans to spend $1.4 billion on capital expenditures, of which $1.2 billion will be spent in North America, including $600 million in the <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Ford_Group" title="Eagle Ford Group">Eagle Ford</a> and $200 million in the <a href="/wiki/Bakken_formation" class="mw-redirect" title="Bakken formation">Bakken formation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>The company owns 277,000 net acres in the <a href="/wiki/Bakken_formation" class="mw-redirect" title="Bakken formation">Bakken formation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marathon_Oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Marathon began as The Ohio Oil Company in 1887. In 1889, it was purchased by <a href="/wiki/John_D._Rockefeller" title="John D. Rockefeller">John D. Rockefeller</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Standard_Oil" title="Standard Oil">Standard Oil</a>. It remained a part of Standard Oil until Standard Oil was broken up in 1911. In 1930, The Ohio Oil Company bought the Transcontinental Oil Company and established the "Marathon" brand name. In 1962, the company changed its name to "Marathon Oil Company".
</p><p>In 1982, <a href="/wiki/Mobil" title="Mobil">Mobil</a> made a <a href="/wiki/Hostile_takeover" class="mw-redirect" title="Hostile takeover">hostile</a> offer to buy the company; however, the board of Marathon Oil rejected the offer and instead sold the company to <a href="/wiki/United_States_Steel" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Steel">United States Steel</a>. A legal battle ensued thereafter.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>After the merger, the headquarters was moved to <a href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Houston, Texas">Houston, Texas</a> in 1990 but the company's refining subsidiary maintained its headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Findlay,_Ohio" title="Findlay, Ohio">Findlay, Ohio</a>.
</p><p>In 1984, Marathon purchased the U.S. unit of <a href="/wiki/Husky_Energy" title="Husky Energy">Husky Energy</a> for $505 million.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In 1998, Marathon and <a href="/wiki/Ashland,_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Ashland, Inc.">Ashland, Inc.</a> contributed their refining operations to Marathon Ashland Petroleum LLC, now <a href="/wiki/Marathon_Petroleum" title="Marathon Petroleum">Marathon Petroleum</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 2001, USX, the holding company that owned <a href="/wiki/United_States_Steel" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Steel">United States Steel</a> and Marathon, spun off the steel business and, in 2002, USX renamed itself Marathon Oil Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 2003, Marathon sold its Canadian operations to <a href="/wiki/Husky_Energy" title="Husky Energy">Husky Energy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 2003, the company sold its interest in the <a href="/wiki/Yates_Oil_Field" title="Yates Oil Field">Yates Oil Field</a> to <a href="/wiki/Kinder_Morgan" title="Kinder Morgan">Kinder Morgan</a> for approximately $225 million.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In late 2003, Marathon Oil and its partners <a href="/wiki/Noble_Energy" title="Noble Energy">Noble Energy</a> and AMPCO started the Bioko Island Malaria Control Project (BIMCP) in <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a>. Malaria control activities included indoor residual spraying, improved diagnosis and case management, and capacity building to contain future outbreaks. BIMCP had proven being successful in reducing malaria transmission, reducing the proportion of children with malaria parasites, and improving iron status. BIMCP is perceived as a model of hands-on corporate involvement in a humanitarian effort with government, non-profits and academic organizations to reduce the burden of malaria in countries located in Equatorial Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The president of <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Obiang_Nguema" class="mw-redirect" title="Obiang Nguema">Obiang Nguema</a>, is one of the world's worst dictators, according to <a href="/wiki/Parade_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Parade Magazine">Parade Magazine</a>. Marathon's humanitarian efforts have mitigated some of the criticism resulting from its dealings with Nguema's regime. In 2008, Marathon Oil and Lestis Private Capital Group started the Central Basin Control Project (CBCP)<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 2007, Marathon acquired Western Oil Sands for $6.6 billion and gained ownership of its 20 percent stake in the <a href="/wiki/Athabasca_Tar_Sands" class="mw-redirect" title="Athabasca Tar Sands">Athabasca</a> Oil Sands Project in northern <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a> and other assets in the <a href="/wiki/Midwestern_United_States" title="Midwestern United States">midwestern United States</a>. The Athabasca project's <a href="/wiki/Albian_Sands" title="Albian Sands">Muskeg River Mine</a> was producing approximately 155,000 barrels a day of <a href="/wiki/Bitumen" class="mw-redirect" title="Bitumen">bitumen</a> at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In 2011, Marathon completed the <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">corporate spin-off</a> of <a href="/wiki/Marathon_Petroleum" title="Marathon Petroleum">Marathon Petroleum</a>, distributing a 100% interest to its shareholders.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2013, Marathon sold its Angolan oil and gas field to <a href="/wiki/Sinopec" title="Sinopec">Sinopec</a> for $1.52 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2013, Marathon announced it would sell a 10% stake in an oil and gas field offshore Angola for around $590 million to <a href="/wiki/Sonangol_Group" title="Sonangol Group">Sonangol Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2014, Marathon Oil Norge AS (“Marathon Norway") was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Det_Norske_Oljeselskap_ASA" class="mw-redirect" title="Det Norske Oljeselskap ASA">Det Norske Oljeselskap ASA</a> for US$2.1 billon.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
