Clients of Chubb consist of multinational corporations and local businesses, individuals, and insurers seeking reinsurance coverage. Chubb provides commercial and personal property and casualty insurance, personal accident and supplemental <a href="/wiki/Health_insurance" title="Health insurance">health insurance</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reinsurance" title="Reinsurance">reinsurance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Life_insurance" title="Life insurance">life insurance</a>. Chubb trades on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">NYSE</a> and is a component of the S&amp;P 500 index. Its core operating insurance companies are rated "AA" (Very Strong) for financial strength by <a href="/wiki/Standard_%26_Poor%27s" title="Standard &amp; Poor's">Standard &amp; Poor's</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and "A++" (Superior) by <a href="/wiki/A._M._Best" class="mw-redirect" title="A. Fitch rates Chubb Limited and its subsidiaries "AA" (Very Strong) for financial strength, "AA-" for issuer default and "A+" for senior debt.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Moody%27s" class="mw-redirect" title="Moody's">Moody's</a> rates the U.S. companies "A1" and the unsecured loan notes "A3".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>On July 1, 2015, ACE announced that it would acquire <a href="/wiki/Chubb_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Chubb Corporation">Chubb Corporation</a> for $28.3 billion in cash and stock.<sup id="cite_ref-Marketwatch_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marketwatch-11">[11]</a></sup> ACE has committed that Chubb's current headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Warren_Township,_New_Jersey" title="Warren Township, New Jersey">Warren, New Jersey</a>, USA, will have a substantial portion of the headquarters function for the combined company's North American Division. The combined company adopted the Chubb name in January 2016 after the acquisition was completed.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_structure">Business structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chubb_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Business structure" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>ACE provides insurance and reinsurance products and services through several operating units.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="North_American">North American</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chubb_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: North American" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>ACE USA provides commercial property insurance and casualty (P&amp;C), risk management and accident and health (A&amp;H) coverages through retail brokers. ACE Agriculture offers crop, farm and agribusiness P&amp;C coverages through agents and brokers. ACE Westchester offers commercial P&amp;C excess and surplus lines through wholesale brokers. 
</p><p>ACE Bermuda provides liability, property, political risk coverages and captive programs through large international brokers. 
</p><p>ACE Private Risk Services offers high-value personal lines coverages through independent agents and brokers. ACE Commercial Risk Services provides specialty small business coverage through agents and brokers.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chubb_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>ACE International provides commercial P&amp;C, A&amp;H, and traditional and specialty personal lines through retail brokers, agents and other channels in 51 countries outside North America. ACE Global Markets offers commercial P&amp;C excess and surplus lines and A&amp;H sold by wholesale brokers and through Lloyd’s. In North America, Combined Insurance, acquired by ACE in 2008, provides personal accident and supplemental health insurance through captive agents.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Reinsurance">Reinsurance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chubb_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reinsurance" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>ACE Tempest Re, based in <a href="/wiki/Stamford,_Connecticut" title="Stamford, Connecticut">Stamford, Connecticut</a>, offers P&amp;C and life reinsurance sold worldwide by reinsurance brokers.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chubb_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Life" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>ACE Life provides protection and savings products through agents, <a href="/wiki/Bancassurance" title="Bancassurance">bancassurance</a> and other channels in <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>. Combined Insurance offers personal accident and supplemental health coverage sold by captive agents in North America.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chubb_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: History" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Chubb">Chubb</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chubb_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Chubb" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1882, Thomas Caldecot Chubb and his son Percy started a marine underwriting business in New York City. They collected $1,000 from 100 prominent merchants and focused on insuring ships and cargoes.<sup id="cite_ref-history_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>The Chubb Corporation was incorporated in 1967 and was listed on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> in 1984.<sup id="cite_ref-history_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>Beginning in 1970, the corporation owned <a href="/wiki/The_Chubb_Institute" class="mw-redirect" title="The Chubb Institute">The Chubb Institute</a>, a chain of commercial technical schools which grew out of the company's employee training program, but the schools were sold for $1 to a partnership of private equity firms Great Hill Partners and the High-Tech Institute in 2004.
</p><p>In 2007, Chubb was named the Readers' Choice winner as "Best Admitted Property/Casualty Insurance Company" by <i>Business Insurance</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In 2010 Chubb was number five on Chicago Business "Best Places to Work" list.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="ACE_Limited">ACE Limited</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chubb_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: ACE Limited" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<h4 class="in-block"><span id="1985.E2.80.931999"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1985–1999">1985–1999</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chubb_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 1985–1999" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>ACE Limited was established in 1985, funded by a group of 34 U.S. companies seeking difficult-to-obtain Excess Liability and Directors and Officers (D&amp;O) insurance coverage.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-19">[19]</a></sup>  That year, ACE and its Bermuda <a href="/wiki/Subsidiary" title="Subsidiary">subsidiary</a>, incorporated in the <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">Cayman Islands</a> and headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Hamilton,_Bermuda" title="Hamilton, Bermuda">Hamilton, Bermuda</a>, wrote its first insurance policy with John Cox as its President and CEO.  In 1987 the company assumed management of Corporate Officers &amp; Directors Assurance Limited (CODA), expanding ACE Bermuda's product line.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>Walter Scott became Chairman, President, and CEO of ACE in 1990 and saw the company listed on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> in 1993. Brian Duperreault succeeded Scott in 1994 as Chairman, President &amp; CEO and worked for the next ten years as ACE went through a series of acquisitions and a diversification process that brought the ACE Group of Companies global status.<sup id="cite_ref-execs_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-execs-20">[20]</a></sup>  One of the multiple acquisitions made during this time was the global property and casualty business of <a href="/wiki/Cigna" title="Cigna">Cigna</a> Corporation (most significantly the <a href="/wiki/Insurance_Company_of_North_America" title="Insurance Company of North America">Insurance Company of North America</a>, known as INA), which was purchased for $3.45 billion in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-cigna_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cigna-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span id="2000.E2.80.932014"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2000–2014">2000–2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chubb_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2000–2014" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In 2004, <a href="/wiki/Evan_G._Greenberg" title="Evan G. Greenberg">Evan G. Greenberg</a> became President and CEO of ACE Ltd.<sup id="cite_ref-greenberg_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-greenberg-22">[22]</a></sup>  In 2004 ACE was also investigated by NY Attorney General <a href="/wiki/Eliot_Spitzer" title="Eliot Spitzer">Eliot Spitzer</a> for participating in a <a href="/wiki/Bid_rigging" title="Bid rigging">bid rigging</a> and <a href="/wiki/Price_fixing" title="Price fixing">price fixing</a> scheme with insurance broker <a href="/wiki/Marsh_%26_McLennan" class="mw-redirect" title="Marsh &amp; McLennan">Marsh &amp; McLennan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ag_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ag-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>In 2008, ACE purchased the accident and health insurance provider <a href="/wiki/Combined_Insurance" title="Combined Insurance">Combined Insurance</a> Company of America (founded by <a href="/wiki/W._Clement_Stone" title="W. Clement Stone">W. Clement Stone</a> in 1919) from <a href="/wiki/Aon_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Aon Corporation">Aon Corporation</a> for $2.56 billion<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> and the high-net-worth personal lines business of the Atlantic Companies.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>Also in 2008, ACE relocated from the Cayman Islands to <a href="/wiki/Zurich" class="mw-redirect" title="Zurich">Zurich</a>, Switzerland. Evan Greenberg described the move as a "natural progression" that would provide ACE with a "better strategic flexibility…and a solid legal and regulatory environment…"<sup id="cite_ref-relocation_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-relocation-27">[27]</a></sup> The re-domestication was completed in July that year.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>In 2010, the ACE company ESIS Inc. was hired by <a href="/wiki/BP" title="BP">BP</a> to process claims made by the victims of the <a href="/wiki/Deepwater_Horizon_oil_spill" title="Deepwater Horizon oil spill">Deepwater Horizon oil spill</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bp_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bp-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>In 2010, ACE Limited purchased Rain and Hail, LLC for $1.1 billion. Rain and Hail Insurance Service, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Johnston,_Iowa" title="Johnston, Iowa">Johnston, Iowa</a>, is an industry leader in crop insurance in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> ACE Limited also acquired the Hong Kong and Korea life insurance operations of New York Life.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>In 2011, ACE Limited purchased agribusiness insurer Penn Millers.
<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012, they purchased Indonesian insurer Asuransi Jaya Proteksi.
<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p><p>In 2013, they purchased Mexican Surety Lines Company Fianzas Monterrey and Mexican Personal Lines Insurer ABA Seguros.
<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2014, ACE Limited acquired a majority stake in Siam Commercial Samaggi Insurance PCL from Siam Commercial Bank.  Following a subsequent tender offer in June 2014, ACE and its local partner owned 93.03% of the Samaggi.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2014, ACE Limited acquired the large commercial property and casualty business of Itaú Unibanco Holding SA. The transaction made ACE the largest property and casualty insurer in Brazil.
<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2015, ACE Limited acquired the Fireman’s Fund high net worth personal lines insurance business in the U.S. from Allianz for $365 million. ACE Private Risk Services is one of the largest high net worth insurers in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="ACE_acquires_Chubb">ACE acquires Chubb</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chubb_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: ACE acquires Chubb" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In July 1, 2015, ACE announced that it would acquire Chubb Corporation for $28.3 billion in cash and ACE stock.<sup id="cite_ref-Marketwatch_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marketwatch-11">[11]</a></sup> Upon completion, ACE shareholders held 70% while Chubb shareholders got 30% of the new combined company. The new company was to be based in Zurich, where ACE Limited's headquarters were. Evan Greenberg of ACE became the Chairman and CEO of the new company, while Chubb Chairman and CEO John Finnegan became Executive Vice Chairman for North America External Affairs. The company's board was expanded to 18, with 4 of them coming from Chubb. The new combined company then decided to adopt the Chubb name. The deal received ACE and Chubb shareholder approval and all required regulatory approval, and closed on January 14, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chubb_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Controversies" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2004_Eliot_Spitzer_investigation">2004 Eliot Spitzer investigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chubb_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2004 Eliot Spitzer investigation" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2004 New York Attorney General Eliot Spitzer conducted an investigation in the insurance industry. ACE, <a href="/wiki/American_International_Group" title="American International Group">American International Group</a> (AIG), <a href="/wiki/Marsh_%26_McLennan_Companies" title="Marsh &amp; McLennan Companies">Marsh &amp; McLennan</a>, and other large insurers and brokerages were named in Spitzer's investigation for possibly participating in questionable insurance practices including the payment of contingent commissions, bid-rigging, price-fixing, and improper accounting.<sup id="cite_ref-spitzer_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-spitzer-44">[44]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-investigation_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-investigation-45">[45]</a></sup></p><p>Spitzer asserted that contingent commissions contributed to a widespread practice of "bid-rigging" where brokers solicited fake bids with deliberately less favorable terms for the consumer than the bid offered by the insurance company paying the highest commissions.<sup id="cite_ref-contingent-commissions_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-contingent-commissions-46">[46]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-guilty_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guilty-47">[47]</a></sup></p><p>As part of an $80 million settlement that abated further inquiry, ACE signed an Assurance of Discontinuance in which they acknowledged their prior conduct and agreed to alter their business practices.  Evan Greenberg himself admitted no fault in Spitzer's allegations although a junior executive did plead guilty to criminal charges.<sup id="cite_ref-restructure_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-restructure-48">[48]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-resignation_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-resignation-49">[49]</a></sup></p><p>Spitzer's insurance industry probe additionally looked in early 2005 into ACE Ltd's subsidiary ACE Tempest Re's reporting to the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission">SEC</a> of a series of reinsurance transactions, together with the SEC reporting of a further 16 unaffiliated re-insurers. Evan Greenberg said that his company's investigation should be completed within a month. According to Greenberg, contracts appeared "generally structured in a way to provide for appropriate risk transfer and accounted for properly."<sup id="cite_ref-reinsurance_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reinsurance-50">[50]</a></sup></p><p>The Spitzer investigations also triggered civil litigation by policyholders who claim they were victimized by the bid rigging and commission schemes.<sup id="cite_ref-civil_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-civil-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Iran_Held_Liable_for_9.2F11"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Iran_Held_Liable_for_9/11">Iran Held Liable for 9/11</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chubb_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Iran Held Liable for 9/11" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/September_11,_2001_attacks" class="mw-redirect" title="September 11, 2001 attacks">September 11, 2001 attacks</a></div>
<p>On March 9, 2016, U.S. District Judge <a href="/wiki/George_B._Daniels" title="George B. Daniels">George B. Daniels</a> issued a default judgment against Iran, ordering it to pay $7.5 billion in damages to families of victims who died in the September 11, 2001 attacks, as well as $3 billion to Chubb Limited &amp; other unnamed insurers that paid out claims resulting from the event. The plaintiffs in the case argued that Iran "provided material support" and training to <a href="/wiki/Al-Qaeda" title="Al-Qaeda">al-Qaeda</a> members, including 9/11 hijackers, through Hezbollah prior to the attacks and was therefore liable.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
