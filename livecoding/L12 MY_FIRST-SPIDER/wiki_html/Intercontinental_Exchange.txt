<p><b>Intercontinental Exchange</b> (<b>ICE</b>) is an American company that owns <a href="/wiki/Futures_exchange" title="Futures exchange">exchanges</a> for <a href="/wiki/Financial_market" title="Financial market">financial</a> and <a href="/wiki/Commodity_market" title="Commodity market">commodity</a> markets, and operates 12 regulated exchanges and marketplaces.  This includes ICE <a href="/wiki/Futures_exchange" title="Futures exchange">futures exchanges</a> in the United States, Canada and Europe, the <a href="/wiki/London_International_Financial_Futures_and_Options_Exchange" title="London International Financial Futures and Options Exchange">Liffe</a> futures exchanges in Europe, the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>, equity options exchanges and <a href="/wiki/Over-the-counter_(finance)" title="Over-the-counter (finance)">OTC</a> energy, credit and equity markets.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p><a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_C._Sprecher" class="mw-redirect" title="Jeffrey C. Sprecher">Jeffrey C. Sprecher</a> was a power plant developer who spotted a need for a seamless market in natural gas used to power generators.<sup id="cite_ref-ReutersICEtimeline_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReutersICEtimeline-2">[2]</a></sup>  In the late 1990s, Sprecher acquired Continental Power Exchange, Inc. with the objective of developing an Internet-based platform to provide a more transparent and efficient market structure for OTC energy commodity trading.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nytimes1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes1-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2000, ICE was founded by Sprecher and backed by <a href="/wiki/Goldman_Sachs" title="Goldman Sachs">Goldman Sachs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Stanley" title="Morgan Stanley">Morgan Stanley</a>, <a href="/wiki/BP" title="BP">BP</a>, <a href="/wiki/Total_S.A." title="Total S.A.">Total</a>, <a href="/wiki/Royal_Dutch_Shell" title="Royal Dutch Shell">Shell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Bank" title="Deutsche Bank">Deutsche Bank</a> and <a href="/wiki/Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9_G%C3%A9n%C3%A9rale" title="Société Générale">Société Générale</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Greenberger2008_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Greenberger2008-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>The new exchange increased price transparency, efficiency, <a href="/wiki/Market_liquidity" title="Market liquidity">liquidity</a>, and had lower costs than manual trading. While the company's original focus was energy products (crude and refined <a href="/wiki/Crude_oil" class="mw-redirect" title="Crude oil">oil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electrical_power_industry" class="mw-redirect" title="Electrical power industry">power</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Emissions_trading" title="Emissions trading">emissions</a>), acquisitions subsequently expanded its activity into soft commodities (<a href="/wiki/Sugar" title="Sugar">sugar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cotton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a>), <a href="/wiki/Foreign_exchange_market" title="Foreign exchange market">foreign exchange</a> and <a href="/wiki/Equity_index" class="mw-redirect" title="Equity index">equity index</a> futures.
</p><p>In a response to <a href="/wiki/Financial_crisis_of_2007%E2%80%932008" class="mw-redirect" title="Financial crisis of 2007–2008">US financial crisis</a> in 2008, Sprecher formed ICE US Trust based in New York, now called ICE Clear Credit LLC, to serve as a limited-purpose bank, a clearing house for <a href="/wiki/Credit_default_swap" title="Credit default swap">credit default swaps</a>. Sprecher worked closely with the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Reserve" title="Federal Reserve">Federal Reserve</a> to serve as its over-the-counter (OTC) derivatives clearing house. "US regulators were keen on the kind of clearing house for opaque over-the-counter (OTC) derivatives as a risk management device. In the absence of a central counterparty - which would guarantee pay-outs should a trading party be unable to do so - there was a high risk of massive market disruption."<sup id="cite_ref-Weitzman_2008_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Weitzman_2008-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>The principal backers for ICE US Trust were the same financial institutions most affected by the crisis, the top ten of the world's largest banks (Goldman Sachs, Bank of America, Citi, Credit Suisse, Deutsche Bank, JPMorgan, Merrill Lynch, Morgan Stanley and UBS). Sprecher's clearing house cleared their global <a href="/wiki/Credit_default_swap" title="Credit default swap">credit default swaps</a> (CDS) in exchange for sharing profits with these banks.<sup id="cite_ref-Weitzman_2008_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Weitzman_2008-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Terhune_2010_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Terhune_2010-8">[8]</a></sup>  By September 30, 2008 the <a href="/wiki/Financial_Post" title="Financial Post">Financial Post</a> warned that the "$54000bn credit derivatives market faced its biggest test in October 2008 as billions of dollars worth of contracts on now-defaulted derivatives would be auctioned by the <a href="/wiki/International_Swaps_and_Derivatives_Association" title="International Swaps and Derivatives Association">International Swaps and Derivatives Association</a> . In his article in the <i><a href="/wiki/Financial_Post" title="Financial Post">Financial Post</a></i>, he described ICE as a "US-based electronic futures exchange" which raised the stakes on October 30, 2008 in its effort to expand in the $54000 bn credit derivatives market.(<a href="#CITEREFWeitzman2008">Weitzman 2008</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-financialtimes1_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-financialtimes1-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>By 2010, Intercontinental Exchange had cleared more than $10 trillion in <a href="/wiki/Credit_default_swap" title="Credit default swap">credit default swaps</a> (CDS) through its subsidiaries, ICE Trust CDS  (now ICE Clear Credit).<sup id="cite_ref-Terhune_2010_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Terhune_2010-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-businessweek1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessweek1-10">[10]</a></sup> By 2017 Intercontinental Exchange had been named to the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)">Fortune Future 50</a> determining the top 50 companies that are best positioned to adapt and deliver growth in a complex environment.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> ICE was also named to the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> in June 2017 as the only exchange operator included in the ranking.
</p><p>On September 6, 2019, <i><a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_L.P." title="Bloomberg L.P.">Bloomberg</a></i> reported that ICE was growing closer to offering <a href="/wiki/Bitcoin" title="Bitcoin">Bitcoin</a> futures trading, as "its Bakkt unit opens its digital-asset custody warehouse today to customers." On September 23, 2019, Bitcoin will be able to be acquired.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Mergers_and_acquisitions">Mergers and acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mergers and acquisitions" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>The Intercontinental Exchange has had a policy to grow through the acquisition of other exchanges, a number of these have been successful while others have failed due to concerns by regulators or others that the new company would have created a monopoly situation.  The major acquisition and attempted acquisitions have included:
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="International_Petroleum_Exchange_.28IPE.29_2001"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="International_Petroleum_Exchange_(IPE)_2001">International Petroleum Exchange (IPE) 2001</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International Petroleum Exchange (IPE) 2001" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In June 2001, ICE expanded its business into futures trading by acquiring the London-based <a href="/wiki/International_Petroleum_Exchange" class="mw-redirect" title="International Petroleum Exchange">International Petroleum Exchange</a> (IPE), now ICE Futures Europe, which operated Europe's leading open-outcry energy futures exchange. Since 2003, ICE has partnered with the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Climate_Exchange" title="Chicago Climate Exchange">Chicago Climate Exchange</a> (CCX) to host its electronic marketplaces. In April 2005, the entire ICE portfolio of energy futures became fully electronic and ICE closed <a href="/wiki/International_Petroleum_Exchange" class="mw-redirect" title="International Petroleum Exchange">International Petroleum Exchange</a>'s high profile and historic trading floor.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes1-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="New_York_Board_of_Trade_.28NYBOT.29_2005"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="New_York_Board_of_Trade_(NYBOT)_2005">New York Board of Trade (NYBOT) 2005</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: New York Board of Trade (NYBOT) 2005" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>ICE became a publicly traded company on November 16, 2005, and was added to the Russell 1000 Index on June 30, 2006.  The company expanded rapidly in 2007, acquiring the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Board_of_Trade" title="New York Board of Trade">New York Board of Trade</a> (NYBOT),<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and ChemConnect (a chemical commodity market).
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Chicago_Board_of_Trade_unsuccessful_bid_2007">Chicago Board of Trade unsuccessful bid 2007</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Chicago Board of Trade unsuccessful bid 2007" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In March 2007 ICE made an unsuccessful $9.9 billion bid for the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Board_of_Trade" title="Chicago Board of Trade">Chicago Board of Trade</a>, which was instead acquired by the <a href="/wiki/CME_Group" title="CME Group">CME Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ChicagoTribuneManor_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ChicagoTribuneManor-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Winnipeg_Commodity_Exchange_.28WCE.29_2007"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Winnipeg_Commodity_Exchange_(WCE)_2007">Winnipeg Commodity Exchange (WCE) 2007</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Winnipeg Commodity Exchange (WCE) 2007" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>IntercontinentalExchange Inc., the "upstart Atlanta-based energy bourse" purchased the privately held 120-year-old <a href="/wiki/Winnipeg_Commodity_Exchange" title="Winnipeg Commodity Exchange">Winnipeg Commodity Exchange</a>, known for its canola futures contract, for $40 million.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>The Winnipeg Commodity Exchange (WCE) was renamed ICE Futures Canada as of January 1, 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
In 2004, the Winnipeg Commodity Exchange had "closed its open-outcry trading floor" becoming "the first North American agricultural futures exchange to trade exclusively on an electronic platform" by trading via the "Chicago Board of Trade's electronic platform, and [using] clearing services from the Kansas City Board of Trade.<sup id="cite_ref-Upstart20070623_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Upstart20070623-17">[17]</a></sup> IntercontinentalExchange converted Winnipeg Commodity Exchange contracts to the IntercontinentalExchange platform.<sup id="cite_ref-Upstart20070623_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Upstart20070623-17">[17]</a></sup> IntercontinentalExchange maintained an office and "small core staff" in Winnipeg, Manitoba. The Manitoba Securities Commission oversee its operations.<sup id="cite_ref-Upstart20070623_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Upstart20070623-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Creditex_2008">Creditex 2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Creditex 2008" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In June 2008, ICE announced that it had entered into a definitive merger agreement to acquire Creditex Group Inc. (Creditex).<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The transaction consideration totaled $625 million comprising approximately $565 million in ICE common stock and $60 million in cash, as well as a working capital adjustment to be finalized at closing. Upon the closing of the transaction, Creditex Group became a wholly owned subsidiary of ICE, operating under the Creditex name.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="TSX_Group.27s_Natural_Gas_Exchange_Partnership_2008"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="TSX_Group's_Natural_Gas_Exchange_Partnership_2008">TSX Group's Natural Gas Exchange Partnership 2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: TSX Group's Natural Gas Exchange Partnership 2008" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In January 2008, ICE partnered with Canada's <a href="/wiki/TSX_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="TSX Group">TSX Group</a>'s Natural Gas Exchange, expanding their offering to clearing and settlement services for physical OTC natural gas contracts.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate_Exchange_2010">Climate Exchange 2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Climate Exchange 2010" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In April 2010, ICE acquired Climate Exchange PLC for 395 million pounds ($622 million) and <a href="/wiki/European_Climate_Exchange" title="European Climate Exchange">European Climate Exchange</a> (ECX) as part of its purchase.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Exchange-traded emissions products were first offered by the European Climate Exchange (ECX), which was established in 2005, by listing products on the ICE Futures Europe's trading platform. ICE Futures Europe is the leading market for <a href="/wiki/Carbon_emission_trading" title="Carbon emission trading">carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions</a>. ICE's ECX products comply with the requirements of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union_Emission_Trading_Scheme" title="European Union Emission Trading Scheme">European Union Emission Trading Scheme</a>.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="NYSE_Euronext_2013">NYSE Euronext 2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: NYSE Euronext 2013" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In February 2011, in the wake of an announced merger of <a href="/wiki/NYSE_Euronext" title="NYSE Euronext">NYSE Euronext</a> with <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_B%C3%B6rse" title="Deutsche Börse">Deutsche Börse</a>, speculation developed that ICE and Nasdaq could mount a counter-bid of their own for NYSE Euronext. ICE was thought to be looking to acquire the American exchange's derivatives business, Nasdaq its cash equities business. As of the time of the speculation, "NYSE Euronext’s market value was $9.75 billion. Nasdaq was valued at $5.78 billion, while ICE was valued at $9.45 billion."<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Late in the month, Nasdaq was reported to be considering asking either ICE or the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Mercantile_Exchange" title="Chicago Mercantile Exchange">Chicago Mercantile Exchange</a> (CME) to join in what would probably be an $11–12 billion counterbid for NYSE.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> On April 1, ICE and Nasdaq made an $11.3 billion offer which was rejected April 10 by NYSE. Another week later, ICE and Nasdaq sweetened their offer, including a $.17 increase per share to $42.67 and a $350 million breakup fee if the deal were to encounter regulatory trouble. The two said the offer was a $2 billion (21%) premium over the Deutsche offer and that they had fully committed financing of $3.8 billion from lenders to finance the deal.<sup id="cite_ref-marketwatchMorcroft2011_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-marketwatchMorcroft2011-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Justice" title="United States Department of Justice">Justice Department</a>, also in April, "initiated an antitrust review of the proposal, which would have brought nearly all U.S. stock listings under a merged Nasdaq-NYSE." In May, saying it "became clear that we would not be successful in securing regulatory approval," the Nasdaq and ICE withdrew their bid.<sup id="cite_ref-marketwatchPollock2011_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-marketwatchPollock2011-24">[24]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> then blocked the Deutsche merger on February 1, 2012, citing the fact that the merged company would have a near monopoly.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2012, ICE announced it would buy NYSE Euronext (this time without the involvement of Nasdaq) for $8.2 billion, pending regulatory approval.<sup id="cite_ref-CBSNews_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBSNews-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[notes 1]</a></sup> Jeffrey Sprecher will retain his position as Chairman and CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-Rothwell20dec2012_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rothwell20dec2012-29">[28]</a></sup> The boards of directors of both ICE and NYSE Euronext approved the acquisition.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="SuperDerivatives_Inc_2014">SuperDerivatives Inc 2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: SuperDerivatives Inc 2014" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In September 2014, ICE announced that it had agreed to acquire <a href="/wiki/SuperDerivatives" title="SuperDerivatives">SuperDerivatives</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes-30">[29]</a></sup> a provider of risk management analytics, financial market data and valuation services. SuperDerivatives was purchased for $350 million on October 7, 2014.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Interactive_Data_Corporation_.28IDC.29_2015"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Interactive_Data_Corporation_(IDC)_2015">Interactive Data Corporation (IDC) 2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Interactive Data Corporation (IDC) 2015" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In October 2015, ICE announced that it had entered into a definitive agreement to acquire <a href="/wiki/Interactive_Data_Corporation" title="Interactive Data Corporation">Interactive Data Corporation</a> (IDC), a provider of financial market data, analytics and related trading solutions<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Use_plain_English#Buzzwords" title="Wikipedia:Use plain English"><span title="The material near this tag may use buzzwords designed solely to impress you or obscure meaning. (July 2019)">buzzword</span></a></i>]</sup>, from Silver Lake, involved in technology investing, and Warburg Pincus, a private equity firm focused on growth investing. The acquisition was valued at approximately $5.2 billion, including $3.65 billion in cash and $1.55 billion in ICE common stock, and builds on ICE's global market data growth strategy by expanding the markets served, adding technology platforms and increasing new data and valuation services. Completion of the transaction was subject to regulatory approval and other customary closing conditions. The transaction completed on December 14, 2015.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Trayport_2015_and_subsequent_divestment">Trayport 2015 and subsequent divestment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Trayport 2015 and subsequent divestment" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In December 2015, ICE acquired Trayport for $650 million from <a href="/wiki/GFI_Group" title="GFI Group">GFI Group</a>. Trayport provides a trading technology platform that serves brokers, exchanges, clearing houses and trading participants, primarily in the European utility markets. Approximately 70%-80% of European utility trades flow through its platform.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[30]</a></sup></p><p>Following the acquisition, the <a href="/wiki/Competition_and_Markets_Authority" title="Competition and Markets Authority">Competition and Markets Authority</a> (CMA) called in the merger for review. In October 2016 it announced its decision to require ICE to sell Trayport, having ruled that the merger could lead to a substantial lessening of competition.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[31]</a></sup>  ICE challenged the decision in the <a href="/wiki/Competition_Appeal_Tribunal" title="Competition Appeal Tribunal">Competition Appeal Tribunal</a> (CAT), but the CAT upheld the CMA's decision.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[32]</a></sup> As a result, ICE sold Trayport to TMX Group in October 2017, in exchange for certain TMX Group assets and cash of £350 million.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[33]</a></sup> The CMA's ruling against ICE was the first time CMA had required that a company sell an asset that it had already bought.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Standard_.26_Poor.E2.80.99s_Securities_Evaluations_.28SPSE.29_2016"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Standard_&amp;_Poor’s_Securities_Evaluations_(SPSE)_2016">Standard &amp; Poor’s Securities Evaluations (SPSE) 2016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Standard &amp; Poor’s Securities Evaluations (SPSE) 2016" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In March 2016, ICE agreed to acquire <a href="/wiki/Standard_%26_Poor%27s" title="Standard &amp; Poor's">Standard &amp; Poor's</a> Securities Evaluations, Inc. (SPSE) and Credit Market Analysis (CMA), two assets under the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_Global" title="S&amp;P Global">S&amp;P Global</a> Market Intelligence business unit from <a href="/wiki/McGraw_Hill_Financial" class="mw-redirect" title="McGraw Hill Financial">McGraw Hill Financial</a>. Most terms of the agreement were not disclosed. In October 2016 it had completed its all-cash acquisition.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="TMX_Atrium_2017">TMX Atrium 2017</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: TMX Atrium 2017" data-section="15" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In February 2017, ICE announced it had entered an agreement to acquire TMX Atrium, an extranet and wireless services business from TMX Group. Terms of the agreement were not disclosed, and the transaction is expected to close within 90 days, subject to regulatory approvals. The financial impact of the transaction will be immaterial and was included in ICE's financial guidance for 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="BofA_Merrill_Lynch_Global_Research_Index_Platform_.28BofAML.29_2017"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="BofA_Merrill_Lynch_Global_Research_Index_Platform_(BofAML)_2017">BofA Merrill Lynch Global Research Index Platform (BofAML) 2017</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: BofA Merrill Lynch Global Research Index Platform (BofAML) 2017" data-section="16" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In February 2017, ICE announced that it had entered into a definitive agreement to acquire the Global Research division's index platform from <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_America_Merrill_Lynch" class="mw-redirect" title="Bank of America Merrill Lynch">Bank of America Merrill Lynch</a>. The BofAML indices are the second most used fixed income indices by assets under management (AUM) globally, and upon closing, the AUM benchmarked against the combined fixed income index business of ICE will be nearly $1 trillion. Upon closing, the indices will be re-branded as the ICE BofAML indices. The terms of the agreement were not disclosed, and the transaction is expected to be completed in the second half of 2017. The financial impact of the transaction is expected to be immaterial in 2017. In October 2017 ICE announced it has completed its acquisition. Furthermore, in August 2019, it announced it had entered into an agreement to acquire a family of fixed income volatility indices, including the prominent Merrill Lynch Option Volatility Estimate (“MOVE”) family of indices.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Euroclear_2017">Euroclear 2017</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Euroclear 2017" data-section="17" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In October 2017, ICE  announced it had acquired a 4.7% stake in <a href="/wiki/Euroclear" title="Euroclear">Euroclear</a> for EUR 275 million. ICE anticipates having one representative join the Board of Euroclear. Euroclear is a leading provider of post-trade services, including settlement, central securities depositories and related services for cross-border transactions across asset classes. It is understood ICE increased its stake to 10% in early 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Virtu_BondPoint_2017">Virtu BondPoint 2017</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Virtu BondPoint 2017" data-section="18" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In October 2017, ICE  announced it had entered into an agreement to acquire Virtu BondPoint from <a href="/wiki/Virtu_Financial" title="Virtu Financial">Virtu Financial</a> for $400 million in cash. The acquisition was reportedly completed on January 2, 2018 as announced by ICE.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[38]</a></sup>  BondPoint is an integrated, automated platform offering additional fixed income execution services and one of the broadest sets of fixed income instruments.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Chicago_Stock_Exchange_.28CHX.29_2018"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Chicago_Stock_Exchange_(CHX)_2018">Chicago Stock Exchange (CHX) 2018</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Chicago Stock Exchange (CHX) 2018" data-section="19" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In April 2018, ICE announced that it had entered into an agreement to acquire the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Stock_Exchange" title="Chicago Stock Exchange">Chicago Stock Exchange</a> (CHX), a full-service stock exchange, including trading, data and corporate listings services. The transaction is expected to close in the second quarter of 2018, subject to regulatory approvals. Terms of the transaction were not disclosed, and the financial impact will not be material to ICE or impact capital return plans.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[39]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="TMC_Bonds_LLC_2018">TMC Bonds LLC 2018</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: TMC Bonds LLC 2018" data-section="20" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In May 2018, ICE announced that it had entered into an agreement to acquire TMC Bonds LLC for $685 million in cash. Established in 2000, TMC Bonds is a fixed income marketplace, supporting anonymous trading in various asset classes including Municipals, Corporates, Treasuries, Agencies and Certificates of Deposit. The transaction is expected to close in the second half of 2018, subject to customary regulatory and anti-trust approvals, and is not expected to materially impact 2018 financial results or capital returns.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="MERSCORP_Holding.2C_Inc_2018"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="MERSCORP_Holding,_Inc_2018">MERSCORP Holding, Inc 2018</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: MERSCORP Holding, Inc 2018" data-section="21" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In October 2018, ICE announced that it had acquired the remaining equity of MERSCORP Holding, Inc., owner of <a href="/wiki/Mortgage_Electronic_Registration_Systems" title="Mortgage Electronic Registration Systems">Mortgage Electronic Registration Systems</a>, Inc. (MERS). MERSCORP owns and operates the MERS System, a national electronic registry that tracks the changes in servicing rights and beneficial ownership interests in U.S.-based mortgage loans. ICE had owned a majority equity interest in MERS since 2016. Earlier this month, ICE successfully moved the MERS System infrastructure to the ICE Mahwah data center, an integral requirement for completing the final acquisition of the business. Price and terms of the transaction were not disclosed and will not be material to ICE's earnings or have an impact on capital return plans.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Simplifile.2C_LC_2019"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Simplifile,_LC_2019">Simplifile, LC 2019</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Simplifile, LC 2019" data-section="22" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In May 2019, ICE announced that it had agreed acquire Simplifile, LC. Simplifile operates a network connecting agents and jurisdictions involved with residential mortgage records.
</p><p>ICE will pay $335 million to acquire Simplifile, which is based in <a href="/wiki/Provo,_Utah" title="Provo, Utah">Provo, Utah</a> and has no debt. The transaction is expected to close in the third quarter of 2019. Upon closing, Simplifile, which has approximately 200 employees, will continue to be based in Provo and operate under the Simplifile name.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[42]</a></sup></p>

<p>In June 2016 Intercontinental Exchange introduced the expanded ICE Data Services, which combined exchange data, valuations, analytics, and other software used by the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> (NYSE), <a href="/wiki/SuperDerivatives" title="SuperDerivatives">SuperDerivatives</a> and <a href="/wiki/Interactive_Data_Corporation" title="Interactive Data Corporation">Interactive Data</a> (IDC).<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[43]</a></sup></p><p>ICE originally formed its ICE Data subsidiary in 2003, recognizing the rising demand for exchange data as markets became increasingly automated. ICE continues to invest in its data services to address evolving customer needs driven by regulatory reform, market fragmentation, passive investing and indexation, along with increased demand for data capacity and security, and independent valuations. Their customers include global financial institutions, asset managers, commercial hedging firms, risk managers, corporate issuers and individual investors.
</p><p>ICE Data Services has offices in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Monica" class="mw-redirect" title="Santa Monica">California</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bedford,_Massachusetts" title="Bedford, Massachusetts">Bedford MA</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tel_Aviv" title="Tel Aviv">Tel Aviv</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, Singapore, <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> and <a href="/wiki/City_of_Melbourne" title="City of Melbourne">Melbourne</a>.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Bakkt">Bakkt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intercontinental_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Bakkt" data-section="25" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bakkt_Logo_2019.svg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Bakkt_Logo_2019.svg/220px-Bakkt_Logo_2019.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="46" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="400" data-file-height="83"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 46px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Bakkt_Logo_2019.svg/220px-Bakkt_Logo_2019.svg.png" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="46" data-srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Bakkt_Logo_2019.svg/330px-Bakkt_Logo_2019.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Bakkt_Logo_2019.svg/440px-Bakkt_Logo_2019.svg.png 2x" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bakkt_Logo_2019.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Logo of Bakkt</div></div></div>
<p>In August 2018 Intercontinental Exchange announced it was forming the new company Bakkt,<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[44]</a></sup> which is intended to leverage <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> online servers to manage digital assets. Bakkt was said to be working with <a href="/wiki/Boston_Consulting_Group" title="Boston Consulting Group">Boston Consulting Group</a> (BCG), <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a>, <a href="/wiki/Starbucks" title="Starbucks">Starbucks</a>, and others to create a software platform. The Bakkt ecosystem is expected to include federally regulated markets and warehousing along with merchant and consumer applications. Its first use cases will be for trading and conversion of <a href="/wiki/Bitcoin" title="Bitcoin">Bitcoin</a> (BTC) versus <a href="/wiki/Fiat_money" title="Fiat money">fiat currencies</a>. <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Loeffler" title="Kelly Loeffler">Kelly Loeffler</a> served as Bakkt's CEO until her appointment to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[45]</a></sup></p><p>As an initial component of the Bakkt offering, Intercontinental Exchange's U.S.-based futures exchange and clearing house plan to launch a 1-day physically delivered BTC contract along with physical warehousing in 2019, subject to CFTC review and approval, which was delayed.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[46]</a></sup> These regulated venues will establish new protocols for managing the specific security and settlement requirements of digital currencies. In addition, the clearing house plans to create a separate guarantee fund that will be funded by Bakkt.
</p><p>In December 2018 Bakkt completed its first round of funding, raising US$182.5 million from investors such as <a href="/wiki/Boston_Consulting_Group" title="Boston Consulting Group">Boston Consulting Group</a>, <a href="/wiki/Li_Ka-shing" title="Li Ka-shing">Li Ka-shing's</a> <a href="/wiki/Horizons_Ventures" title="Horizons Ventures">Horizons Ventures</a>, Intercontinental Exchange, Microsoft's venture capital arm, <a href="/wiki/M12_(venture_capital)" title="M12 (venture capital)">M12</a>, Pantera Capital, <a href="/wiki/PayU" title="PayU">PayU</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Naspers" title="Naspers">Naspers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[47]</a></sup> On May 13, 2019, Bakkt announced a firm date for the launch of the platform in July.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[48]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2019, Bakkt announced that it had entered into an agreement to acquire certain assets of Rosenthal Collins Group (RCG), to close in February.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[49]</a></sup> In April 2019, Bakkt announced that it had acquired Digital Asset Custody Company (DACC). Terms of the transaction have not been disclosed.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[50]</a></sup> In September 2019 the exchange began trading of bitcoin futures.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJBakkt_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJBakkt-52">[51]</a></sup> In February 2020, ICE announced that it agreed to acquire Bridge2 Solutions, a leading provider of loyalty solutions for merchants and consumers. Following the completion of the transaction, Bakkt plans to acquire Bridge2 Solutions from ICE using proceeds from Bakkt's Series B round of funding. <sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[52]</a></sup></p>
