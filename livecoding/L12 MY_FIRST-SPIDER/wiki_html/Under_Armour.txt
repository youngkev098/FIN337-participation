</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Under_Armour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Under_Armour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early history" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kevin_Plank_-_UA_photo.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Kevin_Plank_-_UA_photo.JPG/180px-Kevin_Plank_-_UA_photo.JPG" decoding="async" width="180" height="271" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3399" data-file-height="5112"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 180px;height: 271px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Kevin_Plank_-_UA_photo.JPG/180px-Kevin_Plank_-_UA_photo.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="180" data-height="271" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kevin_Plank_-_UA_photo.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Kevin_Plank" title="Kevin Plank">Kevin Plank</a>, founder of Under Armour</div></div></div>
<p>Under Armour was founded on September 25, 1996 by <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Plank" title="Kevin Plank">Kevin Plank</a>, a then 24-year-old former special teams captain of the <a href="/wiki/Maryland_Terrapins_football" title="Maryland Terrapins football">University of Maryland football team</a>. Plank initially began the business from his grandmother's basement in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a><sup id="cite_ref-our_history_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-our_history-4">[4]</a></sup> He spent his time traveling along the East Coast with nothing but apparel in the trunk of his car. His first team sale came at the end of 1996 with a $17,000 sale. From his grandmother's basement, Plank moved to Baltimore. After a few moves in the city he moved to his current headquarters in Tide Point.<sup id="cite_ref-our_history2_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-our_history2-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>As a <a href="/wiki/Fullback_(gridiron_football)" title="Fullback (gridiron football)">fullback</a> at the University of Maryland, Plank got tired of having to change out of the sweat-soaked T-shirts worn under his jersey; however, he noticed that his <a href="/wiki/Compression_shorts" class="mw-redirect" title="Compression shorts">compression shorts</a> worn during practice stayed dry. This inspired him to make a T-shirt using moisture-wicking <a href="/wiki/Synthetic_fabric" class="mw-redirect" title="Synthetic fabric">synthetic fabric</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ua_performance_apparel_history_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ua_performance_apparel_history-6">[6]</a></sup> After graduating from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Maryland,_College_Park" title="University of Maryland, College Park">University of Maryland</a>, Plank developed his first prototype of the shirt, which he gave to his Maryland teammates and friends who had gone on to play in the <a href="/wiki/NFL" class="mw-redirect" title="NFL">NFL</a>. Plank soon perfected the design creating a new T-shirt built from <a href="/wiki/Microfiber" title="Microfiber">microfibers</a> that wicked moisture and kept athletes cool, dry, and light.<sup id="cite_ref-our_history_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-our_history-4">[4]</a></sup>  Major competing brands including <a href="/wiki/Nike_Inc" class="mw-redirect" title="Nike Inc">Nike</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">Adidas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reebok" title="Reebok">Reebok</a> would soon follow in Plank's footsteps with their own moisture-wicking apparel.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Plank opted to use the British spelling "armour" in the company name because the toll-free <a href="/wiki/Toll-free_telephone_number#Vanity_numbering" title="Toll-free telephone number">vanity number</a> was still available for that version.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>People began to take notice of the brand when a front-page photo of <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a></i> featured <a href="/wiki/Oakland_Raiders" class="mw-redirect" title="Oakland Raiders">Oakland Raiders</a> quarterback <a href="/wiki/Jeff_George" title="Jeff George">Jeff George</a> wearing an Under Armour mock <a href="/wiki/Turtleneck" class="mw-redirect" title="Turtleneck">turtleneck</a>. Following that front page, Under Armour's first major sale came, when an equipment manager from <a href="/wiki/Georgia_Tech_Yellow_Jackets_football" title="Georgia Tech Yellow Jackets football">Georgia Tech</a> requested 10 shirts from Plank. This deal opened the door to a contract with <a href="/wiki/NC_State_Wolfpack_football" title="NC State Wolfpack football">NC State</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arizona_State_Sun_Devils_football" title="Arizona State Sun Devils football">Arizona State</a>, and other <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_I_Football_Bowl_Subdivision" title="NCAA Division I Football Bowl Subdivision">Division I</a> football teams. With positive reviews from players, word began to spread and orders began to increase.<sup id="cite_ref-ua_performance_apparel_history_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ua_performance_apparel_history-6">[6]</a></sup> That same year, Under Armour launched with several new apparel lines including ColdGear, TurfGear, AllseasonGear, and StreetGear.<sup id="cite_ref-ua_performance_apparel_history_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ua_performance_apparel_history-6">[6]</a></sup> By the end of 1996, Under Armour had sold 500 Under Armour HeatGear shirts, generating $17,000 for the company.<sup id="cite_ref-ua_performance_apparel_history_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ua_performance_apparel_history-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1997, Plank had $100,000 in orders to fill and found a factory in Ohio to make the shirts.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Growth">Growth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Under_Armour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Growth" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Under_Armour_headquarters_1.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Under_Armour_headquarters_1.JPG/250px-Under_Armour_headquarters_1.JPG" decoding="async" width="250" height="188" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="4032" data-file-height="3024"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 250px;height: 188px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Under_Armour_headquarters_1.JPG/250px-Under_Armour_headquarters_1.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="250" data-height="188" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Under_Armour_headquarters_1.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Buildings at the Under Armour headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Baltimore,_Maryland" class="mw-redirect" title="Baltimore, Maryland">Baltimore, Maryland</a></div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:UnderArmourCanada.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/UnderArmourCanada.jpg/220px-UnderArmourCanada.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="4535" data-file-height="3024"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 147px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/UnderArmourCanada.jpg/220px-UnderArmourCanada.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="147" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:UnderArmourCanada.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Under Armour office in Canada</div></div></div>
<p>Under Armour received its first big break in 1999 when <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Entertainment,_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Warner Bros. Entertainment, Inc.">Warner Brothers</a> contacted Under Armour to outfit two of its feature films, <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Stone" title="Oliver Stone">Oliver Stone's</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Any_Given_Sunday" title="Any Given Sunday">Any Given Sunday</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Replacements_(film)" title="The Replacements (film)">The Replacements</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-our_history_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-our_history-4">[4]</a></sup> In <i>Any Given Sunday</i>, Willie Beamen (played by <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Foxx" title="Jamie Foxx">Jamie Foxx</a>) wears an Under Armour <a href="/wiki/Jockstrap" title="Jockstrap">jockstrap</a>. Leveraging the release of <i>Any Given Sunday</i>, Plank purchased an ad in <i><a href="/wiki/ESPN_The_Magazine" title="ESPN The Magazine">ESPN The Magazine</a></i>. The ad generated close to $750,000 in sales.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="9 years???? that does not add up. (January 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>The following year, Under Armour became the outfitter of the new <a href="/wiki/XFL_(2001)" title="XFL (2001)">XFL</a> football league, gaining even more attention during the league's debut on national television.<sup id="cite_ref-ua_performance_apparel_history_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ua_performance_apparel_history-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2003, consumer sector focused private equity firm Rosewood Capital invested $12 million into the company.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>  The same year, the company launched its first television commercial, which centered on their motto, "Protect this house."<sup id="cite_ref-our_history_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-our_history-4">[4]</a></sup> The company IPO'd on the NASDAQ in November 2005, raising $153m of capital.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>  In late 2007, Under Armour opened its first full-line full-price retail location at the <a href="/wiki/Westfield_Annapolis" title="Westfield Annapolis">Westfield Annapolis</a> mall in <a href="/wiki/Annapolis,_Maryland" title="Annapolis, Maryland">Annapolis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>It has also opened several specialty stores and factory outlet locations in Canada, China, and 39 states including the opening of its first Brand House in Baltimore in 2013 and second Brand House in <a href="/wiki/Tyson%27s_Corner,_Virginia" class="mw-redirect" title="Tyson's Corner, Virginia">Tyson's Corner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In 2009, baseball Hall of Famer <a href="/wiki/Cal_Ripken_Jr." title="Cal Ripken Jr.">Cal Ripken Jr.</a> formed an alliance under which the company would have significant presence at several venues and events under the auspices of Ripken Baseball, including providing uniforms for the minor league <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen_IronBirds" title="Aberdeen IronBirds">Aberdeen IronBirds</a> and youth teams participating in the Cal Ripken World Series.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>The company is reported to be the major commercial sponsor for the <a href="/wiki/Reality_TV" class="mw-redirect" title="Reality TV">reality TV</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/Duck_Dynasty" title="Duck Dynasty">Duck Dynasty</a></i> and has garnered attention for taking a stand supporting show "patriarch" <a href="/wiki/Phil_Robertson" title="Phil Robertson">Phil Robertson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>Under Armour provided the suits worn by speedskaters in the <a href="/wiki/2014_Winter_Olympics" title="2014 Winter Olympics">2014 Winter Olympics</a>. The US speedskaters were losing while wearing the new Mach 39 speedsuits, but when they reverted to the previous model suits, the skaters continued to lose. Although there did not appear to be a design flaw in the suit that caused the poor results, the news of the suits caused Under Armour stock to drop 2.38%.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj20140218_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj20140218-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>The company, offering a reported <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>250,000,000 over 10 years, also bid hard over Nike to sign NBA MVP <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Durant" title="Kevin Durant">Kevin Durant</a> to an endorsement deal. However, Nike ultimately re-signed Durant after agreeing to structure a contract, offering <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>300,000,000.<sup id="cite_ref-yahoo.com_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo.com-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>On January 21, 2014, it was announced that the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Notre_Dame" title="University of Notre Dame">University of Notre Dame</a> and Under Armour had come to terms on providing uniforms and athletic equipment for the university. This 10-year deal was the largest of its kind in the history of college athletics and became effective July 1, 2014. As of 2014, Under Armour had an operating profit of more than 30%, accelerating from its 2013 pace. The company's share price soared 62.5% that year.<sup id="cite_ref-yahoo.com_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo.com-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>After its November 2013 acquisition of digital app maker MapMyFitness for <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>150,000,000, in February 2015 Under Armour announced it had purchased the calorie and nutrition counting app maker <a href="/wiki/MyFitnessPal" title="MyFitnessPal">MyFitnessPal</a> for $475m, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Fitness_app" title="Fitness app">fitness app</a> maker <a href="/wiki/Endomondo" title="Endomondo">Endomondo</a> for <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>85,000,000.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>On January 6, 2016, Under Armour announced a strategic partnership with <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a> to use IBM <a href="/wiki/Watson_(computer)" title="Watson (computer)">Watson</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Cognitive_computing" title="Cognitive computing">cognitive computing</a> technology to provide meaningful data from its IOT kit and UA Record app.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>On March 3, 2016, the company became the Official Match Ball Partner of the <a href="/wiki/North_American_Soccer_League" title="North American Soccer League">North American Soccer League</a>, starting with their 2016 season.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>On May 26, 2016, Under Armour and <a href="/wiki/UCLA_Bruins" title="UCLA Bruins">UCLA</a> announced their plans for a 15-year, $280 million contract, making this the largest shoe and apparel sponsorship in <a href="/wiki/NCAA_history" class="mw-redirect" title="NCAA history">NCAA history</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2016, Under Armour leased the 53,000-square-foot (4,900 m<sup>2</sup>) space formerly occupied by <a href="/wiki/FAO_Schwarz" title="FAO Schwarz">FAO Schwarz</a> on New York's <a href="/wiki/Fifth_Avenue" title="Fifth Avenue">Fifth Avenue</a>; the store opening is planned for 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-adweek_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adweek-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>  FAO Schwarz had been paying $20 million in rent.<sup id="cite_ref-adweek_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adweek-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>On December 5, 2016, Under Armour reached a 10-year agreement with <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> to become the official on-field uniform provider of the MLB, beginning in 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Under Armour will replace <a href="/wiki/Majestic_Athletic" title="Majestic Athletic">Majestic</a>, who has been MLB's uniform provider since 2004. However, in May 2018, it was reported that Under Armour would be backing out of the deal with MLB, in order to save the company around $50 million. Nike would instead become the on-field uniform supplier of the league.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> MLB's deal with Nike became official on January 25, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>Under Armour has a partnership with NBA athlete <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Curry" title="Stephen Curry">Stephen Curry</a>, who is considered to be the "face of their footwear line".<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Originally signed to <a href="/wiki/Nike,_Inc." title="Nike, Inc.">Nike</a>, Curry joined with Under Armour in the 2013 offseason.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> As Curry became a two-time <a href="/wiki/NBA_Most_Valuable_Player_Award" title="NBA Most Valuable Player Award">NBA Most Valuable Player Award</a> winner and one of the most popular athletes in the world, sales of his shoes have become a major factor for the Under Armour brand, with stock prices rising and falling based on the success of the Curry shoe line.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>Under Armour has a partnership with PGA golfer <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Spieth" One."<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p><p>On October 10, 2018, Under Armour announced that it has signed <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_76ers" title="Philadelphia 76ers">Philadelphia 76ers</a> center <a href="/wiki/Joel_Embiid" title="Joel Embiid">Joel Embiid</a> to a sneaker endorsement deal.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2019, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Plank" title="Kevin Plank">Kevin Plank</a> announced he would be stepping down by 2020, and COO  <a href="/wiki/Patrik_Frisk" title="Patrik Frisk">Patrik Frisk</a> would succeed him as CEO in January 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup <h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_releases">Recent releases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Under_Armour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Recent releases" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Under Armour announced an addition to their ColdGear product line, called Infrared, that was released in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes-44">[44]</a></sup> This line claims to dissipate and re-circulate heat around the wearer's body using ceramic powder. This was purportedly designed so that the wearer's heat signature will not be picked up. Under Armour also had released a product with scent control technology in 2012 titled, "Under Armour Scent Control". This line claims to be designed so that the wearer's scent cannot be detected.<sup id="cite_ref-Shooting_Sports_News_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shooting_Sports_News-45">[45]</a></sup></p><p>Under Armour is inventing a new type of shirt called "Coldblack," which is designed to reflect heat and keep athletes cool in the sun.<sup id="cite_ref-Baltimore_Sun_Under_Armour_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Baltimore_Sun_Under_Armour-46">[46]</a></sup> Under Armour has come out with new styles for football uniforms. In October 2012, Under Armour created "The Wounded Warrior" project for football uniforms.<sup id="cite_ref-Sneaker_Report_Under_Armour_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sneaker_Report_Under_Armour-47">[47]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Hawaii_Rainbow_Warriors_football" title="Hawaii Rainbow Warriors football">University of Hawaii</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boston_College_Eagles_football" title="Boston College Eagles football">Boston College</a> wore the star studded cleats and the American striped jerseys. They were revealed when Boston College played Maryland on October 27, 2012, also when Hawaii played UNLV on November 24, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-Sneaker_Report_Under_Armour_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sneaker_Report_Under_Armour-47">[47]</a></sup></p><p>The company produces the Speedform shoe in a Chinese bra factory. It has no insole and little stitching. Shoes are Under Armour's fastest growing product line, growing 31% from 2011 to $239 million in sales in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, Under Armour launched two new pairs of connected running shoes at CES. Inside each shoe is a Bluetooth module, accelerator and gyroscope.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Recalls">Recalls</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Under_Armour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Recalls" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On April 29, 2009, the United States <a href="/wiki/Consumer_Products_Safety_Commission" class="mw-redirect" title="Consumer Products Safety Commission">Consumer Products Safety Commission</a> announced a voluntary recall of all Under Armour brand athletic cups<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (February 2020)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. The cups might break when hit, posing a risk of serious injury. They were sold from January 2006 to March 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p><p>On November 3, 2011, the United States <a href="/wiki/Consumer_Products_Safety_Commission" class="mw-redirect" title="Consumer Products Safety Commission">Consumer Products Safety Commission</a> announced a voluntary recall of all Under Armour Defender chin straps. The straps pose a laceration hazard when a player contacts the metal snap. They were sold from January 2008 to September 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Sales">Sales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Under_Armour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Sales" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2014, sales of apparel, footwear and accessories represented 74.3%, 14% and 9.9% of net revenues, respectively. Revenue has grown steadily by 30% from 2010–2014.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> As of January 28, 2016, the fourth quarter net revenues for 2015 increased 31% to $1.17 Billion while the full year net revenues increased 28% to $3.96 Billion. Based on these numbers, Under Armour has updated its 2016 net revenues outlook to approximately $4.95 Billion (+25%).<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Community_involvement">Community involvement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Under_Armour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Community involvement" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>UA Give Back is Under Armour's effort to become involved in different facets of the world community. They have different branches in their organization that specialize in each cause that Under Armour supports. They consist of the <i>UA Power in Pink</i>, <i>UA Freedom</i> and <i>UA Win Global</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-UA_Give_Back_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UA_Give_Back-54">[54]</a></sup></p><p><i>UA Power in Pink</i> is an Under Armour program that promotes women using exercise and fitness to fight <a href="/wiki/Breast_cancer_awareness" title="Breast cancer awareness">breast cancer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Under_Armour_and_the_Community_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Under_Armour_and_the_Community-55">[55]</a></sup>  <i>UA Freedom</i> is an Under Armour program that supports the efforts of the <a href="/wiki/Wounded_Warrior_Project" title="Wounded Warrior Project">Wounded Warrior Project</a> whose mission is to honor, raise awareness, and aid wounded veterans and military service members.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup>  <i>UA Win Global</i> is a children's athletics program which focuses on "underserved communities" by enhancing play areas, investing in sports programs and providing support for sports coaches.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p><p>As of 2012, Under Armour was the sponsor of the <a href="/wiki/Baseball_Factory" title="Baseball Factory">Baseball Factory</a>'s "Premium Video Program", which holds more than 100 events for youth athletes across the United States annually.<sup id="cite_ref-Baseball_Factory_Under_Armour_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Baseball_Factory_Under_Armour-58">[58]</a></sup></p><p>After ads of many brands ran on extremist channels promoting <a href="/wiki/Extremism" title="Extremism">extremist</a> content on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, Under Armour pulled its ad from <a href="/wiki/Alphabet_Inc." title="Alphabet Inc.">Alphabet Inc.</a> owned YouTube.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="UA_Fitness_Performance_Center_and_historic_mural_covering">UA Fitness Performance Center and historic mural covering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Under_Armour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: UA Fitness Performance Center and historic mural covering" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2015, Under Armour released plans for their new Under Armour Fitness Performance Center powered by FX Studios at the <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_America_Building_(Baltimore)" title="Bank of America Building (Baltimore)">Bank of America Building</a> at 10 Light Street in Baltimore. The Fitness Performance Center is planned to take place in the building's lobby, covering historic murals of Baltimore's history by art deco artist <a href="/wiki/Hildreth_Meiere" title="Hildreth Meiere">Hildreth Meiere</a> with artificial turf flooring. This plan has been seen as controversial due to the fact that <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Plank" title="Kevin Plank">Kevin Plank</a>, Nate Costa (president of FX Studios) and developer Cary Euwer used state and federal tax credits to restore the historical building, yet the historical murals are being covered up by turf flooring.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> Many historians and local Baltimoreons have expressed public outcry that the design should be reconsidered and the murals should be saved.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Trophy_hunting_video">Trophy hunting video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Under_Armour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Trophy hunting video" data-section="15" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In January 2016 Under Armour initiated a marketing campaign featuring women hunters including an American woman named Sarah Bowmar.<sup id="cite_ref-BusIn_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BusIn-67">[67]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> In August 2016 a video filmed by Bowmar, showing her husband spearing a bear and then rejoicing that he had hit it, went viral. The bear was found dead the next day.<sup id="cite_ref-BusIn_67-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BusIn-67">[67]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> Under Armour was heavily criticized worldwide and as result, the company ended its contract with Bowmar a few days after the video became known.<sup id="cite_ref-BusIn_67-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BusIn-67">[67]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Trump_endorsement_and_public_boycott">Trump endorsement and public boycott</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Under_Armour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Trump endorsement and public boycott" data-section="16" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Trump_accepts_nomination.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Trump_accepts_nomination.jpg/220px-Trump_accepts_nomination.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="720"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 124px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Trump_accepts_nomination.jpg/220px-Trump_accepts_nomination.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="124" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Trump_accepts_nomination.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Plank expressed support for President Trump in February 2017.</div></div></div>
<p>On February 7, 2017, two weeks into the presidency of <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a>, CEO Kevin Plank expressed his support for the administration and its friendliness to corporations.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> Plank's statements immediately drew strong public backlash, with many Under Armour customers boycotting the brand.
</p><p>Numerous athletes contracted with Under Armour also spoke out in opposition to Plank's statements. <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Curry" title="Stephen Curry">Stephen Curry</a>, the most valuable of the company's sponsorships, hinted that his loyalty to the company could be jeopardized if its actions do not reflect his values.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Dwayne_Johnson" title="Dwayne Johnson">Dwayne Johnson</a> voiced disapproval with the CEO's personal attitudes being reflected onto the company's partners and employees.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p><p>On February 15, eight days following Plank's statements, Plank took out a full-page ad in the <i>Baltimore Sun</i> to clarify his position and that of the company. The letter acknowledged that he had used "a choice of words that did not accurately reflect my intent" and described how his company valued diversity, entrepreneurship and job creation. He condemned the administration's ban on travel to and from seven primarily Muslim nations, adding "we will join a coalition of companies in opposition to any new actions that negatively impact our team, their families or our community."<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup></p><p>In a statement issued to reporters on June 2, Plank deplored the Trump Administration's decision to withdraw the United States from the <a href="/wiki/Paris_agreement" class="mw-redirect" title="Paris agreement">Paris agreement</a>:
</p>
<blockquote><p>We at Under Armour are disappointed by the Administration's decision to withdraw from the Paris Climate Agreement as climate change continues to threaten our planet, our cities and our economies. Climate change is real and must be taken seriously by our business community, our customers, our neighbors and our elected officials. Sustainability has always been part of our DNA: it's integral to how we live and work and is essential to our environment. As a business leader concerned with creating American jobs, I disagree with the decision to exit the Paris accord.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>On August 14, 2017, Plank removed himself from President Trump's Manufacturing Jobs Initiative Council,<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> following backlash over Trump's response to the events in <a href="/wiki/Charlottesville,_Virginia" title="Charlottesville, Virginia">Charlottesville, Virginia</a>.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Fireproofing_and_flammability">Fireproofing and flammability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Under_Armour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Fireproofing and flammability" data-section="17" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In June 2018 a Facebook post and subsequent <a href="/wiki/Reddit" title="Reddit">Reddit</a> thread emerged stating that Under Armour clothing was flammable and resulted in severe burn injuries to a child.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> At present there has been no public statement from the company, and public debate seems to circle around the question of what is distinct about fireproof clothing, flammability, and risk of burn injury. This was found out to be false.
</p><p>Though no recall was issued, a 2018 study found that Under Armour's material was more likely to result in a burn injury of the wearer than most other undergarment materials.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Federal_probe">Federal probe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Under_Armour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Federal probe" data-section="18" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In November 2019, the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission">U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission</a> made public that Under Armour's accounting policies had been under scrutiny for more than two years.<sup id="cite_ref-Wall_Street_Journal_78-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wall_Street_Journal-78">[78]</a></sup></p>
