<p><b>The Estée Lauder Companies Inc.</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/ɛ/: 'e' in 'dress'">ɛ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/eɪ/: 'a' in 'face'">eɪ</span></span><span class="wrap"> </span><span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ɔː/: 'au' in 'fraud'">ɔː</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/ər/: 'er' in 'letter'">ər</span></span>/</a></span></span> stylized as <b>ESTĒE LAUDER</b>) is an American <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> manufacturer and marketer of prestige skincare, <a href="/wiki/Makeup" class="mw-redirect" title="Makeup">makeup</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fragrance" class="mw-redirect" title="Fragrance">fragrance</a> and hair care products, based in <a href="/wiki/Midtown_Manhattan" title="Midtown Manhattan">Midtown Manhattan</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. The company owns a diverse portfolio of brands, distributed internationally through both <a href="/wiki/Digital_commerce" class="mw-redirect" title="Digital commerce">digital commerce</a> and <a href="/wiki/Retail" title="Retail">retail channels</a>.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Est%C3%A9e_Lauder_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company began in 1946 when <a href="/wiki/Est%C3%A9e_Lauder_(businesswoman)" title="Estée Lauder (businesswoman)">Estée Lauder</a> and her husband Joseph Lauder began producing cosmetics in New York City. They first carried only four products: Cleansing Oil, Skin Lotion, Super Rich All purpose Creme, and Creme Pack.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Two years later, in 1948 they established their first <a href="/wiki/Department_store" title="Department store">department store</a> account with <a href="/wiki/Saks_Fifth_Avenue" title="Saks Fifth Avenue">Saks Fifth Avenue</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>.
</p><p>Over the next 15 years, they expanded the range and continued to sell their products in the United States. In 1960, the company started its first international account in the London department store <a href="/wiki/Harrods" title="Harrods">Harrods</a>. The following year it opened an office in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>.
</p><p>In 1964, they introduced <a href="/wiki/Aramis_(fragrance)" title="Aramis (fragrance)">Aramis</a>, a line of fragrance and grooming products for men named after an exotic Turkish root originally used as an aphrodisiac.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1967, Estée Lauder herself was named one of ten Outstanding Women in Business in the United States by business and financial editors.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> This was followed by a Spirit of Achievement Award from <a href="/wiki/Albert_Einstein_College_of_Medicine" title="Albert Einstein College of Medicine">Albert Einstein College of Medicine</a> at <a href="/wiki/Yeshiva_University" title="Yeshiva University">Yeshiva University</a> in 1968.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In that year, the company expanded again, opening <a href="/wiki/Clinique" title="Clinique">Clinique</a>,  a <a href="/wiki/Dermatologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Dermatologist">dermatologist</a>-guided (Dr. <a href="/wiki/Norman_Orentreich" title="Norman Orentreich">Norman Orentreich</a>), <a href="/wiki/Allergy" title="Allergy">allergy</a>-tested, fragrance-free cosmetic brand.
</p><p>Estée Lauder's Clinique <a href="/wiki/Brand" title="Brand">brand</a> became the first women's cosmetic company to introduce a second line for men when, in 1976, they began a separate line called "Skin Supplies for Men", which continues to be sold at Clinique counters worldwide. In 1981, the company's products became available in the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>.
</p><p>In the 1990s, brand acquisitions and licensing agreements contributed to explosive growth as the company transformed from a family-owned business to a publicly traded, family-controlled organization. The decade opened with the creation of Origins — the first wellness brand in U.S. department stores. The first licensing agreement for fragrances was with fashion designer <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Hilfiger" title="Tommy Hilfiger">Tommy Hilfiger</a> in 1993, followed by <a href="/wiki/Kiton" title="Kiton">Kiton</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> an Italian fashion house (1995), and with American fashion designer <a href="/wiki/Donna_Karan" title="Donna Karan">Donna Karan</a> (1997).<sup id="cite_ref-Key_moments_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Key_moments-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>Brand acquisitions began with an investment in the Toronto-based <a href="/wiki/MAC_Cosmetics" title="MAC Cosmetics">M•A•C Cosmetics</a> in 1994, which the company then acquired in 1998. <a href="/wiki/Bobbi_Brown_Cosmetics" class="mw-redirect" title="Bobbi Brown Cosmetics">Bobbi Brown Cosmetics</a>, designed by the celebrated makeup artist, was acquired in 1995, as was <a href="/wiki/La_Mer_(cosmetics)" class="mw-redirect" title="La Mer (cosmetics)">La Mer</a> – along with the original recipe for its supreme luxury product, Crème de la Mer, containing the nutrient-rich Miracle Broth™. The company ventured into its first hair care and holistic beauty brand with <a href="/wiki/Aveda" title="Aveda">Aveda</a> in 1997. The renowned fragrance house <a href="/wiki/Jo_Malone_London" title="Jo Malone London">Jo Malone London</a> was acquired in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-Key_moments_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Key_moments-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>On November 16, 1995, The Estée Lauder Companies <a href="/wiki/Went_public" class="mw-redirect" title="Went public">went public</a> on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> at $26.00 a share ($6.50 on a post-split basis).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>Acquisitions and licensing continued in the 2000s as The Estée Lauder Companies bought a majority interest in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bumble_%26_bumble&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bumble &amp; bumble (page does not exist)">Bumble &amp; bumble</a>, the trendy hair care salon, and completed its acquisition in 2006; an exclusive global licensing agreement was signed with fashion designer <a href="/wiki/Michael_Kors" title="Michael Kors">Michael Kors</a> (2003). Designer <a href="/wiki/Tom_Ford" title="Tom Ford">Tom Ford</a> begins a project with the company and later an agreement was signed with him (2005) to develop and distribute fragrances and cosmetics under the Tom Ford Beauty brand.<sup id="cite_ref-Key_moments_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Key_moments-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>On July 1, 2010, the company acquired <a href="/w/index.php?title=Smashbox&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Smashbox (page does not exist)">Smashbox</a> Beauty Cosmetics, Inc., a brand created in Smashbox Studios in <a href="/wiki/Culver_City,_California" title="Culver City, California">Culver City, California</a>, by brothers Dean and Davis Factor (as in <a href="/wiki/Max_Factor" title="Max Factor">Max</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<p>On October 28, 2011, Aramis and Designer Fragrances, a division of The Estée Lauder Companies Inc., and <a href="/wiki/Tory_Burch_LLC" title="Tory Burch LLC">Tory Burch LLC</a> announced the signing of a multiyear agreement for the exclusive worldwide license of the Tory Burch fragrance business. This partnership marked Tory Burch's first step into the beauty industry. The first <a href="/wiki/Tory_Burch" title="Tory Burch">Tory Burch</a> fragrance products were introduced in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012, the company launched <a href="/wiki/Aerin_Lauder" title="Aerin Lauder">AERIN Beauty</a>, a luxury lifestyle beauty and fragrance brand inspired by the signature style of its founder, <a href="/wiki/Aerin_Lauder" title="Aerin Lauder">Aerin Lauder</a>.
</p><p>In 2014, the company acquired two insider beauty brands, RODIN olio lusso, a skin care brand renowned for its "Luxury Face Oil," and <a href="/wiki/Le_Labo" title="Le Labo">Le Labo</a>, a fragrance and sensory lifestyle brand. Later that year, the company also made its first investment in India by buying a minority stake in <a href="/wiki/Forest_Essentials" title="Forest Essentials">Forest Essentials</a>, a luxury cosmetics company specializing in <a href="/wiki/Ayurveda" title="Ayurveda">Ayurvedic</a> products.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 2015, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9ric_Malle#Editions_de_Parfum_Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9ric_Malle" title="Frédéric Malle">Editions de Parfums Frédéric Malle</a>, a fragrance brand, and GLAMGLOW, a Hollywood skin care brand.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In 2016, the company acquired Becca Cosmetics, their first color cosmetic group acquisition since Smashbox in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In November 2016, the company made its largest acquisition to date by acquiring California-based cosmetics company Too Faced for US$1.45 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Est%C3%A9e_Lauder_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Marketing" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>The Estée Lauder brand has had sometimes high-profile spokesmodels, sometimes referred to simply as 'faces'. Past 'faces' for Estée Lauder include <a href="/wiki/Karen_Graham" title="Karen Graham">Karen Graham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Boxleitner" title="Bruce Boxleitner">Bruce Boxleitner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shaun_Casey" title="Shaun Casey">Shaun Casey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Willow_Bay" title="Willow Bay">Willow Bay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paulina_Porizkova" title="Paulina Porizkova">Paulina Porizkova</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Hurley" title="Elizabeth Hurley">Elizabeth Hurley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carolyn_Murphy" title="Carolyn Murphy">Carolyn Murphy</a>, supermodel <a href="/wiki/Liya_Kebede" title="Liya Kebede">Liya Kebede</a> was the first African American 'face' of Estee Lauder, <a href="/wiki/Anja_Rubik" title="Anja Rubik">Anja Rubik</a>, and actress <a href="/wiki/Gwyneth_Paltrow" title="Gwyneth Paltrow">Gwyneth Paltrow</a>. As of 2008<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Est%C3%A9e_Lauder_Companies&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> the main spokesmodel for Estée Lauder was supermodel <a href="/wiki/Hilary_Rhoda" title="Hilary Rhoda">Hilary Rhoda</a>. In 2010, the company added three more faces to the roster, Chinese model <a href="/wiki/Liu_Wen_(model)" title="Liu Wen (model)">Liu Wen</a>, Puerto Rican model <a href="/wiki/Joan_Smalls" title="Joan Smalls">Joan Smalls</a>, and French model <a href="/wiki/Constance_Jablonski" title="Constance Jablonski">Constance Jablonski</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-model.com_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-model.com-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, Estée Lauder signed model <a href="/wiki/Kendall_Jenner" title="Kendall Jenner">Kendall Jenner</a> to promote the brand.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, the company announced Violette Serrat as their Global Beauty Director.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>Alvin Chereskin, the founder of AC&amp;R, was the long-time creative partner of the company.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Finances">Finances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Est%C3%A9e_Lauder_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Finances" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>For fiscal year 2016, the Estée Lauder Companies achieved net sales of $11.26 billion, a 4% increase compared with $10.78 billion in the prior year. Net earnings for the year were $1.11 billion, a 2% increase compared with $1.09 billion last year, and diluted net earnings per common share rose 5% to $2.96, compared with $2.82 reported in the prior year.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> As of 2018, Estée Lauder Companies ranked 258 on the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> list of the largest United States corporations by revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span id="Breast_Cancer_Awareness_Campaign_.28BCA.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Breast_Cancer_Awareness_Campaign_(BCA)">Breast Cancer Awareness Campaign (BCA)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Est%C3%A9e_Lauder_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Breast Cancer Awareness Campaign (BCA)" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>In October 1992, the BCA campaign was launched by <a href="/wiki/Evelyn_Lauder" title="Evelyn Lauder">Evelyn Lauder</a> (Estée's daughter in law) who co-created the "Pink Ribbon"<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> with <i><a href="/wiki/Self_(magazine)" title="Self (magazine)">SELF</a></i> magazine as a symbol of breast health. Since then, millions of people globally have heard the message about the importance of breast health and early detection can save lives.
The Estée Lauder Companies’ annual Breast Cancer Awareness campaign involves all of the 18 brands that make up the Estée Lauder Companies. They collectively represent <a href="/wiki/The_Breast_Cancer_Research_Foundation" class="mw-redirect" title="The Breast Cancer Research Foundation">The Breast Cancer Research Foundation</a>’s first and largest corporate supporter. Estée's daughter-in-law created BCRF's signature pink ribbon.
</p><p>Over $10 million were raised for the Breast Cancer Research Foundation between 1993 and summer 2003. Another $1 million were raised from their retail partners between July 2002 and summer 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(6)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Est%C3%A9e_Lauder_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Controversies" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-6 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-6">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="SOPA">SOPA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Est%C3%A9e_Lauder_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: SOPA" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Estée Lauder Companies appeared on lists of major companies supporting <a href="/wiki/SOPA" class="mw-redirect" title="SOPA">SOPA</a>, the controversial, but unsuccessful, Congressional anti-piracy bill that was considered overreaching by critics.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Child_labor">Child labor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Est%C3%A9e_Lauder_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Child labor" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2001, it was reported that <a href="/wiki/Twelve_Tribes_communities#Child_labor_and_homeschooling_controversies" title="Twelve Tribes communities">children</a> were discovered working in a factory in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_New_York" title="Cambridge, New York">Cambridge, New York</a>, making products for Origins, one of Estee Lauder's natural products brands.  This was true, the owner's granddaughter and grandson. The contracted company was Common Sense Natural Soap &amp; Bodycare, owned by a group led by cult leader <a href="/wiki/Twelve_Tribes_communities" title="Twelve Tribes communities">Yoneq Gene Spriggs</a>.  Estee Lauder says it immediately moved to terminate the contract with the manufacturer it had been in business with for 5 years, claiming it was totally unaware prior to the initial inspection.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Animal_testing">Animal testing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Est%C3%A9e_Lauder_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Animal testing" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The Estée Lauder Companies performs non-animal and human volunteer testing to assess product safety and efficacy. The Estée Lauder Companies does perform <a href="/wiki/Animal_testing" title="Animal testing">animal testing</a> where required by law. The Chinese government <a href="/wiki/Animal_testing_regulations" title="Animal testing regulations">requires</a> testing on animals for many cosmetic products.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> This causes controversy for smaller brands that are "cruelty free" but were bought out by Estée Lauder. Estée Lauder becomes the parent company of the smaller brand and profits through the smaller brand.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Boycott_and_anti-boycott">Boycott and anti-boycott</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Est%C3%A9e_Lauder_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Boycott and anti-boycott" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:ES_val_banner.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/67/ES_val_banner.jpg/150px-ES_val_banner.jpg" decoding="async" width="150" height="100" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="387" data-file-height="258"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 150px;height: 100px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/67/ES_val_banner.jpg/150px-ES_val_banner.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="150" data-height="100" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:ES_val_banner.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>QUIT activists protesting Estée Lauder Companies, 2004.</div></div></div>
<p>Since at least February 2001, Estée Lauder and its brands have been the target of a <a href="/wiki/Boycott" title="Boycott">boycott</a> campaign led by pro-<a href="/wiki/Palestinian_nationalism" title="Palestinian nationalism">Palestine activists</a> who have targeted the corporation because of the <a href="/wiki/Israel_lobby_in_the_United_States" title="Israel lobby in the United States">pro-Israel</a> activities of <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Lauder" title="Ronald Lauder">Ronald Lauder</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> In June 2003, the San Francisco-based <a href="/wiki/Queers_Undermining_Israeli_Terrorism" title="Queers Undermining Israeli Terrorism">Queers Undermining Israeli Terrorism</a> (QUIT) took up the boycott with their "Estée Slaughter" campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The boycott has generated an <a href="/wiki/Anti-boycott" title="Anti-boycott">anti-boycott</a> campaign by supporters of Israel.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
