</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=TransDigm_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<table class="box-Expand_section plainlinks metadata ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs expansion</b>. <small>You can help by <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=TransDigm_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=">adding to it</a>.</small>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">March 2011</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table><h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Beginnings">Beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=TransDigm_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Beginnings" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Transdigm Incorporated was established in 1993 by W. Nicholas Howley and Douglas W. Peacock<sup id="cite_ref-odysseyinvestment1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odysseyinvestment1-4">[4]</a></sup> after <a href="/wiki/Mergers_and_acquisitions" title="Mergers and acquisitions">acquiring</a> Adel Fasteners, Aero Products Component Services, Controlex Corporation and Wiggins Connectors from IMO Industries.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>Four years later, in 1997, the company purchased Marathon Power Technologies.
<sup id="cite_ref-prnewswire1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prnewswire1-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2019, TransDigm Group was announced the 3rd best <a href="/wiki/Stock" title="Stock">stock</a> of the 2010s, with a total return of 2,015%.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisition_of_Transdigm_Incorporated_by_Odyssey_Investment_Partners">Acquisition of Transdigm Incorporated by Odyssey Investment Partners</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=TransDigm_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Acquisition of Transdigm Incorporated by Odyssey Investment Partners" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The next year, Transdigm Incorporated was acquired by Odyssey Investment Partners.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>Then, during the final two years of the century, the company added to its list of acquisitions with Adams Rite Aerospace and Christie Electric.<sup id="cite_ref-prnewswire1_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prnewswire1-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2001, Transdigm Incorporated procured Champion Aviation Products, a supplier of <a href="/wiki/Igniter" class="mw-redirect" title="Igniter">igniters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spark_plugs" class="mw-redirect" title="Spark plugs">spark plugs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oil_filters" class="mw-redirect" title="Oil filters">oil filters</a> from <a href="/wiki/Federal-Mogul_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Federal-Mogul Corporation">Federal-Mogul Corporation</a> for approximately $160 million.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>  It was at this time that Nicholas Howley took over the position of Chief Executive Officer from Douglas Peacock as Peacock transferred into his role as Chairman of the Board.<sup id="cite_ref-odysseyinvestment1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odysseyinvestment1-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2019, Transdigm Inc. purchased Esterline Technologies in a $4 Billion acquisition. 
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation_of_Transdigm_Group_Incorporated">Formation of Transdigm Group Incorporated</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=TransDigm_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Formation of Transdigm Group Incorporated" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>

materialized shortly after TransTechnology Corporation sold Norco, Inc. to Marathon Power Technologies.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>Then, in 2004, Transdigm Group procured Avionic Instruments of <a href="/wiki/Avenel,_New_Jersey" title="Avenel, New Jersey">Avenel, New Jersey</a>, a supplier of specialized <a href="/wiki/Power_conversion" class="mw-redirect" title="Power conversion">power conversion</a> devices for aerospace products.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>The following division of <a href="/wiki/Esterline" title="Esterline">Esterline Technologies</a>, who manufactures flight control valves and pressure valves.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a of fluorescent aircraft lighting, in August 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a <h3 class="in-block"><span id="Inaccurate_filings_with_Department_of_Defense_and_.22hidden_monopoly.22"></span><span class="mw-headline" id='Inaccurate_filings_with_Department_of_Defense_and_"hidden_monopoly"'>Inaccurate filings with Department of Defense and "hidden monopoly"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=TransDigm_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title='Edit section: Inaccurate filings with Department of Defense and "hidden monopoly"' data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On January 20, 2017, <a href="/wiki/Citron_Research" class="mw-redirect" title="Citron Research">Citron Research</a> (CR) published a report on TransDigm highlighting its debt-financed acquisitions followed by deep cost cuts and "price gouging".<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> CR published another report on March 9, 2017, accusing TransDigm of illegal activity, noting that twelve of its subsidiaries failed to report their common ownership on federal forms under penalty of perjury. CR pointed out that this omission could be used to defeat federal procurement cost controls.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>On March 20, 2017, U.S. Representative <a href="/wiki/Ro_Khanna" title="Ro Khanna">Ro Khanna</a> sent a letter to the <a href="/wiki/Office_of_the_Inspector_General,_U.S._Department_of_Defense" title="Office of the Inspector General, U.S. Department of Defense">Department of Defense Inspector General</a> describing reports of "waste, fraud, and abuse" by TransDigm Group and calling for an investigation.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> He wrote that TransDigm was able to operate as a "hidden monopoly" because its subsidiaries failed to report their common corporate ownership on filings with the Department of Defense (DoD). This omission allowed TransDigm to avoid the requirement that monopoly suppliers to DoD report their costs. The letter also cited several large price increases that occurred after the suppliers had been acquired by TransDigm. For example, a cable assembly increased in price from $1737 to $7863. Rep. Khanna's letter asked the DoD Inspector General to investigate (1) TransDigm price increases borne by DoD and whether performance improvements were behind the increases, (2) how procurement officers would have treated TransDigm differently had their filings been accurate, (3) how to lower short-term costs to DoD of procurement from TransDigm subsidiaries.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Price-gouging_accusations">Price-gouging accusations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=TransDigm_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Price-gouging accusations" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3><p>
From 2013-2015, TransDigm charged the Pentagon a markup of 9,400% on a half-inch metal pin.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In February 2019, a DoD Inspector General review of 113 contracts showed that, of 112 contracts, "only one part purchased under one contract was awarded with a reasonable profit of 11 percent. The remaining 112 contracts had profit percentages ranging from 17 to 4,451 percent for 46 parts."<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Subsequently, in May 2019, Congressional charges of price-gouging were levied against the company, and hearings followed. TransDigm was accused of spare parts markups during 2015-2017 as high as 4,451%.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Without admitting any wrongdoing, the company agreed to reimburse the government some $16M.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Acme_Aerospace_Photo.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Acme_Aerospace_Photo.JPG/220px-Acme_Aerospace_Photo.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1920"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Acme_Aerospace_Photo.JPG/220px-Acme_Aerospace_Photo.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Acme_Aerospace_Photo.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Acme Aerospace</div></div></div>
