<p>ServiceNow was founded as Glidesoft, Inc. in 2003 by Fred Luddy (the previous <a href="/wiki/Chief_Technology_Officer" class="mw-redirect" title="Chief Technology Officer">CTO</a> of software companies <a href="/wiki/Peregrine_Systems" title="Peregrine Systems">Peregrine Systems</a> and <a href="/wiki/Remedy_Corporation" title="Remedy Corporation">Remedy Corporation</a>), and later incorporated in California in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4">[4]</a></sup> Luddy had previously served as <a href="/wiki/Chief_technology_officer" title="Chief technology officer">chief technology officer</a> for <a href="/wiki/Peregrine_Systems" title="Peregrine Systems">Peregrine Systems</a>, an enterprise software company based in San Diego, until 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5">[5]</a></sup> In founding the company, Luddy intended to provide the same services previously available from the then defunct Peregrine Systems.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>Until mid-2005, Luddy was the only employee and concentrated on developing the software which the company would offer.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5">[5]</a></sup>
Then came the first hires, five people, and raising <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$2.5 million</span> in <a href="/wiki/Venture_capital_financing" title="Venture capital financing">venture financing</a> from <a href="/wiki/JMI_Equity" title="JMI Equity">JMI Equity</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2006, the company changed its name to ServiceNow.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2007, ServiceNow reported an annual revenue of <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$13 million</span> and opened their first <a href="/wiki/Silicon_Valley" title="Silicon Valley">Silicon Valley</a> office, in <a href="/wiki/San_Jose,_California" title="San Jose, California">San Jose</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-7">[7]</a></sup> 2007 was also the first year that the company "went cash flow positive".<sup id="cite_ref-:2_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>As of January 2011<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=ServiceNow&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the company had 275 employees in its San Diego, Chicago, New York, Atlanta, London and Frankfurt offices, as well as a partnership with <a href="/wiki/Accenture" title="Accenture">Accenture</a> who had more than 100 ServiceNow consultants.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5">[5]</a></sup> At this time, the company was known as "Service-now".<sup id="cite_ref-:2_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5">[5]</a></sup> By April 2011, the company had named Frank Slootman as <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">chief executive officer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2012, ServiceNow became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">publicly traded company</a> following a <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$210 million</span> <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">IPO</a>. Shortly thereafter, the company relocated its headquarters from <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a> to <a href="/wiki/Santa_Clara,_California" title="Santa Clara, California">Santa Clara, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4">[4]</a></sup> It was taken public by <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Stanley" title="Morgan Stanley">Morgan Stanley</a> one month after they took <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> public.<sup id="cite_ref-Spears_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spears-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2013, ServiceNow acquired Mirror42, an Amsterdam-based software developer.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2014, ServiceNow acquired Neebula Systems.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2015, ServiceNow acquired Intréis.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2016, ServiceNow acquired the cloud management software startup ITapp.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2016, ServiceNow acquired Brightport Security.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2017, ServiceNow acquired machine learning startup DxContinuum.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2017, the company acquired Qlue to enhance experiences with artificial intelligence to service desk conversations.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2017, the company acquired the San Diego <a href="/wiki/Human-centered_design" title="Human-centered design">human-centered design</a> firm Telepathy, which had been founded in 2001; the acquisition doubled the size of ServiceNow's internal design agency, the Design Experience Organization.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2017, ServiceNow acquired SkyGiraffe,<sup id="cite_ref-:4_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-16">[16]</a></sup> an enterprise mobility platform, that consequently formed the basis for ServiceNow's mobile platform offering released in March 2019. SkyGiraffe was founded by Boaz Hecht and Itay Braun in 2012, headquartered in California with R&amp;D in Israel.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
In April 2018 ServiceNow acquired VendorHawk, a Software-as-Service-Management Company.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2018, ServiceNow acquired Parlo, an artificial intelligence / natural language understanding workforce solution.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2018, ServiceNow acquired FriendlyData, a company specializing in a form of <a href="/wiki/Natural_language_processing" title="Natural language processing">natural language processing</a> that enables queries to be asked in plain language.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In 2019, ServiceNow acquired Appsee Ltd., an analytics startup, that helped developers understand how users interact with their mobile apps.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2019, the company announced that CEO <a href="/wiki/John_Donahoe" title="John Donahoe">John Donahoe</a> would be succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Bill_McDermott" title="Bill McDermott">Bill McDermott</a>, formerly CEO of <a href="/wiki/SAP_SE" title="SAP SE">SAP SE</a> at the end of the calendar year.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> It also acquired the cognitive search platform and R&amp;D team of Attivio, an enterprise search and business insights company.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2019, ServiceNow acquired Fairchild Resiliency Systems, a 13 person company specializing in business continuity and disaster recovery. Previous to the acquisition, Fairchild Resiliency Systems was owner of the MaestroRS app in the ServiceNow app store.
</p><p>In January 2020, ServiceNow announced its acquisition of Loom Systems and Passage AI.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_model">Business model</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=ServiceNow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Business model" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>ServiceNow is a <a href="/wiki/Software_as_a_service" title="Software as a service">software-as-a-service</a> provider, providing technical management support, such as <a href="/wiki/IT_service_management" title="IT service management">IT service management</a>, to the IT operations of large corporations, including providing <a href="/wiki/Help_desk" title="Help desk">help desk</a> functionality. The company's core business revolves around management of "incident, problem, and change" IT operational events. Their fee model was based on a cost per user (seat) per month, with that cost ranging down from <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$100</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_5-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
