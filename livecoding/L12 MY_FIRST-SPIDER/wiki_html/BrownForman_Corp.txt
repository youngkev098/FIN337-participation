			<div id="bodyContent" class="content"><div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="mw-parser-output"><section class="mf-section-0" id="mf-section-0">
<p>The <b>Brown–Forman Corporation</b> is one of the largest <a href="/wiki/Americans" title="Americans">American</a>-owned companies in the <a href="/wiki/Distilled_beverage" class="mw-redirect" title="Distilled beverage">spirits</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wine" title="Wine">wine</a> business. Based in <a href="/wiki/Louisville,_Kentucky" title="Louisville, Kentucky">Louisville, Kentucky</a>, it manufactures several well known brands throughout the world, including <a href="/wiki/Jack_Daniel%27s" title="Jack Daniel's">Jack Daniel's</a>, <a href="/wiki/Early_Times" title="Early Times">Early Times</a>, <a href="/wiki/Old_Forester" title="Old Forester">Old Forester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Woodford_Reserve" title="Woodford Reserve">Woodford Reserve</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Mist" title="Canadian Mist">Canadian Mist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glendronach_distillery" title="Glendronach distillery">GlenDronach</a>, <a href="/wiki/BenRiach_distillery" title="BenRiach distillery">BenRiach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glenglassaugh_distillery" title="Glenglassaugh distillery">Glenglassaugh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finlandia_(vodka)" title="Finlandia (vodka)">Finlandia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tequila_Herradura" title="Tequila Herradura">Herradura</a>, <a href="/wiki/Korbel_Champagne_Cellars" title="Korbel Champagne Cellars">Korbel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Chambord_(liqueur)" title="Chambord (liqueur)">Chambord</a>. Brown–Forman formerly owned <a href="/wiki/Southern_Comfort" title="Southern Comfort">Southern Comfort</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tuaca" title="Tuaca">Tuaca</a> before selling them off in 2016.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brown%E2%80%93Forman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<table class="box-Expand_section plainlinks metadata ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs expansion</b> with: history between 1870 and 2005. <small>You can help by <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Brown%E2%80%93Forman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=">adding to it</a>.</small>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">March 2015</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table><p>The company was founded in 1870 by George Garvin Brown, a young pharmaceuticals salesman in Louisville, who had the then-novel idea of selling top-grade whiskey in sealed glass bottles.<sup id="cite_ref-Martin2014_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Martin2014-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In 2005, the company sold its <a href="/wiki/Lenox_(company)" title="Lenox (company)">Lenox</a> division (one of the oldest and most famous manufacturers of fine china in the United States), which had been acquired in 1983, to <a href="/wiki/Department_56" title="Department 56">Department 56</a> for $USD 160 million. The income generated by the sale was distributed to the shareholders in the form of a one time special dividend.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Brown-Forman_warehouse_A_and_bottling_house.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Brown-Forman_warehouse_A_and_bottling_house.jpg/220px-Brown-Forman_warehouse_A_and_bottling_house.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="6016" data-file-height="4000"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 146px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Brown-Forman_warehouse_A_and_bottling_house.jpg/220px-Brown-Forman_warehouse_A_and_bottling_house.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="146" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Brown-Forman_warehouse_A_and_bottling_house.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Part of the company's Louisville complex</div></div></div>
<p>In 2006, the company acquired the Chambord liqueur brand (a super-premium black raspberry liqueur produced in France) for $US 255 million.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 2007, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Tequila_Herradura" title="Tequila Herradura">Tequila Herradura</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexican</a> company that produces the Casa Herradura tequila brand (a super-premium tequila produced in Mexico) for $US 776 million,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> while it also sold its Hartman Luggage division (one of the leaders in the travel goods industry and originally a subdivision of Lenox), to Clarion Capitol Partners. One year later, it sold the Bolla and Fontana Candida Italian wine brands to Gruppo Italiano Vini (GIV).  The terms of neither sale were disclosed.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In 2011, the company sold <a href="/wiki/Fetzer_Vineyards" class="mw-redirect" title="Fetzer Vineyards">Fetzer Vineyards</a> and associated brands to the <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chilean</a> wine producer Viña Concha y Toro S.A. for $US 238 million.<sup id="cite_ref-concha-y-toro_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-concha-y-toro-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 2016, the Southern Comfort and Tuaca brands were sold to <a href="/wiki/Sazerac_Company" title="Sazerac Company">Sazerac Company</a> for $543 Million.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 2016, Brown–Forman also reached an agreement to purchase The BenRiach Distillery Company Limited for approximately £285 million. The purchase brought <a href="/wiki/Glendronach_distillery" title="Glendronach distillery">GlenDronach</a>, <a href="/wiki/BenRiach_distillery" title="BenRiach distillery">BenRiach</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Glenglassaugh_distillery" title="Glenglassaugh distillery">Glenglassaugh</a> to Brown–Forman's portfolio.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>The company is a sponsor of the Brown–Forman Retailer of the Year awards given by the American Beverage Licensees.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Stock_Information">Stock Information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brown%E2%80%93Forman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Stock Information" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Brown–Forman has two classes of <a href="/wiki/Common_stock" title="Common stock">common stock</a>, both of which are traded publicly on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Class_A_shares" class="mw-redirect" title="Class A shares">Class A shares</a> carry voting privileges and are thinly traded due to control by the Brown family while the <a href="/wiki/B_share_(NYSE)" title="B share (NYSE)">Class B shares</a> are <a href="/wiki/Non-voting_stock" title="Non-voting stock">non-voting stock</a>.
</p>
