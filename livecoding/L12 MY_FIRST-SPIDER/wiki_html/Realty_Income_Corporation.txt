<p>Realty Income Corporation was founded in 1969 by William E. Clark and Evelyn J. Clark.<sup id="cite_ref-taco_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-taco-3">[3]</a></sup> Its first acquisition was a <a href="/wiki/Taco_Bell" title="Taco Bell">Taco Bell</a> restaurant in early 1970.<sup id="cite_ref-taco_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-taco-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>The company used cash to purchase land needed for stores that required real estate to run, and then leased the property to the stores long term.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In 1994, the company became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>In 1997, William E. Clark, Jr., retired as CEO and was succeeded by Thomas A. Lewis. In 2013, John P. Case succeeded Mr. Lewis as CEO of the company.<sup id="cite_ref-case_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-case-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2009, Clark retired as chairman.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 2013, the company acquired American Realty Capital Trust, founded by <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Schorsch" title="Nicholas Schorsch">Nicholas Schorsch</a>, in a $2.95 billion transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, the company was added to the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500_Index" title="S&amp;P 500 Index">S&amp;P 500 Index</a><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and the S&amp;P High Yield Dividend Aristocrats index.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, Sumit Roy became the company's CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-jn_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jn-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 2019, the company completed a sale-leaseback transaction for 12 properties of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> supermarket chain <a href="/wiki/Sainsbury%27s" title="Sainsbury's">Sainsbury's</a>.  This was the company's first purchase of property outside the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
