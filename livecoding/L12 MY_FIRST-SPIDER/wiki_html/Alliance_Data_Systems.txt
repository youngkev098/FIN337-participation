<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alliance_Data%27s_Plano,TX_Headquarters_Building.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Alliance_Data%27s_Plano%2CTX_Headquarters_Building.jpg/220px-Alliance_Data%27s_Plano%2CTX_Headquarters_Building.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3072" data-file-height="2304"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Alliance_Data%27s_Plano%2CTX_Headquarters_Building.jpg/220px-Alliance_Data%27s_Plano%2CTX_Headquarters_Building.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alliance_Data%27s_Plano,TX_Headquarters_Building.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Alliance Data headquarters in Plano, TX</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:PlanoHQ2010.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f1/PlanoHQ2010.jpg/220px-PlanoHQ2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="232" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="2294" data-file-height="2420"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 232px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f1/PlanoHQ2010.jpg/220px-PlanoHQ2010.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="232" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:PlanoHQ2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Alliance Data headquarters in One Legacy Circle located at 7500 Dallas Parkway in Plano, TX</div></div></div>
<p>Alliance Data was formed from the December 1996 <a href="/wiki/Merger" class="mw-redirect" title="Merger">merger</a> of two entities: <a href="/wiki/J.C._Penney" class="mw-redirect" title="J.C. Penney">J.C. Penney</a>'s credit card processing unit and <a href="/wiki/The_Limited" title="The Limited">The Limited</a>'s credit card bank operation, named World Financial Network National Bank.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p><p>In August 1998, Alliance Data acquired <a href="/wiki/LoyaltyOne" title="LoyaltyOne">LoyaltyOne</a>, then-branded The Loyalty Group Canada, for $250 million.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>  The deal gave Alliance Data two valuable business enhancements, Canada's Air Miles Rewards Program and the company behind the popular Air Miles program, LoyaltyOne.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2007, <a href="/wiki/The_Blackstone_Group" title="The Blackstone Group">The Blackstone Group</a>, a private equity firm, announced plans to acquire Alliance Data.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In April 2008, Alliance Data terminated the merger agreement with Blackstone Affiliates.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>Ed Heffernan was promoted to president and CEO of Alliance Data in February 2009, after being with the company since 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>On June 7, 2019, Ed Heffernan resigned as CEO of Alliance Data and was replaced by Melisa Miller, who previously ran Alliance Data's Card Services division. This change in leadership effectively moved Alliance Data's Headquarters from Plano, TX to Columbus, OH. <sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Businesses">Businesses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alliance_Data&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Businesses" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Alliance_Data_Card_Services">Alliance Data Card Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alliance_Data&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Alliance Data Card Services" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Card Services is a provider of integrated credit and marketing services including private label, co-branded and commercial credit card programs. Alliance Data Card Services manages over 135<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> card programs for retail brands such as <a href="/wiki/Victoria%E2%80%99s_Secret" class="mw-redirect" title="Victoria’s Secret">Victoria’s Secret</a>, <a href="/wiki/J.Crew" title="J.Crew">J.Crew</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Bauer" title="Eddie Bauer">Eddie Bauer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buckle_(clothing_retailer)" title="Buckle (clothing retailer)">Buckle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Home_Shopping_Network" title="Home Shopping Network">HSN</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pottery_Barn" title="Pottery Barn">Pottery Barn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pier_1_Imports" class="mw-redirect" title="Pier 1 Imports">Pier 1 Imports</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pimlott2007_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pimlott2007-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In 2011, Alliance Data, in partnership with NICE Systems, was honored with the Operational Leadership Award for its customer care centers by Ventana Research.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>Alliance Data Card Services maintains two banks to manage the funding and receivables for its clients' credit programs. In September 2012, the banks rebranded their names from WFNNB (World Financial Network [National] Bank) and WFCB (World Financial Capital Bank) to both operate under the Comenity name, as <b>Comenity Bank</b> and <b>Comenity Capital Bank</b>, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="LoyaltyOne">LoyaltyOne</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alliance_Data&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: LoyaltyOne" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/LoyaltyOne" title="LoyaltyOne">LoyaltyOne</a>, located in Canada, provides loyalty marketing programs for North American brands in the retail, financial services, grocery, petroleum, travel, and hospitality industries.
</p><p>Five businesses make up the LoyaltyOne: <a href="/wiki/Air_Miles" title="Air Miles">Air Miles</a> national reward program, in which approximately two-thirds of Canadian households participate;<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Colloquy_(company)" class="mw-redirect" title="Colloquy (company)">Colloquy</a>; LoyaltyOne Consulting; Precima; and Squareknot.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Hewitt_Associates" class="mw-redirect" title="Hewitt Associates">Hewitt Associates</a> named LoyaltyOne in their "2014 List of the 50 Best Employers in Canada."<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Epsilon">Epsilon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alliance_Data&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Epsilon" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Epsilon provides a broad range of loyalty marketing services spanning database marketing, direct mail, <a href="/wiki/Email_marketing" title="Email marketing">email marketing</a>, web development, loyalty programs, analytics, data services, strategic consulting and creative services, among others.
</p><p>Ad Age has ranked Epsilon among the top marketing services firms and direct marketing agencies for several years, and Epsilon has the distinction as being the Top US Agency From All Disciplines and Top US CRM/Direct Marketing Agencies for 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> It sends billions of permission-based emails every year.<sup id="cite_ref-Reuters_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-23">[23]</a></sup> Acquired by Epsilon in 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Aspen Marketing Services is a marketing services agency headquartered in Chicago, Illinois<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> with locations in the United States and Canada. In 2012, Epsilon reached an agreement to acquire the Hyper Marketing (“HMI”) group of companies, the largest privately held digital marketing services agencies in the United States, for about $460 million.
</p><p>In March 2011, Epsilon detected unauthorized access to its email database, resulting in the theft of email addresses of its clients' customers.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>In late February 2013, Facebook announced partnerships with four companies, including Epsilon, to help reshape its targeted advertising strategy.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>Because of Epsilon’s growth over the past several years, the company relocated its headquarters to the former Nokia regional headquarters space in Las Colinas, Texas, near Dallas.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2014, <a href="/wiki/60_Minutes" title="60 Minutes">60 Minutes</a> interviewed Bryan Kennedy, chairman and CEO of Epsilon, as part of its segment, <i>The Data Brokers: Selling your personal information</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-60_Minutes:_The_Data_Brokers_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60_Minutes:_The_Data_Brokers-32">[32]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2019, Publicis Groupe acquired Epsilon for $4.4 billion. This was the second biggest deal in the history of advertising, behind only Dentsu’s $5 billion acquisition of Aegis Group in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
