<p><b>Zoetis Inc.</b> (/zō-EH-tis/<sup id="cite_ref-CBSIPO_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBSIPO-4">[4]</a></sup>) is the world's largest producer of <a href="/wiki/Medicine" title="Medicine">medicine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vaccinations" class="mw-redirect" title="Vaccinations">vaccinations</a> for <a href="/wiki/Pets" class="mw-redirect" title="Pets">pets</a> and <a href="/wiki/Livestock" title="Livestock">livestock</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Zoetis-Inc-Feb-2018-10-K_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zoetis-Inc-Feb-2018-10-K-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CBSIPO_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBSIPO-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The company was a <a href="/wiki/Subsidiary" title="Subsidiary">subsidiary</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pfizer" title="Pfizer">Pfizer</a>, the world's largest <a href="/wiki/Drug_maker" class="mw-redirect" title="Drug maker">drug maker</a>, but with Pfizer's spinoff of its 83% interest in the firm it is now a completely independent company. The company directly markets the products in approximately 45 countries, and sells the products in more than 100 countries. </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zoetis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="1950s_to_2000s">1950s to 2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zoetis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1950s to 2000s" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:ZoetisBuilding.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/ZoetisBuilding.jpg/250px-ZoetisBuilding.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="188" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 250px;height: 188px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/ZoetisBuilding.jpg/250px-ZoetisBuilding.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="250" data-height="188" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:ZoetisBuilding.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Zoetis building in Exton, PA</div></div></div>
<p>In the 1950s, Pfizer began research on several drugs,  including <a href="/wiki/Oxytetracycline" title="Oxytetracycline">oxytetracycline</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SEC_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEC-8">[8]</a></sup> John McKeen, a <a href="/wiki/Chemical_engineer" title="Chemical engineer">chemical engineer</a>, discovered its effective use in livestock. In 1952, the Pfizer Agriculture Division opened a <a href="/wiki/Research_and_development" title="Research and development">research and development</a> facility in <a href="/wiki/Terre_Haute,_Indiana" title="Terre Haute, Indiana">Terre Haute, Indiana</a>, called <b>Vigo</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-SEC_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEC-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> By 1988 the division was renamed <b>Pfizer Animal Health</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-SEC_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEC-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>The acquisition of Norden Laboratories from <a href="/wiki/GlaxoSmithKline" title="GlaxoSmithKline">GlaxoSmithKline</a> in 1995 expanded Pfizer's animal health division into small animal care, including <a href="/wiki/House_pet" class="mw-redirect" title="House pet">domestic pets</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SEC_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEC-8">[8]</a></sup> Secondary research and development centres were opened in <a href="/wiki/Kalamazoo,_Michigan" title="Kalamazoo, Michigan">Kalamazoo, Michigan</a>, in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-SEC_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEC-8">[8]</a></sup> In the same year, Pfizer acquired <a href="/wiki/Pharmacia_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Pharmacia Corporation">Pharmacia Corporation</a> for sixty billion dollars in <a href="/wiki/Stock_option" class="mw-redirect" title="Stock option">stock options</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Between 2007 and 2011, the company acquired Embrex Inc, Catapult Genetics, Bovigen, <a href="/wiki/Wyeth" title="Wyeth">Wyeth</a>, Fort Dodge Animal Health, Vetnex Animal Health Ltd, Synbiotics Corporation, Microtek, <a href="/wiki/King_Pharmaceuticals" title="King Pharmaceuticals">King Pharmaceuticals</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Alpharma" class="mw-redirect" title="Alpharma">Alpharma</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SEC_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEC-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s_to_present">2010s to present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zoetis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2010s to present" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Plans to <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spin off</a> Pfizer Animal Health into a separate company were announced in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-Zoetis-Inc-Oct-2012-S-1/A_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zoetis-Inc-Oct-2012-S-1/A-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CBSIPO_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBSIPO-4">[4]</a></sup> The name <i>Zoetis</i> was derived from the <a href="/wiki/Zoology" title="Zoology">zoological</a> term <i>zoetic</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-CBSIPO_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBSIPO-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>Zoetis Inc.'s revenues exceeded $4.2 billion in 2011 and $4.34 billion in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-Zoetis-Inc-Mar-2013-10-K_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zoetis-Inc-Mar-2013-10-K-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-WSJ2_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ2-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CBSIPO_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBSIPO-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>On 22 May 2013, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a></i> reported that Pfizer planned to sell its majority stake in the company,<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ2_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ2-13">[13]</a></sup> in keeping with Pfizer's decision to shed other non-pharmaceuticals subsidiaries to save costs, raise capital, and pay off debt. The company announced that <a href="/wiki/JPMorgan_Chase" title="JPMorgan Chase">JPMorgan Chase</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_America" title="Bank of America">Bank of America</a> <a href="/wiki/Merrill_Lynch" class="mw-redirect" title="Merrill Lynch">Merrill Lynch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goldman_Sachs" title="Goldman Sachs">Goldman Sachs &amp; Co.</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Stanley" title="Morgan Stanley">Morgan Stanley</a> as lead <a href="/wiki/Underwriter" class="mw-redirect" title="Underwriter">underwriters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Zoetis-Inc-May-2013-425_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zoetis-Inc-May-2013-425-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-WSJ2_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ2-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2014, activist investor <a href="/wiki/Bill_Ackman" title="Bill Ackman">Bill Ackman</a> disclosed that <a href="/wiki/Pershing_Square_Capital_Management" title="Pershing Square Capital Management">Pershing Square Capital Management</a> had taken an 8.5% stake in the company, amassing approximately 41.8 million shares, causing the share price to hit its highest price since the IPO.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On 17 November, the company announced it would acquire a portfolio of pet drugs from <a href="/wiki/Abbott_Laboratories" title="Abbott Laboratories">Abbott Laboratories</a> for approximately $255 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Zoetis-Inc-Nov-2014-8-K_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zoetis-Inc-Nov-2014-8-K-16">[16]</a></sup> On 1 January, 2020, Kristin Peck became CEO of Zoetis.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2012:_IPO">2012: IPO</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zoetis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2012: IPO" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>Pfizer officially filed for registration of a <a href="/wiki/Preferred_stock" title="Preferred stock">Class A stock</a> on 10 August 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-SEC_8-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEC-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Zoetis-Inc-Jan-2013-S-1/A_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zoetis-Inc-Jan-2013-S-1/A-18">[18]</a></sup> Zoetis' <a href="/wiki/IPO" class="mw-redirect" title="IPO">IPO</a> on 1 February 2013 sold 86.1 million shares for <a href="/wiki/US%24" class="mw-redirect" title="US$">US$</a> 2.2 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTIPO_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTIPO-19">[19]</a></sup> Shares sharply rose 19% by the end of the trading day to $35.01 a share, up from $26.<sup id="cite_ref-CBSIPO_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBSIPO-4">[4]</a></sup> At the time, it was the largest IPO from a U.S. company since <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a>'s  <a href="/wiki/Facebook_IPO" class="mw-redirect" title="Facebook IPO">$16 billion IPO</a> on 18 May 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-Facebook-May-2012-424B4_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Facebook-May-2012-424B4-20">[20]</a></sup> Pfizer retained 414 million Class B shares giving it an 83% controlling stake in the firm.<sup id="cite_ref-Zoetis-Jan-2013-S-1/A_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zoetis-Jan-2013-S-1/A-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-WSJIPO_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJIPO-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Stock_trader" title="Stock trader">Stock investors</a> were attracted to the steep <a href="/wiki/Profit_margin" title="Profit margin">profit margin</a> in proportion to revenue and <a href="/wiki/Consumer_confidence" title="Consumer confidence">consumer confidence</a> in potential future growth of the subsidiary.<sup id="cite_ref-CBSIPO_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBSIPO-4">[4]</a></sup> The offering's lead <a href="/wiki/Underwriters" class="mw-redirect" title="Underwriters">underwriters</a> were <a href="/wiki/JPMorgan_Chase" title="JPMorgan Chase">JPMorgan Chase</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_America_Merrill_Lynch" class="mw-redirect" title="Bank of America Merrill Lynch">Bank of America Merrill Lynch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Stanley" title="Morgan Stanley">Morgan Stanley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTIPO_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTIPO-19">[19]</a></sup> Most of the money raised through the IPO was used to pay off existing Pfizer debt.<sup id="cite_ref-CBSIPO_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBSIPO-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zoetis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Acquisitions" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In November 2015, the company announced it would acquire developer of <a href="/wiki/Aquaculture" title="Aquaculture">aquaculture</a> treatments, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pharmaq&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pharmaq (page does not exist)">Pharmaq</a>, for $765 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Zoetis-Nov-2015-8-K_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zoetis-Nov-2015-8-K-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In 2017, Zoetis acquired Ireland-based Nexvet, a company with a <a href="/wiki/Biologics" class="mw-redirect" title="Biologics">biologics</a>-focused technology and product candidate pipeline.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2018, the company announced its intention to acquire <a href="/wiki/Abaxis" title="Abaxis">Abaxis</a> for $1.9 billion in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2020, Zoetis acquired Performance Livestock Analytics. Terms of the deal weren't disclosed.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Products">Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zoetis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Products" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Twenty-eight sites in 11 countries make up Zoetis manufacturing network, each facility designed to meet chemical and infectious agent safety regulatory requirements. Many R&amp;D operations are co-located with manufacturing sites, a collaboration that allows bringing new products to market faster. Zoetis builds on six-decade Pfizer history and aims for high tech innovative manufacturing technologies.
