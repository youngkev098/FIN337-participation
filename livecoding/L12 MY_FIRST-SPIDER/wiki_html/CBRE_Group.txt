</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CBRE_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Services" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>CBRE provides services to both occupiers of and investors in real estate:
</p>
<ul><li>For occupiers, CBRE provide facilities management, project management, transaction (both property sales and leasing) and consulting services, among others.</li>
<li>For investors, CBRE provide capital markets (property sales, commercial mortgage brokerage, loan origination and servicing), property leasing, investment management, property management, valuation and development services, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-AnnualReport2018c_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AnnualReport2018c-9">[9]</a></sup></li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CBRE_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Tucker, Lynch &amp; Coldwell, the earliest predecessor to CBRE, was established on August 27, 1906, following the <a href="/wiki/1906_San_Francisco_earthquake" title="1906 San Francisco earthquake">1906 San Francisco earthquake</a>. The company added Benjamin Arthur Banker as partner in 1913 and renamed itself Coldwell, Cornwall &amp; Banker in 1920. Cornwall resigned as partner in 1940, and the company was renamed Coldwell, Banker &amp; Co, which was eventually shortened to <a href="/wiki/Coldwell_Banker" title="Coldwell Banker">Coldwell Banker</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 1981, Coldwell Banker was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Sears" title="Sears">Sears</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 1989, Sears sold Coldwell Banker's commercial unit to a management-led buyout group including <a href="/wiki/The_Carlyle_Group" title="The Carlyle Group">The Carlyle Group</a> for approximately $300 million.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-15">[15]</a></sup> After the buyout, the company was renamed CB Commercial Real Estate Group.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-16">[16]</a></sup> The residential group retained the Coldwell Banker name.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In 1996, the company became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>, raising approximately $80 million.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In 1997, the company acquired Koll Real Estate Services for $145 million.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In 1998, CB Commercial merged with Richard Ellis International (REI) Limited and changed its name to CB Richard Ellis.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In 2001, CB Richard Ellis was <a href="/wiki/Leveraged_buyout" title="Leveraged buyout">taken private</a> by an investment group led by <a href="/wiki/Blum_Capital" title="Blum Capital">Blum Capital</a> in an $800 million transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In 2003, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Insignia_Financial_Group" title="Insignia Financial Group">Insignia Financial Group</a> for $415 million.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>On June 10, 2004, CB Richard Ellis once again became a public company via an initial public offering.<sup id="cite_ref-ipo_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ipo-24">[24]</a></sup> In 2006, the company's shares were added to the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500_Index" title="S&amp;P 500 Index">S&amp;P 500 Index</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2006, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Trammell_Crow_Company" title="Trammell Crow Company">Trammell Crow Company</a> for $2.2 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>In 2011, the company acquired the real estate investment business of <a href="/wiki/ING_Group" title="ING Group">ING Group</a> for $940 million.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>In 2011, the company changed its name to CBRE Group Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>In 2013 the company acquired Norland Managed Services, a facilities, energy and project management provider in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015 the company acquired Global Workplace Solutions from <a href="/wiki/Johnson_Controls" title="Johnson Controls">Johnson Controls</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/FacilitySource" title="FacilitySource">FacilitySource</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p><p>In 2019, the group acquired the London developer <a href="/wiki/Telford_Homes" title="Telford Homes">Telford Homes</a>.
</p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Rigged_bidding">Rigged bidding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CBRE_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Rigged bidding" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>According to a lawsuit back in 2012, CBRE violated Federal Deposit Insurance Corp (FDIC) rules by letting Florida's Stonegate Bank pay less for a branch than a consultant named Kenneth H. Thomas had bid for it.<sup id="cite_ref-RiggedBidding_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RiggedBidding-33">[33]</a></sup> However, it is unclear if the suit was followed through.
</p>
