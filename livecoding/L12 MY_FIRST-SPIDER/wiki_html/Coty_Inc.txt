</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_overview">Corporate overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coty,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Corporate overview" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Coty is one of the world's largest beauty companies and the largest fragrance company,<sup id="cite_ref-Utroske_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Utroske-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> with over $9 billion in revenue for the fiscal year ending in June 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-SEC_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEC-1">[1]</a></sup> Coty acquired 41 beauty brands from <a href="/wiki/Procter_%26_Gamble" title="Procter &amp; Gamble">Procter &amp; Gamble</a> in 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-Ruckin_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ruckin-5">[5]</a></sup> becoming the global leader in fragrance, the second largest company for <a href="/wiki/Hair_coloring" title="Hair coloring">hair color</a> and styling products,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and the third largest company for color <a href="/wiki/Cosmetics" title="Cosmetics">cosmetics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Wong_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wong-7">[7]</a></sup> The company operates three divisions: Consumer Beauty, which focuses on body care, color cosmetics, <a href="/wiki/Aroma_compound" title="Aroma compound">fragrances</a>, and hair coloring and styling products; Luxury, for <a href="/wiki/Luxury_goods" title="Luxury goods">luxury</a> cosmetic, fragrance, and <a href="/wiki/Skin_care" title="Skin care">skin care</a> products; and Professional Beauty, which services <a href="/wiki/Beauty_salon" title="Beauty salon">beauty salon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nail_salon" title="Nail salon">nail salon</a> professionals.<sup id="cite_ref-CNNMoney_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNNMoney-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Progress_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Progress-9">[9]</a></sup> Coty's mission is to "celebrate and liberate the diversity of beauty".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>The company has approximately 20,000 full-time employees in 46 countries, as of mid 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-SEC_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEC-1">[1]</a></sup> Coty's executive offices are located in London.<sup id="cite_ref-Kilgore_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kilgore-11">[11]</a></sup> The Consumer Beauty, Luxury and Professional Beauty divisions are headquartered in New York City, Paris and Geneva, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-SEC_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEC-1">[1]</a></sup> Peter Harf is Coty's <a href="/wiki/Chairman" class="mw-redirect" title="Chairman">chairman</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Replaces_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Replaces-12">[12]</a></sup> Pierre Laubies is <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">CEO</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Replaces_12-1" class="reference"><a </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coty,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="1900s.E2.80.931920s"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1900s–1920s">1900s–1920s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coty,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1900s–1920s" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Coty_Taponier.png" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Coty_Taponier.png/170px-Coty_Taponier.png" decoding="async" width="170" height="213" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="490" data-file-height="613"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 170px;height: 213px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Coty_Taponier.png/170px-Coty_Taponier.png" data-alt="" data-width="170" data-height="213" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Coty_Taponier.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Coty" title="François Coty">François Coty</a> in 1926</div></div></div>
<p>Coty was founded by <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Coty" title="François Coty">François Coty</a> in Paris in 1904.<sup id="cite_ref-SEC_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEC-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Wohl_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wohl-35">[35]</a></sup> The brand's first fragrance, La Rose Jacqueminot, was launched the same year and was packaged in a bottle designed by <a href="/wiki/Baccarat_(company)" title="Baccarat (company)">Baccarat</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Critchell_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Critchell-36">[36]</a></sup> L'Origan was launched in 1905; according to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Week" title="The Week">The Week</a></i>, the perfume "started a sweeping trend throughout Paris" and was the first example of "a fine but affordable fragrance that would appeal both to the upper classes and to the less affluent, changing the way scents were sold forever".<sup id="cite_ref-TheWeek_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TheWeek-37">[37]</a></sup> Following its early successes, Coty was able to open its first store in 1908 in Paris' <a href="/wiki/Place_Vend%C3%B4me" title="Place Vendôme">Place Vendôme</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Critchell_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Critchell-36">[36]</a></sup> Soon after, Coty began collaborating with French glass designer <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Lalique" title="René Lalique">René Lalique</a> to create custom fragrance bottles, labels, and other packaging materials, launching a new trend in mass-produced fragrance packaging.<sup id="cite_ref-First100_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-First100-38">[38]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Gaffney_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gaffney-39">[39]</a></sup> Coty also established a "Perfume City" in the suburbs of Paris during the early 1910s to handle administration and fragrance production; the site was an early business supporter of female employees and offered benefits including child care.<sup id="cite_ref-Critchell_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Critchell-36">[36]</a></sup></p><p>The company began its global expansion in the early 1910s, first in London and New York.<sup id="cite_ref-Controlling_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Controlling-40">[40]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Scarred_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Scarred-41">[41]</a></sup> Coty established U.S. headquarters at 714 Fifth Avenue in New York City, and commissioned Lalique to design <a href="/wiki/Pressed_glass" title="Pressed glass">pressed glass</a> panels for the building's façade windows, which were installed in 1912.<sup id="cite_ref-First100_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-First100-38">[38]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Gill_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gill-42">[42]</a></sup> Coty remained headquartered in the building until 1941.<sup id="cite_ref-Gill_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gill-42">[42]</a></sup> The structure was later given landmark status by the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Landmarks_Preservation_Commission" title="New York City Landmarks Preservation Commission">New York City Landmarks Preservation Commission</a> during the 1980s for its custom windows.<sup id="cite_ref-Belle_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Belle-43">[43]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Windows_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Windows-44">[44]</a></sup>
Coty began selling other beauty products including <a href="/wiki/Face_powder" title="Face powder">face</a> and <a href="/wiki/Body_powder" title="Body powder">body powders</a> in the 1910s, and launched one of its most successful fragrances, <a href="/wiki/Chypre" title="Chypre">Chypre</a>, in 1917. The company's products gained more attention in the United States as <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> soldiers started returning from France with gifts for loved ones. During the 1920s, Coty launched more than fifteen new fragrances, and expanded into Germany, Italy, Spain, and Switzerland.<sup id="cite_ref-First100_38-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-First100-38">[38]</a></sup> Coty, Inc. was formed in New York in 1922, and became a publicly traded company in 1925.<sup id="cite_ref-Jones_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jones-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="1930s.E2.80.931990s"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1930s–1990s">1930s–1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coty,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1930s–1990s" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>François Coty died in 1934; his family maintained control of the company and served as board members until the 1960s.<sup id="cite_ref-First100_38-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-First100-38">[38]</a></sup></p><p>Coty's Air Spun face powder was launched in 1935.<sup id="cite_ref-Critchell_36-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Critchell-36">[36]</a></sup> The powder has been described by <i><a href="/wiki/Real_Simple" title="Real Simple">Real Simple</a></i> as one of the "best beauty products of all time" and remains mostly unchanged.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p><p>In the 1940s, Coty became a major supporter of the growing <a href="/wiki/Fashion_in_the_United_States" title="Fashion in the United States">American fashion</a> industry, launching the <a href="/wiki/Coty_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Coty Awards">Coty American Fashion Critics' Awards</a> to recognize and promote emerging American fashion designers. Coty discontinued its participation in 1985.<sup id="cite_ref-Discontinued_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Discontinued-47">[47]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p><p>Coty became a key player in the American lipstick market with the launch of Coty 24 in 1955. By the 1960s, Coty had become a leading fragrance manufacturer and marketer and the largest fragrance company in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-First100_38-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-First100-38">[38]</a></sup> It attracted the attention of <a href="/wiki/Pfizer" title="Pfizer">Pfizer</a>, which acquired the company in 1963.<sup id="cite_ref-Wohl_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wohl-35">[35]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-First100_38-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-First100-38">[38]</a></sup></p><p>In 1991, the company had annual sales of approximately $280 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Bryant_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bryant-49">[49]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-JOC_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JOC-50">[50]</a></sup> Pfizer sold Coty to Joh. A. Benckiser (now known as JAB Holding Company) in 1992. Coty was a strategic fit for Benckiser, which had another beauty subsidiary, as well as an international distribution network through which it could market Coty's products.<sup id="cite_ref-Bryant_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bryant-49">[49]</a></sup> Coty's fragrances at the time included Emeraude, Exclamation, L'Effleur, Preferred Stock, Sand &amp; Sable, Tribe, and Wild Musk.<sup id="cite_ref-Bryant_49-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bryant-49">[49]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LAT_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LAT-51">[51]</a></sup> Peter Harf, chairman and CEO of JAB since 1988, was named Coty's CEO in 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-First100_38-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-First100-38">[38]</a></sup> Coty acquired <a href="/wiki/Unilever" title="Unilever">Unilever</a>'s European cosmetic brands, including Rimmel, in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-First100_38-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-First100-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s">2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coty,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2000s" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>During the mid 2000s, the company focused on marketing celebrity-endorsed fragrances, including David Beckham, <a href="/wiki/Celine_Dion" title="Celine Dion"> Céline Dion</a>, Jennifer Lopez, <a href="/wiki/Mary-Kate_and_Ashley_Olsen" title="Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen">Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Jessica_Parker" title="Sarah Jessica Parker">Sarah Jessica Parker</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Shania_Twain" title="Shania Twain">Shania Twain</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Worthen_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Worthen-52">[52]</a></sup> Coty also expanded its portfolio of luxury fragrances. It purchased the fragrance license for fashion designer Marc Jacobs in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-Timeline_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Timeline-53">[53]</a></sup> The company's revenue increased from $1.9 billion to $2.1 billion during 2004–2005.<sup id="cite_ref-Worthen_52-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Worthen-52">[52]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Boorstin_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boorstin-54">[54]</a></sup></p><p>In 2005, Coty purchased additional licenses for Calvin Klein, <a href="/wiki/Cerruti" class="mw-redirect" title="Cerruti">Cerruti</a>, Chloé, Lagerfeld, and <a href="/wiki/Vera_Wang" title="Vera Wang">Vera Wang</a> from Unilever.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> These newest acquisitions, along with existing portfolio licenses including Adidas, Davidoff, and <a href="/wiki/JOOP!" title="JOOP!">JOOP!</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Unilever_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Unilever-56">[56]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MW_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MW-57">[57]</a></sup> made Coty the largest global fragrance maker.<sup id="cite_ref-Wohl_35-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wohl-35">[35]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Boorstin_54-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boorstin-54">[54]</a></sup></p><p>Coty acquired Del Laboratories' parent company, DLI Holding Corp., in 2007, adding the Sally Hansen and NYC New York Color brands to Coty's portfolio.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> The company entered into license agreements with <a href="/wiki/Balenciaga" title="Balenciaga">Balenciaga</a> in 2008 and <a href="/wiki/Bottega_Veneta" title="Bottega Veneta">Bottega Veneta</a> in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-Balenciaga_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Balenciaga-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s">2010s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coty,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2010s" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, Coty purchased <a href="/wiki/Nail_polish" title="Nail polish">nail polish</a> maker <a href="/wiki/OPI_Products" title="OPI Products">OPI Products</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Chon_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chon-62">[62]</a></sup> as well as the skin care brand <a href="/wiki/Philosophy_(brand)" title="Philosophy (brand)">philosophy</a> from <a href="/wiki/The_Carlyle_Group" title="The Carlyle Group">The Carlyle Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Timeline_53-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Timeline-53">[53]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> The company also entered into a license agreement with <a href="/wiki/Miu_Miu" title="Miu Miu">Miu Miu</a>, a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Prada" title="Prada">Prada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Niven_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Niven-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>Coty filed to go public in June 2012, and raised approximately $1 billion during its <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> (IPO) one year later.<sup id="cite_ref-Pandey_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pandey-64">[64]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Zhu_65-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zhu-65">[65]</a></sup> The IPO, held in 2013, was the third largest in title="CNNMoney">CNNMoney</a> described the offering as the "largest U.S.-listed IPO for a consumer products company".<sup id="cite_ref-Zhu_65-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zhu-65">[65]</a></sup> Coty acquired <a href="/wiki/Bourjois" title="Bourjois">Bourjois</a> in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-Wendlandt_66-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wendlandt-66">[66]</a></sup></p><p>During 2015–2016, Coty acquired 41 beauty brands from Procter &amp; Gamble (collectively known as Galleria),<sup id="cite_ref-Ruckin_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ruckin-5">[5]</a></sup> including Clairol, CoverGirl, Gucci, Hugo Boss, Max Factor, and Wella.<sup id="cite_ref-Coolidge_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coolidge-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Kosman_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kosman-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Daneshkhu_21-1" class="reference"><a global seller of cosmetics.<sup id="cite_ref-Wong_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wong-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Wylie_27-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wylie-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> Coty also acquired the <a href="/wiki/Digital_marketing" title="Digital marketing">digital marketing</a> technology agency <a href="/wiki/Beamly" title="Beamly">Beamly</a> in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-Gears_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gears-68">[68]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Borchardt_69-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Borchardt-69">[69]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Fildes_70-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fildes-70">[70]</a></sup></p><p>The company entered into a license agreement with <a href="/wiki/Tiffany_%26_Co." title="Tiffany &amp; Co.">Tiffany &amp; Co.</a> in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> During 2016–2017, Coty acquired Hypermarcas' (now known as <a href="/wiki/Hypera_Pharma" title="Hypera Pharma">Hypera Pharma</a>) beauty and <a href="/wiki/Personal_care" title="Personal care">personal care</a> business, ghd, and became a majority stakeholder in the peer to peer digital beauty company <a href="/wiki/Younique" title="Younique">Younique</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Jump_72-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jump-72">[72]</a></sup> Younique had approximately 80,000 sellers when Coty purchased a 60 percent stake in January 2017, and surpassed 230,000 sellers by December.<sup id="cite_ref-Schiffer_73-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Schiffer-73">[73]</a></sup> Coty announced that it was cutting ties with Younique in August 2019, saying that Younique was "definitely different" from other businesses owned by Coty, and intended to sell its 60% stake in Younique back to its founders.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ-terminate_74-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ-terminate-74">[74]</a></sup></p><p>Coty acquired licenses to <a href="/wiki/Burberry" title="Burberry">Burberry</a>'s cosmetics and fragrance brands in April 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-Buckley_75-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Buckley-75">[75]</a></sup> In July, Coty added the skincare brand <a href="/wiki/Philosophy_(brand)" title="Philosophy (brand)">philosophy</a> to <a href="/wiki/Tmall" title="Tmall">Tmall</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> and launched other brands on the platform as well.<sup id="cite_ref-Collins_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Collins-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Ying_77-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ying-77">[77]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2017, the <a href="/wiki/Court_of_Justice_of_the_European_Union" title="Court of Justice of the European Union">Court of Justice of the European Union</a>'s <a href="/wiki/European_Court_of_Justice" title="European Court of Justice">European Court of Justice</a> ruled that Coty did not violate <a href="/wiki/Competition_law" title="Competition law">competition laws</a> by forbidding German distributor Parfümerie Akzente from selling products via <a href="/wiki/Amazon_(company)" title="Amazon (company)">Amazon</a>, and that luxury brands are allowed to prohibit distributors from selling through third-party platforms.<sup id="cite_ref-Toplensky_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Toplensky-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Meyer_78-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Meyer-78">[78]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Chee_79-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chee-79">[79]</a></sup> Previously, according to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_Committee_on_the_Judiciary" title="United States House Committee on the Judiciary">United States House Committee on the Judiciary</a>, Coty supported the <a href="/wiki/Stop_Online_Piracy_Act" title="Stop Online Piracy Act">Stop Online Piracy Act</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Bill_(law)" title="Bill (law)">bill</a> introduced to expand the ability of U.S. law enforcement to combat <a href="/wiki/Copyright_infringement" title="Copyright infringement">copyright infringement</a> and <a href="/wiki/Counterfeit_consumer_goods" title="Counterfeit consumer goods">counterfeit goods</a> trafficking, in late 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-Cout_80-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cout-80">[80]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sheets_81-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sheets-81">[81]</a></sup></p><p>Coty developed "Let's Get Ready" for the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Echo_Show" title="Amazon Echo Show">Amazon Echo Show</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Echo" title="Amazon Echo">Amazon Echo</a> line of products and designed around <a intelligence)">virtual assistant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Alexa" title="Amazon Alexa">Alexa</a>. The guide debuted in early 2018 and presents looks and products to users, which can be added to <a href="/wiki/Shopping_cart_software" title="Shopping cart software">shopping carts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Echo_82-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Echo-82">[82]</a></sup> In February, Coty created a <a href="/wiki/Startup_accelerator" title="Startup accelerator">startup accelerator</a> focused on <a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">artificial intelligence</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup></p><p>The company underwent refinancing of debt, including debt associated with Galleria, in March 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-Ruckin_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ruckin-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2019, Coty announced to purchase $600 Million Stake in <a href="/wiki/Kylie_Cosmetics" title="Kylie Cosmetics">Kylie Cosmetics</a>, the company of the world's youngest billionaire <a href="/wiki/Kylie_Jenner" title="Kylie Jenner">Kylie Jenner</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_practices_and_social_causes">Environmental practices and social causes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coty,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Environmental practices and social causes" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>Coty and its brands say they are committed to a range of <a href="/wiki/Social_cause" class="mw-redirect" title="Social cause">social causes</a> as well as seeking to minimize its <a href="/wiki/Environmental_impact" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental impact">environmental impact</a>. The company has entered into a long-term partnership with the international advocacy group <a href="/wiki/Global_Citizen" class="mw-redirect" title="Global Citizen">Global Citizen</a> to tackle prejudice and discrimination based on gender, sexual orientation, disability, or ethnicity, and to promote self-expression.<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup> Coty has also joined other beauty companies to launch the Responsible Beauty Initiative to encourage sustainability within the industry.<sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup> Coty has signed the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Global_Compact" title="United Nations Global Compact">United Nations Global Compact</a>, a <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">UN</a> initiative to encourage businesses to adopt sustainable and <a href="/wiki/Corporate_social_responsibility" title="Corporate social responsibility">socially responsible</a> policies. In 2017 Arya Gupta, Coty’s Procurement Director of Global Chemicals &amp; Innovation, said this  “will amplify members’ efforts to boost sustainability in their supply chains, while ensuring the suppliers of the industry have sound ethical, social, and environmental business practices in place.” He also said that the company became a member of the Roundtable for Sustainable Palm Oil (RSPO). "A very important milestone towards responsible category sourcing.”<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup></p>

<p>Coty ranked number 371 on the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)">Fortune</a></i> magazine's annual list of the largest U.S. corporations by total revenue, in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-Fortune_89-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fortune-89">[89]</a></sup> The company ranked number 5 on <i><a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Wear_Daily" title="Women's Wear Daily">Women's Wear Daily</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'s</span> 2017 "Top 100" list of the world's largest beauty manufacturers, estimating $9.15 billion in sales.<sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup> According to <i><a href="/wiki/Advertising_Age" class="mw-redirect" title="Advertising Age">Advertising Age</a></i>, Coty was one of the largest global advertisers in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-WLA_91-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WLA-91">[91]</a></sup> In 2018, Coty ranked number 1,196 on the <a href="/wiki/Forbes_Global_2000" title="Forbes Global 2000"><i>Forbes</i> Global 2000</a>, an annual ranking of the top 2,000 public companies in the world by <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> magazine.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes_92-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes-92">[92]</a></sup> Additionally, Coty ranked number 396 on <i>Forbes</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span> 2018 list of "America's Largest Public Companies".<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes_92-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes-92">[92]</a></sup></p>
