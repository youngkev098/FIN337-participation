</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CDW&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>CDW was originally incorporated in 1984 as "MPK Computing" by its founder <a href="/wiki/Michael_Krasny_(businessman)" title="Michael Krasny (businessman)">Michael Krasny</a>. The idea was born when Krasny took a small ad in a free-circulation newspaper to sell his computer and printer. It later became Computer Discount Warehouse and then simply CDW.<sup id="cite_ref-2014_10-K_p5_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2014_10-K_p5-2">[2]</a></sup></p><p>In early 2006, CDW opened a 513,000-square-foot (47,700 m<sup>2</sup>) distribution center in <a href="/wiki/North_Las_Vegas" class="mw-redirect" title="North Las Vegas">North Las Vegas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a>.  The <a href="/wiki/Vernon_Hills" class="mw-redirect" title="Vernon Hills">Vernon Hills</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a> distribution center is roughly 450,000 square feet (42,000 m<sup>2</sup>). In October 2006 CDW acquired <a href="/wiki/Berbee" class="mw-redirect" title="Berbee">Berbee</a>, a top tier reseller of <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cisco" class="mw-redirect" title="Cisco">Cisco</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> products and services. It was CDW's second major acquisition after purchasing Micro Warehouse in September 2003.
</p><p>On 12 October 2007, Chicago based private equity firms <a href="/wiki/Madison_Dearborn_Partners" title="Madison Dearborn Partners">Madison Dearborn Partners</a> and <a href="/wiki/Providence_Equity_Partners" title="Providence Equity Partners">Providence Equity Partners</a> completed a $7 billion acquisition of CDW.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It went public again through an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> on the NASDAQ market on July 2, 2013 under the name CDW Corporation (the parent company since 2010).<sup id="cite_ref-2014_10-K_p5_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2014_10-K_p5-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> CDW LLC and CDW Finance Corporation are wholly owned subsidiaries.<sup id="cite_ref-2014_10-K_p65_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2014_10-K_p65-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>CDW operates in Canada as CDW Canada Incorporated, based in Etobicoke, Ontario.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> CDW Canada was recognized by the Great Place to Work Institute as one of the best workplaces in Canada in 2011, ranked 25th in the category of large and multinational companies.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>On 3 August 2015 the company announced that it had acquired the remaining 65 percent of Kelway <a href="/wiki/Types_of_business_entity#United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Types of business entity">Ltd.</a>, a <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> based multinational business with significant presence in the <a href="/wiki/IT" class="mw-redirect" title="IT">IT</a> sector. Eight months earlier, CDW had bought a 35 percent stake in Kelway.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>As of 2018, CDW ranked 189th on the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> list of the largest United States corporations by revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 2005, CDW launched <a href="/wiki/BizTech" title="BizTech">BizTech magazine</a>, which it publishes on quarterly basis.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Berbee_Information_Networks_Corporation">Berbee Information Networks Corporation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CDW&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Berbee Information Networks Corporation" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Berbee Information Networks Corporation, an American company specializing in <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">IT</a>, was purchased by CDW in 2006. Prior to that it was a <a href="/wiki/Privately_held_company" title="Privately held company">privately held company</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Fitchburg,_Wisconsin" title="Fitchburg, Wisconsin">Fitchburg, Wisconsin</a>. Berbee was founded in 1993 by James G. Berbee and grew to over 300 million <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a> in revenue and more than 800 employees. Berbee's IT solutions included network infrastructure, systems and storage, productivity applications, communication and collaboration, application development, and security. Its data centers delivered hosted applications, co-location, and managed services. Berbee had offices in Illinois, Indiana, Michigan, Minnesota, Ohio, and Wisconsin. In October 2006, Berbee accepted an $175 million all-cash acquisition offer from CDW.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> It became a division of CDW and is now known as CDW Advanced Technology Services. The Berbee Voice-Over IP software product group, creator of the <a href="/wiki/InformaCast" title="InformaCast">InformaCast</a> family of IP notification solutions, was spun off to form the new company <a href="/wiki/Singlewire_Software" title="Singlewire Software">Singlewire Software</a> in April, 2009.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Non-profit">Non-profit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CDW&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Non-profit" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>CDW Non-Profit, a sector founded in 2007, assists in providing affordable technology tools and services to associations, foundations, governmental departments, religious and social service organizations.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/The_Brookings_Institution" class="mw-redirect" title="The Brookings Institution">The Brookings Institution</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salvation_Army" class="mw-redirect" title="Salvation Army">Salvation Army</a>, PCAOB, Aids Healthcare Foundation, The Metropolitan Museum of Art and <a href="/wiki/American_Heart_Association" title="American Heart Association">American Heart Association</a> are some of their clients.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
