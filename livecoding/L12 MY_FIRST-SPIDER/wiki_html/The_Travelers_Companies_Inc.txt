<p><b>The Travelers Companies, Inc.</b>, commonly known as <b>Travelers</b>, is an American <a href="/wiki/Insurance" title="Insurance">insurance</a> company. It is the second-largest writer of U.S. commercial property casualty insurance, and the sixth-largest writer of U.S. personal insurance through independent agents.  Travelers is incorporated in Minnesota, with headquarters in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, and its largest office in <a href="/wiki/Hartford,_Connecticut" title="Hartford, Connecticut">Hartford, Connecticut</a>. Travelers also maintains a large office in <a href="/wiki/St._Paul,_Minnesota" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Paul, Minnesota">St. Paul, Minnesota</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HQ_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HQ-1">[1]</a></sup> It has been a component of the <a href="/wiki/Dow_Jones_Industrial_Average" title="Dow Jones Industrial Average">Dow Jones Industrial Average</a> since June 8, Travelers ranked No. 106 in the 2018 <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> list of the largest United States corporations with total revenue of $28.9 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>Travelers, through its subsidiaries and approximately 14,000 independent agents and brokers, provides commercial and personal property and casualty insurance products and services to businesses, government units, associations, and individuals. The company offers insurance through three segments:
</p>
<ul><li><i>Personal Insurance</i>, which includes home, auto and other insurance products for individuals</li>
<li><i>Business Insurance</i>, which includes a broad array of property and casualty insurance and insurance-related services in the United States</li>
<li><i>Bond and Specialty Insurance</i>, which includes surety, crime, and financial liability businesses which primarily use credit-based <a href="/wiki/Underwriting" title="Underwriting">underwriting</a> processes, as well as property and casualty products that are predominantly marketed on an international basis.</li></ul><div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none"><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2 id="mw-toc-heading">Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Travelers_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:150px-sttravlogo.png" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/a/a0/150px-sttravlogo.png" decoding="async" width="150" height="96" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="150" data-file-height="96"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 150px;height: 96px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/a/a0/150px-sttravlogo.png" data-alt="" data-width="150" data-height="96" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:150px-sttravlogo.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Top</i>: The familiar umbrella logo of the Travelers, used until its spinoff from Citigroup. <i>Bottom</i>: The logo that The St. Paul used prior to the merger with Travelers.</div></div></div>
<p>The main predecessor companies of The Travelers Companies, Inc. are The St. Paul Companies, Inc. and Travelers Property Casualty Corporation.
</p><p><b>Saint Paul Fire and Marine Insurance Co.</b> was founded March 5, 1853, in St. Paul, Minnesota, serving local customers who were having a difficult time getting claim payments in a timely manner from insurance companies on the east coast of the United States. It barely survived the <a href="/wiki/Panic_of_1857" title="Panic of 1857">Panic of 1857</a> by dramatically paring down its operations and later reorganizing itself into a <a href="/wiki/Joint-stock_company" title="Joint-stock company">stock company</a> (as opposed to a <a href="/wiki/Mutual_company" class="mw-redirect" title="Mutual company">mutual company</a>). It soon spread its operations across the country. In 1998 it acquired <a href="/wiki/USF%26G" title="USF&amp;G">USF&amp;G</a>, known formerly as United States Fidelity and Guaranty Company, an insurance company based in <a href="/wiki/Baltimore,_Maryland" class="mw-redirect" title="Baltimore, Maryland">Baltimore, Maryland</a>, for $3.9 billion in stock and assumed debt.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> By buying USF&amp;G, they went from the 13th to the eighth largest property and casualty insurance company in the United States. Through economies of scale between the two companies, and a difficult business environment, they downsized the company substantially over the coming years by selling certain business units to focus on more profitable business units.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p><b>The Travelers Insurance Company</b> was founded in Hartford by James G. Batterson, a <a href="/wiki/Stonemasonry" title="Stonemasonry">stone contractor</a><sup id="cite_ref-Campbell_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Campbell-9">[9]</a></sup> who became aware for the first time of <a href="/wiki/Accident_insurance" title="Accident insurance">accident insurance</a> for travelers (i.e., an early form of <a href="/wiki/Travel_insurance" title="Travel insurance">travel insurance</a>) while traveling in England in 1859 from <a href="/wiki/Leamington_Spa" title="Leamington Spa">Leamington</a> to London.<sup id="cite_ref-Trav40_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trav40-10">[10]</a></sup>  His railway ticket included <a href="/wiki/Accidental_death" title="Accidental death">accidental death</a> insurance coverage up to the amount of £1,000, and lesser indemnities for non-fatal injuries.<sup id="cite_ref-Trav40_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trav40-10">[10]</a></sup> Batterson visited the London and Paris offices of European insurers to learn about the accident insurance business, then went home to Hartford and raised $500,000 in capital to launch a company to provide accident insurance to American travelers.<sup id="cite_ref-Trav40_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trav40-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>Travelers obtained its official state charter on June 17, 1863.<sup id="cite_ref-Trav40_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trav40-10">[10]</a></sup>  The company did not issue its first regular insurance policy until April 5, 1864, but informally entered into its first insurance agreement a month earlier.<sup id="cite_ref-Trav40_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trav40-10">[10]</a></sup>  On March 1, 1864, local banker James Bolter jokingly inquired of Batterson how much it would cost to insure him up to $5,000 for accidental death for the journey from the post office to his home.<sup id="cite_ref-Trav40_10-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trav40-10">[10]</a></sup>  Batterson replied, "Two cents," which Bolter promptly tendered; those coins have been kept by Travelers ever since.<sup id="cite_ref-Trav40_10-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trav40-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Gilpin_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gilpin-11">[11]</a></sup>  The original company logo was a <a href="/wiki/Plate_armour" title="Plate armour">knight's suit of armor</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Rand_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rand-12">[12]</a></sup>  The now-iconic red umbrella first appeared in Travelers advertising as early as 1870, but at that time was not yet the official logo.<sup id="cite_ref-Gilpin_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gilpin-11">[11]</a></sup>  The company changed its official logo from a suit of armor to the Travelers Tower in 1920, and then replaced that with the red umbrella in 1959.<sup id="cite_ref-Rand_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rand-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>During the 20th century, Travelers was responsible for many insurance industry firsts, including the first automobile policy (1897), the first air travel policy (1919), and the first space travel policy (late 1960s, for astronauts in the <a href="/wiki/Apollo_program" title="Apollo program">Apollo program</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-Gilpin_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gilpin-11">[11]</a></sup>  In 1954, the company established the world's first privately owned weather research facility, the <a href="/wiki/Travelers_Weather_Research_Center" title="Travelers Weather Research Center">Travelers Weather Research Center</a>, the first organization to make weather predictions using probabilities ("20% chance of rain").<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>By the early 1990s, Travelers was predominantly a general property and casualty insurer that also happened to do some travel insurance on the side, and in February 1994, Travelers quietly exited its original travel insurance business by selling the unit to a retiring Travelers executive.<sup id="cite_ref-Biesaida_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biesaida-13">[13]</a></sup>  The resulting company is now known as Travel Insured International, <a href="/wiki/Fairfax_Financial" title="Fairfax Financial">a Crum and Forster Company</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Biesaida_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biesaida-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Travelers_1993_logo.png" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/3b/Travelers_1993_logo.png/220px-Travelers_1993_logo.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="59" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="458" data-file-height="123"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 59px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/3b/Travelers_1993_logo.png/220px-Travelers_1993_logo.png" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="59" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Travelers_1993_logo.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Travelers logo, ca. 1993</div></div></div>
<p>In the 1990s, Travelers went through a series of mergers and acquisitions. It was bought by <a href="/wiki/Primerica" title="Primerica">Primerica</a> in December 1993, but the resulting company retained the Travelers name.<sup id="cite_ref-Gilpin_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gilpin-11">[11]</a></sup> In 1995 it became <b>The Travelers Group</b>. It bought <a href="/wiki/Aetna" title="Aetna">Aetna</a>'s property and casualty business in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In April 1998, the Travelers Group merged with Citicorp to form <a href="/wiki/Citigroup" title="Citigroup">Citigroup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Gilpin_11-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gilpin-11">[11]</a></sup> However, the synergies between the banking and insurance arms of the company did not work as well as planned, so Citigroup spun off Travelers Property and Casualty into a subsidiary company in 2002,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> although it kept the red <a href="/wiki/Umbrella" title="Umbrella">umbrella</a> <a href="/wiki/Logo" title="Logo">logo</a>. Three years later, Citigroup sold Travelers Life &amp; Annuity to <a href="/wiki/MetLife" title="MetLife">MetLife</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In 2003, Travelers bought renewal rights for Royal &amp; SunAlliance Personal Insurance and Commercial businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In 2004, the St. Paul and Travelers Companies merged and renamed itself <b>St. Paul Travelers</b>, with the headquarters set in St. Paul, Minnesota.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In August of that year, it was charged of misleading statements associated with the merger.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Despite many assurances from CEO Jay Fishman that the newly formed company would retain the St. Paul name, the corporate name only lasted until 2007, when the company repurchased the rights to the famous red umbrella logo from Citigroup and readopted it as its main corporate symbol, while also changing the corporate name to <b>The Travelers Companies</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>Many of Travelers' ancestor companies, such as St. Paul and <a href="/wiki/USF%26G" title="USF&amp;G">USF&amp;G</a>, are technically still around today, and still write policies and accept claims in their own names (under the overarching Travelers brand name). As is typical of most <a href="/wiki/Insurance_in_the_United_States" title="Insurance in the United States">insurers in the United States</a>, Travelers never dissolved the various companies it acquired, but simply made them wholly owned subsidiaries and trained its employees to act on behalf of those subsidiaries. This is a common risk management strategy used by U.S. insurance groups. If any one company in the group gets hit with too many claims, the situation can be easily contained to that one company (which is placed in runoff and allowed to run its policies to completion), while the remainder of the group continues to operate normally.
</p><p>In November 2010, Travelers entered into a <a href="/wiki/Joint_venture" title="Joint venture">joint venture</a> agreement under which the company would invest in J. Malucelli Participações em Seguros e Resseguros S.A., the market leader in the surety insurance business in Brazil. The transaction closed in June 2011 with Travelers acquiring a 43.4 percent interest. Travelers' investment in newly issued shares significantly increased J. Malucelli's capital level, positioning it for substantial growth in Brazil. At the time, Travelers had the option to increase its investment to retain a 49.5 percent interest, which the company later did in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2013, Travelers announced the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/The_Dominion_of_Canada_General_Insurance_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="The Dominion of Canada General Insurance Company">Dominion of Canada General Insurance Company</a> from E-L Financial Corporation Limited (TSX: ELF).<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The transaction later closed in November 2013. The combined organization, referred to as Travelers Canada, remains headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>The company's joint venture with J. Malucelli in Brazil completed the acquisition of a majority interest in Cardinal Compañía de Seguros, a Colombian start-up surety provider in September 2015. The business, which is based in <a href="/wiki/Bogot%C3%A1" title="Bogotá">Bogotá</a>, operates under the co-branded name J. Malucelli Travelers.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2015, Travelers acquired a <a href="/wiki/Controlling_interest" title="Controlling interest">majority interest</a> in the property casualty business of its J. Malucelli joint venture in Brazil. J. Malucelli commenced writing property casualty business in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The property casualty business operates under the Travelers brand and focuses on property, general liability, construction and financial insurance products. The business is based in São Paulo.
</p><p>As a result of the transaction, Travelers now owns 95 percent of the property casualty business with Paraná Banco, the parent company of J. Malucelli, retaining a five percent interest. Travelers’ interest in the surety business of the J. Malucelli joint venture remains at 49.5 percent.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2017, Travelers agreed to acquire UK-based Simply Business from Aquiline Capital Partners for approximately $490 million. Simply Business is a leading U.K. distributor of small business insurance policies, offering products online on behalf of a broad panel of carriers. It has more than 425,000 microbusiness customers covering more than 1,000 classes of business, and was named “Best Company to Work For” by The Sunday Times in 2015 and 2016. The transaction adds to Travelers' digital capabilities.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, Travelers ranked 106 on the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> list of largest U.S. companies.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> On June 8, 2009, Travelers replaced its former parent Citigroup on the <a href="/wiki/Dow_Jones_Industrial_Average" title="Dow Jones Industrial Average">Dow Jones Industrial Average</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p><p>On August 4, 2015, the company announced that Alan Schnitzer would succeed Jay Fishman as Chief Executive Officer effective December 1, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p><p>On August 4, 2017, Travelers completed its previously announced acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Simply_Business" title="Simply Business">Simply Business</a>, a leading provider of small business insurance policies in the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p><p>On August 15, 2018, Travelers acquired the majority stake of Zensurance, a digital business insurance brokerage in Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Alleged_anticompetitive_practices">Alleged anticompetitive practices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Travelers_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Alleged anticompetitive practices" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In January 2007, Travelers agreed to pay US$77 million to six states to settle a class action suit and end investigations into its insurance practices.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> The charges involved paying the insurance broker <a href="/wiki/Marsh_%26_McLennan_Companies" title="Marsh &amp; McLennan Companies">Marsh &amp; McLennan Companies</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Contingent_commission&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Contingent commission (page does not exist)">contingent commissions</a> to win business without the knowledge of clients, thus creating a conflict of interest.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Additionally, the investigation examined whether Travelers had created the illusion of competition by submitting <a href="/wiki/Bid_rigging" title="Bid rigging">fake bids</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> thus misleading clients into believing they were receiving competitive commercial premiums.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Football_League_lawsuit">National Football League lawsuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Travelers_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: National Football League lawsuit" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>In August 2012, Travelers sued the <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">National Football League</a> for forcing the company and its subsidiaries to pay to defend the league for <a href="/wiki/Concussions_in_American_football" title="Concussions in American football">failing to protect players from brain injury</a>, in a case filed in the <a href="/wiki/New_York_State_Supreme_Court" class="mw-redirect" title="New York State Supreme Court">New York State Supreme Court</a> called Discover Property &amp; Casualty Co. et al. vs. National Football League et al., New York State Supreme Court, New York County, No. 652933/2012. The league had sued over three dozen insurance companies the week before in an attempt to cover  the claims that players made against the league.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Advertising">Advertising</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Travelers_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Advertising" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:205px-SPTLogotrans.png" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a7/205px-SPTLogotrans.png/200px-205px-SPTLogotrans.png" decoding="async" width="200" height="68" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="205" data-file-height="70"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 200px;height: 68px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a7/205px-SPTLogotrans.png/200px-205px-SPTLogotrans.png" data-alt="" data-width="200" data-height="68" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:205px-SPTLogotrans.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>St. Paul Travelers logo used until February 2007</div></div></div>
<p>The logo of the red umbrella was created in 1870 when it appeared in a newspaper ad for the young insurance company. It was revived in the early 1960s, when it was given its signature red color by Harry W. Knettell, then the account executive for The Travelers and Vice President at the Charles Brunelle advertising agency. During the late 1960s Charles Brunelle was the largest advertising agency in Hartford, a city known as "the insurance capital of the world," "the Hollywood of insurance," or "America's file cabinet" due to the many insurance companies in that town. The Travelers was one of their many insurance company clients.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="The citation does not appear to be a published secondary source (September 2014)">better source needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In 2006, a Travelers commercial titled Snowball was nominated for an Emmy. Snowball featured a man, walking down a steep <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco</a> sidewalk, who trips and knocks over a table of items at a garage sale. The man and the items roll down the street, forming a ball which gathers garbage cans, pedestrians, construction materials, motorcycles, light poles, and other items, in a manner very reminiscent of the familiar cartoon "snowball" effect or the cult video game <i><a href="/wiki/Katamari_Damacy" title="Katamari Damacy">Katamari Damacy</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-engadget:Real-life_Katamari_in_Travelers_Insurance_ad_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-engadget:Real-life_Katamari_in_Travelers_Insurance_ad-49">[49]</a></sup> The creators of the ad say it is simply based on the <a href="/wiki/Snowball_effect" title="Snowball effect">snowball effect</a>, they have never heard of the game, and that the resulting similarity was a surprise to them.<sup id="cite_ref-engadget:Travelers_ad_imitating_Katamari:_just_coincidence_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-engadget:Travelers_ad_imitating_Katamari:_just_coincidence-50">[50]</a></sup></p><p>In 2007, the company secured naming rights for an annual golf event to be called the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.travelerschampionship.com">Travelers Championship</a> golf tournament, formerly the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Hartford_Open" class="mw-redirect" title="Greater Hartford Open">Greater Hartford Open</a>, on the <a href="/wiki/PGA_Tour" title="PGA Tour">PGA Tour</a>.
</p><p>In April 2008, The Travelers repurchased the rights to the famous red umbrella, which is featured in several commercials starring <a href="/wiki/Paul_Freeman_(actor)" title="Paul Freeman (actor)">Paul Freeman</a> as well as other advertisements.  In July 2008, the spot "Delivery," also starring Freeman, was nominated for an Emmy <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Commercial" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Commercial"> Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Commercial</a>.
</p>
