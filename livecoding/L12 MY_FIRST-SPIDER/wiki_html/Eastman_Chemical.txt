</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_segments">Business segments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastman_Chemical_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Business segments" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Eastman manufactures and markets chemicals, fibers and plastics. It provides <a href="/wiki/Coating" title="Coating">coatings</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adhesive" title="Adhesive">adhesives</a> and specialty <a href="/wiki/Plastic" title="Plastic">plastics</a> products, is a major supplier of <a href="/wiki/Cellulose_acetate" title="Cellulose acetate">cellulose acetate</a> fibers, and produces <a href="/wiki/Copolyester" title="Copolyester">copolyesters</a> for packaging.
</p><p>The company's products and operations are currently managed and reported in four operating segments: Additives &amp; Functional Products, Advanced Materials, Chemical Intermediates and Fibers.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<dl><dt>Additives &amp; Functional Products</dt></dl><p>In this segment, Eastman manufactures chemicals and adhesive resins for products in the coatings and tires industries in transportation, building and construction, durable goods, health and wellness, and <a href="/wiki/Consumables" title="Consumables">consumables</a> markets.
</p>
<dl><dt>Advanced Materials</dt></dl><p>In this segment, Eastman produces and markets specialty <a href="/wiki/Copolyester" title="Copolyester">copolyesters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cellulose_ester" class="mw-redirect" title="Cellulose ester">cellulose esters</a>, interlayers, and aftermarket window film products for use in transportation, consumables, building and construction, durable goods, health and wellness, and electronics.
</p>
<dl><dt>Chemical Intermediates</dt></dl><p>The Chemical Intermediates segment leverages large scale and vertical integration from the acetyl, olefins, and alkylamine streams and proprietary manufacturing technology for specialty fluids to manufacture diversified products that are sold externally for use in markets such as industrial chemicals and processing; building and construction; health and wellness; energy, fuels, and water; consumables; agriculture, as well as used internally by other Eastman segments.
</p>
<dl><dt>Fibers</dt></dl><p>In the Fibers segment, Eastman manufactures and sells Estron™ acetate tow and Estrobond™ <a href="/wiki/Triacetin" title="Triacetin">triacetin</a> <a href="/wiki/Plasticizer" title="Plasticizer">plasticizers</a> for use primarily in the manufacture of cigarette filters; Estron™ natural and Chromspun™ solution-dyed <a href="/wiki/Acetate" title="Acetate">acetate</a> yarns for use in apparel, home furnishings and industrial fabrics; and cellulose acetate flake and acetyl raw materials for other acetate fiber producers.
</p>

After the war ended, <a href="/wiki/Eastman_Kodak" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastman Kodak">Eastman Kodak</a> founder <a href="/wiki/George_Eastman" title="George Eastman">George Eastman</a> began working to have an independent supply of chemicals for his photographic processes. His search for suitable quantities of <a href="/wiki/Methanol" title="Methanol">methanol</a> and acetone led him to the southern United States.
</p><p>In 1920, Tennessee Eastman was founded with two major platforms – organic chemicals and <a href="/wiki/Acetyl" class="mw-redirect" title="Acetyl">acetyls</a>.   Products such as <a href="/wiki/Calcium_acetate" title="Calcium acetate">calcium acetate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sodium_acetate" title="Sodium acetate">sodium acetate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Acetic_acid" title="Acetic acid">acetic acid</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Acetic_anhydride" title="Acetic anhydride">acetic anhydride</a> became the basis for the company’s platforms.
</p><p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, <a href="/wiki/RDX" title="RDX">RDX</a>, a powerful <a href="/wiki/Explosive" title="Explosive">explosive</a>, was manufactured for the U.S. government at <a href="/wiki/Holston_Ordnance_Works" class="mw-redirect" title="Holston Ordnance Works">Holston Ordnance Works</a> at Tennessee Eastman sites. At the peak of production  near the end of the war, the ordnance plant was producing a million and a half pounds of explosives each day. Tennessee Eastman was responsible for managing the <a href="/wiki/Y-12_National_Security_Complex" title="Y-12 National Security Complex">Y-12 National Security Complex</a> at <a href="/wiki/Oak_Ridge,_Tennessee" title="Oak Ridge, Tennessee">Oak Ridge, Tennessee</a> which produced enriched uranium for the <a href="/wiki/Manhattan_Project" title="Manhattan Project">Manhattan Project</a>, from 1943 to May 1947.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Since_the_1980s">Since the 1980s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastman_Chemical_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Since the 1980s" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1983, Eastman opened the first commercial <a href="/wiki/Coal_gasification" title="Coal gasification">coal gasification</a> facility in the United States at its Kingsport plant site to produce chemicals from <a href="/wiki/Syngas" title="Syngas">syngas</a> rather than petroleum. The "Chemicals from Coal Facility" at the Kingsport plant was recognized as a <a href="/wiki/National_Historic_Chemical_Landmarks" title="National Historic Chemical Landmarks">National Historic Chemical Landmark</a> by the <a href="/wiki/American_Chemical_Society" title="American Chemical Society">American Chemical Society</a> in 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 1994, Eastman Chemical Company spun off from Eastman Kodak and became an independent corporation.
In early 2005 Eastman broke ground on the first world-scale manufacturing facility using <a href="/w/index.php?title=IntegRex&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="IntegRex (page does not exist)">IntegRex</a>, a technology that reduces the number of intermediate process steps in producing PET resin. This technology is now owned by <a href="/w/index.php?title=DAK_Americas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="DAK Americas (page does not exist)">DAK Americas</a>.
</p><p>In July 2012, Eastman Chemical Corporation completed its acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Solutia" title="Solutia">Solutia</a> Inc., a manufacturer of performance materials and specialty chemicals, for $4.8 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-11K_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11K-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="October_2017_explosion">October 2017 explosion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastman_Chemical_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: October 2017 explosion" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On the morning of October 4, 2017, an explosion occurred at its Kingsport Plant around the coal gasification building.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In April 2018, the Tennessee Department of Environment and Conservation determined the cause to be a valve blockage due to slurry or debris intrusion.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Circular_Economy">Circular Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastman_Chemical_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Circular Economy" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2019, Eastman began commercial-scale chemical recycling for a broad set of waste plastics that would otherwise be placed in a landfill, incinerated, or worse, wind up in the environment.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Eastman Advanced Circular Recycling technologies complement mechanical recycling by processing a wide spectrum of waste plastics that traditional recycling methods cannot, including polyesters, polypropylene, polyethylene, and polystyrene. These waste plastics are derived from a variety of sources, including single-use plastics, textiles, and cuttings from discarded carpet. Eastman's recycling technologies - carbon renewal technology and polyester renewal technology - provide a true circular solution of infinite recycling for materials, allowing them to be reused repeatedly. Eastman began commercial operation of carbon renewal technology in October 2019 at the company's largest manufacturing facility in Kingsport, Tenn. In 2020, Eastman will use carbon renewal technology to recycle millions of pounds of polyester carpet that would otherwise have been placed in a landfill.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Sustainability_and_Corporate_Responsibility">Sustainability and Corporate Responsibility</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastman_Chemical_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Sustainability and Corporate Responsibility" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<p>As of 2006 Eastman was ranked twenty-seventh among U.S. corporate producers of <a href="/wiki/Air_pollution" title="Air pollution">air pollution</a> according to a 2010 study conducted by the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Massachusetts_Amherst" title="University of Massachusetts Amherst">University of Massachusetts Amherst</a> <a href="/wiki/Political_Economy_Research_Institute" title="Political Economy Research Institute">Political Economy Research Institute</a>. Eastman facilities in four U.S. states released 7.02 million pounds of chemicals into the air in that year.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="United States Environmental Protection Agency">Environmental Protection Agency</a> has also linked Eastman to several <a href="/wiki/Superfund" title="Superfund">Superfund</a> toxic waste sites, according to the <a href="/wiki/Center_for_Public_Integrity" title="Center for Public Integrity">Center for Public Integrity</a>.
</p><p>Eastman has been a member of Responsible Care®, a global voluntary initiative developed autonomously by the chemical industry to improve health, safety, and environmental performance, for more than 25 years. In 2013, Eastman was named Responsible Care Company of the Year in the large company category by the American Chemistry Council (ACC).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Eastman was awarded the 2009 Presidential Green Chemistry Challenge Award presented by the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). The award recognizes technologies that incorporate the principles of green chemistry into chemical design, manufacture, and use.
</p><p>Through 2015, Eastman improved energy intensity by approximately 8% compared to a 2008 baseline. The company established a goal to improve energy intensity 20% by 2020. In 2014 and early 2016, Eastman converted two boilers at its Kingsport, Tennessee, site from coal to natural gas combustion.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-16">[16]</a></sup> Three additional boilers will be converted by 2018 for a total of five. The Kingsport boiler conversion project is the most significant air pollution control project in the history of Eastman and will diversify the Kingsport facility’s energy mix from 90% coal to 50% coal and 50% natural gas. Eastman also converted a coal-fired boiler at its Indian Orchard facility in Springfield, Massachusetts, to natural gas in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>As of 2019, Eastman has been recognized by the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) as an ENERGY STAR Partner of the Year eight consecutive years. Eastman has also been recognized three consecutive years by Glassdoor as an Employees’ Choice Best Place to Work and by Ethisphere Institute as a World’s Most Ethical Company.
