</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominion_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company's asset portfolio includes 27,000 <a href="/wiki/Megawatt" class="mw-redirect" title="Megawatt">megawatts</a> of power generation, 6,000 miles (9,700 km) of electric transmission lines, 14,000 miles (23,000 km) of natural gas transmission, gathering and storage pipeline, and 1.2 trillion cubic feet (34 km<sup>3</sup>) equivalent of natural gas and oil reserves. Dominion also operates the nation's largest natural gas storage facility,<sup id="cite_ref-Dimensions_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dimensions-10">[10]</a></sup> amounting to more than 975 billion cubic feet (2.76<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup>10</sup> m<sup>3</sup>) of storage capacity.<sup id="cite_ref-2008report_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2008report-6">[6]</a></sup> The company's <a href="/wiki/Dominion_Cove_Point_LNG,_LP" class="mw-redirect" title="Dominion Cove Point LNG, LP">Cove Point</a> <a href="/wiki/Liquefied_natural_gas" title="Liquefied natural gas">liquefied natural gas</a> (LNG) import terminal on the <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay" title="Chesapeake Bay">Chesapeake Bay</a> is one of the nation's largest and busiest facilities of its kind. Dominion serves more than 5 million retail energy customers in the Midwest, mid-Atlantic and Northeast regions of the U.S.
</p><p>In 2017, Dominion was listed at #238 on the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> A book about the company's 100-year history, <i>Dominion’s First Century: A Legacy of Service</i>, was published in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Generation_statistics">Generation statistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominion_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Generation statistics" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>18 percent of Dominion's total electric production comes from <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a>, 22 percent comes from <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_power" title="Nuclear power">nuclear power</a>, 32 percent comes from <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a>, 9 percent comes from <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">oil</a>, 12 percent comes from Hydro and other renewables, and 7 percent from other sources.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> A strategy is being developed for <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a> sources, primarily <a href="/wiki/Wind_power" title="Wind power">wind</a> and <a href="/wiki/Biomass" title="Biomass">biomass</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Energy_conservation" title="Energy conservation">conservation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">efficiency</a> programs to play an increasingly important role in meeting future energy needs and minimizing the company's environmental footprint.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominion_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Dominion's corporate roots reach back to the Colonial era through predecessor companies that operated canal and river barging, street lighting, railways, and electric trolleys. In 1787, the Virginia General Assembly created the Appomattox Trustees to promote navigation along the <a href="/wiki/Appomattox_River" title="Appomattox River">Appomattox River</a>. In 1795, the trustees formed the Upper Appomattox Company to build dams along the river for industrial use, beginning Dominion's history.<sup id="cite_ref-history_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-15">[15]</a></sup> In 1901, the water rights passed to the newly formed Virginia Passenger &amp; Power Company.<sup id="cite_ref-funun_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-funun-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>Dominion's closest direct corporate ancestor, Virginia Railway &amp; Power Company, was founded by <a href="/wiki/Frank_Jay_Gould" title="Frank Jay Gould">Frank Jay Gould</a> on June 29, 1909. It bought Virginia Passenger &amp; Power soon afterward. In 1925, the name was changed to the Virginia Electric and Power Company (VEPCO), a regulated <a href="/wiki/Monopoly" title="Monopoly">monopoly</a>. In 1940, VEPCO doubled its service territory by merging with the Virginia Public Service Company. The <a href="/wiki/Greater_Richmond_Transit_Company" title="Greater Richmond Transit Company">transit operations</a> were sold in 1944.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In 1980, VEPCO began branding itself as "Virginia Power," while branding its North Carolina operations as "North Carolina Power." Three years later, VEPCO reorganized as a holding company, Dominion Resources.<sup id="cite_ref-history_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-funun_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-funun-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>By 1985, Dominion split its distribution operations among three operating companies: Virginia Power, North Carolina Power, and West Virginia Power. In 1986, Dominion gained territory by expanding in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Virginia" title="Northern Virginia">Northern Virginia</a> after purchasing the Virginia distribution territory of <a href="/wiki/Pepco" title="Pepco">Potomac Electric Power Company</a> (PEPCO). In 1987, the West Virginia Power division was later sold to <a href="/wiki/Utilicorp_United" class="mw-redirect" title="Utilicorp United">Utilicorp United</a>, but Dominion retained ownership of the <a href="/wiki/Mount_Storm_Power_Station" title="Mount Storm Power Station">Mount Storm Power Station</a> in West Virginia. (In 1999, West Virginia Power would be sold to <a href="/wiki/Allegheny_Energy" title="Allegheny Energy">Allegheny Energy</a> and folded into its Monongahela Power subsidiary;<sup id="cite_ref-Allegheny-Energy-Sep-1999-8-K_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Allegheny-Energy-Sep-1999-8-K-18">[18]</a></sup> it and other Allegheny Energy subsidiaries have since been acquired in 2010 by <a href="/wiki/FirstEnergy" title="FirstEnergy">FirstEnergy</a>.)
<sup id="cite_ref-funun_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-funun-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>Throughout the 1980s and 1990s, Dominion initiated a series of expansions into regulated and non-regulated energy businesses, both domestically and internationally. During that era, the company also established itself as a world-class operator of <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_power" title="Nuclear power">nuclear</a> <a href="/wiki/Power_station" title="Power station">power stations</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Washingtonian_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Washingtonian-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In 2000, Dominion bought <a href="/w/index.php?title=Consolidated_Natural_Gas_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Consolidated Natural Gas Company (page does not exist)">Consolidated Natural Gas Company</a> (CNG) of <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh,_Pennsylvania" class="mw-redirect" title="Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania">Pittsburgh</a>, and added natural gas service to its energy delivery network in the energy-intensive markets in the Northeastern quadrant of the U.S. In 2001, Dominion bought <a href="/wiki/Louis_Dreyfus_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Louis Dreyfus Group">Louis Dreyfus Natural Gas Company</a>, adding to its natural gas delivery network.<sup id="cite_ref-history_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>Dominion re-branded all of its operations in 2000 to Dominion from Virginia and North Carolina Power as well as Consolidated Gas in order to create a more unified energy company. In 2007, as part of another effort to refocus on core electric and gas operations, Dominion sold most of its <a href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Houston, Texas">Houston</a>-based natural gas and oil exploration and production business for pre-tax proceeds of nearly $14 billion. Its onshore US oil and gas reserves were sold in separate deals to <a href="/wiki/Loews_Corporation" title="Loews Corporation">Loews Corporation</a> and to <a href="/wiki/XTO_Energy" title="XTO Energy">XTO Energy</a>, while its <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> reserves were sold to <a href="/wiki/Eni" title="Eni">Eni</a>, and its Canadian reserves were sold to two Canadian trusts. Dominion still retains some production areas in <a href="/wiki/Appalachia" title="Appalachia">Appalachia</a>, however.<sup id="cite_ref-history_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2016, Dominion Resources announced that they would be acquiring <a href="/wiki/Questar_Corporation_(gas_company)" title="Questar Corporation (gas company)">Questar Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The acquisition was completed in September 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-Questar_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Questar-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, Dominion Resources rebranded itself to Dominion Energy, following with a new logo.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2018, <a href="/wiki/Reuters" title="Reuters">Reuters</a> reported that Dominion Energy would be buying <a href="/wiki/SCANA" title="SCANA">SCANA Corporation</a> for $7.9 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-Swamynathan_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Swamynathan-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2018, Dominion Energy announced that it would be acquiring SCANA Corporation<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>; the acquisition was completed in January 2019.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In the summer of 2018, Dominion Energy launched a "grid transformation program." The program's aim was to build 3,000 megawatts worth of new solar and wind energy by the year 2022. The program was launched under the authority of the Grid Transformation &amp; Security Act, a state law signed by Virginia Governor <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Northam" title="Ralph Northam">Ralph Northam</a>. "The law paves the way for expanded investments in <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a>, smart grid technology, a stronger, more secure grid and energy efficiency programs . . ."<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>

<p>Dominion has three operating businesses:<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Dominion_Generation">Dominion Generation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominion_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Dominion Generation" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Dominion generates electricity for both regulated sale in its Virginia and North Carolina markets, and also for wholesale in other markets in the Northeast and Midwest United States. Electricity generation is the largest unit of Dominion.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Dominion_Virginia_and_North_Carolina_Power">Dominion Virginia and North Carolina Power</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominion_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Dominion Virginia and North Carolina Power" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Dominion is a regulated electric utility that transmits, and distributes electricity from its power plants in <a href="/wiki/Virginia_City_Hybrid_Energy_Center" title="Virginia City Hybrid Energy Center">Virginia</a>, North Carolina, Connecticut, and West Virginia to customers.
</p>

<p>Dominion Exploration and Production was the <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oil" title="Oil">oil</a> exploration and production subsidiary of Dominion, and by 2007 was one of the largest independent natural gas and oil operators.  During 2007, Dominion sold the majority of its oil and natural gas exploration and production assets to put additional focus on growing its electric generation and energy distribution, transmission, storage and retail businesses mainly in <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia" title="West Virginia">West Virginia</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion_plans">Expansion plans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominion_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Expansion plans" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>Dominion is a stakeholder in a joint venture planning to build the 600 miles (970 km) <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Coast_Pipeline" title="Atlantic Coast Pipeline">Atlantic Coast Pipeline</a>, a natural gas pipeline that will run between West Virginia and North Carolina and is planned to be in service in late 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-ogj_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ogj-28">[28]</a></sup> Though the project has faced stiff opposition from environmental and community heritage groups along its route, ground was broken on the pipeline in Lewis County, West Virginia, in May 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Contributions">Contributions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominion_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Contributions" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Political">Political</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominion_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Political" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The Dominion Political Action Committee (PAC) has been very active in donating to Virginia candidates. In 2009, the Dominion PAC donated a total of $814,885 with 56% going to <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_of_Virginia" title="Republican Party of Virginia">Republicans</a> and 41% to <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_of_Virginia" title="Democratic Party of Virginia">Democrats</a>. In 2008, the PAC donated $539,038 with 50% going to Republicans and 47% to Democrats.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> In the 2016 election cycle, the PAC contributed $1,276,016.17 to various political candidates and committees.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Charitable">Charitable</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominion_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Charitable" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Dominion's social investment program is carried out primarily through the Dominion Foundation, which gives about $20 million each year to charities in the states in which Dominion does business.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p><p>Dominion also has the Benjamin J. Lambert, III, Volunteer of the Year Program.  2017 was the thirty-third year of the program recognizing top company volunteers.  Dominion honors the volunteers by paying $1000 to the charity of the individual's choice.  In 2016, twelve employees from Ohio, Pennsylvania, Virginia and West Virginia were selected.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>In 2020, Dominion responded to the coronavirus pandemic by stopping service disconnects for non-payment and helping customers who had been disconnected for not making payments to reconnect to its service. It is also waiving late and reconnection fees. The company has also directed its charitable foundation to provide $1 million in aid to help individuals and organizations fighting COVID-19. The $1 million will be offered to national groups like the American Red Cross and local organizations the company identifies.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(6)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominion_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Controversies" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-6 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-6">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="High_voltage_lines">High voltage lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominion_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: High voltage lines" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>A number of controversies have surrounded the company's decisions to develop new routes for its <a href="/wiki/Electric_power_transmission" title="Electric power transmission">electric power transmission</a> lines. On February 13, 2007, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Washington_Post" title="The Washington Post">The Washington Post</a></i> reported that the power company was planning to change the route of one 500 <a href="/wiki/Kilovolt" class="mw-redirect" title="Kilovolt">kV</a> transmission line to appease critics in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Virginia" title="Northern Virginia">Northern Virginia</a> from a route that would cut through protected <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forest</a> and <a href="/wiki/Farm" title="Farm">farmland</a> to a southern route that would bypass nature preserves and <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">Civil War</a> sites by running adjacent to existing power lines. However, <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">U.S. Congressional Representative</a> <a href="/wiki/Frank_Wolf_(politician)" title="Frank Wolf (politician)">Frank Wolf</a> (<a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">R</a> - <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">VA</a>) and Governor <a href="/wiki/Tim_Kaine" title="Tim Kaine">Tim Kaine</a> (<a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">D</a>) remained opposed to the line, saying that there was no real need, and that Dominion was trying to bring cheap electricity from the <a href="/wiki/Midwest" class="mw-redirect" title="Midwest">Midwest</a>. Dominion contested, saying that the line would bring needed electricity to growing <a href="/wiki/Northern_Virginia" title="Northern Virginia">Northern Virginia</a>. The proposal was accepted by the <a href="/wiki/State_Corporation_Commission" class="mw-redirect" title="State Corporation Commission">State Corporation Commission</a> (SCC) on October 7, 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p><p>On February 15, 2008, the SCC approved a controversial proposal for a 230 kV Dominion Virginia transmission line that would travel above ground for 1.8 miles (2.9 km) along a <a href="/wiki/Woodland" title="Woodland">wooded</a> portion of the <a href="/wiki/Washington_%26_Old_Dominion_Railroad_Trail" class="mw-redirect" title="Washington &amp; Old Dominion Railroad Trail">Washington &amp; Old Dominion Railroad Trail</a> between <a href="/wiki/Leesburg,_Virginia" title="Leesburg, Virginia">Leesburg</a> and Clarks Gap in <a href="/wiki/Loudoun_County,_Virginia" title="Loudoun County, Virginia">Loudoun County</a>, which Dominion again claimed was necessary for power reliability.<sup id="cite_ref-wpst_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wpst-38">[38]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Less than three weeks later, on March 4 and March 5, 2008, the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Virginia" title="Senate of Virginia">Senate</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Virginia_House_of_Delegates" title="Virginia House of Delegates">House of Delegates</a> of the Virginia General Assembly <a href="/wiki/Unanimity" title="Unanimity">unanimously</a> passed emergency legislation that ordered the SCC to approve the underground construction of the line along that section of the trail as part of a four-part statewide <a href="/wiki/Pilot_(experiment)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pilot (experiment)">pilot program</a> for the development of underground transmission lines. Sponsored by Delegate <a href="/wiki/Joe_T._May" title="Joe T. May">Joe T. May</a> (<a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">R</a> - <a href="/wiki/Loudoun_County,_Virginia" title="Loudoun County, Virginia">Loudoun</a>), the legislation exempted the project from any requirements for further SCC analyses relating to the impacts of the route, including environmental impacts and impacts upon historical resources. The legislation went into effect when <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Virginia" title="Governor of Virginia">Virginia Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Tim_Kaine" title="Tim Kaine">Tim Kaine</a> approved it on April 2, 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-wpst_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wpst-38">[38]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Wise_County_power_plant">Wise County power plant</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominion_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Wise County power plant" data-section="15" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The company began constructing a 605 <a href="/wiki/MWe" class="mw-redirect" title="MWe">MWe</a> <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a> fired <a href="/wiki/Power_station" title="Power station">power station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Wise_County,_Virginia" title="Wise County, Virginia">Wise County, Virginia</a> in June 2008.  As of December 2009<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Dominion_Energy&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the construction had reached the halfway point, with the plant scheduled to be fully operational in mid-2012.<sup id="cite_ref-times_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-42">[42]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dom_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dom-43">[43]</a></sup> Dominion calls the plant the "<a href="/wiki/Virginia_City_Hybrid_Energy_Center" title="Virginia City Hybrid Energy Center">Virginia City Hybrid Energy Center</a>," which has been criticized by environmentalists as a way to make the plant sound environmentally friendly.<sup id="cite_ref-hampton_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hampton-44">[44]</a></sup> The plant does however have the most stringent air permit for any coal fired power plant in the nation currently. The plant will burn up to 20% <a href="/wiki/Biomass" title="Biomass">biomass</a> along with coal and a small amount of waste coal known as <a href="/wiki/Spoil_tip" title="Spoil tip">gob</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dom_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dom-43">[43]</a></sup>  The plant's <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> emissions are currently projected to be 5.4 million tons per year.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> High levels of <a href="/wiki/Mercury_(element)" title="Mercury (element)">mercury</a> emitted from the plant, which is controlled by activated carbon injection to reduce the emissions, as well as a <a href="/wiki/Fly_ash" title="Fly ash">fly ash</a> dump near the <a href="/wiki/Clinch_River" title="Clinch River">Clinch River</a> (a source of drinking water) are also of concern. The plant would also continue to support <a href="/wiki/Mountaintop_removal" class="mw-redirect" title="Mountaintop removal">mountaintop removal</a> coal mining. Supporters, including Governor <a href="/wiki/Tim_Kaine" title="Tim Kaine">Tim Kaine</a>, stated that as one of the largest importers of electricity, Virginia could become less dependent on importing electricity from other states with a new power plant.<sup id="cite_ref-hampton_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hampton-44">[44]</a></sup> The plant brought 1000 workers to build, and will employ 130 full-time, as well as pay 4 to 7 million dollars of <a href="/wiki/Tax" title="Tax">tax</a> revenue yearly to economically depressed Wise County.<sup id="cite_ref-times_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-42">[42]</a></sup> In September 2008, the site was blockaded by activists from the <a href="/wiki/Rainforest_Action_Network" title="Rainforest Action Network">Rainforest Action Network</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Cove_Point_LNG_import_facility">Cove Point LNG import facility</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominion_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Cove Point LNG import facility" data-section="16" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2001 when Dominion's <a href="/wiki/Dominion_Cove_Point_LNG,_LP" class="mw-redirect" title="Dominion Cove Point LNG, LP">Dominion Cove Point LNG, LP</a> subsidiary was scheduled to reopen, many local residents were concerned about the proximity (only 3 miles) to the <a href="/wiki/Calvert_Cliffs_Nuclear_Power_Plant" title="Calvert Cliffs Nuclear Power Plant">Calvert Cliffs Nuclear Power Plant</a>, and the damage that could be caused by an attack or an explosion at the plant. Residents thought that the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Energy_Regulatory_Commission" title="Federal Energy Regulatory Commission">Federal Energy Regulatory Commission</a> did not consider the risks before opening the plant.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p><p>In 2005, <a href="/wiki/Washington_Gas" class="mw-redirect" title="Washington Gas">Washington Gas</a> claimed that the <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a> <a href="/wiki/Import" title="Import">imported</a> at the plant was too "hot," meaning that it contained fewer heavy <a href="/wiki/Hydrocarbons" class="mw-redirect" title="Hydrocarbons">hydrocarbons</a> and burned hotter. Washington Gas said that the hot gas caused problems for its customers and caused many of its mains to break. Dominion denied that the imported gas was the cause of the breaks and stated that expanding the area serviced by the imported gas would not cause additional leaks in the <a href="/wiki/District_of_Columbia" class="mw-redirect" title="District of Columbia">District of Columbia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Virginia" title="Northern Virginia">Northern Virginia</a> suburbs.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_record">Environmental record</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominion_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Environmental record" data-section="17" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:322px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Brayton_Point_Power_Station.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Brayton_Point_Power_Station.JPG/320px-Brayton_Point_Power_Station.JPG" decoding="async" width="320" height="180" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1920" data-file-height="1080"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 320px;height: 180px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Brayton_Point_Power_Station.JPG/320px-Brayton_Point_Power_Station.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="320" data-height="180" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Brayton_Point_Power_Station.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Brayton Point Power Station in <a href="/wiki/Somerset,_Massachusetts" title="Somerset, Massachusetts">Somerset, Mass</a></div></div></div>
<p>In 2010, the <a href="/wiki/Political_Economy_Research_Institute" title="Political Economy Research Institute">Political Economy Research Institute</a> ranked Dominion Resources 51st among corporations emitting <a href="/wiki/Airborne_pollutants_in_the_United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="Airborne pollutants in the United States">airborne pollutants in the United States</a>. Dominion's Toxic Score of 16,656 (pounds released x toxicity x population exposure) represents a significant improvement from both the 2008 report (Dominion ranked 27th with a Toxic Score of 58,642) and the 2005 report (Dominion ranked 19th with a Toxic Score of 117,712)<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> In December 2007, a settlement between the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="United States Environmental Protection Agency">United States Environmental Protection Agency</a> (EPA) and Dominion Energy of Brayton Point called for the company's power generating plant to install new closed cycle cooling towers that provided significant protection to aquatic organisms in <a href="/wiki/Mount_Hope_Bay" title="Mount Hope Bay">Mount Hope Bay</a>, which flows into <a href="/wiki/Narragansett_Bay" title="Narragansett Bay">Narragansett Bay</a>. The 2007 settlement resolved an ongoing dispute that began in 2003. The EPA issued a final discharge permit called a National Pollution Discharge Elimination System (NPDES) for the Brayton Point Power Station requiring significant reductions in thermal discharges to, and water intake from, <a href="/wiki/Mount_Hope_Bay" title="Mount Hope Bay">Mount Hope Bay</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> In 2002, Dominion was responsible for 1,110,703 pounds of <a href="/wiki/Gastrointestinal" class="mw-redirect" title="Gastrointestinal">gastrointestinal</a> or liver toxicant emissions, 1,440,000 pounds of <a href="/wiki/Musculoskeletal" class="mw-redirect" title="Musculoskeletal">musculoskeletal</a> toxicant emissions, and 1,489,763 pounds of suspected respiratory toxicant emissions, and 1,478,383 pounds of suspected skin or <a href="/wiki/Sense_organ" class="mw-redirect" title="Sense organ">sense organ</a> toxicant emissions among other emissions that are suspected to be hazardous.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
