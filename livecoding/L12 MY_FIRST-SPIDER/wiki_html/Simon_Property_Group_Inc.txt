It owns or has an interest in more than 325<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> properties comprising approximately 241,000,000 square feet (22,400,000 m<sup>2</sup>) of gross leasable area in North America and Asia. The company is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis,_Indiana" class="mw-redirect" title="Indianapolis, Indiana">Indianapolis, Indiana</a> and employs more than 5,000 people. It is publicly traded on the NYSE under the symbol SPG and is part of the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_100" title="S&amp;P 100">S&amp;P 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Property_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Simon Property Group was formed in 1993<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> when the majority of the shopping center interests of Melvin Simon &amp; Associates became a publicly-traded company. Melvin Simon &amp; Associates, owned by brothers <a href="/wiki/Melvin_Simon" title="Melvin Simon">Melvin Simon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Simon_(real_estate)" title="Herbert Simon (real estate)">Herbert Simon</a>, was founded in 1960 in Indianapolis, Indiana, and had long been one of the top shopping center developers in the United States.
</p><p>In 1996, Simon DeBartolo Group was created when Simon Property merged with former rival DeBartolo Realty Corp.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> This was shortly after DeBartolo Realty became a publicly-traded company encompassing the shopping mall interests of the <a href="/wiki/Edward_J._DeBartolo_Sr." title="Edward J. DeBartolo Sr.">Edward J. DeBartolo Sr.</a> family, another leading developer. Simon DeBartolo rapidly acquired assets in the then-fragmented industry. Notable acquisitions included The Retail Property Trust and a group of properties held by <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a>'s pension plan in 1997 and <a href="/wiki/Corporate_Property_Investors" title="Corporate Property Investors">Corporate Property Investors</a> (CPI) in 1998. Following the CPI acquisition in 1998, the company announced it was reverting to its original name, Simon Property Group, as the DeBartolo family was resuming its private real-estate development operation while retaining their interest in Simon.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Simon_HQs.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Simon_HQs.jpg/220px-Simon_HQs.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="2304" data-file-height="1728"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Simon_HQs.jpg/220px-Simon_HQs.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Simon_HQs.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Simon corporate headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis" title="Indianapolis">Indianapolis</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Simon continued to be a prolific acquirer of shopping centers, including a portfolio from New England Development in 1999; several prime properties from Rodamco North America in 2002 (including <a href="/wiki/Houston_Galleria" class="mw-redirect" title="Houston Galleria">Houston Galleria</a> in <a href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Houston, Texas">Houston, Texas</a> and <a href="/wiki/SouthPark_Mall_(Charlotte,_North_Carolina)" title="SouthPark Mall (Charlotte, North Carolina)">SouthPark Mall</a> in <a href="/wiki/Charlotte,_North_Carolina" title="Charlotte, North Carolina">Charlotte, North Carolina</a>); several high-profile properties such as <a href="/wiki/Dadeland_Mall" title="Dadeland Mall">Dadeland Mall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fashion_Valley_Mall" title="Fashion Valley Mall">Fashion Valley Mall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Copley_Place" title="Copley Place">Copley Place</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fashion_Centre_at_Pentagon_City" title="Fashion Centre at Pentagon City">Fashion Centre at Pentagon City</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Stanford_Shopping_Center" title="Stanford Shopping Center">Stanford Shopping Center</a>; and in 2004, Chelsea Premium Outlets (now simply named "Premium Outlets"). In 2003, Simon became a co-owner of The Kravco Company, which became Kravco Simon. On April 3, 2007, a partnership including Simon agreed to acquire the <a href="/wiki/Mills_Corporation" title="Mills Corporation">Mills Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2011, Simon entered into a partnership with Nintendo to provide complimentary 3DS Wi-Fi hotspots at nearly 200 of its malls.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> This was later expanded or changed to provide compatibility for laptops and mobile devices. In September 2011, Simon acquired the historic <a href="/wiki/Southdale_Center" title="Southdale Center">Southdale Center</a>, located in <a href="/wiki/Edina,_Minnesota" title="Edina, Minnesota">Edina, Minnesota</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2013, Simon announced it would form a <a href="/wiki/REIT" class="mw-redirect" title="REIT">REIT</a> of its smaller malls and community shopping centers called Washington Prime Group (Initially to be named SpinCo). The spin-off was created in May 2014 and was headed by Mark Ordan who was the final CEO of the Simon-bought <a href="/wiki/Mills_Corporation" title="Mills Corporation">Mills Corporation</a>. The regional malls in WPG were still managed by Simon and flagged as Simon Properties on websites and inside their malls until early 2016, while Washington Prime managed the "strip centers" of the portfolio in-house.<sup id="cite_ref-Simon_Plans_Spin-off_of_Smaller_Properties_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Simon_Plans_Spin-off_of_Smaller_Properties-11">[11]</a></sup> In September 2014, WPG announced to acquire <a href="/wiki/Glimcher_Realty_Trust" title="Glimcher Realty Trust">Glimcher Realty Trust</a> and its properties, in which Washington Prime Group would be renamed <a href="/wiki/WP_Glimcher" class="mw-redirect" title="WP Glimcher">WP Glimcher</a> when the deal was made.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The deal was completed in January 2015. As part of the deal, Simon acquired <a href="/wiki/Jersey_Gardens" class="mw-redirect" title="Jersey Gardens">Jersey Gardens</a> (renamed to "The Mills at Jersey Gardens" and added to the Mills portfolio) in <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth,_New_Jersey" title="Elizabeth, New Jersey">Elizabeth, New Jersey</a> and University Park Village in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Worth,_Texas" title="Fort Worth, Texas">Fort Worth, Texas</a>, while WP Glimcher acquired <a href="/wiki/Brunswick_Square_(East_Brunswick,_New_Jersey)" class="mw-redirect" title="Brunswick Square (East Brunswick, New Jersey)">Brunswick Square</a> in <a href="/wiki/East_Brunswick,_New_Jersey" title="East Brunswick, New Jersey">East Brunswick, New Jersey</a> from Simon.
</p><p>On February 10, 2020 it was announced that Simon Property Group had entered into a deal to acquire competing <a href="/wiki/Taubman_Centers" title="Taubman Centers">Taubman Centers</a> for $52.50 a share, in a deal valued at $3.6 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>On March 18, 2020 the company announced in a press release that they would close all their U.S. shopping malls until March 29, in response to the <a href="/wiki/Coronavirus_disease_2019" title="Coronavirus disease 2019">Coronavirus disease 2019</a> pandemic.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisition_offers">Acquisition offers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Property_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acquisition offers" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Simon_Property_Group_properties" title="List of Simon Property Group properties">List of Simon Property Group properties</a></div>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="General_Growth_Properties">General Growth Properties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Property_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: General Growth Properties" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On November 29, 2009, <i><a href="/wiki/Financial_Times" title="Financial Times">Financial Times</a></i> reported that Simon may have attempted to acquire its failing rival General Growth Properties, which was operating under <a href="/wiki/Chapter_11_bankruptcy_protection" class="mw-redirect" title="Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection">Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection</a>. Should GGP have been acquired in its entirety, such deal would be worth up to $30 billion at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Simon hired property investment firm Cohen &amp; Steers, J.P. Morgan, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Lazard" title="Lazard">Lazard</a> investment bank and the <a href="/wiki/Wachtell,_Lipton,_Rosen_%26_Katz" title="Wachtell, Lipton, Rosen &amp; Katz">Wachtell, Lipton, Rosen &amp; Katz</a> law firm to explore the possibility of acquiring GGP.
</p><p>On February 16, 2010, Simon announced that it placed a bid on February 8 to acquire General Growth Properties in a deal worth $10 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> However, the bid was rejected by General Growth twice during the week it was announced. On February 19, 2010, one GGP shareholder filed suit <i>(Young v. Bucksbaum)</i> against the company's board of directors for rejecting Simon's bid, accusing chairman John Bucksbaum and six other board members of breaching their fiduciary duty to GGP's investors.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The General Growth board favored an investment offer from <a href="/wiki/Brookfield_Asset_Management" title="Brookfield Asset Management">Brookfield Asset Management</a> worth $2.6 billion.
</p><p>Simon's next step, on April 14, 2010, was to announce a $2.5 billion equity investment offer which equaled the price per share of Brookfield's offer.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Simon claimed that the deal was more favorable to GGP and its equity holders than Brookfield's offer, stating that it would eliminate the highly dilutive warrants that GGP would issue to Brookfield, Pershing Square and Fairholme Capital. Simon's offer also included a co-investment commitment by Paulson &amp; Co worth $1 billion. Simon Property Group had not ruled out a full takeover of General Growth, claiming that their investment offer would give them more time to work out their differences concerning antitrust issues.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>On May 7, 2010, Simon Property Group decided to withdraw its acquisition and recapitalization proposals for General Growth Properties.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Instead, General Growth (and its spinoff Rouse Properties) were acquired several years later by competitor <a href="/wiki/Brookfield_Property_Partners" title="Brookfield Property Partners">Brookfield Property Partners</a>, making Brookfield the number 2 mall operator in the USA behind Simon.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Prime_Outlets">Prime Outlets</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Property_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Prime Outlets" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On December 8, 2009, Simon Property Group was offered the sale of <a href="/wiki/Prime_Retail" class="mw-redirect" title="Prime Retail">Prime Retail</a>'s Prime Outlets portfolio for $2.24 billion, which included centers at <a href="/wiki/Williamsburg,_Virginia" title="Williamsburg, Virginia">Williamsburg, Virginia</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Marcos,_Texas" title="San Marcos, Texas">San Marcos, Texas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hagerstown,_Maryland" title="Hagerstown, Maryland">Hagerstown, Maryland</a>. In May 2010, Simon surprisingly acquired Prime Outlets-Puerto Rico in <a href="/wiki/Barceloneta,_Puerto_Rico" title="Barceloneta, Puerto Rico">Barceloneta</a>, which was renamed "Puerto Rico Premium Outlets". However, at the time, Simon did not acquire the whole portfolio, excluding Prime Outlet's center at <a href="/wiki/St._Augustine,_Florida" title="St. Augustine, Florida">St. Augustine, Florida</a> where Simon's Premium Outlet mall was already located.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Finally, on August 30, 2010, Simon officially acquired the Prime Retail portfolio of twenty-one outlet malls, including the Barceloneta, Puerto Rico location acquired in May 2010. Three properties were not included: the indoor St. Augustine, Florida property, and developments at Grand Prairie, Texas and Livermore Valley, California.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The acquired properties received Premium Outlet branding just after the completion of the deal. The developments at Grand Prairie, Texas and Livermore Valley, California were acquired in 2012, just months after their openings, and were rebranded as "Premium Outlets".
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Capital_Shopping_Centres">Capital Shopping Centres</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Property_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Capital Shopping Centres" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>At the end of 2010, Simon Property Group attempted to acquire UK-based retail investment group <a href="/wiki/Intu_Properties" title="Intu Properties">Capital Shopping Centres</a> (CSCG), after CSCG signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/Peel_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Peel Group">Peel Group</a> to acquire the Trafford Centre.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> This offer was later declined by Capital Shopping Centres shareholders in an <a href="/wiki/Extraordinary_general_meeting" title="Extraordinary general meeting">EGM</a>, with the shareholders, many of whom are South African, voting instead for the deal with the Peel Group.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Premium_Outlets_expansion_into_Canada">Premium Outlets expansion into Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Property_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Premium Outlets expansion into Canada" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On August 1, 2013, <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Premium_Outlets" title="Toronto Premium Outlets">Toronto Premium Outlets</a> opened in <a href="/wiki/Halton_Hills" title="Halton Hills">Halton Hills</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Premium_Outlets_Montreal" title="Premium Outlets Montreal">Premium Outlets Montreal</a>, which is the second Premium Outlet Center located in Canada opened in October, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Premium_Outlet_Collection_YEG" title="Premium Outlet Collection YEG">Premium Outlet Collection YEG</a> is now open at <a href="/wiki/Edmonton_International_Airport" title="Edmonton International Airport">Edmonton International Airport</a>.
</p>
