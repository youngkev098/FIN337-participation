</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colgate-Palmolive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:162px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Palmolive_soap_1922_advertisement_ladies_home_journal.jpeg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Palmolive_soap_1922_advertisement_ladies_home_journal.jpeg/160px-Palmolive_soap_1922_advertisement_ladies_home_journal.jpeg" decoding="async" width="160" height="207" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1467" data-file-height="1901"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 160px;height: 207px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Palmolive_soap_1922_advertisement_ladies_home_journal.jpeg/160px-Palmolive_soap_1922_advertisement_ladies_home_journal.jpeg" data-alt="" data-width="160" data-height="207" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Palmolive_soap_1922_advertisement_ladies_home_journal.jpeg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>1922 advertisement for Palmolive soap</div></div></div>
<p>In 1806, devout <a href="/wiki/Baptist" class="mw-redirect" title="Baptist">Baptist</a> English immigrant soap and candle maker <a href="/wiki/William_Colgate" title="William Colgate">William Colgate</a> established a starch, soap, and candle factory on <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dutch Street (page does not exist)">Dutch Street</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> under the name William Colgate &amp; Company.  In 1833, he suffered a severe heart attack, stopping his business's sales; after a <a href="/wiki/Convalescence" title="Convalescence">convalescence</a> he continued with his business.  In the 1840s, the company began selling individual cakes of soap in uniform weights.  In 1857, Colgate died and the company was reorganized as Colgate &amp; Company under the management of his devout Baptist son <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Colgate" title="Samuel Colgate">Samuel Colgate</a>, who did not want to continue the business but thought it would be the right thing to do.  In 1872, he introduced Cashmere Bouquet, a perfumed soap.  In 1873, the company introduced its first <a href="/wiki/Colgate_(toothpaste)" title="Colgate (toothpaste)">Colgate Toothpaste</a>, an aromatic toothpaste sold in jars.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1896, the company sold the first toothpaste in a tube, named Colgate Ribbon Dental Cream (invented by dentist <a href="/wiki/Washington_Sheffield" title="Washington Sheffield">Washington Sheffield</a>). Also in 1896, Colgate hired <a href="/wiki/Martin_Ittner" title="Martin Ittner">Martin Ittner</a> and under his direction founded one of the first applied research labs.<sup id="cite_ref-NJ_history_page_160_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NJ_history_page_160-5">[5]</a></sup> By 1908, they initiated mass sales of toothpaste in tubes. William Colgate's other son, <a href="/wiki/James_Boorman_Colgate" title="James Boorman Colgate">James Boorman Colgate</a>, was a primary trustee of <a href="/wiki/Colgate_University" title="Colgate University">Colgate University</a> (formerly Madison University).
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Palmolive1915.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/Palmolive1915.jpg/220px-Palmolive1915.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="316" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="810" data-file-height="1162"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 316px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/Palmolive1915.jpg/220px-Palmolive1915.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="316" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Palmolive1915.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>1915 magazine ad</div></div></div>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee" title="Milwaukee">Milwaukee</a>, Wisconsin, the B.J. Johnson Company was making a soap entirely of <a href="/wiki/Palm_oil" title="Palm oil">palm oil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olive_oil" title="Olive oil">olive oil</a>, the formula of which was developed by B.J. Johnson in 1898. The soap was popular enough to rename their company after it — Palmolive.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Around the start of the 20th century, Palmolive, which contained both palm and olive oils, was the world's best-selling soap. Extensive advertising included the radio programs <i><a href="/wiki/The_Palmolive_Hour" title="The Palmolive Hour">The Palmolive Hour</a></i> (1927-1931) and <i><a href="/wiki/Palmolive_Beauty_Box_Theater" title="Palmolive Beauty Box Theater">Palmolive Beauty Box Theater</a></i> (1934-1937). A <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Kansas" title="Kansas City, Kansas">Kansas City, Kansas</a>-based soap manufacturer known as Peet Brothers, who were originally from Wisconsin, merged with Palmolive to become Palmolive-Peet. In 1928, Palmolive-Peet acquired the Colgate Company to create the Colgate-Palmolive-Peet Company. In 1953, Peet was dropped from the name, leaving only Colgate-Palmolive Company, the current name.
</p><p>Colgate-Palmolive has long been in competition with <a href="/wiki/Procter_%26_Gamble" title="Procter &amp; Gamble">Procter &amp; Gamble</a> (P&amp;G), the world's largest <a href="/wiki/Soap" title="Soap">soap</a> and <a href="/wiki/Detergent" title="Detergent">detergent</a> maker. P&amp;G introduced its <a href="/wiki/Tide_(brand)" title="Tide (brand)">Tide</a> <a href="/wiki/Laundry_detergent" title="Laundry detergent">laundry detergent</a> shortly after <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, and thousands of consumers turned from Colgate's soaps to the new product. Colgate lost its number one place in the <a href="/wiki/Toothpaste" title="Toothpaste">toothpaste</a> market when P&amp;G added <a href="/wiki/Fluoride" title="Fluoride">fluoride</a> to its toothpaste (Colgate has since re-claimed the #1 sales position).<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In the beginning of TV, Colgate-Palmolive wished to compete with P&amp;G as a sponsor of <a href="/wiki/Soap_operas" class="mw-redirect" title="Soap operas">soap operas</a> and sponsored many soaps in full or in part including <i><a href="/wiki/The_Doctors_(soap_opera)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Doctors (soap opera)">The Doctors</a></i>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/George_Henry_Lesch" title="George Henry Lesch">George Henry Lesch</a>, president, <a href="/wiki/CEO" class="mw-redirect" title="CEO">CEO</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Chairman_of_the_board" class="mw-redirect" title="Chairman of the board">chairman of the board</a> of Colgate-Palmolive in the 1960s and 1970s, during that time, transformed it into a modern company with major restructuring.
</p><p>In 2005, Colgate sold the under-performing brands Fab, Dynamo, Arctic Power, ABC, <a href="/wiki/Cold_Power" title="Cold Power">Cold Power</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fresh_Start_(detergent)" title="Fresh Start (detergent)">Fresh Start</a>, as well as the license of the <a href="/wiki/Ajax_(cleaning_product)" title="Ajax (cleaning product)">Ajax</a> brand for laundry detergents in the <a href="/wiki/US" class="mw-redirect" title="US">US</a>, Canada and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, to <a href="/w/index.php?title=Phoenix_Brands&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Phoenix Brands (page does not exist)">Phoenix Brands</a>, LLC as part of their plan to focus on their higher margin oral, personal, and pet care products.<sup id="cite_ref-Colgate-Palmolive-Jul-2005-8-K_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Colgate-Palmolive-Jul-2005-8-K-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 2006, Colgate-Palmolive announced the intended acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Tom%27s_of_Maine" title="Tom's of Maine">Tom's of Maine</a>, a leading maker of natural toothpaste, for US $100 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Colgate-Palmolive-Apr-2006-10-Q_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Colgate-Palmolive-Apr-2006-10-Q-10">[10]</a></sup> Tom's of Maine was founded by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Chappell" title="Tom Chappell">Tom Chappell</a> in 1970.
</p><p>Today, Colgate has numerous subsidiary organizations spanning 200 countries, but it is publicly listed in only two, the United States and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.
</p><p>On October 25, 2012, the company announced it would cut 2,310 workers, or 6% of its workforce, by the end of 2016 in a push to make the consumer products company more efficient.<sup id="cite_ref-Colgate-Palmolive-Oct-2012-8-K_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Colgate-Palmolive-Oct-2012-8-K-11">[11]</a></sup> The company ranked 184th on the 2018 <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> list of the largest United States corporations by revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Educational_and_community_involvement">Educational and community involvement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colgate-Palmolive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Educational and community involvement" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1890, Madison University in New York State was renamed <a href="/wiki/Colgate_University" title="Colgate University">Colgate University</a> in honor of the Colgate family following decades of financial support and involvement.<sup id="cite_ref-americana_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-americana-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>The Colgate-Palmolive Company has sponsored a <a href="/wiki/Non-profit" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-profit">non-profit</a> track meet open to women of all ages called the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Colgate_Women%27s_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Colgate Women's Games (page does not exist)">Colgate Women's Games</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The Colgate Women's Games is the nation's largest amateur track series open to all girls from elementary school through college. Held at <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Pratt_Institute" title="Pratt Institute">Pratt Institute</a>, competitors participate in preliminary meets and semi-finals over five weekends throughout January. Finalists compete for trophies and educational grants-in-aid from Colgate-Palmolive Company at New York City's <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">Madison Square Garden</a> in February. For more than 20 years, the company supports the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Starlight_Children_Foundation&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Starlight Children Foundation (page does not exist)">Starlight Children Foundation</a> which is a non profit organization dedicated to help seriously ill children and their families. The mission is to help children to cope with pain, fear and isolation through entertainment, family activities and education.(2012)
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Ethics">Ethics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colgate-Palmolive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ethics" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2011, Colgate-Palmolive was one of the first companies recognized by <a href="/wiki/PETA" class="mw-redirect" title="PETA">PETA</a> (People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals) under the new "working for regulatory change" category for companies that test on animals only when mandated by government regulations and are actively seeking alternatives to animal testing.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> This relates to the corporation's decision to continue to participate in the profitable Chinese market, where some animal testing is still a regulatory requirement.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>  Other companies have chosen to decline entry to this market.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In 2006, Colgate-Palmolive acquired an 84% stake in <a href="/wiki/Tom%27s_of_Maine" title="Tom's of Maine">Tom's of Maine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Colgate-Palmolive-Apr-2006-10-Q_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Colgate-Palmolive-Apr-2006-10-Q-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-usatoday_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-18">[18]</a></sup> In 2011, the company chose to retain the use of the antibacterial agent <a href="/wiki/Triclosan" title="Triclosan">triclosan</a> in its market-leading Total toothpaste range, despite withdrawing it from several other product ranges, following concerns about triclosan's impact on health and the environment.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_record">Environmental record</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colgate-Palmolive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Environmental record" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2019, <a href="/w/index.php?title=BreakFreeFromPlastic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="BreakFreeFromPlastic (page does not exist)">BreakFreeFromPlastic</a> cited Colgate-Palmolive as one of the world's top ten plastic polluters.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Previously, Colgate-Palmolive had committed to 100% recyclability of plastics in packaging across all its product categories by 2025, but made no commitment to reducing the use of virgin plastic in packaging.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>Products of the Colgate-Palmolive company, specifically "Total" brand toothpaste contain <a href="/wiki/Triclosan" title="Triclosan">triclosan</a>. This ingredient has a questionable impact on waterways and marine life.
</p><p>Colgate-Palmolive, as a successor to <a href="/wiki/Mennen" title="Mennen">The Mennen Company</a>, is one of about 300 companies held potentially responsible for <a href="/wiki/Hazardous_waste" title="Hazardous waste">hazardous waste</a> at the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chemsol&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Chemsol (page does not exist)">Chemsol</a> federal <a href="/wiki/Superfund_site" class="mw-redirect" title="Superfund site">Superfund site</a> in <a href="/wiki/Piscataway_Township,_New_Jersey" class="mw-redirect" title="Piscataway Township, New Jersey">Piscataway</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>  Their involvement in this site may have contributed to the <a href="/wiki/Contamination" title="Contamination">contamination</a> of an estimated 18,500 cubic yards (14,100 m<sup>3</sup>) of soil with <a href="/wiki/Volatile_organic_compounds" class="mw-redirect" title="Volatile organic compounds">volatile organic compounds</a> (VOCs), <a href="/wiki/Polychlorinated_biphenyl" title="Polychlorinated biphenyl">PCBs</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lead" title="Lead">lead</a> off-site. A proposed $23 million agreement with the government and state of New Jersey would require Colgate-Palmolive and the other involved companies to pay for the cleanup of this hazardous waste that is contaminating the soil as well as the <a href="/wiki/Groundwater" title="Groundwater">groundwater</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The company completed a LEED Silver certified manufacturing plant in <a href="/wiki/Morristown,_Tennessee" title="Morristown, Tennessee">Morristown, Tennessee</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> in March 2008 which was the first of its kind in the U.S. The new LEED silver certified plant in Tennessee produces the Colgate Total toothpaste.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>Colgate Palmolive received the 2012 <a href="/wiki/Safe-in-Sound_Excellence_in_Hearing_Loss_Prevention_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Safe-in-Sound Excellence in Hearing Loss Prevention Award">Safe-in-Sound Excellence in Hearing Loss Prevention Award</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Anti-competitive_practices">Anti-competitive practices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colgate-Palmolive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Anti-competitive practices" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colgate-Palmolive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Facilities" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<p>In the U.S., the company operates approximately 60 properties, of which 14 are owned.<sup id="cite_ref-Colgate-Palmolive-Feb-2013-10-K_60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Colgate-Palmolive-Feb-2013-10-K-60">[60]</a></sup> Major U.S. manufacturing and warehousing facilities used by the oral, personal and home care segment of Colgate-Palmolive are located in <a href="/wiki/Morristown,_New_Jersey" title="Morristown, New Jersey">Morristown, New Jersey</a> (previously the headquarters of the Mennen company prior to their 1991 buyout, and still HQ of the Mennen division); <a href="/wiki/Morristown,_Tennessee" title="Morristown, Tennessee">Morristown, Tennessee</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Ohio" title="Cambridge, Ohio">Cambridge, Ohio</a>. The pet nutrition segment has major facilities in <a href="/wiki/Bowling_Green,_Kentucky" title="Bowling Green, Kentucky">Bowling Green, Kentucky</a>; <a href="/wiki/Emporia,_Kansas" title="Emporia, Kansas">Emporia, Kansas</a>; <a href="/wiki/Topeka,_Kansas" title="Topeka, Kansas">Topeka, Kansas</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Indiana" title="Richmond, Indiana">Richmond, Indiana</a>. The primary research center for oral, personal and home care products is located in <a href="/wiki/Piscataway,_New_Jersey" title="Piscataway, New Jersey">Piscataway, New Jersey</a> and the primary research center for pet nutrition products is located in Topeka, Kansas.
</p><p>Overseas, the company operates approximately 280 properties of which 80 are owned in over 70 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-Colgate-Palmolive-Feb-2013-10-K_60-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Colgate-Palmolive-Feb-2013-10-K-60">[60]</a></sup> Major overseas facilities used by the Oral, Personal and Home Care segment are located in Australia, Brazil, China, Colombia, France, Guatemala, India, Italy, Malaysia, Mexico, Pakistan, Poland, South Africa, Thailand, Venezuela, Vietnam and elsewhere throughout the world.
</p><p>Colgate-Palmolive has closed or is in the process of phasing out production at certain facilities under a restructuring program initiated in 2004 and has built new state-of-the-art plants to produce toothpaste in the U.S., Mexico and Poland.<sup id="cite_ref-Colgate-Palmolive-Feb-2008-10-K_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Colgate-Palmolive-Feb-2008-10-K-61">[61]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p><p>Colgate-Palmolive's chief manufacturing plant is located in <a href="/wiki/Burlington,_New_Jersey" title="Burlington, New Jersey">Burlington, New Jersey</a>, producing all of the fragrance and flavor oils for the company's facilities around the world.
</p>
