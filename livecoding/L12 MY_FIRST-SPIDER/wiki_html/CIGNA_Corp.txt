</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cigna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Connecticut_General_Life_Insurance_Company_Headquarters.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Connecticut_General_Life_Insurance_Company_Headquarters.JPG/220px-Connecticut_General_Life_Insurance_Company_Headquarters.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="109" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="2623" data-file-height="1304"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 109px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Connecticut_General_Life_Insurance_Company_Headquarters.JPG/220px-Connecticut_General_Life_Insurance_Company_Headquarters.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="109" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Connecticut_General_Life_Insurance_Company_Headquarters.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Connecticut_General_Life_Insurance_Company_Headquarters" title="Connecticut General Life Insurance Company Headquarters">Cigna's corporate headquarters</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bloomfield,_Connecticut" title="Bloomfield, Connecticut">Bloomfield, Connecticut</a></div></div></div>
<p>Cigna was formed by the 1982 merger of the Connecticut General Life Insurance Company (CG) and INA Corporation (the parent corporation of <a href="/wiki/Insurance_Company_of_North_America" title="Insurance Company of North America">Insurance Company of North America</a>, the first stock insurance company in America).<sup id="cite_ref-www.cignaglobalhealth.com/_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-www.cignaglobalhealth.com/-12">[12]</a></sup> The company name, Cigna, is a mix of letters from the merging companies, CG and INA.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Insurance Company of North America was formed in 1792 and is a corporate ancestor of Cigna. The CG was created in 1865 by a special act of the <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Connecticut" class="mw-redirect" title="Governor of Connecticut">Governor of Connecticut</a>. In October 1871, the <a href="/wiki/Great_Chicago_Fire" title="Great Chicago Fire">great Chicago Fire</a> burned for two days, destroyed 2,000 acres and left 100,000 people in Illinois homeless. INA paid $650,000, one of only 51 insurance companies (out of a total of 202) to pay claims in full.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>Before selling its domestic and international property and casualty business to the Bermuda-based <a href="/wiki/ACE_Limited" class="mw-redirect" title="ACE Limited">ACE Insurance</a> company in the late 1990s, Cigna was among the companies with a large international network comparable to those of AIG, Allianz, and Zurich. The strategy behind the sale was to concentrate on core business. ACE – at that time a key player at the Bermuda and Lloyds insurance market – on the other hand was interested on expanding the international network in the traditional insurance market.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>Cigna sold the majority of its life insurance operations to <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_National_Corporation" title="Lincoln National Corporation">Lincoln National Corporation</a> in 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-Cigna-Jul-1997-8-K_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cigna-Jul-1997-8-K-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Cigna now operates in 30 countries, has approximately 35,800 employees and manages around US$53.734 billion in assets.<sup id="cite_ref-Cigna-Feb-2013-10-K_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cigna-Feb-2013-10-K-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In 2002, it was alleged in violation of the <a href="/wiki/Securities_Exchange_Act" class="mw-redirect" title="Securities Exchange Act">Securities Exchange Act</a> for earnings manipulation. Its common stock price plummeted significantly as a result.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In October 2011, Cigna agreed to buy <a href="/wiki/HealthSpring" class="mw-redirect" title="HealthSpring">HealthSpring</a> Inc. for $3.8 billion to jump-start its business selling Medicare plans from 46,000 Medicare Advantage members to almost 400,000 Medicare Advantage members.<sup id="cite_ref-Cigna-Oct-2011-8-K_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cigna-Oct-2011-8-K-19">[19]</a></sup> The payment would come from an issue of new equity to cover about 20 percent of the value, with the rest funded by additional cash and debt.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2015, U.S. health insurer <a href="/wiki/Anthem_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Anthem Inc.">Anthem Inc.</a> announced an offer to acquire Cigna for more than $47 billion in cash and stock.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Anthem confirmed it had reached a deal to buy Cigna on July 24, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-Anthem-Cigna_merger_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Anthem-Cigna_merger-23">[23]</a></sup> On July 21, 2016, the US Justice Department filed an antitrust suit to block the proposed merger,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and a District Court ruling on February 8, 2017 blocked the merger on anticompetitive grounds.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> That month, Cigna Corp. called off its $48 billion merger agreement with <a href="/wiki/Anthem_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Anthem Inc.">Anthem Inc.</a>, with Anthem stating it would "continue to enforce its rights under the merger agreement and remains committed to closing the transaction."<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Products_and_services">Products and services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cigna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Products and services" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>CGHB global health plans typically include medical, dental, behavioral and disability, as well as business travel and life components. Expatriates are defined as employees of multinational companies working outside their home country on short, and long-term international assignments.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> CGHB maintains its own, in-house international claims platform, and offers a network of physicians and hospitals for its members (including 550,000 in the U.S. and more than 141,000 outside the U.S.).
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cigna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Controversies" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Nataline_Sarkisyan" class="mw-redirect" title="Nataline Sarkisyan">Nataline Sarkisyan</a></div>
<p>In December 2007, Cigna was criticized after the company refused to pay for a <a href="/wiki/Liver_transplant" class="mw-redirect" title="Liver transplant">liver transplant</a> of a California teenage girl, <a href="/wiki/Nataline_Sarkisyan" class="mw-redirect" title="Nataline Sarkisyan">Nataline Sarkisyan</a>, justifying their refusal to pay by claiming that the procedure was experimental, even though there was a liver ready and waiting to be transplanted and doctors estimated she had a 65% chance of surviving at least six months.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In response to much protest and public scrutiny, Cigna reversed its decision, though Ms. Sarkisyan died awaiting the transplant.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Cigna notes that it had no financial stake in the decision to authorize the transplant because it merely administers the insurance plan of Mr. Sarkisyan's employer and would not bear the cost of any operation. However, Cigna offered to pay for the transplant itself when it made the exception to the policy.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>Even though liver transplants have been performed since 1963 and are a well accepted treatment option for end-stage liver disease and acute liver failure, Cigna defended its actions by claiming that there was insufficient data to show that a transplant for a patient in Sarkisyan's condition would be safe and effective.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ-30">[30]</a></sup> The California court agreed with Cigna’s position that the Sarkisyans’ claims regarding Cigna's decision-making were preempted by federal <a href="/wiki/ERISA" class="mw-redirect" title="ERISA">ERISA</a> law. On April 16, 2009, the United States District Court for the Central District of California dismissed all of the claims against Cigna related to the coverage determination.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>In 2011, the California Nurses Association determined that Cigna denies roughly 39.6% of all claims (compared to competitors such as <a href="/wiki/Aetna" title="Aetna">Aetna</a> who denied about 5.9% of all claims in the same time frame).<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2012, Cigna fought and lost their argument in court that <a href="/wiki/ABA_therapy" class="mw-redirect" title="ABA therapy">ABA therapy</a> for autistic children is "experimental". The courts decided the therapy is a known treatment for autistic children, and Cigna’s arguments had no merit. As of 2011, Cigna was fighting to have this decision reversed.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2017, a lawsuit including Cigna as one of the defendants was filed.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The lawsuit states that Cigna engaged in activity prohibited by the mail/wire fraud implications of <a href="/wiki/RICO" class="mw-redirect" title="RICO">RICO</a><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> by paying members of the <a href="/wiki/Infectious_Diseases_Society_of_America" title="Infectious Diseases Society of America">Infectious Diseases Society of America</a> to develop clinical guidelines to not treat chronic <a href="/wiki/Lyme_disease" title="Lyme disease">Lyme disease</a>. The plaintiffs are suing Cigna, along with the IDSA and other health-insurance companies for damages. The case has been assigned to United States Judge for the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_District_of_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastern District of Texas">Eastern District of Texas</a> Texarkana Division Robert Schroeder.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Judge Schroeder granted a jury trial, and hearings have been scheduled for 2019.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Accounting_and_investor_relations">Accounting and investor relations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cigna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Accounting and investor relations" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>On February 9, 2005, Cigna elected to adopt in the fourth quarter of 2004 fair value accounting for its stock options in accordance with SFAS No. 123 and to restate prior periods.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_relations_and_lobbying">Public relations and lobbying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cigna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Public relations and lobbying" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<p>The UK newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">Guardian</a></i> in their <i>Esc and Ctrl</i> videoblog about control of the Internet by corporations, documented an incident with <a href="/wiki/Nataline_Sarkisyan" class="mw-redirect" title="Nataline Sarkisyan">Nataline Sarkisyan</a> and the former vice president of Cigna talking about <a href="/wiki/Astroturfing" title="Astroturfing">astroturfing</a>, the practice of creating <a href="/wiki/Fake_blog" class="mw-redirect" title="Fake blog">fake blogs</a> by interested groups, e.g. health insurance companies, to push claims that are profitable for said company into media, e.g. dismissing universal health care.<sup id="cite_ref-Esc_and_Ctrl:_Jon_Ronson_investigates_astroturfing_-_video_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Esc_and_Ctrl:_Jon_Ronson_investigates_astroturfing_-_video-39">[39]</a></sup></p><p>Cigna spent more than $4.4 million from 2005 to 2009 on <a href="/wiki/Lobbying" title="Lobbying">lobbying</a> to attain legislation that the company favors. This includes $720,000 spent in 2009 alone, when it had 20 lobbyists at five firms working on their behalf.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p><p>In 2008, the head of Cigna's public relations, <a href="/wiki/Wendell_Potter" title="Wendell Potter">Wendell Potter</a>, resigned, becoming a <a href="/wiki/Whistleblower" title="Whistleblower">whistleblower</a> who gave testimony in 2009 to the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Senate_Committee_on_Commerce,_Science_and_Transportation" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation">U.S. Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation</a> in favor of reform of the health care industry.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(6)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cigna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Awards" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-6 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-6">
<p>Cigna received gold in the 2009 <a href="/wiki/Gartner" title="Gartner">Gartner</a> &amp; 1to1 Customer Experience Excellence Award. The awards are given to the companies that "most clearly demonstrate exemplary customer relationship strategy and an unrivaled level of excellence in delivering the customer experience".<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2010, Cigna received the <a href="/wiki/JD_Power" class="mw-redirect" title="JD Power">JD Power</a> award for customer service for all of its call centers for the fourth time in a row.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(7)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Community_and_civic_affairs_and_charitable_giving">Community and civic affairs and charitable giving</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cigna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Community and civic affairs and charitable giving" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-7 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-7">
<p>In 2008, the Cigna Foundation contributed $2,533,535 to charitable activities that promote wellness.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-44">[44]</a></sup> Since 1995, Cigna and its employees have contributed $22.3 million to the <a href="/wiki/March_of_Dimes" title="March of Dimes">March of Dimes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-44">[44]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2010, Cigna began giving each company employee eight hours of <a href="/wiki/Paid_time_off" title="Paid time off">paid time off</a> annually to volunteer with non-profit health and community service organizations.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Salvation_Army" class="mw-redirect" title="Salvation Army">Salvation Army of Greater Philadelphia</a> helped kick off the program in front of Cigna's <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> headquarters with the Christmas in July campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(8)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Strategic_alliances">Strategic alliances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cigna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Strategic alliances" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-8 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-8">
<p>On April 16, 2010, Cigna announced an alliance with <a href="/wiki/Humana" title="Humana">Humana</a> group to offer a streamlined <a href="/wiki/Medicare_Advantage" title="Medicare Advantage">Medicare Advantage</a> offering through employer clients for retirees.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2011, Cigna and <a href="/wiki/TTK_Group" title="TTK Group">TTK Group</a>, an Indian business conglomerate focused on healthcare, formed a joint venture called <a href="/wiki/Cigna_TTK" class="mw-redirect" title="Cigna TTK">Cigna TTK</a> to develop a health insurance business in India subject to obtaining government approvals.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
