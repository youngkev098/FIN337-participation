BD  also provides consulting and analytics services in certain geographies. Founded in 1897 and <a href="/wiki/Becton,_Dickinson_and_Company_headquarters" title="Becton, Dickinson and Company headquarters">headquartered</a> in <a href="/wiki/Franklin_Lakes,_New_Jersey" title="Franklin Lakes, New Jersey">Franklin Lakes, New Jersey</a>, BD employs nearly 70,000 people in more than 50 countries throughout the world.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In the fiscal year ending September 30, 2019, more than 40% of BD sales were generated from non-U.S. markets.

</p><p>The company's customers include <a href="/wiki/Health_care" title="Health care">health care</a> institutions, science researchers, <a href="/wiki/Medical_laboratory" title="Medical laboratory">clinical laboratories</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical_industry" title="Pharmaceutical industry">pharmaceutical industry</a>, and the general public. BD was one of the first companies to sell U.S.-made glass syringes. It was also a pioneer in the production of hypodermic needles. Today, BD is divided into three segments: BD Medical, BD Life Sciences and BD Interventional.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> BD is ranked #195 in the 2019 <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> list.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>The firm in October 2014 agreed to acquire <a href="/wiki/CareFusion" title="CareFusion">CareFusion</a> for a price of $12.2 billion in cash and stock.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In April 2017 Becton Dickinson agreed to acquire <a href="/wiki/C._R._Bard" title="C. R. Bard">C. R. Bard</a> for $24 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2020, BD was one of ten companies recognized at the Inaugural Manufacturing Awards by <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey_Business_magazine" title="New Jersey Business magazine">New Jersey Business magazine</a> and the <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey_Business_and_Industry_Association" title="New Jersey Business and Industry Association">New Jersey Business &amp; Industry Association</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_development">Historical development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=BD_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Historical development" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="1897.E2.80.931947"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1897–1947">1897–1947</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=BD_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1897–1947" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>BD was founded in 1897 by <a href="/wiki/Maxwell_Becton" title="Maxwell Becton">Maxwell W. Becton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fairleigh_S._Dickinson" title="Fairleigh S. Dickinson">Fairleigh S. Dickinson</a>, two American businessmen who met on a sales trip. The same year the company made its first sale, a Luer-all-glass <a href="/wiki/Syringe" title="Syringe">syringe</a> that sold for $2.50. In 1898, BD acquired its first <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patent</a> for a medical product. In 1899 the company introduced its first logo which was meant to symbolize guaranteed superior quality of the products provided by BD. In 1904, BD acquired the Philadelphia Surgical Company. This very first acquisition and other early acquisitions significantly expanded the company's manufacturing ability and product offering. Two years later, BD incorporated in the state of <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> and built a manufacturing facility in <a href="/wiki/East_Rutherford,_New_Jersey" title="East Rutherford, New Jersey">East Rutherford</a>, the first in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">U.S.</a> created specifically for the production of <a href="/wiki/Thermometer" title="Thermometer">thermometers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hypodermic_needle" title="Hypodermic needle">hypodermic needles</a>, and syringes. In 1924, BD manufactured its first syringe designed specifically for <a href="/wiki/Insulin" title="Insulin">insulin</a> injection. One year later, BD introduced the BD Yale Luer-Lok Syringe, designed and patented by Fairleigh S. Dickinson, Sr. It provided a simple and secure method of attaching and removing the needle to and from the syringe. Today, <a href="/wiki/Luer_taper" title="Luer taper">Luer taper</a> connectors are the standard for syringes in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">U.S.</a> In 1947 Joseph Kleiner developed the Evacutainer—a device used to draw blood by vacuum through a needle into a test tube. This product eventually evolved to become the BD <a href="/wiki/Vacutainer" title="Vacutainer">Vacutainer</a> Blood Collection system.<sup id="cite_ref-hist_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hist-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="1948.E2.80.931970"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1948–1970">1948–1970</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=BD_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1948–1970" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1948, BD faced new leadership as <a href="/wiki/Fairleigh_Dickinson,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Fairleigh Dickinson, Jr.">Fairleigh S. Dickinson, Jr.</a> and Henry P. Becton, sons of the founders, took over the company. Their 24-year tenure was a time of rapid development for BD. The company grew within the U.S. as well as internationally. In 1949, it opened its first manufacturing site outside of New Jersey in <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Nebraska" title="Columbus, Nebraska">Columbus, Nebraska</a>. In 1961, it established a manufacturing facility in <a href="/wiki/Canaan,_Connecticut" title="Canaan, Connecticut">Canaan, Connecticut</a>. BD also soon expanded to <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> (1951), <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> (1952), <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> (1955), and <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> (1956). The tenure of Fairleigh S. Dickinson, Jr. and Henry P. Becton was also a time of significant product innovation. In 1950, BD's first sterile disposable product, a blood collection set, was developed and sold to the American Red Cross. In 1954, BD introduced the first completely disposable syringe made of glass: BD Hypak. This innovative product was used in a large-scale field test of the <a href="/wiki/Poliomyelitis" class="mw-redirect" title="Poliomyelitis">polio</a> <a href="/wiki/Vaccine" title="Vaccine">vaccine</a> developed by <a href="/wiki/Jonas_Salk" title="Jonas Salk">Dr. Jonas Salk</a>. One year later, BD entered the <a href="/wiki/Microbiology" title="Microbiology">microbiology</a> field through the acquisition of the Baltimore Biological Laboratory. In 1961, the company introduced its disposable BD Plastipak syringe. Under the new leadership the company also went public (1962) and appeared for the first time in the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> list (1970).<sup id="cite_ref-hist_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hist-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="1973.E2.80.931995"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1973–1995">1973–1995</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=BD_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1973–1995" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1973, BD added another U.S. facility — a research center in <a href="/wiki/Durham,_North_Carolina" title="Durham, North Carolina">Durham, North Carolina</a> in <a href="/wiki/Research_Triangle_Park" title="Research Triangle Park">Research Triangle Park</a>. The center was established to help the company meet <a href="/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="United States Environmental Protection Agency">United States Environmental Protection Agency</a> (EPA) requirements, research improved manufacturing and process methods, and develop future innovative technologies. In 1975, BD Pharmaceutical Systems Europe acquired an important patent for a prefilled syringe injecting <a href="/wiki/Heparin" title="Heparin">heparin</a>. In 1980, BD developed its first automated system for <a href="/wiki/Mycobacterium" title="Mycobacterium">mycobacteria</a> testing—the BD BACTEC 460TB System. The next year brought significant signs of the company's development as sales surpassed the $1 billion milestone and a new plant in <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> was constructed to serve the European markets. Five years later BD moved its corporate headquarters to <a href="/wiki/Franklin_Lakes,_New_Jersey" title="Franklin Lakes, New Jersey">Franklin Lakes, New Jersey</a> (<a href="/wiki/Becton,_Dickinson_and_Company_headquarters" title="Becton, Dickinson and Company headquarters">current HQ location</a>.) Also in 1986, BD acquired Fabersanitas Industrial, a major Spanish syringe manufacturer as well as Deseret Medical, a <a href="/wiki/Blood_vessel" title="Blood vessel">vascular</a> access devices manufacturer. The last three years of the 1980s also showed growth in Asia (manufacturing facilities in <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.) Furthermore, BD became the first company to introduce a safety-engineered syringe, the BD Safety-Lok (1988), and acquired the Beaver Blade Company, a manufacturer of high-quality surgical blades. The first half of the next decade was very important to BD's development. The company opened a central distribution center in <a href="/wiki/Temse" title="Temse">Temse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a> (1992) and entered <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> (1995). BD also introduced a device that offered needle stick protection following <a href="/wiki/Venipuncture" title="Venipuncture">blood-drawing</a> procedures, the BD Vacutainer Safety-Lok Blood Collection Set.<sup id="cite_ref-hist_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hist-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="1999.E2.80.932006"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1999–2006">1999–2006</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=BD_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1999–2006" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1999, as BD passed more than 100 years of presence in the medical devices industry, BD announced its new <a href="/wiki/Corporate_identity" title="Corporate identity">corporate identity</a>. Numerous independent brand names were replaced by a single name: BD. In addition, the company symbol that is used today was introduced. BD also named Edward J. Ludwig as the company's <a href="/wiki/President_(corporate_title)" title="President (corporate title)">president</a>. Ludwig was also named <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">Chief Executive Officer</a> (2000) and <a href="/wiki/Chairman" class="mw-redirect" title="Chairman">Chairman of the Board of Directors</a> (2002). On February 9, 1999, BD announced the acquisition of Biometric Imaging, Inc. (BMI), a privately held company serving the transfusion medicine, infectious diseases, and oncology markets.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On December 20, 2000, BD signed an agreement to acquire Gentest Corporation, a privately held company serving the life sciences market in the areas of drug metabolism and toxicology testing of pharmaceutical candidates.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> During that time, BD also got heavily involved in global health issues announcing a five-year maternal and neonatal tetanus worldwide elimination partnership with <a href="/wiki/UNICEF" title="UNICEF">UNICEF</a> (1999), launching the BD Safety Compliance Initiative (2000), and pledging $1 million contribution to the <a href="/wiki/International_AIDS_Vaccine_Initiative" title="International AIDS Vaccine Initiative">International AIDS Vaccine Initiative</a> (2002.) In 2003 and 2004, BD introduced several innovative products: BD FACSAria Cell Sorter—the first entirely new instrument in the next-generation portfolio of flow cytometers (2003), BD Accuspray—a nasal drug delivery system for administering vaccines (2003), BD.id—a patient identification system designed to limit the potential for medical errors in <a href="/wiki/Laboratory_specimen" title="Laboratory specimen">specimen</a> collection (2003), and the world's first "intelligent" <a href="/wiki/Insulin_pump" title="Insulin pump">insulin pump</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blood_glucose_monitoring" title="Blood glucose monitoring">glucose monitoring</a> system, a wireless system consisting of a Medtronic MiniMed Paradigm 512 Insulin Pump and Paradigm Link Blood Glucose Monitor, co-developed with BD. In the beginning of the 21st century BD also invested in growing its business by acquiring several strategically important companies. In 2001, it acquired the Gentest Corporation, a leading in drug metabolism and toxicology testing company. In 2004, BD completed the acquisition of Atto Bioscience Acquired, a company specializing in <a href="/wiki/Optics" title="Optics">optical</a> instrumentation, software, and <a href="/wiki/Reagent" title="Reagent">reagents</a> for real-time analysis of interactions taking place in living <a href="/wiki/Cell_(biology)" title="Cell (biology)">cells</a>. In 2005 BD entered the field of <a href="/wiki/Proteomics" title="Proteomics">proteomics</a> through its acquisition of FFE Weber GmbH, which specialized in the <a href="/wiki/Separation_process" title="Separation process">separation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fractionation" title="Fractionation">fractionation</a> of complex proteins. 2006 was also an important year to document BD's growth as the company acquired GeneOhm Sciences—a leader in the development of <a href="/wiki/Molecule" title="Molecule">molecular</a> diagnostic testing for the rapid detection of <a href="/wiki/Bacteria" title="Bacteria">bacterial</a> organisms and TriPath Imaging, a <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">cancer</a> diagnostics company.<sup id="cite_ref-hist_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hist-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>Becton Dickinson was formally divided into two business segments, namely BD Medical (52% of FY 09 revenues) and BD Life Sciences (44% of FY 09 revenues)<sup id="cite_ref-annual_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-annual-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>Currently there are three business segments.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="BD_Medical">BD Medical</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=BD_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: BD Medical" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>BD Medical focuses on providing innovative solutions to reduce the spread of infection, enhance diabetes treatment, advance drug delivery, improve surgical procedures and provide effective and safe medication management.​
</p><p>The BD Medical segment consists of four divisions or units, namely Diabetes Care, Medication Delivery Solutions, Medication Management Solutions, and Pharmaceutical Systems. The products of this segment include: <a href="/wiki/Hypodermic_needle" title="Hypodermic needle">needles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Syringe" title="Syringe">syringes</a>, intravenous <a href="/wiki/Catheter" title="Catheter">catheters</a>, safety-engineered and auto-disable devices, prefillable drug delivery systems, prefilled IV flush syringes, insulin syringes and pen needles, regional anesthesia needles, and anesthesia trays. This segment primarily sells to: <a href="/wiki/Hospital" title="Hospital">hospitals</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clinic" title="Clinic">clinics</a>, physicians' office practices, consumers and retail <a href="/wiki/Pharmacy" title="Pharmacy">pharmacies</a>, governmental and nonprofit public health agencies, <a href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical_industry" title="Pharmaceutical industry">pharmaceutical companies</a>, and health care workers.<sup id="cite_ref-annual_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-annual-12">[12]</a></sup>.
</p><p>In certain places, BD Medical also offers consulting and analytics related services. BD Medical's Consulting services are primarily targeted at hospitals, healthcare systems and networks of healthcare providers.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="BD_Life_Sciences">BD Life Sciences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=BD_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: BD Life Sciences" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>BD Life Sciences delivers innovative solutions from discovery to diagnosis, continually advancing science and clinical outcomes across infectious disease and cancer. 
</p><p>Business units include Diabetes Care​, Medication Delivery Solutions​, Medication Management Solutions​, and Pharmaceutical Systems.
</p><p>Offerings include preanalytical solutions for sample management; immunology research solutions, including flow cytometry and multiomics tools; microbiology and molecular diagnostics; lab automation and informatics solutions; and differentiated reagents and assays. 
</p><p><br></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="BD_Interventional">BD Interventional</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=BD_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: BD Interventional" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>BD Interventional focuses on developing innovative surgical, endovascular, urological and critical care interventions that not only meet clinical needs but also deliver value to health systems and improve patients’ lives.  Business units include Peripheral Intervention, Surgery​, Urology and Critical Care
</p><p><br>
BD Biosciences designs, manufactures, and sells fluorescence-activated cell sorters and analyzers, <a href="/wiki/Monoclonal" class="mw-redirect" title="Monoclonal">monoclonal</a> <a href="/wiki/Antibody" title="Antibody">antibodies</a>, and kits for cell analysis, reagent systems for life science research, cell imaging systems, laboratory products for tissue culture and fluid handling, and cell culture media supplements for <a href="/wiki/Biopharmaceutical" title="Biopharmaceutical">biopharmaceutical</a> manufacturing. BD Biosciences serves the following customers: research and clinical laboratories, academic and government institutions, pharmaceutical and <a href="/wiki/Biotechnology" title="Biotechnology">biotechnology</a> companies, hospitals, and blood banks.<sup id="cite_ref-annual_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-annual-12">[12]</a></sup> The company's line of plastic conical screwtop <a href="/wiki/Test_tube" title="Test tube">test tubes</a>, known as 'Falcon tubes', is popular and the term is sometimes used as a generic term for such tubes.<sup id="cite_ref-BD_Falcon_Tubes_and_Pipets_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BD_Falcon_Tubes_and_Pipets-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DeLongBurkhart2013_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DeLongBurkhart2013-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Anti-competitive_practices">Anti-competitive practices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=BD_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Anti-competitive practices" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, BD agreed to pay out $100 million to settle allegations from competitor <a href="/w/index.php?title=Retractable_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Retractable Technologies (page does not exist)">Retractable Technologies</a> that it had engaged in anti-competitive behavior to prevent the distribution of Retractable's syringes, which are designed to prevent <a href="/wiki/Needlestick_injury" title="Needlestick injury">needlestick injury</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The lawsuit touched off a series of legal conflicts between the companies. Retractable would accuse BD of patent infringement after BD released a retractable needle of its own. Later Retractable would claim BD was falsely advertising its own version retractable needs as being the “world’s sharpest needle”.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The debate between the two companies was partially the basis for the 2011 movie <i><a href="/wiki/Puncture_(film)" title="Puncture (film)">Puncture</a></i>.
</p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_track_record">Environmental track record</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=BD_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Environmental track record" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>As of February 2010, BD was ranked 18th in the EPA Fortune 500 List of Green Power Purchasers.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> BD was also listed among the top 100 companies in <a href="/wiki/Newsweek" title="Newsweek">Newsweek's</a> 2009 Green Rankings<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> ranking of the 500 largest American corporations based on environmental performance, policies, and reputation. BD placed third in the health care sector and 83rd overall.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> In addition, BD has been a component of the <a href="/wiki/Dow_Jones_Sustainability_Indices" title="Dow Jones Sustainability Indices">Dow Jones Sustainability World Index</a> and the Dow Jones Sustainability North America Index for the four and five consecutive years, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2007_Discardit_II_incident_in_Poland">2007 Discardit II incident in Poland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=BD_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: 2007 Discardit II incident in Poland" data-section="15" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In mid-2007, the firm's Discardit II series of syringes numbered 0607186 was withdrawn from hospitals and other medical services around <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, about half a year after the discovery of remains of dark dust in some syringes, which were alleged to have been from this series.<sup id="cite_ref-dziennik_BDDiscardit_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dziennik_BDDiscardit-38">[38]</a></sup> The newspaper Dziennik Online claimed that other series such as 06022444, 0603266, and 0607297 were also suspected of being contaminated.  BD recalled and tested the syringes in question, and revealed sterile particulates in 0.013 percent of the products.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Legal proceedings related to the incident started in December 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-dziennik_BDDiscardit_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dziennik_BDDiscardit-38">[38]</a></sup> According to an article published in Dziennik on Monday, 21 April 2008, the prosecutor's office in Lublin discontinued the inquiry, because the disposable syringes were not dangerous.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_Q-Syte_Luer_and_IV_Catheter_partial_recall">2010 Q-Syte Luer and IV Catheter partial recall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=BD_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: 2010 Q-Syte Luer and IV Catheter partial recall" data-section="16" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In February 2010 BD announced a voluntary product recall of certain lots of BD Q-Syte Luer Access Devices and BD Nexiva Closed IV Catheter Systems. BD stated that the use of the affected devices may cause an air embolism or leakage of blood and/or therapy, which may result in serious injury or death. The approximately 2.8 million BD Q-Syte and 2.9 million BD Nexiva units containing 5 million BD Q-Syte devices that were recalled were distributed in the United States, Asia, Canada, Europe, Mexico, the Middle East, South Africa, and South America. The recall was initiated on Oct. 28, 2009 after BD received complaints of problems due to air entry through a part of the device. BD stated that the cause of the problem was manufacturing deviation and claimed that it corrected the problem. BD announced that it notified customers about the recall by letter and has been working with the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Food_and_Drug_Administration" title="Food and Drug Administration">Food and Drug Administration</a> and worldwide health agencies to coordinate recall activities.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_and_safety_issues">Health and safety issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=BD_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Health and safety issues" data-section="17" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>

They found repeat and serious violations of health and safety law that had resulted in two employees having partial finger amputations.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
