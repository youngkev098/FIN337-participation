more than 600,000 clients in a wide array of fields. Management consulting is also an integral part of their services. The company operates approximately 330 distribution facilities worldwide; providing service to over 90 countries.
</p><p>The company was founded by Herbert Irving, John F. Baugh and Harry Rosenthal in 1969.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The company became public on March 3, 1970. On July 20, 2009, <i><a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)">Fortune</a></i> magazine ranked Sysco No. 204 in the annual <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> companies in world based on sales volume. On May 3, 2010, <i>Fortune</i> ranked Sysco as the 7th largest Fortune 500 Company in Texas and 55th largest in the U.S. by total revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-money.cnn.com_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-money.cnn.com-4">[4]</a></sup> Sysco is also the largest non-oil related company in Houston and the third largest non-tech related company in Texas (behind <a href="/wiki/AT%26T_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="AT&amp;T Inc.">AT&amp;T</a> and  <a href="/wiki/Dell" title="Dell">Dell</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-money.cnn.com_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-money.cnn.com-4">[4]</a></sup> The company ranked No. 54 in the 2018 <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> list of the largest United States corporations by total revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2013, Sysco announced an $8.2 billion planned acquisition of its next-largest food distribution rival, <a href="/wiki/US_Foods" title="US Foods">US Foods</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Federal_Trade_Commission" title="Federal Trade Commission">Federal Trade Commission</a> challenged the acquisition as a violation of the <a href="/wiki/Clayton_Antitrust_Act" class="mw-redirect" title="Clayton Antitrust Act">Clayton Antitrust Act</a> that would substantially lessen competition. The court ruled that the combined company would likely reduce competition because it would control 75% of the U.S. food service industry, Sysco terminated its merger with US Foods.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sysco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>In 1981, SYSCO Food Services bought out the assets of Lankford Produce, based in <a href="/wiki/West_Pocomoke,_Maryland" title="West Pocomoke, Maryland">West Pocomoke, Maryland</a> and founded in 1964 by Stanley E. Lankford Jr., forming the Lankford-SYSCO division in the <a href="/wiki/Delmarva_Peninsula" title="Delmarva Peninsula">Delmarva Peninsula</a>, renamed Sysco Eastern Maryland in 2008.
</p><p>In 1988, Sysco acquired <a href="/wiki/CFS_Continental" title="CFS Continental">CFS Continental</a> which had recently been acquired from <a href="/wiki/Tate_%26_Lyle" title="Tate &amp; Lyle">Tate &amp; Lyle</a> through their purchase of <a href="/wiki/A._E._Staley" title="A. E. Staley">A. E. Staley</a> in Illinois.
</p><p>In 1996 Sysco was the third largest company in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>. It had over 30,000 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-HasselChap7_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HasselChap7-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 2002, Sysco purchased <b>SERCA Foodservices</b> and renamed it Sysco Canada. SERCA had been owned by the supermarket giant <a href="/wiki/Oshawa_Group" title="Oshawa Group">Oshawa Group</a>, who were acquired by <a href="/wiki/Sobeys" title="Sobeys">Sobeys</a> in 1998.
</p><p>In 2003, Sysco purchased <b>Asian Foods</b>, the largest Asian foods distribution in North America. The first group of Asian Foods joined Sysco Kansas City in 2006, followed by Asian Foods Chicago joining Sysco Chicago in Sep, 2009.
</p><p>In 2009 Sysco acquired <b>Pallas Foods</b>, the largest food distributor in Ireland. Continuing the expansion of their Irish offerings, in 2012 Sysco purchased Crossgar Foodservice for an undisclosed amount.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>On December 9, 2013, Sysco announced they would acquire US Foods for a total of $3.5 billion. But on June 24, 2015, US Federal Judge <a href="/wiki/Amit_Mehta" title="Amit Mehta">Amit Mehta</a> ruled that the combined Sysco-US Foods would control 75% of the U.S. food service industry and would stifle competition. On June 29, 2015, Sysco terminated its merger with US Foods.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Subsidiaries">Subsidiaries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sysco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subsidiaries" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>The SYGMA Network, Inc. is a wholly owned subsidiary of Sysco that provides food and non-food products to chain restaurants throughout the United States.
</p><p>SYGMA was founded in January 1984 when senior officers of Sysco Corporation started discussions for forming a new distribution company. The first products were shipped in May 1985 from SYGMA’s first distribution center in Oklahoma. In 1988, Sysco acquired CFS, increasing head count at the organization.
</p><p>SYGMA's customers include approximately 14,000 restaurants representing 26 concepts. Operating from 14 distribution centers, SYGMA is one of the largest chain distributors in the United States with sales over $6.7 billion. 186 million cases of product are delivered each year. SYGMA currently employs over 4,000 employees.
</p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sysco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Controversies" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>Beginning in the Summer of 2013, the <a href="/wiki/California_Department_of_Public_Health" title="California Department of Public Health">California Department of Public Health</a> was investigating a total of 21 sheds which were never regulated and never inspected.  This food included overnight drop-offs of chicken, pork, beef, bacon, and milk.<sup id="cite_ref-The-Packer-July15-2013_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The-Packer-July15-2013-12">[12]</a></sup>  An investigative report by <a href="/wiki/KNTV" title="KNTV">KNTV</a> recorded food sitting in these sheds for up to five hours, in temperatures as hot as 81 degrees Fahrenheit, before the food was delivered to hotels and restaurants.  An employee speaking anonymously stated that sheds in Spokane, Washington, had refrigerators which were not large enough, and that most of the time there were cases of frozen food sitting on the floor outside the refrigerators.<sup id="cite_ref-The-Packer-July15-2013_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The-Packer-July15-2013-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NBC-Bay-Area-Sept-18-2013_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NBC-Bay-Area-Sept-18-2013-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>On July 16, 2014, Sysco agreed to pay fines totaling $19.4 million in California and admitted to the allegations made by KNTV's news team and investigated by the California Department of Public Health.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
