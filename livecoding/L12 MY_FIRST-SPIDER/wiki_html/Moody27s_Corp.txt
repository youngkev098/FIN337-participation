<p><b>Moody's Corporation</b>, often referred to as <b>Moody's</b>, is an American business and financial services company. It is the <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a> for <a href="/wiki/Moody%27s_Investors_Service" title="Moody's Investors Service">Moody's Investors Service</a> (MIS), an American <a href="/wiki/Credit_rating_agency" title="Credit rating agency">credit rating agency</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Moody%27s_Analytics" title="Moody's Analytics">Moody's Analytics</a> (MA), an American provider of financial analysis software and services.

href="/wiki/John_Moody_(financial_analyst)" title="John Moody (financial analyst)">John Moody</a> in 1909 to produce manuals of statistics related to stocks and bonds and <a </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span id="History_of_Moody.27s"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_Moody's">History of Moody's</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moody%27s_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History of Moody's" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Moody's Corporation traces its history back to two publishing companies established by John Moody, the inventor of modern <a href="/wiki/Bond_credit_rating" title="Bond credit rating">bond credit ratings</a>. It was first published in 1900 by John Moody, nine years before he founded Moody's Corporation. Initially called <i>Moody's Manual of Industrial and Miscellaneous Securities</i>, it was later superseded by <i>Moody's Manual of Railroads and Corporation Securities</i>, then by <i>Moody's Analyses of Investments</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In 1900, Moody published his first market assessment, called <i>Moody's Manual of Industrial and Miscellaneous Securities</i>, and established John Moody &amp; Company.<sup id="cite_ref-Sinclair_05_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sinclair_05-6">[6]</a></sup> The publication provided detailed statistics relating to stocks and bonds of financial institutions, government agencies, manufacturing, mining, utilities, and food companies. It experienced early success, selling out its first print run in its first two months.
</p><p>By 1903, <i><a href="/wiki/Moody%27s_Manual" title="Moody's Manual">Moody's Manual</a></i> was a nationally recognized publication.<sup id="cite_ref-Moody's_History_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moody's_History-7">[7]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Panic_of_1907" title="Panic of 1907">1907 financial crisis</a> fueled several changes in the markets, including the creation of the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Reserve_System" class="mw-redirect" title="Federal Reserve System">Federal Reserve System</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Sinclair_05_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sinclair_05-6">[6]</a></sup> Meanwhile, Moody was forced to sell his business, due to a shortage of capital.<sup id="cite_ref-Moody's_History_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moody's_History-7">[7]</a></sup> Moody returned in 1909 with a new publication focused solely on railroad bonds, <i>Analysis of Railroad Investments</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-JEP_Spring_10_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JEP_Spring_10-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Yasuyuki_Litan_06_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Yasuyuki_Litan_06-9">[9]</a></sup> and a new company, Moody's Analyses Publishing Company.<sup id="cite_ref-Sinclair_05_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sinclair_05-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 1962, <a href="/wiki/Moody%27s_Investors_Service" title="Moody's Investors Service">Moody's Investors Service</a> was bought by <a href="/wiki/Dun_%26_Bradstreet" title="Dun &amp; Bradstreet">Dun &amp; Bradstreet</a>, a firm engaged in the related field of <a href="/wiki/Credit_bureau" title="Credit bureau">credit reporting</a>, although they continued to operate largely as independent companies.<sup id="cite_ref-Sylla_01_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sylla_01-10">[10]</a></sup> By the late 1990s, Moody's superior performance compared to its parent company brought investor pressure to separate the businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-Bond_Buyer_Dec_99_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bond_Buyer_Dec_99-11">[11]</a></sup> In December 1999, Dun &amp; Bradstreet announced it would spin off Moody's Investors Service into a separate <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">publicly traded company</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_Dec_99_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_Dec_99-12">[12]</a></sup> The spin-off was completed on September 30, 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-Airfinance_00_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Airfinance_00-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span id="Moody.27s_Investors_Service"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Moody's_Investors_Service">Moody's Investors Service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moody%27s_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Moody's Investors Service" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Moody%27s_Investors_Service" title="Moody's Investors Service">Moody's Investors Service</a></div>
<p>Moody's Investors Service is the <a href="/wiki/Bond_credit_rating" title="Bond credit rating">bond credit rating</a> business of Moody's Corporation, representing the company's traditional line of business and its historical name. Moody's Investors Service rates <a href="/wiki/Security_(finance)" title="Security (finance)">debt securities</a> in several market segments related to <a href="/wiki/Government_debt" title="Government debt">public</a> and <a href="/wiki/Commercial_paper" title="Commercial paper">commercial</a> securities in the <a href="/wiki/Bond_market" title="Bond market">bond market</a>. These include <a href="/wiki/Government_bond" title="Government bond">government</a>, <a href="/wiki/Municipal_bond" title="Municipal bond">municipal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Corporate_bond" title="Corporate bond">corporate bonds</a>; managed investments such as <a href="/wiki/Money_market_fund" title="Money market fund">money market funds</a>, fixed-income funds and <a href="/wiki/Hedge_fund" title="Hedge fund">hedge funds</a>; financial institutions including banks and non-bank finance companies; and asset classes in <a href="/wiki/Structured_finance" title="Structured finance">structured finance</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Moody's_Market_Segments_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moody's_Market_Segments-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>Moody's Investors Service's closest competitors are <a href="/wiki/Standard_%26_Poor%27s" title="Standard &amp; Poor's">Standard &amp; Poor's</a> (S&amp;P) and <a href="/wiki/Fitch_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Fitch Group">Fitch Group</a>. Together, they are sometimes referred to as the <a href="/wiki/Big_Three_(credit_rating_agencies)" title="Big Three (credit rating agencies)">Big Three</a> credit rating agencies. Moody's Investors Service and its close competitors play a key role in global <a href="/wiki/Capital_market" title="Capital market">capital markets</a> as a supplementary credit analysis provider for banks and other financial institutions in assessing the credit risk of particular securities.<sup id="cite_ref-FSB_Oct_10_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FSB_Oct_10-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>According to Moody's, the purpose of its ratings is to "provide investors with a simple system of gradation by which future relative creditworthiness of securities may be gauged". To each of its ratings from Aa through Caa, Moody's appends numerical modifiers 1, 2 and 3; the lower the number, the higher-end the rating. Aaa, Ca and C are not modified this way.<sup id="cite_ref-Moody's_Definitions_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moody's_Definitions-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IOSCO_03_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IOSCO_03-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span id="Moody.27s_Analytics"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Moody's_Analytics">Moody's Analytics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moody%27s_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Moody's Analytics" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Moody%27s_Analytics" title="Moody's Analytics">Moody's Analytics</a></div>
<p>Moody's Analytics is a subsidiary of Moody's Corporation established in 2007 to focus on non-rating activities.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> It performs economic research related to <a href="/wiki/Credit_analysis" title="Credit analysis">credit analysis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Performance_management" title="Performance management">performance management</a>, <a href="/wiki/Financial_risk_modeling" title="Financial risk modeling">financial modeling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Structured_analysis" title="Structured analysis">structured analysis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Financial_risk_management" title="Financial risk management">financial risk management</a>. Moody's Analytics also offers software and consulting services, including proprietary economic models and software tools, as well as <a href="/wiki/Professional_development" title="Professional development">professional training</a> for the financial services sector, particularly <a href="/wiki/Financial_risk_management" title="Financial risk management">risk management</a> accreditation.
</p><p>Moody's Analytics started in 1995 as a business unit providing <a href="/wiki/Quantitative_analyst" class="mw-redirect" title="Quantitative analyst">quantitative analysis</a> services, including credit risk assessment software and services, called Moody's Risk Management Service (MRMS),<sup id="cite_ref-Sinclair_05_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sinclair_05-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Airfinance_00_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Airfinance_00-13">[13]</a></sup> and grew through partnerships and acquisitions in the late 1990s and 2000s, expanding its client base and capabilities.<sup id="cite_ref-Airfinance_00_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Airfinance_00-13">[13]</a></sup> Acquisitions included KMV, Economy.com, Wall Street Analytics, Fermat International, Enb Consulting Ltd., The Institute of Risk Standards and Qualifications (iRSQ), <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Securities_Institute" title="Canadian Securities Institute">CSI Global Education Inc.</a><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>, and Bureau van Dijk<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Wikipedia_and_sources_that_mirror_or_use_it" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="This claim cites another Wikipedia article. Articles need references to reliable third-party sources. (January 2020)">circular reference</span></a></i>]</sup>.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span id="The_Moody.27s_Foundation"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Moody's_Foundation">The Moody's Foundation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moody%27s_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Moody's Foundation" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>In 2002, Moody's Corporation created a corporate philanthropy program, The Moody's Foundation, focused on educational initiatives in mathematics, economics and finance. The organization offers grants to 501(c)(3) non-profits and equivalent international organizations, accredited schools and some governmental organizations.<sup id="cite_ref-TMF_FAQs_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TMF_FAQs-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>Since 2006, its main program is the annual <a href="/wiki/Moody%27s_Mega_Math_Challenge" class="mw-redirect" title="Moody's Mega Math Challenge">Moody's Mega Math Challenge</a> (M<sup>3</sup> Challenge), a student academic challenge co-sponsored with the <a href="/wiki/Society_for_Industrial_and_Applied_Mathematics" title="Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics">Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics</a> (SIAM),<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_Apr_06_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_Apr_06-22">[22]</a></sup> in which several hundred teams of high school students use <a href="/wiki/Quantitative_analyst" class="mw-redirect" title="Quantitative analyst">quantitative analysis</a> and modeling to solve problems related to real-life financial topics such as <a href="/wiki/Social_security" title="Social security">Social Security</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Economic_Stimulus_Act_of_2008" title="Economic Stimulus Act of 2008">Economic Stimulus Act of 2008</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Teens_Apr_29_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Teens_Apr_29-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Trenton_May_09_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trenton_May_09-24">[24]</a></sup> Since 2010<sup id="cite_ref-FC_Nov_09_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FC_Nov_09-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span id="Moody.27s_Research_Labs"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Moody's_Research_Labs">Moody's Research Labs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moody%27s_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Moody's Research Labs" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<p>Moody's Research Labs, Inc. was a <a href="/wiki/Business_incubator" title="Business incubator">business incubator</a> focused on <a href="/wiki/Research_and_development" title="Research and development">research and development</a> specializing in <a href="/wiki/Financial_risk_modeling" title="Financial risk modeling">financial risk modeling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Risk_analysis_(business)" title="Risk analysis (business)">analysis</a>, focused on developing such products for use by other divisions of Moody's Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-MRL_Info_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MRL_Info-26">[26]</a></sup> Its president was Roger Stein.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In March 2011 <a href="/wiki/Moody%27s_Analytics" title="Moody's Analytics">Moody's Analytics</a> announced the release of a software program developed by Moody's Research Labs, the Mortgage Portfolio Analyzer, to assist portfolio managers in managing credit risk.<sup id="cite_ref-MPA_Mar_11_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MPA_Mar_11-28">[28]</a></sup>
Moody's Research Labs was dissolved in February 2012.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(6)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Lawsuit_Settlements">Lawsuit Settlements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moody%27s_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Lawsuit Settlements" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-6 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-6">
<p>October 2011 - Moody’s reached a settlement resolving claims by the state of Connecticut that the credit rating company unfairly gave lower ratings to public bonds.<sup id="cite_ref-October_2011_lawsuit_settlement_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-October_2011_lawsuit_settlement-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>July 2012 - Moody’s said it reached a settlement with stockholders in lawsuits filed over structured finance ratings.<sup id="cite_ref-July_2012_lawsuit_settlement_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-July_2012_lawsuit_settlement-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>April 2013 - Moody's reached a settlement avoiding what would have been their first jury trial over crisis-era ratings. The fourteen plaintiffs, were led by Abu Dhabi Commercial Bank and King County, Washington. They claimed lawsuits filed in 2008 and 2009 that Moody's misled them by allegedly inflating ratings on two so-called structured investment vehicles they purchased.<sup id="cite_ref-April_2013_lawsuit_settlement_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-April_2013_lawsuit_settlement-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(7)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moody%27s_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Acquisitions" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-7 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-7">
<p>In 2019, Moody's Corporation purchased the majority share in the California-based <a href="/wiki/Climate_risk" title="Climate risk">climate risk</a> data firm, <a href="/wiki/Four_Twenty_Seven" title="Four Twenty Seven">Four Twenty Seven</a> (427), that  "measures the physical risks" of "<a href="/wiki/Climate_change" class="mw-redirect" title="Climate change">climate change</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_Flavelle_20190725_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_Flavelle_20190725-32">[32]</a></sup> This acquisition is the "latest indication that global warming can threaten the creditworthiness of governments and companies" globally, according to a July 25, 2019 article in <i>The New York Times</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_Flavelle_20190725_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_Flavelle_20190725-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
