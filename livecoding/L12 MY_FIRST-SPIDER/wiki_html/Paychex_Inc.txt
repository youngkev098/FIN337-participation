<p><b>Paychex, Inc. </b>is an American provider of <a href="/wiki/Human_resources" title="Human resources">human resource</a>, <a href="/wiki/Payroll" title="Payroll">payroll</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Employee_benefits" title="Employee benefits">benefits</a> outsourcing services  for small- to medium-sized businesses.<a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://fitsmallbusiness.com/paychex-reviews-pricing/">[1]</a> With headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_New_York" title="Rochester, New York">Rochester, New York</a>, the company has more than 100 offices serving approximately 670,000 payroll clients in the U.S. and Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
In 2019, Paychex ranked in position 700 on the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> list of largest corporations by revenue<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>, and the company’s revenue for fiscal year 2020 is projected to exceed $4.1 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Paychex was founded in 1971 by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Golisano" title="Tom Golisano">Tom Golisano</a>, who started the company with only $3,000.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>The operation grew to include 18 franchises and partnerships, which were eventually consolidated into one private company in 1979. Paychex held an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> in 1983 and began trading on the <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ_Stock_Market" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ Stock Market">NASDAQ</a> (PAYX). <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
In the first quarter of fiscal year 2020, the company repurchased $171.9 million of its own stock<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
and currently has a <a href="/wiki/Market_capitalization" title="Market capitalization">market capitalization</a> of $30.155 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_acquisitions">Recent acquisitions</span></h3>
<p>In December 2018, Paychex closed on its acquisition of Oasis Outsourcing Acquisition Corporation for $1.2 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> At the time of the Oasis acquisition, Paychex had 650,000 payroll clients and 14,500 employees. Oasis, a <a href="/wiki/Professional_employer_organization" title="Professional employer organization">professional employer organization</a> (PEO) brought 8,400 PEO clients, 1,100 employees, and an annual revenue that exceeded $9 billion in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Following the acquisition of Oasis, Paychex provides HR outsourcing services to 1.4 million worksite employees.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Small_Business_Employment_Watch">Small Business Employment Watch</span></h3>
<p>Paychex and <a href="/wiki/IHS_Markit" title="IHS Markit">IHS Markit</a> launched a Small Business Jobs Index in 2014 that measures the  health of businesses employing 50 people or less.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup>
Today, the Paychex | IHS Markit Small Business Employment Watch shares small business jobs data going back to 2005 and wage data from 2011 to present.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup>
The Employment Watch is frequently used by financial experts, analysts, and journalists assessing the economic outlook.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup>
Paychex CEO Martin Mucci regularly appears in the media to provide analysis of Small Business Employment Watch data, as well as insight into what the health of Paychex as a company says about the health of small business and the economy in general.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
