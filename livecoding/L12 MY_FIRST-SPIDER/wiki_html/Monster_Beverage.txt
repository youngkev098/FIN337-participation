<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">"Hansen's" redirects here. For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/Hansen_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Hansen (disambiguation)">Hansen</a>.</div>
<p><b>Monster Beverage Corporation</b><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> is an American <a href="/wiki/Beverage_company" class="mw-redirect" title="Beverage company">beverage company</a> that manufactures <a href="/wiki/Energy_drink" title="Energy drink">energy drinks</a> including <a href="/wiki/Monster_Energy" title="Monster Energy">Monster Energy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Relentless_(drink)" title="Relentless (drink)">Relentless</a> and <a href="/wiki/Burn_(energy_drink)" title="Burn (energy drink)">Burn</a>. The company was originally founded as <b>Hansen's</b> in 1935 in <a href="/wiki/Southern_California" title="Southern California">Southern California</a>, originally selling juice products. The company renamed itself as Monster Beverage in 2012, and then sold their Hansen's juices and sodas and their other non-energy drink brands to <a href="/wiki/The_Coca-Cola_Company" title="The Coca-Cola Company">The Coca-Cola Company</a> in 2015.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monster_Beverage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Hansen's was founded in 1935.<sup id="cite_ref-hansens_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hansens-4">[4]</a></sup> In the 1930s, Hubert Hansen and his three sons began selling juice to film studios and retailers in Southern California under the Hansen's name. In the 1970s, Tim Hansen (the grandson of Hubert) developed and marketed a variety of sodas and juices, also under the Hansen's label.<sup id="cite_ref-story_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-story-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>The company became Hansen's Juices, and later The Fresh Juice Company of California. The plant that was opened in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> in 1946 was used until operations were moved to a new plant in <a href="/wiki/Azusa,_California" title="Azusa, California">Azusa, California</a> in 1993. The company filed for bankruptcy in 1988, and was acquired by the California CoPackers Corporation and renamed Hansen Natural Company. In 1998, the company moved from <a href="/wiki/Anaheim,_California" title="Anaheim, California">Anaheim, California</a> to <a href="/wiki/Corona,_California" title="Corona, California">Corona, California</a>. On January 5, 2012, shareholders agreed to change the name of the company from Hansen's Natural to Monster
Beverage Corporation, under the new ticker MNST. Shareholders also approved an increase in the number of authorized shares of common stock to 240,000,000 shares from 120,000,000 shares.<sup id="cite_ref-Shares_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shares-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2008, the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Food_and_Drug_Administration" title="Food and Drug Administration">Food and Drug Administration</a> ruled products containing <a href="/wiki/High-fructose_corn_syrup" title="High-fructose corn syrup">high-fructose corn syrup</a> could not be labeled "natural".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Shortly after, Hansen's Natural Corporation announced they had begun using cane sugar instead.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2009, Hansen brand Monster Energy sent a <a href="/wiki/Cease_and_desist" title="Cease and desist">cease and desist</a> letter to <a href="/wiki/Rock_Art_Brewery" title="Rock Art Brewery">Rock Art Brewery</a>, demanding the <a href="/wiki/Microbrewery" class="mw-redirect" title="Microbrewery">microbrewery</a> stop selling its new Vermonster beer, drop its pursuit of a federal trademark for the name, and pay Hansen's lawyer fees. This resulted in a boycott of all Hansen products by a few Vermont retailers.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2015, <a href="/wiki/The_Coca-Cola_Company" title="The Coca-Cola Company">The Coca-Cola Company</a> took ownership of Hansen’s juice products and sodas, Hubert’s Lemonade, Blue Sky sodas, 
Peace Tea and other non-energy drink brands, in exchange for their energy drink brands.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Sponsorship">Sponsorship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monster_Beverage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sponsorship" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p><a href="/wiki/NASCAR" title="NASCAR">NASCAR</a> announced on December 1, 2016 a multi-year deal that will make Monster Energy only the third entitlement sponsor in its <a href="/wiki/Monster_Energy_NASCAR_Cup_Series" class="mw-redirect" title="Monster Energy NASCAR Cup Series">premier series</a> history.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> However, this deal ended after the 2019 race season, and the series will be known as simply the "NASCAR Cup Series" from 2020 onward. The company is expected to continue sponsoring individual drivers, including <a href="/wiki/Kurt_Busch" title="Kurt Busch">Kurt Busch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ty_Gibbs" title="Ty Gibbs">Ty Gibbs</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Riley_Herbst" title="Riley Herbst">Riley Herbst</a> into the future.
</p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Natural_soda">Natural soda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monster_Beverage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Natural soda" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><i>Natural Soda</i> comes in twelve flavors: Original Cola, Ginger Ale, Key Lime Twist, Cherry Vanilla Creme, Grapefruit, Kiwi Strawberry, Vanilla Cola, Mandarin Lime, Creamy Root Beer, Raspberry, Mango Orange, and Pomegranate.<sup id="cite_ref-product_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-product-13">[13]</a></sup> All Hansen's sodas were made with <a href="/wiki/Cane_sugar" class="mw-redirect" title="Cane sugar">cane sugar</a>.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Diet_soda">Diet soda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monster_Beverage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Diet soda" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Their <i><a href="/wiki/Diet_soda" class="mw-redirect" title="Diet soda">Diet Soda</a></i> is available in ten flavors: Cola, Black Cherry, Peach, Kiwi Strawberry, Vanilla Cherry Créme,Tangerine Lime, Ginger Ale, Root Beer, Grapefruit, and Pomegranate. Hansen's diet soda is sweetened with <a href="/wiki/Acesulfame_potassium" title="Acesulfame potassium">acesulfame potassium</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sucralose" title="Sucralose">sucralose</a>. All diet sodas are OU Kosher certified.<sup id="cite_ref-hansens_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hansens-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (December 2016)">failed verification</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Drink_mixers">Drink mixers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monster_Beverage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Drink mixers" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Monster Beverage sells three flavors of <a href="/wiki/Drink_mixer" title="Drink mixer">soda mixer</a>: Tonic, Ginger Ale, and Club Soda.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Fruit_juices">Fruit juices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monster_Beverage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Fruit juices" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The company sells thirteen fruit juices in 64-ounce <a href="/wiki/Polyethylene_terephthalate" title="Polyethylene terephthalate">PET</a> bottles, several of which are available in smaller bottles for kids. These include Apple, Grape, White Grape, Pineapple, Apple Grape, Apple Strawberry, Orange, Cranberry Apple, Ruby Red Grapefruit, Pomegranate, Organic Apple White Grape, Organic Apple Wild Berry and Organic Apple. Hansen's Natural Organic Apple Juice is made solely from concentrates the company obtains from <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. Hansen's uses concentrates from China in other varieties of its juices, despite the availability of such juice concentrates made from fruit grown and processed in the United States, which grows an abundance of apples, as well as many of the other fruits used in Hansen's juice products. These juices contain 120% of the United States Recommended Daily Allowances (USRDA) for Vitamin C (except Pomegranate, which contains 100%).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In 2009, Monster Beverage introduced Hansen's Natural Lo-Cal 64-ounce juice cocktails, in four flavors. They are sweetened with <a href="/wiki/Truvia" title="Truvia">Truvia</a>.
</p><p>Hansen's fruit juice smoothies contain approximately 25% juice and provide 100% of the recommended daily adult intake of Vitamins A, C, and E. They are packaged in 11.5-ounce aluminum cans.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Juices_for_children">Juices for children</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monster_Beverage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Juices for children" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Hansen's Juice Blast line of children's juice drinks was launched in conjunction with <a href="/wiki/Costco" title="Costco">Costco</a> and sold in Costco stores. Hansen also has a Juice Slam line of children's juice drinks. Both lines are sold in 6.75-ounce boxes.
</p><p>In May 2001, Hansen's acquired the <a href="/wiki/Junior_Juice" class="mw-redirect" title="Junior Juice">Junior Juice</a> beverage business.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Hansen's Junior Juice is 100% juice, sold in 4.23-ounce cartons and targeted at toddlers and preschoolers. Certain flavors of Junior Juice have calcium added and all flavors contain 100% of the daily recommended allowance of Vitamin C. The brand was introduced in 1991 by <a href="/wiki/McCain_Foods" title="McCain Foods">McCain Foods</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and sold to the Pasco Beverage Group in 2000<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> before being sold again, to Hansen's. Hansen's replaced the original four mascots, Uncle Froggy, Emily the Mouse, Ronald Rabbit, and Nick the Fox,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> with <a href="/wiki/Clifford_the_Big_Red_Dog" title="Clifford the Big Red Dog">Clifford the Big Red Dog</a>.
</p><p>In July 2008, Hansen's introduced Hansen's Organic <a href="/wiki/Junior_Water" class="mw-redirect" title="Junior Water">Junior Water</a>, in 4.23-ounce packages aimed at children between two and six years old. The Hansen's Organic Junior Water line of flavored water contains 100% of the daily recommended allowance of Vitamin C and thirty calories per serving. Also in 2008, organic Junior Water was introduced. Hansen's later introduced Coconut Water Twist, containing <a href="/wiki/Coconut_water" title="Coconut water">coconut water</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-hansensjunior_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hansensjunior-18">[18]</a></sup> and Garden Twist, a combination of fruits and vegetables, which is the only Junior Juice variant to be sold in bottles.<sup id="cite_ref-hansensjunior_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hansensjunior-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_beverages">Other beverages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monster_Beverage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Other beverages" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><i>Smoothies</i>, <i>Rumba Energy Juice,</i> <i>Energade,</i> and <i>Energy Formula,</i> are minor products. Monster owns <a href="/wiki/Blue_Sky_Beverage_Company" title="Blue Sky Beverage Company">Blue Sky Beverage Company</a>, which manufactures several soft drinks. Monster produces the <i>Monster</i>, <i>Lost</i>, and <i>Rumba</i> <a href="/wiki/Energy_drink" title="Energy drink">energy drinks</a>, which were previously distributed in the United States by <a href="/wiki/Anheuser_Busch" class="mw-redirect" title="Anheuser Busch">Anheuser Busch</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On November 10, 2008, distribution of the Monster Energy and Monster Java products was turned over to <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola_Enterprises" title="Coca-Cola Enterprises">Coca-Cola Enterprises</a>.
</p><p>In August 2009, Hansen's introduced the SELF Beauty Elixir line, a low-calorie beverage containing vitamins, minerals, natural fruit &amp; botanical extracts, antioxidants and 30% fruit juice. The beverage contains 35 calories and a low amount of sodium per 8-ounce aluminum can.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Signature_Soda">Signature Soda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monster_Beverage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Signature Soda" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Hansen's <i>Signature Soda</i> line included Black Cherry, Vanilla Cream, Orange Cream, <a href="/wiki/Ginger_Beer" class="mw-redirect" title="Ginger Beer">Ginger Beer</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sarsaparilla_(soft_drink)" title="Sarsaparilla (soft drink)">Sarsaparilla</a> flavors.<sup id="cite_ref-hansens_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hansens-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Natural_and_diet_tea_sodas">Natural and diet tea sodas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monster_Beverage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Natural and diet tea sodas" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Hansen's Natural and Diet Soda flavors included: Black Cherry, Tangerine, Tropical, Orange Mango, Peach Mango, Peach, Cherry, Cranberry, Clear Root Beer, Vanilla Coffee, Low Cal Wildberry, Low Cal Cola, and Low Cal Creamy Cola.
</p><p>In 2006, Hansen's introduced a line of iced teas in 16-ounce PET bottles. Some varieties were sweetened with cane sugar and some were unsweetened. Flavors included Lemon Mint, Pomegranate, Ginger, and Tangerine. There were also three flavors of diet <a href="/wiki/Green_tea" title="Green tea">green tea</a> soda: Lemon Mint, Ginger and Tangerine. Each bottle of tea contained 75 mg of <a href="/wiki/Epigallocatechin_gallate" title="Epigallocatechin gallate">Epigallocatechin gallate</a>. Hansen's also made <a href="/wiki/Tea_bag" title="Tea bag">tea bags</a>.
</p>
