</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=ADP_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:ADP_Model_4500_timecard_reader.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/ADP_Model_4500_timecard_reader.JPG/220px-ADP_Model_4500_timecard_reader.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1536" data-file-height="2048"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 293px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/ADP_Model_4500_timecard_reader.JPG/220px-ADP_Model_4500_timecard_reader.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="293" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:ADP_Model_4500_timecard_reader.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An ADP Model 4500 timecard reader</div></div></div>
<p>In 1949 <a href="/wiki/Henry_Taub" title="Henry Taub">Henry Taub</a> founded Automatic Payrolls, Inc. as a manual payroll processing business with his brother Joe Taub. <a href="/wiki/Frank_Lautenberg" title="Frank Lautenberg">Frank Lautenberg</a> joined the brothers in the company's infancy. In 1957, Lautenberg, after successfully serving in sales and marketing, became a full-fledged partner with the two brothers. In 1961, the company changed its name to Automatic Data Processing, Inc. (ADP), and began using <a href="/wiki/Punched_card" title="Punched card">punched card</a> <a href="/wiki/Unit_record_equipment" title="Unit record equipment">machines</a>, check printing machines, and <a href="/wiki/Mainframe_computer" title="Mainframe computer">mainframe computer</a>s.  ADP <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">went public</a> in 1961 with 300 clients, 125 employees, and revenues of approximately $400,000 USD.<sup id="cite_ref-MakingITWork-2017_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MakingITWork-2017-5">[5]</a></sup> The company established a <a href="/wiki/Subsidiary" title="Subsidiary">subsidiary</a> in the United Kingdom in 1965. In 1970, Lautenberg was noted as being the <a href="/wiki/President_(corporate_title)" title="President (corporate title)">president</a> of the company.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">[6]</a></sup> Also in 1970, the company's stock transitioned from trading on <a href="/wiki/American_Stock_Exchange" class="mw-redirect" title="American Stock Exchange">American Stock Exchange</a> to trading on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">[6]</a></sup> It acquired the pioneering online computer services company Time Sharing Limited (TSL) in 1974 and <a href="/wiki/Cyphernetics" title="Cyphernetics">Cyphernetics</a> in 1975. Lautenberg continued in his roles as Chairman and CEO until elected to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a> from New Jersey in 1982.
</p><p>From 1985 onward, ADP’s annual revenues exceeded the $1 billion mark, with paychecks processed for about 20% of the U.S. workforce. In the 1990s, ADP began acting as a <a href="/wiki/Professional_employer_organization" title="Professional employer organization">professional employer organization</a> (PEO).  Around this time, the company acquired Autonom, a herman company,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and the payroll and human resource services company, GSI, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In September 1998, ADP acquired UK-based <a href="/wiki/Chessington_Computer_Centre" title="Chessington Computer Centre">Chessington Computer Centre</a> that supplied administration services to the <a href="/wiki/UK_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Government">UK Government</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>Kerridge Computer Co. Ltd., a dealer management systems (DMS) provider to auto dealers primarily in the UK was acquired in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2007, the ADP Brokerage Service Group was spun off to form <a href="/wiki/Broadridge_Financial_Solutions" title="Broadridge Financial Solutions">Broadridge Financial Solutions</a>, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-Automatic_Data_Processing-March-2007-8-K_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Automatic_Data_Processing-March-2007-8-K-10">[10]</a></sup> On February 27, 2017, Automatic Data Processing Inc. confirmed it was closing its office in west <a href="/wiki/Ann_Arbor" class="mw-redirect" title="Ann Arbor">Ann Arbor</a> on March 30, 2017. At the time, ADP stated that it had around 56,000 employees worldwide, and that the year prior it had revenues of $12 billion,<sup id="cite_ref-mlive_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mlive-11">[11]</a></sup> or $11.7 billion USD in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-Automatic_Data_Processing-2012-10-K_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Automatic_Data_Processing-2012-10-K-12">[12]</a></sup> Also in early 2017, ADP described itself as the "largest provider of HR services in North America, Europe, Latin America and the Pacific Rim".<sup id="cite_ref-mlive_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mlive-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2018, ADP acquired <a href="/wiki/WorkMarket" title="WorkMarket">WorkMarket</a>, a <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>-based software platform company that helps businesses manage freelancers, contractors, and consultants.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Divisions_and_spinoffs">Divisions and spinoffs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=ADP_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Divisions and spinoffs" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadridge_Financial_Solutions">Broadridge Financial Solutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=ADP_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Broadridge Financial Solutions" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2007, the ADP Brokerage Service Group was spun off to form <a href="/wiki/Broadridge_Financial_Solutions" title="Broadridge Financial Solutions">Broadridge Financial Solutions</a>, Inc. (<a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">NYSE</a>: <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.nyse.com/quote/XNYS:BR">BR</a>), removing about $2 billion USD from ADP's total yearly revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-Automatic_Data_Processing-March-2007-8-K_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Automatic_Data_Processing-March-2007-8-K-10">[10]</a></sup>  ADP distributed one share of Broadridge common stock for every four shares of ADP common stock held by shareholders of record as of the close of business on March 23, 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-Automatic_Data_Processing-April-2007-8-K_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Automatic_Data_Processing-April-2007-8-K-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="CDK_Global.2C_LLC"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="CDK_Global,_LLC">CDK Global, LLC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=ADP_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: CDK Global, LLC" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:ADPDealerServices2.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/ADPDealerServices2.JPG/250px-ADPDealerServices2.JPG" decoding="async" width="250" height="166" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="4928" data-file-height="3264"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 250px;height: 166px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/ADPDealerServices2.JPG/250px-ADPDealerServices2.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="250" data-height="166" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:ADPDealerServices2.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>ADP Dealer Services in Ontario</div></div></div>
<p>Formerly called ADP Dealer Services, CDK Global was formed October 1, 2014 and provides integrated technology services and solutions to over 27,000 automotive dealerships internationally, as well as vehicle manufacturers.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
In 2010, ADP acquired the automotive marketing company Cobalt.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>BZ Results (Automotive Dealer Services), winner of the 2006 “Innovative Company of the Year”,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> was purchased by ADP in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-adp_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adp-18">[18]</a></sup> At the time, BZ Results was valued at $125 million. On April 7, 2014, ADP laid off several Dealer Services associates in a reorganization and 3 days later announced plans to spin off the Dealer Services division as a standalone company.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On August 19, 2014, ADP Dealer Services announced that the name of the new company, post-spinoff, would be CDK Global (an acronym for Cobalt, Dealer Services, Kerridge).<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>Both S&amp;P and Moody's downgraded ADP to AA in April 2014, after the dealer services unit was spun off.<sup id="cite_ref-Mead_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mead-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>ADP ranked 243 on the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> list of the largest United States corporations by revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=ADP_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Controversy" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>ADP fired salesperson David Schwartz in 2018 after he questioned the legality of ADP's policies and then reported the company to the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission">U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission</a> and <a href="/wiki/Financial_Industry_Regulatory_Authority" title="Financial Industry Regulatory Authority">Financial Industry Regulatory Authority</a>. ADP fired Schwartz on July 18, 2018.  Since Schwartz's reporting these policies to the Federal Government, ADP sued the salesperson to gag him for "misappropriation of trade secrets" and "breach of duty of loyalty" among other claims and he countered with Whistleblower charges against ADP under the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&amp;URL=0100-0199/0112/Sections/0112.3187.html">Florida Whistleblower's Protection Act</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>   Among Schwartz’s claims was the allegation that ADP trains and pressures its salespeople to push <a href="/wiki/SIMPLE_IRA" title="SIMPLE IRA">Simple IRAs</a> rather than <a href="/wiki/SEP-IRA" title="SEP-IRA">SEP IRAs</a> "allowing ADP to hold billions of dollars and generate compounded interest on the money (client employer contributions each payroll period) while they hold it."  Another Schwartz claim was that ADP trains, incentivizes, and pressures its salespeople to send  "falsifying"<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> social security numbers of new client participants to governments on tax forms so that ADP collects payroll deposits from the client as soon as possible.
</p>
