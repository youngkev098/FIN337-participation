<p><b>Capri Holdings Limited</b> (formerly <b>Michael Kors Holdings Limited</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> fashion holding company, incorporated in the <a href="/wiki/British_Virgin_Islands" title="British Virgin Islands">British Virgin Islands</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> with principal executive offices in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and operational headquarters in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>. It was founded in 1981 by American designer <a href="/wiki/Michael_Kors" title="Michael Kors">Michael Kors</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The company sells clothes, shoes, watches, <a href="/wiki/Handbag" title="Handbag">handbags</a>, and other accessories. Michael Kors handbags have been a popular fashion trend among women of all ages. In 2015, the company had more than 550 stores and over 1500 in-store boutiques in various countries.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Michael_Kors" title="Michael Kors">Michael Kors</a></div>
<p>Founder Michael Kors enrolled at the <a href="/wiki/Fashion_Institute_of_Technology" title="Fashion Institute of Technology">Fashion Institute of Technology</a> in New York City in 1977, but dropped out after nine months.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Taking a job as a sales person at a boutique called Lothar's across from <a href="/wiki/Bergdorf_Goodman" title="Bergdorf Goodman">Bergdorf Goodman</a> on <a href="/wiki/57th_Street_(Manhattan)" title="57th Street (Manhattan)">57th Street</a> in <a href="/wiki/Midtown_Manhattan" title="Midtown Manhattan">Midtown Manhattan</a>, he later became both the designer and visual display head for the store.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capri_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Noticing the displays and garments, Bergdorf's fashion director <a href="/wiki/Dawn_Mello" title="Dawn Mello">Dawn Mello</a> asked if he would show his collection to Bergdorf Goodman's buyers.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In 1981, Kors launched his Michael Kors women's label at Bergdorf Goodman. In 1990, the company launched KORS Michael Kors as a licensee.
</p><p>In 1993, the company was forced into a <a href="/wiki/Chapter_11,_Title_11,_United_States_Code" title="Chapter 11, Title 11, United States Code">Chapter 11</a> filing, caused by the closure of the licensing partner for KORS Michael Kors. Putting the KORS line on hold, he designed for other labels before relaunching in 1997 with a lower-priced line, and in the same year was named the first women's <a href="/wiki/Ready-to-wear" title="Ready-to-wear">ready-to-wear</a> designer for French house <a href="/wiki/Celine_(brand)" title="Celine (brand)">Celine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 2002, Kors launched his menswear line. Kors left Celine in October 2003 to concentrate on his own brand via holding company Michael Kors Holdings Limited (MKH Ltd), relaunched with a majority $100M investment from Canadian fashion investor <a href="/wiki/Lawrence_Stroll" title="Lawrence Stroll">Lawrence Stroll</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>-based partner <a href="/wiki/Silas_Chou" title="Silas Chou">Silas Chou</a>, who had previously purchased <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Hilfiger_(company)" title="Tommy Hilfiger (company)">Tommy Hilfiger</a> in 1989.<sup id="cite_ref-businessoffashion.com_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessoffashion.com-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ejinsight.com_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ejinsight.com-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/John_D._Idol" title="John D. Idol">John D. Idol</a> was subsequently appointed CEO of the company, with a shareholding interest. The MICHAEL Michael Kors line was launched in 2004, which included women's handbags and shoes as well as women's ready-to-wear apparel.
</p><p>In 2011, Stroll and Chou led an <a href="/wiki/Initial_Public_Offering" class="mw-redirect" title="Initial Public Offering">Initial Public Offering</a> in MKH Ltd on to the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>, making them and Michael Kors billionaires.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In June 2018, Chou sold the last of his investment in Kors.<sup id="cite_ref-ejinsight.com_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ejinsight.com-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capri_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Operations" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>By 2014, the annual revenue for the company was $3.2 billion, with a <a href="/wiki/Net_income" title="Net income">net income</a> of $670 million.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> By April 2017, the company had 827 full-price or outlet stores and 133 licensed stores.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Since 2017, the company said that it would no longer use animal <a href="/wiki/Fur" title="Fur">fur</a> in any of its products.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In 2018, Michael Kors announced two new stores, one in <a href="/wiki/Waterloo,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Waterloo, New York">Waterloo, New York</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> as well as the <a href="/wiki/Fairview_Park_Mall" class="mw-redirect" title="Fairview Park Mall">Fairview Park Mall</a> in Kitchener, Ontario.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span id="Purchases:_Jimmy_Choo.2C_Versace"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Purchases:_Jimmy_Choo,_Versace">Purchases: Jimmy Choo, Versace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capri_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Purchases: Jimmy Choo, Versace" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>In July 2017, MKH Ltd bought <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Choo_Ltd" title="Jimmy Choo Ltd">Jimmy Choo Ltd</a> for £897 million.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In September 2018 MKH Ltd  announced a deal to take over <a href="/wiki/Versace" title="Versace">Versace</a>. The Italian fashion group is valued at $2.1 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On January 2, 2019 after closing the deal, the company was renamed to <b>Capri Holdings</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Trademark_protection">Trademark protection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capri_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Trademark protection" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<p>In Canada, there had been multiple counterfeit Michael Kors bags, wallets, and other items in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, Quebec. In an attempt to end the counterfeit production and to enforce trademark rights, the brand has commenced actions against a major network of counterfeit suppliers and vendors, and raided multiple locations throughout the Greater Montreal Area.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
