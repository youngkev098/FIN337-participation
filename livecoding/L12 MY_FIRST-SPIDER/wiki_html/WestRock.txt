<p><b>WestRock</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Corrugated_packaging" class="mw-redirect" title="Corrugated packaging">corrugated packaging</a> company. It was formed in July 2015 after the merger of <a href="/wiki/MeadWestvaco" title="MeadWestvaco">MeadWestvaco</a> and <a href="/wiki/RockTenn" title="RockTenn">RockTenn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> WestRock is the 2nd largest American packaging company. It is one of the world's largest paper and packaging companies with <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$</span>15 billion in annual revenue and 42,000 employees in 30 countries. The company is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlanta, Georgia">Atlanta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>, consolidating offices from <a href="/wiki/Norcross,_Georgia" title="Norcross, Georgia">Norcross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">Richmond</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_acquisitions">History and acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=WestRock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and acquisitions" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>MeadWestvaco stockholders received 0.78 shares of the combined company. Rock-Tenn stockholders chose either one share of the combined company or a specific cash amount for each of their shares.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Winning_Together.png" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Winning_Together.png/200px-Winning_Together.png" decoding="async" width="200" height="201" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="8833" data-file-height="8861"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 200px;height: 201px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Winning_Together.png/200px-Winning_Together.png" data-alt="" data-width="200" data-height="201" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Winning_Together.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Winning together with WestRock</div></div></div>
<p>In October 2015, WestRock purchased SP Fiber Holdings, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2016, the company acquired Cenveo Packaging for $105 million which manufactures folded cartons and litho-laminated display packaging like MiraFoil, coil foil, and low migration ink systems.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2017 WestRock purchased Star Pizza box, largest US manufacturer and distributor of pizza boxes.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2017, WestRock completed the sale of its dispensing systems operations for $1.025 billion to <a href="/wiki/Silgan_Holdings" title="Silgan Holdings">Silgan Holdings</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2017, the company acquired Multi Packaging Solutions International (MPS) for an enterprise value of $2.28 billion. MPS has 59 locations across North America, Europe, and Asia and will enhance Westrock's print, graphics, and design capabilities for more penetration in spirits, confectionery, beauty and cosmetics packaging.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2018, the company acquired Plymouth Packaging, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Later that month, WestRock announced that it would acquire rival pulp and paper company <a href="/wiki/Kapstone" title="Kapstone">Kapstone</a>. The deal is expected to close by the end of the third quarter of 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
The company ranked 194th on the 2018 <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> of the largest United States corporations by revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
