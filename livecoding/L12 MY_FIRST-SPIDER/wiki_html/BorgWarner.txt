</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Company_history">Company history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=BorgWarner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Company history" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The original Borg-Warner Corporation was formed in 1928<sup id="cite_ref-BW_History_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW_History-1">[1]</a></sup> by the merger of <b>Warner Gear</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-BW_History_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW_History-1">[1]</a></sup> which itself was founded by Thomas Warner in 1901,<sup id="cite_ref-BW_History_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW_History-1">[1]</a></sup> and <b>Borg &amp; Beck</b><sup id="cite_ref-BW_History_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW_History-1">[1]</a></sup> founded by Charles Borg and Marshall Beck in 1903,<sup id="cite_ref-BW_History_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW_History-1">[1]</a></sup> Marvel Schelber Carburetor Co founded in 1905 by George Schebler and the Mechanics Universal Joint Co.  The company was best known as the supplier of Warner Gear <a href="/wiki/Overdrive_(mechanics)" title="Overdrive (mechanics)">overdrive</a> units for <a href="/wiki/Automobile" class="mw-redirect" title="Automobile">cars</a> of the 1930s to the 1970s,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and as the developer of Studebaker's three-speed <a href="/wiki/Automatic_transmission" title="Automatic transmission">automatic transmission</a> introduced in 1950,<sup id="cite_ref-BW_History_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW_History-1">[1]</a></sup> along with '<a href="/wiki/Holley_Performance_Products" title="Holley Performance Products">Holley</a>' brand Borg &amp; Beck <a href="/wiki/Carburetor" title="Carburetor">carburetors</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BW_History_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW_History-1">[1]</a></sup> Ford took up development of the transmission in Studebaker's place in 1955. The early Ford automatics are derivatives of the Borg Warner.
</p><p>The Warner Gear name was still being used for the marine and industrial transmissions supplied by Borg Warner under the trade name 'Velvet Drive' into the 1980s. The 'Velvet Drive' production was transferred to Regal-Beloit Corporation in 1995, who trade as "Velvet Drive Transmissions".
</p><p>In the 21st century, the company changed the styling of its name from Borg-Warner to BorgWarner.
</p><p>The company is also notable for co-developing a <a href="/wiki/Variable-geometry_turbocharger" title="Variable-geometry turbocharger">variable-geometry turbocharger</a> with <a href="/wiki/Porsche" title="Porsche">Porsche</a>, the Variable Turbine Geometry (VTG) system, used in the <a href="/wiki/Porsche_997" title="Porsche 997">2007 911 Turbo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MotorAuthority_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MotorAuthority-12">[12]</a></sup> BorgWarner claims this technology will reach mainstream cars within 10 years.<sup id="cite_ref-MotorAuthority_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MotorAuthority-12">[12]</a></sup> From 2002, <a href="/wiki/Peugeot" title="Peugeot">Peugeot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Renault" title="Renault">Renault</a> became major customers for the company's turbochargers<sup id="cite_ref-BW_History_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW_History-1">[1]</a></sup> with the <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Group" title="Volkswagen Group">Volkswagen Group</a> on-board since 1999, choosing to exclusively use the BorgWarner K-Series turbochargers for their then-new <a href="/wiki/List_of_Volkswagen_Group_petrol_engines#1.8_20vT" title="List of Volkswagen Group petrol engines">1.8T power plant</a>.
</p><p>BorgWarner supplies wet clutches and hydraulic modules for <a href="/wiki/Dual-clutch_transmission" title="Dual-clutch transmission">dual-clutch transmission</a> systems.  With their DualTronic<sup id="cite_ref-BW_History_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW_History-1">[1]</a></sup> technology, BorgWarner has gained contracts with European automakers<sup id="cite_ref-BW_History_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW_History-1">[1]</a></sup> that want to use dual clutch transmissions.  The German automotive <a href="/wiki/Concern_(business)" title="Concern (business)">concern</a> Volkswagen Group is BorgWarner's largest customer (by sales),<sup id="cite_ref-Facts_and_Figures_8-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Facts_and_Figures-8">[8]</a></sup> and Europe is their largest market.<sup id="cite_ref-Facts_and_Figures_8-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Facts_and_Figures-8">[8]</a></sup> Light vehicles (<a href="/wiki/Automobile" class="mw-redirect" title="Automobile">automobiles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sedan_delivery" class="mw-redirect" title="Sedan delivery">car-derived vans</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Light_commercial_vehicle" title="Light commercial vehicle">light commercial vehicles</a>) account for 72% of BorgWarner's total sales.<sup id="cite_ref-Facts_and_Figures_8-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Facts_and_Figures-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>The company's world headquarters is in <a href="/wiki/Auburn_Hills,_Michigan" title="Auburn Hills, Michigan">Auburn Hills, Michigan</a>. <a href="/wiki/Robert_S._Ingersoll" title="Robert S. Ingersoll">Robert S. Ingersoll</a> formerly served as the company's CEO and Chairman.  BorgWarner's Powertrain Technical Center (PTC) is also based in Auburn Hills.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Borg-Warner_Trophy" title="Borg-Warner Trophy">BorgWarner Indianapolis 500 Trophy</a> is presented to the winner of the annual <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_500" title="Indianapolis 500">Indianapolis 500</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">auto racing</a> event; this has been provided by the company since 1936,<sup id="cite_ref-BW_Indy500_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW_Indy500-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> as a legacy of <a href="/wiki/Louis_H._Schwitzer" title="Louis H. Schwitzer">Louie Schwitzer</a> who won the first automobile racing event held at the facility, in 1909.<sup id="cite_ref-BW_History_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW_History-1">[1]</a></sup>
It was announced by the Izod Indycar Series that BorgWarner has been selected as the official supplier for the turbochargers to be used in the 2.2L turbocharged engine formula for the series which took effect in 2012<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.
On October 2, 2006, BorgWarner announced the purchase of the European Transmission and Engine Controls product lines from <a href="/wiki/Eaton_Corporation" title="Eaton Corporation">Eaton Corporation</a>.
</p>

<p>In 2002, BorgWarner Inc. was split into two key business divisions:<sup id="cite_ref-BW_History_1-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW_History-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<ul><li><b><a href="/w/index.php?title=BorgWarner_Engine_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="BorgWarner Engine Group (page does not exist)">BorgWarner Engine Group</a></b>:<sup id="cite_ref-BW_Profile_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW_Profile-7">[7]</a></sup> This is responsible for the <a href="/wiki/Research_and_development" title="Research and development">research and development</a> of the company's <a href="/wiki/Internal_combustion_engine" title="Internal combustion engine">internal combustion engine</a>-related components, along with operational efficiency, including fuel consumption, emissions and performance. Key products are <a href="/wiki/Turbocharger" title="Turbocharger">turbochargers</a> and other related engine operation components.<sup id="cite_ref-BW_Profile_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW_Profile-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/w/index.php?title=BorgWarner_Drivetrain_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="BorgWarner Drivetrain Group (page does not exist)">BorgWarner Drivetrain Group</a></b>:<sup id="cite_ref-BW_Profile_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW_Profile-7">[7]</a></sup> The fundamental core of this division is its automotive <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(mechanics)" title="Transmission (mechanics)">transmission</a> systems. These include <a href="/wiki/Manual_transmission" title="Manual transmission">manual transmissions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Automatic_transmission" title="Automatic transmission">automatic transmissions</a>, and the critically acclaimed <a href="/w/index.php?title=BorgWarner_DualTronic_transmission&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="BorgWarner DualTronic transmission (page does not exist)">DualTronic</a> <a href="/wiki/Dual_clutch_transmission" class="mw-redirect" title="Dual clutch transmission">dual clutch transmission</a>, which is more commonly known as <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Group" title="Volkswagen Group">Volkswagen Group</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Direct-Shift_Gearbox" class="mw-redirect" title="Direct-Shift Gearbox">Direct-Shift Gearbox</a>. Other responsibilities are its <a href="/wiki/Four-wheel_drive" title="Four-wheel drive">four-wheel drive</a> systems and transmission electronic control units.<sup id="cite_ref-BW_Profile_7-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW_Profile-7">[7]</a></sup></li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Core_products">Core products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=BorgWarner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Core products" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">

<p>On April 12, 2005, BorgWarner won a 2005 Automotive News PACE Award at a ceremony in Detroit. The award recognized automotive suppliers for superior innovation, technological advancement and business performance.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(7)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Motorsports">Motorsports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=BorgWarner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Motorsports" data-section="18" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-7 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-7">
<p>Since <a href="/wiki/2012_IndyCar_Series_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 IndyCar Series season">2012 season</a>, BorgWarner currently supplies the turbochargers for the <a href="/wiki/IndyCar_Series" title="IndyCar Series">IndyCar Series</a> with designation EFR7163. Since 1936 BorgWarner has also sponsored the <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_500" title="Indianapolis 500">Indianapolis 500</a>-winning trophy.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Each year, the winning driver is presented with a miniature replica during a reception in January, about seven months after the race. Prior to the trophy's inception, the <a href="/wiki/Wheeler-Schebler_Trophy_Race" title="Wheeler-Schebler Trophy Race">Wheeler-Schebler Trophy</a> was awarded to the leader at the 400-mile mark, but was retired when car owner <a href="/wiki/Harry_Hartz" title="Harry Hartz">Harry Hartz</a> claimed it three times. Since <a href="/wiki/2014_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2014 Formula One season">2014 season</a>, BorgWarner currently supplies turbochargers for <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault Sport Energy F1 V6 Turbo Power Units</a> as an expertisement of hybrid turbochargers. BorgWarner also supplied turbochargers for all <a href="/wiki/Renault_Sport_R.S._01" title="Renault Sport R.S. 01">Renault Sport R.S. 01</a> cars for both seasons of the  <a href="/wiki/Renault_Sport_Trophy" title="Renault Sport Trophy">Renault Sport Trophy</a>.
</p><p>The company sponsors the <a href="/wiki/Louis_Schwitzer_Award" title="Louis Schwitzer Award">Louis Schwitzer Award</a> and prize money.
</p>
