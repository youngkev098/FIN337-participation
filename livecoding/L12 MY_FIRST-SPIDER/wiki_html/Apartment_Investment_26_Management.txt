</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aimco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Aimco traces its roots to The Considine Company, formed in 1975 by <a href="/wiki/Terry_Considine" title="Terry Considine">Terry Considine</a>.
</p><p>Aimco was incorporated on January 10, 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>On July 29, 1994, the company became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p><p>In 1994, Aimco acquired Homecorp, a management company with 5,300 units based in <a href="/wiki/Montgomery,_Alabama" title="Montgomery, Alabama">Montgomery, Alabama</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In 1996, Aimco acquired Walters Management Company<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and the J.W. English Company.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>Aimco also acquired a portion of the general partnership of Balcor Asset Management resulting in the addition of 67 properties in 3 states.
</p><p>In 1997, Aimco acquired National Housing Partnership Incorporated, which owned 87,659 apartments.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In October 1997, Aimco acquired 8,175 apartment units from Winthrop.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 1998, Aimco acquired <a href="/wiki/Insignia_Financial_Group" title="Insignia Financial Group">Insignia Financial Group</a> in a $910 million transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 2000, the company acquired Oxford Realty Financial Group, which owned interests in 36,662 apartment units, for $314 million.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Also in 2000, the company acquired the Oxford Tax-Exempt Fund for $206 million.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In 2002, Aimco acquired Casden Properties, founded by <a href="/wiki/Alan_Casden" title="Alan Casden">Alan Casden</a>. The $1.5 billion acquisition included 17,383 <a href="/wiki/Apartments" class="mw-redirect" title="Apartments">apartments</a>, including 6,356 conventional apartment units located in <a href="/wiki/Southern_California" title="Southern California">Southern California</a> and 11,027 affordable apartment units in 25 states.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In 2003, the company was added to the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500_Index" title="S&amp;P 500 Index">S&amp;P 500 Index</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
