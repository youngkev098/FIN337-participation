<p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p>
<p><b>DuPont de Nemours, Inc.</b>, commonly known as <b>DuPont</b>, is an American company formed by the merger of <a href="/wiki/Dow_Chemical_Company" title="Dow Chemical Company">Dow Chemical</a> and <a href="/wiki/DuPont_(1802-2017)" class="mw-redirect" title="DuPont (1802-2017)">DuPont</a> on August 31, 2017, and then subsequent spinoffs of <a href="/wiki/Dow_Inc." title="Dow Inc.">Dow Inc.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Corteva" title="Corteva">Corteva</a>. Prior to the spinoffs it was the world's largest chemical company in terms of sales.

<p>Within 18 months of the merger the company was split into three publicly traded companies with focuses on the following: <a href="/wiki/Agribusiness" title="Agribusiness">agriculture</a>, materials science, and specialty products.<sup id="cite_ref-reumerg_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reumerg-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cne_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cne-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-miradio_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-miradio-4">[4]</a></sup> The agriculture division is named Corteva, the materials science spin-off is named Dow Inc., and the specialty products division is named DuPont.<sup id="cite_ref-dvisions_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dvisions-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Fettig" class="mw-redirect" title="Jeff Fettig">Jeff Fettig</a> is executive chairman of the company,<sup id="cite_ref-chair_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chair-6">[6]</a></sup> and Edward Breen is the CEO. The merger has been reported to be worth an estimated $130 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-reumerg_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reumerg-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cne_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cne-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-miradio_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-miradio-4">[4]</a></sup> With 2018 total revenue of $86 billion, DowDuPont ranked No. 35 on the 2019 <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> list of the largest United States public corporations.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=DuPont&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>On December 11, 2015, <a href="/wiki/DuPont_(1802%E2%80%932017)" title="DuPont (1802–2017)">"Old" DuPont</a> announced a merger with Dow Chemical Company, in an <a href="/wiki/Stock_swap" title="Stock swap">all-stock</a> transaction. The combined company, DowDuPont, had an estimated value of $130 billion, being equally held by both companies’ shareholders, while also maintaining its two headquarters. The merger of the two largest U.S. chemical companies closed on August 31, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-cne_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cne-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-miradio_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-miradio-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-blmberg_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blmberg-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>Both companies' boards of directors decided that following the merger, DowDuPont would pursue a separation into three independent, publicly traded companies: an agriculture, a materials science, and a specialty products company. 
</p>
<ul><li>The agriculture business—Corteva Agriscience<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>— unites Dow and DuPont's seed and <a href="/wiki/Crop_protection" title="Crop protection">crop protection</a> unit, with an approximate revenue of $16 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-Monsanto_heat_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Monsanto_heat-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li>The materials science segment—called Dow—consists of DuPont's Performance Materials unit, together with Dow's Performance Plastics, Materials and Chemicals, Infrastructure and Consumer Solutions, but excludes Dow's Electronic Materials business. Combined revenue for this branch totals an estimated $51 billion.</li>
<li>The specialty products unit—called DuPont— includes DuPont's Nutrition &amp; Health, Industrial Biosciences, Safety &amp; Protection and Electronics &amp; Communications, as well as Dow's aforementioned Electronic Materials business. Combined revenue for Specialty Products total approximately $12 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-Sell_buysiness_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sell_buysiness-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Pain_gain_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pain_gain-12">[12]</a></sup></li></ul><p>Advisory Committees were established for each of the businesses. DuPont CEO Ed Breen would lead the Agriculture and Specialty Products Committees, and Dow CEO Andrew Liveris would lead the Materials Science Committee. These Committees were intended to oversee their respective businesses, and would work with both CEOs on the scheduled separation of the businesses’ standalone entities.<sup id="cite_ref-Market_merger_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Market_merger-13">[13]</a></sup> Announced in February 2018, DowDuPont's agriculture division is named Corteva Agriscience, its materials science division is named Dow, and its specialty products division is named DuPont.<sup id="cite_ref-dvisions_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dvisions-5">[5]</a></sup> In March 2018, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Fettig" class="mw-redirect" title="Jeff Fettig">Jeff Fettig</a> would become executive chairman of DowDuPont on July 1, 2018, and <a href="/wiki/Jim_Fitterling" title="Jim Fitterling">Jim Fitterling</a> would become CEO of Dow Chemical on April 1, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In October 2018, the company's agricultural unit recorded a $4.6 billion loss in the third quarter after lowering its long-term sales and profits targets.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In 2019, DuPont completed its spin off from DowDuPont.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2020, DuPont announced that it is bringing back Edward D. Breen as its CEO after removing former Chief Executive Mark Doyle and CFO Jeanmarie Desmond less than a year after they assumed their roles. Lori D. Koch, previously head of investor relations, assumes the CFO position.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Regulation">Regulation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=DuPont&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Regulation" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>The <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> opened a probe to assess whether the proposed merger was in line with the EU's respective regulations. The Commission investigated whether the deal reduced competition in areas such as crop protection, seeds and petrochemicals.<sup id="cite_ref-European_Commission_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-European_Commission-18">[18]</a></sup> The closing date for the merger was repeatedly delayed due to these regulatory inquiries.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes_stock_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes_stock-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Trump_advisor_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trump_advisor-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>Ed Breen said the companies were negotiating possible <a href="/wiki/Divestiture" class="mw-redirect" title="Divestiture">divestitures</a> in their <a href="/wiki/Pesticide" title="Pesticide">pesticide</a> operations to win approval for the deal. As part of their EU counterproposal, the companies offered to dispose of a portion of DuPont's crop protection business and associated R&amp;D, as well as Dow's acrylic acid copolymers and <a href="/wiki/Ionomer" title="Ionomer">ionomers</a> businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-Push_back_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Push_back-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Concessions_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Concessions-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>The remedy submission in turn delayed the Commission's review deadline to April 4, 2017. The intended spins of the company businesses were expected to occur about 18 months after closing.<sup id="cite_ref-Concessions_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Concessions-22">[22]</a></sup> According to the <i><a href="/wiki/Financial_Times" title="Financial Times">Financial Times</a></i>, the merger was "on track for approval in March" 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-Syngenta_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Syngenta-23">[23]</a></sup> Dow Chemical and DuPont postponed the planned deadline during late March, as they struck an $1.6 billion asset swap with <a href="/wiki/FMC_Corporation" title="FMC Corporation">FMC Corporation</a> in order to win the <a href="/wiki/Antitrust" class="mw-redirect" title="Antitrust">antitrust</a> clearances. DuPont acquired the Corporation's health and nutrition business, while selling its herbicide and insecticide properties.<sup id="cite_ref-FMC_Deal_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FMC_Deal-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Chemical_and_Engineering_News_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chemical_and_Engineering_News-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>The Commission conditionally approved the merger as of April, 2017, although the decision was said to consist of over a thousand pages and was expected to take several months to be released publicly. As part of the approval, Dow must also sell off two acrylic acid co-polymers manufacturing facilities in Spain and the US. China conditionally cleared the merger in May, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-CWorld_EU_approval_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CWorld_EU_approval-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Chemical_and_Engineering_News_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chemical_and_Engineering_News-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Conditional_China_nod_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Conditional_China_nod-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=DuPont&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>According to former United States Secretary of Agriculture during the Clinton administration, <a href="/wiki/Dan_Glickman" title="Dan Glickman">Dan Glickman</a>, and former <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Nebraska" class="mw-redirect" title="Governor of Nebraska">Governor of Nebraska</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Johanns" title="Mike Johanns">Mike Johanns</a>, by creating a single, independent, U.S.-based and - owned pure agriculture company, Dow and DuPont would be able to compete against their still larger global peers.<sup id="cite_ref-Foreign_Competition_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Foreign_Competition-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>On the other hand, if <a href="/wiki/Monsanto" title="Monsanto">Monsanto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bayer" title="Bayer">Bayer</a>, the 1st and 3rd largest <a href="/wiki/Biotech" class="mw-redirect" title="Biotech">biotech</a> and seed firms, together with Dow and DuPont being the 4th and 5th largest biotechnology and seed companies in the world respectively, both went through with the mergers, the so-called "Big Six" in the industry would control 63 percent of the global seed market and 76 percent of the global agriculture chemical market. They would also control 95 percent of corn, soybeans, and cotton traits in the US. Both duopolies would become the "big two" industry dominators.
</p><p>The merger formed the world's largest chemical company in terms of sales.  DuPont is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_Delaware" title="Wilmington, Delaware">Wilmington, Delaware</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-splitforbes_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-splitforbes-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>Since the spin off, the company has adapted its marketing and branding in order to establish a new identity that is "fundamentally different" from DowDuPont. To that end, the company in November 2019 published a list of sustainability commitments to be achieved by 2030.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
