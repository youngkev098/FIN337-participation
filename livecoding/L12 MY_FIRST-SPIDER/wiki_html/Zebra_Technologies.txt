</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zebra_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Zebra was incorporated in 1969 as <b>Data Specialties Incorporated</b>, a manufacturer of high-speed electromechanical products. The company changed its focus to specialty on-demand labeling and ticketing systems in 1982 and became Zebra Technologies Corporation in 1986. Zebra became a publicly traded company in 1991. Since 2008, Zebra has outsourced the majority of its printer manufacturing to <a href="/wiki/Jabil_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Jabil Circuit">Jabil Circuit</a> in Guangzhou, China.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In 1998 Zebra Technologies  with Eltron International, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2000 Comtec Information Systems was ,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> followed in 2003 by the  of Atlantek, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 2004 the company expanded into RFID smart label manufacturing,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>  Retail Systems International the next year.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In the following years Swecoin, WhereNet Corp,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Proveo AG,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and Navis Holdings (later divested in 2011)<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> were .
