<p><b>Constellation Brands, Inc.</b>, a <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> company,<sup id="cite_ref-auto_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-3">[3]</a></sup> is an international producer and marketer of beer, wine and spirits. Constellation is the largest beer import company in the US, measured by sales,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and has the third-largest market share (7.4 percent) of all major beer suppliers.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It also has investments in medical marijuana.<sup id="cite_ref-cnbc18_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnbc18-6">[6]</a></sup> Based in <a href="/wiki/Victor,_New_York" title="Victor, New York">Victor, New York</a>, Constellation has about 40 facilities and </p><p>Constellation Brands is currently embroiled in a water rights dispute over a proposed manufacturing facility in the desert city of Mexicali, Baja California, Mexico. This water comes from the Colorado River, which not only provides Mexicali and the agricultural valley but is also piped to the mountain and coastal cities of Tijuana, Tecate, Rosarito and to some areas of Ensenada. <sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_overview">Historical overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Constellation_Brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Historical overview" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<dl><dd><ul><li><i>See 'Acquisitions and Divestments' section, below, for details of investments.</i></li></ul></dd></dl><p>The company was established in 1945 by <a href="/wiki/Marvin_Sands" title="Marvin Sands">Marvin Sands</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Finger_Lakes" title="Finger Lakes">Finger Lakes</a> region of New York as Canandaigua Industries, selling bulk wine to bottlers in the eastern United States.<sup id="cite_ref-newyorktimes_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newyorktimes-9">[9]</a></sup> In its first year, the company sold approximately 200,000 gallons of wine and had gross sales of $150,000.<sup id="cite_ref-companyheritage_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-companyheritage-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>The company was incorporated as Canandaigua Wine Company, Inc. in 1972 and went public in 1973.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Marvin's son <a href="/wiki/Richard_Sands_(businessman)" title="Richard Sands (businessman)">Richard Sands</a> became president in 1993 and CEO in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-newyorktimes_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newyorktimes-9">[9]</a></sup> In 1999, Marvin Sands died following a brief illness.<sup id="cite_ref-newyorktimes_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newyorktimes-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 2000, the company changed its name to Constellation Brands, Inc. to reflect the scope of the company and its range of brands.<sup id="cite_ref-companyheritage_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-companyheritage-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2007, <a href="/wiki/Rob_Sands" title="Rob Sands">Rob Sands</a> was named president and CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-grapecollective_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grapecollective-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, the company began investing in medical marijuana. In March 2019, Bill Newlands became CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Wine_fraud">Wine fraud</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Constellation_Brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Wine fraud" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In 2010, a French court in Carcassonne, France, convicted 12 wine traders and producers for selling fake <a href="/wiki/Pinot_noir" title="Pinot noir">Pinot noir</a> wine to buyers in the US, amongst them Constellation, in a <a href="/wiki/Pinot_noir_passing-off_controversy" title="Pinot noir passing-off controversy">scheme</a> that lasted from January 2006 to March 2008. Constellation later faced and settled a <a href="/wiki/Class_action_lawsuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Class action lawsuit">class action lawsuit</a> because it "should have known" that the wine was fake.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions_and_divestments">Acquisitions and divestments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Constellation_Brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Acquisitions and divestments" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Constellation_Brands_headquarters.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Constellation_Brands_headquarters.jpg/220px-Constellation_Brands_headquarters.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="2576" data-file-height="1932"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Constellation_Brands_headquarters.jpg/220px-Constellation_Brands_headquarters.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Constellation_Brands_headquarters.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Constellation Brands moved its headquarters to a 120,000 square feet (11,000 m<sup>2</sup>) building on 11 acres (4.5 ha) in <a href="/wiki/Victor,_New_York" title="Victor, New York">Victor, New York</a> in 2009</div></div></div>
<p>Constellation Brands has expanded by <a href="/wiki/Mergers_and_acquisitions" title="Mergers and acquisitions">acquiring</a> other drinks companies.
</p><p>In 1987, the company purchased the <a href="/wiki/Manischewitz" title="Manischewitz">Manischewitz</a> winery in <a href="/wiki/Canandaigua,_New_York" title="Canandaigua, New York">Canandaigua, New York</a>, and continues to license the Manischewitz name from R.A.B. Foods.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In 1998 it acquired <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Clark_plc" class="mw-redirect" title="Matthew Clark plc">Matthew Clark plc</a> in the United Kingdom, and after selling off its <a href="/wiki/Cider" title="Cider">cider</a> business, the <a href="/wiki/Gaymer_Cider_Company" title="Gaymer Cider Company">Gaymer Cider Company</a>, sold half of the business to <a href="/wiki/Punch_Taverns" class="mw-redirect" title="Punch Taverns">Punch Taverns</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>During the 2000s, there were multiple acquisitions, with some divestments later in the decade. Acquisitions included <a href="/wiki/Accolade_Wines" title="Accolade Wines">BRL Hardy</a> (Australia)<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Nikola_Nobilo" title="Nikola Nobilo">Nobilo</a> (New Zealand) in 2003; <a href="/wiki/Robert_Mondavi" title="Robert Mondavi">Robert Mondavi</a> Corp. for $1 billion in 2004;<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Vincor International, Canada's largest wine company, for $1.44 billion in 2006;<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Spirits Marque One (owner of Svedka Vodka) in 2007;<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and Beam Wines Estates, the wine operations of <a href="/wiki/Fortune_Brands" title="Fortune Brands">Fortune Brands</a> (which included several major brands such as Clos du Bois) for $885 million in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-cbuy_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbuy-20">[20]</a></sup> The company later moved to a more premium wine portfolio, divesting <a href="/wiki/Almaden_Vineyards" title="Almaden Vineyards">Almaden Vineyards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inglenook_Winery" class="mw-redirect" title="Inglenook Winery">Inglenook Winery</a>, and the Paul Masson winery in <a href="/wiki/Madera,_California" title="Madera, California">Madera, California</a>, in 2008,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> and its value spirits portfolio to the <a href="/wiki/Sazerac_Company" title="Sazerac Company">Sazerac Company</a> in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In 2013, Constellation acquired Grupo Modelo's US beer business from <a href="/wiki/Anheuser-Busch_InBev" class="mw-redirect" title="Anheuser-Busch InBev">Anheuser-Busch InBev</a>. The transaction included full ownership of Crown Imports LLC which provided Constellation with complete, independent control of all aspects of the US commercial business; a brewery in Mexico; exclusive perpetual brand license in the US to import, market and sell Corona and the Modelo brands and the freedom to develop brand extensions and innovations for the US market.<sup id="cite_ref-grapecollective_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grapecollective-12">[12]</a></sup> Constellation had formerly imported Corona and other Modelo brands to the United States and acquired the US rights to those brands as a part of an anti-trust settlement permitting Modelo's acquisition by Anheuser-Busch InBev. It now produces its own versions of those products for the US, with Modelo serving all other countries. In 2014, Constellation finalized a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Owens-Illinois" class="mw-redirect" title="Owens-Illinois">Owens-Illinois</a> and completed the acquisition of Anheuser-Busch InBev's glass production plant, located adjacent to the company's brewery in <a href="/wiki/Nava,_Mexico" class="mw-redirect" title="Nava, Mexico">Nava, Mexico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2015, Constellation announced the $1 billion acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Ballast_Point_Brewing_Company" title="Ballast Point Brewing Company">Ballast Point Brewing</a> of San Diego.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2016, Constellation announced the $160 million dollar acquisition of High West Distillery of <a href="/wiki/Park_City,_Utah" title="Park City, Utah">Park City, Utah</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2017, Constellation announced the acquisition of Funky Buddha Brewery of Oakland Park, Florida.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2017, Constellation agreed to pay about C$245 million ($191 million) for a 9.9% stake in <a href="/wiki/Canopy_Growth_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Canopy Growth Corporation">Canopy Growth Corporation</a>, a Canadian seller of medicinal-marijuana products.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> At the time of the agreement, Constellation became the first Fortune 500 company and the first major alcoholic beverage maker to take a minority stake in a marijuana business.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2018, <a href="/wiki/Accolade_Wines" title="Accolade Wines">Accolade Wines</a> (acquired under the name BRL Hardy) was sold to private equity firm <a href="/wiki/The_Carlyle_Group" title="The Carlyle Group">The Carlyle Group</a>. Constellation had already in 2011 reduced its holdings in Accolade to 20 per cent.<sup id="cite_ref-AFR2018_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AFR2018-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-InDaily2018_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-InDaily2018-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2018, the company announced that it would invest an additional US$4 billion in Canopy Growth Corporation in advance of the legalization of recreational <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_in_Canada" title="Cannabis in Canada">cannabis in Canada</a> in October that year. The investment will increase its share in the Canadian Company to 38%. Canopy Growth president Bruce Linton said the additional funds would be used for international expansion and that future marketing plans included products such as cannabis-infused beverages and sleep aids.<sup id="cite_ref-cnbc18_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnbc18-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2019, Constellation announced it had acquired a minority stake in Black Button Distilling, a craft spirits distillery in Rochester, NY.
</p><p>In April 2019, Constellation announced a deal to sell wine brands, including Clos du Bois and Mark West, to <a href="/wiki/E._%26_J._Gallo_Winery" class="mw-redirect" title="E. &amp; J. Gallo Winery">E. &amp; J. Gallo Winery</a> for $1.7 billion. <sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2019, Constellation announced it had acquired a minority stake in Montanya Distillers, a craft rum distillery in Crested Butte, CO and a majority stake in Nelson's Green Brier Distillery, a revival of the original Green Brier Tennessee Whiskey and Belle Meade Bourbon labels in Nashville, TN.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2019, the sale of <a href="/wiki/Ballast_Point_Brewing_Company" title="Ballast Point Brewing Company">Ballast Point Brewing</a> to Kings &amp; Convicts Brewery of Illinois was announced.  Terms were not disclosed.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> The transition is expected in 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
