</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Extra_Space_Storage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>In 1975, Kenneth Woolley built his first self-storage facility under the name Secure-It Mini Storage in <a href="/wiki/Billings,_Montana" title="Billings, Montana">Billings, Montana</a> with Bill Nielson.
</p><p>In 1977, Woolley, Nielson, and Richard S. Tanner formed Extra Space Development Co.
</p><p>In 1998, after 21 years of developing and selling storage properties across the U.S., Woolley partnered with Spencer F. Kirk – who previously retired after co-founding Megahertz, which was bought out by <a href="/wiki/USRobotics" title="USRobotics">USRobotics</a> and later <a href="/wiki/3Com" title="3Com">3Com</a>.
</p><p>Through the new partnership formed between Woolley and Kirk, Extra Space Storage went on to create several joint ventures partnerships including with <a href="/wiki/PGIM" title="PGIM">Prudential Real Estate</a>.
</p><p>In August 2004, the company became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>, raising $252 million.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ipo_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ipo-6">[6]</a></sup> Woolley was named CEO and Chairman of the Board.
</p><p>In July 2005, in a <a href="/wiki/Joint_venture" title="Joint venture">joint venture</a> with PGIM, Extra Space acquired Storage USA from <a href="/wiki/GE_Commercial_Finance" title="GE Commercial Finance">GE Commercial Finance</a> for $2.3 billion. The purchase added more than 400 additional properties and expanded Extra Space Storage's portfolio into 34 states. At the time of the purchase, it was the largest transaction made within the self-storage industry.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 2009, Kenneth Woolley stepped down as CEO and succeeded by Kirk, who had previously served as the Company's President and had been a Director on the Extra Space Storage board of directors since 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2013, the company acquired a 19-building portfolio in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> for $195.1 million.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2014, the company sold a facility in <a href="/wiki/Waterloo,_Iowa" title="Waterloo, Iowa">Waterloo, Iowa</a> to <a href="/wiki/U-Haul" title="U-Haul">U-Haul</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2015, the company acquired properties in <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin, Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2015, the company acquired SmartStop Self Storage for $1.4 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2016, the company opened its first facility in <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2016, the company acquired a property near the <a href="/wiki/Daniel_K._Inouye_International_Airport" title="Daniel K. Inouye International Airport">Daniel K. Inouye International Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Honolulu" title="Honolulu">Honolulu</a> for $29 million.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The company also opened a facility in <a href="/wiki/Newark,_New_Jersey" title="Newark, New Jersey">Newark, New Jersey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2016, the company acquired 2 buildings in <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2016, the company was added to the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500_Index" title="S&amp;P 500 Index">S&amp;P 500 Index</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>On December 31, 2016, Kirk announced his retirement. Effective on January 1, 2017, Joseph D. Margolis succeeded Kirk as the company's third CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2018, the company acquired 3 buildings in <a href="/wiki/Broward_County,_Florida" title="Broward County, Florida">Broward County, Florida</a> for $11.8 million.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2018, the company acquired a building in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Lauderdale,_Florida" title="Fort Lauderdale, Florida">Fort Lauderdale, Florida</a> for $24.7 million.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2019, the company leased 36 properties from <a href="/wiki/W._P._Carey" title="W. P. Carey">W. P. Carey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The company also acquired a 15-property portfolio in <a href="/wiki/Broward_County,_Florida" title="Broward County, Florida">Broward County, Florida</a> for $104 million.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
