</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pentair&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Pentair was founded in August 1966 as Pentair Industries, Inc., with the name derived from the Greek root word "penta-" (alluding to the company's five founders), and went public several months later.<sup id="cite_ref-timeline_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeline-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Mergers_and_acquisitions">Mergers and acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pentair&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mergers and acquisitions" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Pentair expanded its water product business, acquiring the Fleck Controls control valve business in November 1995. In August 1997, Pentair acquired the General Signal Pump Group.<sup id="cite_ref-Essef_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Essef-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 1999 under chairman, president, and chief executive officer, Winslow H. Buxton, Pentair made a series of acquisitions. In April 1999, PNR purchased Essef, the global leader in the manufacture of composite water tanks, pumps, filters, and other water equipment used in pools and spas, for the cash-equivalent price of approximately $312 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Essef_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Essef-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In August 1999, Pentair bought the DeVilbiss Air Power Company for $460 million in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> DeVilbiss makes air compressors, pressure washers and generators, which complemented to Pentair's professional <a href="/wiki/Pneumatic" class="mw-redirect" title="Pneumatic">Pneumatics</a> tools that were powered by air compressors such as <a href="/wiki/DeWalt" title="DeWalt">DeWalt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Porter-Cable" title="Porter-Cable">Porter-Cable</a>.
</p><p>In 2004, Pentair bought WICOR Industries, the former water systems subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin_Energy_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Wisconsin Energy Corporation">Wisconsin Energy</a>, for $850 million.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> WICOR made water pumps, filters, and pool equipment components under the Sta-Rite, SHURflo, and Hypro brands.
</p><p>In 2005, Pentair bought some of the assets of APW for $144 million, including McLean Thermal Management, Aspen Motion Technologies, and Electronic Solutions,<sup id="cite_ref-etimes_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-etimes-13">[13]</a></sup> all of which provide thermal management products<sup id="cite_ref-pentairthermalrussia_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pentairthermalrussia-14">[14]</a></sup> and integration services to the telecom, medical, and security industries. This broadened Pentair's technical products line to include markets outside of the traditional electrical and electronics businesses.
</p><p>In 2006, Pentair purchased Germany-based Jung Pumpen GmbH,<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fabw_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fabw-16">[16]</a></sup> which makes pumps and other products for <a href="/wiki/Industrial_wastewater_treatment" title="Industrial wastewater treatment">wastewater processing</a>. The deal was completed in early 2007 for $227 million.<sup id="cite_ref-sec_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sec-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>Other acquisitions in 2005 and 2006 included Acu-Trol, Inc.,<sup id="cite_ref-psn_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-psn-18">[18]</a></sup> Delta Environmental Products, selected assets of Krystal Klear, and a 70% stake in Beijing Jieming Tiandi Environmental Equipment (which was later renamed to Beijing Pentair Water Jieming), which produces <a href="/wiki/Fiberglass" title="Fiberglass">fiberglass</a>-reinforced pressure tanks and filters.
</p><p>The company has announced an agreement to acquire <a href="/wiki/Water_purification" title="Water purification">water filtration</a> and separation firm Porous Media for approximately $225 million. It has said that it plans to continue an active course of acquisitions, especially to grow its business outside the American market.
</p><p>In 2012, Pentair Inc. (PNR) and Tyco International Ltd. (TYC)’s flow control business formally combined to create Pentair Ltd. The new company is described as a global manufacturer of “water and fluid solutions, valves and controls, equipment protection and thermal management products". Tyco shareholders now own approximately 52.5% and former Pentair Inc. shareholders own approximately 47.5% of Pentair Ltd. Following this merger the new company was based in Schaffhausen, Switzerland, with 30,000 employees and 100 manufacturing plants in 30 countries. Currently the headquarters are in Worsley, United Kingdom.
</p><p>In August 2016, Pentair announced that it has entered into an agreement to sell the Valves &amp; Controls business to <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Electric" title="Emerson Electric">Emerson</a>, an American diversified global industrial company based in St. Louis, Missouri.  The transaction was completed in April 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-pentaircom_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pentaircom-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Discontinued_operations">Discontinued operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pentair&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Discontinued operations" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Pentair has discontinued its Century/Lincoln and Lincoln Industrial service equipment operations. It also exited the tools business with the sale of its <a href="/wiki/Power_tool" title="Power tool">power tools</a> group (which had previously accounted for 40% of sales) in October 2004 to <a href="/wiki/Black_%26_Decker" class="mw-redirect" title="Black &amp; Decker">Black &amp; Decker</a> for nearly $800 million.<sup id="cite_ref-psn_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-psn-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Separation_of_nVent">Separation of nVent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pentair&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Separation of nVent" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In May 2018, Pentair created a sequel company to take control of its electrical division named <a href="/w/index.php?title=NVent&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="NVent (page does not exist)">nVent</a>. The electrical division of Pentair did over $2.1 billion in revenue in 2016. Pentair 2 went public on the NYSE on May 1, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
