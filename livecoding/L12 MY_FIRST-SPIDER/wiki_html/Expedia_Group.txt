</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedia_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Founded as a division of <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> in October 1996,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Expedia was <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spun off</a> into a public company in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 2001, <a href="/wiki/IAC/InterActiveCorp" class="mw-redirect" title="IAC/InterActiveCorp">IAC/InterActiveCorp</a> (known at the time as USA Networks Inc) bought a controlling interest in the company for an estimated $1.5 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 2003, it was fully purchased by IAC/InterActiveCorp.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2005, IAC spun off Expedia Inc., which owned its travel group of businesses, including <a href="/wiki/Expedia" title="Expedia">Expedia</a>, Expedia Corporate Travel (now Egencia), <a href="/wiki/TripAdvisor" title="TripAdvisor">TripAdvisor</a>, Classic Vacations, eLong, <a href="/wiki/Hotels.com" title="Hotels.com">Hotels.com</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hotwire.com" title="Hotwire.com">Hotwire.com</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2011, Expedia, Inc. spun out TripAdvisor Media Group, retaining its portfolio of travel transaction brands.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>On December 21, 2012, Expedia bought a majority stake in travel metasearch engine <a href="/wiki/Trivago" title="Trivago">Trivago</a> in a combined cash and stock deal worth €477 million (approximately $630 million).<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012, Expedia's Egencia unit acquired Via Travel, the largest travel company in Norway.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In 2014, Expedia acquired <a href="/wiki/Wotif.com" title="Wotif.com">Wotif.com</a> for $658 million.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2015, Expedia acquired <a href="/wiki/Travelocity" title="Travelocity">Travelocity</a> from Sabre Corp for $280 million. Expedia previously partnered with Travelocity to provide the technology platform for Travelocity's US and Canada points of sale.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, Expedia acquired <a href="/wiki/Orbitz" title="Orbitz">Orbitz</a> for $1.6 billion in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2015, Expedia and <a href="/wiki/Marc_Benioff" title="Marc Benioff">Marc Benioff</a>, CEO of Salesforce.com participated in an investment of $11M in <a href="/wiki/Wingz_(company)" title="Wingz (company)">Wingz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, Expedia bought <a href="/wiki/HomeAway" title="HomeAway">HomeAway</a> for $3.9 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2017, <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_Clinton" title="Chelsea Clinton">Chelsea Clinton</a> was named to the board of Expedia. In her newly named position, Ms. Clinton will get $45,000 a year in cash, plus $250,000 a year in stock vesting over three years.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2017, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Okerstrom" title="Mark Okerstrom">Mark Okerstrom</a> became the President and CEO of Expedia, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2018, Expedia, Inc. announced that it had changed its name to Expedia Group, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>On Dec. 4, 2019, Mark Okerstrom and Alan Pickerill resigned as CEO and CFO, respectively. At that time, Expedia Group's chairman, Barry Diller, took over day-to-day operations, while the Chief Strategy Officer, Eric Hart, became acting CFO.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Mergers_and_acquisitions">Mergers and acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedia_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mergers and acquisitions" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Expedia's first acquisition was Travelscape for <a href="/wiki/US%24" class="mw-redirect" title="US$">US$</a>89.75 million and VacationSpot.com for US$80 million on March 17, 2000. It subsequently acquired Classic Custom Vacations in March 2002 for $78 million. The company has made four divestments, in which parts of the company are sold to another company. On December 31, 2000, Technology Crossover Ventures acquired a 7% minority stake in Expedia for $50 million. <a href="/wiki/IAC_(company)" title="IAC (company)">IAC</a> acquired a 65% majority stake in the company on February 5, 2002, for $1.372 billion, and a year later, on August 8, 2003, USA Interactive acquired Expedia for $3.636 billion. Expedia was ultimately spun off as a separate entity with a value of $7.981 billion. The company made the most acquisitions in 2002 when it acquired three companies: Classic Custom Vacations, Metropolitan Travel, and Newtrade Technologies.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2015, Expedia acquired the online travel agency <a href="/wiki/Travelocity" title="Travelocity">Travelocity</a> from the tech firm <a href="/wiki/Sabre_Corporation" title="Sabre Corporation">Sabre Corporation</a> for $280 million.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>

<td style="text-align:center;"><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[47]</a></sup></td></tr></tbody></table><h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Carolina_sales_tax_suit">South Carolina sales tax suit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedia_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: South Carolina sales tax suit" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On January 18, 2011, Travelscape, a subsidiary of Expedia Inc. based in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley" title="Las Vegas Valley">Las Vegas</a>, was ordered to pay $6.3 million in back sales taxes to <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a> by the <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina_Supreme_Court" title="South Carolina Supreme Court">state's supreme court</a>. Travelscape argued that South Carolina's efforts to tax online retailers located out-of-state violate the <a href="/wiki/Dormant_Commerce_Clause" title="Dormant Commerce Clause">Dormant Commerce Clause</a>. In a unanimous ruling, the court determined that the company has a presence in the state sufficient to be required to collect sales tax. While Travelscape does not have physical facilities in South Carolina, the court determined that frequent sales trips made by its employees and the fact that the company furnished hotel rooms in the state establish its presence for tax purposes.<sup id="cite_ref-vegas_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vegas-53">[48]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Office_locations">Office locations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedia_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Office locations" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Expedia Group has its headquarters on <a href="/wiki/Elliott_Bay" title="Elliott Bay">Elliott Bay</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>.
</p><p>On April 2, 2015, Expedia announced that it would move its headquarters to the <a href="/wiki/Interbay,_Seattle" title="Interbay, Seattle">Interbay</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a> by the end of 2018, purchasing the <a href="/wiki/Amgen" title="Amgen">Amgen</a> campus on the <a href="/wiki/Elliott_Bay" title="Elliott Bay">Elliott Bay</a> waterfront for $228.9 million.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[49]</a></sup> As part of the move, Expedia is proposing an expansion of office space at the 41-acre (17 ha) campus to 1.23 million square feet (114,000 m<sup>2</sup>) to accommodate employees, designed by ZGF Architects.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[50]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[51]</a></sup></p><p>The move was later delayed to 2019, citing some logistical hurdles including the commute from the <a href="/wiki/Eastside_(King_County,_Washington)" title="Eastside (King County, Washington)">Eastside</a> to Seattle as well as traffic congestion near the campus.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[52]</a></sup> Incentives for employees to seek alternative forms of transportation were also announced, with the company exploring company shuttle services from <a href="/wiki/Park_and_ride" title="Park and ride">park and rides</a> in <a href="/wiki/Redmond,_Washington" title="Redmond, Washington">Redmond</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[53]</a></sup></p><p>In addition to the campus, Expedia gained ownership of the Helix bridge, now named Expedia Group Bridge, and a plot of land now used as a transportation hub for Expedia employees.
</p>
