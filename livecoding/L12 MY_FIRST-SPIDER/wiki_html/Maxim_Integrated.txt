The company is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/San_Jose,_California" title="San Jose, California">San Jose, California</a>, and has design centers, manufacturing facilities, and sales offices throughout the world. In the fiscal year 2019, it had US$2.31 <a href="/wiki/1,000,000,000" title="1,000,000,000">billion</a> in sales, 7131 employees, and 35,000 customers worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-2014-EPR_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2014-EPR-4">[4]</a></sup> Maxim is a Fortune 1000 company and its stock is a component of the <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ-100" title="NASDAQ-100">NASDAQ-100</a> stock market index. </p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maxim_Integrated&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Maxim was founded in April 1983.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Its nine initial team members had a variety of experience in semiconductors design and sales. The founding team included <a href="/wiki/Jack_Gifford" title="Jack Gifford">Jack Gifford</a>, an industry pioneer since the 1960s; Fred Beck, an IC sales and distribution pioneer; Dave Bingham, General Electric’s Scientist of the Year in 1982; Steve Combs, a pioneer in wafer technologies and manufacturing; Lee Evans, also a pioneer in CMOS analog microchip design and General Electric’s Scientist of the Year in 1982; Dave Fullagar, inventor of the first internally compensated <a href="/wiki/Operational_amplifier" title="Operational amplifier">operational amplifier</a> circuit; Roger Fuller, yet another pioneer in CMOS microchip design; Rich Hood, development director for some of the first microprocessor-controlled semiconductor test systems; and Dick Wilenken, who is acknowledged as the father of key analog switch and multiplexer technologies.<sup id="cite_ref-San_Jose_Magazine_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-San_Jose_Magazine-6">[6]</a></sup> Based on a two-page business plan, they obtained US$9 million in venture capital to establish the company.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In the first year, the company developed 24 <a href="/wiki/Second_source" title="Second source">second-source</a> products. After that, Maxim designed proprietary products that offered greater differentiation and higher profits.<sup id="cite_ref-San_Jose_Magazine_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-San_Jose_Magazine-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Maxim-logo-240-wt.png" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/91/Maxim-logo-240-wt.png/250px-Maxim-logo-240-wt.png" decoding="async" width="250" height="56" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="450" data-file-height="100"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 250px;height: 56px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/91/Maxim-logo-240-wt.png/250px-Maxim-logo-240-wt.png" data-alt="" data-width="250" data-height="56" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Maxim-logo-240-wt.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Logo prior to September 2012</div></div></div>
<p>In 1985, the industry was introduced to the MAX600, the first proprietary product to win an industry award and start decades of technical innovation. Maxim recorded its first profitable fiscal year in 1987, with the help of a product called <a href="/wiki/MAX232" title="MAX232">MAX232</a>, and posted a profit every year since it went public in 1988. Annual revenue reached $500 million in fiscal year 1998 and in fiscal 2011 totaled over  $2.47 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-about_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-about-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2005, Maxim became a Fortune 1000 company. Three years later, the company established its Chief Technical Office, and the number of patents rose by 50% over the next two years. In 2010, the company shipped its first analog product on a 300mm wafer.
</p><p>Maxim's product portfolio now includes power and battery management ICs, sensors, analog ICs, interface ICs, communications solutions, digital ICs, embedded security, and microcontrollers. 
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maxim_Integrated&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acquisitions" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<ul><li>1990: Purchased first wafer fabrication (fab) facility in Sunnyvale, California.</li>
<li>1994: Acquired <a href="/wiki/Tektronix" title="Tektronix">Tektronix</a> Semiconductor Division in <a href="/wiki/Beaverton,_Oregon" title="Beaverton, Oregon">Beaverton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a>, giving Maxim high-speed bipolar processes for wireless RF and fiber-optic products.</li>
<li>1997: Purchased an additional wafer fab from IC Works in <a href="/wiki/San_Jose,_California" title="San Jose, California">San Jose</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, to increase fab capacity.</li>
<li>2001: Acquired <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Semiconductor" title="Dallas Semiconductor">Dallas Semiconductor</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, to gain expertise in digital and mixed-signal <a href="/wiki/CMOS" title="CMOS">CMOS</a> design, as well as an additional wafer fab.</li>
<li>2003: Purchased submicrometre CMOS fab from <a href="/wiki/Philips" title="Philips">Philips</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio" title="San Antonio">San Antonio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, to ramp up capacity and support processes down to the 0.25-micrometre level.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li>2007: Purchased 0.18-micrometre fab from <a href="/wiki/Atmel" title="Atmel">Atmel</a> in <a href="/wiki/Irving,_Texas" title="Irving, Texas">Irving</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, approximately doubling fab capacity.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li>2007: Acquired <a href="/wiki/Vitesse_Semiconductor" title="Vitesse Semiconductor">Vitesse Semiconductor</a>’s Storage Products Division<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Springs,_Colorado" title="Colorado Springs, Colorado">Colorado Springs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a>, adding <a href="/wiki/Serial_ATA" title="Serial ATA">Serial ATA</a> (SATA), <a href="/wiki/Serial_Attached_SCSI" title="Serial Attached SCSI">Serial Attached SCSI</a> (SAS), and enclosure-management products to Maxim’s product portfolio.</li>
<li>2008: Acquired Mobilygen in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Clara,_California" title="Santa Clara, California">Santa Clara</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, to add <a href="/wiki/H.264" class="mw-redirect" title="H.264">H.264</a> video-compression technology to its portfolio.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li>2009: Acquired Innova Card, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/La_Ciotat" title="La Ciotat">La Ciotat</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, for the financial transaction terminal semiconductor market.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li>2009: Acquired two product lines from <a href="/wiki/Zilog" title="Zilog">Zilog</a>, Inc. Maxim purchased the Secure Transactions product line, featuring the Zatara family and the hardware portion of Zilog's Wireless Control product line, commonly found in universal remote controls.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
<li>2010: Acquired privately held <a href="/wiki/Teridian_Semiconductor" title="Teridian Semiconductor">Teridian Semiconductor</a> Corporation for approximately $315 million in cash. Teridian was a fabless semiconductor company located in <a href="/wiki/Irvine,_California" title="Irvine, California">Irvine</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, supplying <a href="/wiki/System-on-a-chip" class="mw-redirect" title="System-on-a-chip">systems on a chip</a> (SoC) for the <a href="/wiki/Smart_meter" title="Smart meter">smart meter</a> market.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li>
<li>2010: Maxim acquired the technology and employees of Trinity Convergence Limited, a software company based in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge" title="Cambridge">Cambridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">U.K.</a> Trinity was part of the ecosystem to bring <a href="/wiki/Skype" title="Skype">Skype</a> <a href="/wiki/Video_conferencing" class="mw-redirect" title="Video conferencing">video conferencing</a> to the <a href="/wiki/LCD_TV" class="mw-redirect" title="LCD TV">LCD TV</a> market.</li>
<li>2010: Maxim acquired Phyworks, a supplier of optical transceiver chips for the <a href="/wiki/Broadband" title="Broadband">broadband</a> communications market.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li>2011: Maxim acquired <a href="/wiki/SensorDynamics" title="SensorDynamics">SensorDynamics</a>, a semiconductor company that develops proprietary sensor and <a href="/wiki/Microelectromechanical_systems" title="Microelectromechanical systems">microelectromechanical systems</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li>2012: Maxim acquired Genasic Design Systems Ltd., a fabless RF chip company that makes chips for LTE applications.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></li>
<li>2013: Maxim acquired <a href="/wiki/Volterra_Semiconductor" title="Volterra Semiconductor">Volterra Semiconductor</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></li>
<li>2018: Maxim acquired Icron Technologies.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Temporary_delisting">Temporary delisting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maxim_Integrated&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Temporary delisting" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>From October 2007 to October 2008, Maxim's common stock was delisted from the Nasdaq Stock Exchange due to the company's inability to file financial statements related to stock option backdating. Maxim's stock was traded <a href="/wiki/Over-the-counter_(finance)" title="Over-the-counter (finance)">over-the-counter</a> and quoted on the <a href="/wiki/Pink_Sheets_LLC" class="mw-redirect" title="Pink Sheets LLC">Pink Sheets</a> until the company completed its restatement in 2008. Maxim's CFO Carl Jasper resigned due to an investigation into the issue by Maxim's board of directors.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>Maxim restated its earnings in September 2008 and was relisted on the Nasdaq Stock Exchange on October 8, 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
