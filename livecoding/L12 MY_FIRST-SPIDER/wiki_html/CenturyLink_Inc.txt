<p>CenturyLink's products and services focus on 3 key segments: Enterprise Business, Small Business, and Residential.<sup id="cite_ref-CenturyLink_Home_Page_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CenturyLink_Home_Page-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="CenturyLink_Enterprise_Business">CenturyLink Enterprise Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CenturyLink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: CenturyLink Enterprise Business" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>CenturyLink Enterprise Business provides products and services around Network, Cloud, Security, Voice, and Managed Services to enterprise customers.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
CenturyLink's Network services include SD WAN, MPLS/IPVPN, Hybrid WAN, Ethernet, Internet Access, Wavelength services, Dark Fiber, and Private Lines.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
CenturyLink Cloud provides Big Data as a Service, Internet of Things (IoT), Multi-Cloud Management, Private Cloud, Public Cloud, Bare Metal, SaaS Applications, and Cloud Connect.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
CenturyLink Security monitors more than a billion security events daily.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Services include: Cloud, Infrastructure, DDoS, Web Application, Email, and Web Security. The company also provides Analytics and Threat Management, Risk and Compliance support, and Threat Research Labs.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
CenturyLink offers voice products ranging from traditional landlines to Unified Communications and Collaboration (UC&amp;C) services and was recognized in 2018 by Frost &amp; Sullivan for “growth excellence in VoIP access and SIP Trunking”.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
CenturyLink's Managed Services include Advanced Professional Services, IT consulting, and strategic partnerships.<sup id="cite_ref-centurylink.com_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-centurylink.com-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="CenturyLink_Small_Business">CenturyLink Small Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CenturyLink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: CenturyLink Small Business" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>CenturyLink Small Business provides products and services around Internet, Phone, TV, and Cloud Applications.<sup id="cite_ref-centurylink.com_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-centurylink.com-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="CenturyLink_Residential">CenturyLink Residential</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CenturyLink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: CenturyLink Residential" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Fiber">Fiber</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CenturyLink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Fiber" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>CenturyLink Fiber is a <a href="/wiki/Fiber_to_the_x" title="Fiber to the x">fiber to the premises</a> service in the United States, providing broadband Internet and <a href="/wiki/Prism_TV" title="Prism TV">Prism TV</a> to a small and very slowly growing number of locations. The service was first introduced to <a href="/wiki/Omaha" class="mw-redirect" title="Omaha">Omaha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nebraska" title="Nebraska">Nebraska</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and next rolled out to <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> with plans for expansion to several other markets.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>  Unlike the company's existing high speed Internet deployments, which utilize <a href="/wiki/Fiber_to_the_x" title="Fiber to the x">fiber-to the node/neighborhood</a> to increase the speed of ADSL2+ speeds up to 20/2 Mbit/s, Vectored VDSL2+ speeds up to 140/10Mbit/s, in these markets CenturyLink now installs their fiber optic cable all the way to the home or business with speeds up to 1,000 Mbit/s download and 1,000 Mbit/s upload<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> using Calix Optical Network Terminals.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>  On Feb. 2, 2014, CenturyLink announced the availability of Gigabit fiber service to multi-tenant businesses in Salt Lake City and surrounding communities.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> On Aug. 5, 2014, CenturyLink announced the expansion of its gigabit fiber service to 16 additional markets.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> On Sep, 15, 2015, CenturyLink announced the expansion of its gigabit fiber service to residential and business customers in six additional states, increasing the company's service coverage to select areas of 17 states.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p><b>Gigabit Fiber markets</b>

On October 27, 2008, <a href="/wiki/Embarq" title="Embarq">Embarq</a> announced that it would be acquired by CenturyTel, Inc. in an all-stock transaction valued at about $6 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-Wall_Street_Journal_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wall_Street_Journal-67">[67]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Centurylink-Inc-Jun-2009-8-K_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Centurylink-Inc-Jun-2009-8-K-68">[68]</a></sup> CenturyTel's CEO <a href="/wiki/Glen_Post" title="Glen Post">Glen Post</a> would remain CEO of the merged company following the acquisition,<sup id="cite_ref-CenturyLink-Inc-Oct-2008-8-K_69-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CenturyLink-Inc-Oct-2008-8-K-69">[69]</a></sup> and remained CEO as of 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_70-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-70">[70]</a></sup>  Embarq was the former landline business of Sprint and served cities in 18 states, including Nevada, Florida, North Carolina, and Ohio.<sup id="cite_ref-centurylink1_71-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-centurylink1-71">[71]</a></sup> The deal made CenturyTel the third-largest landline phone provider in <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a> behind Verizon (through both <a href="/wiki/Verizon_Pennsylvania" title="Verizon Pennsylvania">Verizon Pennsylvania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Verizon_North" title="Verizon North">Verizon North</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Comcast" title="Comcast">Comcast</a>.</p><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Network-map.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/03/Network-map.jpg/220px-Network-map.jpg" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/03/Network-map.jpg/220px-Network-map.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="166" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div <p>On April 22, 2010, CenturyLink (at this point still legally known as CenturyTel, Inc.) announced it would acquire <a href="/wiki/Qwest" title="Qwest">Qwest</a> in a stock-for-stock transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-CenturyLink-Inc-Apr-2010-8-K_72-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CenturyLink-Inc-Apr-2010-8-K-72">[72]</a></sup> Under the agreement, CenturyLink would swap 0.1664 of its shares for each share of Qwest; as a result, CenturyLink shareholders prior to the merger wound up with 50.5% share of ownership in the combined company, while former Qwest shareholders gained the remaining 49.5%.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> The valuation of CenturyLink's purchase was $12 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-Wall_Street_Journal_67-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wall_Street_Journal-67">[67]</a></sup>  The merger was completed on April 1, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-Centurylink-Inc-Apr-2011-8-K_74-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Centurylink-Inc-Apr-2011-8-K-74">[74]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-komonews.com_75-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-komonews.com-75">[75]</a></sup></p><p>The addition of Qwest allowed CenturyLink to become the third largest telecommunications company in the United States, and the largest <a href="/wiki/Landline" title="Landline">landline</a> phone provider in the state of Colorado.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_70-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-70">[70]</a></sup>  The new company has 17 million access lines, 5 million broadband customers, and 1.4 million video subscribers across 37 states.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> The merger also made CenturyLink owner of one of the so-called <a href="/wiki/Regional_Bell_Operating_Company" title="Regional Bell Operating Company">Baby Bells</a>: Qwest included what was once <a href="/wiki/US_West" title="US West">US West</a>, the Baby Bell for much of the western United States.
</p><p>On July 15, 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-centurylink1_71-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-centurylink1-71">[71]</a></sup> CenturyLink acquired <a href="/wiki/Savvis" title="Savvis">Savvis</a>, Inc., a global provider of <a href="/wiki/Cloud_infrastructure" class="mw-redirect" title="Cloud infrastructure">cloud infrastructure</a> and hosted IT services for $2 billion, which represented all outstanding shares of Savvis common stock at $40 per share.<sup id="cite_ref-Wall_Street_Journal_67-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wall_Street_Journal-67">[67]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CenturyLink-Inc-Jul-2011-8-K_77-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CenturyLink-Inc-Jul-2011-8-K-77">[77]</a></sup> This acquisition allowed CenturyLink to provide expanded <a href="/wiki/Managed_hosting" class="mw-redirect" title="Managed hosting">managed hosting</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cloud_services" class="mw-redirect" title="Cloud services">cloud services</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></p><p>On December 4, 2012,<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> CenturyLink launched an integrated suite of cloud services called Savvisdirect.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup> Savvisdirect was an expansion of CenturyLink's portfolio of  <a href="/wiki/Savvis" title="Savvis">Savvis</a> cloud services and includes cloud application hosting,<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> cloud servers,<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup> cloud storage,<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup> and private cloud<sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup> for small businesses, IT administrators, and developers. CenturyLink later shuttered the savvisdirect subsidiary, consolidating their cloud service offerings internally.
</p><p>On October 16, 2012 Savvis acquired ITO Business Division of Ciber thereby adding managed services to the portfolio.<sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup></p><p>On June 14, 2013, CenturyLink announced the acquisition of AppFog, a <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Platform_as_a_Service" class="mw-redirect" title="Platform as a Service">Platform as a Service</a> used by over 100,000 developers to automate the deployment of software on public clouds such as Amazon Web Services and OpenStack.<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup></p><p>On November 19, 2013, CenturyLink announced the acquisition of Tier 3, a <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Infrastructure_as_a_service" title="Infrastructure as a service">infrastructure as a service</a> (IaaS), platform, and advanced cloud management company<sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup> based on <a href="/wiki/Cloud_Foundry" title="Cloud Foundry">Cloud Foundry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup></p><p>On December 8, 2014, CenturyLink announced the acquisition of DataGardens, Inc., a Disaster Recovery as-a-Service (DRaaS) provider based in Edmonton, Alberta, Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup></p><p>On December 11, 2014, CenturyLink announced the acquisition of Cognilytics, a predictive analytics and big data service provider.<sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup></p><p>On March 30, 2016, CenturyLink announced the acquisition of netAura, a security services company that focuses on cybersecurity, security information and event management (SIEM), analytics, and vulnerability management.<sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup></p><p>On January 9, 2017, CenturyLink announced the acquisition of Edison, New Jersey-based SEAL Consulting, a leading SAP services provider. This expanded CenturyLink's existing integrated SAP capabilities of hosting and managed services to include integration and software implementation.<sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup></p><p>On October 31, 2016, CenturyLink announced its intent to acquire <a href="/wiki/Level_3_Communications" title="Level 3 Communications">Level 3 Communications</a> in a deal valued at around $25 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt-l3centurylink_93-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-l3centurylink-93">[93]</a></sup> After securing the necessary regulatory approvals, CenturyLink closed the transaction on November 1, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup></p><p>On September 10, 2019, CenturyLink announced the acquisition of Streamroot, a provider of disruptive technology to improve video and static content delivery within bandwidth constrained areas. <sup id="cite_ref-95" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup></p>

<p>The earliest predecessor of CenturyLink was the Oak Ridge Telephone Company in <a href="/wiki/Oak_Ridge,_Louisiana" title="Oak Ridge, Louisiana">Oak Ridge, Louisiana</a>, which was owned by F. E. Hogan, Sr. In 1930, Hogan sold the company, with 75 paid subscribers, to William Clarke and Marie Williams, for $500. They moved the <a href="/wiki/Telephone_switchboard" title="Telephone switchboard">switchboard</a> to the Williams family front parlor. In 1946, the Williams' son, Clarke McRae Williams, received ownership of the family's telephone company as a wedding gift.<sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97">[97]</a></sup> In 1947, Clarke Williams learned the telephone company in <a href="/wiki/Marion,_Louisiana" title="Marion, Louisiana">Marion, Louisiana</a> was for sale. With a loan from business associate Joe Sydney Carter, Clarke purchased the Marion Telephone Company and eventually made it his base of operation as he grew his company through more acquisitions. CenturyLink still maintains offices in the former headquarters building.<sup id="cite_ref-98" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-98">[98]</a></sup> The company remained as a family-operated business until it became incorporated in 1968.<sup id="cite_ref-99" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99">[99]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-100" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-100">[100]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="1967-1999">1967-1999</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CenturyLink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 1967-1999" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>By 1967, Oak Ridge Telephone Company served three states with 10,000 access lines. That year, the company was incorporated as Central Telephone and Electronics. Clarke M. Williams served as president and chairman of the board.<sup id="cite_ref-101" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-101">[101]</a></sup> Between 1972 and 1975, Clarke gradually moved his headquarters from Marion to <a href="/wiki/Monroe,_Louisiana" title="Monroe, Louisiana">Monroe, Louisiana</a>, to access the larger employee base and to be near the airport.
</p><p>In 1971, the company was renamed as Century Telephone Enterprises, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-102" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-102">[102]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-103" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-103">[103]</a></sup> In 1972, Century Telephone acquired the La Crosse Telephone Corporation, of Wisconsin.<sup id="cite_ref-104" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-104">[104]</a></sup></p><p>On October 24, 1978, Century Telephone moved to the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> for the first time and began to trade under the ticker symbol CTL.<sup id="cite_ref-105" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-105">[105]</a></sup></p><p>Century Telephone performed well during the long bear market of the 1970s, as its stock rose over fourfold. The company provided telephone service in parts of 14 states by that time.<sup id="cite_ref-106" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-106">[106]</a></sup></p><p>In 1981, Century Telephone acquired <a href="/wiki/War_Telephone" title="War Telephone">War Telephone</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia" title="West Virginia">West Virginia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-107" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-107">[107]</a></sup></p><p>In 1982, Century Telephone's earnings peaked at $14 million, then declined in 1983 following the <a href="/wiki/Early_1980s_recession" title="Early 1980s recession">early 1980s recession</a>, and finally began to recover in 1984. However, the 1983 decline led to a loss of half the value of the company's stock in 1984.<sup id="cite_ref-108" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-108">[108]</a></sup></p><p>In 1985, both earnings and the stock price had recovered to new record highs. But by then, the company had accumulated $206 million in long-term debt.<sup id="cite_ref-109" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-109">[109]</a></sup> Century Telephone sold the operations of <a href="/wiki/War_Telephone" title="War Telephone">War Telephone</a> and two other companies to Colonial Telephone for $4.66 million.<sup id="cite_ref-110" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-110">[110]</a></sup></p><p>In 1987, the stock price nearly doubled from its low that year, and from its old 1982 record high, before falling back by a third in the <a href="/wiki/1987_stock_market_crash" class="mw-redirect" title="1987 stock market crash">1987 stock market crash</a>. Earnings had steadily grown each year from their 1983 low, and by 1987 reached nearly US$20 million.<sup id="cite_ref-111" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-111">[111]</a></sup></p><p>In 1989, Century Telephone Enterprises acquired Universal Telephone, Inc. for US$90 million in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-112" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-112">[112]</a></sup> During the late 1980s the company began a long trend in which it performed extremely well. The stock split three-for-two twice in this period, as earnings steadily grew, through the <a href="/wiki/1990-1991_recession" class="mw-redirect" title="1990-1991 recession">1990-1991 recession</a>, and by year-end 1991, they reached nearly US$40 million, double from what they had been in 1987.<sup id="cite_ref-113" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-113">[113]</a></sup></p><p>In 1992, Century Telephone acquired <a href="/wiki/CenturyTel_of_Ohio" title="CenturyTel of Ohio">Central Telephone Company of Ohio</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Centel" title="Centel">Centel</a> subsidiary, for $135 million. The acquisition served more than 65,000 access lines, and added 20% to Century's access line total. Also that year <a href="/wiki/Glen_F._Post,_III" class="mw-redirect" title="Glen F. Post, III">Glen F. Post III</a> became <a href="/wiki/Chief_Executive_Officer" class="mw-redirect" title="Chief Executive Officer">Chief Executive Officer</a> and, named Vice Chairman of the Board of Century Telephone.<sup id="cite_ref-114" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-114">[114]</a></sup></p><p>In 1993, Century Telephone revenues were over $425 million, up from about $350 million in 1992. 1993 earnings were nearly $80 million, up from about $70 million in 1992, excluding a nearly $16 million charge in 1992 due to the cumulative effect from an accounting change that year.<sup id="cite_ref-115" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-115">[115]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-116" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-116">[116]</a></sup> Also in 1993 the company split its stock three-for-two yet again. However, by that time, the company had accumulated nearly $520 million in long-term debt.<sup id="cite_ref-117" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-117">[117]</a></sup></p><p>By 1995, Century Telephone Enterprises had been added to the S&amp;P MidCap 400 index. Earnings had continued their steady growth trend through the 1994 economic <a href="/wiki/Soft_landing_(economics)" title="Soft landing (economics)">soft landing</a>, and by 1995 they reached over US$115 million. But the long-term debt continued to grow as well, reaching US$623 million that year.<sup id="cite_ref-118" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-118">[118]</a></sup></p><p>In 1997, Century Telephone acquired Delta Security Alarm Co., Inc. of Monroe, Louisiana, and its largest acquisition up until that time, <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Telecom" title="Pacific Telecom">Pacific Telecom</a>, doubling its size with 660,000 additional telephone access lines in 12 states.<sup id="cite_ref-119" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-119">[119]</a></sup> Pacific Telecom, Inc., would continue existence and was renamed CenturyTel of the Northwest, Inc.
</p><p>In 1998, Century Telephone split its stock three-for-two once again. The company acquired another Monroe, Louisiana security company, Century Protection Systems, and also acquired 89,000 access lines and 19 exchanges in 21 northern <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">Wisconsin</a> communities from <a href="/wiki/Ameritech" title="Ameritech">Ameritech</a>. The affected customers had formerly been served by <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin_Bell" title="Wisconsin Bell">Wisconsin Bell</a>. Ameritech's directory publishing operations serving those customers were also acquired.<sup id="cite_ref-120" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-120">[120]</a></sup></p><p>In 1999, the company was renamed as CenturyTel, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-121" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-121">[121]</a></sup> It split its stock three-for-two once more, and was added to the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">Standard &amp; Poor's 500</a> Index that year.<sup id="cite_ref-122" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-122">[122]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2000-Present">2000-Present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CenturyLink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2000-Present" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2000, CenturyTel acquired 230,500 <a href="/wiki/GTE" title="GTE">GTE</a> lines in Arkansas, and also bought 127,000 GTE lines in Missouri in partnership with Spectra Communications.<sup id="cite_ref-123" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-123">[123]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-124" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-124">[124]</a></sup> In Wisconsin, it acquired 133,000 additional lines, and 70,500 access lines for US$195 million from <a href="/wiki/Verizon" class="mw-redirect" title="Verizon">Verizon</a>. That year CenturyTel also bought 62,650 lines for US$170 million in partnership with <a href="/wiki/Telephone_USA_of_Wisconsin" title="Telephone USA of Wisconsin">Telephone USA of Wisconsin</a>, LLC.<sup id="cite_ref-125" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-125">[125]</a></sup></p><p>In 2001, CenturyTel acquired CSW Net, Inc. of <a href="/wiki/Russellville,_Arkansas" title="Russellville, Arkansas">Russellville, Arkansas</a>, and fended off a <a href="/wiki/Hostile_take-over" class="mw-redirect" title="Hostile take-over">hostile take-over</a> attempt by <a href="/wiki/ALLTEL" class="mw-redirect" title="ALLTEL">ALLTEL</a>, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-126" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-126">[126]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-127" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-127">[127]</a></sup></p><p>In 2002, longtime chairman Clarke M. Williams died. He was succeeded by then vice chairman Glen F. Post III. The company sold its wireless business to ALLTEL in order to concentrate on its rural landline business. Also that year CenturyTel acquired 300,000 Verizon access lines in Alabama, and 354,000 Verizon access lines in Missouri, bringing its total operations to 22 states with 2.5 million access lines.<sup id="cite_ref-128" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-128">[128]</a></sup></p><p>In 2003, CenturyTel acquired half ownership of SkyComm International, Inc. in <a href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Houston, Texas">Houston, Texas</a>, in March, to form a satellite teleport for its global Network Access Point (NAP) system. In June, CenturyTel also acquired the fiber network of Digital Teleport, Inc., a 5,700-mile (9,200 km) route running from Illinois to Texas, and adjoining states.<sup id="cite_ref-129" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-129">[129]</a></sup> CenturyTel renamed the network company LightCore.<sup id="cite_ref-130" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-130">[130]</a></sup> Closing out the year, in December CenturyTel acquired the Midwest Fiber Optic Network (MFON) from <a href="/wiki/Level_3_Communications" title="Level 3 Communications">Level 3 Communications</a>, Inc. in December, a stand-alone system in the same core central states as LightCore.<sup id="cite_ref-131" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-131">[131]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2004, it partnered with <a href="/wiki/EchoStar_Communications" class="mw-redirect" title="EchoStar Communications">EchoStar Communications</a> Corporation for <a href="/wiki/DISH_Network" class="mw-redirect" title="DISH Network">DISH Network</a> multi-channel <a href="/wiki/Digital_TV" class="mw-redirect" title="Digital TV">digital TV</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-132" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-132">[132]</a></sup> In September, CenturyTel began a relationship with <a href="/wiki/Cingular_Wireless" class="mw-redirect" title="Cingular Wireless">Cingular Wireless</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-133" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-133">[133]</a></sup></p><p>In 2005, CenturyTel began a wireless voice and data service, and bought a number of fiber networks in the central United States, from KMC Telecom Holdings, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-134" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-134">[134]</a></sup></p><p>In 2006, CenturyTel sold its Arizona assets to Hopi Telecommunications, Inc., bringing total operations to 23 states.<sup id="cite_ref-135" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-135">[135]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2007, CenturyTel acquired Madison River Communications, based out of Mebane, NC and parent company to four LECs (AL, GA, IL, and NC) as well as CLEC operations in IL, LA, and NC. The LECs included Mebtel Communications, a telephone company serving <a href="/wiki/Alamance_County,_North_Carolina" title="Alamance County, North Carolina">Alamance County, North Carolina</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-136" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-136">[136]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-137" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-137">[137]</a></sup> GulfTel Communications, based out of Foley, AL and serving Baldwin and surrounding counties;<sup id="cite_ref-138" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-138">[138]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-139" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-139">[139]</a></sup> Coastal Communications, based out of Hinesville, GA; and Gallatin River Communications, serving the Dixon, Galesburg, and Pekin areas of IL.
</p><p>In late 2007, the Customer Respect Group, an international research and consulting firm that focuses on how corporations treat their online customers, ranked CenturyTel the best among six leading communications providers.
</p><p>On June 2, 2009, a press release announced that the combined CenturyTel/Embarq entity would be called CenturyLink.<sup id="cite_ref-140" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-140">[140]</a></sup> Denver-based Monigle Associates was retained to formulate the new brand strategy. The acquisition was completed on July 1, 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-141" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-141">[141]</a></sup></p><p>On October 19, 2009, CenturyTel and Embarq brandings were retired, and all business was officially conducted under the CenturyLink banner, continuing to trade on the NYSE under the CenturyTel stock ticker CTL. The new corporate name, CenturyLink, Inc., did not become official until May 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-142" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-142">[142]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-143" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-143">[143]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism_and_controversy">Criticism and controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CenturyLink&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Criticism and controversy" data-section="15" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Federal_Communications_Commission" title="Federal Communications Commission">Federal Communications Commission</a> ordered CenturyLink to pay a record $16 million for failing to alert authorities of a preventable programming error that left nearly 11 million people in seven states without access to emergency services for six hours in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-144" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-144">[144]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-145" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-145">[145]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2018, CenturyLink faced criticism for requiring residential customers in Utah to, via <a href="/wiki/DNS_hijacking" title="DNS hijacking">DNS hijacking</a>, view and acknowledge a notice advertising its security and <a href="/wiki/Parental_control" class="mw-redirect" title="Parental control">parental control</a> software, before they could connect to the internet again. The provider claimed that this was required by a recently enacted state law, which requires all ISPs to inform users that they provide "the ability to block material harmful to minors". Bill sponsor and <a href="/wiki/Utah_State_Senate" title="Utah State Senate">Utah State Senate</a> member <a href="/wiki/Todd_Weiler" title="Todd Weiler">Todd Weiler</a> stated that the law did not require that service be disrupted until the notice is acknowledged; the law only requires that this notice be delivered in a "conspicuous" manner (such as an advertisement within a bill or invoice) and does not require disruption of service.<sup id="cite_ref-146" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-146">[146]</a></sup></p><p>On December 27, 2018, a “nationwide outage” caused <a href="/wiki/9-1-1" title="9-1-1">9-1-1</a> service to be disrupted across the country. The <a href="/wiki/Federal_Communications_Commission" title="Federal Communications Commission">Federal Communications Commission</a> says it will investigate.<sup id="cite_ref-147" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-147">[147]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-148" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-148">[148]</a></sup> In some areas the outage lasted nearly twelve hours and was the third shutdown of the year following outages in April and November 2018. ATM and point of sale credit card machines were also widely affected.<sup id="cite_ref-ind_149-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ind-149">[149]</a></sup></p>
