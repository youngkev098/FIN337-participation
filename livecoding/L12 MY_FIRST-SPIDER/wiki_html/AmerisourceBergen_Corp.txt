<p><b>AmerisourceBergen Corporation</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Wholesale" class="mw-redirect" title="Wholesale">drug wholesale</a> company that was formed by the merger of Bergen Brunswig and AmeriSource in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-AmerisourceBergen-Aug-2001-8-K_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AmerisourceBergen-Aug-2001-8-K-2">[2]</a></sup> They provide drug distribution and related services designed to reduce costs and improve patient outcomes, distribute a line of brand name and generic <a href="/wiki/Pharmaceuticals" class="mw-redirect" title="Pharmaceuticals">pharmaceuticals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Over-the-counter_drug" title="Over-the-counter drug">over-the-counter</a> (OTC) health care products and home health care supplies and equipment to a wide variety of health care providers located throughout the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, including acute care <a href="/wiki/Hospitals" class="mw-redirect" title="Hospitals">hospitals</a> and <a href="/wiki/Health_systems" class="mw-redirect" title="Health systems">health systems</a>, independent and chain retail <a href="/wiki/Pharmacies" class="mw-redirect" title="Pharmacies">pharmacies</a>, mail-order facilities, <a href="/wiki/Physicians" class="mw-redirect" title="Physicians">physicians</a>, clinics and other alternate site facilities, as well as skilled nursing and <a href="/wiki/Assisted_living" title="Assisted living">assisted living</a> centers. They also provide pharmaceuticals and pharmacy services to long-term care, workers' compensation and specialty drug patients.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=AmerisourceBergen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>AmerisourceBergen was formed in 2001 following the merger of AmeriSource Health Corporation and Bergen Brunswig Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>  AmeriSource and Bergen were both successful pharmaceutical distributors that served similar markets but covered somewhat different geographies.  Bringing the two together gave the company a strong national presence that was geared especially towards serving independent community pharmacies, regional retail chain pharmacies, hospitals, physician offices, clinics and other alternate care facilities.
</p><p>AmerisourceBergen has 26 pharmaceutical distribution centers in the US, nine distribution centers in Canada, four specialty distribution centers in the US, and over 1 million square feet of packaging production capacity in the US and the UK. With the addition of World Courier<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>, the largest specialty courier company in the world, over 150 company-owned offices around the globe were added to the company.
</p><p>AmerisourceBergen is among several distributors that have been sued by <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia" title="West Virginia">West Virginian</a> governments for contributing to the <a href="/wiki/Opioid_epidemic" title="Opioid epidemic">Opioid epidemic</a>, by shipping inordinate amounts of pain medication into the state.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>On January 3, 2018, AmerisourceBergen acquired H. D. Smith, the largest privately-held national pharmaceutical wholesaler in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Finances">Finances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=AmerisourceBergen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Finances" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">

<p>AmerisourceBergen operates its pharmaceutical distribution business under four primary units: AmerisourceBergen Drug Corporation (ABDC), AmerisourceBergen Specialty Group (ABSG), AmerisourceBergen Consulting Services (ABCS) and World Courier. In March 2016 Walgreens Boots Alliance Inc. announced it would exercise an option to purchase 22.7 million shares of AmerisourceBergen stock and thereby control 15% of the company.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Good_Neighbor_Pharmacy">Good Neighbor Pharmacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=AmerisourceBergen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Good Neighbor Pharmacy" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p><a href="/wiki/Good_Neighbor_Pharmacy" title="Good Neighbor Pharmacy">Good Neighbor Pharmacy</a> is an American retailers' cooperative network of more than 3,400 independently owned and operated pharmacies. It has a business affiliation with AmerisourceBergen, which sponsors the network and owns the name "Good Neighbor Pharmacy." Good Neighbor Pharmacy is the sponsor for "Thought Spot" the annual trade show held in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley" title="Las Vegas Valley">Las Vegas</a>.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="ThoughtSpot">ThoughtSpot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=AmerisourceBergen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: ThoughtSpot" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>ThoughtSpot is a four-day trade show put on by Good Neighbor Pharmacy held in or around July in Las Vegas aimed for and limited to community and independent pharmacies.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>Exhibitors are represented throughout the <a href="/wiki/Healthcare_industry" title="Healthcare industry">healthcare industry</a> including, brand name, generic and over the counter products.
ThoughtSpot 2013 was hosted at the MGM Grand, Las Vegas from July 24 to July 27.  Special guest musical performance by <a href="/wiki/Bret_Michaels" title="Bret Michaels">Bret Michaels</a> capped off the week on July 27 at a special attendee-only closing event.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
