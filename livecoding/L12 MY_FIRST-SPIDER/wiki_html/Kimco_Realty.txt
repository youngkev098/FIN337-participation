</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kimco_Realty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Kimco Realty Corporation was founded in 1966 by a group of real estate investors, including <a href="/wiki/Martin_Kimmel" title="Martin Kimmel">Martin Kimmel</a> and Milton Cooper, who merged their retail assets. 
</p><p>In 1991, the company became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>, raising $120 million.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p><p>In 1998, the company acquired Price REIT for $535 million in stock.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2003, the company acquired Mid-Atlantic Realty Trust for $441 million.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In 2007, the company acquired Birchwood Development Company (NY) for $92 million.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
