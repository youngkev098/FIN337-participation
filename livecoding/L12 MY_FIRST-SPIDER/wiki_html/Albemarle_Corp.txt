</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albemarle_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>In February 1994, <a href="/wiki/Ethyl_Corporation" title="Ethyl Corporation">Ethyl Corporation</a> spun off its chemical businesses to create an independent, publicly traded company named Albemarle Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-Albemarle-Mar-1997-10-K_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Albemarle-Mar-1997-10-K-3">[3]</a></sup> Albemarle was headquartered in Richmond, VA, until 2008 when it announced plans to move its corporate headquarters to Baton Rouge, LA.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2015 the company announced the relocation of its headquarters from Baton Rouge, Louisiana to Charlotte, North Carolina.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Albemarle_Nieuwendammerkade_Amsterdam-Noord.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Albemarle_Nieuwendammerkade_Amsterdam-Noord.jpg/200px-Albemarle_Nieuwendammerkade_Amsterdam-Noord.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="152" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="606"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 200px;height: 152px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Albemarle_Nieuwendammerkade_Amsterdam-Noord.jpg/200px-Albemarle_Nieuwendammerkade_Amsterdam-Noord.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="200" data-height="152" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Albemarle_Nieuwendammerkade_Amsterdam-Noord.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Albemarle facility located in Amsterdam.</div></div></div>
<p>In July 1994, The Albemarle Corporation expanded itself by acquiring the Asano Corporation, a <a href="/wiki/Sales" title="Sales">sales</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marketing" title="Marketing">marketing</a> company headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo,_Japan" class="mw-redirect" title="Tokyo, Japan">Tokyo, Japan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Albemarle-Mar-1997-10-K_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Albemarle-Mar-1997-10-K-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In 1998, the Albemarle Corporation bought a custom manufacturing and oilfield chemicals plant in <a href="/wiki/Teesport" title="Teesport">Teesport</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Albemarle-Mar-1999-10-K_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Albemarle-Mar-1999-10-K-6">[6]</a></sup> In the same year, a <a href="/wiki/Joint_venture" title="Joint venture">joint venture</a> was signed between Jordan Dead Sea Industries Company, Arab Potash Company, and one of Albemarle Corporation's <a href="/wiki/Subsidiary" title="Subsidiary">subsidiaries</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Albemarle-Mar-1999-10-K_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Albemarle-Mar-1999-10-K-6">[6]</a></sup> Another joint venture was signed in 2000 by the corporation and Jinhai Chemical and Industry Company, located in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Albemarle-Feb-2001-10-K405_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Albemarle-Feb-2001-10-K405-7">[7]</a></sup> More chemical <a href="/wiki/Chemical_plant" title="Chemical plant">plants</a> were opened within the next five years in Port-de-Bouc, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> and Bergheim, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> (after the acquisition of Martinswerk GmbH).<sup id="cite_ref-Albemarle-Mar-2005-10-K_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Albemarle-Mar-2005-10-K-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>Since 2000, Albemarle Corporation has acquired assets of Ferro Corporation's PYRO-CHEK flame retardant business; Martinswerk GmbH; the custom and fine chemicals businesses of ChemFirst Inc.; the Ethyl Corporation's fuel and lubricant <a href="/wiki/Antioxidants" class="mw-redirect" title="Antioxidants">antioxidants</a> business; the phosphorus-based polyurethane flame retardants businesses of Rhodia; Atofina S.A.'s (Paris) bromine fine chemicals business; Taerim International Corporation, and the refinery <a href="/wiki/Catalyst" class="mw-redirect" title="Catalyst">catalyst</a> business of <a href="/wiki/Akzo_Nobel" class="mw-redirect" title="Akzo Nobel">Akzo Nobel N.V.</a><sup id="cite_ref-Albemarle-Mar-2005-10-K_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Albemarle-Mar-2005-10-K-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>Also in 2000, Albemarle Corporation, Cytec Industries Inc., and GE Specialty Chemicals, Inc., a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/General_Electric_Company" title="General Electric Company">General Electric Company</a>, announced their intention to form a new business-to-business <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">internet</a> joint venture, PolymerAdditives.com. The creation of this venture was intended to help provide materials faster and more efficiently directly from trusted suppliers.<sup id="cite_ref-Albemarle-Feb-2001-10-K405_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Albemarle-Feb-2001-10-K405-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In the years following, Albemarle acquired its refinery catalysts platform from Akzo Nobel N.V. in 2004 with sites and/or joint ventures in the Netherlands, Houston, France, Brazil, Japan and Singapore.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">[4]</a></sup> In partnership with UOP, Albemarle created the Hydroprocessing Alliance in 2006 to deliver integrated refinery <a href="/wiki/Solution" title="Solution">solutions</a> and hydroprocessing technologies and catalysts to the refining industry.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>Albemarle continued its international expansion in 2007, opening a new regional sales office in Dubai to meet growing market needs in India and the Middle East. In 2008, Albemarle and Weifang Sinobrom Import and Export Company, Ltd. (Sinobrom) reached an agreement in principle to form a new joint venture that will combine the existing business of Sinobrom, a leading marketer of bromine derivatives in China, with Albemarle's global bromine expertise in the specialty chemical industry, forming Sinobrom Albemarle Bromine Chemicals (Shandong) Company Ltd.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">[4]</a></sup> in 2009, Albemarle and Saudi Basic Industries Corporation (SABIC) Affiliate, TAYF, create Saudi Organometallic Chemicals Company (SOCC), a Strategic Catalysts Joint Venture, to build a world-scale organometallics production facility strategically located in the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a> industrial city of Al-Jubail.
</p><p>In 2012, Albemarle introduced its Electronic Materials business unit and expanded its production capabilities for finished polyolefin catalysts in Baton Rouge. Albemarle also expanded its South Haven API production site and upgraded its multi-product cGMP active pharmaceutical ingredient (API) manufacturing facility. In 2014, Albemarle completed the sale of its antioxidant, ibuprofen and propofol businesses to <a href="/w/index.php?title=SI_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="SI Group (page does not exist)">SI Group</a>, allowing an increased focus on its bromine and catalyst franchises.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>Albermarle was one of the three different chemical companies that backed the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Citizens_for_Fire_Safety&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Citizens for Fire Safety (page does not exist)">Citizens for Fire Safety</a> industry group that influenced fire-safety policy by lobbying for use of flame retardant, one of their leading products, in consumer products like furniture and baby products.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Ownership">Ownership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albemarle_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ownership" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>As of 2017 Albemarle Corporation shares are mainly held by institutional investors (<a href="/wiki/The_Vanguard_Group" title="The Vanguard Group">The Vanguard Group</a>, <a href="/wiki/BlackRock" title="BlackRock">BlackRock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Franklin_Resources" class="mw-redirect" title="Franklin Resources">Franklin Resources</a> and others<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>)
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisition_of_Rockwood_Holdings">Acquisition of Rockwood Holdings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albemarle_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Acquisition of Rockwood Holdings" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>In July 2014 Albemarle and Rockwood Holdings, Inc. announced a definitive agreement under which Albemarle would acquire all outstanding shares of Rockwood in a cash and stock transaction valued at approximately $6.2 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In the wake of completing the acquisition, Albemarle announced it would realign its global business units: Chemetall Surface Treatment, Refining Solutions and Performance Chemicals.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> During the following two years, Albemarle announced both the separation of the bromine and lithium businesses, previously together under Performance Chemicals,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and the sale of the Chemetall Surface Treatment business to <a href="/wiki/BASF" title="BASF">BASF</a> in a cash transaction valued at approximately $3.2 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Manufactured_products">Manufactured products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albemarle_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Manufactured products" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Br,35.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Br%2C35.jpg/220px-Br%2C35.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="112" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="306"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 112px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Br%2C35.jpg/220px-Br%2C35.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="112" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Br,35.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The element <a href="/wiki/Bromine" title="Bromine">Bromine</a></div></div></div>
<p>Though beginning as a blotting paper manufacturer, The Ethyl Corporation later abandoned paper manufacturing when it sold Oxford Paper in 1976. Since the late 1990s, Albemarle Corporation has manufactured <a href="/wiki/Bromine" title="Bromine">bromine</a> in countries such as <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a> and <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.
</p><p>Albemarle Corporation became a global leader in the <a href="/wiki/Flame-retardant" class="mw-redirect" title="Flame-retardant">flame-retardant</a> <a href="/wiki/Chemicals" class="mw-redirect" title="Chemicals">chemicals</a> technologies, with production plants in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="The United Kingdom">the United Kingdom</a>.  The corporation also has a line of antioxidants and blends which concentrate on improving storage life and stability of fuel and other <a href="/wiki/Lubricant" title="Lubricant">lubricant</a> products. The acquisition of the phosphorus-based chemistry of Rhodia S.A. included products used in rigid and flexible <a href="/wiki/Polyurethane" title="Polyurethane">polyurethane</a> foam applications and <a href="/wiki/Ammonium" title="Ammonium">ammonium</a> <a href="/wiki/Polyphosphate" title="Polyphosphate">polyphosphate</a> products. Later on, the acquisition of Martinswerk GmbH added <a href="/wiki/Pigment" title="Pigment">pigments</a> for paper applications, <a href="/wiki/Aluminum_oxide" class="mw-redirect" title="Aluminum oxide">aluminum oxides</a> used for flame-retardant, polishing agent, catalyst, and niche ceramic applications, as well as <a href="/wiki/Magnesium_hydroxide" title="Magnesium hydroxide">magnesium hydroxide</a> mainly used as a flame-retardant.<sup id="cite_ref-Albemarle-Mar-2005-10-K_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Albemarle-Mar-2005-10-K-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>Since the acquisition of the Catalysts Division of <a href="/wiki/AkzoNobel" title="AkzoNobel">AkzoNobel</a> in 2004, Albemarle Corporation is one of the world's largest producers of hydro processing catalysts (HPC) and <a href="/wiki/Fluidized_catalytic_cracking" class="mw-redirect" title="Fluidized catalytic cracking">fluidized catalytic cracking</a> (FCC) <a href="/wiki/Catalyst" class="mw-redirect" title="Catalyst">catalysts</a> used in the petroleum refining industry.<sup id="cite_ref-Albemarle-Aug-2004-8-K_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Albemarle-Aug-2004-8-K-17">[17]</a></sup> Production locations (excluding joint ventures in Brasil and Japan) are: <a href="/wiki/Bayport_Industrial_District" title="Bayport Industrial District">Bayport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>Following the acquisition of Rockwood Holdings in 2015,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Albemarle has become a producer of lithium products, including lithium salts and metals, organo-lithium and cesium and specialty metals. Albemarle continues to penetrate lithium-based energy storage products, including e-mobility batteries and batteries for the automotive industry. By 2016 Albemarle was one of the big four lithium mining companies which produced 75% of the world's lithium.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Sustainability">Sustainability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albemarle_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sustainability" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<p>Albemarle's new businesses in 2008 focused on the manufacture of custom and proprietary fine chemicals and chemical services for the <a href="/wiki/Pharmacy" title="Pharmacy">pharmaceutical</a> and life sciences industries.  The Alternative Fuel Technologies division participates in the market for biofuels; gas-to-liquids and coal-to-liquids. In 2008 Albemarle purchased Sorbent Technologies Corporation,<sup id="cite_ref-Albemarle-Nov-2008-10-Q_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Albemarle-Nov-2008-10-Q-22">[22]</a></sup> whose technology controls mercury emissions from coal-fired power plants. 
</p>
