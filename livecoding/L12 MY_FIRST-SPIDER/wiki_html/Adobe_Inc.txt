In 1985, <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple Computer</a> licensed PostScript for use in its <a href="/wiki/LaserWriter" title="LaserWriter">LaserWriter</a> printers, which helped spark the <a href="/wiki/Desktop_publishing" title="Desktop publishing">desktop publishing</a> revolution.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>As of 2019<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Adobe_Inc.&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, Adobe has more than 21,000 employees worldwide,<sup id="cite_ref-fastfacts_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fastfacts-4">[4]</a></sup> about 40% of whom work in San Jose.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Adobe also has major development operations in <a href="/wiki/Newton,_Massachusetts" title="Newton, Massachusetts">Newton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> New York City; <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis</a>; <a href="/wiki/Lehi,_Utah" title="Lehi, Utah">Lehi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Utah" title="Utah">Utah</a>; Seattle; and San Francisco in the United States. It also has major development operations in <a href="/wiki/Noida" title="Noida">Noida</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bangalore" title="Bangalore">Bangalore</a> in India.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adobe_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Adobe_Systems_logo_and_wordmark.svg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Adobe_Systems_logo_and_wordmark.svg/100px-Adobe_Systems_logo_and_wordmark.svg.png" decoding="async" width="100" height="115" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="1145"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 100px;height: 115px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Adobe_Systems_logo_and_wordmark.svg/100px-Adobe_Systems_logo_and_wordmark.svg.png" data-alt="" data-width="100" data-height="115" data-srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Adobe_Systems_logo_and_wordmark.svg/150px-Adobe_Systems_logo_and_wordmark.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Adobe_Systems_logo_and_wordmark.svg/200px-Adobe_Systems_logo_and_wordmark.svg.png 2x" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Adobe_Systems_logo_and_wordmark.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Adobe logo from 1993 to 2017</div></div></div>
<p>The company was started in John Warnock's garage.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The name of the company, <i>Adobe</i>, comes from <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Creek_(Santa_Clara_County,_California)" class="mw-redirect" title="Adobe Creek (Santa Clara County, California)">Adobe Creek</a> in <a href="/wiki/Los_Altos,_California" title="Los Altos, California">Los Altos</a>, California, which ran behind Warnock's house.<sup id="cite_ref-fastfacts_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fastfacts-4">[4]</a></sup> Adobe's corporate logo features a stylized "A" and was designed by Marva Warnock, a graphic designer who is also John Warnock's wife.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p><a href="/wiki/Steve_Jobs" title="Steve Jobs">Steve Jobs</a> attempted to buy the company for $5 million<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> in 1982, but Warnock and Geschke refused. Their investors urged them to work something out with Jobs, so they agreed to sell him shares worth 19 percent of the company. Jobs paid a five-times multiple of their company's valuation at the time, plus a five-year license fee for PostScript, in advance. The purchase and advance made Adobe the first company in the history of Silicon Valley to become profitable in its first year.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>Warnock and Geschke considered various business options including a copy-service business and a turnkey system for office printing. Then they chose to focus on developing specialized printing software and created the Adobe PostScript page description language.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>PostScript was the first truly international standard for computer printing as it included algorithms describing the letter-forms of many languages. Adobe added kanji printer products in 1988.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Warnock and Geschke were also able to bolster the credibility of Postscript by connecting with a typesetting manufacturer. They weren't able to work with Compugraphic, but then worked with Linotype to license the Helvetica and Times Roman fonts (through the Linotron 100).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> By 1987, PostScript had become the industry-standard printer language with more than 400 third-party software programs and licensing agreements with 19 printer companies.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>Warnock described the language as "extensible", in its ability to apply graphic arts standards to office printing.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>Adobe's first products after <a href="/wiki/PostScript" title="PostScript">PostScript</a> were <a href="/wiki/Digital_font" class="mw-redirect" title="Digital font">digital fonts</a>, which they released in a proprietary format called <a href="/wiki/Type_1_font" class="mw-redirect" title="Type 1 font">Type 1</a>, worked on by Bill Paxton after he left Stanford. Apple subsequently developed a competing standard, <a href="/wiki/TrueType" title="TrueType">TrueType</a>, which provided full scalability and precise control of the <a href="/wiki/Pixel" title="Pixel">pixel</a> pattern created by the font's outlines, and licensed it to <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a>.
</p><p>In the mid-1980s, Adobe entered the consumer software market with <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Illustrator" title="Adobe Illustrator">Illustrator</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Vector_graphics" title="Vector graphics">vector</a>-based drawing program for the <a href="/wiki/Apple_Macintosh" class="mw-redirect" title="Apple Macintosh">Apple Macintosh</a>. Illustrator, which grew from the firm's in-house font-development software, helped popularize PostScript-enabled laser printers.
</p><p>Adobe entered <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a> in August 1986. Its revenue has grown from roughly $1 billion in 1999 to $4 billion in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-google_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-google-18">[18]</a></sup> Adobe's fiscal years run from December to November.  For example, the 2007 fiscal year ended on November 30, 2007.
</p><p>In 1989, Adobe introduced what was to become its <a href="/wiki/Flagship" title="Flagship">flagship</a> product, a graphics editing program for the Macintosh called <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Photoshop" title="Adobe Photoshop">Photoshop</a>. Stable and full-featured, Photoshop 1.0 was ably marketed by Adobe and soon dominated the market.
</p><p>In 1993, Adobe introduced <a href="/wiki/PDF" title="PDF">PDF</a>, the Portable Document Format, and its <a href="/wiki/Adobe_acrobat" class="mw-redirect" title="Adobe acrobat">Adobe Acrobat and Reader</a> software. PDF is now an International Standard: <a href="/wiki/PDF" title="PDF">ISO 32000-1:2008</a>.
</p><p>In December 1991, Adobe released Adobe Premiere, which Adobe rebranded as <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Premiere_Pro" title="Adobe Premiere Pro">Adobe Premiere Pro</a> in 2003. In 1992, Adobe acquired OCR Systems, Inc. In 1994, Adobe acquired <a href="/wiki/Aldus" title="Aldus">Aldus</a> and added <a href="/wiki/Adobe_PageMaker" title="Adobe PageMaker">PageMaker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adobe_After_Effects" title="Adobe After Effects">After Effects</a> to its product line later in the year; it also controls the <a href="/wiki/TIFF" title="TIFF">TIFF</a> file format. In the same year, Adobe acquired LaserTools Corp and Compution Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In 1995, Adobe added <a href="/wiki/Adobe_FrameMaker" title="Adobe FrameMaker">FrameMaker</a>, the long-document DTP application, to its product line after Adobe acquired Frame Technology Corp. In 1996, Adobe Inc added <a href="/wiki/Ares" title="Ares">Ares</a> Software Corp.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In 2002, Adobe acquired Canadian company Accelio (also known as <a href="/wiki/JetForm" title="JetForm">JetForm</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2003 Adobe purchased Audio editing and multitrack recording software Cool Edit Pro from Syntrillium Software for $16.5 million,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> as well as a large <a href="/wiki/Music_loop" class="mw-redirect" title="Music loop">loop</a> library called "Loopology". Adobe then renamed Cool Edit Pro to "<a href="/wiki/Adobe_Audition" title="Adobe Audition">Adobe Audition</a>" and included it in the Creative Suite.
</p><p>On December 3, 2005, Adobe acquired its main rival, <a href="/wiki/Macromedia" title="Macromedia">Macromedia</a>, in a <a href="/wiki/Stock_swap" title="Stock swap">stock swap</a> valued at about $3.4 billion, adding <a href="/wiki/Adobe_ColdFusion" title="Adobe ColdFusion">ColdFusion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Contribute" title="Adobe Contribute">Contribute</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Captivate" title="Adobe Captivate">Captivate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Connect" title="Adobe Connect">Adobe Connect</a> (formerly Macromedia Breeze), <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Director" title="Adobe Director">Director</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Dreamweaver" title="Adobe Dreamweaver">Dreamweaver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Fireworks" title="Adobe Fireworks">Fireworks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Flash" title="Adobe Flash">Flash</a>, <a href="/wiki/FlashPaper" title="FlashPaper">FlashPaper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Flex" class="mw-redirect" title="Adobe Flex">Flex</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adobe_FreeHand" title="Adobe FreeHand">FreeHand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adobe_HomeSite" class="mw-redirect" title="Adobe HomeSite">HomeSite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adobe_JRun" title="Adobe JRun">JRun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Presenter" title="Adobe Presenter">Presenter</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Authorware" title="Adobe Authorware">Authorware</a> to Adobe's product line.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>Adobe released Adobe Media Player in April 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> On April 27, Adobe discontinued development and sales of its older HTML/web development software, <a href="/wiki/Adobe_GoLive" title="Adobe GoLive">GoLive</a> in favor of <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Dreamweaver" title="Adobe Dreamweaver">Dreamweaver</a>. Adobe offered a discount on Dreamweaver for GoLive users and supports those who still use GoLive with online tutorials and migration assistance. On June 1, Adobe launched <a href="/wiki/Acrobat.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Acrobat.com">Acrobat.com</a>, a series of <a href="/wiki/Web_application" title="Web application">web applications</a> geared for collaborative work.<sup id="cite_ref-AcrobatDotCom_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AcrobatDotCom-25">[25]</a></sup> Creative Suite 4, which includes Design, Web, Production Premium, and Master Collection came out in October 2008 in six configurations at prices from about US$1,700 to $2,500<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> or by individual application.<sup id="cite_ref-Carlson_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carlson-27">[27]</a></sup> The Windows version of Photoshop includes 64-bit processing.<sup id="cite_ref-Carlson_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carlson-27">[27]</a></sup> On December 3, 2008, Adobe laid off 600 of its employees (8% of the worldwide staff) citing the <a href="/wiki/Late_2000s_recession" class="mw-redirect" title="Late 2000s recession">weak economic environment</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Adobe_Systems_Ottawa_office.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Adobe_Systems_Ottawa_office.jpg/220px-Adobe_Systems_Ottawa_office.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="155" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="2586" data-file-height="1824"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 155px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Adobe_Systems_Ottawa_office.jpg/220px-Adobe_Systems_Ottawa_office.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="155" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Adobe_Systems_Ottawa_office.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Adobe Systems Canada in Ottawa, Ontario (not far from archrival <a href="/wiki/Corel" title="Corel">Corel</a>)</div></div></div><p>On November 10, 2009, the company laid off a further 680 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>Adobe's 2010 was marked by continuing front-and-back arguments with Apple over the latter's non-support for Adobe Flash on its <a href="/wiki/IPhone" title="IPhone">iPhone</a>, <a href="/wiki/IPad" title="IPad">iPad</a> and other products.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Former Apple CEO Steve Jobs claimed that Flash was not reliable or secure enough, while Adobe executives have argued that Apple wish to maintain control over the iOS platform. In April 2010, Steve Jobs published a post titled "Thoughts on Flash" where he outlined his thoughts on <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Flash" title="Adobe Flash">Flash</a> and the rise of <a href="/wiki/HTML_5" class="mw-redirect" title="HTML 5">HTML 5</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Steve_Jobs_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Steve_Jobs-30">[30]</a></sup>
In July 2010, Adobe bought <a href="/wiki/Day_Software" title="Day Software">Day Software</a><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> integrating their line of CQ Products: WCM,<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> DAM,<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> SOCO,<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> and Mobile<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2011, Adobe acquired DemDex, Inc. with the intent of adding DemDex's audience-optimization software to its online marketing suite.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> At Photoshop World 2011, Adobe unveiled a new mobile photo service.<sup id="cite_ref-Carousel_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carousel-37">[37]</a></sup> Carousel is a new application for iPhone, iPad, and <a href="/wiki/Macintosh" title="Macintosh">Mac</a> that uses Photoshop Lightroom technology for users to adjust and fine-tune images on all platforms.<sup id="cite_ref-Carousel_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carousel-37">[37]</a></sup> Carousel will also allow users to automatically sync, share and browse photos.<sup id="cite_ref-Carousel_37-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carousel-37">[37]</a></sup> The service was later renamed to "Adobe Revel".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2011, Adobe acquired Nitobi Software, the makers of the <a href="/wiki/Mobile_application" class="mw-redirect" title="Mobile application">mobile application</a> development framework <i>PhoneGap</i>. As part of the acquisition, the source code of PhoneGap was submitted to the <a href="/wiki/Apache_Foundation" class="mw-redirect" title="Apache Foundation">Apache Foundation</a>, where it became <a href="/wiki/Apache_Cordova" title="Apache Cordova">Apache Cordova</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2011, Adobe announced that they would cease development of Flash for mobile devices following version 11.1. Instead, it would focus on HTML 5 for mobile devices.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> In December 2011, Adobe announced that it entered into a definitive agreement to acquire privately-held Efficient Frontier.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2012, Adobe opened a new 280,000 square foot corporate campus in <a href="/wiki/Lehi,_Utah" title="Lehi, Utah">Lehi, Utah</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p><p>In 2013, Adobe endured a major security breach. Vast portions of the source code for the company's software were stolen and posted online<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> and over 150 million records of Adobe's customers have been made readily available for download.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> In 2012, about 40 million sets of payment card information were compromised by a hack of Adobe.<sup id="cite_ref-Top_2014_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Top_2014-45">[45]</a></sup></p><p>A <a href="/wiki/Class-action_lawsuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Class-action lawsuit">class-action lawsuit</a> alleging that the company suppressed employee compensation was <a href="/wiki/High-Tech_Employee_Antitrust_Litigation" title="High-Tech Employee Antitrust Litigation">filed against Adobe</a>, and three other <a href="/wiki/Silicon_Valley" title="Silicon Valley">Silicon Valley</a>-based companies in a <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> federal district court in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> In May 2014, it was revealed the four companies, Adobe, <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple</a>, <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Intel" title="Intel">Intel</a> had reached agreement with the plaintiffs, 64,000 employees of the four companies, to pay a sum of $324.5 million to settle the suit.
</p><p>In March 2018, at Adobe Summit, the company and NVIDIA publicized a key association to quickly upgrade their industry-driving AI and profound learning innovations. Expanding on years of coordinated effort, the organizations will work to streamline the Adobe Sensei AI and machine learning structure for NVIDIA GPUs. The joint effort will speed time to showcase and enhance the execution of new Sensei-powered services for Adobe Creative Cloud and Experience Cloud clients and engineers.
</p><p>Adobe and NVIDIA have co-operated for over 10 years on empowering GPU quickening for a wide arrangement of Adobe's creative and computerized encounter items. This incorporates Sensei-powered features, for example, auto lip sync in Adobe Character Animator CC and face aware editing in Photoshop CC, and also cloud-based AI/ML items and features, for example, picture investigation for Adobe Stock and Lightroom CC and auto-labeling in Adobe Experience Supervisor.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2018, Adobe stated they would buy e-commerce services provider <a href="/wiki/Magento" title="Magento">Magento Commerce</a> from private equity firm Permira for $1.68 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> This deal will help bolster its Experience Cloud business, which provides services including analytics, advertising, and marketing. The deal is expected to close during Adobe's fiscal third quarter in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2018, Adobe announced its acquisition of marketing automation software company <a href="/wiki/Marketo" title="Marketo">Marketo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2018, Adobe officially changed its name from Adobe Systems Incorporated to Adobe Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2019, Adobe announced its acquisition of 3D texturing company Allegorithmic.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p><p>In 2020, the annual Adobe live summit was canceled due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The event is said to take place online this year.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>

<p>From 1995–2013, <i><a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)">Fortune</a></i> ranked Adobe as "an outstanding place to work". Adobe was rated the 5th best U.S. company to work for in 2003, 6th in 2004, 31st in 2007, 40th in 2008, 11th in 2009, 42nd in 2010, 65th in 2011, 41st in 2012, and 83rd in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> In October 2008, Adobe Systems Canada Inc. was named one of "<a href="/wiki/Canada%27s_Top_100_Employers" title="Canada's Top 100 Employers">Canada's Top 100 Employers</a>" by Mediacorp Canada Inc. and was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Maclean%27s" title="Maclean's">Maclean's</a></i> newsmagazine.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p><p>Adobe has a five-star <a href="/wiki/Privacy" title="Privacy">privacy</a> rating from the <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Frontier_Foundation" title="Electronic Frontier Foundation">Electronic Frontier Foundation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Pricing">Pricing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adobe_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Pricing" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Adobe has been criticized for its pricing practices,<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> with retail prices being up to twice as much in non-US countries.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> For example, it is significantly cheaper to pay for a return airfare ticket to the United States and purchase one particular collection of Adobe's software there than to buy it locally in Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup></p><p>After Adobe revealed the pricing for the Creative Suite 3 Master Collection, which was £1,000 higher for European customers,<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> a petition to protest over "unfair pricing" was published and signed by 10,000 users.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> In June 2009, Adobe further increased its prices in the UK by 10% in spite of weakening of the pound against the dollar,<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> and UK users were not allowed to buy from the US store.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup></p><p>Adobe's <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Reader" class="mw-redirect" title="Adobe Reader">Reader</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Flash" title="Adobe Flash">Flash</a> programs were listed on "The 10 most hated programs of all time" article by <i><a href="/wiki/TechRadar" title="TechRadar">TechRadar</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Security">Security</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adobe_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Security" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Hackers have exploited vulnerabilities in Adobe programs, such as Adobe Reader, to gain unauthorized access to computers.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> Adobe's Flash Player has also been criticized for, among other things, suffering from performance, memory usage and security problems (see <a href="/wiki/Flash_player#Criticism" class="mw-redirect" title="Flash player">criticism of Flash Player</a>). A report by security researchers from <a href="/wiki/Kaspersky_Lab" title="Kaspersky Lab">Kaspersky Lab</a> criticized Adobe for producing the products having top 10 security vulnerabilities.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p><p>Observers noted that Adobe was spying on its customers by including <a href="/wiki/Spyware" title="Spyware">spyware</a> in the Creative Suite 3 software and quietly sending user data to a firm named <a href="/wiki/Omniture" title="Omniture">Omniture</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> When users became aware, Adobe explained what the suspicious software did and admitted that they: "could and should do a better job taking security concerns into account".<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> When a security flaw was later discovered in Photoshop CS5, Adobe sparked outrage by saying it would leave the flaw unpatched, so anyone who wanted to use the software securely would have to pay for an upgrade.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> Following a fierce backlash Adobe decided to provide the software patch.<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></p><p>Adobe has been criticized for pushing unwanted software including third-party browser toolbars and free virus scanners, usually as part of the <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Flash" title="Adobe Flash">Flash</a> update process,<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> and for pushing a third-party <a href="/wiki/Scareware" title="Scareware">scareware</a> program designed to scare users into paying for unneeded system repairs.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Customer_data_breach">Customer data breach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adobe_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Customer data breach" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On October 3, 2013, the company initially revealed that 2.9 million customers' sensitive and personal data was stolen in security breach which included encrypted credit card information.<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup> Adobe later admitted that 38 million active users have been affected and the attackers obtained access to their IDs and encrypted passwords, as well as to many inactive Adobe accounts.<sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup> The company did not make it clear if all the personal information was encrypted, such as email addresses and physical addresses, though <a href="/wiki/Data_privacy" class="mw-redirect" title="Data privacy">data</a> <a href="/wiki/Privacy_law" title="Privacy law">privacy laws</a> in 44 states require this information to be encrypted.<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup></p><p>A 3.8 GB file stolen from Adobe and containing 152 million usernames, reversibly encrypted passwords and unencrypted password hints was posted on AnonNews.org.<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/LastPass" title="LastPass">LastPass</a>, a password security firm, said that Adobe failed to use best practices for securing the passwords and has not <a href="/wiki/Salt_(cryptography)" title="Salt (cryptography)">salted</a> them.<sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup> Another security firm, <a href="/wiki/Sophos" title="Sophos">Sophos</a>, showed that Adobe used a weak encryption method permitting the recovery of a lot of information with very little effort.<sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup> According to <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">IT</a> expert Simon Bain, Adobe has failed its customers and 'should hang their heads in shame'.<sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup></p><p>Many of the credit cards were tied to the <a href="/wiki/Creative_Cloud" class="mw-redirect" title="Creative Cloud">Creative Cloud</a> software-by-subscription service.<sup id="cite_ref-93" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-93">[93]</a></sup> Adobe offered its affected US customers a free membership in a credit monitoring service, but no similar arrangements have been made for non-US customers.<sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-95" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup> When a <a href="/wiki/Data_breach" title="Data breach">data breach</a> occurs in the US, penalties depend on the state where the victim resides, not where the company is based.<sup id="cite_ref-96" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-96">[96]</a></sup></p><p>After stealing the customers' data, cyber-thieves also accessed Adobe's <a href="/wiki/Source_code" title="Source code">source code</a> repository, likely in mid-August 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97">[97]</a></sup> Because <a href="/wiki/Hacker_(computer_security)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hacker (computer security)">hackers</a> acquired copies of the source code of Adobe <a href="/wiki/Proprietary_software" title="Proprietary software">proprietary</a> products,<sup id="cite_ref-98" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-98">[98]</a></sup> they could find and exploit any potential weaknesses in its security, computer experts warned.<sup id="cite_ref-99" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99">[99]</a></sup> Security researcher <a href="/wiki/Alex_Holden" title="Alex Holden">Alex Holden</a>, chief information security officer of Hold Security, characterized this Adobe breach, which affected <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Acrobat" title="Adobe Acrobat">Acrobat</a>, <a href="/wiki/ColdFusion" class="mw-redirect" title="ColdFusion">ColdFusion</a> and numerous other applications, as "one of the worst in US history".<sup id="cite_ref-100" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-100">[100]</a></sup> Adobe also announced that hackers stole parts of the source code of <a href="/wiki/Photoshop" class="mw-redirect" title="Photoshop">Photoshop</a>, which according to commentators could allow programmers to copy its engineering techniques<sup id="cite_ref-101" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-101">[101]</a></sup> and would make it easier to pirate Adobe's expensive products.<sup id="cite_ref-102" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-102">[102]</a></sup></p><p>Published on a server of a Russian-speaking <a href="/wiki/Hacker_group" title="Hacker group">hacker group</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-103" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-103">[103]</a></sup> the "disclosure of encryption algorithms, other security schemes, and software vulnerabilities can be used to bypass protections for individual and corporate data" and may have opened the gateway to new generation <a href="/wiki/Zero-day_attack" class="mw-redirect" title="Zero-day attack">zero-day attacks</a>. Hackers already used ColdFusion exploits to make off with usernames and encrypted passwords of <a href="/wiki/PR_Newswire" title="PR Newswire">PR Newswire</a>'s customers, which has been tied to the Adobe security breach.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> They also used a ColdFusion exploit to breach Washington state court and expose up to 200,000 Social Security numbers.<sup id="cite_ref-104" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-104">[104]</a></sup></p>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Creative_Cloud#Criticism" title="Adobe Creative Cloud">Adobe Creative Cloud § Criticism</a></div>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Anti-competitive_practices">Anti-competitive practices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adobe_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Anti-competitive practices" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1994, Adobe acquired Aldus Corp., a software vendor that sold FreeHand, a competing product.<sup id="cite_ref-law360_105-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-law360-105">[105]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-twff_106-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twff-106">[106]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Freehand" class="mw-redirect" title="Adobe Freehand">Freehand</a> was direct competition to <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Illustrator" title="Adobe Illustrator">Adobe Illustrator</a>, Adobe's flagship vector-graphics editor.<sup id="cite_ref-law360_105-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-law360-105">[105]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-twff_106-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twff-106">[106]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Federal_Trade_Commission" title="Federal Trade Commission">Federal Trade Commission</a> intervened and forced Adobe to sell FreeHand back to Altsys, and also banned Adobe from buying back FreeHand or any similar program for the next 10 years (1994-2004).<sup id="cite_ref-law360_105-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-law360-105">[105]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-twff_106-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twff-106">[106]</a></sup> Altsys was then bought by <a href="/wiki/Macromedia" title="Macromedia">Macromedia</a>, which released versions 5 to 11.<sup id="cite_ref-twff_106-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twff-106">[106]</a></sup> When Adobe acquired Macromedia in December 2005, it stalled development of <a href="/wiki/Adobe_FreeHand" title="Adobe FreeHand">Freehand</a> in 2007, effectively rendering it obsolete.<sup id="cite_ref-law360_105-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-law360-105">[105]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cwff_107-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cwff-107">[107]</a></sup> With FreeHand and Illustrator, Adobe controlled the only two products that compete in the professional illustration program market for Macintosh operating systems.<sup id="cite_ref-law360_105-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-law360-105">[105]</a></sup></p><p>In 2011, a group of 5,000 Freehand graphic designers convened under the banner <i>Free Freehand</i>, and filed a civil antitrust complaint in the US District Court for the Northern District of California against Adobe.<sup id="cite_ref-law360_105-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-law360-105">[105]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-twff_106-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twff-106">[106]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-quarkff_108-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quarkff-108">[108]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-macff_109-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-macff-109">[109]</a></sup> The suit alleged that <i>Adobe has violated federal and state antitrust laws by abusing its dominant position in the professional vector graphic illustration software market</i> and that <i>Adobe has engaged in a series of exclusionary and anti-competitive acts and strategies designed to kill FreeHand, the dominant competitor to Adobe's Illustrator software product, instead of competing on the basis of product merit according to the principals of free market capitalism.</i><sup id="cite_ref-law360_105-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-law360-105">[105]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-quarkff_108-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quarkff-108">[108]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-macff_109-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-macff-109">[109]</a></sup> Adobe had no response to the claims and the lawsuit was eventually settled.<sup id="cite_ref-law360_105-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-law360-105">[105]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cwff_107-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cwff-107">[107]</a></sup> The FreeHand community believes Adobe should release the product to an open-source community if it cannot update it internally.<sup id="cite_ref-twff_106-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twff-106">[106]</a></sup></p><p>As of 2010<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Adobe_Inc.&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, on its FreeHand product page, Adobe stated, "While we recognize FreeHand has a loyal customer base, we encourage users to migrate to the new Adobe Illustrator CS4 software which supports both PowerPC and Intel-based Macs and Microsoft Windows XP and Windows Vista." As of 2016<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Adobe_Inc.&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the Freehand page no longer exists; instead, it simply redirects to the Illustrator page. Adobe's software FTP server still contains a directory for FreeHand, but it is empty.
</p>
