<p><b>Textron Inc.</b> is an American industrial  <a href="/wiki/Conglomerate_(company)" title="Conglomerate (company)">conglomerate</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Providence,_Rhode_Island" title="Providence, Rhode Island">Providence, Rhode Island</a>. Textron's subsidiaries include <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Cat" title="Arctic Cat">Arctic Cat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bell_Helicopter" class="mw-redirect" title="Bell Helicopter">Bell Helicopter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Textron_Aviation" title="Textron Aviation">Textron Aviation</a> (which itself includes the <a href="/wiki/Beechcraft" title="Beechcraft">Beechcraft</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hawker_Aircraft" title="Hawker Aircraft">Hawker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cessna" title="Cessna">Cessna</a> brands), and <a href="/wiki/Lycoming_Engines" title="Lycoming Engines">Lycoming Engines</a>. It was founded by <a href="/wiki/Royal_Little" title="Royal Little">Royal Little</a> in 1923 as the <b>Special Yarns Company</b>. In 2018, Textron employed over 37,000 people worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Textron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Textron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early history" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Charlotte_J_Sternberg_-_Textron_advertising_-_Ladies%27_Home_Journal,_1948.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Charlotte_J_Sternberg_-_Textron_advertising_-_Ladies%27_Home_Journal%2C_1948.jpg/220px-Charlotte_J_Sternberg_-_Textron_advertising_-_Ladies%27_Home_Journal%2C_1948.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="300" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3405" data-file-height="4637"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 300px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Charlotte_J_Sternberg_-_Textron_advertising_-_Ladies%27_Home_Journal%2C_1948.jpg/220px-Charlotte_J_Sternberg_-_Textron_advertising_-_Ladies%27_Home_Journal%2C_1948.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="300" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Charlotte_J_Sternberg_-_Textron_advertising_-_Ladies%27_Home_Journal,_1948.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Textron fabrics ad from 1948.</div></div></div>
<p>Textron started as a textile company in 1923, when 27-year-old <a href="/wiki/Royal_Little" title="Royal Little">Royal Little</a> founded the <b>Special Yarns Corporation</b> in <a href="/wiki/Boston,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston, Massachusetts">Boston, Massachusetts</a>. The company manufactured synthetic <a href="/wiki/Yarn" title="Yarn">yarns</a>, a niche product at the time. By the start of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the company was known as <b>Atlantic Rayon Corporation</b> and manufactured <a href="/wiki/Parachute" title="Parachute">parachutes</a>. As war production wound down, the company started making civilian products as well and was renamed <b>Textron</b>: “Tex" for "textiles" and "tron" from synthetics such as "Lustron". The company was listed on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">NYSE</a> in 1947.<sup id="cite_ref-textron.com1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-textron.com1-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>Royal Little began the process of turning Textron into a conglomerate in 1953,  with the purchase of Burkart Manufacturing Company (upholstery filling for automotive industry) in September 1953, followed by the purchase of Dalmo-Victor (airborne Radar Antennae) and MB Manufacturing Company in early 1954.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The push for diversification would see Textron purchase various other manufacturing companies. In 1960, the company also bought Bell Aerospace and E-Z-Go.<sup id="cite_ref-textron.com1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-textron.com1-4">[4]</a></sup> The textile division was sold to <a href="/wiki/Milliken_%26_Company" title="Milliken &amp; Company">Deering Milliken</a> in 1963.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>Later CEOs included <a href="/wiki/G._William_Miller" title="G. William Miller">G. William Miller</a> (1968–1977), Joseph Collinson (1977–1979) and Robert P. Straetz (1979–1986). In 1984, Textron took on more debt and bought <a href="/wiki/Avco" title="Avco">Avco</a>, a conglomerate almost as big as itself. Later on, James Hardymon took over as CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-textron.com1_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-textron.com1-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Campbell_era">Campbell era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Textron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Campbell era" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Hardymon brought in <a href="/wiki/Lewis_B._Campbell" title="Lewis B. Campbell">Lewis B. Campbell</a>, who became CEO in 1998.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Starting in 2000, Campbell led a company-wide restructuring program. The share price fell to as low as $13/share in March 2003 after the economic downturn following the collapse of Internet companies and the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">terrorist attacks on the World Trade Center</a>. Diminished demand for helicopters and airplanes led to layoffs at <a href="/wiki/Cessna" title="Cessna">Cessna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bell_Helicopter" class="mw-redirect" title="Bell Helicopter">Bell Helicopter</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 2004, <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> magazine named Campbell as the fifth worst-performing CEO in the country.
</p><p>In 2007, the <i><a href="/wiki/Wall_Street_Journal" class="mw-redirect" title="Wall Street Journal">Wall Street Journal</a></i> reported that Campbell had received $494,700 in compensation in the form of his use of a corporate jet to travel between his home and office, which made him the most expensive CEO in the country in terms of use of jet travel. Some shareholders have questioned whether it is a good use of shareholder dollars to pay for the personal lifestyle choice of the CEO to live in one state and work in another.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Shares in Textron plummeted to as low as $10.09 per share in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Recession" title="Great Recession">2008 economic downturn</a>, driving its market capitalization down to just $3.17 billion.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> While the company lost 75% of its value in the first ten years of Campbell's leadership, he managed to take home over $120 million in compensation. His salary in 2008 was $25 million, making him the highest paid executive of a conglomerate. Campbell managed to sell over $40 million in Textron stock in April and May 2008, at prices over $60 per share.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="After_Campbell">After Campbell</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Textron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: After Campbell" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Scott_C._Donnelly" title="Scott C. Donnelly">Scott C. Donnelly</a> became CEO in December 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
Textron acquired Mechtronix in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal, Quebec</a> and OPINICUS in <a href="/wiki/Tampa,_Florida" title="Tampa, Florida">Tampa, Florida</a>, in 2013. Donnelly combined these flight simulation companies, along with Textron’s AAI Logistics &amp; Technical Services, to form TRU Simulation &amp; Training in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-textron.com1_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-textron.com1-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>On December 26, 2013, Textron agreed to purchase <a href="/wiki/Beechcraft" title="Beechcraft">Beechcraft</a>, including the discontinued Hawker jet line, for $1.4 billion. The sale was expected to be concluded in the first half of 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-avweb1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-avweb1-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Niles30Dec13_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Niles30Dec13-12">[12]</a></sup> The company formed a new company called <a href="/wiki/Textron_Aviation" title="Textron Aviation">Textron Aviation</a> to market the products of Beechcraft, <a href="/wiki/Cessna" title="Cessna">Cessna</a> and Hawker as individual <a href="/wiki/Brand" title="Brand">brands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-textron.com1_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-textron.com1-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>From 2013 to 2016, <a href="/wiki/R%26D" class="mw-redirect" title="R&amp;D">R&amp;D</a> investments were 4.3%, 4.0%, 4.6% and 4.2% of its revenues ($13.78 billion in 2016) and totaled more than $2.2 billion as it develops seven aircraft: the <a href="/wiki/Bell_525_Relentless" title="Bell 525 Relentless">Bell 525 Relentless</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bell_V-280_Valor" title="Bell V-280 Valor">Bell V-280 Valor</a> tiltrotor, <a href="/wiki/Cessna_Citation_Longitude" title="Cessna Citation Longitude">Cessna Citation Longitude</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cessna_Citation_Hemisphere" title="Cessna Citation Hemisphere">Cessna Citation Hemisphere</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cessna_Denali" title="Cessna Denali">Cessna Denali</a> single-engine turboprop, the <a href="/wiki/Cessna_SkyCourier" class="mw-redirect" title="Cessna SkyCourier">Cessna SkyCourier</a> twin cargo hauler and the <a href="/wiki/Textron_Scorpion" class="mw-redirect" title="Textron Scorpion">Textron Scorpion</a> close support jet after the certification of the <a href="/wiki/Bell_505_Jet_Ranger_X" title="Bell 505 Jet Ranger X">Bell 505 Jet Ranger X</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Able_Aerospace_Services">Able Aerospace Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Textron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Able Aerospace Services" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Able_Aerospace_Services" class="mw-redirect" title="Able Aerospace Services">Able Aerospace Services</a> is an international business and $70 million aerospace enterprise that is performing more than 8,000 proprietary <a href="/wiki/Federal_Aviation_Administration" title="Federal Aviation Administration">FAA</a>-approved repairs on aircraft and components.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Arctic_Cat">Arctic Cat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Textron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Arctic Cat" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On March 6, 2017, Textron bought out <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Cat" title="Arctic Cat">Arctic Cat</a> for US$247M. Arctic Cat is a manufacturer of snowmobiles, all-terrain vehicles and <a href="/wiki/Side_by_Side_(UTV)" class="mw-redirect" title="Side by Side (UTV)">side-by-sides</a>. Textron operates Arctic Cat as a subsidiary of Textron Specialized Vehicles.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Bell_Flight">Bell Flight</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Textron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Bell Flight" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bell_429.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Bell_429.JPG/220px-Bell_429.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1200"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Bell_429.JPG/220px-Bell_429.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bell_429.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Bell_429" class="mw-redirect" title="Bell 429">Bell 429</a> Helicopter</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bell_Flight" class="mw-redirect" title="Bell Flight">Bell Flight</a> is an American <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rotorcraft" title="Rotorcraft">rotorcraft</a> manufacturer headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Worth,_Texas" title="Fort Worth, Texas">Fort Worth, Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="E-Z-GO">E-Z-GO</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Textron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: E-Z-GO" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Acquired by Textron in 1960,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> E-Z-GO is a designer and manufacturer of light transportation vehicles for golf courses, planned communities, campuses and other uses. Products include electric and internal combustion golf carts, <a href="/wiki/Low_Speed_Vehicle" class="mw-redirect" title="Low Speed Vehicle">low speed vehicles</a><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and other multipurpose utility vehicles under the E-Z-Go, <a href="/wiki/Cushman_(company)" title="Cushman (company)">Cushman</a> and Bad Boy Buggy brands.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Greenlee">Greenlee</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Textron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Greenlee" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Greenlee" title="Greenlee">Greenlee Textron</a> is an industrial and electrical tool company headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Rockford,_Illinois" title="Rockford, Illinois">Rockford, Illinois</a>. It was founded in 1862 by brothers Robert and Ralph Greenlee to manufacture their invention, a drill surrounded by four chisel blades, used in making the pockets for a mortise and tenon joint for the furniture industry in Rockford. This device is still used in cabinetmaking. The brothers later diversified into a variety of hand woodworking tools as well as machinery for making wooden barrels. The company was acquired by Textron in 1986. Greenlee produces various wire and cable installation tools that are used in a variety of fields. On April 18, 2018 Textron announced that it would sell its Greenlee brand to <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Electric" title="Emerson Electric">Emerson Electric</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Jacobsen">Jacobsen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Textron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Jacobsen" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Textron purchased <a href="/wiki/Jacobsen_Manufacturing" title="Jacobsen Manufacturing">Jacobsen Manufacturing</a> around 1975 and continued to produce Jacobsen garden tractors into the 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Today, Jacobsen sells various products used for turf care: maintenance equipment, vehicles and other products.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Kautex_Textron">Kautex Textron</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Textron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Kautex Textron" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Kautex Textron is a supplier to the <a href="/wiki/Automotive_industry" title="Automotive industry">automotive industry</a>. The company produces plastic <a href="/wiki/Fuel_tank" title="Fuel tank">fuel systems</a>, selective catalytic reduction systems, windshield and headlamp washer systems and other products. As of Aug 2019, Textron Inc. is looking to spin off or sell this division to focus on higher margin parts of their business.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Textron_AirLand">Textron AirLand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Textron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Textron AirLand" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><b>Textron AirLand, LLC</b> is a joint venture between Textron Inc. and AirLand Enterprises, LLC that is currently developing the <a href="/wiki/Textron_AirLand_Scorpion" title="Textron AirLand Scorpion">Textron AirLand Scorpion</a> aircraft as a private venture.<sup id="cite_ref-avweb2_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-avweb2-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Textron_Aviation">Textron Aviation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Textron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Textron Aviation" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Textron_Aviation" title="Textron Aviation">Textron Aviation</a> is a new venture formed in March 2014 from Beechcraft and Cessna, retaining the Beechcraft, Cessna and Hawker aircraft type names as brands.<sup id="cite_ref-textronaviation_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-textronaviation-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Beechcraft_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Beechcraft Corporation">Beechcraft Corporation</a> is an American manufacturer of <a href="/wiki/General_aviation" title="General aviation">general aviation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Military_aircraft" title="Military aircraft">military aircraft</a>, ranging from light, single-engined aircraft to twin-engined turboprop transports and military trainers.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Cessna" title="Cessna">Cessna Aircraft Company</a> is an American general aviation aircraft manufacturing corporation headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Wichita,_Kansas" title="Wichita, Kansas">Wichita, Kansas</a>. It was merged into Textron Aviation in March 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-textronaviation_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-textronaviation-24">[24]</a></sup></li></ul><h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Textron_Systems">Textron Systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Textron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Textron Systems" data-section="15" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:USMC-01522.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/USMC-01522.jpg/220px-USMC-01522.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="131" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1563" data-file-height="934"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 131px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/USMC-01522.jpg/220px-USMC-01522.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="131" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:USMC-01522.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/RQ-7B_Shadow" class="mw-redirect" title="RQ-7B Shadow">RQ-7B Shadow</a> UAV</div></div></div>
<p>Textron Systems is an aerospace and defense development and manufacturing firm headquartered in Providence, Rhode Island. The company reported 2012 annual sales in the Textron Systems segment as $1.7 billion.
</p><p>Its operating units are:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/AAI_Corporation" title="AAI Corporation">Support Solutions, Electronic Solutions, Unmanned Systems</a> (<a href="/wiki/Hunt_Valley,_Maryland" title="Hunt Valley, Maryland">Hunt Valley, Maryland</a>)</li>
<li>Advanced Information Solutions (<a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin, Texas</a>)</li>
<li>Geospatial Solutions (<a href="/wiki/Sterling,_Virginia" title="Sterling, Virginia">Sterling, Virginia</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lycoming_Engines" title="Lycoming Engines">Lycoming Engines</a> (<a href="/wiki/Williamsport,_Pennsylvania" title="Williamsport, Pennsylvania">Williamsport, Pennsylvania</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Textron_Marine_%26_Land_Systems" title="Textron Marine &amp; Land Systems">Marine &amp; Land Systems</a> (<a href="/wiki/Slidell,_Louisiana" title="Slidell, Louisiana">Slidell, Louisiana</a>)</li>
<li>TRU Simulation &amp; Training (<a href="/wiki/Goose_Creek,_South_Carolina" title="Goose Creek, South Carolina">Goose Creek, South Carolina</a>)</li>
<li>Weapon &amp; Sensor Systems (<a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_Massachusetts" title="Wilmington, Massachusetts">Wilmington, Massachusetts</a>): a maker of weapons such as anti-vehicle mines <sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> and surveillance systems.</li></ul><p>Textron bought and acquired <a href="/wiki/AAI_Corporation" title="AAI Corporation">AAI Corporation</a> in 2007, which has now evolved into the Support Solutions, Electronic Systems and Unmanned Systems business units mentioned above. AAI Corporation has developed and fielded products such as the <a href="/wiki/AAI_RQ-7_Shadow" title="AAI RQ-7 Shadow">RQ-7B Shadow</a> UAV and various ground control technologies such as the One System Ground Control Station (OSGCS) and the Universal Ground Control Station (UGCS).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
