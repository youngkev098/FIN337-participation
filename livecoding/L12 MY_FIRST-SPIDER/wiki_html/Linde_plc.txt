<p><b>Linde plc</b> is an Irish-domiciled <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> chemical company formed by the merger of Linde AG of Germany (founded in 1879) and <a href="/wiki/Praxair" title="Praxair">Praxair</a> (founded in 1907 as Linde Air Products Company) of the United States. It is the world's largest <a href="/wiki/Industrial_gas" title="Industrial gas">industrial gas</a> company by market share as well as revenue. Linde shares are traded in Germany and the United States, and included in those countries' <a href="/wiki/DAX" title="DAX">DAX</a> 30 and <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a> major stock market indices.

<p>The Linde Group has over 600 affiliated companies in more than 100 countries,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> with customers in the industrial, retail, trade, science, research, and public sectors.
</p><p>In September 2006 the company acquired its UK based competitor <a href="/wiki/The_BOC_Group" title="The BOC Group">The BOC Group</a>, and subsequently disposed of its non-gas interests. Linde's former materials handling business was rebranded as <a href="/wiki/KION_Group" title="KION Group">KION Group</a> in September 2006 and sold in November 2006 to <a href="/wiki/Kohlberg_Kravis_Roberts" title="Kohlberg Kravis Roberts">KKR</a> and <a href="/wiki/Goldman_Sachs" title="Goldman Sachs">Goldman Sachs</a> for €4bn.  In March 2007 the BOC Edwards semiconductor equipment business was sold to <a href="/wiki/CCMP_Capital" title="CCMP Capital">CCMP Capital</a> for €685m.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>Linde's revenue in 2016 were €16.948 billion, with 59,715 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-AR2016_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AR2016-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>Following the BOC acquisition, The Linde Group has become the world's largest industrial gas company. In 2005, Linde AG and BOC together had 21% of the world's market in industrial gases followed by <a href="/wiki/Air_Liquide" title="Air Liquide">Air Liquide</a> with 19%, <a href="/wiki/Praxair" title="Praxair">Praxair</a> with 13%, <a href="/wiki/Air_Products_%26_Chemicals" title="Air Products &amp; Chemicals">Air Products &amp; Chemicals</a> with 10%, <a href="/wiki/Taiyo_Nippon_Sanso_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Taiyo Nippon Sanso Corporation">Taiyo Nippon Sanso Corporation</a> with 4%, <a href="/wiki/Airgas" title="Airgas">Airgas</a> with 3% and <a href="/wiki/Messer_Group" title="Messer Group">Messer Group</a> with 1%.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_activities">Business activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linde_plc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Business activities" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The Linde Group today has two principal business areas: gas (<a href="/wiki/Industrial_gases" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrial gases">industrial gases</a> and medical gases), and <a href="/wiki/Engineering,_procurement,_and_construction" title="Engineering, procurement, and construction">engineering</a>.  In the industrial gas area the group trades under a number of established brand names including Linde, AGA, BOC, TIG, <a href="/wiki/Mox-Linde_Gases" title="Mox-Linde Gases">MOX</a>, Afrox and PanGas.  HiQ is used as an identifier for high purity and premium specialty gases across all of these business brand names.
In the medical gas area, Linde is known with the following names, Linde Gas Therapeutics, AGA Medical, INO Therapeutics, Linde Homecare, Farmadomo and Life Gas.
</p>
<ul><li>Linde Gas supplies industrial gases, medical gases, specialty gases, refrigerants and other chemicals.  Depending on the gas and the quantity required, these may be supplied in portable high-pressure gas cylinders, in liquefied form by road tanker, from on-site gas generators or in gaseous form via pipeline to large customers.</li>
<li>Linde Engineering designs and builds large-scale chemical plants for the production of <a href="/wiki/Industrial_gas" title="Industrial gas">industrial gases</a> including <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nitrogen" title="Nitrogen">nitrogen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Argon" title="Argon">argon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">hydrogen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carbon_monoxide" title="Carbon monoxide">carbon monoxide</a>, as well as large plants associated with the processing of natural gas, <a href="/wiki/LNG" class="mw-redirect" title="LNG">LNG</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liquefied_petroleum_gas" title="Liquefied petroleum gas">LPG</a> and the manufacture of <a href="/wiki/Alkene" title="Alkene">olefins</a>.</li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linde_plc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Trinity_-_Explosion_15s.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/Trinity_-_Explosion_15s.jpg/220px-Trinity_-_Explosion_15s.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="175" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="635"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 175px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/Trinity_-_Explosion_15s.jpg/220px-Trinity_-_Explosion_15s.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="175" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Trinity_-_Explosion_15s.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Uranium used to produce the United States' first atom bombs from 1942 to 1948 was processed by Linde Air Products in <a href="/wiki/Tonawanda_(city),_New_York" title="Tonawanda (city), New York">Tonawanda, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> This company originally had been founded as U.S. branch of the German “Gesellschaft für Linde´s Eismaschinen Aktiengesellschaft” in 1907, but had been expropriated in 1917 and was integrated into <a href="/wiki/Union_Carbide" title="Union Carbide">Union Carbide</a> &amp; Carbon Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>82-83</span></sup>  The <i>New York Times</i> reported in 1981 that 37 million U.S. gallons (140 million liters) of radioactive caustic wastes from the <a href="/wiki/Manhattan_Project" title="Manhattan Project">Manhattan Project</a> was dumped into nearby shallow wells specifically chosen "to hide the source of the contamination".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p>On 21 June 1879, <a href="/wiki/Carl_von_Linde" title="Carl von Linde">Carl von Linde</a> founded the <i>Gesellschaft für Linde's Eismaschinen Aktiengesellschaft</i> to develop further his work in developing mechanical refrigeration systems for the brewing and food industries.  Following success in this market, he moved on to developing lower temperature systems resulting in 1895 in a patent covering the liquefaction of air.  Out of this work his company developed equipment for the separation of air and other gases. Linde's process was patented in 1902 and immediately exploited by the first large-scale air separation plant installed in Linde's work in <a href="/wiki/H%C3%B6llriegelskreuth" class="mw-redirect" title="Höllriegelskreuth">Höllriegelskreuth</a>, near Munich in 1903.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>67</span></sup> In 1906, Carl Von Linde decided to expand his oxygen extraction company oversees, targeting America, where no other companies had attempted industrial scale oxygen extraction.  He opened his first plant in America at <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo, New York</a> in 1907, known as the <a href="/wiki/Linde_Air_Products_Factory" title="Linde Air Products Factory">Linde Air Products Factory</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NysNrhpNom_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NysNrhpNom-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In addition to plants for air separation, in 1906 Linde engineers started working with others on processes to separate the constituents of <a href="/wiki/Water_gas" title="Water gas">water gas</a>. This work developed into capabilities in the recovery of <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">hydrogen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carbon_monoxide" title="Carbon monoxide">carbon monoxide</a>, which were further key feedstocks for the emerging chemicals industry.
</p><p>In addition to his interests in refrigeration, Carl von Linde had also partnered with Hugo Güldner and <a href="/wiki/Georg_Krau%C3%9F" title="Georg Krauß">Georg von Krauß</a> and others in 1904 to form the Güldner Motoren-Gesellschaft mbH in Munich, which was moved to Aschaffenburg in 1906.  Linde took full ownership of the company in 1929, and from this origin developed a business manufacturing first engines and tractors, and then from the 1950s onwards, a range of mechanical handling equipment such as <a href="/wiki/Fork_lift" class="mw-redirect" title="Fork lift">fork lift</a> trucks.  Linde also acquired the Aktiengesellschaft für Industriegasverwertung (<a href="/wiki/English_Wikipedia" title="English Wikipedia">english</a>: Corporation for Industry Gas Utilization ), commonly referred to as the <a href="/wiki/Mittelwerk" title="Mittelwerk">Heylandt Works</a>.
</p><p>In the years of <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi</a> Germany, Linde AG benefited from the country's attempt to become <a href="/wiki/Autarky" title="Autarky">self-sufficient</a> by refraining from imports of synthetic petrol and rubber. In 1935 general manager Friedrich Linde received the title of <a href="/wiki/Wehrwirtschaftsf%C3%BChrer" title="Wehrwirtschaftsführer"><i>Wehrwirtschaftsführer</i></a>, which on the one hand underlined the national importance of Linde AG and on the other hand allowed the company to further benefit from the enlarged armament program.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>99</span></sup> Among the closest colleagues of the general director was Dr. Alfred Hess, plant manager in <a href="/wiki/H%C3%B6llriegelskreuth" class="mw-redirect" title="Höllriegelskreuth">Höllriegelskreuth</a> and uncle of Deputy Führer of the Nazi Party <a href="/wiki/Rudolf_Hess" title="Rudolf Hess">Rudolf Hess</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>100</span></sup></p><p>Before and during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, all departments of Linde AG were in some way involved in the armaments production: The Heylandt-Gesellschaft für Apparatebau supplied the <a href="/wiki/Aggregat_(rocket_family)" title="Aggregat (rocket family)">German rocket program</a>, other branches produced welding equipment and engines for the armaments industry.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>111</span></sup> In 1941 Linde manufactured oxygen and helium installations for <a href="/wiki/IG_Farben" title="IG Farben">IG Farben</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Auschwitz_concentration_camp" title="Auschwitz concentration camp">Auschwitz concentration camp</a>. Three further planned installations were not installed due to the course of war.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>113-114</span></sup> According to estimates of the company for mid-1944, between 400 and 500 people were used as <a href="/wiki/Forced_labour_under_German_rule_during_World_War_II" title="Forced labour under German rule during World War II">forced labourers</a> in production plants.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>115</span></sup></p><p>Linde Air Products, since 1917 part of the Union Carbide &amp; Carbon Corporation sued another company in 1950 for violating their patent on a welding process by simply substituting a similar material.  In <i><a href="/wiki/Graver_Tank_%26_Manufacturing_Co._v._Linde_Air_Products_Co." title="Graver Tank &amp; Manufacturing Co. v. Linde Air Products Co.">Graver Tank &amp; Manufacturing Co. v. Linde Air Products Co.</a></i>, the United States Supreme Court established the <a href="/wiki/Doctrine_of_equivalents" title="Doctrine of equivalents">doctrine of equivalents</a> which found that, while not in the literal scope of the patent, the concept discovered by Linde was still being unfairly encroached upon without compensation.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>A complex history of takeovers, mergers and divestments occurred, and in 2000 completed the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/AGA_AB" title="AGA AB">AGA AB</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>.  This was followed in 2004 with the divestment of the original core of the Linde business – the refrigeration division – to <a href="/wiki/Carrier_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Carrier Corporation">Carrier Corporation</a> of the USA.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In March 2006, Linde made a proposal to acquire <a href="/wiki/The_BOC_Group" title="The BOC Group">The BOC Group</a> for €11.7 billion in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The acquisition was completed on 5 September 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Following the acquisition, the combined gas and engineering group was rebranded as <i>The Linde Group</i>, and the materials handling activities separated and rebranded as KION Group. It was also announced that the BOC Edwards equipment business was to be sold.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In 2008, the head office of Linde AG was relocated from its historic headquarters in Wiesbaden to Munich; the new company headquarters are located in the <a href="/wiki/Angerhof" title="Angerhof">Angerhof</a> building in downtown Munich; the Group's new name is The Linde Group.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In 2010 the group acquired over 95% of the shares of Sri Lanka based, Ceylon Oxygen Ltd.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2011, PT Linde <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, a subsidiary of the Linde group, announced its plan to build an air separation plant worth Rp.1 trillion ($117.33 million) in <a href="/wiki/Cilegon" title="Cilegon">Cilegon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Banten" title="Banten">Banten</a>, to supply industrial gas to PT <a href="/wiki/Krakatau_Steel" title="Krakatau Steel">Krakatau Posco's</a> steel plant.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2012, the company acquired the Continental-European homecare business of the industrial gases company Air Products at an <a href="/wiki/Enterprise_value" title="Enterprise value">enterprise value</a> of €590 million.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> This business achieved annual sales of €210 million in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The transaction comprises Air Products' homecare operations in Belgium, France, Germany, Portugal and Spain with around 850 employees. In August 2012, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Lincare_Holdings_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Lincare Holdings Inc.">Lincare Holdings Inc.</a>, a healthcare gas provider in the USA for US$4.6 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-ft.com_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ft.com-21">[21]</a></sup> Lincare Holdings had sales of US$1.8 billion in 2011. In August, the Linde Group completed the acquisition and became the largest home care gas supplier in North America.<sup id="cite_ref-ft.com_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ft.com-21">[21]</a></sup> In December, the company acquired homecare company Calea France SAS.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2016, Lincare Holdings acquired American HomePatient, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In December, Linde Korea completed the acquisition of Air Liquide Korea's industrial merchant &amp; electronics and liquid bulk air gases divisions, based in South Korea.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>On August 9, 2019 Linde has partnered with CarbonCure Technologies.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Praxair_merger">Praxair merger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linde_plc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Praxair merger" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Praxair" title="Praxair">Praxair</a></div>
<p>In August 2016 it was publicly announced that the German company was in official negotiations to merge with the American competitor <a href="/wiki/Praxair" title="Praxair">Praxair</a>, which would form the world's largest gas supplier.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The following month in September the negotiations were halted and suspended indefinitely.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> As of mid December 2016 Praxair resumed negotiations with Linde for a potential merger.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> In late December an official announcement was made that Praxair had reached an agreement to merge with Linde in a deal valued at $35 billion and<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> with an overall merger value of $65 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-40">[40]</a></sup> In April 2017 Linde again rejected a request for a shareholder vote at its annual general meeting in May – this is the second time the company has rejected Praxair's request.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-40">[40]</a></sup> In May 2017, both companies reached a deal in principle on an all-share merger of equals valued at $75 billion,<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> the deal will still need the approval of Praxair's board of directors as well as Linde's management and executive boards<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> and if approved, will form a new <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> In early June 2017, both companies agreed to the merger.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p><p>On October 31, 2018 the Company declared that business combination between Praxair and Linde AG had been successfully completed except finalising divestitures required by the respective antitrust authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p><p>On March 1, 2019 the Company declared it completed divestitures required by US antitrust authorities and will now fully integrate its business globally.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p><p><br></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Subgroups_and_service_companies">Subgroups and service companies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linde_plc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Subgroups and service companies" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Linde AG comprises three subgroups. The largest division, Gases, has four operating segments, Western Europe, the Americas, Asia &amp; Eastern Europe, and South Pacific &amp; Africa. These segments are subdivided into eight Regional Business Units (RBUs).
</p><p>The Gases Division also includes the two Global Business Units (GBUs) – Healthcare (medical gases) and Tonnage (on-site) – and the two Business Areas (BAs) – Merchant &amp; Packaged Gases (liquefied and cylinder gases) and Electronics (electronic gases).
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Industrial_Gases_and_Health_Care">Industrial Gases and Health Care</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linde_plc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Industrial Gases and Health Care" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>Linde Industrial Gases produces and distributes the atmospheric gases oxygen, nitrogen and argon, all of which are manufactured in Linde's air separation plants.
</p><p>The product range includes hydrogen, acetylene, <a href="/wiki/Carbon_monoxide" title="Carbon monoxide">carbon monoxide</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shielding_gas" title="Shielding gas">shielding gases</a> for <a href="/wiki/Welding" title="Welding">welding</a> applications, <a href="/wiki/Noble_gas" title="Noble gas">noble gases</a> and specialty gases.
</p><p>Linde Healthcare provides pharmaceutical and medical gas products and services for healthcare professionals.
</p><p>Linde Healthcare is present in 70 countries.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Pharmaceutical and medical gases are used in the diagnosis or treatment of a number of clinical conditions. In addition to <a href="/wiki/Oxygen_therapy" title="Oxygen therapy">oxygen therapy</a>, aerosol therapy and <a href="/wiki/Anaesthesia" class="mw-redirect" title="Anaesthesia">anaesthesia</a>, Linde Healthcare provides medical gas for <a href="/wiki/Chronic_obstructive_pulmonary_disease" title="Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease">chronic obstructive pulmonary disease</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asthma" title="Asthma">asthma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sleep_apnoea" class="mw-redirect" title="Sleep apnoea">sleep apnoea</a> and pain.
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Linde_Engineering">Linde Engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linde_plc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Linde Engineering" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>The Engineering Division develops process plants in the <a href="/wiki/Engineering,_procurement_and_construction" class="mw-redirect" title="Engineering, procurement and construction">engineering, procurement and construction</a> (EPC) business worldwide. Linde Engineering focus on market segments such as plants for the production of hydrogen and synthesis gas, oxygen and <a href="/wiki/Olefin" class="mw-redirect" title="Olefin">olefins</a> as well as plants for natural gas treatment.
</p><p>The group has more than 1,000 process engineering patents and 4,000 completed plant projects.
<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p><p>The product range includes:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Air_separation" title="Air separation">Air separation</a> plants</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ethylene_Cracker_Units&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ethylene Cracker Units (page does not exist)">Ethylene Cracker Units</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lng" class="mw-redirect" title="Lng">Lng</a> and <a href="/wiki/Natural-gas_processing" title="Natural-gas processing">natural gas processing plants</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">Hydrogen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Synthesis_gas" class="mw-redirect" title="Synthesis gas">synthesis gas</a> plants</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Chemical_plant" title="Chemical plant">Chemical and petrochemical plants</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Adsorption" title="Adsorption">Adsorption</a> plants</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cryogenics" title="Cryogenics">Cryogenic</a> plants</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Carbon_capture_and_storage" title="Carbon capture and storage">CCS</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">CO<sub>2</sub></a> plants</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Furnace" title="Furnace">Furnaces</a>, <a href="/wiki/Incineration" title="Incineration">incinerators</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heater" class="mw-redirect" title="Heater">heaters</a></li>
<li>Plant components<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></li></ul><h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Gist_Limited">Gist Limited</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linde_plc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Gist Limited" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:266px;"><a href="/wiki/File:A_Gist_articulated_lorry.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="Gist lorry" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/A_Gist_articulated_lorry.jpg/264px-A_Gist_articulated_lorry.jpg" decoding="async" width="264" height="176" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="850" data-file-height="567"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 264px;height: 176px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/A_Gist_articulated_lorry.jpg/264px-A_Gist_articulated_lorry.jpg" data-alt="Gist lorry" data-width="264" data-height="176" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:A_Gist_articulated_lorry.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Gist's vehicles deliver to stores and warehouses across the UK, Ireland, the Netherlands, France, Czech Republic and the USA</div></div></div><p>Gist Limited, headquartered at <a href="/wiki/Chineham" title="Chineham">Chineham</a> Business Park, <a href="/wiki/Basingstoke" title="Basingstoke">Basingstoke</a>, offers <a href="/wiki/Supply_chain" title="Supply chain">supply chain</a> services including end-to-end management and customer fulfilment through transport and warehousing. The company employs approximately 5,000 people in 40 locations in Great Britain, France, Czech Republic, the Netherlands, and the USA, with customers including <a href="/wiki/Marks_%26_Spencer" title="Marks &amp; Spencer">Marks &amp; Spencer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Waitrose_%26_Partners" title="Waitrose &amp; Partners">Waitrose &amp; Partners</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tesco" title="Tesco">Tesco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Starbucks" title="Starbucks">Starbucks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morrisons" title="Morrisons">Morrisons</a><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup>, <a href="/wiki/Eat_(restaurant)" title="Eat (restaurant)">EAT</a>, <a href="/wiki/Young%27s_Seafood" title="Young's Seafood">Young's Seafood</a><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Arla_Foods" title="Arla Foods">Arla Foods</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p><p>Gist also manages <a href="/wiki/Cargo" title="Cargo">Global Freight</a> movements across the world for a variety of clients, including Intergreen and Manchester Tank. 
</p><p>Gist Limited's UK operations reported an annual revenue of £437m in December 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p><p>In partnership with <a href="/wiki/Brake_(charity)" title="Brake (charity)">Brake</a>, Gist's Child Road Safety scheme, established in 2011, has seen more than 120,000 safety sessions delivered to children across the UK, warning of the dangers of <a href="/wiki/Large_goods_vehicle" title="Large goods vehicle">HGVs</a> on the road.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p><p>The history of Gist Limited dates back as far as 1920, when <a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Littmoden&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="George Littmoden (page does not exist)">George Littmoden</a> began moving fruit and vegetables to <a href="/wiki/Covent_Garden" title="Covent Garden">Covent Garden</a> market by horse and cart. George's business, called GL Baker, began transporting fresh fruit for Marks &amp; Spencer in 1932. In 1969, <a href="/wiki/The_BOC_Group" title="The BOC Group">BOC</a> acquired GL Baker, after it expressed interest in its use of liquid nitrogen in chilled containers. The new company was titled <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://companycheck.co.uk/company/01721313/BOC-TRANSHIELD-LIMITED/companies-house-data">BOC Transhield</a>. The new company consisted of 1,100 employees, operating from five locations across the UK.  The company was renamed BOC Distribution Services in 1991, before being rebranded as Gist Limited (Gist being the 'heart of logistics') and moving its headquarters to Chineham in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p><p>After The Linde Group took over BOC in 2006<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup>, it also acquired Gist. Following the group's merger with <a href="/wiki/Praxair" title="Praxair">Praxair</a> to form Linde plc, Gist continues to operate as a separate entity under Linde.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
