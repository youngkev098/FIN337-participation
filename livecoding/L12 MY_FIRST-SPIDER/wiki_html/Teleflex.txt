<p><b>Teleflex Incorporated</b>, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Wayne,_Pennsylvania" title="Wayne, Pennsylvania">Wayne, Pennsylvania</a>, is an American provider of specialty medical devices for a range of procedures in critical care and surgery. Teleflex has annual revenues of $2.4 billion, operations in 40 countries, and more than 12,000 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> By 2011, the company had substantially realigned to focus on its current business as a medical-device manufacturer, having undergone several years of active acquisitions and divestitures.<sup id="cite_ref-10Q_20120731_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10Q_20120731-3">[3]</a></sup> Teleflex has been associated with Irish corporate tax avoidance tools.<sup id="cite_ref-teleflex_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-teleflex-5">[5]</a></sup> Teleflex's current <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">chief executive officer</a> (CEO) is Liam J. </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teleflex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Teleflex was founded in 1943.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Over time the business expanded into a conglomerate operating in multiple markets, often through acquisitions, such as:
</p>
<ul><li>Franklin Medical Ltd (1985)</li>
<li>Warne Surgical Products Ltd (1985)</li>
<li>Willy Rüsch AG (1989)</li>
<li>Pilling (1991)</li>
<li>Access Surgical (1993)</li>
<li>Nextex (1993)</li>
<li>Unimed (1994)</li>
<li>General Medical OEM Division (1994)</li>
<li>Europe Medical (1994)</li>
<li>Fehling (1994)</li>
<li>Simal (1994)</li>
<li>Endoscopy Specialists, Inc. (1995)</li>
<li>Asid Bonz (1996)</li>
<li>Comcorp Technologies, Inc. (1997)</li></ul><p>In general these acquisitions moved the business further into the design, build and supply of medical devices, a move that had begun in 1981 with the construction of the Applied Polymer plant in New Hampshire. Several acquisitions were not in line with this strategy. Comcorp, for example, was a supplier of pedals and other components for passenger cars and light-duty trucks <sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and Nextex a fiber-optic communications business.
</p><p>Refocus on the medical device sector began in March 2009 when Teleflex sold its marine and industrial instrumentation (<a href="/wiki/Gauge_(instrument)" title="Gauge (instrument)">gauge</a>) business to Veethree, and planned to exit its <a href="/wiki/Lakewood_Ranch,_Florida" title="Lakewood Ranch, Florida">Lakewood Ranch, Florida</a> facility.<sup id="cite_ref-TBBJ20090401_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBBJ20090401-9">[9]</a></sup>  Veethree subsequently continued production of former Teleflex products at its <a href="/wiki/Manatee_County,_Florida" title="Manatee County, Florida">Manatee County, Florida</a> facility and hired approximately 50 workers from Teleflex.<sup id="cite_ref-TBBJ20090401_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBBJ20090401-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2011, Teleflex sold its marine division, Teleflex Marine, to <a href="/wiki/H.I.G._Capital" title="H.I.G. Capital">H.I.G. Capital</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2012, Teleflex substantively finalized an agreement to acquire all assets of the medical device maker <a href="/w/index.php?title=LMA_International&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="LMA International (page does not exist)">LMA International</a>, thereby expanding the company's <a href="/wiki/General_anaesthesia" title="General anaesthesia">anesthesia</a> device franchise.<sup id="cite_ref-PBJ20120814_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PBJ20120814-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2013, Teleflex completed the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Vidacare_Corporation" title="Vidacare Corporation">Vidacare Corporation</a>, integrating Vidacare's <a href="/wiki/Intraosseous_infusion" title="Intraosseous infusion">intraosseous</a> EZ-IO and OnControl medical devices into Teleflex's Arrow product line.<sup id="cite_ref-VCDearValued13_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VCDearValued13-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ZERTeleflex13_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ZERTeleflex13-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>On September 5, 2017, Teleflex announced that it had entered into a definitive agreement to acquire NeoTract in a transaction valued at up to $1.1B USD. Under the terms of the agreement, Teleflex will acquire NeoTract for an up-front cash payment of $725M USD and up to an additional $375M USD contingent on the achievement of certain sales-related commercial milestones through December 31, 2020. The transaction was unanimously approved by the Boards of Directors for both Teleflex and NeoTract.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>On January 18, 2019, the S&amp;P Dow Jones Indices added Teleflex to its famous stock market index, the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a>. The company replaced <a href="/wiki/PG%26E" class="mw-redirect" title="PG&amp;E">PG&amp;E</a>, a gas and power company, after the utility company announced it would declare bankruptcy on January 14, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Patent_case">Patent case</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teleflex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Patent case" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>It was a party in the <i><a href="/wiki/KSR_International_Co._v._Teleflex_Inc." title="KSR International Co. v. Teleflex Inc.">KSR v. Teleflex</a></i> patent case decided by the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_States" title="Supreme Court of the United States">Supreme Court of the United States</a> concerning the issue of <a href="/wiki/Inventive_step_and_non-obviousness" title="Inventive step and non-obviousness">obviousness as applied to patent claims</a>.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Irish_tax_schemes">Irish tax schemes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teleflex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Irish tax schemes" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>In September 2018, the <i><a href="/wiki/Irish_Times" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish Times">Irish Times</a></i> revealed that Teleflex had created a new <i><a href="/wiki/Double_Irish_arrangement#Replacement_by_single_malt" title="Double Irish arrangement">single malt</a></i> Irish corporate tax avoidance scheme in July 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-teleflex_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-teleflex-5">[5]</a></sup>  The article noted that Teleflex had previously used a <i><a href="/wiki/Double_Irish" class="mw-redirect" title="Double Irish">double Irish</a></i> Irish corporate tax avoidance scheme,<sup id="cite_ref-teleflex_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-teleflex-5">[5]</a></sup> and that such tax schemes had contributed to the Teleflex aggregate corporate tax rate falling to circa 3%.<sup id="cite_ref-teleflex_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-teleflex-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
