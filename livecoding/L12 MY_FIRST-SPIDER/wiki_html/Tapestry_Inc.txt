</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tapestry,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Coach_New_York">Coach New York</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tapestry,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Coach New York" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Coach_New_York" title="Coach New York">Coach</a> was founded in 1941. Starting as a workshop, it grew into a company with over 17,000 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6">[6]</a></sup> Seeking wider recognition and a larger market, the company acquired brands such as Kate Spade and Stuart Weitzman. This broadened the appeal to consumers of all three brands. The company changed names, reducing the share price because investors felt that the name change was not well received by customers. The share price later recovered.<sup id="cite_ref-:02_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:02-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Kate_Spade_New_York">Kate Spade New York</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tapestry,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Kate Spade New York" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>American fashion designer <a href="/wiki/Kate_Spade" title="Kate Spade">Kate Spade</a> founded <a href="/wiki/Kate_Spade_New_York" title="Kate Spade New York">Kate Spade New York</a> in 1993.  It was initially similar to the Coach company. The company was purchased by Coach in 2017 in a $2.4 billion deal. In 2018, Spade took her own life at age 55, devastating the company and its customers.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Stuart_Weitzman">Stuart Weitzman</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tapestry,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Stuart Weitzman" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Stuart Weitzman started out in the late 1950s as a luxury shoe company named after one of the brothers who started it. It grew into a large operation in Manhattan. The brand was purchased by Coach in 2015 for $574 million. It was made to broaden Coach's product line.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Tapestry.2C_Inc."></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Tapestry,_Inc.">Tapestry, Inc.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tapestry,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tapestry, Inc." data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>This business originally named Coach changed its name to Tapestry to become better match the fashion industry and  its customers.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">[8]</a></sup> The company officially changed its name and ticker symbol on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> from COH to TPR on October 31, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The company wanted to include more than just Coach-brand handbags, the company switched its name to represent a wider range of products. Most of these products are accessories, such as handbags, key-chains, shoes and other small fashion items. Most of these items are <a href="/wiki/Leather_goods" class="mw-redirect" title="Leather goods">leather-goods</a> and are produced by the individual subsidiaries.<sup id="cite_ref-:02_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:02-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<li>In September 2019, Tapestry Inc. replaced Luis as CEO, announcing he would leave immediately and be succeeded by board Chairman Jide Zeitlin. Mr. Zeitlin is a former Goldman Sachs executive who has been on the board since 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>

In this role, Jide is responsible for the execution of the Company’s strategic agenda and financial performance.
