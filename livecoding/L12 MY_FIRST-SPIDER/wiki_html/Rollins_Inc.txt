</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rollins,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company started as Rollins Broadcasting in 1948, founded by <a href="/wiki/Entrepreneur" class="mw-redirect" title="Entrepreneur">entrepreneur</a> <a href="/wiki/John_W._Rollins" title="John W. Rollins">John W. Rollins</a> and his brother, <a href="/wiki/O._Wayne_Rollins" title="O. Wayne Rollins">O. Wayne Rollins</a>.  The company, originally financed in large part by John Rollins' auto dealerships, began as Rollins Broadcasting when the brothers purchased a 1460 <a href="/wiki/WRAD_(AM)" title="WRAD (AM)">WRAD</a>, an AM radio station based in the rural town of <a href="/wiki/Radford,_Virginia" title="Radford, Virginia">Radford, Virginia</a>.  The company grew to include other radio stations, a television station, Orkin, Inc., Western Pest Services, The Industrial Fumigant Company, a trucking concern (later sold to <a href="/wiki/Penske_Truck_Leasing" title="Penske Truck Leasing">Penske Truck Leasing</a>), a hazardous waste disposal service, an <a href="/wiki/Oil_services" class="mw-redirect" title="Oil services">oil services</a> business, and a cable television company.  In 1984 the company spun off its oil services business, RPC, Inc. (<a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">NYSE</a>: <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.nyse.com/quote/XNYS:RES">RES</a>) to shareholders.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p><a href="/wiki/Gary_Rollins" title="Gary Rollins">Gary Rollins</a>, son of O. Wayne Rollins, has been the CEO since July 24, 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-Bloomberg_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bloomberg-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
