</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quest_Diagnostics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Counter_space_at_Quest_Diagnostics_in_Franconia.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Counter_space_at_Quest_Diagnostics_in_Franconia.jpg/220px-Counter_space_at_Quest_Diagnostics_in_Franconia.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Counter_space_at_Quest_Diagnostics_in_Franconia.jpg/220px-Counter_space_at_Quest_Diagnostics_in_Franconia.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Counter_space_at_Quest_Diagnostics_in_Franconia.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Interior of a Quest Diagnostics office in <a href="/wiki/Franconia,_Virginia" title="Franconia, Virginia">Franconia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a></div></div></div>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="1960.E2.80.931995"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1960–1995">1960–1995</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quest_Diagnostics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1960–1995" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Originally founded as Metropolitan Pathology Laboratory, Inc., in 1967 by <a href="/wiki/Paul_A._Brown,_MD" class="mw-redirect" title="Paul A. Brown, MD">Paul A. Brown, MD</a>, the clinical laboratory underwent a variety of rebranding processes.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1969, the company's name changed to MetPath, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> with headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Teaneck,_New_Jersey" title="Teaneck, New Jersey">Teaneck, New Jersey</a>. By 1982, MetPath was acquired by what was then known as <a href="/wiki/Corning_Glass_Works" class="mw-redirect" title="Corning Glass Works">Corning Glass Works</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and subsequently renamed Corning Clinical Laboratories.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="1996.E2.80.932000"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1996–2000">1996–2000</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quest_Diagnostics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1996–2000" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On December 31, 1996, Quest Diagnostics became an independent company as a <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spin-off</a> from <a href="/wiki/Corning_Incorporated" class="mw-redirect" title="Corning Incorporated">Corning</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Kenneth Freeman was appointed as <a href="/wiki/CEO" class="mw-redirect" title="CEO">CEO</a> during this transition.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Over the next year, Quest acquired a clinical laboratory division of <a href="/wiki/Branford,_Connecticut" title="Branford, Connecticut">Branford, CT</a>-based Diagnostic Medical Laboratory, Inc. (DML).<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Two years later in 1999, Quest added <a href="/wiki/GlaxoSmithKline" title="GlaxoSmithKline">SmithKline Beecham</a> Clinical Laboratories to their subsidiaries;<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> which includes a joint venture ownership with CompuNet Clinical Laboratory.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="2001.E2.80.932015"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2001–2015">2001–2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quest_Diagnostics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2001–2015" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>From May 2004-April 2012, Surya Mohapatra served as the company's President and CEO. In 2007 Quest acquired diagnostic testing equipment company AmeriPath.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In response to Mohapatra's resignation after eight years with Quest, former Philips Healthcare CEO Stephen Rusckowski was appointed.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Under Rusckowski, Quest Diagnostics teamed up with central New England's largest health care system, <a href="/wiki/UMass_Memorial_Health_Care" title="UMass Memorial Health Care">UMass Memorial Health Care</a>, to purchase its clinical outreach laboratory.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="2016.E2.80.93present"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–present">2016–present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quest_Diagnostics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2016–present" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2016 Quest collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Safeway_Inc." title="Safeway Inc.">Safeway</a> to bring testing services to twelve stores of its stores in California, Maryland, Virginia, Texas and Colorado.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>By the end of 2017 Quest, in partnership with <a href="/wiki/Walmart" title="Walmart">Walmart</a>, incorporated laboratory testing in about 15 of their locations in Texas and Florida.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2018 the company announced it will become an in-network laboratory provider to <a href="/wiki/UnitedHealthcare" class="mw-redirect" title="UnitedHealthcare">UnitedHealthcare</a> starting in 2019, providing access to 48 million plan members.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2018 Quest moved its headquarters from Madison, where it was located since 2007, to <a href="/wiki/Secaucus,_New_Jersey" title="Secaucus, New Jersey">Secaucus, New Jersey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quest_Diagnostics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Acquisitions" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<ul><li>2001: Completes acquisition of Ohio-based MedPlus, Inc. (<a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a>: <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.nasdaq.com/symbol/medp">MEDP</a>), a healthcare technology company.</li>
<li>2002: Completes acquisition of Virginia-based American Medical Laboratories, Inc. (AML) and an affiliated company of AML, LabPortal, Inc., for approximately $500 million in cash.</li>
<li>2003: Completes acquisition of California-based Unilab Corporation (<a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a>: <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.nasdaq.com/symbol/ulab">ULAB</a>) in a transaction valued at approximately $800 million.</li>
<li>2005: Completes acquisition of Lenexa, Kansas-based LabOne, Inc., which includes the Employer Solutions drug testing division and the ExamOne paramedical testing disivion, (<a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a>: <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.nasdaq.com/symbol/labs">LABS</a>) for approximately $934 million.</li>
<li>2005: Forms a <a href="/wiki/Strategic_alliance" title="Strategic alliance">strategic alliance</a> with Ciphergen Biosystems to commercialize novel proteomic tests.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></li>
<li>2006: Completes acquisition of Virginia-based Focus Diagnostics, Inc., an infectious and immunologic disease laboratory, for approximately $185 million in cash.</li>
<li>2007: Completes acquisition of Sweden-based <a href="/wiki/Hemocue" class="mw-redirect" title="Hemocue">Hemocue</a>, a point-of-care diagnostic testing company.</li>
<li>2007: Acquires AmeriPath (and subsidiary Specialty Laboratories) from <a href="/wiki/Welsh,_Carson,_Anderson_%26_Stowe" title="Welsh, Carson, Anderson &amp; Stowe">Welsh, Carson, Anderson &amp; Stowe</a>, becoming the leading provider of cancer diagnostic testing services.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></li>
<li>2011: Acquires Athena Diagnostics from <a href="/wiki/Thermo_Fisher_Scientific" title="Thermo Fisher Scientific">Thermo Fisher Scientific</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></li>
<li>2011: Completes acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Celera_Corporation" title="Celera Corporation">Celera Corporation</a>, a company that became famous by its sequencing of the <a href="/wiki/Human_Genome" class="mw-redirect" title="Human Genome">Human Genome</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></li>
<li>2014: Acquires Solstas Lab Partners Group (and subsidiaries)<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></li>
<li>2014: Acquires Summit Health, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></li>
<li>2017: Acquires Cleveland HeartLab, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></li>
<li>2018: Acquires U.S. laboratory services business of Oxford Immunotec.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quest_Diagnostics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Controversies" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>Quest Diagnostics set a record in April 2009 when it paid $302 million to the government to settle a <a href="/wiki/Medicare_(United_States)" title="Medicare (United States)">Medicare</a> fraud case alleging the company sold faulty medical testing kits.  It was the largest <a href="/wiki/Qui_tam" title="Qui tam">qui tam</a> (<a href="/wiki/Whistleblower" title="Whistleblower">whistleblower</a>) settlement paid by a medical lab for manufacturing and distributing a faulty product.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> In May 2011, Quest paid $241 million to the state of California to settle a <a href="/wiki/False_Claims_Act" title="False Claims Act">False Claims Act</a> case that alleged the company had overcharged <a href="/wiki/Medi-Cal" title="Medi-Cal">Medi-Cal</a>, the state's <a href="/wiki/Medicaid" title="Medicaid">Medicaid</a> program, and provided illegal kickbacks as incentives for healthcare providers to use Quest labs.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>In 2018, Quest Diagnostics was among a number of US based labs linked to inaccuracies of over 200 women's cervical <a href="/wiki/Smear_test" class="mw-redirect" title="Smear test">smear tests</a> for <a href="/wiki/CervicalCheck" class="mw-redirect" title="CervicalCheck">CervicalCheck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a>'s national <a href="/wiki/Cancer_screening" title="Cancer screening">screening</a> programme.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p><p>On June 3, 2019 Quest announced that American Medical Collection Agency (AMCA), a billing collections service provider, had informed Quest Diagnostics that an unauthorized user had access to AMCA’s system containing personal information AMCA received from various entities, including from Quest.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> AMCA provides billing collections services to Optum360, which in turn is a Quest contractor.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
