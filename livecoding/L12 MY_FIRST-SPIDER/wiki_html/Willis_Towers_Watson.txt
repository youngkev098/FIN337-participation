</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Willis_Towers_Watson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Willis Towers Watson operates in more than 140 countries and has a workforce of more than 45,000 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-WTW_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WTW-1">[1]</a></sup> It has joined the <a href="/wiki/Hedge_Fund_Standards_Board" class="mw-redirect" title="Hedge Fund Standards Board">Hedge Fund Standards Board</a> and follows the voluntary code of <a href="/wiki/International_standards" class="mw-redirect" title="International standards">standards</a> of best practice endorsed by its members.<sup id="cite_ref-HFSB001_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HFSB001-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Willis_Towers_Watson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Willis Towers Watson was formed as a <a href="/wiki/Merger_of_equals" class="mw-redirect" title="Merger of equals">merger of equals</a> between <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> based <a href="/wiki/Willis_Group" title="Willis Group">Willis Group Holdings plc</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arlington_County,_Virginia" title="Arlington County, Virginia">Arlington, VA</a> based <a href="/wiki/Towers_Watson" title="Towers Watson">Towers Watson &amp; Co</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Merger_process">Merger process</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Willis_Towers_Watson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Merger process" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Rationale">Rationale</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Willis_Towers_Watson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Rationale" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>The merging companies announced the merger on June 30, 2015<sup id="cite_ref-:3_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-4">[4]</a></sup> in a deal valued at $18 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> Willis Towers Watson would maintain its domicile in <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> lowering taxes and list on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>. Later in 2015 the company moved its domicile to Virginia in the US and delisted from The NYSE and relisted on The NASDAQ. Willis Group exercised its right to acquire the remainder of Gras Savoye and agreed to purchase 85% of Miller, the leading London independent wholesale insurance broker.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Willis_Towers_Watson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Criticism" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>Critics to the deal pointed out that the original offer would not be beneficial to Towers Watson shareholders as they would receive package of shares and a special cash dividend that is valued at $125.13 per share.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">[6]</a></sup> This value was 9.3% lower than the trading price of Towers Watson's stock was at the time of the announcement of the deal.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The revised offer in November increased the value to $130.26 per share, which was still lower than the trading price of Towers Watson shares at the time of the announcement.  The deal also gives Willis Group shareholders more control despite the firm having a lower market capitalization compared to Towers Watson.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In an open letter, investment adviser <a href="/wiki/Driehaus_capital_management" class="mw-redirect" title="Driehaus capital management">Driehaus Capital Management</a> urged Towers Watson shareholders to vote against a proposed merger.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Driehaus argued that Towers Watson was worth between 39% and 53% more as a standalone company than by merging with Willis Group.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It was also reported that Towers Watson CEO John Haley had disposed of his shares in the company in early March 2015 while the merger negotiations were ongoing.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>On November 18, 2015, the board of Towers Watson failed to get enough investor support for the deal, with only 40% of shareholders voting in favor of the proposed merger with Willis Group.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> This rejection led to Willis Group increasing its special cash dividend for Towers Watson shareholders to USD $10 per share. This revised offer was approved by Towers Watson shareholders on December 11, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Completion">Completion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Willis_Towers_Watson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Completion" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The merger was closed on 5 January 2016 once all regulatory approvals were received. Willis Towers Watson publicly announced their name change on 5 January.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Willis Group shareholders owned 50.1% while those of Towers Watson shareholders owned 49.9% of the combined company.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>On completion, Towers Watson CEO, John Haley, became the CEO, Willis Group CEO, Dominic Casserley, became the President and Deputy CEO while Willis Group Chairman, James McCann, became the chairman of the merged group with the twelve board seats shared equally between the two companies.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>For the first fiscal quarter of 2016, the first earnings release since the merger completed, Willis Towers Watson reported that merger and acquisition costs remain significant and are likely to total between $150 million and $175 million.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>Three years after the merger, the company reported that cost-saving synergies have exceeded expectations, reaching $175 million versus previous estimates of $100-$125mn.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
