</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Equity_Residential&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company had its origins in Equity Finance and Management Company, founded in 1969 by real-estate investor <a href="/wiki/Sam_Zell" title="Sam Zell">Sam Zell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In 1993, the company acquired a large portfolio of properties from <a href="/wiki/Barry_Sternlicht" title="Barry Sternlicht">Barry Sternlicht</a> in exchange for a 20% stake in the company. Sternlicht had acquired the properties via government auctions after the <a href="/wiki/Savings_and_loan_crisis" title="Savings and loan crisis">savings and loan crisis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>On August 11, 1993, the company became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> At that time, the company owned 22,000 apartments.<sup id="cite_ref-crocker_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-crocker-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 1997, the company acquired Wellsford Residential Property Trust for $620 million in stock and the assumption of $346 million of debt<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and acquired Evans Withycombe Residential for $625 million in stock and the assumption of $432 million in debt.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 1998, the company acquired a portfolio of 5,774 apartments from Lincoln Property Company for $465 million<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and acquired Merry Land, which owned 34,990 units in the southeast United States, for $1.17 billion in stock as well as $656 million in debt and $270 million in preferred stock.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In 1999, the company acquired Lexford Residential Trust for $203 million in <a href="/wiki/Stock" title="Stock">stock</a> and the assumption of $530 million of debt. Lexford owned mainly one-story <a href="/wiki/Affordable_housing" title="Affordable housing">affordable housing</a> units, rented to households with incomes between $25,000 and $35,000.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In 2001, the company was added to the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500_Index" title="S&amp;P 500 Index">S&amp;P 500 Index</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2003, CEO Douglas Crocker retired. During his tenure, the company increased its ownership from 22,000 apartments to 227,000 apartments.<sup id="cite_ref-crocker_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-crocker-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 2006, the company sold its Lexford affordable housing division, which included 27,115 apartment units, for $1.09 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>On February 27, 2013, Equity Residential and <a href="/wiki/AvalonBay_Communities" title="AvalonBay Communities">AvalonBay Communities</a> closed a $9 billion deal to acquire <a href="/wiki/Archstone" title="Archstone">Archstone</a> from <a href="/wiki/Lehman_Brothers" title="Lehman Brothers">Lehman Brothers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In 2013, the company sold 8,010 apartment units to a joint venture of <a href="/wiki/Goldman_Sachs" title="Goldman Sachs">Goldman Sachs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greystar_Real_Estate_Partners" title="Greystar Real Estate Partners">Greystar Real Estate Partners</a> for $1.5 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In 2016, the company sold 23,262 apartments to <a href="/wiki/Starwood_Capital_Group" title="Starwood Capital Group">Starwood Capital Group</a> for $5.365 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>Equity Residential was sued in a class action in 2017 due to allegations that it charged late payment fees in violation of California law.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a> attorney general <a href="/wiki/Karl_Racine" title="Karl Racine">Karl Racine</a> also filed a suit against the company in December 2017, challenging the rent concessions offered by Equity Residential for the purpose of attracting new tenants.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
