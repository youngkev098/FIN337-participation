<p><b>Live Nation Entertainment</b> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> global <a href="/wiki/Entertainment" title="Entertainment">entertainment company</a>, founded in 2010, following the <a href="/wiki/Mergers_and_acquisitions" title="Mergers and acquisitions">merger</a> of <a href="/wiki/Live_Nation_(events_promoter)" title="Live Nation (events promoter)">Live Nation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ticketmaster" title="Ticketmaster">Ticketmaster</a>. The company promotes, operates, and manages ticket sales for live entertainment in the United States and internationally. It also owns and operates entertainment venues, and manages the careers of music artists.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Live_Nation_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>In 2009, <a href="/wiki/Live_Nation_(events_promoter)" title="Live Nation (events promoter)">Live Nation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ticketmaster" title="Ticketmaster">Ticketmaster</a>, a concert promotion firm and ticketing company, reached an agreement to merge. The new company received regulatory approval and was named Live Nation Entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Michael_Rapino" title="Michael Rapino">Michael Rapino</a>, then-CEO of Live Nation, became the new company’s CEO, while Ticketmaster CEO <a href="/wiki/Irving_Azoff" title="Irving Azoff">Irving Azoff</a> was named executive chairman.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>The merger was approved first in Norway and Turkey in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">[6]</a></sup> The United Kingdom's <a href="/wiki/Competition_Commission" title="Competition Commission">Competition Commission</a> provisionally ruled against the merger, <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> but reversed its decision on December 22, 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>The merger was opposed in the U.S. by some regulators, artists, fans, and competing firms, who argued it would reduce competition in the industry and increase ticket costs.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Artist <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Springsteen" title="Bruce Springsteen">Bruce Springsteen</a> was one vocal opponent of the merger at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>On January 25, 2010, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Justice_Department" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Justice Department">United States Justice Department</a> approved the merger pending certain conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-Chmielewski_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chmielewski-11">[11]</a></sup> Ticketmaster had to sell ownership of its self-ticketing company, <a href="/wiki/Paciolan" class="mw-redirect" title="Paciolan">Paciolan</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Chmielewski_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chmielewski-11">[11]</a></sup> and license its software to Anschutz Entertainment Group (AEG), which would allow it to compete "head-to-head" with Ticketmaster for business.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-13">[13]</a></sup> AEG was given the option after five years to buy the software, replace it with something else, or partner with another ticketing company.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-12">[12]</a></sup> Additionally, Live Nation Entertainment was placed under a 10-year court order prohibiting it from retaliating against venues that choose to accept competing ticket contracts.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Investments_and_growth">Investments and growth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Live_Nation_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Investments and growth" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, the company acquired Voodoo Music &amp; Arts Experience and announced a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Insomniac_Events" class="mw-redirect" title="Insomniac Events">Insomniac Events</a>, a promoter focused on <a href="/wiki/Electronic_dance_music" title="Electronic dance music">electronic dance music</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The company later acquired <a href="/wiki/C3_Presents" title="C3 Presents">C3 Presents</a> (2014),<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Bonnaroo Music and Arts Festival (2015),<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and Founders Entertainment (2016), the parent company of Governors Ball Music Festival.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In October 2016, Live Nation Entertainment bought AC Entertainment, a Knoxville Tennessee music company, <sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> as well as several international companies.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-21">[21]</a></sup> The company continued to invest in music festivals and promoters in 2017, purchasing a controlling interest in <a href="/wiki/BottleRock_Napa_Valley" title="BottleRock Napa Valley">BottleRock Napa Valley</a> Music Festival,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Salt Lake City-based concert promoter United Concerts, <sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> and CT Touring.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In 2017, Live Nation Entertainment reported revenue of $10.3 billion. <sup id="cite_ref-:4_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> In 2018, the company expanded its concert promotion segment by acquiring a majority stake in a number of companies including Frank Productions,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Emporium Presents,<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> and Red Mountain Entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>Also in 2018, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Justice" title="United States Department of Justice">United States Department of Justice</a> launched an investigation following complaints by AEG that Live Nation pressured them into using <a href="/wiki/Ticketmaster" title="Ticketmaster">Ticketmaster</a> and intentionally avoided booking acts for AEG venues.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-30">[30]</a></sup> Live Nation stated that decisions in selecting venues were not punitive, and were instead based on size and management.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>Live Nation Entertainment has acquired a majority stake in the David Grutman owned Groot Hospitality, a Miami-based company. The portfolio includes LIV nightclub located inside the historic Fontainebleau Hotel, which opened in 2008 as host to the annual Victoria's Secret Fashion Show in 2008. The portfolio includes restaurants and nightlife venues, to include Story (2012), Komodo (2015), Swan &amp; Bar Bevy with Pharrell Williams(2018) and Papi Steak (2019). <sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Operating_divisions">Operating divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Live_Nation_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Operating divisions" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Live Nation Entertainment’s business segments are concerts, ticketing, and sponsorship and advertising. <sup id="cite_ref-:4_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-25">[25]</a></sup> The company promotes and operates live music events and manages artists under its concerts division <a href="/wiki/Live_Nation_(events_promoter)" title="Live Nation (events promoter)">Live Nation Concerts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-25">[25]</a></sup> Live Nation Entertainment’s artist management arm, called Artist Nation, is included within its concerts division <sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:4_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-25">[25]</a></sup> and also includes Front Line Management and <a href="/wiki/Roc_Nation" title="Roc Nation">Roc Nation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Live Nation Entertainment owns and operates venues, including the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Blues" title="House of Blues">House of Blues</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> The company sells tickets to live events through <a href="/wiki/Ticketmaster" title="Ticketmaster">Ticketmaster</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_25-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Live_Nation_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>In 2012, the company announced a partnership with Creativeman Productions, based in Tokyo, Japan.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2013, Live Nation was among those charged with violating Ontario health and safety laws following <a href="/wiki/Radiohead_stage_collapse" title="Radiohead stage collapse">a stage collapse</a> at a <a href="/wiki/Radiohead" title="Radiohead">Radiohead</a> concert that killed one crew member.<sup id="cite_ref-Technician_death_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Technician_death-36">[36]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> A 2019 inquest returned a verdict of <a href="/wiki/Accidental_death" title="Accidental death">accidental death</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2015, Live Nation announced it would form Live Nation Germany, to be created in partnership with German promoter Marek Lieberberg. Live Nation Germany would also have oversight over Live Nation events in Austria and Switzerland.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2016, Live Nation acquired Big Concerts International, South Africa's leading concert promoter.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> The following year, the company acquired majority interests in Israeli promoter Blue Stone Entertainment and the UK’s Cuffe &amp; Taylor.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2018, Live Nation Entertainment acquired a majority stake in Brazil's <a href="/wiki/Rock_in_Rio" title="Rock in Rio">Rock in Rio</a> festival (including from previous stakeholder <a href="/wiki/SFX_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="SFX Entertainment">SFX Entertainment</a>), with its founder Roberto Medina continuing to manage the festival's operations, and providing consulting to Live Nation.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-41">[41]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-41">[41]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
