<p>Keysight's products include hardware and software for benchtop, modular, and field instruments.<sup id="cite_ref-Hill2014_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hill2014-7">[7]</a></sup> Instruments include <a href="/wiki/Oscilloscope" title="Oscilloscope">oscilloscopes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Multimeter" title="Multimeter">multimeters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Logic_analyzer" title="Logic analyzer">logic analyzers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Signal_generator" title="Signal generator">signal generators</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spectrum_analyzer" title="Spectrum analyzer">spectrum analyzers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Network_analyzer_(electrical)" title="Network analyzer (electrical)">vector network analyzers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atomic_force_microscopy" title="Atomic force microscopy">atomic force microscopes</a> (AFM), <a href="/wiki/Automated_optical_inspection" title="Automated optical inspection">automated optical inspection</a>, <a href="/wiki/Automated_X-ray_inspection" title="Automated X-ray inspection">automated X-ray inspection</a> (<a href="/wiki/5DX" title="5DX">5DX</a>), <a href="/wiki/In-circuit_test" title="In-circuit test">in-circuit testers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Power_supply" title="Power supply">power supplies</a> and handheld tools.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In addition, it produces <a href="/wiki/Electronic_design_automation" title="Electronic design automation">electronic design automation</a> (EDA) software (<a href="/wiki/EEsof" class="mw-redirect" title="EEsof">EEsof</a> division).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It mainly serves the telecommunications, aerospace/defense, industrial, computer, and semiconductor industries.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Keysight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Prior to its existence as an independent company, the group that became Keysight was the electronic test and measurement division of first <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard</a>, and later Agilent. HP began as a company making electronic test equipment, with the computer and life sciences products coming later.  In 1999, HP spun-off all test and measurement products into Agilent and retained the computer and printer businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-Rowe2014_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rowe2014-11">[11]</a></sup>  On November 1, 2014, the formal separation of Agilent and Keysight Technologies was completed, with Agilent retaining the life science businesses. The separation was implemented through a spinoff of Keysight’s common stock. Agilent shareholders received one share of Keysight common stock for every two shares of Agilent common stock held October 22, 2014.
</p><p>In June 2015, Keysight announced it would acquire the UK’s <a href="/wiki/Anite" title="Anite">Anite</a> PLC in a deal worth £388 million.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2015, Keysight announced the acquisition of the UK’s Electroservices Enterprises UK Ltd comprising Electroservices (Midlands) Ltd. and Micro Movements Ltd.<sup id="cite_ref-Keys2015_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Keys2015-13">[13]</a></sup>
Micro Movements Ltd., a bespoke electronic measurement business, was originally incorporated in 1994. The company was incorporated into Electroservices Enterprises UK Ltd in 2013.
</p><p>In 2017, Keysight acquired <a href="/wiki/Ixia_(company)" title="Ixia (company)">Ixia</a> for about $1.6 billion in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Baker2017_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Baker2017-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Keys-2017-04-18_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Keys-2017-04-18-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2017, its headquarters sustained some damage in the <a href="/wiki/Tubbs_Fire" title="Tubbs Fire">Tubbs Fire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Keys-2017-10-09_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Keys-2017-10-09-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Avalos2017_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Avalos2017-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Lee2017_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lee2017-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2018, Keysight has acquired a subsidiary of Thales Group, Thales Calibration Services in Melbourne Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-Keys-2018-08-13_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Keys-2018-08-13-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2019, Keysight announced a focus on <a href="/wiki/DevOps#TestOps" title="DevOps">TestOps</a>, specifically its PathWave toolchains.<sup id="cite_ref-pathwave_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pathwave-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Keysight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>Keysight won the 2014 Global Frost &amp; Sullivan award for market leadership with $300 million in instrumentation software revenue. The citation states R&amp;D investment of 12% of revenue ($365 million in 2013) as an important factor.<sup id="cite_ref-Frost2014_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Frost2014-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
