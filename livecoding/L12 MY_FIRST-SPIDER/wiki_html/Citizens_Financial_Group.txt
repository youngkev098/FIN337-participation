<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Citizens_Financial_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early history" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Citizens was established in 1828 as the High Street Bank in Providence, Rhode Island.<sup id="cite_ref-timeline_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeline-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-history_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1871, the Rhode Island legislature gave a second charter to establish the <b>Citizens Savings Bank</b> which eventually acquired its parent group to form Citizens Trust Company.<sup id="cite_ref-timeline_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeline-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-history_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-5">[5]</a></sup> The bank then expanded through Rhode Island, opening a total of 29 branches in that state. It established Citizens Financial Group as a holding company when the bank acquired The Greenville Trust Company in 1954.
</p><p>In 1985, Citizens changed status from a <a href="/wiki/Mutual_savings_bank" title="Mutual savings bank">mutual savings bank</a> to a federal stock savings bank. Expansion into other states began with Massachusetts in 1986.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="RBS_ownership">RBS ownership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Citizens_Financial_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: RBS ownership" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>

<p>In 1988, <a href="/wiki/Royal_Bank_of_Scotland_Group" title="Royal Bank of Scotland Group">Royal Bank of Scotland Group</a> acquired Citizens.<sup id="cite_ref-history_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-5">[5]</a></sup> Under RBS ownership, Citizens acquired several smaller banks in <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a> to become the second largest bank in the region. In 1996, in conjunction with the acquisition of First NH Bank, the <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_Ireland" title="Bank of Ireland">Bank of Ireland</a> gained a 23.5% stake in Citizens, which RBS then acquired two years later to resume 100% ownership. 
</p><p>In 1999, Citizens acquired the United States Trust Company of Boston and the retail banking business of <a href="/wiki/State_Street_Corporation" title="State Street Corporation">State Street Corporation</a>, significantly increasing its footprint in <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>Expansion outside New England began in 2001, when RBS purchased the <a href="/wiki/Retail_banking" title="Retail banking">retail banking</a> division of <a href="/wiki/Mellon_Financial_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Mellon Financial Corporation">Mellon Financial Corporation</a> in Pennsylvania, New Jersey and Delaware for $2 billion. At one stroke, Citizens Bank became the second-largest bank in Pennsylvania, and a major bank in both <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2003, the bank purchased the naming rights to the new home field of the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Phillies" title="Philadelphia Phillies">Philadelphia Phillies</a>, which was named <a href="/wiki/Citizens_Bank_Park" title="Citizens Bank Park">Citizens Bank Park</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>On January 17, 2003, Citizens Financial Group purchased Commonwealth Bancorp, the holding company for Commonwealth Bank, based in <a href="/wiki/Norristown,_Pennsylvania" title="Norristown, Pennsylvania">Norristown, Pennsylvania</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 2004, RBS purchased the credit card division of Connecticut-based People's Bank.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> This purchase allowed Citizens to issue and market its own credit cards.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Charter_One_Financial">Charter One Financial</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Citizens_Financial_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Charter One Financial" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Charter_One_Bank_Ypsilanti.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Charter_One_Bank_Ypsilanti.JPG/220px-Charter_One_Bank_Ypsilanti.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="4752" data-file-height="3168"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 147px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Charter_One_Bank_Ypsilanti.JPG/220px-Charter_One_Bank_Ypsilanti.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="147" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Charter_One_Bank_Ypsilanti.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Charter One Bank branch, downtown <a href="/wiki/Ypsilanti,_Michigan" title="Ypsilanti, Michigan">Ypsilanti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a></div></div></div>
<p>In August 2004, Citizens Financial acquired <a href="/wiki/Cleveland" title="Cleveland">Cleveland</a>-based Charter One Financial, parent company of Charter One Bank, with branches in <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Upstate_New_York" title="Upstate New York">upstate New York</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a> for $10.5 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-completescharter_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-completescharter-13">[13]</a></sup> Because <a href="/wiki/Citizens_Republic_Bancorp" title="Citizens Republic Bancorp">Citizens Republic Bancorp</a> of <a href="/wiki/Flint,_Michigan" title="Flint, Michigan">Flint, Michigan</a> already operated under the Citizens Bank name in most of Charter One's territory, Citizens Financial elected to keep the Charter One name in Charter One's Midwestern footprint. However, it re-branded the New York and Vermont branches as Citizens Bank. This purchase made Citizens Financial the 12th largest bank in the United States with over $131 billion in assets and 1,530 branches across 13 states.<sup id="cite_ref-completescharter_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-completescharter-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In early 2005, the Charter One name replaced the Citizens Bank banner on seven branches in <a href="/wiki/Butler_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Butler County, Pennsylvania">Butler County, Pennsylvania</a>. This rebranding resolved a 3½-year-old name dispute with <a href="/wiki/Butler,_Pennsylvania" title="Butler, Pennsylvania">Butler</a>-based Citizens National Bank. By mid 2005, Citizens National and Citizens Financial agreed to a compromise. Citizens National Bank changed its name to <a href="/wiki/NexTier_Bank" title="NexTier Bank">NexTier Bank</a>, while the Citizens Financial Group branches reverted to the "Citizens Bank" name.<sup id="cite_ref-olsen_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-olsen-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>A new corporate logo designed to show Citizens Bank's connection to the Royal Bank of Scotland debuted on April 26, 2005.
</p><p>In July 2006, Citizens Bank eliminated the mortgage department in Michigan and terminated over 100 employees.
</p><p>On September 1, 2007, the individual banks under Citizens Financial Group, excluding Citizens Bank of Pennsylvania, merged into RBS Citizens, N.A.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2008, Charter One sold its network of 65 branches in Indiana to <a href="/wiki/Old_National_Bank" title="Old National Bank">Old National Bank</a> which rebranded them under the Old National Bank banner.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The transaction closed in June 2010.
</p><p>In 2014, Citizens sold 94 branches in metropolitan Chicago to <a href="/wiki/U.S._Bancorp" title="U.S. Bancorp">U.S. Bancorp</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p><a href="/wiki/Citizens_Republic_Bancorp" title="Citizens Republic Bancorp">Citizens Republic Bancorp</a> was founded in <a href="/wiki/Flint,_Michigan" title="Flint, Michigan">Flint, Michigan</a> in 1871 and merged with Republic Bank in 2006.  In 2007, Citizens Republic prevailed in a case to prevent Citizens Financial from using the similar name in Michigan and Ohio.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/FirstMerit_Bank" class="mw-redirect" title="FirstMerit Bank">FirstMerit Bank</a> acquired Citizens Republic in 2013 and rebranded all branches as FirstMerit until 2016 when <a href="/wiki/Huntington_Bancshares" title="Huntington Bancshares">Huntington Bancshares</a> acquired FirstMerit.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>  With conflicting names no longer an issue, Citizens Bank announced June 30, 2014, that Charter One branches in Michigan and Ohio would be rebranded as Citizens Bank. The name change officially took place on April 27, 2015, bringing to an end the name Charter One in <a href="/wiki/Cleveland" title="Cleveland">Cleveland</a>; the city in which it was founded.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Financial_crisis">Financial crisis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Citizens_Financial_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Financial crisis" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In May 2008, Citizens Financial Group failed to publicly announce that it was under investigation by the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission">Securities and Exchange Commission</a> (SEC) for its involvement in the sub-prime mortgage crisis that devastated the U.S. housing market and bond investors around the world.<sup id="cite_ref-msmith_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-msmith-24">[24]</a></sup> The SEC only investigated banks if suspected of involvement in the purchase and sale of subprime securities.<sup id="cite_ref-winnett_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-winnett-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>In 2008, the company lost $929 million and anticipated writing off $2 billion in bad loans.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Royal Bank of Scotland posted the biggest loss in British corporate history and announced cost-cutting measures at Citizens.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>A Philadelphia developer sued Citizens Bank January 27, 2010, for $8 billion, under a claim that the bank used sham accusations of default to recall loans in an effort to prop up its failing parent companies, Citizens Financial Group and "its ultimate parent, The Royal Bank of Scotland Group."<sup id="cite_ref-hurdle_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hurdle-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Road_to_IPO">The Road to IPO</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Citizens_Financial_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The Road to IPO" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Following the effective <a href="/wiki/Nationalization" title="Nationalization">nationalization</a> of RBS in 2008, speculation arose as to whether RBS would retain Citizens Bank. In 2012, public pressure in the United Kingdom grew for RBS to focus on its home market and sell foreign assets, including Citizens Bank, in order for UK taxpayers to earn their money back. Substantial interest in Citizens Bank had been rumored from other foreign banks such as <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Ita%C3%BA_Unibanco" title="Itaú Unibanco">Itaú Unibanco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Toronto-Dominion_Bank" title="Toronto-Dominion Bank">Toronto-Dominion Bank</a>, the latter of which already had an existing <a href="/wiki/TD_Bank,_N.A." title="TD Bank, N.A.">U.S. branch network</a> that overlapped with the existing Citizen footprint east of the <a href="/wiki/Appalachian_Mountains" title="Appalachian Mountains">Appalachian Mountains</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-colchester_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colchester-29">[29]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_Montreal" title="Bank of Montreal">Bank of Montreal</a>, with its <a href="/wiki/BMO_Harris_Bank" title="BMO Harris Bank">existing U.S. presence</a>, was also mentioned as a possible buyer.<sup id="cite_ref-Sale_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sale-30">[30]</a></sup> U.S. banks mentioned as potential Citizens buyers included <a href="/wiki/JPMorgan_Chase" title="JPMorgan Chase">JPMorgan Chase</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wells_Fargo" title="Wells Fargo">Wells Fargo</a>, <a href="/wiki/U.S._Bancorp" title="U.S. Bancorp">U.S. Bancorp</a>, <a href="/wiki/PNC_Financial_Services" title="PNC Financial Services">PNC Financial Services</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fifth_Third_Bank" title="Fifth Third Bank">Fifth Third Bank</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Sale_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sale-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wallack_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wallack-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2013, RBS confirmed at least a partial spinoff of Citizens through an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> within the next two years. Then in October 2013, RBS announced it would sell its <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>-area branches, which represented 6% of its U.S. deposits;<sup id="cite_ref-sabatini_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sabatini-32">[32]</a></sup> Citizens later agreed to sell those branches to <a href="/wiki/U.S._Bancorp" title="U.S. Bancorp">U.S. Bancorp</a> on January 7, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Finally in November 2013, RBS announced that it would divest all of Citizens Financial Group.<sup id="cite_ref-divest_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-divest-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>The bank began trading on the New York Stock Exchange under the ticker symbol CFG on September 24, 2014, raising $3 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> By April 2015, RBS Group's shareholding in the bank had dropped to 45.6%.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> A further sale in July 2015 reduced RBS' stake to 23.4%.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> RBS sold its remaining stake in the bank in October 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> However, Citizens still uses the RBS "daisy wheel" logo.
</p><p>In June 2016, it was announced that Citizens Financial Group had joined the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> for the first time.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Misappropriation_of_depositor_funds">Misappropriation of depositor funds</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Citizens_Financial_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Misappropriation of depositor funds" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In August 2015, the <a href="/wiki/Consumer_Financial_Protection_Bureau" title="Consumer Financial Protection Bureau">Consumer Financial Protection Bureau</a> (CFPB) and other federal regulators levied nearly $35 million in penalties against Citizens Bank "for failing to credit to customers’ accounts the full amount of their deposits". The regulators declared that, from 2008 to 2013, "The bank’s actions resulted in consumers being shorted millions of dollars".<sup id="cite_ref-wsj15_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj15-40">[40]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Cordray_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cordray-41">[41]</a></sup> Joint action by the CFPB, the <a href="/wiki/FDIC" class="mw-redirect" title="FDIC">FDIC</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Comptroller_of_the_Currency" class="mw-redirect" title="Comptroller of the Currency">Comptroller of the Currency</a> determined that Citizens Bank had engaged in "deceptive practices" by "fail[ing] to give customers their full deposits at times when the size of the deposit didn’t match the number written on deposit slips", despite promising to correct such discrepancies, instead pocketing the difference.<sup id="cite_ref-wapo15_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wapo15-42">[42]</a></sup> The bank was required to reimburse its customers for lost funds as well as pay roughly $20 million in fines to the three agencies.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj15_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj15-40">[40]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> said that Citizens Bank's misconduct "is appalling precisely because it is so basic", demonstrating that "regulators like the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau are a necessary defense against a system prone to abuses".<sup id="cite_ref-nyt15_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt15-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Paul_Manafort_investigation">Paul Manafort investigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Citizens_Financial_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Paul Manafort investigation" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On May 12, 2017, <a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a> reported that the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Justice" title="United States Department of Justice">United States Department of Justice</a> requested from numerous banks, including Citizens, <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump_presidential_campaign,_2016" class="mw-redirect" title="Donald Trump presidential campaign, 2016">Trump 2016 campaign</a> manager <a href="/wiki/Paul_Manafort" title="Paul Manafort">Paul Manafort's</a> transaction records. According to the article, the request was related to the investigation into alleged <a href="/wiki/Russian_interference_in_the_2016_United_States_elections" title="Russian interference in the 2016 United States elections">Russian interference in the 2016 United States elections</a>. Manafort reportedly secured a 2.7 million-dollar loan from Citizens in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p><p>On February 26, 2018, <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_News" title="Bloomberg News">Bloomberg News</a> reported that Citizens' stock price dropped 4.1 percent after U.S. Special Counsel <a href="/wiki/Robert_Mueller" title="Robert Mueller">Robert Mueller</a>’s indictment of Manafort was released, appearing to indicate that Citizens was the "Lender B" cited as having lent 3.4 million dollars to the former campaign manager of President Trump.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Franklin_American_Mortgage">Franklin American Mortgage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Citizens_Financial_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Franklin American Mortgage" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In August 2018, Citizens completed its acquisition of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Franklin_American_Mortgage&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Franklin American Mortgage (page does not exist)">Franklin American Mortgage</a> in a deal valued at $511 Million, expanding its presence into Tennessee and Texas.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Citizens_Financial_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Services" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Citizens_Financial.png" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Citizens_Financial.png/220px-Citizens_Financial.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="5656" data-file-height="3744"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 146px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Citizens_Financial.png/220px-Citizens_Financial.png" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="146" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Citizens_Financial.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Citizens' branch footprint</div></div></div>
<p>Like several other banks, Citizens has agreements with several supermarket chains to locate bank branches in the stores. The greatest number of these are in <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Giant_Eagle" title="Giant Eagle">Giant Eagle</a> stores, most of which were acquired in the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Mellon_Financial_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Mellon Financial Corporation">Mellon Financial Corporation</a>'s retail banking division. Citizens also operates branches inside many <a href="/wiki/Stop_%26_Shop" title="Stop &amp; Shop">Stop &amp; Shop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shaw%27s" class="mw-redirect" title="Shaw's">Shaw's</a> stores in New England. One unique aspect at Citizens branches inside supermarkets is full-service banking from 10 am to 3 pm on Sunday, when banks, including Citizens standalone branches, are traditionally closed. This made Citizens one of the first banks in North America to operate on Sundays. In September 2010, Giant Eagle announced a new agreement with <a href="/wiki/Huntington_Bancshares" title="Huntington Bancshares">Huntington Bank</a> in which Huntington would open branches in several stores in Ohio and West Virginia and will replace existing branches of other banks when the store's agreements with those banks expire.<sup id="cite_ref-Huntington_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Huntington-47">[47]</a></sup></p><p>On June 25, 2008, Citizens Bank announced would sell 18 branches in upstate New York as it prepared to open 57 branches downstate. Community Bank System Inc. of <a href="/wiki/DeWitt,_New_York" title="DeWitt, New York">DeWitt, New York</a> purchased the branches in Ausable Forks, Champlain, Fort Covington, Indian Lake, Lake Placid, Lyons Falls, Long Lake, Malone, Newcomb, North Creek, Plattsburgh, Saranac Lake, Ticonderoga, Tupper Lake and Whitehall.<sup id="cite_ref-heath_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-heath-48">[48]</a></sup> The sale completed on November 7, 2008.
</p><p>In October 2009, Citizens Bank opened a full-service branch inside a <a href="/wiki/Dunkin%27_Donuts" title="Dunkin' Donuts">Dunkin' Donuts</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bellingham,_Massachusetts" title="Bellingham, Massachusetts">Bellingham, Massachusetts</a>. The branch offers teller windows, a full-service <a href="/wiki/Automated_teller_machine" title="Automated teller machine">automated teller machine</a> (ATM) and Dunkin' Donuts promotions for using the bank's services.<sup id="cite_ref-citizendonut_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-citizendonut-49">[49]</a></sup></p><p>Citizens was one of the first banks in the US to use <a href="/wiki/Radio-frequency_identification" title="Radio-frequency identification">Radio-frequency identification</a> (RFID) technology on its ATM cards, via MasterCard <a href="/wiki/MasterCard#PayPass" class="mw-redirect" title="MasterCard">PayPass</a>. In late 2009, however, Citizens began reissuing its cards under <a href="/wiki/Visa_Inc." title="Visa Inc.">Visa</a> without the RFID technology.
</p>
