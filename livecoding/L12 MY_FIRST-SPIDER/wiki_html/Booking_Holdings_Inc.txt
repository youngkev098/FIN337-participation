<p><b>Booking Holdings Inc.</b> is an American company organized in <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a> and based in <a href="/wiki/Norwalk,_Connecticut" title="Norwalk, Connecticut">Norwalk, Connecticut</a>, that owns and operates several <a href="/wiki/Travel_website#Fare_aggregators_and_metasearch_engines" title="Travel website">travel fare aggregators</a> and travel fare <a href="/wiki/Metasearch_engine" title="Metasearch engine">metasearch engines</a> including namesake and flagship <a href="/wiki/Booking.com" title="Booking.com">Booking.com</a>, <a href="/wiki/Priceline.com" title="Priceline.com">Priceline.com</a>, <a href="/wiki/Agoda.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Agoda.com">Agoda.com</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kayak.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Kayak.com">Kayak.com</a>,
<a href="/wiki/Cheapflights" title="Cheapflights">Cheapflights</a>, Rentalcars.com, <a href="/wiki/Momondo" title="Momondo">Momondo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/OpenTable" title="OpenTable">OpenTable</a>. It operates websites in about 40 <a href="/wiki/Language" title="Language">languages</a> and 200 countries. </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Booking_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>In 1997, <a href="/wiki/Jay_S._Walker" title="Jay S. Walker">Jay S. Walker</a> founded the company in <a href="/wiki/Stamford,_Connecticut" title="Stamford, Connecticut">Stamford, Connecticut</a>, which launched <a href="/wiki/Priceline.com" title="Priceline.com">Priceline.com</a>, an online travel site, that used a <a href="/wiki/Name_Your_Own_Price" title="Name Your Own Price">Name Your Own Price</a> bidding model.<sup id="cite_ref-founder_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-founder-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In 1999, the company became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>, making Walker, who owned a 35% stake in the company, a multi-billionaire.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-founder_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-founder-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>The company experimented with selling other products and services such as groceries, gasoline, home mortgages, and cars, but these offerings were discontinued in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>On April 1, 2014, the name of the company was changed from priceline.com Incorporated to The Priceline Group Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2017, the company acquired the Momondo Group.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Newswire_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Newswire-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2017, KAYAK acquired the assets of Mundi, a Brazilian metasearch company.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>On February 21, 2018, the name of the company was changed from The Priceline Group Inc. to Booking Holdings.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On February 27, 2018, the company changed its ticker symbol from "PCLN" to "BKNG".
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Management">Management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Booking_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Management" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Jeff Boyd was named Chief Executive Officer in 2002 and remained in that role until becoming Chairman of the Board for Booking Holdings in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>Effective January 1, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Darren_Huston" title="Darren Huston">Darren Huston</a> was named Chief Executive Officer of the company, replacing Jeff Boyd.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On April 28, 2016, Huston was forced to resign following an extramarital affair and Boyd was named interim CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>Effective January 1, 2017, Glenn D. Fogel was named Chief Executive Officer and President.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
