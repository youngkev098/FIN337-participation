<p><b>Regency Centers Corporation</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Real_estate_investment_trust" title="Real estate investment trust">real estate investment trust</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville,_Florida" title="Jacksonville, Florida">Jacksonville, Florida</a> and is one of the largest operators of shopping centers with <a href="/wiki/Grocery_store" title="Grocery store">grocery stores</a> as <a href="/wiki/Anchor_tenant" title="Anchor tenant">anchor tenants</a>. As of December 31, 2019, the company owned 419 properties comprising 52.6 million square feet of space. Notable properties owned by the company include <a href="/wiki/Serramonte_Center" title="Serramonte Center">Serramonte Center</a> and a 30% interest in <a href="/wiki/Cameron_Village" title="Cameron </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Regency_Centers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>In 1963, the company was founded as Regency Square Properties by Martin and Joan Stein. Four years later, the company built Jacksonville's first regional mall, Regency Square.
</p><p>In 1993, the company became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a>, raising $108 million in an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p><p>In 1997, the company acquired Branch Properties, a Publix developer and leading owner of shopping centers in Atlanta, Georgia.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In 2004, the company acquired a $400 million property portfolio from Branch Properties.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>On December 27, 2004, the company and California State Teachers' Retirement System (CalSTRS) formed a new co-investment partnership to acquire over $200 million in neighborhood and community shopping centers.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In 2005, the company, in partnership with Macquarie CountryWide Trust, acquired 101 centers from First Washington Realty, Inc. and California Public Employees' Retirement System for $2.74 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> First Washington Realty Inc. and the California Public Employees’ Retirement System bought back a 60% interest in most of the portfolio in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2013, the company sold a portfolio of 7 grocery-oriented shopping centers to a joint venture between <a href="/wiki/Blackstone_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Blackstone Group">Blackstone Group</a> and DDR Corp. (now <a href="/wiki/SITE_Centers" title="SITE Centers">SITE Centers</a>) for $332 million.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Equity_One" title="Equity One">Equity One</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
