</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=AutoZone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="1970s">1970s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=AutoZone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1970s" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Originally a division of Memphis-based wholesale grocer Malone &amp; Hyde, the company was known as Auto Shack. After the sale of the grocery operation to the <a href="/wiki/Fleming_Companies,_Inc" title="Fleming Companies, Inc">Fleming Companies</a> of <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City,_Oklahoma" class="mw-redirect" title="Oklahoma City, Oklahoma">Oklahoma City, Oklahoma</a>, the name of the company was changed to AutoZone to reflect the new focus and to settle a lawsuit brought by <a href="/wiki/Tandy_Corporation" title="Tandy Corporation">Tandy Corporation</a> for infringing on Tandy's "<a href="/wiki/Radio_Shack" class="mw-redirect" title="Radio Shack">Radio Shack</a>" trademark.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>On July 4, 1979, the first store opened in <a href="/wiki/Forrest_City,_Arkansas" title="Forrest City, Arkansas">Forrest City, Arkansas</a><sup id="cite_ref-investabout_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-investabout-4">[4]</a></sup> under the name of Auto Shack.<sup id="cite_ref-fortune_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fortune-5">[5]</a></sup> Doc Crain was the store's first manager. Sales that first day totaled $300.00.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="1980s">1980s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=AutoZone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1980s" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1981, Express Parts or VDP is implemented to get the customers hard to find parts by special ordering them through wholesalers. Total stores was 73 in 7 states.
</p><p>In 1984, the company became the first auto parts retailer to create a quality control program for its parts. Total stores was 194 in 13 states.
</p><p>In 1985, Doc Crain coined the term WITTDTJR, which stands for "What it takes to do the job right." Total store count is 263 in 14 states. Peter Formanek stepped in as president. He oversaw the auto parts firm's daily operations and worked on growth strategy.<sup id="cite_ref-Autozone_Commercial_Appeal_March_2020_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autozone_Commercial_Appeal_March_2020-2">[2]</a></sup></p><p>By 1986, expansion had made the company grow into a large store chain across the South and the Midwest. That year, Darren Reltherford, manager of Auto Shack's <a href="/wiki/Memphis,_Tennessee" title="Memphis, Tennessee">Memphis, Tennessee</a> store, received the first <i>Extra Miler</i> award, which has since been given to AutoZoners who show their dedication to customer satisfaction by "going the extra mile" for customer service. The Duralast line of alternators and starters is released. 
The Loan-A-Tool program begins allowing customers the ability to borrow specific tools for jobs. The 4th Distribution Center in <a href="/wiki/Greenville,_South_Carolina" title="Greenville, South Carolina">Greenville, South Carolina</a> opens. Total of stores is 339 in 15 states.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Auto_Zone_sign.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Auto_Zone_sign.JPG/220px-Auto_Zone_sign.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1920" data-file-height="1080"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 124px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Auto_Zone_sign.JPG/220px-Auto_Zone_sign.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="124" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Auto_Zone_sign.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>AutoZone store sign in <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix, Arizona</a></div></div></div>
<p>In 1987, Auto Shack officially changed its name to AutoZone.<sup id="cite_ref-fortune_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fortune-5">[5]</a></sup> The first AutoZone store was in <a href="/wiki/Enid,_Oklahoma" title="Enid, Oklahoma">Enid, Oklahoma</a>. That year also, the company introduced WITT-JR, an electronic catalog used to look up parts and keep warranty information. Total of stores is now 459 in 16 states.
</p><p>In 1989, the company began using a computerized store management system (SMS). The Duralast battery line is released consisting of Sub-Zero, Desert and long life. Total of stores is 513 in 17 states.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="1990s">1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=AutoZone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1990s" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1991, its stock began trading on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> using the ticker symbol "AZO." <sup id="cite_ref-fortune_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fortune-5">[5]</a></sup> It opened up at $27.50 a share. The 5th DC opens in <a href="/wiki/Lafayette,_Louisiana" title="Lafayette, Louisiana">Lafayette, Louisiana</a>.  The company also became the first auto parts retailer to register customer warranties in a computer database.
</p><p>In 1994, AutoZone began using <a href="/wiki/Satellite" title="Satellite">satellites</a> to facilitate communication between stores and the corporate office. Sales hit $1.5 billion.
</p><p>In 1995, AutoZone opened its 1,000th store in <a href="/wiki/Louisville,_Kentucky" title="Louisville, Kentucky">Louisville, Kentucky</a>. Also, the <i>Duralast</i> trademark made its debut with the <i>Duralast</i> and <i>Duralast Gold</i> batteries. Total of stores is now 1,143 in 26 states.<sup id="cite_ref-AutoZone-Nov-1996-10-K_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutoZone-Nov-1996-10-K-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>1996 was the year when the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a> era arrived at the company, when AutoZone opened its company Web site. The new commercial program debuted in <a href="/wiki/Germantown,_Tennessee" title="Germantown, Tennessee">Germantown, Tennessee</a>. <a href="/wiki/ALLDATA" title="ALLDATA">ALLDATA</a>, a software company based in <a href="/wiki/Elk_Grove,_California" title="Elk Grove, California">Elk Grove, California</a> that provides automotive diagnostic and repair information, was acquired.<sup id="cite_ref-AutoZone-May-1996-10-Q_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutoZone-May-1996-10-Q-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>Company founder <a href="/wiki/Pitt_Hyde" title="Pitt Hyde">Pitt Hyde</a> retired as chairman and CEO in 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-fortune_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fortune-5">[5]</a></sup>  John Adams became the new chairman and CEO.
</p><p>In 1998,<sup id="cite_ref-AutoZone-Jun-1998-10-Q_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutoZone-Jun-1998-10-Q-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutoZone-Jul-1998-8-K_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutoZone-Jul-1998-8-K-10">[10]</a></sup> AutoZone acquired Auto Palace, an auto parts chain in the northeastern United States with 112 stores,<sup id="cite_ref-prn1998jan14_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prn1998jan14-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ac1998feb_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ac1998feb-12">[12]</a></sup> TruckPro L.P., a chain with 43 stores in 14 states, and the 560-store <a href="/wiki/Chief_Auto_Parts" title="Chief Auto Parts">Chief Auto Parts</a> Inc., a chain with a presence in 5 states.<sup id="cite_ref-lat-1998may12_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lat-1998may12-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lat-1998jul01_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lat-1998jul01-14">[14]</a></sup> At the commencement of fiscal 1999, AutoZone made another acquisition by purchasing 100 Express stores from <a href="/wiki/Pep_Boys" title="Pep Boys">The Pep Boys—Manny, Moe &amp; Jack</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AutoZone-Nov-1998-10-K_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutoZone-Nov-1998-10-K-15">[15]</a></sup> The company began a process of internationalization with their first store abroad, which opened in <a href="/wiki/Nuevo_Laredo" title="Nuevo Laredo">Nuevo Laredo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AutoZone-Nov-1998-ARS_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutoZone-Nov-1998-ARS-16">[16]</a></sup> AutoZone closed the 1990s by debuting at the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> list in 1999.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s">2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=AutoZone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2000s" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Steve_Odland" title="Steve Odland">Steve Odland</a> became AutoZone's third CEO in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-AutoZone-Jan-2001-8-K_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutoZone-Jan-2001-8-K-17">[17]</a></sup> Also in 2001, AutoZone sold TruckPro to Paratus Capital Management.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2008-09-11_AutoZone_in_Hillsborough,_NC.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/2008-09-11_AutoZone_in_Hillsborough%2C_NC.jpg/220px-2008-09-11_AutoZone_in_Hillsborough%2C_NC.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3888" data-file-height="2592"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 147px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/2008-09-11_AutoZone_in_Hillsborough%2C_NC.jpg/220px-2008-09-11_AutoZone_in_Hillsborough%2C_NC.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="147" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2008-09-11_AutoZone_in_Hillsborough,_NC.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>AutoZone store in <a href="/wiki/Hillsborough,_North_Carolina" title="Hillsborough, North Carolina">Hillsborough, North Carolina</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>In 2002, AutoZone developed a network of "hub, feeder, and satellite" stores to have more product in the market area, while reducing inventory investment. Sales hit $5.33 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-AutoZone-Oct-2002-10-K_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutoZone-Oct-2002-10-K-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In 2003, the <i>Duralast tool</i> line was introduced. This was a year of important negotiations for AutoZone, as the company partnered with other important auto parts industry companies, such as <a href="/wiki/CarMax" title="CarMax">CarMax</a> and Midas. AutoZone de Mexico opens the first DC in <a href="/wiki/Nuevo_Laredo" title="Nuevo Laredo">Nuevo Laredo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>. Total stores  number 3,219 in 48 states and 49 in Mexico.<sup id="cite_ref-AutoZone-Oct-2003-8-K_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutoZone-Oct-2003-8-K-20">[20]</a></sup> In 2004, founder <a href="/wiki/Pitt_Hyde" title="Pitt Hyde">J.R. "Pitt" Hyde III</a> was inducted into the Automotive Hall of Fame.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In 2005, <a href="/wiki/William_C._Rhodes_(businessman)" title="William C. Rhodes (businessman)">William C. Rhodes III</a> was named president and CEO. Steve Odland left to become the chairman and CEO of <a href="/wiki/Office_Depot" title="Office Depot">Office Depot</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AutoZone-Mar-2005-8-K_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutoZone-Mar-2005-8-K-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In 2007, Bill Rhodes, then 42, was named chairman, president and chief executive officer of AutoZone, Inc. on June 6.<sup id="cite_ref-AutoZone-Jun-2007-8-K_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutoZone-Jun-2007-8-K-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>In 2008, AutoZone opened their 4,000th store in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>. Sales hit $6.2 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-AutoZone-Oct-2007-10-K_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutoZone-Oct-2007-10-K-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s">2010s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=AutoZone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2010s" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Interior_of_AutoZone_store_3816.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Interior_of_AutoZone_store_3816.jpg/220px-Interior_of_AutoZone_store_3816.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="1836"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 124px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Interior_of_AutoZone_store_3816.jpg/220px-Interior_of_AutoZone_store_3816.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="124" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Interior_of_AutoZone_store_3816.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Interior of an AutoZone store in <a href="/wiki/North_Port,_Florida" title="North Port, Florida">North Port, Florida</a></div></div></div>
<p>On December 15, 2011, <a href="/wiki/ALLDATA" title="ALLDATA">ALLDATA LLC</a>, an operating unit of AutoZone, expanded its direct presence in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> to better serve its growing customer base.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>On August 17, 2012, AutoZone expanded into their 49th state <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> by opening their 5,000th store in <a href="/wiki/Wasilla,_Alaska" title="Wasilla, Alaska">Wasilla, Alaska</a>
</p><p>In 2012 AutoZone opened their first store in Brazil.<sup id="cite_ref-AutoZone-Dec-2012-8-K_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutoZone-Dec-2012-8-K-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2012, AutoZone purchased AutoAnything.com, an ecommerce leader in <a href="/wiki/Aftermarket_automotive_parts" class="mw-redirect" title="Aftermarket automotive parts">aftermarket automotive parts</a> based in <a href="/wiki/San_Diego,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Diego, California">San Diego, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AutoZone-Dec-2012-8-K_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutoZone-Dec-2012-8-K-28">[28]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutoZone-Dec-2012-10-Q_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutoZone-Dec-2012-10-Q-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>By April 2017, AutoZone had been the largest retailer of automotive parts in North America for three consecutive years.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> As of August 2017, AutoZone had 5,465 locations in the United States, 524 locations in Mexico, and 14 locations in Brazil, for a total of 6,003.<sup id="cite_ref-10-K_Aug_2017_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10-K_Aug_2017-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>On October 22, 2018, Pitt Hyde announced that he would be stepping down from AutoZone's board of directors.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p><br></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_affairs">Corporate affairs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=AutoZone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Corporate affairs" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>AutoZone is <a href="/wiki/Incorporation_(business)" title="Incorporation (business)">incorporated</a> in the state of <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10-K_Aug_2017_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10-K_Aug_2017-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Headquarters">Headquarters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=AutoZone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Headquarters" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Since October 1995, AutoZone has been headquartered in its J.R. Hyde, III Store Support Center (SSC), a 270,000-square-foot (25,000 m<sup>2</sup>), eight-story building in <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Memphis" class="mw-redirect" title="Downtown Memphis">Downtown</a> <a href="/wiki/Memphis,_Tennessee" title="Memphis, Tennessee">Memphis, Tennessee</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-earthquakres_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-earthquakres-32">[32]</a></sup> As of 2013 there were over 1,200 employees there.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p><p>The project manager of the building's construction was Rob Norcross, a principal at LRK Inc. The building has the capability to withstand a 9.0 magnitude earthquake because it has a special base isolation system that had a price tag of $950,000.<sup id="cite_ref-earthquakres_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-earthquakres-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Private_labels">Private labels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=AutoZone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Private labels" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>Valucraft, Duralast, and Duralast Gold are AutoZone's <a href="/wiki/Private_label" title="Private label">private label</a> brands for lead-acid <a href="/wiki/Car_battery" class="mw-redirect" title="Car battery">automotive batteries</a> (manufactured primarily by <a href="/wiki/Johnson_Controls" title="Johnson Controls">Johnson Controls</a>, but also East Penn, Exide, and other manufacturers). Duralast Platinum is an <a href="/wiki/Absorbent_glass_mat" class="mw-redirect" title="Absorbent glass mat">AGM</a> line of batteries. AutoZone also sells tools under the Duralast brand which carry a lifetime warranty.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p><br>
Brake pad labels include (ranging from least to most expensive):
</p>
<ul><li><b>Duralast</b> - OEM-like performance; semi-metallic or organic.</li>
<li><b>Duralast Gold</b> - OEM design and performance; semi-metallic or ceramic.</li>
<li><b>Duralast Max</b> - superior to OEM design and performance; ceramic.</li>
<li><b>Duralast GT Street</b> - performance pads for select applications.</li></ul><p>Valucraft pads are being phased out, as of early 2015. Duralast GT Street pads introduced early 2018.
</p><p>The Valucraft, Duralast, and Duralast Gold names are used on various other parts and accessories as well.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Retail_stores">Retail stores</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=AutoZone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Retail stores" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>Not <a href="/wiki/Franchising" title="Franchising">franchises</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-about_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-about-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (March 2020)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> AutoZone's 6,000+ retail outlets<sup id="cite_ref-investabout_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-investabout-4">[4]</a></sup> throughout the United States, Mexico and Brazil stock a variety of aftermarket parts.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Sponsorships">Sponsorships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=AutoZone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sponsorships" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<p>In 2004, AutoZone celebrated its 25th anniversary and announced a corporate sponsorship agreement with <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">auto racing</a> association <a href="/wiki/NASCAR" title="NASCAR">NASCAR</a>.
</p><p>In 2007, AutoZone sponsored <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Harvick" title="Kevin Harvick">Kevin Harvick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Timothy_Peters" title="Timothy Peters">Timothy Peters</a> in the <a href="/wiki/NASCAR_Busch_Series" class="mw-redirect" title="NASCAR Busch Series">NASCAR Busch Series</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>AutoZone holds the <a href="/wiki/Naming_rights" title="Naming rights">naming rights</a> to the downtown Memphis <a href="/wiki/AutoZone_Park" title="AutoZone Park">baseball stadium</a> that is the home of the <a href="/wiki/Memphis_Redbirds" title="Memphis Redbirds">Memphis Redbirds</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Coast_League" title="Pacific Coast League">Pacific Coast League</a>. The company also sponsors the AutoZone <a href="/wiki/Liberty_Bowl" title="Liberty Bowl">Liberty Bowl</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The AutoZone Liberty Bowl, alongside the College Football Playoff Foundation donated $250,136.03 to the <a href="/wiki/St._Jude_Children%27s_Research_Hospital" title="St. Jude Children's Research Hospital">St. Jude Children's Research Hospital</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> The AutoZone Liberty Bowl awarded 2018's Distinguished Citizen Award to <a href="/wiki/Priscilla_Presley" title="Priscilla Presley">Priscilla Presley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p><p>They are an official sponsor of <a href="/wiki/Bellator_MMA" title="Bellator MMA">Bellator MMA</a>, the world's second largest <a href="/wiki/Mixed_Martial_Arts" class="mw-redirect" title="Mixed Martial Arts">Mixed Martial Arts</a> promotion.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(6)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=AutoZone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Controversies" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-6 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-6">
<p>AutoZone paid $3.3 million to settle a lawsuit to the families of a family who was killed due to being rear-ended by an AutoZone-owned truck. The suit alleges that AutoZone was negligent in training the driver of the truck.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p><p>AutoZone faced a lawsuit for gender discrimination, where the plaintiff alleges that men treated her differently when she was promoted and that she feared revealing a pregnancy to her superior. When the pregnancy was discovered, she alleges that her district manager pressured her to step down from her position. She was demoted in February 2006 and fired in November 2011. The jury on the case ruled in 2014 in favor of the plaintiff, awarding her $185 million in punitive damages as well as approximately $873,000 in back wages. AutoZone has announced its intention to appeal the verdict.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a>'s Jacob Gershman suggested that the verdict would be scaled back, noting that the jury verdict is not the end-all be-all.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p><p>A Georgia woman was fired after a customer insulted her and used multiple racial epithets against her. She alleges that the district manager told her to "suck it up," and is filing a lawsuit against AutoZone for violating her Civil Rights.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p><p>AutoZone came under fire due to allegations that a district manager had told an employee to remove a flag displayed on the company's premises for the sake of improved diversity. AutoZone denies this, claiming that it was due to the poor means by which the flag was put up, using duct tape and suction cups.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2019 AutoZone entered into an $11 million settlement against with the State of California to resolve allegations that the company violated state laws governing hazardous waste, hazardous materials, and confidential consumer information. AutoZone is charged with illegally disposing of millions of hazardous waste items, including used motor oil and automotive fluids, at landfills not authorized to accept hazardous waste.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
