</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=McKesson_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Founded in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> as <i>Charles M. Olcott</i> in 1828 and later as <i>Olcott, McKesson &amp; Co.</i> by Charles Olcott and McKesson in 1833,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> the business began as an <a href="/wiki/International_trade" title="International trade">importer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wholesale" class="mw-redirect" title="Wholesale">wholesaler</a> of botanical drugs. A third partner, McKesson joined the enterprise as it grew, and it was renamed <i>McKesson &amp; Robbins</i> following Olcott's death in 1853.
</p><p>The company successfully emerged from one of the most notorious business/accounting scandals of the 20th century—the <a href="/wiki/McKesson_%26_Robbins_scandal_(1938)" title="McKesson &amp; Robbins scandal (1938)">McKesson &amp; Robbins scandal</a>, a watershed event that led to major changes in American auditing standards and securities regulations after being exposed in 1938. In the 1960s, McKesson &amp; Robbins merged with Foremost Dairies of San Francisco to form Foremost-McKesson Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Foremost was sold in 1982.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>Since the mid-20th century, McKesson has derived an increasing proportion of its income from <a href="/wiki/Medical_technology" class="mw-redirect" title="Medical technology">medical technology</a>, rather than pharmaceuticals. This culminated in its purchase of medical information systems firm HBO &amp; Company (HBOC) in 1999; the combined firm was briefly known as <i>McKessonHBOC</i>. Accounting irregularities at HBOC reduced the company's share price by half, and resulted in the dismissal and prosecution of many HBOC executives. The firm's name reverted to "McKesson" in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <i>McKesson Technology Solutions</i>, as the information technology branch of the company is now known, has continued to increase its market share through acquisitions, notably Per Se Technologies, RelayHealth, and Practice Partner.
</p><p>In 2010, McKesson acquired leading cancer services company US Oncology, Inc. for $2.16 billion, which was integrated into the McKesson Specialty Health business.
</p><p>On June 24, 2013, <i>The Wall Street Journal</i> reported that McKesson Chairman and CEO <a href="/wiki/John_Hammergren" title="John Hammergren">John Hammergren</a>'s pension benefits of $159 million had set a record for "the largest pension on file for a current executive of a public company, and almost certainly the largest ever in corporate America". A study in 2012 by GMI Ratings, which tracks executive pay, found that 60% of CEOs at <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500_Index" title="S&amp;P 500 Index">S&amp;P 500</a> companies have pensions, and their value averages $11.5 million.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In addition to its offices throughout North America, McKesson also has international offices in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. Today, McKesson is one of the oldest continually operating businesses in the United States.
</p><p>In 2014, McKesson acquired <a href="/wiki/Celesio" class="mw-redirect" title="Celesio">Celesio</a> to become one of the world's largest health care companies, with over $179 billion in annual revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>As of August 23, 2016, McKesson has decided to merge a majority of its IT business with <a href="/wiki/Change_Healthcare" title="Change Healthcare">Change Healthcare</a>.
</p><p>In 2017, McKesson was involved in a number of lawsuits against the state of Arkansas over the supply of <a href="/wiki/Vecuronium_bromide" title="Vecuronium bromide">vecuronium bromide</a>. McKesson was under contract by <a href="/wiki/Pfizer" title="Pfizer">Pfizer</a> not to sell to any correctional facility that authorized and carried out <a href="/wiki/Capital_punishment" title="Capital punishment">Capital punishment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2018, the company announced it would relocate its headquarters from San Francisco to <a href="/wiki/Irving,_Texas" title="Irving, Texas">Irving</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> in April 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>On April 1, 2019, the company officially moved their headquarters to <a href="/wiki/Irving,_Texas" title="Irving, Texas">Irving</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> This new campus is easy to drive from either of the two main <a href="/wiki/Dallas/Fort_Worth_International_Airport" title="Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport">Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Love_Field" title="Dallas Love Field">Dallas Love Field</a> airports.
</p><p>In February 2020, three years since the merger, McKesson Corp announced that it will part ways with Change Healthcare. McKesson is set to give up its three seats on Change's board of directors, and as an entity will no longer own any portion of Change.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="McKesson_Provider_Technologies">McKesson Provider Technologies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=McKesson_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: McKesson Provider Technologies" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>McKesson Provider Technologies is the retail name for McKesson Technology Solutions; the software development division of McKesson. Their customer base in the United States includes 50% of all health systems, 20% of all physician practices, 25% of home care agencies, and 77% of health systems with more than 200 beds.
</p><p>On June 20, 2005, McKesson Provider Technologies acquired Medcon, Ltd., an <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israeli</a> company which provides <a href="/wiki/Picture_archiving_and_communication_system" title="Picture archiving and communication system">Web-based cardiac image and information management solutions</a> for heart centers, that includes: diagnostic digital image management, archiving, procedure reporting, and workflow management.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2013, McKesson agreed to buy a 50% stake in German peer <a href="/wiki/Celesio" class="mw-redirect" title="Celesio">Celesio</a> for $8.3 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="McKesson_Medical_Supplies_and_Equipment">McKesson Medical Supplies and Equipment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=McKesson_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: McKesson Medical Supplies and Equipment" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>

<p>McKesson Medical-Surgical (MMS) offers a large selection of national health care brands, along with McKesson's exclusive brand of medical products.
</p><p>Their online medical supply ordering platform serves the needs of physician offices, surgery centers, home health agencies, DMEs, labs, and long-term-care facilities.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015 McKesson Medical-Surgical opened its new headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">Richmond, Virginia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Health_Mart" title="Health Mart">Health Mart</a> is a network of over 4,000<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> independently owned and operated <a href="/wiki/Pharmacies" class="mw-redirect" title="Pharmacies">pharmacies</a>. It is a wholly owned <a href="/wiki/Subsidiary" title="Subsidiary">subsidiary</a> of McKesson Corporation, which owns the name "Health Mart". McKesson acquired Health Mart owner FoxMeyer in October 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-McKesson-Corp-Nov-1996-8-K_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McKesson-Corp-Nov-1996-8-K-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_divisions">Former divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=McKesson_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Former divisions" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>McKesson operated the Mosswood Wine Company from 1978 until 1987, when the division was sold to maintain their focus on pharmaceuticals. The division was founded and run by wine writer <a href="/wiki/Gerald_Asher" title="Gerald Asher">Gerald Asher</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="NDC_Health">NDC Health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=McKesson_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: NDC Health" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p><b>NDC</b> (from the initials of its former identity as <i>National Data Corporation</i>) became NDC-Health Corp in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h5 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Data_Corporation">National Data Corporation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=McKesson_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: National Data Corporation" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h5>
<p><b>National Data Corporation</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Time-sharing" title="Time-sharing">Time-sharing</a> company that began in 1967 and subsequently absorbed competitor <i>Rapidata</i>. Rapidata<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> held on, and became part of <a href="/wiki/National_Data_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="National Data Corporation">National Data Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> It was still of sufficient interest in 1982 to be the focus of "A User's Guide to Statistics Programs: The Rapidata Timesharing System".<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Even as revenue fell by 66%<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and National Data subsequently developed its own problems, attempts were made to keep this timesharing business going.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>Rapidata is listed in 'The AUERBACH Guide to Time Sharing (1973).'<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="McKesson_Canada">McKesson Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=McKesson_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: McKesson Canada" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:McKessonCanada.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/McKessonCanada.jpg/220px-McKessonCanada.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="4928" data-file-height="3264"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 146px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/McKessonCanada.jpg/220px-McKessonCanada.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="146" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:McKessonCanada.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>McKesson Canada</div></div></div>
<p>In 1991, McKesson Corporation acquired a 100 percent interest in Medis Health and Pharmaceutical Services from <a href="/wiki/Provigo" title="Provigo">Provigo</a>. In 2002, the McKesson Canada name was adopted. McKesson Canada is a wholly owned subsidiary of McKesson Corporation. It includes various business units: McKesson Pharmaceutical, McKesson Automation, McKesson Specialty, McKesson Health Solutions and McKesson Information Solutions.
</p><p>In March 2016, McKesson agreed to purchase Canadian pharmacy chain Rexall from the <a href="/wiki/Katz_Group_of_Companies" title="Katz Group of Companies">Katz Group of Companies</a> for $3 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The deal was finalized in December 2016 following approval received under the Investment Canada Act.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2018, McKesson Canada closed 40 <a href="/wiki/Rexall" title="Rexall">Rexall</a> locations in Ontario and Western Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=McKesson_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: United Kingdom" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In the United Kingdom, McKesson (operating as McKesson Information Solutions UK Ltd) was a provider of <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">information technology</a> services to the health care industry. In addition to numerous clinical software systems and finance and procurement services, McKesson also was responsible for developing the <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Staff_Record" title="Electronic Staff Record">Electronic Staff Record</a> system for the <a href="/wiki/National_Health_Service" title="National Health Service">National Health Service</a> which provided an integrated <a href="/wiki/Payroll" title="Payroll">payroll</a> system for NHS's 1.3 million staff, making it the world's largest single payroll IT system. McKesson Shared Services also provided payroll services for over 20 NHS Trusts, paying over 100,000 NHS members.
</p><p>McKesson's United Kingdom base was in <a href="/wiki/Warwick" title="Warwick">Warwick</a> with data centers in <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_upon_Tyne" title="Newcastle upon Tyne">Newcastle upon Tyne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brent_Cross" title="Brent Cross">Brent Cross</a> and offices in <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bangor,_Gwynedd" title="Bangor, Gwynedd">Bangor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vauxhall" title="Vauxhall">Vauxhall</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>. Across the United Kingdom, it employed over 500 people.
</p><p>In June/July 2014 McKesson sold most of their healthcare software business to the private equity firm <a href="/wiki/Symphony_Technology_Group" title="Symphony Technology Group">Symphony Technology Group</a> and indicated also that they would not be re-bidding for the Electronic Staff Record contract.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> This came after the company had posted significant year on year losses in revenue (16% in the 2012/13 financial year<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup>) after taking over a very successful British operation in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia_and_New_Zealand">Australia and New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=McKesson_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Australia and New Zealand" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, McKesson Asia-Pacific was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Medibank" title="Medibank">Medibank Private Ltd.</a><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p><p>McKesson ANZ is a fully owned subsidiary of McKesson Corporation. McKesson expanded its footprint in Australia and New Zealand by acquiring Emendo in November, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-computerworld.co.nz_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-computerworld.co.nz-44">[44]</a></sup> McKesson ANZ develops and sells healthcare optimization services and software. The company has traditionally been focused on the public markets in Australia and New Zealand. The majority of the <a href="/wiki/District_Health_Board" class="mw-redirect" title="District Health Board">District Health Boards</a> in NZ use one or more of McKesson's Capacity Management solutions.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Christchurch" title="Christchurch">Christchurch</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, is one of McKesson's global Capacity Management R&amp;D centers of excellence. All of McKesson's R&amp;D for McKesson Capacity Planner is performed in New Zealand. The company employs approximately 40 team members across Australia and New Zealand including general management, R&amp;D, sales, services and support employees.
</p><p>McKesson Capacity Planner (formerly Emendo CapPlan) is used in more than 40 hospitals in Australia, New Zealand, Britain, Canada and the US<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> to forecast future patient activity and help health systems to allocate resources efficiently and identify unnecessary costs.<sup id="cite_ref-computerworld.co.nz_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-computerworld.co.nz-44">[44]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
