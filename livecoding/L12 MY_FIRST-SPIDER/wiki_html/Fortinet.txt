and Michael Xie. The company's first product was FortiGate, a firewall, later adding <a href="/wiki/Wireless_access_point" title="Wireless access point">wireless access points</a>, sandboxing, and messaging security. By 2004, Fortinet had raised over $90 million in funding. The company went public in November 2009, raising $156 million through an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2016, Fortinet released its Security Fabric architecture that included integration and automation with other network security devices and third-party vendors. The product was later adapted to include multi-cloud, IoT, and SD-WAN capabilities.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_history">Corporate history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fortinet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Corporate history" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fortinet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early history" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Prior to Fortinet, <a href="/wiki/Ken_Xie" title="Ken Xie">Ken Xie</a> founded and served as an executive for <a href="/wiki/NetScreen" class="mw-redirect" title="NetScreen">NetScreen</a>, and Michael Xie served as an executive for ServeGate.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-seven_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-seven-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2000, they co-founded Appligation Inc. The company was renamed to ApSecure in December 2000 and later renamed again to Fortinet, based on the phrase "Fortified Networks."<sup id="cite_ref-one_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-one-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2004, amid fundraising for Fortinet, NetScreen was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Juniper_Networks" title="Juniper Networks">Juniper Networks</a> for $4 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>Fortinet spent two years in <a href="/wiki/Research_and_development" title="Research and development">research and development</a><sup id="cite_ref-nine_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nine-8">[8]</a></sup> before introducing its first product, FortiGate, in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-seven_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-seven-6">[6]</a></sup> The company raised $13 million in private funding from 2000 to early 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-seven_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-seven-6">[6]</a></sup> An additional $30 million in financing was raised in August 2003, followed by $50 million in March 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Fortinet's first channel program was established in October 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-three_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-three-10">[10]</a></sup> The company began distributing its products in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> in December 2003, through Westcon Canada, and in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a> in February 2004 through Norwood Adam.<sup id="cite_ref-seven_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-seven-6">[6]</a></sup> By 2004 Fortinet had offices in <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nine_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nine-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2005, a German court issued a preliminary injunction against Fortinet's UK subsidiary in relation to source code for its <a href="/wiki/GNU_General_Public_License" title="GNU General Public License">GPL</a>-licensed elements.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The dispute ended a month later after Fortinet agreed to make the source code available upon request.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Growth_and_expansion">Growth and expansion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fortinet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Growth and expansion" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Fortinet became profitable in the third quarter of 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Late that year, the company acquired the database security and auditing intellectual property of IPLocks, and extended job offers to the company's 28 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In August 2009, it acquired the intellectual property and other assets of Woven Systems, an ethernet switching company.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>According to market research firm IDC, by November 2009 it held over 15 percent of the unified threat management market.<sup id="cite_ref-public_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-public-17">[17]</a></sup> Also in 2009, <i>CRN Magazine</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s survey-based annual report card (ARC) placed Fortinet first in network security hardware, up from seventh in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2009, Fortinet had an initial public offering, wherein the company planned to raise $52.4 million through the sale of 5.8 million shares.<sup id="cite_ref-public_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-public-17">[17]</a></sup> Over 6 million shares were also sold by stockholders.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Just before the first day of trading, Fortinet increased the share price from $9 to $12.50 and the price increased in the market to $16.62. By the end of the first day of trading the company had raised $156 million in financing.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>By 2010, Fortinet had $324 million in annual revenues<sup id="cite_ref-seven_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-seven-6">[6]</a></sup> and held the largest share of the market according to IDC.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-21">[21]</a></sup> In July 2013, Fortinet made changes to its reseller program, Security Operations Center "SOC" in a Box, in order to provide monthly financing options for <a href="/wiki/Managed_security_service" title="Managed security service">managed security service</a> vendors with less experience and less capital.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The program had previously been reorganized in 2006 for enterprise companies.<sup id="cite_ref-seven_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-seven-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>Fortinet made three notable acquisitions from 2012 to 2015. The company acquired app-hosting service XDN (formerly known as 3Crowd) in December 2012,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Coyote_Point_Systems" title="Coyote Point Systems">Coyote Point</a> in 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> and Wi-Fi hardware company <a href="/wiki/Meru_Networks" title="Meru Networks">Meru Networks</a> in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> In June 2016, Fortinet acquired IT security, monitoring and analytics software vendor, AccelOps.<sup id="cite_ref-Kuranda_2016_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kuranda_2016-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_history">Recent history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fortinet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Recent history" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In July 2014, Fortinet announced a technical certification program called Network Security Expert (NSE) that included eight levels of certification.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Later, in March 2016, the company launched a Network Security Academy to help fill open cyber security jobs in the U.S. Fortinet donated equipment and provided information to universities to help train students for jobs in the field.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Also in 2016, Fortinet launched a program called FortiVet to recruit military veterans for cybersecurity jobs.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2017, it was announced that Philip Quade, a former member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Security_Agency" title="National Security Agency">NSA</a>, would become the company's chief information officer.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> At the end of 2017, Fortinet reported $416.7 million in revenue, a 15 percent increase from the previous year.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-32">[32]</a></sup> As of December 31, 2017 Fortinet had 467 U.S. foreign-issued patents and 291 pending.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> In June 2018, Fortinet acquired Bradford Networks, a maker of access control and IoT security solutions.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> In January 2019, it was announced that Fortinet and founder Ken Xie would participate in the annual World Economic Forum held in Davos, Switzerland.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Products_and_research">Products and research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fortinet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Products and research" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Fortinet released its first product, FortiGate, a firewall, in 2002, followed by anti-spam and anti-virus software.<sup id="cite_ref-seven_6-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-seven-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> FortiGate was updated to use application-specific integrated circuit (ASIC) architecture.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Fortinet products later merged network security, including firewalls, and anti-spam and anti-virus software, into one appliance.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-21">[21]</a></sup> In April 2016, Fortinet began building its Security Fabric architecture so multiple network security products could communicate as one platform.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Later that year, the company added <a href="/wiki/Security_Information_and_Event_Management" class="mw-redirect" title="Security Information and Event Management">Security Information and Event Management</a> (SIEM) products, which analyzed hardware and software security alerts high gigabit port density, and consolidated network security features for mid-sized businesses and enterprise branch locations, in addition to cloud security.<sup id="cite_ref-Gagliordi_2016_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gagliordi_2016-39">[39]</a></sup> In September 2016, the company announced it would integrate the SIEM products with the security systems of other vendors.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, Fortinet announced the addition of switches, access points, analyzers, sandboxes and cloud capabilities to the Security Fabric, in addition to endpoints and firewalls.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Later in 2017, Fortinet created a standalone subsidiary, Fortinet Federal, to develop cybersecurity products for government agencies.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Fortinet has historically received security effectiveness certifications through NSS Labs.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gartner" title="Gartner">Gartner</a>, a research and consulting firm, has ranked Fortinet within the top three companies in its <a href="/wiki/Magic_Quadrant" title="Magic Quadrant">Magic Quadrant</a> for enterprise network firewalls, which measures market trends and direction.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2018, the company launched FortiGate SD-WAN, its proprietary SD-WAN service.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> FortiGate SD-WAN was included within Gartner's Magic Quadrant for WAN Edge Infrastructure later that year.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Later in 2018, Fortinet released FortiGuard (AI) to better detect new and unknown threats, and also announced the 6.0 version of its FortiOS security operating system with enhanced centralized management and expanded cloud capabilities.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>

Presented by Fortinet, February 5, 2018<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p>In May 2004, <a href="/wiki/Trend_Micro" title="Trend Micro">Trend Micro</a>, a competing cyber security and defense company, filed a legal complaint against Fortinet. Though the International Trade Commission initially ruled against Fortinet,<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> the Trend Micro patents at the center of the dispute were later declared as invalid in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> In 2005, Fortinet responded to questions related to an employee's relationship with Burma's Prime Minister and the use of Fortinet appliances for internet censorship in Myanmar. Fortinet stated that their products are sold by third party resellers, and that they acknowledged US embargoes.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fortinet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Research" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2005, Fortinet created the FortiGuard Labs internal security research team.<sup id="cite_ref-SearchSecurity_2016_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SearchSecurity_2016-53">[53]</a></sup> In 2008, Fortinet researchers sent a report to Facebook highlighting a widget from Zango that appeared to be tricking users into downloading spyware.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> By 2014, Fortinet had four research and development centers in Asia, as well as others in the US, Canada and <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> In March 2014, Fortinet founded the Cyber Threat Alliance (CTA) with <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto_Networks" title="Palo Alto Networks">Palo Alto Networks</a> in order to share security threat data across vendors.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> It was later joined by <a href="/wiki/McAfee" title="McAfee">McAfee</a> and <a href="/wiki/NortonLifeLock" title="NortonLifeLock">Symantec</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> In 2015, the CTA published a white paper on the CryptoWall ransomware, which detailed how attackers obtained $325 million through ransoms paid by victims to regain access to their files.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2015, Fortinet provided threat intelligence to <a href="/wiki/Interpol" title="Interpol">Interpol</a> in order to help apprehend the ringleader of several online scams based in <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a>. The scams, which resulted in compromise of business emails and CEO fraud, had cost one business over $15 million.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> The following year, in March 2016, Fortinet and technology company, <a href="/wiki/Cisco_Systems" title="Cisco Systems">Cisco</a>, joined <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> in a data-sharing agreement to improve their information security capabilities.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2017, Fortinet worked with Interpol to conduct an investigation into web security in several southeast Asian countries. The investigation identified compromised websites, including government-operated web servers.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> Later that month, Fortinet researchers discovered a spyware that scammed victims by impersonating the <a href="/wiki/IRS" class="mw-redirect" title="IRS">IRS</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> Also in 2017, researchers helped identify malware, called Rootnik, and <a href="/wiki/Ransomware" title="Ransomware">ransomware</a>, called MacRansom, that targeted Android and MacOS systems respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> In 2018, Fortinet entered into an information-sharing agreement with Interpol.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fortinet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Controversy" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="SSH_backdoor">SSH backdoor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fortinet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: SSH backdoor" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2019 security researchers found a SSH backdoor in FortiOS (<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2019-16153">CVE-2019-16153</a>). The backdoor entailed a hard coded password that allowed access to affected Fortinet products. Initially Fortinet did not acknowledge the backdoor and instead characterised it as a "management authentication issue".<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Whistleblower_lawsuit">Whistleblower lawsuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fortinet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Whistleblower lawsuit" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Fortinet has settled a whistleblower lawsuit in 2019 regarding sales of mislabeled equipment to U.S. government end users. The U.S. Marine Corps was one of the affected agencies.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup></p>
