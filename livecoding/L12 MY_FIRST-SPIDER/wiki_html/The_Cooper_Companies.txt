<p><b>The Cooper Companies, Inc.</b>, branded as <b>CooperCompanies</b>, is a global medical device company, publicly traded on the NYSE (NYSE:COO). With its headquarters in <a href="/wiki/San_Ramon,_California" title="San Ramon, California">San Ramon</a>, California, it has a workforce of more than 12,000 employees<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and consists of two business units, <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://www.coopervision.com">[1]</a> CooperVision (CVI) which manufactures contact lenses for the vision care market, and <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.coopersurgical.com/">CooperSurgical</a> (CSI) which manufactures medical devices and fertility and genomic products for the women’s health care market. </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Cooper_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>CooperCompanies was founded in 1958 as Martin H. Smith Co. and later incorporated as Cooper Tinsley Laboratories Inc. in 1961. The company changed its name to Cooper Laboratories Inc. in 1967. Cooper Laboratories entered the contact lens business in 1972 when it acquired British lens maker GlobalVision.
</p><p>In 1980, Cooper Laboratories reorganized into three business groups: CooperVision, CooperCare and CooperBiomedical, with Cooper Medical Devices Corp. added as a fourth group one year later. 
</p><p>The company was renamed The Cooper Companies in 1987. Three years later, the company was further restructured into three business units: CooperVision, CooperSurgical and CooperVision Pharmaceuticals. The latter was dissolved in 2003. 
</p><p>Today, CooperCompanies operates as two business units: CooperVision and CooperSurgical, which have continued to grow through various acquisitions and expansions.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>Albert G. White III was named President and CEO of CooperCompanies in May 2018, leading the company toward continued investments in product innovation, strategic acquisitions, and geographic expansion to create a better future for its customers, patients and stakeholders.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_Units">Business Units</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Cooper_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Business Units" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>CooperVision is the contact lens unit of CooperCompanies, serving contact lens wearers and eye care practitioners. CooperVision was incorporated in 1980 and today is one of the world’s largest manufacturers of soft contact lenses. Its contact lenses and related products are sold to eye care practices in over 100 countries. Products include a range of daily, two-week and monthly disposable contact lenses under the brand names MyDay®, clariti® 1 day, Avaira Vitality™, and Biofinity®, and other spherical, toric and multifocal lenses for astigmatism, nearsightedness and farsightedness, and presbyopia.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>CooperSurgical is the medical device and fertility and genomics unit of CooperCompanies, serving the health care needs of women, babies and families. CooperSurgical became a business unit of the company in 1990. It has over 600 products primarily used by women’s health care providers in their offices, clinics, operating rooms, labor, and delivery suites and reproductive IVF clinics worldwide. <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_and_Wellness">Health and Wellness</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Cooper_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Health and Wellness" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>CooperCompanies launched its employee wellness program in 2009 to support the well-being of its employees and the communities where they live and work. The robust program takes a holistic approach to wellness, providing a breadth of activities and programs organized locally at sites around the world. 
CooperCompanies offer a robust, global wellness program that provides a breadth of activities and programs organized locally at sites around the world that have a meaningful impact. The company takes a holistic approach to wellness and employees take an active role in driving this long-standing program. 
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Cooper_Giving">Cooper Giving</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Cooper_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cooper Giving" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>CooperCompanies selected Optometry Giving Sight and March of Dimes as the beneficiaries of its global charitable giving program. Through the Cooper Giving program, company employees can donate year-round to both non-profits, with Cooper providing a one-for-one match.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>CooperCompanies is also active in local philanthropic efforts, supporting communities in which employees live and work. 
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Sustainability">Sustainability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Cooper_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sustainability" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<p>As part of its global sustainability efforts, over a two-year period, CooperVision converted its three Rochester, N.Y.-area sites to 100 percent renewable electricity usage.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> As of 2019, the sites now completely rely on wind power.
</p><p>The Puerto Rico Manufacturers Association (PRMA) twice awarded the CooperVision Juana Díaz, Puerto Rico site with the Environmental Innovation Project of the Year Award. <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2017, CooperVision was recognized for minimizing raw materials usage, waste, and emissions at its Puerto Rico facility by more than 60 percent. The site also received the Puerto Rico Aqueduct and Water Authority’s (PRASA) Pre-Treatment Excellence Compliance Award for its water conservation efforts for four consecutive years.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>The CooperVision contact lens manufacturing facility in Alajuela, Costa Rica, which opened in February 2016, was awarded LEED® Silver certification for its environmentally-conscious design and operation. <sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>These contributions are part of Cooper’s alignment with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, including a focus on good health and well-being as well as establishing complementary partnerships.
</p>
