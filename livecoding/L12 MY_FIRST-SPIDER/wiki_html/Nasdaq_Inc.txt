<p><b>Nasdaq, Inc.</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> <a href="/wiki/Financial_services" title="Financial services">financial services</a> corporation that owns and operates (and is listed on) the <a href="/wiki/Nasdaq" title="Nasdaq">Nasdaq</a> stock market and eight European Stock Exchanges, including the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Stock_Exchange" class="mw-redirect" title="Armenian Stock Exchange">Armenian Stock Exchange</a>, <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen_Stock_Exchange" title="Copenhagen Stock Exchange">Copenhagen Stock Exchange</a>, <a href="/wiki/Helsinki_Stock_Exchange" title="Helsinki Stock Exchange">Helsinki Stock Exchange</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iceland_Stock_Exchange" title="Iceland Stock Exchange">Iceland Stock Exchange</a>, <a href="/wiki/Riga_Stock_Exchange" title="Riga Stock Exchange">Riga Stock Exchange</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_Stock_Exchange" title="Stockholm Stock Exchange">Stockholm Stock Exchange</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tallinn_Stock_Exchange" title="Tallinn Stock Exchange">Tallinn Stock Exchange</a>, and <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ_OMX_Vilnius" title="NASDAQ OMX Vilnius">NASDAQ OMX Vilnius</a>. It is headquartered in New York City, and its president and chief executive officer is  <a href="/wiki/Adena_Friedman" title="Adena Friedman">Adena Friedman</a>.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasdaq,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Nasdaq, Inc. is the owner of the <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a> listing, founded in 1975.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Merger_attempt_with_London_Stock_Exchange">Merger attempt with London Stock Exchange</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasdaq,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Merger attempt with London Stock Exchange" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In December 2005, the <a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange_Group" title="London Stock Exchange Group">London Stock Exchange Group</a> (LSE) rejected a £1.6 billion takeover offer from <a href="/wiki/Macquarie_Bank" class="mw-redirect" title="Macquarie Bank">Macquarie Bank</a>. The LSE described the offer as "derisory". It then received a bid in March 2006 for £2.4 billion from NASDAQ, which was also rejected by the LSE. NASDAQ later pulled its bid, and less than two weeks later on April 11, 2006, struck a deal with LSE's largest shareholder, <a href="/wiki/Ameriprise_Financial" title="Ameriprise Financial">Ameriprise Financial</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Ameriprise_Financial" title="Ameriprise Financial">Threadneedle Asset Management</a> unit, to acquire all of that firm's stake, consisting of 35.4 million shares, at £11.75 per share.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj_20060411_nasdaq_stake_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj_20060411_nasdaq_stake-2">[2]</a></sup> NASDAQ also purchased 2.69 million additional shares, resulting in a total stake of 15%. While the seller of those shares was undisclosed, it occurred simultaneously with a sale by <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Widows" title="Scottish Widows">Scottish Widows</a> of 2.69 million shares.<sup id="cite_ref-forbes_nasdaq_sw_20060412_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes_nasdaq_sw_20060412-3">[3]</a></sup> The move was seen as an effort to force LSE to negotiate either a partnership or eventual merger, as well as to block other suitors such as <a href="/wiki/NYSE_Euronext" title="NYSE Euronext">NYSE Euronext</a>, owner of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bbg_nasdaq_stake_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbg_nasdaq_stake-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>Subsequent purchases increased NASDAQ's stake to 29%, holding off competing bids for several months. However, only a further 0.4% of shareholders accepted the offer by the deadline and therefore the offer was rejected<sup id="cite_ref-lse_rejects_nasdaq_offer_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lse_rejects_nasdaq_offer-5">[5]</a></sup> on February 10, 2007.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Boston_and_Philadelphia_Exchanges_purchase">Boston and Philadelphia Exchanges purchase</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasdaq,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Boston and Philadelphia Exchanges purchase" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On October 2, 2007, NASDAQ purchased the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Stock_Exchange" title="Boston Stock Exchange">Boston Stock Exchange</a>. On November 7, NASDAQ announced an agreement to purchase the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Stock_Exchange" title="Philadelphia Stock Exchange">Philadelphia Stock Exchange</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="OMX_purchase">OMX purchase</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasdaq,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: OMX purchase" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On May 25, 2007, NASDAQ agreed to buy <a href="/wiki/OMX" class="mw-redirect" title="OMX">OMX</a>, the Swedish-Finnish financial company that controls 7 Nordic and Baltic stock exchanges, for US$3.7 billion to form NASDAQ OMX Group.<sup id="cite_ref-Reuters2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters2-6">[6]</a></sup> As of February 27, 2008, the deal was completed.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Sustainable_Stock_Exchanges_initiative">Sustainable Stock Exchanges initiative</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasdaq,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sustainable Stock Exchanges initiative" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On June 18, 2012, NASDAQ became a founding member of the United Nations <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_Stock_Exchanges_Initiative" title="Sustainable Stock Exchanges Initiative">Sustainable Stock Exchanges initiative</a> on the eve of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Conference_on_Sustainable_Development" title="United Nations Conference on Sustainable Development">United Nations Conference on Sustainable Development (Rio+20)</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisition_of_Thomson_Reuters_businesses">Acquisition of Thomson Reuters businesses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasdaq,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Acquisition of Thomson Reuters businesses" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On December 12, 2012, NASDAQ OMX announced that it would acquire <a href="/wiki/Thomson_Reuters" title="Thomson Reuters">Thomson Reuters</a>' investor relations, public relations and multimedia businesses for $390 million in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> NASDAQ OMX completed the purchase on June 3, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Launch_of_Nasdaq_Ventures">Launch of Nasdaq Ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasdaq,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Launch of Nasdaq Ventures" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In April 2017, Nasdaq launched Nasdaq Ventures, a venture investment program focused on companies that will help Nasdaq grow its product lines and open new market segments. The first 3 companies announced as part of the program are Chain, a blockchain technology company; <a href="/wiki/Digital_Reasoning" title="Digital Reasoning">Digital Reasoning</a>, cognitive computing technology; and Hanweck, real-time risk analytics.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Bid_for_Oslo_Stock_Exchange">Bid for <a href="/wiki/Oslo_Stock_Exchange" title="Oslo Stock Exchange">Oslo Stock Exchange</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasdaq,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Bid for Oslo Stock Exchange" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>During Christmas of 2018, shareholders representing 25% of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Oslo_B%C3%B8rs_VPS_Holding&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Oslo Børs VPS Holding (page does not exist)">Oslo Børs VPS Holding</a> (the Norwegian Stock Exchange and national CSD operator) <sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> held an private auction of share sale. <a href="/wiki/Nasdaq" title="Nasdaq">Nasdaq</a> did not participate in the auction due the hostile nature of the bid (held without Oslo Børs boards knowledge or approval). <a href="/wiki/Euronext" title="Euronext">Euronext</a> has won the auction, and later secured another 24,6% of shareholder support, totalling 49.6%. Following, Nasdaq acquired 32.5% shares in open market (mainly from individual shareholders/employees),<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and submitted an official bid, with unanimous recommendations from board and some key shareholders,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> to acquire remaining shares for 152 NOK, and later increased offer to 158 NOK (or almost 44% premium of Dec 17th, 2018 closing price, to mach Euronext offer),<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> additionally making the case to Norway's markets regulator that in cases like this, 2/3 of the share control may be necessary to comply with any applicable regulatory requirement. In the end regulator did not side with the two-thirds requirements, and general majority was deemed to be applicable. Euronext by that time had acquired or secured control of 50.5% shares,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and Nasdaq had announced on May 25, 2019 that they were pulling out of the Oslo Bors battle, handing Euronext the victory.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Additional_services">Additional services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasdaq,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Additional services" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>NASDAQ Inc. partners with stock exchanges all over the world. One of the most recent partnerships was signed with Astana International Financial Centre (AIFC) in May 2017. According to the agreement, NASDAQ will power Kazakhstan's nascent stock exchange, the AIFC Exchange.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Global_Information_Services">Global Information Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasdaq,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Global Information Services" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In January 2013, NASDAQ OMX announced that it would combine its global data products and index businesses into a unit called Global Information Services, as part of an ongoing effort to broaden its portfolio.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Directors_Desk">Directors Desk</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasdaq,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Directors Desk" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On June 29, 2007, NASDAQ entered into an agreement to acquire <a href="/wiki/Directors_Desk" title="Directors Desk">DirectorsDesk</a>.com, a management suite for boards of directors.<sup id="cite_ref-AquireDirectorsDesk_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AquireDirectorsDesk-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="GlobeNewswire">GlobeNewswire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasdaq,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: GlobeNewswire" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/GlobeNewswire" title="GlobeNewswire">GlobeNewswire</a> (previously PrimeNewswire) provides press release, editing and wire services. It was founded in 1998 and acquired by NASDAQ OMX in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="SMARTS">SMARTS</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasdaq,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: SMARTS" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On July 27, 2010, NASDAQ OMX Group, Inc. acquired SMARTS Group, the world-leading technology provider of <a href="/wiki/Market_surveillance" class="mw-redirect" title="Market surveillance">market surveillance</a> solutions to exchanges, regulators and brokers.<sup id="cite_ref-AquireSMARTS_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AquireSMARTS-21">[21]</a></sup> SMARTS Group had been a private company operating in Sydney, Australia, incorporating the market analysis software of <a href="/wiki/Michael_James_Aitken" title="Michael James Aitken">Michael James Aitken</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> By 2017 SMARTS remained the leading market surveillance software,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and was employed by thirteen regulators on forty-five exchanges.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Carpenter_Moore">Carpenter Moore</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nasdaq,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Carpenter Moore" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>NASDAQ OMX sold its stake in the Carpenter Moore D&amp;O Insurance in 2009.
</p>
