</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexandria_Real_Estate_Equities&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In 1993, the partners of <a href="/wiki/Jacobs_Engineering_Group" title="Jacobs Engineering Group">Jacobs Engineering Group</a> approached Joel S. Marcus, a lawyer and CPA, with the idea of starting a company to provide laboratories and office space to biotech firms. The company was founded in 1994 and Jacobs funded the new company with $5 million.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>Its first purchase was of 4 buildings in San Diego.<sup id="cite_ref-trends_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-trends-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In 1997, it became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>, raising $155 million.<sup id="cite_ref-trends_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-trends-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2002, the company acquired the headquarters of <a href="/wiki/ZymoGenetics" title="ZymoGenetics">ZymoGenetics</a> for $52 million in a <a href="/wiki/Leaseback" title="Leaseback">leaseback</a> transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 2007, the company began development of the West Tower of the <a href="/wiki/MaRS_Discovery_District" title="MaRS Discovery District">MaRS Discovery District</a> in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The company stopped construction during the <a href="/wiki/Financial_crisis_of_2007-08" class="mw-redirect" title="Financial crisis of 2007-08">financial crisis of 2007-08</a>, and in 2014, it sold its interest to the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Ontario" title="Government of Ontario">Government of Ontario</a> for $65 million.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2013, the company sold a research facility in Seattle to <a href="/wiki/Trammell_Crow_Company" title="Trammell Crow Company">Trammell Crow Company</a> for $42.6 million.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2017, it was added to the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500_Index" title="S&amp;P 500 Index">S&amp;P 500 Index</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2018, the company acquired an office building leased to <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon.com">Amazon.com</a> in Seattle from <a href="/wiki/The_Blackstone_Group" title="The Blackstone Group">The Blackstone Group</a> for $95 million.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2018, the company acquired 219 East 42nd Street, the headquarters of <a href="/wiki/Pfizer" title="Pfizer">Pfizer</a>, for $203 million in a <a href="/wiki/Leaseback" title="Leaseback">leaseback</a> transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
