<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">This article is about the enterprise focused company that was spun-off from Hewlett-Packard. For the predecessor company, see <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard</a>. For the printer and personal computer company, see <a href="/wiki/HP_Inc." title="HP Inc.">HP Inc.</a></div>
<p>The <b>Hewlett Packard Enterprise Company</b> (commonly referred to as <b>HPE</b>) is an American multinational enterprise <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">information technology</a> company based in <a href="/wiki/San_Jose,_California" title="San Jose, California">San Jose</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> founded on 1 November 2015 as part of splitting of the <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard</a> company. HPE is a business-focused organization with two divisions: Enterprise Group, which works in servers, storage, networking, consulting and support, and Financial Services. On 31 October HPE reported FY2019 annual revenue of $29.135 billion, down 5.57% from the prior year period.<sup id="cite_ref-2019-revenue-macrotrends_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fy_10-k-1">[1]</a></sup></td></tr></tbody></table><p>The split was structured so that the former <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard Company</a> would change its name to <a href="/wiki/HP_Inc." title="HP Inc.">HP Inc.</a> and spin off Hewlett Packard Enterprise as a newly created company. HP Inc. retained the old HP's personal computer and printing business, as well as its stock-price history and original <a href="/wiki/NYSE" class="mw-redirect" title="NYSE">NYSE</a> ticker symbol for Hewlett-Packard; Enterprise trades under its own ticker symbol: HPE. According to notes from 2015,<sup id="cite_ref-2019-revenue-macrotrends_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2019-revenue-macrotrends-3">[3]</a></sup> HPE's revenue was slightly less than that of HP Inc. In 2017, it spun off its Enterprise Services business and merged it with <a href="/wiki/Computer_Sciences_Corporation" title="Computer Sciences Corporation">Computer Sciences Corporation</a> to become <a href="/wiki/DXC_Technology" title="DXC Technology">DXC Technology</a>. It also spun off its software business and merged it with <a href="/wiki/Micro_Focus" title="Micro Focus">Micro Focus</a>. HPE ranked No. 107 in the 2018 <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> list of the largest United States corporations by total revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Naming">Naming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hewlett_Packard_Enterprise&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Naming" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The full name for the company is "Hewlett Packard Enterprise Company", which drops the hyphen that previously existed between the "Hewlett" and "Packard" of the former Hewlett-Packard Company. The company is commonly referred to as "Hewlett Packard Enterprise" or by its initialism "HPE". The company has also been referred to as "HP Enterprise" by some media outlets<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and has even been incorrectly referred to as "HP Enterprises".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hewlett_Packard_Enterprise&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In May 2016, the company announced it would sell its enterprise services division to one of its competitors, <a href="/wiki/Computer_Sciences_Corporation" title="Computer Sciences Corporation">Computer Sciences Corporation</a> in a deal valued at <span style="white-space: nowrap"><a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>8.5 billion</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-St._Louis_Post-Dispatch:_HP_Enterprise_selling_tech_services_business_to_rival_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-St._Louis_Post-Dispatch:_HP_Enterprise_selling_tech_services_business_to_rival-7">[7]</a></sup> The merger of HPE Enterprise Services with CSC, to form a new company <a href="/wiki/DXC_Technology" title="DXC Technology">DXC Technology</a>, was completed on 10 March 2017. Approximately 100,000 current HPE employees are affected. More than 30,000 services employees from other areas of the HPE business will remain at HPE including technology services support and consulting as well as software professional services.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In August 2016, the company announced plans to acquire <a href="/wiki/Silicon_Graphics_International" title="Silicon Graphics International">Silicon Graphics International</a> (SGI), known for their capabilities in high performance computing.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On 1 November 2016, HPE announced it completed the acquisition, for <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$7.75</span> per share in cash,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> a transaction valued at approximately <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$275 million</span>, net of cash and debt.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>On 7 September 2016, HPE announced a "spin-merge" with <a href="/wiki/Micro_Focus" title="Micro Focus">Micro Focus</a>, where Micro Focus would acquire its "non-core" software (which included the <a href="/wiki/HP_Autonomy" title="HP Autonomy">HP Autonomy</a> unit), and HPE shareholders would own 50.1 percent of the merged company, which would retain its current name.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The merger concluded on 1 September 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2016, <i><a href="/wiki/PC_World" title="PC World">PC World</a></i> wrote "HPE, and before it, Hewlett-Packard, failed to develop middleware tools to really make a dent in the software market, where other companies like <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a>, <a href="/wiki/SAP_SE" title="SAP SE">SAP</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Oracle_Corporation" title="Oracle Corporation">Oracle</a> are excelling" and that "without major software product lines, HPE’s integrated offerings won’t be as strong as competitors like Dell, which have the software and hardware assets, adding that "If all HPE is doing at this point is focusing largely on hardware, you have to ask what the end game here is."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2016, Hewlett Packard Enterprise transferred two patents to a Texas-based wholly owned shell company Plectrum LLC. These two patents were originated at the 3Com Corporation, which was bought by HP in 2010, along with about 1,400 patents. <span><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//www.google.com/patents/US6205149">US Patent No. 6,205,149</a></span> is entitled "Quality of service control mechanism and apparatus," while <span><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//www.google.com/patents/US5978951">US Patent No. 5,978,951</a></span> describes the use of a "high speed cache management unit" which replaces some software-based systems with hardware in order to reduce latency time.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>On 11 April 2017, it was reported that <a href="/wiki/Synack" title="Synack">Synack</a> had raised <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$21 million</span> in a round of funding that included Hewlett Packard Enterprise.<sup id="cite_ref-ft-hacker_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ft-hacker-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2017, the company acquired data management platform SimpliVity, the developer of the OmniCube <a href="/wiki/Hyper-converged_infrastructure" title="Hyper-converged infrastructure">hyper-converged infrastructure</a> appliance, for US$650M.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2017, Hewlett Packard Enterprise completed its acquisition of hybrid flash and all flash manufacturer, <a href="/wiki/Nimble_Storage" title="Nimble Storage">Nimble Storage Inc</a>, for <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$1.2 billion</span> or <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$12.50</span> per share. In October, <a href="/wiki/Reuters" title="Reuters">Reuters</a> reported that the company had allowed a Russian defense agency to examine a cyber-defense system used by <a href="/wiki/The_Pentagon" title="The Pentagon">The Pentagon</a>. The report noted: "Six former U.S. intelligence officials, as well as former ArcSight [Hewlett Packard Enterprise] employees and independent security experts, said the source code review could help Moscow discover weaknesses in the software, potentially helping attackers to blind the U.S. military to a cyber attack."<sup id="cite_ref-Reuters171002_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters171002-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2017, <a href="/wiki/Meg_Whitman" title="Meg Whitman">Meg Whitman</a> announced that she will be stepping down as CEO, after six years at the helm of HP and HPE, stating that, on 1 February 2018, Antonio Neri<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> officially became HPE's President and Chief Executive Officer. The announcement created controversy leading to a 6% drop in stock price, which quickly recovered during the next few days.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2018, Hewlett Packard Enterprise launched a <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_cloud" class="mw-redirect" title="Hybrid cloud">hybrid cloud</a> service called GreenLake Hybrid Cloud, which is built on top of HPE's OneSphere cloud management <a href="/wiki/Software_as_a_Service" class="mw-redirect" title="Software as a Service">SaaS</a> offering.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> GreenLake Hybrid Cloud is designed to provide cloud management, cost control, and compliance control capabilities, and will run on <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Web_Services" title="Amazon Web Services">AWS</a> and <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Azure" title="Microsoft Azure">Microsoft Azure</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2019, Meg Whitman announced that she will not be seeking re-election to the board of directors, ending her professional involvement in HPE.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2019, Hewlett Packard Enterprise announced plans to acquire <a href="/wiki/Cray" title="Cray">Cray</a> for nearly <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$1.3 billion</span> or <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$35</span> per share, soon after Cray landed a <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$600 million</span> <a href="/wiki/US_Department_of_Energy" class="mw-redirect" title="US Department of Energy">US Department of Energy</a> contract to supply the <a href="/wiki/Frontier_(supercomputer)" title="Frontier (supercomputer)">Frontier supercomputer</a> to <a href="/wiki/Oak_Ridge_National_Laboratory" title="Oak Ridge National Laboratory">Oak Ridge National Laboratory</a> in 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2019, Hewlett Packard Enterprise completed the acquisition of Cray for <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$1.4 billion</span><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
