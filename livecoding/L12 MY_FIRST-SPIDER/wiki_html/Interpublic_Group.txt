<p><b>The Interpublic Group of Companies, Inc.</b> (<b>IPG</b>) is an American publicly traded advertising company. The company consists of five major networks: <a href="/wiki/FCB_(advertising_agency)" title="FCB (advertising agency)">FCB</a>, IPG Mediabrands, McCann Worldgroup, <a href="/wiki/MullenLowe_Group" title="MullenLowe Group">MullenLowe Group</a>, and Marketing Specialists, as well as a number of independent specialty agencies in the areas of public relations, sports marketing, talent representation, and healthcare.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>  It is one of the "Big Four" agency companies, alongside <a href="/wiki/WPP_plc" title="WPP plc">WPP</a>, <a href="/wiki/Publicis" title="Publicis">Publicis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Omnicom_Group" title="Omnicom Group">Omnicom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Advertising's_Big_Four:_It's_Their_World_Now_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Advertising's_Big_Four:_It's_Their_World_Now-4">[4]</a></sup>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Interpublic_Group_of_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>On October 2, 1930, IPG was founded in New York City as <a href="/wiki/McCann_Erickson" class="mw-redirect" title="McCann Erickson">McCann-Erickson</a>, when H.K. McCann Co. (founded in 1911) and Erickson Co. (founded in 1902) merged.<sup id="cite_ref-businessinsider1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessinsider1-5">[5]</a></sup> At the time, it was the largest agency in the ad industry.<sup id="cite_ref-adage100_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adage100-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 1960, McCann was restructured into four operating units, each reporting to a new holding company. The four units were McCann-Erickson Advertising (U.S.), McCann-Erickson Corp. (international), McCann-Marschalk and Communications Affiliates.  The latter consisted of diversified communications agencies.<sup id="cite_ref-adage100_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adage100-6">[6]</a></sup> In January 1961, McCann was renamed Interpublic Group (IPG) and created the first marketing services management <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a> with McCann-Erickson as a subsidiary.<sup id="cite_ref-adage101_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adage101-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In Fall 1973, as clients began focusing more on international strategies, McCann-Erickson combined its domestic and international branches into a single worldwide agency under IPG.<sup id="cite_ref-adage100_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adage100-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 1997, the McCann-Erickson World Group was formed with several of IPG's different units. <sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2000, Deutsch Inc, reported by the New York Times at the time to be the largest and last big independent agency, was acquired by IPG to be an autonomous unit.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 2001, IPG acquired <b>True North Communications</b>, the holding company for <a href="/wiki/FCB_(advertising_agency)" title="FCB (advertising agency)">Foote Cone &amp; Belding</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> By then, in addition to True North, Interpublic's primary holdings included two wholly owned global advertising agency networks, McCann-Erickson Worldwide and the Lowe Group; five global specialized-services networks, Initiative Media Worldwide, Draft Worldwide, NFO Worldwide, Octagon, and Zentropy Partners; an Internet-services company; and Allied Communications Group, which managed Interpublic's specialized communications companies.<sup id="cite_ref-adage102_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adage102-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 2003, IPG agreed to pay $115 million to settle class action lawsuits brought by its shareholders. The shareholders had sued Interpublic after accounting irregularities led to restatements of financial results, causing the company's stock to fall.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2004, IPG renamed its McCann-Erickson World Group as McCann Worldgroup.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>On September 15, 2005, IPG announced plans to restate earnings for fiscal 2000 through 2004, due to problems in accounting for revenue, acquisitions and lease expenses.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>  On March 22, 2006, IPG posted a loss for the fourth quarter and restated results for the first three quarters of 2005, which reduced revenue by $14.1 million. It also announced its controller and chief accounting officer was leaving the company.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>IPG disposed of 51 businesses in 2005 and 2006, primarily outside the U.S.; the company said it exited 23 "loss-making international affiliates" in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> IPG in April 2007 announced it agreed to buy Reprise Media, a search engine marketing firm.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In July of that same year, FCB-Ulka Advertising Private Limited, a subsidiary of IPG, was renamed DRAFTFCB+Ulka Advertising Pvt. Ltd.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2008, IPG formed a media buying and planning unit called Mediabrands (later IPG Mediabrands).<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2014, IPG draftfcb was renamed as IPG's international network FCB.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2016, the merger of IPG groups Mullen in the US and Lowe and Partners worldwide was renamed as MullenLowe Group.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2018, IPG announced they were buying database marketing company <a href="/wiki/LiveRamp" title="LiveRamp">Acxiom</a>'s Marketing Solutions business for $USD2.3 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Companies">Companies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Interpublic_Group_of_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Companies" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>IPG consists of dozens of businesses organized into six groups. There are three global networks: Foote, Cone &amp; Belding (FCB); McCann Worldgroup; and MullenLowe; a media services company, IPG Mediabrands; a marketing services group, Marketing Specialists; and several independent domestic digital agencies.<sup id="cite_ref-2018filings_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2018filings-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/FCB_(advertising_agency)" title="FCB (advertising agency)">FCB</a>, the former Draftfcb, formed by a merger between Draft Worldwide, and Foote, Cone &amp; Belding Worldwide. Currently owned by Syed Gousul Alam Shaon. MD and country head of Grey Dhaka.</li>
<li>FCB Health, a healthcare-focused agency formed by combining three healthcare agencies: ICC, Trio and Pace.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
<li>HelloComputer, a <a href="/wiki/Cape_Town" title="Cape Town">Cape Town</a>-based digital agency, which merged with Mesh Interactive in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></li>
<li>New Honor Society, a <a href="/wiki/St._Louis,_Missouri" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Louis, Missouri">St. Louis, Missouri</a>-based marketing agency, formerly known at Rivet/US, itself formed with a merger between marketing agency Zipatoni Company and Toronto-based FCB Direct.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></li></ul><h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="IPG_Mediabrands">IPG Mediabrands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Interpublic_Group_of_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: IPG Mediabrands" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
