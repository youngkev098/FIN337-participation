<p><b>NextEra Energy, Inc.</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Fortune_200" class="mw-redirect" title="Fortune 200">Fortune 200</a> energy company with about 45,900 megawatts of generating capacity, revenues of over $17 billion in 2017, and about 14,000 employees throughout the United States and Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-info_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-info-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-annual_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-annual-2">[2]</a></sup> It is the largest electric utility holding company by market capitalization. Its subsidiaries include <a href="/wiki/Florida_Power_%26_Light" title="Florida Power &amp; Light">Florida Power &amp; Light</a>, <a href="/wiki/NextEra_Energy_Resources" title="NextEra Energy Resources">NextEra Energy Resources</a>, <a href="/wiki/NextEra_Energy_Partners" title="NextEra Energy Partners">NextEra Energy Partners</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Power_Company" title="Gulf Power Company">Gulf Power Company</a>, and NextEra Energy Services. FPL, the largest of the subsidiaries, delivers rate-regulated electricity to approximately 5 million customer accounts, or an estimated 10 million people, across nearly half of <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> and is the third largest electric utility company in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-divisions_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-divisions-3">[3]</a></sup> NEER, together with its affiliated entities, is the world's largest generator of renewable energy from the wind and sun.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In addition to wind and solar, NextEra Energy Resources owns and operates generating plants powered by natural gas, nuclear energy, and oil.<sup id="cite_ref-divisions_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-divisions-3">[3]</a></sup>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_history">Corporate history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=NextEra_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Corporate history" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Florida_Power_%26_Light#History" title="Florida Power &amp; Light">Florida Power &amp; Light § History</a></div>
<p>The predecessor company <a href="/wiki/Florida_Power_%26_Light" title="Florida Power &amp; Light">Florida Power &amp; Light Company</a> was founded in 1925.<sup id="cite_ref-annual_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-annual-2">[2]</a></sup> The holding company <b>FPL Group Inc.</b> was formed in 1984, after the company began efforts to diversify its business. <sup id="cite_ref-palm_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-palm-1">[1]</a></sup> FPL Group changed its name to NextEra Energy Inc. in March 2010. At this time, the stock ticker symbol also changed from FPL to NEE.
</p><p>Nextera Energy is incorporated in Florida.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Mergers_and_acquisitions">Mergers and acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=NextEra_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mergers and acquisitions" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In March 2000, FPL Group and Spanish company <a href="/wiki/Iberdrola" title="Iberdrola">Iberdrola</a> SA were reported to be in "extensive merger discussions".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The  talks were terminated and the merger transaction was not consummated due to opposition from Iberdrola's board, according to news reports. <sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2000, FPL Group and Entergy Corporation announced plans to merge. The companies terminated the merger in April 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2005, FPL Energy, a subsidiary of FPL Group, acquired <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Gexa_Energy" title="Gexa Energy">Gexa Energy</a> in June 2005 for $80.6 million.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2005, FPL Group announced an $11 billion all-stock acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Constellation_Energy" class="mw-redirect" title="Constellation Energy">Constellation Energy</a> Group.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The companies mutually agreed to terminate the sale in October 2006 due to uncertainty over regulatory and judicial matters in Maryland.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>On December 4, 2014, NextEra Energy announced its plans to purchase <a href="/wiki/Hawaiian_Electric_Industries" title="Hawaiian Electric Industries">Hawaiian Electric Industries</a> for $4.3 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> However, in July 2016, <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>'s Public Utilities Commission rejected the offer in a 2-0 vote over doubts of NextEra Energy's commitment to the state's renewable energy goal, which terminated the merger agreement.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The proposed merger had support from over 25 local groups, including the <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a> State <a href="/wiki/AFL%E2%80%93CIO" title="AFL–CIO">AFL–CIO</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a> <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Commerce" class="mw-redirect" title="Chamber of Commerce">Chamber of Commerce</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>NextEra Energy and <a href="/wiki/Energy_Future_Holdings" title="Energy Future Holdings">Energy Future Holdings</a>, parent company of <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>-based utility <a href="/wiki/Oncor_Electric_Delivery" title="Oncor Electric Delivery">Oncor Electric Delivery</a>, reached a $18.7 billion merger agreement on July 29, 2016, but the agreement was terminated in July 2017 after the Texas Public Utility Commission rejected the offer over disagreement on the control of Oncor's board of directors. Two other competing bids were submitted in July 2017 for purchasing Oncor, including a $17.5 billion bid from <a href="/wiki/Berkshire_Hathaway_Energy" title="Berkshire Hathaway Energy">Berkshire Hathaway Energy</a> and a $18.5 billion bid from <a href="/wiki/Elliott_Management_Corporation" title="Elliott Management Corporation">Elliott Management Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2018, NextEra Energy expressed interest with members of the <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina_Senate" title="South Carolina Senate">South Carolina Senate</a> over a possible purchase of <a href="/wiki/SCANA" title="SCANA">SCANA</a> and <a href="/wiki/Santee_Cooper" title="Santee Cooper">Santee Cooper</a>. <a href="/wiki/Dominion_Energy" title="Dominion Energy">Dominion Energy</a> offered $14.6 billion to buy SCANA, but South Carolina lawmakers harshly criticized the proposal over a lack of future taxpayer relief.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In February 2018, NextEra Energy floated a $15.9 billion proposal to buy Santee Cooper and briefed South Carolina lawmakers.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2018, NextEra Energy announced that it planned to buy <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Power_Company" title="Gulf Power Company">Gulf Power Company</a>, the largest electricity producer in Northwest <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Southern_Company" title="Southern Company">Southern Company</a> in a $6.4 billion deal, pending approval from regulators.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The acquisition, which expanded NextEra Energy's combined residential customer base in Florida to approximately 51 percent of the state's population, was completed on January 1, 2019.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_affairs">Corporate affairs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=NextEra_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Corporate affairs" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Litigation">Litigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=NextEra_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Litigation" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In January 2018, NextEra Energy, along with <a href="/wiki/Entergy" title="Entergy">Entergy</a>, withdrew from the <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_Energy_Institute" title="Nuclear Energy Institute">Nuclear Energy Institute</a> (NEI) over disagreements on the trade group's agenda.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In February 2018, NextEra Energy filed a lawsuit against NEI, accusing the trade group of "retaliatory action" and "<a href="/wiki/Extortion" title="Extortion">extortion</a>", claiming that NEI revoked its ability to access the Personnel Access Data System (PADS), the nuclear industry personnel database, unless $860,000 was paid. The president and CEO of NEI responded that the organization "vehemently denies all of the allegations in NextEra’s lawsuit and will vigorously defend our position in court" and that "NextEra lost the ability to participate in PADS upon choosing to discontinue its NEI membership".<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2018, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Court_of_Appeals_for_the_Eleventh_Circuit" title="United States Court of Appeals for the Eleventh Circuit">United States Court of Appeals for the Eleventh Circuit</a> rejected NextEra Energy's claim for a $97 million <a href="/wiki/Tax_deduction" title="Tax deduction">tax deduction</a> for $200 million paid in contract fees to the federal government toward the Nuclear Waste Fund. NextEra Energy sought to deduct payments made between 2003 and 2010 for "the disposal of radioactive waste produced by nuclear power plants operated by subsidiaries Florida Power &amp; Light Co. and NextEra Energy Resources", but the court reasoned that the contract fees "do not qualify as specified liability losses”.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2018, NextEra Energy received a <a href="/wiki/Cease_and_desist" title="Cease and desist">cease and desist</a> order by <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a> state officials concerning the construction of <a href="/wiki/Wind_turbine" title="Wind turbine">wind turbines</a> violating state law. The Oklahoma state law, which took effect in May 2018 to protect open air space, states that developers obtain either a "no hazard" determination for each turbine from the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Aviation_Administration" title="Federal Aviation Administration">Federal Aviation Administration</a> (FAA) or work out a mitigation plan with the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Defense" title="United States Department of Defense">United States Department of Defense</a> (DoD), and then submit notification of such with the Oklahoma Corporation Commission before construction may begin. NextEra Energy had filed obstruction evaluation cases for the construction of wind turbines in Oklahoma with the FAA in March 2018, but the FAA had yet to issue determinations at the time that the cease and desist order was issued.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Finances">Finances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=NextEra_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Finances" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>For the fiscal year 2017, NextEra Energy reported earnings of US$5.378 billion, with an annual revenue of US$17.195 billion, an increase of 6.4% over the previous fiscal cycle. NextEra Energy's shares traded at over $97 per share, and its market capitalization was valued at over US$80.4 billion in October 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Charitable_causes">Charitable causes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=NextEra_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Charitable causes" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In September 2017, the NextEra Energy Foundation, the charitable arm of NextEra Energy, donated $1 million to the <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> Disaster Fund and matched individual contributions of employees in the wake of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Irma" title="Hurricane Irma">Hurricane Irma</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
