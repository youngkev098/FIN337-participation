</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weyerhaeuser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">

Unsourced material may be challenged and removed.<br><small><span class="plainlinks"><i>Find sources:</i> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//www.google.com/search?as_eq=wikipedia&amp;q=%22Weyerhaeuser%22">"Weyerhaeuser"</a> – <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//www.google.com/search?tbm=nws&amp;q=%22Weyerhaeuser%22+-wikipedia">news</a> <b>·</b> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//www.google.com/search?&amp;q=%22Weyerhaeuser%22+site:news.google.com/newspapers&amp;source=newspapers">newspapers</a> <b>·</b> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//www.google.com/search?tbs=bks:1&amp;q=%22Weyerhaeuser%22+-wikipedia">books</a> <b>·</b> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//scholar.google.com/scholar?q=%22Weyerhaeuser%22">scholar</a> <b>·</b> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.jstor.org/action/doBasicSearch?Query=%22Weyerhaeuser%22&amp;acc=on&amp;wc=on">JSTOR</a></span></small></span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">September 2014</span>)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
    </div>
</div><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table><p>In 1904, after years of successful Mississippi River-based lumber and mill operations with <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Denkmann" title="Frederick Denkmann">Frederick Denkmann</a> and others, <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Weyerhaeuser" class="mw-redirect" title="Frederick Weyerhaeuser">Frederick Weyerhäuser</a> moved west to fresh timber areas and founded the Weyerhäuser Timber Company. Fifteen partners and 900,000 acres (3,600 km²) of <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a> timberland were involved in the founding,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and the land was purchased from <a href="/wiki/James_J._Hill" title="James J. Hill">James J. Hill</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Northern_Railway_(U.S.)" title="Great Northern Railway (U.S.)">Great Northern Railway</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>  In 1929, the company built what was then the world's largest sawmill in <a href="/wiki/Longview,_Washington" title="Longview, Washington">Longview, Washington</a>. Weyerhäuser's pulp mill in Longview, which began production in 1931, sustained the company financially during the <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a>. In 1959, the company eliminated the word "Timber" from its name to better reflect its operations. In 1965, Weyerhäuser built its first bleached <a href="/wiki/Kraft_pulp" class="mw-redirect" title="Kraft pulp">kraft pulp</a> mill in Canada. Weyerhäuser implemented its High Yield Forestry Plan in 1967 which drew upon 30 years of forestry research and field experience. It called for the planting of seedlings within one year of a harvest, <a href="/wiki/Fertilizer" title="Fertilizer">soil fertilization</a>, thinning, rehabilitation of brushlands, and, eventually, genetic improvement of trees. In 1975 the company bought the 3,200 acres of land of the Northwest Landing and developed the town of DuPont using a <a href="/wiki/New_Urbanism" title="New Urbanism">New Urbanism</a> model.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>Weyerhäuser consolidated its core businesses in the late 1990s and ended its services in <a href="/wiki/Mortgage_bank" title="Mortgage bank">mortgage banking</a>, personal care products, financial services, and information systems consulting. Weyerhäuser also expanded into <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, Australia, and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. In 1999, Weyerhäuser purchased <a href="/wiki/MacMillan_Bloedel_Limited" class="mw-redirect" title="MacMillan Bloedel Limited">MacMillan Bloedel Limited</a>, a large Canadian forestry company. Then in 2002 after a protracted hostile buyout, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Willamette_Industries,_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Willamette Industries, Inc.">Willamette Industries, Inc.</a> of <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland, Oregon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On August 23, 2006, Weyerhäuser announced a deal which spun off its fine paper business to be combined with <a href="/wiki/Domtar" title="Domtar">Domtar</a>, a $3.3 billion cash and stock deal leaving Weyerhauser stock holders with 55 percent ownership of the new Domtar company.
</p><p>In March 2008, Weyerhäuser Company announced the sale of its Containerboard Packaging and Recycling business to <a href="/wiki/International_Paper" title="International Paper">International Paper</a> for $6 billion in cash, subject to post closing adjustments. The transaction included nine containerboard mills, 72 packaging locations, 10 specialty-packaging plants, four kraft bag and sack locations and 19 recycling facilities. The transaction affected approximately 14,300 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-Weyerhaeuser-Timber-Company-Mar-2008-8-K_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Weyerhaeuser-Timber-Company-Mar-2008-8-K-9">[9]</a></sup> The deal closed on August 4, 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-Weyerhaeuser-Timber-Company-Aug-2008-10-Q_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Weyerhaeuser-Timber-Company-Aug-2008-10-Q-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>Weyerhäuser converted into a real estate investment trust to avoid all federal income taxes when it filed its 2010 tax return.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 2013,  Weyerhäuser purchased Longview Timber for $2.65 billion including debt from Brookfield Asset Management. The acquisition added 645,000 acres of timberland to Weyerhaeuser's holdings in Oregon and Washington.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In 2014, Weyerhäeuser spun off its home building unit to <a href="/wiki/TRI_Pointe_Group" title="TRI Pointe Group">TRI Pointe Homes</a> in a $2.8 billion transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The company also announced its intention to sell its <a href="/wiki/Federal_Way,_Washington" title="Federal Way, Washington">Federal Way</a> headquarters and relocate to Seattle's <a href="/wiki/Pioneer_Square,_Seattle" title="Pioneer Square, Seattle">Pioneer Square</a> in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The sale and move were completed in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>On November 8, 2015, it was announced that Weyerhäuser would buy <a href="/wiki/Plum_Creek_Timber" title="Plum Creek Timber">Plum Creek Timber</a> for $8.4 billion, forming the largest private owner of timberland in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The transaction closed on February 19, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> At the time of the merger the combined companies own about 13 million acres of timberlands.
</p><p>In 2018, it won its <a href="/wiki/Weyerhaeuser_Company_v._United_States_Fish_and_Wildlife_Service" title="Weyerhaeuser Company v. United States Fish and Wildlife Service">case</a> in the Supreme Court regarding whether private land can be classified as critical habitat if the land is not currently suitable as habitat for the protected species.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weyerhaeuser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Operations" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>The company's operations are divided into three major business segments:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">Timberlands</a>—growing and harvesting trees in renewable cycles.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Wood_product" class="mw-redirect" title="Wood product">Wood products</a>—manufacturing and distribution of building materials for homes and other structures.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Real_estate" title="Real estate">Real estate</a>, energy and <a href="/wiki/Natural_resource" title="Natural resource">natural resources</a><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>—all surface and subsurface resources in timberlands that are worth more than the timber itself.</li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_governance">Corporate governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weyerhaeuser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Corporate governance" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
