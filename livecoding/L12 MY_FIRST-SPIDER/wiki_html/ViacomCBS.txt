<p><b>ViacomCBS Inc.</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> <a href="/wiki/Media_conglomerate" title="Media conglomerate">media conglomerate</a> based in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. The corporation was formed via the <a href="/wiki/2019_merger_of_CBS_and_Viacom" title="2019 merger of CBS and Viacom">re-merger</a> of <a href="/wiki/CBS_Corporation" title="CBS Corporation">CBS Corporation</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Viacom_(2005%E2%80%932019)" title="Viacom (2005–2019)">second incarnation of Viacom</a> on December 4, 2019, the two of which were created from the split of the <a href="/wiki/Viacom_(1952%E2%80%932006)" title="Viacom (1952–2006)">original Viacom</a> in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is involved in <a href="/wiki/Film_industry" title="Film industry">film</a>, <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a>, <a href="/wiki/Publishing" title="Publishing">publishing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Digital_media" title="Digital media">digital media</a>.

</p><p>Headquartered at <a href="/wiki/One_Astor_Plaza" title="One Astor Plaza">One Astor Plaza</a> in <a href="/wiki/Midtown_Manhattan" title="Midtown Manhattan">Midtown Manhattan</a>, New York City, the company operates approximately over 170 networks and reaches approximately 700 million subscribers in approximately 160 countries, as of 2020.
</p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=ViacomCBS&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Viacom_(1952%E2%80%932006)" title="Viacom (1952–2006)">Viacom (1952–2006)</a>, <a href="/wiki/CBS_Corporation" title="CBS Corporation">CBS Corporation</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Viacom_(2005%E2%80%932019)" title="Viacom (2005–2019)">Viacom (2005–2019)</a></div>
<p>In 1952, <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> formed <b>CBS Films</b>, a division which handled syndication rights for CBS’ library of television series. This division was renamed <b>CBS Enterprises Inc.</b> in January 1968, and again renamed <b><a href="/wiki/Viacom_(1952%E2%80%932006)" title="Viacom (1952–2006)">Viacom</a></b> in 1970. In 1971, this syndication division was <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spun off</a> amid new <a href="/wiki/Federal_Communications_Commission" title="Federal Communications Commission">FCC</a> rules forbidding <a href="/wiki/Television_network" title="Television network">television networks</a> from <a href="/wiki/Financial_Interest_and_Syndication_Rules" title="Financial Interest and Syndication Rules">owning syndication companies</a> (the rules were later repealed). In 1986, Viacom was acquired by its present owner, theater operator company <a href="/wiki/National_Amusements" title="National Amusements">National Amusements</a>. Throughout the 1980s and 1990s, Viacom made several acquisitions, including <a href="/wiki/MTV_Networks" class="mw-redirect" title="MTV Networks">MTV Networks</a> (former <a href="/wiki/Warner-Amex_Satellite_Entertainment" title="Warner-Amex Satellite Entertainment">Warner-Amex Satellite Entertainment</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Communications" class="mw-redirect" title="Paramount Communications">Paramount Communications</a>. In 1999, Viacom made its biggest acquisition to date by announcing plans to merge with its former parent CBS Corporation (the renamed <a href="/wiki/Westinghouse_Electric_Corporation" title="Westinghouse Electric Corporation">Westinghouse Electric Corporation</a>, which had merged with CBS in 1995). The merger was completed in 2000, resulting in CBS reuniting with its former syndication division. On January 3, 2006, Viacom was split into two companies: <a href="/wiki/CBS_Corporation" title="CBS Corporation">CBS Corporation</a>, the former's corporate successor and the <a href="/wiki/Viacom_(2005%E2%80%932019)" title="Viacom (2005–2019)">spun-off Viacom</a> company.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=ViacomCBS&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/2019_merger_of_CBS_and_Viacom" title="2019 merger of CBS and Viacom">2019 merger of CBS and Viacom</a></div>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=ViacomCBS&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Formation" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On September 29, 2016, <a href="/wiki/National_Amusements" title="National Amusements">National Amusements</a>, the parent company of CBS Corporation and Viacom, sent a letter to Viacom and CBS encouraging the two companies to merge back into one company.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On December 12, the deal was called off.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>On January 12, 2018, <a href="/wiki/CNBC" title="CNBC">CNBC</a> reported that Viacom had re-entered talks to merge back into CBS Corporation, after the merger of <a href="/wiki/AT%26T" title="AT&amp;T">AT&amp;T</a>-<a href="/wiki/WarnerMedia" title="WarnerMedia">Time Warner</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">Disney</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Acquisition_of_21st_Century_Fox_by_Disney" title="Acquisition of 21st Century Fox by Disney">proposed acquisition</a> of most of <a href="/wiki/21st_Century_Fox" title="21st Century Fox">21st Century Fox</a>'s assets were announced. Viacom and CBS also faced heavy competition from companies such as <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amazon_(company)" title="Amazon (company)">Amazon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Shortly afterward, it was reported that the combined company could be a suitor for acquiring the film studio <a href="/wiki/Lionsgate_Films" title="Lionsgate Films">Lionsgate</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Viacom and Lionsgate were both interested in acquiring <a href="/wiki/The_Weinstein_Company" title="The Weinstein Company">The Weinstein Company</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-thewrap.com_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thewrap.com-6">[6]</a></sup> Following the <a href="/wiki/Weinstein_effect" title="Weinstein effect">Weinstein effect</a>, Viacom was listed as one of 22 potential buyers that were interested in acquiring TWC.<sup id="cite_ref-thewrap.com_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thewrap.com-6">[6]</a></sup> They lost the bid, and on March 1, 2018, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Maria_Contreras-Sweet" title="Maria Contreras-Sweet">Maria Contreras-Sweet</a> would acquire all of TWC's assets for $500 million.<sup id="cite_ref-latimes20180301_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-latimes20180301-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-deadline1202307564_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-deadline1202307564-8">[8]</a></sup> Lantern Capital would later acquire the Studio.
</p><p>On March 30, 2018, CBS made an all-stock offer slightly below Viacom's market value, insisting that its existing leadership, including long-time chairman and CEO <a href="/wiki/Les_Moonves" title="Les Moonves">Les Moonves</a>, oversee the re-combined company. Viacom rejected the offer as too low, requesting a $2.8 billion increase and that <a href="/wiki/Robert_Bakish" title="Robert Bakish">Bob Bakish</a> be maintained as president and COO under Moonves. These conflicts had resulted from <a href="/wiki/Shari_Redstone" title="Shari Redstone">Shari Redstone</a> seeking more control over CBS and its leadership.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>Eventually, on May 14, 2018, CBS Corporation sued National Amusements and accused Redstone of abusing her voting power in the company and <a href="/wiki/Hostile_takeover" class="mw-redirect" title="Hostile takeover">forcing a merger that was not supported by it or Viacom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> CBS also accused Redstone of discouraging <a href="/wiki/Verizon_Communications" title="Verizon Communications">Verizon Communications</a> from acquiring it, which could have been beneficial to its shareholders.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>On May 23, 2018, Les Moonves stated that he considered the Viacom channels to be an "<a href="/wiki/Albatross_(metaphor)" title="Albatross (metaphor)">albatross</a>," and while he favored more content for <a href="/wiki/CBS_All_Access" title="CBS All Access">CBS All Access</a>, he believed that there were better deals for CBS than the Viacom deal, such as <a href="/wiki/Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer" title="Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer">Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer</a>, Lionsgate or <a href="/wiki/Sony_Pictures" title="Sony Pictures">Sony Pictures</a>. Moonves also considered Bakish a threat because he did not want an ally of Shari Redstone as a board member of the combined company.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>On September 9, 2018, Les Moonves exited CBS following multiple accusations of sexual assault. National Amusements agreed to make no proposal of a CBS-Viacom merger for at least two years after the date of the settlement.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>On May 30, 2019, CNBC reported that CBS Corporation and Viacom would explore merger discussions in mid-June 2019. CBS's board of directors was revamped with people who were open to merging; the re-merger was made possible with the resignation of Moonves, who had opposed all merger attempts. The talks had started following rumors of CBS acquiring <a href="/wiki/Starz_Inc." title="Starz Inc.">Starz</a> from Lionsgate.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Reports said that CBS and Viacom reportedly set August 8 as an informal deadline for reaching an agreement to recombine the two media companies.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> CBS announced to acquire Viacom as part of the re-merger deal for up to $15.4 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>On August 2, 2019, it was reported that CBS and Viacom agreed to merge back into one entity. Both companies came to an agreement on the management team for the merger, with Bob Bakish serving as CEO of the combined company with president and acting CEO of CBS, <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Ianniello" title="Joseph Ianniello">Joseph Ianniello</a>, overseeing CBS-branded assets.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On August 7, 2019, CBS and Viacom separately reported their quarterly earnings as the talks about the re-merger continued.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Beginning_of_operations">Beginning of operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=ViacomCBS&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Beginning of operations" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On August 13, CBS and Viacom officially announced their merger; the combined company was to be named ViacomCBS, with Shari Redstone serving as chairwoman.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ViacomCBSOfficialPDF_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ViacomCBSOfficialPDF-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>Upon the merger agreement, Viacom and CBS jointly announced that the transaction is expected to close by the end of 2019, pending regulatory and shareholder approvals.<sup id="cite_ref-ViacomCBSOfficialPDF2_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ViacomCBSOfficialPDF2-26">[26]</a></sup> The merger must be approved by the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Trade_Commission" title="Federal Trade Commission">Federal Trade Commission</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ViacomCBSOfficialPDF2_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ViacomCBSOfficialPDF2-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>On October 28, the merger was approved by National Amusements, which then announced the deal would close in early December; the recombined company will trade its shares on <a href="/wiki/Nasdaq" title="Nasdaq">Nasdaq</a> under the symbols "VIAC" and "VIACA" after CBS Corporation delist its shares on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>On November 25, Viacom and CBS announced the merger would close on December 4 and begin trading NASDAQ on December 5.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> On December 4, 2019, Bakish confirmed that the ViacomCBS merger had closed.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>On December 10, Bakish announced that ViacomCBS would look to <a href="/wiki/Divestment" title="Divestment">divest</a> <a href="/wiki/CBS_Building" title="CBS Building">Black Rock</a>, the building that held CBS's headquarters since 1964. He stated, "Black Rock is not an asset we need to own and we believe that money would be put to better use elsewhere..."<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> That same day, the company and its <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Pictures" title="Paramount Pictures">Paramount Pictures</a> subsidiary resumed talks with <a href="/wiki/Miramax" title="Miramax">Miramax</a> (and its owner <a href="/wiki/BeIN_Media_Group" title="BeIN Media Group">beIN Media Group</a>) to acquire a stake in the company.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> On December 20, 2019, the company agreed to purchase a 49% stake in Miramax (beIN maintained a 51% majority stake), with Paramount announcing an exclusive agreement for their library and upcoming films.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>On February 6, 2020, CNBC reported that ViacomCBS was preparing to launch a streaming service that would blend CBS All Access, Paramount films and its media properties such as <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a>, <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pluto_TV" title="Pluto TV">Pluto TV</a>, among others, which have been licensed to other services like Netflix and Amazon's <a href="/wiki/Prime_Video" title="Prime Video">Prime Video</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2020, EVP Dana McClintock announced his resignation from the company after 27 years in CBS Communications.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> CEO Bob Bakish announced on March 4 that the company would potentially sell the <a href="/wiki/Simon_%26_Schuster" title="Simon &amp; Schuster">Simon &amp; Schuster</a> publishing unit, stating that "it does not have significant connection for our broader business.”<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Company_units">Company units</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=ViacomCBS&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Company units" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For a more comprehensive list, see <a href="/wiki/List_of_assets_owned_by_ViacomCBS" title="List of assets owned by ViacomCBS">List of assets owned by ViacomCBS</a>.</div>
<p>ViacomCBS comprises four major units: <b>CBS Entertainment Group</b>, <b><a href="/wiki/ViacomCBS_Domestic_Media_Networks" title="ViacomCBS Domestic Media Networks">Domestic Media Networks</a></b>, <b><a href="/wiki/ViacomCBS_International_Media_Networks" title="ViacomCBS International Media Networks">Networks International</a></b>, and <b>Global Distribution Group</b>.
</p><p>CBS Entertainment Group consists of CBS-branded assets, including the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> television network, <a href="/wiki/CBS_News" title="CBS News">CBS News</a>, <a href="/wiki/CBS_Sports" title="CBS Sports">CBS Sports</a>, <a href="/wiki/CBS_Television_Studios" title="CBS Television Studios">CBS Television Studios</a>, <a href="/wiki/CBS_Television_Stations" title="CBS Television Stations">CBS Television Stations</a>, and <a href="/wiki/CBS_Interactive" title="CBS Interactive">CBS Interactive</a>. The unit also has a 50% interest in <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">The CW</a> television network joint venture co-owned by <a href="/wiki/AT%26T" title="AT&amp;T">AT&amp;T</a> subsidiary <a href="/wiki/WarnerMedia" title="WarnerMedia">WarnerMedia</a> through its <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros." title="Warner Bros.">Warner Bros.</a> division.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p><p>Domestic Media Networks encompasses the pay television channels provided in the United States, such as <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a>, <a href="/wiki/BET" title="BET">BET</a>, <a href="/wiki/Comedy_Central" title="Comedy Central">Comedy Central</a>, <a href="/wiki/TV_Land" title="TV Land">TV Land</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Network" title="Paramount Network">Paramount Network</a>, <a href="/wiki/Logo_TV" title="Logo TV">Logo</a>, <a href="/wiki/CMT_(American_TV_channel)" title="CMT (American TV channel)">CMT</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pop_(American_TV_channel)" title="Pop (American TV channel)">Pop TV</a>, <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Movie_Channel" title="The Movie Channel">The Movie Channel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Flix_(TV_network)" title="Flix (TV network)">Flix</a>. The unit also includes the <a href="/wiki/Smithsonian_Channel" title="Smithsonian Channel">Smithsonian Channel</a>, which is controlled via a joint venture with the <a href="/wiki/Smithsonian_Institution" title="Smithsonian Institution">Smithsonian Institution</a>. ViacomCBS Domestic additionally controls production facilities for the channels listed above, including <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon_Animation_Studio" title="Nickelodeon Animation Studio">Nickelodeon Animation Studio</a>.
</p><p>Networks International encompasses certain international versions of the company's domestic channels, as well as region-specific networks, such as <a href="/wiki/Channel_5_(British_TV_channel)" title="Channel 5 (British TV channel)">Channel 5</a> in the United Kingdom, <a href="/wiki/Network_10" title="Network 10">Network 10</a> in Australia, and <a href="/wiki/Telefe" title="Telefe">Telefe</a> in Argentina. ViacomCBS International also owns a third<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> of the <a href="/wiki/Rainbow_S.r.l." title="Rainbow S.r.l.">Rainbow S.r.l.</a> television studio in Italy, as well as a 49% stake in the <a href="/wiki/Viacom_18" title="Viacom 18">Viacom 18</a> joint venture with <a href="/wiki/TV18" title="TV18">TV18</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> This unit also includes all CBS-branded channels across Europe, which are co-owned with <a href="/wiki/AMC_Networks_International" title="AMC Networks International">AMC Networks International</a>.
</p><p>Global Distribution Group focuses on the global distribution of all programs produced by all ViacomCBS production studios. The division consists of <a href="/wiki/CBS_Television_Distribution" title="CBS Television Distribution">CBS Television Distribution</a>, <a href="/wiki/CBS_Studios_International" title="CBS Studios International">CBS Studios International</a> and ViacomCBS International Studios.
</p><p>Other assets owned by ViacomCBS are film studio <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Pictures" title="Paramount Pictures">Paramount Pictures</a>, over-the-top ad-supported video-on-demand platform <a href="/wiki/Pluto_TV" title="Pluto TV">Pluto TV</a>, book publisher <a href="/wiki/Simon_%26_Schuster" title="Simon &amp; Schuster">Simon &amp; Schuster</a>, multi-genre online video conference <a href="/wiki/VidCon" title="VidCon">VidCon</a>, mixed martial arts promotor <a href="/wiki/Bellator_MMA" title="Bellator MMA">Bellator</a>, and media and entertainment company <a href="/wiki/AwesomenessTV" title="AwesomenessTV">AwesomenessTV</a>. As of November 2019, AwesomenessTV is overseen by <a href="/wiki/Brian_Robbins" title="Brian Robbins">Brian Robbins</a>, an executive for ViacomCBS Domestic.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
