<p><b>Norwegian Cruise Line Holdings</b> (<b>NCLH</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a> that is domiciled in <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> and based in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-investor.ncl.com_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-investor.ncl.com-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> It operates three <a href="/wiki/Cruise_line" title="Cruise line">cruise lines</a> as wholly owned subsidiaries: <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Cruise_Line" title="Norwegian Cruise Line">Norwegian Cruise Line</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oceania_Cruises" title="Oceania Cruises">Oceania Cruises</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Regent_Seven_Seas_Cruises" title="Regent Seven Seas Cruises">Regent Seven Seas Cruises</a>. With its subsidiaries combined, it is the third-largest cruise operator in the world. It is a publicly traded company listed on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norwegian_Cruise_Line_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>NCLH was incorporated on February 21, 2011<sup id="cite_ref-profile_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-profile-1">[1]</a></sup> in anticipation of a planned <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> (IPO).<sup id="cite_ref-:2_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-4">[4]</a></sup> At the time, <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Cruise_Line" title="Norwegian Cruise Line">Norwegian Cruise Line</a> was owned by a consortium of <a href="/wiki/Genting_Hong_Kong" title="Genting Hong Kong">Genting Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apollo_Management" class="mw-redirect" title="Apollo Management">Apollo Management</a> and <a href="/wiki/TPG_Capital" title="TPG Capital">TPG Capital</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-4">[4]</a></sup> Following the completion of the IPO in January 2013 and its debut on the <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Norwegian Cruise Line and NCLH underwent a corporate reorganisation that gave NCLH full ownership of Norwegian Cruise Line,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-7">[7]</a></sup> while Norwegian Cruise Line's previous owners exchanged their stakes for shares in NCLH.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>Immediately after the initial public offering, public shareholders controlled 13.3% of NCLH, while Genting Hong Kong owned a 43.4% stake, Apollo 32.5%, and TPG 10.8%.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-7">[7]</a></sup> In the following years, those three sponsors progressively reduced their stakes through a series of secondary public offerings.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> By the end of 2014, nearly half of NCLH's shares were publicly owned, with Genting, Apollo and TPG controlling 25%, 24% and 7%, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> One year later, combined ownership among the three firms had been cut nearly in half, with Apollo now holding the largest individual stake (15.8%), followed by Genting (11.1%) and TPG (2.4%).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Eventually, on 3 December 2018, Apollo and Genting sold off their remaining stakes in the company, marking the end of a relationship that lasted more than a decade.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2014, NCLH announced that it had purchased Prestige Cruise Holdings, the parent company of <a href="/wiki/Oceania_Cruises" title="Oceania Cruises">Oceania Cruises</a> and <a href="/wiki/Regent_Seven_Seas_Cruises" title="Regent Seven Seas Cruises">Regent Seven Seas Cruises</a> in cash and stock for a total transaction consideration of $3.025 billion, including the assumption of debt.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>On 9 January 2015, Kevin Sheehan, President and CEO, was succeeded by Frank Del Rio, co-founder of Oceania Cruises.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>On 14 December 2015, NCLH was added to the <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ-100" title="NASDAQ-100">NASDAQ-100</a> index.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> However, it later switched its listing from NASDAQ to the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> on 19 December 2017,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and it was removed from the index.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2018, NCLH's Chairman Walter Revell stepped down,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and was replaced by Miami Beach developer Russell Galbut.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>As of 2018, it is the third-largest cruise operator in the world, collectively controlling 9.5% of the cruise market by passengers and 12.6% by revenue across its three subsidiaries.<sup id="cite_ref-2018-market-share_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2018-market-share-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Subsidiaries">Subsidiaries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norwegian_Cruise_Line_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subsidiaries" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>NCLH fully owns three subsidiary cruise lines:
</p>
