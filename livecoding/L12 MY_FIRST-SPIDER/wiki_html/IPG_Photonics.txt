Its products are sold globally and primarily used for materials processing, advanced technologies, telecommunications, and medical applications.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>The company is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Oxford,_Massachusetts" title="Oxford, Massachusetts">Oxford, Massachusetts</a>, with more than 25 facilities around the world.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=IPG_Photonics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>IPG was founded in 1990 in Russia by physicist <a href="/wiki/Valentin_Gapontsev" title="Valentin Gapontsev">Valentin Gapontsev</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Photonics_Entrepreneur_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Photonics_Entrepreneur-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>Building on previous demonstrations to improve the efficiencies of fiber lasers, in 1990, Valentin Gapontsev and Igor Samartsev first proposed using fiber laser technology to produce a high power laser beam, demonstrating 2W CW Er doped fiber lasers at 1.54μm and a 5W laser in 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-Gapontsev_1991_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gapontsev_1991-12">[12]</a></sup> Using a side-pumping technique, Gapontsev and Samartsev's unique fiber laser architecture enabled many semiconductor laser diodes to pump through one single-mode fiber.
</p><p>This created a high power, infrared laser, with single mode beam quality.<sup id="cite_ref-Gapontsev_1991_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gapontsev_1991-12">[12]</a></sup> This discovery also resulted in high power optical amplifiers. Optical amplifiers convert a small light signal into a powerful beam, often a thousand times brighter, but otherwise identical to the original signal. In 2018, these amplifiers are used in telecommunications and Internet networks to transmit data over optical fiber and free space.
</p><p>In December 1991, Gapontsev and Alexander Shestakov founded NTO IRE-Polus.<sup id="cite_ref-:02_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:02-4">[4]</a></sup> The company was based in Fryazino, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>. Shestakov left the company in 1994. Gapontsev started using the acronym IPG to refer to his company. It stood for IRE-Polus Group.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In 1993, IPG won a contract with <a href="/wiki/Italtel" title="Italtel">Italtel</a>, a large Italian telecommunications carrier.<sup id="cite_ref-spie.org_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-spie.org-14">[14]</a></sup> IPG developed a 200 mW erbium doped amplifier for Italtel, which became IPG's first marketable product. IPG then developed erbium fiber amplifiers using new a new pump design and fiber solution.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In order to introduce the technology to the market, Italtel convinced Gapontsev to transfer component production to Italy. This became IPG's first European subsidiary.
</p><p>In 1994, <a href="/wiki/Daimler_Benz_Aerospace" class="mw-redirect" title="Daimler Benz Aerospace">Daimler Benz Aerospace</a> (DBA), a Germany company, hired IPG to create a laser-based obstacle warning system for a helicopter. DBA funded IPG's development of a new fiber laser based solution. IPG opened a manufacturing and research facility in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> to develop to transmitter. A year later, IPG opened a facility near <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-spie.org_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-spie.org-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In 1996, IPG launched industrial-quality, diffraction-limited (single mode) 10-W fiber lasers.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> IPG continued developing new products and selling them to customers in Japan, the United States, and Europe.
</p><p>In 1998, IPG established the IPG Photonics Corporation headquartered in Oxford, Massachusetts.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>By 2000, IPG was a $52 million company. Its customers included Alcatel, Fujitsu, Lucent, Siemens, and Marconi (Reltec). During this time, IPG started developing its mutli-kilowatt diode-pumped fiber lasers.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In 2000, IPG introduced a 100-W diffraction-limited fiber laser using its multi-fiber side-coupling technology. In comparison, the conventional diode pumped solid state lasers (DPSSLs) on the market used diode bars as the pump source, with each bar typically producing 40 W of power.<sup id="cite_ref-Gapontsev_2004_901_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gapontsev_2004_901-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>By the end of 2000, capital spending by telecommunications providers was reduced and IPG revenue from communications applications declined. IPG invested much of its remaining capital in the development of: (1) high-power industrial fiber lasers; (2) mass production lines; and (3) semiconductor pump laser diode technology.  IPG pursued raising the maximum wattage of fiber laser technology.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>Between 2002 and 2003, IPG developed multi-kW industrial class fiber lasers. This was accomplished by combining the output beams of several 100-W fiber lasers.<sup id="cite_ref-Gapontsev_2004_901_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gapontsev_2004_901-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In 2006 IPG raised more than $90 million, net of proceeds, in an initial public offering.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The company continued to expand, opening offices near Detroit, Michigan in 2006, Beijing, China in 2007 and its Silicon Valley Technology Center in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Beginning in 2010, the company introduced its first quasi-continuous wave (QCW) lasers into the market. In 2017, worldwide IPG revenue grew to $1.4 billion, a 40% increase over the previous year. In 2018 the company was added to the S&amp;P 500 stock market index.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>The company had its initial public offering in 2006 and is now listed on the <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a> stock exchange.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
