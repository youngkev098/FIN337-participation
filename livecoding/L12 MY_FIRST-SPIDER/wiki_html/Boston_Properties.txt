</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boston_Properties&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company was founded in 1970 by <a href="/wiki/Mortimer_B._Zuckerman" class="mw-redirect" title="Mortimer B. Zuckerman">Mortimer B. Zuckerman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Edward_H._Linde" class="mw-redirect" title="Edward H. Linde">Edward H. Linde</a> in <a href="/wiki/Boston,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston, Massachusetts">Boston, Massachusetts</a>.
</p><p>In 1985, the company outbid <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a> to re-develop the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Coliseum" title="New York Coliseum">New York Coliseum</a> at <a href="/wiki/Columbus_Circle" title="Columbus Circle">Columbus Circle</a>. However, the company did not proceed and sold the property, which is now the site of the <a href="/wiki/Time_Warner_Center" title="Time Warner Center">Time Warner Center</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p><p>In 1986, the company completed <a href="/wiki/599_Lexington_Avenue" title="599 Lexington Avenue">599 Lexington Avenue</a>, its first development in New York City.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In 1990, the company began construction of the <a href="/wiki/NASA_Headquarters" title="NASA Headquarters">NASA Headquarters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It was sold to <a href="/wiki/Hana_Financial_Group" title="Hana Financial Group">Hana Financial Group</a> in 2002.
</p><p>In June 1997, the company became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In October 1997, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/100_East_Pratt_Street" title="100 East Pratt Street">100 East Pratt Street</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 1998, it acquired <a href="/wiki/Embarcadero_Center" title="Embarcadero Center">Embarcadero Center</a> for about $1.22 billion and acquired <a href="/wiki/Prudential_Tower" title="Prudential Tower">Prudential Tower</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Shops_at_Prudential_Center" title="The Shops at Prudential Center">The Shops at Prudential Center</a> for $519 million.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 2000 and 2001, the company completed development of several office buildings in <a href="/wiki/Reston_Town_Center" title="Reston Town Center">Reston Town Center</a>.
</p><p>From 2002 to 2005, it constructed <a href="/wiki/901_New_York_Avenue" title="901 New York Avenue">901 New York Avenue</a> NW in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2008, the company acquired the <a href="/wiki/General_Motors_Building_(New_York)" class="mw-redirect" title="General Motors Building (New York)">General Motors Building</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> for $2.8 billion, the highest paid for an American office building.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2010, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/200_Clarendon_Street" class="mw-redirect" title="200 Clarendon Street">200 Clarendon Street</a> (formerly John Hancock Tower) for $930 million.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012, the company partnered with <a href="/wiki/Hines_Interests_Limited_Partnership" title="Hines Interests Limited Partnership">Hines Interests Limited Partnership</a> to develop <a href="/wiki/Salesforce_Tower" title="Salesforce Tower">Salesforce Tower</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> By 2019, it acquired 100% of the property.
</p>
