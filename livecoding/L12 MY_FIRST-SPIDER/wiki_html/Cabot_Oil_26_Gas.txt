</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cabot_Oil_%26_Gas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> in 1990.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p><p>In 1994, the company acquired Washington Energy Resources in a $180 million stock transaction.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In May 1995, the company ousted John Lollar, its <a href="/wiki/Chairman" class="mw-redirect" title="Chairman">chairman</a> and <a href="/wiki/CEO" class="mw-redirect" title="CEO">CEO</a>, in part due to the ill-timing of the acquisition of Washington Energy Resources.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In 1997, the company sold oil reserves and land in northwest Pennsylvania for $92.5 million.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In 2001, the company acquired Cody Energy for $230 million.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2008, the company was added to the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500_Index" title="S&amp;P 500 Index">S&amp;P 500 Index</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 2013, the company sold its assets in the Marmaton play of <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Texas" title="West Texas">West Texas</a> for $160 million.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2018, the company sold its assets in the <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Ford_Group" title="Eagle Ford Group">Eagle Ford Group</a>  to <a href="/wiki/KKR" class="mw-redirect" title="KKR">KKR</a> and Venado.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cabot_Oil_%26_Gas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Controversies" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_damage">Environmental damage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cabot_Oil_%26_Gas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Environmental damage" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, the company was cited for violations in regard to spills of toxic <a href="/wiki/Hydraulic_fracturing" title="Hydraulic fracturing">hydraulic fracturing</a> fluids in <a href="/wiki/Northeastern_Pennsylvania" title="Northeastern Pennsylvania">Northeastern Pennsylvania</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012, the company was cited for improper well construction as a result of polluted drinking water.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Restraining_order_on_anti-fracking_activist">Restraining order on anti-fracking activist</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cabot_Oil_%26_Gas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Restraining order on anti-fracking activist" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In January 2014, Cabot sought, and a judge granted, a temporary injunction barring anti-fracking activist Vera Scroggins from entering on any land in Pennsylvania owned or leased by Cabot. "In total, 312.5 sq miles are no-go areas for Scroggins under a sweeping court order granted by a local judge that bars her from any properties owned or leased by one of the biggest drillers in the Pennsylvania natural gas rush, Cabot Oil &amp; Gas Corporation." It was noted by Cabot during court testimony that Scroggins had violated no laws and that this lawsuit was based on her being considered a "nuisance" due to her reporting and activism at Cabot sites.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
