<p>Union Pacific was located in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> from the company's re-founding in 1969 until Drew Lewis became CEO in the mid-1980s. He relocated the corporate headquarters to <a href="/wiki/Bethlehem,_Pennsylvania" title="Bethlehem, Pennsylvania">Bethlehem, Pennsylvania</a>. Later the headquarters was shifted to <a href="/wiki/Dallas,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Dallas, Texas">Dallas, Texas</a>, before relocating the corporate headquarters to Omaha to join the Union Pacific Railroad headquarters.<sup id="cite_ref-history_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lance_M._Fritz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lance M. Fritz (page does not exist)">Lance M. Fritz</a> is the current chairman, president and chief executive officer.
</p><p>The Union Pacific Corporation has a portfolio of acquiring the <a href="/wiki/Missouri_Pacific_Railroad" title="Missouri Pacific Railroad">Missouri Pacific Railroad</a> which included the <a href="/wiki/Missouri%E2%80%93Kansas%E2%80%93Texas_Railroad" title="Missouri–Kansas–Texas Railroad">Missouri–Kansas–Texas Railroad</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_and_North_Western_Transportation_Company" title="Chicago and North Western Transportation Company">Chicago and North Western Transportation Company</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Western_Pacific_Railroad" title="Western Pacific Railroad">Western Pacific Railroad</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Denver_and_Rio_Grande_Western_Railroad" title="Denver and Rio Grande Western Railroad">Denver and Rio Grande Western Railroad</a>, the <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Southwestern_Railway" title="St. Louis Southwestern Railway">St. Louis Southwestern Railway</a>, the <a href="/wiki/SPCSL_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="SPCSL Corporation">SPCSL Corporation</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Pacific_Transportation_Company" title="Southern Pacific Transportation Company">Southern Pacific Transportation Company</a>. All railroads not including the Southern Pacific Transportation Company were merged into the previous form of the Union Pacific Railroad. As part of the UP-SP merger which included the merging of the Denver and Rio Grande Western Railroad, the St. Louis Southwestern Railway and the SPCSL Corporation into the previous form of the Union Pacific Railroad, the Union Pacific Corporation merged the previous form of the Union Pacific Railroad into the Southern Pacific Transportation Company and renamed the Southern Pacific Transportation Company to the current form of the Union Pacific Railroad.
</p><p>The Union Pacific Corporation's main competitor is <a href="/wiki/Burlington_Northern_Santa_Fe,_LLC" title="Burlington Northern Santa Fe, LLC">Burlington Northern Santa Fe, LLC</a>, the parent company of <a href="/wiki/BNSF_Railway" title="BNSF Railway">BNSF Railway</a>. BNSF is the nation's second largest freight rail network and primarily services the same territory as Union Pacific, west of the <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_River" title="Mississippi River">Mississippi River</a>. Together, Union Pacific and BNSF have a <a href="/wiki/Duopoly" title="Duopoly">duopoly</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute#Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute"><span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. (January 2019)">dubious</span></a> <span class="metadata"> – <a href="/wiki/Talk:Union_Pacific_Corporation#Dubious" title="Talk:Union Pacific Corporation">discuss</a></span></i>]</sup> on all long-haul freight rail service in the <a href="/wiki/Western_United_States" title="Western United States">Western United States</a>.
</p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Union_Pacific_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Union_Pacific_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origins" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The first Union Pacific Railroad, pronounced "<a href="/wiki/Union_Pacific_Rail_Road" class="mw-redirect" title="Union Pacific Rail Road">Union Pacific Rail Road</a>" was incorporated on July 1, 1862, under an act of Congress entitled <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Railway_Acts" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Railway Acts">Pacific Railroad Act of 1862</a>. The act was approved by President <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a>, and it provided for the construction of railroads from the <a href="/wiki/Missouri_River" title="Missouri River">Missouri River</a> to the Pacific as a war measure for the preservation of the Union.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It was constructed westward from <a href="/wiki/Council_Bluffs,_Iowa" title="Council Bluffs, Iowa">Council Bluffs, Iowa</a> to meet the <a href="/wiki/Central_Pacific_Railroad" title="Central Pacific Railroad">Central Pacific Railroad</a> line, which was constructed eastward from <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay" title="San Francisco Bay">San Francisco Bay</a>. The combined Union Pacific-Central Pacific line became known as the <a href="/wiki/First_Transcontinental_Railroad" title="First Transcontinental Railroad">First Transcontinental Railroad</a> and <a href="/wiki/Overland_Route_(Union_Pacific_Railroad)" title="Overland Route (Union Pacific Railroad)">Overland Route</a>. The original UP was entangled in the <a href="/wiki/Cr%C3%A9dit_Mobilier_of_America_scandal" class="mw-redirect" title="Crédit Mobilier of America scandal">Crédit Mobilier scandal</a>, exposed in 1872. Later, the original UP was in financial troubles because of the <a href="/wiki/Panic_of_1873" title="Panic of 1873">financial crisis of 1873</a> but did not go into bankruptcy.
</p><p>The original company was taken over by the new <a href="/wiki/Union_Pacific_Railway" class="mw-redirect" title="Union Pacific Railway">Union Pacific Railway</a> on January 24, 1880 and the original company was merged into the Union Pacific Railway. The Union Pacific Railway declared bankruptcy during the <a href="/wiki/Panic_of_1893" title="Panic of 1893">Panic of 1893</a>.
</p><p>In 1897 a new "<a href="/wiki/Union_Pacific_Railroad" title="Union Pacific Railroad">Union Pacific Railroad</a>" (pronounced "Railroad") was formed and the Union Pacific Railway was merged into the new Union Pacific Railroad.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> This Union Pacific Railroad is the third incarnation, and the third incarnation makes up the bulk of the Union Pacific history. The third Union Pacific Railroad lasted until 1998 when it was replaced by the fourth incarnation, formerly the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Pacific_Transportation_Company" title="Southern Pacific Transportation Company">Southern Pacific Transportation Company</a>, the last incarnation of the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Pacific" class="mw-redirect" title="Southern Pacific">Southern Pacific</a> railroad. The Union Pacific Corporation was established during the tenure of the third Union Pacific Railroad.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Establishment_and_current_ownership_of_the_current_Union_Pacific_Railroad">Establishment and current ownership of the current Union Pacific Railroad</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Union_Pacific_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Establishment and current ownership of the current Union Pacific Railroad" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The Union Pacific Corporation was established in 1969 with its incorporation in <a href="/wiki/Utah" title="Utah">Utah</a> and it was established to take control of the then third incarnation of the <a href="/wiki/Union_Pacific_Railroad" title="Union Pacific Railroad">Union Pacific Railroad</a> later referred to as Mark I and its non-railroad subsidiaries.
</p><p>The Union Pacific Corporation was established the same year the current Union Pacific railroad began. The current Union Pacific Railroad is the fourth incarnation and it is referred to as Mark II; the current incarnation of the Union Pacific Railroad is formerly known as the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Pacific_Transportation_Company" title="Southern Pacific Transportation Company">Southern Pacific Transportation Company</a>, the last incarnation of the Southern Pacific railroad. The Union Pacific Corporation merged the third Union Pacific Railroad into the Southern Pacific Transportation Company and then renamed the Southern Pacific Transportation Company to the current Union Pacific Railroad.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Subsidiary_history">Subsidiary history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Union_Pacific_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Subsidiary history" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The Union Pacific Corporation is the parent company of all UP subsidiaries and operating companies besides the current <a href="/wiki/Union_Pacific_Railroad" title="Union Pacific Railroad">Union Pacific Railroad</a> (the largest operating company). The Union Pacific Corporation, under the current Union Pacific Railroad, owns the <a href="/wiki/Alton_and_Southern_Railway" title="Alton and Southern Railway">Alton and Southern Railway</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Switching_railroad" class="mw-redirect" title="Switching railroad">switching railroad</a>.
</p><p>The Union Pacific Corporation operated the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Pacific_Rail_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Southern Pacific Rail Corporation">Southern Pacific Rail Corporation</a> (formerly the second <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_Industries" title="Rio Grande Industries">Rio Grande Industries</a> and former parent company for the current Union Pacific railroad) until 2015 when it was merged into the current primary railroad subsidiary.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>Union Pacific Corporation used to have short lived subsidiaries: 
</p>
<ul><li><b>UP Leasing Corporation</b></li>
<li><b>UP Rail</b> (or <b>UP Rail, Inc.</b>)</li>
<li><b>UP Holdings</b> (or <b>UP Holdings, Inc.</b>), originally known as <b>Union Pacific Holdings</b> (or <b>Union Pacific Holdings, Inc.</b>)</li>
<li><b>UP Holding Company</b> (or <b>UP Holding Company, Inc.</b> and <b>UP Holding</b>)</li>
<li><b>Union Pacific Merger Company</b> (<b>Union Pacific Merger Co.</b>, or <b>UP Merger</b> and <b>UP Mergerco</b>)</li>
<li><b>UP Acquisition Corporation</b> (or <b>UP Acquisition</b>)</li>
<li><b>Union Pacific Resources Group</b> (<b>Union Pacific Resources Group, Inc.</b> or simply <b>Resources</b> and <b>Union Pacific Resources</b>) <sup id="cite_ref-getfilings.com_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-getfilings.com-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Overnite_Transportation_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="Overnite Transportation Company">Overnite Transportation Company</a></b> (or "Overnite Transportation"),<sup id="cite_ref-getfilings.com_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-getfilings.com-9">[9]</a></sup> includes Overnite subsidiary <b><a href="/wiki/Motor_Cargo" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor Cargo">Motor Cargo</a></b>.</li></ul><p>The Union Pacific Corporation operated the Overnite Transportation Company, a trucking company, until it was sold to <a href="/wiki/United_Parcel_Service" title="United Parcel Service">United Parcel Service</a> (UPS) and renamed <a href="/wiki/UPS_Freight" title="UPS Freight">UPS Freight</a>.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Merger_history_and_subsidiary_involvement_during_the_merger_history">Merger history and subsidiary involvement during the merger history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Union_Pacific_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Merger history and subsidiary involvement during the merger history" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The Union Pacific merger history began with the Union Pacific Corporation acquiring the <a href="/wiki/Missouri_Pacific_Railroad" title="Missouri Pacific Railroad">Missouri Pacific Railroad</a> (which included the <a href="/wiki/Missouri%E2%80%93Kansas%E2%80%93Texas_Railroad" title="Missouri–Kansas–Texas Railroad">Missouri–Kansas–Texas Railroad</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Western_Pacific_Railroad" title="Western Pacific Railroad">Western Pacific Railroad</a>. The Union Pacific Corporation merged the Western Pacific Railroad into the third Union Pacific Railroad and then merged the Missouri–Kansas–Texas Railroad into the Missouri Pacific Railroad and transferred direct ownership of the Missouri Pacific Railroad to the third Union Pacific Railroad. The Missouri Pacific Railroad continued operations until January 1, 1997 when it was merged into the third Union Pacific Railroad by the Union Pacific Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-sec.gov_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sec.gov-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>The subsidiaries UP Leasing Corporation, UP Rail and UP Holdings were part of the Union Pacific take over of the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_and_North_Western_Transportation_Company" title="Chicago and North Western Transportation Company">Chicago and North Western Transportation Company</a> which was renamed to its original name <a href="/wiki/Chicago_and_North_Western_Railway" class="mw-redirect" title="Chicago and North Western Railway">Chicago and North Western Railway</a>, and its holding company, the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_and_North_Western_Holdings_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Chicago and North Western Holdings Corporation">Chicago and North Western Holdings Corporation</a> which was renamed to the second Chicago and North Western Transportation Company.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In April 1995, the former Chicago and North Western Holdings Corporation (the second Chicago and North Western Transportation Company), along with the Chicago and North Western Railway (formerly the first Chicago and North Western Transportation Company), was acquired by the Union Pacific Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>UP Holdings was merged into UP Rail.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The Union Pacific Corporation merged UP Rail into the third Union Pacific Railroad. Finally, the Union Pacific Corporation merged the second Chicago and North Western Transportation Company (formerly Chicago and North Western Holdings Corporation) and the Chicago and North Western Railway (formerly the first Chicago and North Western Transportation Company) into the third Union Pacific Railroad, the Chicago and North Western system is now part of the Union Pacific Railroad system.<sup id="cite_ref-sec.gov_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sec.gov-10">[10]</a></sup> A joint UP-CNW subsidiary, <a href="/wiki/Western_Railroad_Properties" class="mw-redirect" title="Western Railroad Properties">Western Railroad Properties</a> (or "Western Railroad Properties, Inc."), was also merged into the Union Pacific system.
</p><p>The subsidiaries UP Holding Company, Union Pacific Merger Company and UP Acquisition Corporation were part of the Union Pacific take over of the Southern Pacific Rail Corporation (formerly the second Rio Grande Industries) and the Southern Pacific Transportation Company, the current Union Pacific Railroad.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Southern Pacific had financial problems and its mileage was dropped to 13,715 miles (22,072 km) by 1996.
</p><p>The Union Pacific Corporation purchased the Southern Pacific Rail Corporation which included the Southern Pacific Transportation Company, the <a href="/wiki/Denver_and_Rio_Grande_Western_Railroad" title="Denver and Rio Grande Western Railroad">Denver and Rio Grande Western Railroad</a>, the <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Southwestern_Railway" title="St. Louis Southwestern Railway">St. Louis Southwestern Railway</a> and the <a href="/wiki/SPCSL_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="SPCSL Corporation">SPCSL Corporation</a>. The Union Pacific Corporation originally purchased a portion of the Southern Pacific Rail Corporation under the UP Acquisition Corporation subsidiary; the UP Acquisition Corporation subsidiary originally acquired 25 percent of SP's outstanding common shares for $25 per share cash. In June 1996, the UP Acquisition Corporation was merged into the Union Pacific Corporation, the 25 percent of SP's outstanding common shares is now controlled by the Union Pacific Corporation, leaving only 75 percent of SP common shares not owned by the Union Pacific Corporation that was originally going to the UP Acquisition Corporation subsidiary. In September 1996, the Union Pacific Corporation acquired the remaining 75 percent of SP common shares not previously owned by the Union Pacific Corporation. On September 10, 1996, the Union Pacific Merger Company was merged into the Union Pacific Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-sec.gov_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sec.gov-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>The Union Pacific Corporation merged the Denver and Rio Grande Western Railroad, the St. Louis Southwestern Railway and the SPCSL Corporation into the third Union Pacific Railroad.
</p><p>The Union Pacific Corporation merged the third Union Pacific Railroad into the Southern Pacific Transportation Company in 1998; the Southern Pacific Transportation Company becomes the surviving railroad and at the same time the Union Pacific Corporation renamed the Southern Pacific Transportation Company to fourth incarnation of the Union Pacific Railroad. The former Southern Pacific Transportation Company is now operating as the current incarnation of the Union Pacific Railroad. The Southern Pacific Rail Corporation remained a subsidiary of the Union Pacific Corporation until 2015 when it was merged into the former Southern Pacific Transportation Company, the current Union Pacific Railroad.
</p><p>According to the Utah corporation database, it has been suggested that the Southern Pacific Rail Corporation was merged into UP Holding Company<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and UP Holding Company was renamed Southern Pacific Rail Corporation, becoming a second incarnation of the Southern Pacific Rail Corporation. It has also been suggested that UP Holding Company was originally known as "CNW Holdings" (or "CNW Holdings, Inc.").<sup id="cite_ref-auto_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-8">[8]</a></sup> CNW Holdings could be mistaken for the Chicago and North Western Holdings Corporation, the former parent company of the Chicago and North Western.
</p>

Union Pacific's shares traded at over $31 per share, and its market capitalization was valued at US$105.2 billion in October 2018.
