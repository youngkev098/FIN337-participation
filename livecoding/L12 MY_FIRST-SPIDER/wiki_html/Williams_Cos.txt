<p>The <b>Williams Companies, Inc.</b>, is an American energy company based in <a href="/wiki/Tulsa,_Oklahoma" title="Tulsa, Oklahoma">Tulsa, Oklahoma</a>. Its core business is <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a> processing and transportation, with additional petroleum and <a href="/wiki/Electricity_generation" title="Electricity generation">electricity generation</a> assets. A <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> company,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> its common stock is a component of the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a>.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Williams_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>It was founded as <b>Williams Brothers</b> in 1908 by <a href="/wiki/Miller_Williams_(oil_baron)" class="mw-redirect" title="Miller Williams (oil baron)">Miller</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Williams_(oil_baron)" class="mw-redirect" title="David Williams (oil baron)">David Williams</a> in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Smith,_Arkansas" title="Fort Smith, Arkansas">Fort Smith, Arkansas</a>, and soon expanded to building nationwide pipelines for natural gas and petroleum. The company relocated to Tulsa in 1919.  In 1949, John H. Williams, a nephew of the founders, together with his brother Charles Williams and David's son David Williams Jr., bought the business from the founders; John H. Williams remained as president of the company until 1971 and CEO until 1979.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>The company went public in 1957 under the Williams Brothers name.  As it diversified in the 1970s, it was renamed The Williams Companies, Inc.  Since 1997, their <a href="/wiki/Brand_identity" class="mw-redirect" title="Brand identity">brand identity</a> has been simplified to "Williams".
</p><p>In 1966, Williams bought the then-largest petroleum products pipeline in America, known as the Great Lakes Pipe Line Company, for about $287 million. In 1982, it expanded into natural gas transportation with the purchase of Northwest Energy Company, and extended their reach to the East Coast with the 1995 purchase of Transco Energy Company.
</p><p>In 2001, Williams acquired Barrett Resources,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> which provided them with additional national gas reserves.
</p><p>In 2002, the company found itself in <a href="/wiki/Financial_distress" title="Financial distress">financial distress</a> due to changed market conditions and the large debt of its subsidiary Williams Communications Group.  The company obtained and paid off an emergency high interest loan from <a href="/wiki/Warren_Buffett" title="Warren Buffett">Warren Buffett</a> to stay out of <a href="/wiki/Bankruptcy" title="Bankruptcy">bankruptcy</a>, and redirected its focus toward natural gas production, processing, and transportation as well as increasing its resource holdings.  One of the moves it made around that time (2004) was the sale of two of Canada's largest natural gas straddle plants, and its interest in another to <a href="/wiki/Inter_Pipeline_Fund" class="mw-redirect" title="Inter Pipeline Fund">Inter Pipeline Fund</a> for US$540 million.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In 2010, the company underwent a major restructuring that included a reorganization of its extensive pipeline holdings in Williams Partners LP.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On October 2010, Williams and Williams Partners L.P. announced that Chairman and Chief Executive Officer Steve Malcolm would retire at the end of the year. The Board of Directors at Williams said it had elected Alan Armstrong to succeed Malcolm as CEO effective January 3, 2011. Armstrong had served as senior vice president of Williams since 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>On February 16, 2011, Williams' board of directors had approved pursuing a plan to separate the company's businesses into two stand-alone, publicly traded corporations. The plan calls for Williams to separate its exploration and production business via an initial public offering (IPO) in third-quarter 2011 of up to 20 percent of its interest and, in 2012, a tax-free spinoff to Williams shareholders of its remaining interest.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="this also copies/closely paraphrases press release (May 2015)">needs update</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Telecommunications">Telecommunications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Williams_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Telecommunications" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>The company helped to get the modern <a href="/wiki/Telecommunications" class="mw-redirect" title="Telecommunications">telecommunications</a> industry off the ground by running <a href="/wiki/Fiber_optic" class="mw-redirect" title="Fiber optic">fiber optic</a> cable through its decommissioned pipelines.  It built two nationwide networks, which subsequently <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spun off</a> into separate companies.  The first was sold in 1995 to <a href="/wiki/Long_Distance_Discount_Services" class="mw-redirect" title="Long Distance Discount Services">LDDS</a>, which would become <a href="/wiki/WorldCom" class="mw-redirect" title="WorldCom">WorldCom</a> &amp; then <a href="/wiki/MCI_Inc." title="MCI Inc.">MCI</a>). The second was spun off in 2001 as <b>Williams Communications</b>, filed for bankruptcy the following year<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> adopted the name WilTel Communications, and ultimately was acquired by and consolidated into <a href="/wiki/Level_3_Communications" title="Level 3 Communications">Level 3 Communications</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Lawsuits_and_fines">Lawsuits and fines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Williams_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Lawsuits and fines" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>In 2002, Williams Communications Group was sued for that company officials did not properly disclose the failing company's true financial condition, and that officials' public statements belied the firm's plummeting fiscal picture.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In 2007, the Williams Companies agreed to pay $290 million.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Boardwalk_Pipeline_Partners" class="mw-redirect" title="Boardwalk Pipeline Partners">Boardwalk Pipeline Partners</a> and the Williams Companies were fined $2.4 million for 18 incidents that took place between 2006 and 2013 that include failing to monitor corrosion and waiting to repair a natural gas line showing metal loss in Kentucky.<sup id="cite_ref-wdrb_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wdrb-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Restatement">Restatement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Williams_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Restatement" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>On March 1, 1999, Jack D. McCarthy, chief financial officer, said the company's additional review and its annual audit process resulted in
the previously announced 1998 pre-tax income being adjusted downward by $21.2 million.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>On September 16, 2004, Williams Cos. said it amended its fiscal 2003 and first-quarter 2004 filings with the Securities and Exchange Commission to show a reclassification to its discontinued operations and a segment reporting change.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
