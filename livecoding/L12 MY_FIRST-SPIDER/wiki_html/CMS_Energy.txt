</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CMS_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>CMS Energy was formed in 1987 as a holding company with its principal subsidiaries as Consumers Energy and CMS Enterprises.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On October 23, 1987, CMS Energy became a publicly owned company and was listed at the New York Stock Exchange. The company's history dates back to 1886, when William Augustine Foote and Samuel Jarvis initially approached Jackson, Michigan officials and asked them to support efforts to illuminate Jackson's downtown with electric arc lights. They formed a company, Jackson Electric Light Works in 1888. In 1889, they built a hydro plant project at the Trowbridge Dam on the Kalamazoo River.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Foote, after seeing some early success, began consolidating his small power companies and formed Commonwealth Power Company.<sup id="cite_ref-powertechnology_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-powertechnology-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 1907, Foote and Jarvis built <a href="/wiki/Croton_Dam_(Michigan)" title="Croton Dam (Michigan)">Croton Hydro project</a> on the Muskegon River,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> the first project in the world that could transmit 110,000 volts of electricity.<sup id="cite_ref-mlive-mc_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mlive-mc-12">[12]</a></sup> In 1920, Foote and Jarvis offered preferred shares of their company to the public to secure sufficient financing to enter other energy and power generation sectors.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Over the next decades the company built several coal-fired, natural gas, nuclear and electric facilities. In 1946, the company formed a strategic partnership with Panhandle Company to convert former natural gas fields to underground natural gas storage areas.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> CMS Energy's primary subsidiary, Consumers Energy, announced Michigan's first nuclear power plant in 1961, <a href="/wiki/Big_Rock_Point" class="mw-redirect" title="Big Rock Point">Big Rock Point</a> in Charlevoix, Michigan.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>The company announced another nuclear plant, <a href="/wiki/Palisades_Nuclear_Generating_Station" title="Palisades Nuclear Generating Station">Palisades Nuclear Generating Station</a> on Lake Michigan,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> which first produced electricity in December, 1971 to serve 400,000 people a day. CMS Energy owned the plant until 2006, when it was sold to Entergy.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> It then  announced a gas reforming plant to furnish heat for Michigan homes in 1971.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> This plant was the first of its kind in North America to produce synthetic gas, converting impure liquid petroleum into pipeline-quality natural gas.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In 1987, CMS Energy, a holding company with its principal subsidiaries as Consumers Power and CMS Enterprises was formed. Consumers Power was rebranded Consumers Energy in 1997. The firm was one of the first companies to introduce an online billing system in the late 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>In 2001, the firm entered into an exploration agreement with <a href="/wiki/Eritrea" title="Eritrea">Eritrea</a> for oil exploration in the Dismin Block of the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>In 2003, the firm moved its headquarters to One Energy Plaza in Jackson, Michigan. In 2007, the company announced plans to invest $6 billion in Michigan's energy future to support new power plant technology and identify new energy sources.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In 2014, Consumers Energy opened Cross Winds Energy Park in Michigan's Tuscola County and signed a contract with General Electric to supply 621.79-kilowatt wind turbine engines for the project. Which has a total generating capacity of 105 megawatts.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Sustainability_and_Environment">Sustainability and Environment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CMS_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sustainability and Environment" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In 2015, 9 percent of CMS Energy's power comes from renewable energy sources.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Primary CMS Energy subsidiary Consumers Energy joined the Wildlife Habitat Council as an active member.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> By 2007, all power facilities received the Clean Corporate Citizen award from the Michigan Department of Environmental Quality.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CMS_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>Consumers Energy, CMS Energy's primary subsidiary, was named Energy Star Partner of the Year in 2014 and 2015 for excellence in sustainable <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">energy efficiency</a> programs. In 2014, Consumers Energy  was ranked among the top 15 most sustainable energy providers. Later that year, CMS Energy received Edison Electric Institute's Index Award for top cumulative shareholder return in the small cap category.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup>  The firm received the 2015 Emergency Recovery Award<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> for its electricity restoration efforts during the <a href="/wiki/December_2013_North_American_storm_complex" title="December 2013 North American storm complex">December 2013 North American storm complex</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CMS_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Criticism" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>In December 2011, the non-partisan organization <a href="/wiki/Public_Campaign#Reports" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Campaign">Public Campaign</a> criticized <b>CMS Energy</b> for spending $3.48 million on <a href="/wiki/Lobbying" title="Lobbying">lobbying</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tax_avoidance" title="Tax avoidance">not paying any taxes</a> during 2008-2010, instead getting $29 million in tax rebates, despite making a profit of $415 million, and increasing executive pay by 79% to $900.4 million in 2010 for its top 5 executives.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Philanthropy">Philanthropy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=CMS_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Philanthropy" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<p>CMS Energy has helped establish and finance numerous research, educational and cultural institutes in the United States. Consumers Energy was given the William Booth Award in 2013 by The Salvation Army in recognition of its long history of philanthropic activities and leadership for the PeopleCare assistance program.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p><p>CMS Energy participates in internship and cooperative education programs to build career opportunities in Michigan. Consumers Energy has also worked on early childhood development and education in Michigan and has also received Wildlife Habitat Council (WHC) compliant certificates for its efforts to promote environmental education.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
