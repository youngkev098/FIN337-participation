<p><b>Altria Group, Inc.</b> (previously known as <b>Philip Morris Companies, Inc.</b>) is an American corporation and one of the world's largest producers and marketers of <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">tobacco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cigarettes" class="mw-redirect" title="Cigarettes">cigarettes</a> and related products. It operates worldwide and is headquartered in unincorporated <a href="/wiki/Henrico_County,_Virginia" title="Henrico County, Virginia">Henrico County, Virginia</a>, just outside the city of <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_VA" class="mw-redirect" title="Richmond, VA">Richmond</a>.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Altria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Philip_Morris_USA#History" title="Philip Morris USA">Philip Morris USA § History</a></div>
<p>Altria emerged from Philip Morris. The onset of "<a href="/wiki/Rebranding" title="Rebranding">rebranding</a>" of Philip Morris Companies to Altria took place in 2003 (Philip Morris would later split, with PM USA remaining Altria's primary and only consistently held asset). Altria was created because Philip Morris wished to emphasize that its business portfolio had come to consist of more than Philip Morris USA and Philip Morris International; at the time, it owned an 84% stake in Kraft,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> although that business has since been spun off.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The name "Altria" is claimed to come from the Latin word for "high" and was part of a trend of companies rebranding to names that previously did not exist, <a href="/wiki/Accenture" title="Accenture">Accenture</a> (previously
Andersen Consulting) and <a href="/wiki/Verizon" class="mw-redirect" title="Verizon">Verizon</a> being notable examples,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> though linguist <a href="/wiki/Steven_Pinker" title="Steven Pinker">Steven Pinker</a> suggests that in fact the name is an "egregious example" of <a href="/wiki/Phonesthesia" class="mw-redirect" title="Phonesthesia">phonesthesia</a> — with the company attempting to "switch its image from bad people who sell addictive carcinogens to a place or state marked by <a href="/wiki/Altruism" title="Altruism">altruism</a> and other lofty values".<sup id="cite_ref-Pinker_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pinker-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>The company's branding consultants, the <a href="/wiki/Wirthlin_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Wirthlin Group">Wirthlin Group</a>, said: “The name change alternative offers the possibility of masking the negatives associated with the tobacco business,” thus enabling the company to improve its image and raise its profile without sacrificing tobacco profits.<sup id="cite_ref-ncbi.nlm.nih.gov_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncbi.nlm.nih.gov-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>Philip Morris executives thought a name change would insulate the larger corporation and its other operating companies from the political pressures on tobacco.<sup id="cite_ref-ncbi.nlm.nih.gov_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncbi.nlm.nih.gov-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>The rebranding took place amidst social, legal, and financially troubled circumstances.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Vagueness" title="Wikipedia:Vagueness"><span title="This information is too vague. (August 2013)">vague</span></a></i>]</sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2003 Altria was ranked <i><a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)">Fortune</a></i> number 11, and has steadily declined since. In 2010 Altria Group (MO) ranked at <i>Fortune</i> number 137, whereas its former asset, Philip Morris International, was ranked 94th.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In 2006, a United States court found that Philip Morris "publicly ... disputed scientific findings linking smoking and disease knowing their assertions were false." <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In a 2006 ruling, a federal court found that Altria, along with R.J. Reynolds Tobacco, Lorillard, and Philip Morris were found guilty of misleading the public about the dangers of smoking.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Within this ruling, it was noted that “defendants altered the chemical form of nicotine delivered in mainstream cigarette smoke for the purpose of improving nicotine transfer efficiency and increasing the speed with which nicotine is absorbed by smokers.“<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> This was done by manipulating smoke pH with ammonia. Adding ammonia increases the smoke pH, in a process called “freebasing” which causes smokers to be “exposed to higher internal nicotine doses and become more addicted to the product.”<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>On March 30, 2007, Altria's 88.1% stake in <a href="/wiki/Kraft_Foods" title="Kraft Foods">Kraft Foods</a> was <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spun off</a>, through a distribution of the remaining stake of shares (88.1%) to Altria shareholders. That same year, Altria began selling all its shares of Philip Morris International to Altria stockholders, a spin-off that was completed on March 28, 2008. Again in 2007 the company began the acquisition of cigar manufacturer <a href="/wiki/John_Middleton_Co." title="John Middleton Co.">John Middleton Co.</a> from Bradford Holdings, which was complete in 2008. After Philip Morris International spun off, the former international subsidiaries halted the purchase of tobacco from America, which was a major factor in the closing of a newly renovated plant in <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>, an approximately 50% reduction in manufacturing, large-scale layoffs, and induced early retirements.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In 2008, Altria officially moved its headquarters from <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> to <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">Richmond, Virginia</a> after Philip Morris sold its downtown offices in New York City a decade earlier. With a few exceptions, all manufacturing, commercial, and executive employees had long been based in and around Richmond. Currently the company is headquartered in an <a href="/wiki/Unincorporated_area" title="Unincorporated area">unincorporated area</a> within Henrico County, less than five miles west of the city limits of <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">Richmond</a> and less than ten miles from its downtown Richmond campus.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Altria_Center_For_Research_and_Technology.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Altria_Center_For_Research_and_Technology.jpg/220px-Altria_Center_For_Research_and_Technology.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="4032" data-file-height="3024"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Altria_Center_For_Research_and_Technology.jpg/220px-Altria_Center_For_Research_and_Technology.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Altria_Center_For_Research_and_Technology.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Altria Center for Research and Technology in Downtown Richmond</div></div></div>
<p>Aside from the Philip Morris/Altria headquarters, some of their other buildings included the Altria Center for Research and Technology in downtown Richmond, their manufacturing center in South Richmond, and the adjacent operations center which began shutting down in 2007-2008, as a result of the loss of demand from PMI member companies. The layoffs beginning in 2007 affected thousands of Altria, Altria Client Services, Philip Morris USA, and contracted employees in Richmond and North Carolina.
</p><p>In 2009, Altria finalized its purchase of <a href="/wiki/UST_Inc." title="UST Inc.">UST Inc.</a>, whose products included <a href="/wiki/Dipping_tobacco" title="Dipping tobacco">smokeless tobacco</a> (made by <a href="/wiki/U.S._Smokeless_Tobacco_Company" title="U.S. Smokeless Tobacco Company">U.S. Smokeless Tobacco Company</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Wine" title="Wine">wine</a> (made by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ste._Michelle_Wine_Estates&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ste. Michelle Wine Estates (page does not exist)">Ste. Michelle Wine Estates</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> This ended a short era of competition between the new <a href="/wiki/Marlboro_(cigarette)" title="Marlboro (cigarette)">Marlboro</a> smokeless tobacco products such as <a href="/wiki/Snus" title="Snus">snus</a>, and those produced by UST Inc.
</p><p>On December 8, 2018, Altria has announced its intent to acquire a 45% stake in Cronos Group for $1.8 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>On December 20, 2018, Altria finalized the acquisition of a 35% stake in <a href="/wiki/Juul" title="Juul">JUUL Labs</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Electronic_cigarette" title="Electronic cigarette">e-cigarette</a> company based out of San Francisco, California, for $12.8 billion dollars.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On November 3, 2019, it was reported that Altria was taking a $4.5 billion writedown on its stake in Juul, 35% of its original value.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>

<p>Prior to being based in Virginia, Philip Morris had its headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Midtown_Manhattan" title="Midtown Manhattan">Midtown Manhattan</a>, New York City.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In 2003, Philip Morris announced that it would move its headquarters to Virginia. The company said that it planned to keep around 750 employees in its former headquarters. Brendan McCormick, a spokesperson for Philip Morris, said that the company estimated that the move would save the company over $60 million each year.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The company now has its head offices in an <a href="/wiki/Unincorporated_area" title="Unincorporated area">unincorporated area</a> of <a href="/wiki/Henrico_County,_Virginia" title="Henrico County, Virginia">Henrico County, Virginia</a>, near Richmond.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In addition, the company has a 450,000-square-foot, $350 million Center for Research and Technology located in downtown Richmond at the <a href="/wiki/Virginia_BioTechnology_Research_Park" title="Virginia BioTechnology Research Park">Virginia BioTechnology Research Park</a> that employs approximately 600 scientists, engineers and support staff.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_influence">Political influence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Altria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Political influence" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Center_for_Public_Integrity" title="Center for Public Integrity">Center for Public Integrity</a>, Altria spent around $101 million on <a href="/wiki/Lobbying_in_the_United_States" title="Lobbying in the United States">lobbying the United States government</a> between 1998 and 2004, making it the second most active organization in the nation.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>Altria also funded <a href="/wiki/The_Advancement_of_Sound_Science_Coalition" class="mw-redirect" title="The Advancement of Sound Science Coalition">The Advancement of Sound Science Coalition</a> which lobbied against the <a href="/wiki/Scientific_consensus" title="Scientific consensus">scientific consensus</a> on <a href="/wiki/Scientific_opinion_on_climate_change" class="mw-redirect" title="Scientific opinion on climate change">anthropogenic climate change</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>Daniel Smith, representing Altria, sits on the Private Enterprise Board of the <a href="/wiki/American_Legislative_Exchange_Council" title="American Legislative Exchange Council">American Legislative Exchange Council</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
