It mainly owns industrial warehouses.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Business">Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duke_Realty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Business" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Duke Realty primarily owns industrial warehouses used for distribution.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-1">[1]</a></sup>  Many of its properties are used by e-commerce retailers.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-1">[1]</a></sup>  It also owned medical offices and other offices, but it has sold almost all of these properties.  It owns properties throughout the United States, with a concentration in the Midwest and the South.  As of 2016, the three largest markets by square feet were Indianapolis, Chicago, and Atlanta, with 12.5%, 10.7%, and 9.5% of total square feet.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duke_Realty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Duke Realty was founded in 1972 by John Rosebrough, Phil Duke, and John Wynne.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> Its first development was in the Park 100 neighborhood in the northwest part of Indianapolis.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup>  In 1993, Duke Realty held an IPO on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>, raising $310 million.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup>  In 1999, it merged with Weeks Corporation, another real estate investment trust with properties primarily in the US Southwest.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>  Weeks shareholders ended up with 28% of the new company.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4">[4]</a></sup>  In 2006, it bought 32 buildings in the Washington, D.C. area from the Mark Winkler Company.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>  In May 2017, it sold its medical office properties to Healthcare Trust of America for $2.8 billion, in order to focus on its industrial properties.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>  In July 2017, Duke Realty joined the S&amp;P 500.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Properties">Properties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duke_Realty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Properties" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>Duke Realty is developing the Chesapeake Commerce Center, an industrial park, on the former site of the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Assembly" title="Baltimore Assembly">General Motors Baltimore Assembly Plant</a> in <a href="/wiki/Baltimore" title="Baltimore">Baltimore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>  As of 2016, five buildings had been built, and a sixth was under construction.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>  <a href="/wiki/Amazon_(company)" title="Amazon (company)">Amazon</a> runs a distribution center in one of the warehouses in the center.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>  It is also developing the Legacy Center, another industrial park, on the site of the former General Motors Linden Assembly in <a href="/wiki/Linden,_New_Jersey" title="Linden, New Jersey">Linden</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>  As of 2016, three buildings have been constructed.
</p><p>Duke Realty was one of the developers of the <a href="/wiki/Captrust_Tower" title="Captrust Tower">Captrust Tower</a> in <a href="/wiki/Raleigh,_North_Carolina" title="Raleigh, North Carolina">Raleigh</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Scripps_Center" title="Scripps Center">Scripps Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati" title="Cincinnati">Cincinnati</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>.
</p>
