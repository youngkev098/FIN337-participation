</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=PulteGroup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>In 1950, when he was 18 years old, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Pulte" title="Bill Pulte">Bill Pulte</a> began building and selling houses.<sup id="cite_ref-obit_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1956, the company was formed.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup> It was based in <a href="/wiki/Bloomfield_Hills,_Michigan" title="Bloomfield Hills, Michigan">Bloomfield Hills, Michigan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-obit_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1972, the company became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>. In 1998, PulteGroup acquired Divosta for an estimated $150 million.<sup id="cite_ref-divosta_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-divosta-5">[5]</a></sup> The company also acquired Radnor Homes.<sup id="cite_ref-radnor_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-radnor-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2001, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Del_E._Webb_Construction_Company" title="Del E. Webb Construction Company">Del E. Webb Construction Company</a>, founded by <a href="/wiki/Del_Webb" title="Del Webb">Del Webb</a>, for $1.8 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-web_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-web-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2003, the company acquired Sivage-Thomas Homes.<sup id="cite_ref-sivage_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sivage-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2009, Pulte Group acquired Centex for $1.3 billion in stock.<sup id="cite_ref-centex_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-centex-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-centexx_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-centexx-10">[10]</a></sup> The company moved its headquarters to Atlanta, Georgia in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In 2016, Ryan Marshall became president and CEO of the company.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In April 2019, the company acquired the homebuilding operations of American West Homes for $150 million.<sup id="cite_ref-awh_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-awh-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Assault_of_union_workers_by_another_employee">Assault of union workers by another employee</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=PulteGroup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Assault of union workers by another employee" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In May 2007, during a protest at a Pulte Homes construction site, an employee used a water truck owned by the company to repeatedly assault a group of union workers picketing outside a home with a high pressure water hose.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Construction_defects_reported_in_customer_survey">Construction defects reported in customer survey</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=PulteGroup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Construction defects reported in customer survey" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2008, Building Justice, a project of the <a href="/wiki/International_Union_of_Painters_and_Allied_Trades" title="International Union of Painters and Allied Trades">International Union of Painters and Allied Trades</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Sheet_Metal_Workers%27_International_Association" title="Sheet Metal Workers' International Association">Sheet Metal Workers' International Association</a>, with support from the <a href="/wiki/AFL%E2%80%93CIO" title="AFL–CIO">AFL–CIO</a>, published a survey titled "Poorly Built by Pulte, No Different at Del Webb: Homeowner Dissatisfaction in Arizona, Nevada and California", in which 63% of the 872 Pulte and Del Webb home owners in Arizona, Nevada, and California that responded to the survey reported construction defects in their homes.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Dismissed_lawsuit_regarding_propping_up_sale_prices">Dismissed lawsuit regarding propping up sale prices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=PulteGroup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Dismissed lawsuit regarding propping up sale prices" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In October 2009, a <a href="/wiki/Class_action" title="Class action">class action</a> lawsuit was filed by <a href="/wiki/Steve_Berman_(lawyer)" title="Steve Berman (lawyer)">Steve Berman</a> accusing the company of artificially propping up house sales prices and contributing to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_housing_bubble" title="United States housing bubble">United States housing bubble</a>. The lawsuit was dismissed by the court.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
