<p><b>Ulta Beauty, Inc.</b>, formerly known as <b>Ulta Salon, Cosmetics &amp; Fragrance Inc.</b>, is an American chain of beauty stores in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Bolingbrook,_Illinois" title="Bolingbrook, Illinois">Bolingbrook, Illinois</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Ulta Beauty carries <a href="/wiki/Cosmetics" title="Cosmetics">cosmetics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Skincare" class="mw-redirect" title="Skincare">skincare</a> brands, men's and women's <a href="/wiki/Fragrances" class="mw-redirect" title="Fragrances">fragrances</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nail_(anatomy)" title="Nail (anatomy)">nail</a> products, <a href="/wiki/Bathing" title="Bathing">bath</a> and body products, beauty tools and <a href="/wiki/Haircare" class="mw-redirect" title="Haircare">haircare</a> products. Each store is also equipped with a salon, <a href="/wiki/Benefit_Cosmetics" title="Benefit Cosmetics">Benefit</a> brow bar and <a href="/wiki/Dermalogica" title="Dermalogica">Dermalogica</a> skin bar. George, the former President of <a href="/wiki/Osco_Drug,_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Osco Drug, Inc.">Osco Drug, Inc.</a>, and Terry Hanson. <sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">[5]</a></sup> George left work at Osco in 1989, beginning his attempt to create a business plan for what would later be Ulta.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">[5]</a></sup> His business plan included a new concept for retail that offers higher-end and lower-end products.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">[5]</a></sup> Other Osco executives joined George and Hanson in the business, and raised $11.5 million in venture capital, and launched Ulta officially in 1990.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">[5]</a></sup> In September 1996, Hanson hired Charles "Rick" Weber, another Osco veteran, as Chief Financial Officer.  In the later years, Terry Hanson became president of the company. In December 1999, Lyn Kirby, formerly of Sears Circle of Beauty, became the President and Chief Executive Officer and Weber became Senior Executive Vice President, Chief Operating Officer and Chief Financial Officer. The two ran the business together until Weber left in September 2006 when he was selected by Tom Stemberg, founder of Staples, to become Chairman and Chief Executive officer of Rec Room Furniture and Games, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-aboutus_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aboutus-6">[6]</a></sup> On October 25, 2007, the company became publicly traded on the <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a>. In 2008, the company opened a second distribution center in <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix, Arizona</a>. On April 26, 2010, Ulta announced that Carl "Chuck" Rubin would be appointed as Chief Operating Officer, President, and as a member of the Board of Directors, effective May 10, 2010. According to public filings, Rubin earned over $11.1MM in 2010, making him one of the 20 highest paid CEOs in Chicago.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<p>On June 24, 2013, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Mary_Dillon_(businesswoman)" title="Mary Dillon (businesswoman)">Mary Dillon</a> would be appointed as Chief Executive Officer and a member of the Board of Directors, effective July 1, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Dillon was previously the chief of <a href="/wiki/U.S._Cellular" title="U.S. Cellular">U.S. Cellular</a> and a senior executive at <a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s" title="McDonald's">McDonald's</a> and <a href="/wiki/PepsiCo" title="PepsiCo">PepsiCo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>As of February 2017, the company had a net income of $269 million, and has stock in over 20,000 products including makeup, fragrances, and nail, skin, and hair products. Ulta Beauty offers products from about 500 beauty brands across all categories and price points. Ulta also employs over 35,000 associates, where 88% are female. <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>Ultimately, Ulta Beauty was designed for customers to go into freestanding stores, such as found in strip malls, that allow them to feel comfortable shopping in a makeup store, with no workers handing out samples. In 1994, Ulta had a makeover that eliminated products and items that were typical of drugstores, such as toothbrushes and vitamins. The store moved strictly to beauty products. In 1999, Ulta Beauty changed its name from Ulta3, changing the brand almost entirely.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ulta_Beauty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Operations" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In 2013, Ulta opened 125 stores in the United States, bringing their total number of locations to 675 stores. They also announced plans to open 100 more locations by the end of 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In August 4, 2018, Ulta operated 1,124 stores in 49 states and the District of Columbia. <sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>As of February 2, 2019, Ulta operates 1,174 retail stores across 50 states.<sup id="cite_ref-Company_Overview_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Company_Overview-1">[1]</a></sup> A majority of Ulta Beauty stores are located in the East Coast region, although California also has a large presence of company-owned stores.<sup id="cite_ref-Ulta_Beauty_store_count_by_state_U.S._2018_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ulta_Beauty_store_count_by_state_U.S._2018-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>Ulta Beauty offers both high-end and drugstore cosmetics, skincare, and fragrances,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> in addition to its own brand of makeup, bath and body products, skincare, haircare, nail polish, fragrances and some men's products. In May 2017, <a href="/wiki/MAC_Cosmetics" title="MAC Cosmetics">MAC Cosmetics</a> became available for purchase online,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> with products scheduled to be in stores beginning in June 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kylie_Cosmetics" title="Kylie Cosmetics">Kylie Cosmetics</a> debuted in Ulta stores November 17th, 2018. <sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Also, <a href="/wiki/ColourPop_Cosmetics" title="ColourPop Cosmetics">Colourpop</a> Cosmetics launched in Ulta stores on February 25th, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>Ulta has a salon located in every store nationwide. The store format includes a 950 square feet salon area with eight to ten stations. The salon has a concierge desk, skin treatment room, and semi-private shampoo and hair color processing areas. It offers services including haircuts and coloring from <a href="/wiki/Redken" title="Redken">Redken</a>-trained hair stylists, skin treatments and facials from Dermalogica-trained skin therapists, and manicures from OPI-trained professionals. They also provide professional makeup applications.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> They additionally include the Benefit Brow Bar, which includes services such as brow arching, brow tinting, facial waxing, and eyelash application.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>Ulta Beauty sales were $713.8 million for the first quarter ending in May 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> At the end of the second quarter ending in August 2014, Ulta Beauty reported that total sales increased by 22.2 percent and comparable store sales increased 9.6 percent.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Wall Street expected the company's total sales to reach $3.19 billion for the fiscal year ending in February 2015, which would be almost three times its 2009 sales levels.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> As of February 2, 2019 net sales for Ulta Beauty have increased 9.7% to $2,124.7 million.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>In response to the COVID19 pandemic, which started to widely impact the United States retail market in March of 2020, Ulta was one of the first retailers to proactively close their retail store locations. This was to protect their employees and guests as well as reduce the spread of the virus. Store closers started on March 19, 2020 and are schedule to reopen on March 30, 2020. Although given the current fluidity of the situation, Ulta is expected to be re-evaluate the opening timeline as information becomes available and more time progresses.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Charitable_giving">Charitable giving</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ulta_Beauty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Charitable giving" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>The Ulta Beauty Charitable Foundation (UBCF), formed in 2016, aims to support the education and well-being of women and their families by partnering with the Breast Cancer Research Foundation (BCRF), Dress for Success and additional local charities. In 2009, Ulta Beauty partnered with BCRF and in the 10 years since, has raised more than $22 million to support its groundbreaking research. UBCF is among BCRF’s top three major donors and is currently helping to fund 16 breast cancer research projects. UBCF partnered with Dress for Success in 2017 to help empower women to achieve economic independence by providing them with professional attire and the resources needed to help them progress their careers. Ulta Beauty provides financial support, products and valuable time to local nonprofit organizations, especially in relation to family and child care, and many leaders serve on the board of various nonprofits organizations. UBCF also has an Associate Relief Program that helps employees at Ulta Beauty affected by a financial hardship.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
