<p><b>Carnival Corporation &amp; plc</b> is a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a>-<a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> cruise operator, currently the world's largest travel leisure company, with a combined fleet of over 100 vessels across 10 <a href="/wiki/Cruise_line" title="Cruise line">cruise line</a> brands. A <a href="/wiki/Dual-listed_company" title="Dual-listed company">dual-listed company</a>, Carnival is composed of two companies, US-based Carnival Corporation and UK-based Carnival plc, which function as one entity. Carnival Corporation is listed on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> and Carnival plc is listed on the <a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange" title="London Stock Exchange">London Stock Exchange</a>. As such, Carnival is the only company in the world to be included in both the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a> and <a href="/wiki/FTSE_100" class="mw-redirect" title="FTSE 100">FTSE 100</a> indices.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carnival_Corporation_%26_plc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Carnival Corporation was founded as <a href="/wiki/Carnival_Cruise_Line" title="Carnival Cruise Line">Carnival Cruise Line</a> in 1972. The company grew steadily throughout the 1970s and 1980s, making an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> in 1987. The capital generated was used to finance acquisitions. Between 1989 and 1999, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Holland_America_Line" title="Holland America Line">Holland America Line</a>, <a href="/wiki/Windstar_Cruises" title="Windstar Cruises">Windstar Cruises</a>, Westours, <a href="/wiki/Seabourn_Cruise_Line" title="Seabourn Cruise Line">Seabourn Cruise Line</a>, <a href="/wiki/Costa_Cruises" title="Costa Cruises">Costa Cruises</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cunard_Line" title="Cunard Line">Cunard Line</a>. The name Carnival Corporation was adopted in 1993, to distinguish the parent company from its flagship cruise line subsidiary.<sup id="cite_ref-timeline_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeline-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p><a href="/wiki/P%26O_Princess_Cruises" title="P&amp;O Princess Cruises">P&amp;O Princess Cruises</a> plc was formed in 2000, following the <a href="/wiki/Demerger" title="Demerger">demerger</a> of the cruise ship division of the <a href="/wiki/P%26O_(company)" title="P&amp;O (company)">P&amp;O</a> group.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Originating as the <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_and_Oriental_Steam_Navigation_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="Peninsular and Oriental Steam Navigation Company">Peninsular and Oriental Steam Navigation Company</a> in England in 1837, P&amp;O operated the world's first commercial cruise ships. Restructuring of the P&amp;O group in the 20th century led to its cruise operations being rebranded as <a href="/wiki/P%26O_Cruises" title="P&amp;O Cruises">P&amp;O Cruises</a> and <a href="/wiki/P%26O_Cruises_Australia" title="P&amp;O Cruises Australia">P&amp;O Cruises Australia</a>, with the company acquiring <a href="/wiki/Princess_Cruises" title="Princess Cruises">Princess Cruises</a> in 1974. Following the demerger in 2000, the company also acquired <a href="/wiki/AIDA_Cruises" title="AIDA Cruises">AIDA Cruises</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> as well as establishing the <a href="/wiki/A%27Rosa_Cruises" title="A'Rosa Cruises">A'Rosa Cruises</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ocean_Village_(company)" title="Ocean Village (company)">Ocean Village</a> brands.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In 2003, Carnival Corporation acquired <a href="/wiki/P%26O_Princess_Cruises" title="P&amp;O Princess Cruises">P&amp;O Princess Cruises</a> plc.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> It was agreed that P&amp;O Princess Cruises plc would remain a separate company, listed on the <a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange" title="London Stock Exchange">London Stock Exchange</a> and retaining its British shareholder body and management team. The company was renamed Carnival plc, with the operations of the two companies merged into one entity. Carnival Corporation and Carnival plc jointly own all the operating companies in the Carnival group.<sup id="cite_ref-P&amp;Odeal_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P&amp;Odeal-2">[2]</a></sup> Prior to Carnival Corporation's acquisition, P&amp;O Princess Cruises plc had agreed to a merger with <a href="/wiki/Royal_Caribbean_Cruises_Ltd." class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Caribbean Cruises Ltd.">Royal Caribbean Cruises Ltd</a>. The deal unraveled as Carnival Corporation initiated a hostile takeover with improved terms for British shareholders.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>Carnival sold <a href="/wiki/Windstar_Cruises" title="Windstar Cruises">Windstar Cruises</a> to <a href="/wiki/Ambassadors_Group" title="Ambassadors Group">Ambassadors Group</a> in February 2007<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Swan_Hellenic" title="Swan Hellenic">Swan Hellenic</a> to <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Sterling,_Baron_Sterling_of_Plaistow" title="Jeffrey Sterling, Baron Sterling of Plaistow">Lord Sterling</a> in March 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2015, <a href="/wiki/CSSC_Carnival_Cruise_Shipping" title="CSSC Carnival Cruise Shipping">CSSC Carnival Cruise Shipping</a>, a joint venture between Carnival, the <a href="/wiki/China_Investment_Corporation" title="China Investment Corporation">China Investment Corporation</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/China_State_Shipbuilding_Corporation" title="China State Shipbuilding Corporation">China State Shipbuilding Corporation</a>, was founded, with operations expected to commence in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-Reuters_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2018, Carnival Corporation announced its intention to invest in the construction of a new terminal in the port of Sasebo, Japan. It is expected to open in 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2018, Carnival Corporation announced that it had acquired the <a href="/wiki/White_Pass_and_Yukon_Route" title="White Pass and Yukon Route">White Pass and Yukon Route</a> from <a href="/wiki/TWC_Enterprises_Limited" class="mw-redirect" title="TWC Enterprises Limited">TWC Enterprises Limited</a> for US$290 million. The properties acquired were port, railroad and retail operations in <a href="/wiki/Skagway,_Alaska" title="Skagway, Alaska">Skagway, Alaska</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="AIDA_Cruises">AIDA Cruises</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carnival_Corporation_%26_plc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: AIDA Cruises" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/AIDA_Cruises" title="AIDA Cruises">AIDA Cruises</a></div>
<p>AIDA Cruises originated from the state-owned German shipping conglomerate Deutsche Seereederei, established in <a href="/wiki/Rostock,_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Rostock, Germany">Rostock, Germany</a>, in 1952.<sup id="cite_ref-aida_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aida-21">[21]</a></sup> The company entered the passenger market in the 1960s, but after the unification of Germany in 1990, the company was privatised and its passenger ships acquired by Deutsche Seetouristik. In 1996, the company launched its first new cruise ship <i>AIDA</i>, but after failing to achieve a profit, the ship was sold to <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Cruise_Line" title="Norwegian Cruise Line">Norwegian Cruise Line</a>, continuing operations under a charter agreement.<sup id="cite_ref-aida_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aida-21">[21]</a></sup> In 1999, Deutsche Seetouristik was acquired by British shipping company <a href="/wiki/P%26O_(company)" title="P&amp;O (company)">P&amp;O</a>, with the <i>AIDA</i> name being repurchased from NCL. P&amp;O subsequently formed AIDA Cruises as a subsidiary brand, with two new ships ordered to form a fleet. <i>AIDA</i> was renamed <i><a href="/wiki/AIDAcara" title="AIDAcara">AIDAcara</a></i>, with <i><a href="/wiki/AIDAvita" title="AIDAvita">AIDAvita</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/AIDAaura" title="AIDAaura">AIDAaura</a></i> launched in 2002 and 2003 respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-aida_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aida-21">[21]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Carnival_Cruise_Line">Carnival Cruise Line</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carnival_Corporation_%26_plc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Carnival Cruise Line" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Carnival_Cruise_Line" title="Carnival Cruise Line">Carnival Cruise Line</a></div>
<p>Carnival Cruise Line was founded in 1972 as a subsidiary of American International Travel Service (AITS), by <a href="/wiki/Ted_Arison" title="Ted Arison">Ted Arison</a> and <a href="/wiki/Meshulam_Riklis" title="Meshulam Riklis">Meshulam Riklis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-timeline_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeline-3">[3]</a></sup> Due to mounting debts, Riklis sold his stake in the company to Arison for $1 in 1974. However through the acquisition of existing ships, the company continued to grow and, in 1980, Carnival ordered its first new commission, the <i><a href="/wiki/Ocean_Dream_(1982_ship)" title="Ocean Dream (1982 ship)">Tropicale</a></i>, which was completed in 1981/2.<sup id="cite_ref-timeline_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeline-3">[3]</a></sup> Three further ships were commissioned during the 1980s, the <i><a href="/wiki/MS_Magellan_(ship,_1985)" class="mw-redirect" title="MS Magellan (ship, 1985)">Holiday</a></i> (1985), <a href="/wiki/Henna_(ship)" title="Henna (ship)">Jubilee</a> (1986) and <a href="/wiki/MS_Grand_Celebration" title="MS Grand Celebration">Celebration</a> (1987). In 1987, Carnival completed an initial public offering of 20 percent of its common stock on the New York Stock Exchange, raising approximately $400m in capital.<sup id="cite_ref-timeline_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeline-3">[3]</a></sup> The capital raised was used to finance acquisitions, so in 1993 the business was restructured as a holding company, under the name Carnival Corporation, with Carnival Cruise Line becoming its principal subsidiary.<sup id="cite_ref-timeline_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeline-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Costa_Cruises">Costa Cruises</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carnival_Corporation_%26_plc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Costa Cruises" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Costa_Cruises" title="Costa Cruises">Costa Cruises</a></div>
<p>Costa Cruises originates from a cargo shipping company founded by Giacomo Costa fu Andrea in Genoa, Italy in 1854.<sup id="cite_ref-costa_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-costa-22">[22]</a></sup> Better known as Costa Line or C Line by the 1920s, its first passenger carrier was <i><a href="/wiki/USS_Rappahannock_(AF-6)" title="USS Rappahannock (AF-6)">Maria C</a></i>, a former <a href="/wiki/U.S._Navy" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Navy">U.S. Navy</a> stores ship that was partly converted for passenger use and served various routes to North and South America from 1947 to 1953.<sup id="cite_ref-costa_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-costa-22">[22]</a></sup> The company's first dedicated passenger ship was the <i><a href="/wiki/HMS_Southern_Prince_(M47)" class="mw-redirect" title="HMS Southern Prince (M47)">Anna C</a></i>, a cargo vessel that was requisitioned for war time use by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> and refitted as an accommodation ship before returning to merchant use.<sup id="cite_ref-costa_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-costa-22">[22]</a></sup> Costa purchased the ship in 1947 and it operated between Italy and South America from 1948, later converting to full-time cruising and serving with the company until 1971. From the late 1960s until the 1980s, Costa rapidly developed its passenger operations into what we now recognise as modern cruise ships. Subsequently, in 1987, it consolidated its cruise ship operations into a new company, Costa Cruises, which at its peak, was the largest cruise ship operator in the world. The takeover of Costa Cruises by Carnival Corporation began in 1997, as a 50/50 deal between Carnival and the British tour operator Airtours.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup>  On 13 January 2012, <a href="/wiki/Costa_Concordia" title="Costa Concordia">Costa Concordia</a>, a ship operated by the line, hit an underwater rock and <a href="/wiki/Costa_Concordia_disaster" title="Costa Concordia disaster">ran aground</a> in  <a href="/wiki/Isola_del_Giglio" title="Isola del Giglio">Isola del Giglio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tuscany" title="Tuscany">Tuscany</a>, resulting in 32 deaths. Investigations proved that the captain and general bad organization was responsible for the disaster, and the company was fined.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Carnival_CSSC">Carnival CSSC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carnival_Corporation_%26_plc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Carnival CSSC" data-section="16" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/CSSC_Carnival_Cruise_Shipping" title="CSSC Carnival Cruise Shipping">CSSC Carnival Cruise Shipping</a></div>
<p>CSSC Carnival Cruise Shipping (Carnival China) is a Chinese-American cruise line that is scheduled to begin operation in 2019. CSSC Carnival was founded in October 2015 as a joint venture, worth about $4 billion over ten years, between American cruise line Carnival Corporation &amp; plc, Chinese sovereign wealth fund China Investment Corporation, and Chinese shipbuilder China State Shipbuilding Corporation (CSSC). CSSC Carnival is headquartered in Hong Kong and is majority owned by the Chinese shareholders, which collectively own 60% of the company, with Carnival holding the remainder.<sup id="cite_ref-Reuters_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Cunard_Line">Cunard Line</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carnival_Corporation_%26_plc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Cunard Line" data-section="17" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Cunard_Line" title="Cunard Line">Cunard Line</a></div>
<p>The second oldest brand in the Carnival group after P&amp;O, Cunard Line originated in 1840 and celebrated its 179th anniversary in 2019. It was founded by <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Cunard" title="Samuel Cunard">Samuel Cunard</a>, who was awarded the first trans-Atlantic mail contract in 1837 and established the British and North American Royal Mail Steam-Packet Company in 1840.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> After initially dominating the trans-Atlantic route, the need for new capital led to the company being re-organised as the Cunard Steamship Company Ltd in 1879.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The early 1900s saw increased competition for speed, particularly from Germany, which led the British government to subsidise the building of <i><a href="/wiki/RMS_Mauretania_(1906)" title="RMS Mauretania (1906)">Mauretania</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/RMS_Lusitania" title="RMS Lusitania">Lusitania</a></i>, which both won the <a href="/wiki/Blue_Riband" title="Blue Riband">Blue Riband</a>. Competition continued to increase however and by the early 1930s, Cunard was experiencing financial difficulties.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> To secure further government subsidy, it agreed to merge with its chief rival <a href="/wiki/White_Star_Line" title="White Star Line">White Star Line</a>, to form <a href="/wiki/Cunard-White_Star_Line" title="Cunard-White Star Line">Cunard-White Star Line</a> in 1934.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Cunard later purchased the remaining White Star shares in 1947, reverting to the name Cunard Line in 1949. Cunard continued to operate independently until 1971, when it was acquired by the conglomerate <a href="/wiki/Trafalgar_House_(company)" title="Trafalgar House (company)">Trafalgar House</a>, which was in turn taken over by the Norwegian company <a href="/wiki/Kv%C3%A6rner" title="Kværner">Kværner</a> in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> In 1998, Carnival Corporation purchased a controlling stake in Cunard, completing the acquisition in 1999 to become sole shareholder.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup>  Since that time, Cunard has been one of Carnival's most high-profile brands, with the continued popularity of the famous <a href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_2" title="Queen Elizabeth 2">Queen Elizabeth 2</a> and the development of the world's largest trans-Atlantic Ocean liner <a href="/wiki/Queen_Mary_2" title="Queen Mary 2">Queen Mary 2</a>, which continues to be the flagship of the fleet.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>

<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Holland_America_Line" title="Holland America Line">Holland America Line</a></div>
<p>Holland America Line originated as Plate, Reuchlin &amp; Company, founded in <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, in 1871. Initially struggling to survive, the company went public in 1873, renamed Nederlandsch Amerikaansche Stoomvaart Maatschappij (NASM).<sup id="cite_ref-hal_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hal-32">[32]</a></sup> The company grew quickly in the early years, acquiring several new ships, including SS <i>Rotterdam</i>, which operated the company's first passenger cruise in 1895. The company also quickly became known by its shortened English name, Holland America Line, which was officially adopted in 1869. By the early 1900s, the company had separated its cargo and passenger operations, with its passenger ships being identifiable by names ending with <i>dam</i>, a tradition which continues with Holland America cruise ships today. The development of container shipping in the 1960s, forced the company to make a decision between investing in new cargo ships, or cruise ships. It ultimately sold its cargo operations, becoming exclusively a cruise ship company in 1973.<sup id="cite_ref-hal_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hal-32">[32]</a></sup> Holland America continued to thrive well into the 1980s, consolidating its business with the acquisitions of Westours, <a href="/wiki/Windstar_Cruises" title="Windstar Cruises">Windstar Cruises</a> and <a href="/wiki/Home_Lines" title="Home Lines">Home Lines</a>. In 1989 however, it was itself the subject of an acquisition, when it was purchased in full by Carnival Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-hal_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hal-32">[32]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span id="P.26O_Cruises"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="P&amp;O_Cruises">P&amp;O Cruises</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carnival_Corporation_%26_plc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: P&amp;O Cruises" data-section="21" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/P%26O_Cruises" title="P&amp;O Cruises">P&amp;O Cruises</a></div>
<p>P&amp;O Cruises was founded in 1977 as a <a href="/wiki/Subsidiary" title="Subsidiary">subsidiary</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Freight_transport" title="Freight transport">shipping</a> company <a href="/wiki/P%26O_(company)" title="P&amp;O (company)">P&amp;O</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-founded1977_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-founded1977-33">[33]</a></sup> and traces its heritage to P&amp;O's first passenger operations in 1837.<sup id="cite_ref-firstcruises_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-firstcruises-34">[34]</a></sup> Along with <a href="/wiki/P%26O_Cruises_Australia" title="P&amp;O Cruises Australia">P&amp;O Cruises Australia</a>, a sister company also founded by P&amp;O and owned by Carnival Corporation &amp; plc, it has the oldest heritage of any cruise line in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-P&amp;Oaustralia_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P&amp;Oaustralia-35">[35]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-oldestheritage_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oldestheritage-36">[36]</a></sup> P&amp;O Cruises was <a href="/wiki/Divestment" title="Divestment">divested</a> from P&amp;O in 2000, becoming a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/P%26O_Princess_Cruises" title="P&amp;O Princess Cruises">P&amp;O Princess Cruises</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-P&amp;Oprincess_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P&amp;Oprincess-37">[37]</a></sup> before coming under the ownership of Carnival Corporation &amp; plc in 2003, following the merger between P&amp;O Princess Cruises and Carnival Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-P&amp;Odeal_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P&amp;Odeal-2">[2]</a></sup> P&amp;O Cruises is based in <a href="/wiki/Southampton" title="Southampton">Southampton</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, and operates a fleet of six ships, dedicated to serving the British market.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 2002 the Carnival Corporation pleaded guilty in United States District Court in Miami to criminal charges related to falsifying records of the oil-contaminated bilge water that six of its ships dumped into the sea from 1996 through 2001. The Carnival Corporation was ordered to pay $18 million in fines and perform community service, received five years' probation and must submit to a court-supervised worldwide environmental-compliance program for each of its cruise ships.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p><p>For dumping oiled waste into the seas and lying to cover it up, Princess Cruise Lines was fined $40 million in 2016. According to federal authorities, it was the "largest-ever criminal penalty" for intentional vessel pollution. Officials said that these practices began in 2005 and persisted until August 2013, when a newly hired engineer blew the whistle. As part of its plea agreement, ships of the parent company Carnival Cruise lines were subjected to a court supervised environmental compliance plan for five years.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p><p>For violation of the probation terms of 2016 Carnival and its Princess line were ordered to pay an additional $20 million penalty in 2019. The new violations included discharging plastic into waters in the Bahamas, falsifying records, and interfering with court supervision.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>

<p>Carnival has had various notable ships in the past including:
</p>
<ul><li><i><a href="/wiki/Pacific_Princess_(1971)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Princess (1971)">Pacific Princess</a></i> (Princess Cruises) – Became famous for appearing in the romantic comedy anthology TV series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Love_Boat" title="The Love Boat">The Love Boat</a></i> and was featured in nearly every episode. She sailed for <a href="/wiki/Princess_Cruises" title="Princess Cruises">Princess Cruises</a> from 1975–2002.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/RMS_Queen_Elizabeth_2" class="mw-redirect" title="RMS Queen Elizabeth 2"><i>Queen Elizabeth 2</i></a> (Cunard Line) – The flagship of Cunard Line when acquired by Carnival Corporation. She was sold to <a href="/wiki/Istithmar_World" title="Istithmar World">Istithmar World</a> in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/SS_Rotterdam" title="SS Rotterdam"><i>Rotterdam</i></a> (Holland America Line) – The flagship of Holland America Line for 40 years. She was sold to <a href="/wiki/Premier_Cruises" title="Premier Cruises">Premier Cruises</a> in 1997 and renamed <i>Rembrandt</i>. She currently operates under her original name as a floating hotel and museum in <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Costa_Concordia" title="Costa Concordia">Costa Concordia</a></i> (Costa Cruises) – Struck a rock and sank off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Isola_del_Giglio" title="Isola del Giglio">Isola del Giglio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> in 2012, in the <a href="/wiki/Costa_Concordia_disaster" title="Costa Concordia disaster"><i>Costa Concordia</i> disaster</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-costaconcordia_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-costaconcordia-45">[45]</a></sup> 32 passengers and crew members were killed, along with 1 salvage operator, while 64 other people were injured.<sup id="cite_ref-costaconcordia_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-costaconcordia-45">[45]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/MV_Adonia" class="mw-redirect" title="MV Adonia">Adonia</a></i> (Fathom and P&amp;O Cruises) – First cruise ship to sail to <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> from the United States mainland since the <a href="/wiki/United_States_embargo_against_Cuba" title="United States embargo against Cuba">United States embargo against Cuba</a> over 40 years ago.<sup id="cite_ref-adoniacuba_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adoniacuba-46">[46]</a></sup> She was sold to <a href="/wiki/Royal_Caribbean_Cruises_Ltd.#Azamara_Club_Cruises" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Caribbean Cruises Ltd.">Azamara Club Cruises</a> in 2018 and renamed <i>Azamara Pursuit</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-azamarapursuit_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-azamarapursuit-47">[47]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/MV_Piano_Land" title="MV Piano Land">Oriana</a></i> (P&amp;O Cruises) – The first cruise ship commissioned for the UK market.<sup id="cite_ref-oriana_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oriana-48">[48]</a></sup> She was sold to Astro Ocean in 2019 and renamed <i>Piano Land</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-pianoland_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pianoland-49">[49]</a></sup></li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(6)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carnival_Corporation_%26_plc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: References" data-section="29" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-6 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-6">
