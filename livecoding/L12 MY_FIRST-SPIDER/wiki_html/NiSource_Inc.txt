<p><b>NiSource Inc.</b> is one of the largest fully regulated utility companies in the United States, serving approximately 3.5 million natural gas customers and 500,000 electric customers across seven states through its local <b>Columbia Gas</b> and <b>NIPSCO</b> brands. The company, based in <a href="/wiki/Merrillville,_Indiana" title="Merrillville, Indiana">Merrillville, Indiana</a>, United States, has more than 8,000 employees.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Company_operations">Company operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=NiSource&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Company operations" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>NiSource's natural gas utilities provide domestically produced supplies of natural gas to residential, commercial and industrial customers via nearly 60,000 miles of pipeline and related facilities in seven states: Indiana, Kentucky, Maryland, Massachusetts, Ohio, Pennsylvania and Virginia.
</p><p>NiSource provides electric energy to nearly 500,000 customers, all located in northern Indiana. NiSource's electric operations include power generation, transmission and local distribution, as well as wholesale and electric transmission transactions. NiSource uses both traditional and renewable generation sources, including natural gas, hydroelectric, wind, and coal generated supplies, providing a total system operating net capability of more than 3,000 megawatts.
</p><p>NiSource has had a <a href="/wiki/Climate_change_policy_of_the_United_States" title="Climate change policy of the United States">climate change policy</a> in place since 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, NiSource was named to the Dow Jones Sustainability—North America Index for the second year in a row and for the ninth time since 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Internal_organization">Internal organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=NiSource&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Internal organization" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>NiSource operates seven local utilities in its service region:
</p>
<ul><li>Northern Indiana Public Service Co. (NIPSCO)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Columbia_Gas_of_Massachusetts" title="Columbia Gas of Massachusetts">Columbia Gas of Massachusetts</a> (previously Bay State Gas)</li>
<li>Columbia Gas of Ohio</li>
<li>Columbia Gas of Kentucky</li>
<li>Columbia Gas of Pennsylvania</li>
<li>Columbia Gas of Maryland</li>
<li>Columbia Gas of Virginia</li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_corporate_actions">Recent corporate actions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=NiSource&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Recent corporate actions" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>On July 1, 2015, NiSource separated <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Pipeline_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Columbia Pipeline Group">Columbia Pipeline Group</a> (NYSE: CPGX) into a stand-alone publicly traded company. Each NiSource shareholder at the time of the separation received one share of Columbia Pipeline Group for each share of NiSource. The separation of Columbia Pipeline Group included Columbia Gas Transmission, Columbia Gulf Transmission, Columbia Midstream Group, its ownership in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Columbia_Pipeline_Partners&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Columbia Pipeline Partners (page does not exist)">Columbia Pipeline Partners</a> (NYSE: CPPL), and other natural gas pipeline, storage and midstream holdings.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2019, the company announced that it would accelerate retirement of its five coal-fired units, and focus on "<a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy_in_the_United_States" title="Renewable energy in the United States">renewable energy</a> resources, such as <a href="/wiki/Solar_energy" title="Solar energy">solar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wind_power" title="Wind power">wind energy</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Grid_energy_storage" title="Grid energy storage">battery storage</a> technology," as part of an initiative titled  "Your Energy, Your Future", with a goal of "reducing <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">carbon emissions</a> by more than 90 percent by 2028."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=NiSource&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Criticism" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>In December 2011, the nonpartisan organization <a href="/wiki/Public_Campaign#Reports" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Campaign">Public Campaign</a> criticized NiSource for spending $1.83 million on <a href="/wiki/Lobbying" title="Lobbying">lobbying</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tax_avoidance" title="Tax avoidance">not paying any taxes</a> during 2008–2010, instead getting $227 million in tax rebates, despite making a profit of $1.4 billion, and increasing executive pay by 33% to $11.2 million in 2010 for its top five executives.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> One rule NiSource, among other companies, benefitted from was a bonus depreciation rule that lowered the federal tax expense.  NiSource stated, "This law, enacted by Congress, encouraged companies like NiSource to accelerate capital investments to spur economic recovery by permitting portions of these investments to be deducted at an accelerated rate. Only the timing of the deductions was changed, and not the amount that could be deducted.  This means our income tax expense will likely be higher in the future."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Massachusetts_gas_line_explosions_and_exit_from_Massachusetts">Massachusetts gas line explosions and exit from Massachusetts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=NiSource&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Massachusetts gas line explosions and exit from Massachusetts" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_gas_explosions" class="mw-redirect" title="Massachusetts gas explosions">Massachusetts gas explosions</a></div>
<p>On September 13, 2018, 80 homes in <a href="/wiki/Andover,_Massachusetts" title="Andover, Massachusetts">Andover, Massachusetts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lawrence,_Massachusetts" title="Lawrence, Massachusetts">Lawrence, Massachusetts</a>, and <a href="/wiki/North_Andover,_Massachusetts" title="North Andover, Massachusetts">North Andover, Massachusetts</a>, simultaneously exploded and caught fire due to issues with gas lines owned by <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Gas_of_Massachusetts" title="Columbia Gas of Massachusetts">Columbia Gas of Massachusetts</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> a subsidiary of NiSource. 
At least 25 people were injured and one was killed; residents from parts of three towns had to be evacuated for a few days and electricity service turned off until the area was inspected for gas. Gas service to 8,600 customers was disrupted, in some cases for months. Columbia Gas admitted that maintenance workers mishandled sensors that monitored gas line pressure, and pled guilty to violating the federal Pipeline Safety Act. The company paid a fine of $53 million, in addition to a $143 million settlement with residents and businesses, $83 million paid to municipalities, undisclosed amounts paid for injuries and one death, and direct spending on replacing damaged pipelines and appliances. NiSource agreed to sell its Massachusetts gas business to competitor <a href="/wiki/Eversource" class="mw-redirect" title="Eversource">Eversource</a>, at a loss compared to what it paid to acquire Columbia Gas.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
