<p><b>Berkshire Hathaway</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> <a href="/wiki/Conglomerate_(company)" title="Conglomerate (company)">conglomerate</a> <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Omaha" class="mw-redirect" title="Omaha">Omaha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nebraska" title="Nebraska">Nebraska</a>, United States. The company wholly owns <a href="/wiki/GEICO" title="GEICO">GEICO</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duracell" title="Duracell">Duracell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dairy_Queen" title="Dairy Queen">Dairy Queen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burlington_Northern_Santa_Fe,_LLC" title="Burlington Northern Santa Fe, LLC">BNSF</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lubrizol" title="Lubrizol">Lubrizol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fruit_of_the_Loom" title="Fruit of the Loom">Fruit of the Loom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Helzberg_Diamonds" title="Helzberg Diamonds">Helzberg Diamonds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Long_%26_Foster" title="Long &amp; Foster">Long &amp; Foster</a>, FlightSafety International, <a href="/wiki/Pampered_Chef" title="Pampered Chef">Pampered Chef</a>, <a href="/wiki/Forest_River_(company)" title="Forest River (company)">Forest River</a> and <a href="/wiki/NetJets" title="NetJets">NetJets</a>, and also owns 38.6% of <a href="/wiki/Pilot_Flying_J" title="Pilot Flying J">Pilot Flying J</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-Pilot_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pilot-3">[3]</a></sup> 26.7% of the <a href="/wiki/Kraft_Heinz" title="Kraft Heinz">Kraft Heinz Company</a>, and significant minority holdings in <a href="/wiki/American_Express" title="American Express">American Express</a> (17.6%), <a href="/wiki/Wells_Fargo" title="Wells Fargo">Wells Fargo</a> (9.9%), <a href="/wiki/The_Coca-Cola_Company" title="The Coca-Cola Company">The Coca-Cola Company</a> (9.32%), <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_America" title="Bank of America">Bank of America</a> (6.8%), and <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple</a> (5.22%).<sup id="cite_ref-reuters_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-4">[4]</a></sup> Since 2016, the company has acquired large holdings in the major US airline carriers, and is currently the largest shareholder in <a href="/wiki/United_Airlines" title="United Airlines">United Airlines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Delta_Air_Lines" title="Delta Air Lines">Delta Air Lines</a>, and a top three shareholder in <a href="/wiki/Southwest_Airlines" title="Southwest Airlines">Southwest Airlines</a> and <a href="/wiki/American_Airlines" title="American Airlines">American Airlines</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Berkshire Hathaway has averaged an annual growth in book value of 19.0% to its shareholders since 1965 (compared to 9.7% from the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a> with dividends included for the same period), while employing large amounts of capital, and </p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Forbes_Global_2000" title="Forbes Global 2000">Forbes Global 2000</a> list and formula, Berkshire Hathaway is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_public_corporations_by_market_capitalization" title="List of public corporations by market capitalization">third largest public company in the world</a>, the tenth largest conglomerate by revenue and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_largest_financial_services_companies_by_revenue" title="List of largest financial services companies by revenue">largest financial services company by revenue</a> in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>As of March 2020, Berkshire's <a href="/wiki/Class_B_share" title="Class B share">Class B stock</a> is the fifth-largest component of the S&amp;P 500 Index (which is based on <a href="/wiki/Free-float" class="mw-redirect" title="Free-float">free-float</a> market capitalization) and the company is famous for having the most expensive share price in history with <a href="/wiki/Class_A_share" title="Class A share">Class A shares</a> costing around $300,000 each. This is because there has never been a <a href="/wiki/Stock_split" title="Stock split">stock split</a> in its Class A shares<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and Buffett stated in a 1984 letter to shareholders that he does not intend to split the stock.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Oliver_Chace.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Oliver_Chace.jpg/220px-Oliver_Chace.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="230" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="325" data-file-height="340"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 230px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Oliver_Chace.jpg/220px-Oliver_Chace.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="230" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Oliver_Chace.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Oliver Chace (1769–1852) founder of the Valley Falls Company in 1839</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Berkshire_Cotton.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Berkshire_Cotton.jpg/220px-Berkshire_Cotton.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="106" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1500" data-file-height="720"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 106px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Berkshire_Cotton.jpg/220px-Berkshire_Cotton.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="106" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Berkshire_Cotton.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Berkshire Cotton Mills, Adams, Mass.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hathaway_Mills.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Hathaway_Mills.jpg/220px-Hathaway_Mills.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="178" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1500" data-file-height="1214"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 178px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Hathaway_Mills.jpg/220px-Hathaway_Mills.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="178" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hathaway_Mills.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hathaway Mills, New Bedford, Mass.</div></div></div>
<p>Berkshire Hathaway traces its roots to a <a href="/wiki/Textile_manufacturing" title="Textile manufacturing">textile manufacturing</a> company established by <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Chace" title="Oliver Chace">Oliver Chace</a> in 1839 as the <a href="/wiki/Valley_Falls_Company" title="Valley Falls Company">Valley Falls Company</a> in <a href="/wiki/Valley_Falls,_Rhode_Island" title="Valley Falls, Rhode Island">Valley Falls, Rhode Island</a>. Chace had previously worked for <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Slater" title="Samuel Slater">Samuel Slater</a>, the founder of the first successful textile mill in America. Chace founded his first textile mill in 1806. In 1929, the Valley Falls Company merged with the Berkshire Cotton Manufacturing Company established in 1889, in <a href="/wiki/Adams,_Massachusetts" title="Adams, Massachusetts">Adams, Massachusetts</a>. The combined company was known as <a href="/wiki/Berkshire_Fine_Spinning_Associates" title="Berkshire Fine Spinning Associates">Berkshire Fine Spinning Associates</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 1955, <a href="/wiki/Berkshire_Fine_Spinning_Associates" title="Berkshire Fine Spinning Associates">Berkshire Fine Spinning Associates</a> merged with the <a href="/wiki/Hathaway_Mills" title="Hathaway Mills">Hathaway Manufacturing Company</a> which had been founded in 1888 in <a href="/wiki/New_Bedford,_Massachusetts" title="New Bedford, Massachusetts">New Bedford, Massachusetts</a> by <a href="/wiki/Horatio_Hathaway" title="Horatio Hathaway">Horatio Hathaway</a> with profits from <a href="/wiki/Whaling" title="Whaling">whaling</a> and the <a href="/wiki/China_Trade" class="mw-redirect" title="China Trade">China Trade</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Hathaway had been successful in its first decades, but it suffered during a general decline in the <a href="/wiki/Textile_industry" title="Textile industry">textile industry</a> after World War I. At this time, Hathaway was run by <a href="/wiki/Seabury_Stanton" title="Seabury Stanton">Seabury Stanton</a>, whose investment efforts were rewarded with renewed profitability after the Great Depression. After the merger, Berkshire Hathaway had 15 plants employing over 12,000 workers with over $120 million in revenue, and was headquartered in <a href="/wiki/New_Bedford,_Massachusetts" title="New Bedford, Massachusetts">New Bedford</a>. However, seven of those locations were closed by the end of the decade, accompanied by large layoffs.
</p><p>In 1962, Warren Buffett began buying stock in Berkshire Hathaway after noticing a pattern in the price direction of its stock whenever the company closed a mill. Eventually, Buffett acknowledged that the textile business was waning and the company's financial situation was not going to improve.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Vagueness" title="Wikipedia:Vagueness"><span title="This information is too vague. (January 2020)">vague</span></a></i>]</sup> In 1964, Stanton made an oral tender offer of $11​<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>2</sub></span> per share for the company to buy back Buffett's shares. Buffett agreed to the deal. A few weeks later, Warren Buffett received the tender offer in writing, but the tender offer was for only $11​<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>3</sup>⁄<sub>8</sub></span>. Buffett later admitted that this lower, undercutting offer made him angry.<sup id="cite_ref-cnbc.com_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnbc.com-13">[13]</a></sup> Instead of selling at the slightly lower price, Buffett decided to buy more of the stock to take control of the company and fire Stanton (which he did). However, this put Buffett in a situation where he was now the majority owner of a textile business that was failing.
</p><p>Buffett initially maintained Berkshire's core business of textiles, but by 1967, he was expanding into the insurance industry and other investments. Berkshire first ventured into the insurance business with the purchase of <a href="/wiki/National_Indemnity_Company" title="National Indemnity Company">National Indemnity Company</a>. In the late 1970s, Berkshire acquired an <a href="/wiki/Equity_(finance)" title="Equity (finance)">equity stake</a> in the Government Employees Insurance Company (<a href="/wiki/GEICO" title="GEICO">GEICO</a>), which forms the core of its insurance operations today (and is a major source of capital for Berkshire Hathaway's other investments). In 1985, the last textile operations (Hathaway's historic core) were shut down.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In 2010, Buffett claimed that purchasing Berkshire Hathaway was the biggest investment mistake he had ever made, and claimed that it had denied him compounded investment returns of about $200 billion over the subsequent 45 years.<sup id="cite_ref-cnbc.com_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnbc.com-13">[13]</a></sup> Buffett claimed that had he invested that money directly in insurance businesses instead of buying out Berkshire Hathaway (due to what he perceived as a slight by an individual), those investments would have paid off several hundredfold.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_affairs">Corporate affairs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Corporate affairs" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Berkshire's <a href="/wiki/Class_A_share" title="Class A share">class A shares</a> sold for $339,188 as of January 8, 2020 making them the highest-priced shares on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>, in part because they have never had a <a href="/wiki/Stock_split" title="Stock split">stock split</a> and have only paid a <a href="/wiki/Dividend" title="Dividend">dividend</a> once since Warren Buffett took over, retaining corporate earnings on its balance sheet in a manner that is impermissible for <a href="/wiki/Mutual_fund" title="Mutual fund">mutual funds</a>. Shares closed over $100,000 for the first time on October 23, 2006. Despite its size, Berkshire had for many years not been included in broad stock market indices such as the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a> due to the lack of liquidity in its shares; however, following a 50-to-1 split of Berkshire's class B shares in January 2010, and Berkshire's announcement that it would acquire the <a href="/wiki/Burlington_Northern_Santa_Fe_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Burlington Northern Santa Fe Corporation">Burlington Northern Santa Fe Corporation</a>, parent of <a href="/wiki/BNSF_Railway" title="BNSF Railway">BNSF Railway</a>, Berkshire replaced BNSF in the S&amp;P 500 on February 16, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>Buffett's annual letters are published. <i><a href="/wiki/Barron%27s_(newspaper)" title="Barron's (newspaper)">Barron's</a></i> said Berkshire was the most respected company in the world in 2007, based on a survey of American money managers.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In 2008, Berkshire invested in <a href="/wiki/Preferred_stock" title="Preferred stock">preferred stock</a> of <a href="/wiki/Goldman_Sachs" title="Goldman Sachs">Goldman Sachs</a> as part of a recapitalization of the investment bank.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Buffett defended <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Blankfein" title="Lloyd Blankfein">Lloyd Blankfein</a>'s decisions as CEO of Goldman Sachs.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>As of July 13, 2016<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, Buffett owned 31.7% aggregate voting power of Berkshire's shares outstanding and 18.0% of the economic value of those shares.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Berkshire's vice-chairman, <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Munger" title="Charlie Munger">Charlie Munger</a>, also holds a stake big enough to make him a billionaire, and early investments in Berkshire by <a href="/wiki/David_Gottesman" title="David Gottesman">David Gottesman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Franklin_Otis_Booth,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Franklin Otis Booth, Jr.">Franklin Otis Booth, Jr.</a> resulted in their becoming billionaires as well. The <a href="/wiki/Bill_and_Melinda_Gates_Foundation" class="mw-redirect" title="Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation">Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation</a> is a large shareholder of Berkshire, owning 4.0% of class B shares. <sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>Berkshire Hathaway has never <a href="/wiki/Stock_split" title="Stock split">split</a> its Class A shares because of management's desire to attract long-term investors as opposed to short-term <a href="/wiki/Speculation" title="Speculation">speculators</a>. However, Berkshire Hathaway created a Class B stock, with a per-share value originally kept (by specific management rules) close to ​<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>30</sub></span> of that of the original shares (now Class A) and ​<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>200</sub></span> of the per-share voting rights, and after the January 2010 split, at ​<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>1,500</sub></span> the price and ​<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>10,000</sub></span> the voting rights of the Class-A shares. Holders of class A stock are allowed to convert their stock to Class B, though not vice versa. Buffett was reluctant to create the class B shares but did so to thwart the creation of <a href="/wiki/Unit_investment_trust" title="Unit investment trust">unit trusts</a> that would have marketed themselves as Berkshire look-alikes. As Buffett said in his 1995 shareholder letter: "The unit trusts that have recently surfaced fly in the face of these goals. They would be sold by brokers working for big commissions, would impose other burdensome costs on their shareholders, and would be marketed <i>en masse</i> to unsophisticated buyers, apt to be seduced by our past record and beguiled by the publicity Berkshire and I have received in recent years. The sure outcome: a multitude of investors destined to be disappointed."
</p><p>The salary for Buffett is $100,000 per year with no stock options, which is among the lowest salaries<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> for CEOs of large companies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>Berkshire's annual shareholders' meetings take place at the <a href="/wiki/CHI_Health_Center_Omaha" title="CHI Health Center Omaha">CHI Health Center</a> in Omaha, Nebraska. Attendance has grown over the years with recent numbers<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (February 2020)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> totaling over 40,000 people a year.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The 2007 meeting had an attendance of approximately 27,000. The meetings, nicknamed "<a href="/wiki/Woodstock" title="Woodstock">Woodstock</a> for Capitalists", are considered Omaha's largest annual event along with the baseball <a href="/wiki/College_World_Series" title="College World Series">College World Series</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Known for their humor and light-heartedness, the meetings typically start with a movie made for Berkshire shareholders. The 2004 movie featured <a href="/wiki/Arnold_Schwarzenegger" title="Arnold Schwarzenegger">Arnold Schwarzenegger</a> in the role of "The Warrenator" who travels through time to stop Buffett and Munger's attempt to save the world from a "mega" corporation formed by <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a>-<a href="/wiki/Starbucks" title="Starbucks">Starbucks</a>-<a href="/wiki/Wal-Mart" class="mw-redirect" title="Wal-Mart">Wal-Mart</a>. Schwarzenegger is later shown arguing in a gym with Buffett regarding <a href="/wiki/California_Proposition_13_(1978)" class="mw-redirect" title="California Proposition 13 (1978)">Proposition 13</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The 2006 movie depicted actresses <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Lee_Curtis" title="Jamie Lee Curtis">Jamie Lee Curtis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nicollette_Sheridan" title="Nicollette Sheridan">Nicollette Sheridan</a> lusting after Munger.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The meeting, scheduled to last 6–8 hours, is an opportunity for investors to ask Buffett and Munger questions.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Governance" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The current members of the board of directors of Berkshire Hathaway are <a href="/wiki/Warren_Buffett" title="Warren Buffett">Warren Buffett</a> (Chairman), <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Munger" title="Charlie Munger">Charlie Munger</a> (Vice Chairman), <a href="/wiki/Walter_Scott,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Walter Scott, Jr.">Walter Scott, Jr.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Murphy_(broadcasting)" title="Thomas Murphy (broadcasting)">Thomas S. Murphy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Howard_Graham_Buffett" title="Howard Graham Buffett">Howard Graham Buffett</a> (Warren's son), <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Olson" title="Ronald Olson">Ronald Olson</a>, Charlotte Guyman, <a href="/wiki/David_Gottesman" title="David Gottesman">David Gottesman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Gates" title="Bill Gates">Bill Gates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Burke_(businessman)" title="Steve Burke (businessman)">Steve Burke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Susan_Decker" title="Susan Decker">Susan Decker</a>, Meryl Witmer, <a href="/wiki/Ajit_Jain" title="Ajit Jain">Ajit Jain</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Greg_Abel" title="Greg Abel">Greg Abel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>On March 13 2020, Gates announced that he is leaving the board of directors of both <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> and Berkshire Hathaway in order to focus more on his philanthropic efforts.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Succession_plans">Succession plans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Succession plans" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In May 2010, 3 months away from his 80th birthday, Buffett said he would be succeeded at Berkshire Hathaway by a team consisting of a CEO and three or four investment managers, each of the latter would be responsible for a "significant portion of Berkshire's investment portfolio".<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Five months later, Berkshire announced that <a href="/wiki/Todd_Combs" title="Todd Combs">Todd Combs</a>, manager of the <a href="/wiki/Hedge_fund" title="Hedge fund">hedge fund</a> Castle Point Capital, would join them as an <a href="/wiki/Investment_management" title="Investment management">investment manager</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> On September 12, 2011, Berkshire Hathaway announced that 50-year-old <a href="/wiki/Ted_Weschler" title="Ted Weschler">Ted Weschler</a>, founder of Peninsula Capital Advisors, will join Berkshire in early 2012 as a second investment manager.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p><p>In Berkshire Hathaway's annual shareholder letter dated February 25, 2012, Buffett said that his successor as CEO had been chosen internally but not named publicly. While the intent of this message was to bolster confidence in the leadership of a "Buffett-less Berkshire", critics have noted that this strategy of choosing a successor without a concrete exit strategy for the sitting CEO often leaves an organization with fewer long term options, while doing little to calm shareholder fear.
</p><p>In June 2014, the firm's cash and cash equivalents rose past $50 billion, the first time it finished a quarter above that level since Buffett became chairman and chief executive officer.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> At the end of 2017, the firm's cash and cash equivalent holdings rose to $116 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p><p>On January 10, 2018, Berkshire Hathaway appointed <a href="/wiki/Ajit_Jain" title="Ajit Jain">Ajit Jain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greg_Abel" title="Greg Abel">Greg Abel</a> to Vice-Chairman roles. Abel is going to be vice chairman for non-insurance business operations, and Jain is going to become vice chairman of insurance-operations.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> While Buffett has not further elaborated on his succession plans, he praised his two top executives in an annual shareholder letter, fuelling speculation that Jain and Abel are the logical successors.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Finance">Finance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Finance" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Insurance_group">Insurance group</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Insurance group" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Insurance and <a href="/wiki/Reinsurance" title="Reinsurance">reinsurance</a> business activities are conducted through approximately 70 domestic and foreign-based insurance companies. Berkshire's insurance businesses provide insurance and reinsurance of property and casualty risks primarily in the United States. In addition, as a result of the <a href="/wiki/General_Re" class="mw-redirect" title="General Re">General Re</a> acquisition in December 1998, Berkshire's insurance businesses also includes life, accident, and health reinsurers, as well as internationally based property and casualty reinsurers. Berkshire's insurance companies maintain capital strength at exceptionally high levels. This strength differentiates Berkshire's insurance companies from their competitors. Collectively, the aggregate statutory surplus of Berkshire's U.S.-based insurers was approximately $48 billion as of December 31, 2004<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>. All of Berkshire's major insurance subsidiaries are rated <a href="/wiki/Corporate_credit_rating" class="mw-redirect" title="Corporate credit rating">AAA</a> by <a href="/wiki/Standard_%26_Poor%27s" title="Standard &amp; Poor's">Standard &amp; Poor's Corporation</a>, the highest Financial Strength Rating assigned by Standard &amp; Poor's, and are rated A++ (superior) by <a href="/wiki/A._M._Best" class="mw-redirect" title="A. M. Best">A. M. Best</a> with respect to their financial condition and operating performance.
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/GEICO" title="GEICO">GEICO</a> – Berkshire acquired GEICO in January 1996. GEICO is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Chevy_Chase,_Maryland" title="Chevy Chase, Maryland">Chevy Chase, Maryland</a>, and its principal insurance subsidiaries include; Government Employees Insurance Company, GEICO General Insurance Company, GEICO Indemnity Company, and GEICO Casualty Company. Over the past five years, these companies have offered primarily private passenger automobile insurance to individuals in all 50 states and the District of Columbia. GEICO markets its policies primarily through direct response methods in which applications for insurance are submitted directly to the companies via the Internet or by telephone.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gen_Re" title="Gen Re">Gen Re</a> – Berkshire acquired General Re in December 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> General Re held a 91% ownership interest in Cologne Re as of December 31, 2004<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>. General Re subsidiaries currently conduct global reinsurance business in approximately 72 cities and provide reinsurance coverage worldwide. General Re operates the following reinsurance businesses: North American property/casualty, international property/casualty, which principally consists of Cologne Re and the Faraday operations, and life/health reinsurance. General Re's reinsurance operations are primarily based in <a href="/wiki/Stamford,_Connecticut" title="Stamford, Connecticut">Stamford, Connecticut</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne, Germany</a>. General Re is one of the largest reinsurers in the world based on net premiums written and capital.</li>
<li>NRG (Nederlandse Reassurantie Groep)<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> – Berkshire acquired NRG, a Dutch life reinsurance company, from <a href="/wiki/ING_Group" title="ING Group">ING Group</a> in December 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-marketwatch_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-marketwatch-46">[46]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Berkshire_Hathaway_Assurance" title="Berkshire Hathaway Assurance">Berkshire Hathaway Assurance</a> – Berkshire created a government <a href="/wiki/Bond_insurance" title="Bond insurance">bond insurance</a> company to insure municipal and <a href="/wiki/Government_bond" title="Government bond">state bonds</a>. These type bonds are issued by local governments to finance public works projects such as schools, hospitals, roads, and sewer systems.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Few companies are capable of competing in this area.<sup id="cite_ref-marketwatch_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-marketwatch-46">[46]</a></sup></li></ul><p>On June 8, 2017, it was announced that Berkshire Hathaway had settled with California's insurance regulator, allowing its Applied Underwriters unit to sell a revised version of its "controversial" compensation insurance policies for workers in the state.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p><p>In 2003, <a href="/wiki/Pepsi" title="Pepsi">Pepsi</a> paid Berkshire between $1 million and $10 million to insure against a <a href="/wiki/Pepsi_Billion_Dollar_Sweepstakes" class="mw-redirect" title="Pepsi Billion Dollar Sweepstakes">contest Pepsi held</a> which had a potential $1 billion prize. The prize had a very small chance of being won and it was not won by anyone.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Utilities_and_energy_group">Utilities and energy group</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Utilities and energy group" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Berkshire currently holds 89.8% of <a href="/wiki/Berkshire_Hathaway_Energy" title="Berkshire Hathaway Energy">Berkshire Hathaway Energy</a>. At the time of purchase, Berkshire's <a href="/wiki/Voting_interest" title="Voting interest">voting interest</a> was limited to 10% of the company's shares, but this restriction ended when the <a href="/wiki/Public_Utility_Holding_Company_Act_of_1935" title="Public Utility Holding Company Act of 1935">Public Utility Holding Company Act of 1935</a> was repealed in 2005. A major subsidiary of Berkshire Hathaway Energy is <a href="/wiki/Northern_Powergrid" title="Northern Powergrid">Northern Powergrid</a>, which operates in the UK.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>Until a name change on April 30, 2014, Berkshire Hathaway Energy was known as MidAmerican Energy Holdings Co.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Manufacturing.2C_service_and_retailing"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Manufacturing,_service_and_retailing">Manufacturing, service and retailing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Manufacturing, service and retailing" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Recreational_Vehicles">Recreational Vehicles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Recreational Vehicles" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>On June 21, 2005, Berkshire Hathaway agreed to purchase Forest River INC from Pete Liegl. Forest River is the largest seller of recreational vehicles (RV's),  in the world.
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Clothing">Clothing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Clothing" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>Berkshire's clothing businesses include manufacturers and distributors of a variety of clothing and footwear. Businesses engaged in the manufacture and distribution of clothing include <a href="/wiki/Fruit_of_the_Loom" title="Fruit of the Loom">Union Underwear Corp. – Fruit of the Loom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Garanimals" title="Garanimals">Garan</a>, Fechheimer Brothers and <a href="/wiki/Russell_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Russell Corporation">Russell Corporation</a>. Berkshire's footwear businesses include <a href="/wiki/H.H._Brown_Shoe_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="H.H. Brown Shoe Group">H.H. Brown Shoe Group</a>, <a href="/wiki/Acme_Boots" title="Acme Boots">Acme Boots</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brooks_Sports" title="Brooks Sports">Brooks Sports</a> and <a href="/wiki/Justin_Brands" class="mw-redirect" title="Justin Brands">Justin Brands</a>. Justin Brands is made up of <a href="/wiki/Chippewa_Boots" title="Chippewa Boots">Chippewa Boots</a>, <a href="/wiki/Justin_Boots" title="Justin Boots">Justin Boots</a>, Justin Original Workboots, <a href="/wiki/Nocona_Boots" title="Nocona Boots">Nocona Boots</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tony_Lama_Boots" title="Tony Lama Boots">Tony Lama Boots</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> Berkshire acquired <a href="/wiki/Fruit_of_the_Loom" title="Fruit of the Loom">Fruit of the Loom</a> on April 29, 2002 for $835 million in cash. Fruit of the Loom, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Bowling_Green,_Kentucky" title="Bowling Green, Kentucky">Bowling Green, Kentucky</a>, is a vertically integrated manufacturer of basic clothing. Berkshire acquired <a href="/wiki/Russell_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Russell Corporation">Russell Corporation</a> on August 2, 2006 for $600 million or $18.00 per share.
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Building_products">Building products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Building products" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In August 2000, Berkshire Hathaway entered the building products business with the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Acme_Brick" title="Acme Brick">Acme Building Brands</a>. Acme, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Worth,_Texas" title="Fort Worth, Texas">Fort Worth, Texas</a>, manufactures and distributes clay bricks (Acme Brick), concrete block (Featherlite) and cut limestone (Texas Quarries). It expanded its building products business in December 2000, when it acquired <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Moore_%26_Co." title="Benjamin Moore &amp; Co.">Benjamin Moore &amp; Co.</a> of <a href="/wiki/Montvale,_New_Jersey" title="Montvale, New Jersey">Montvale, New Jersey</a>. Moore formulates, manufactures and sells <a href="/wiki/Architectural_coatings" title="Architectural coatings">architectural coatings</a> that are available primarily in the United States and Canada.
</p><p>In 2001, Berkshire acquired three additional building products companies. In February, it purchased <a href="/wiki/Johns_Manville" title="Johns Manville">Johns Manville</a> which was established in 1858 and manufactures fiberglass wool insulation products for homes and commercial buildings, as well as pipe, duct and equipment insulation products. In July, Berkshire acquired a 90% equity interest in <a href="/w/index.php?title=MiTek&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="MiTek (page does not exist)">MiTek</a> Inc., which makes engineered connector products, engineering software and services, and manufacturing machinery for the truss fabrication segment of the building components industry and is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Chesterfield,_Missouri" title="Chesterfield, Missouri">Chesterfield, Missouri</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> Finally in 2001, Berkshire acquired 87 percent of <a href="/wiki/Dalton,_Georgia" title="Dalton, Georgia">Dalton, Georgia</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Shaw_Industries" title="Shaw Industries">Shaw Industries</a>, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> Shaw is the world's largest carpet manufacturer based on both revenue and volume of production and designs and manufactures over 3,000 styles of tufted and woven carpet and laminate flooring for residential and commercial use under approximately 30 brand and trade names and under certain private labels. In 2002, Berkshire acquired the remaining 12.7 percent of Shaw.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p><p>On August 7, 2003, Berkshire acquired <a href="/wiki/Clayton_Homes" title="Clayton Homes">Clayton Homes</a>, Inc. Clayton, headquartered near <a href="/wiki/Knoxville,_Tennessee" title="Knoxville, Tennessee">Knoxville, Tennessee</a>, is a vertically integrated manufactured housing company.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> At year-end 2004, Clayton operated 32 manufacturing plants in 12 states. Clayton's homes are marketed in 48 states through a network of 1,540 retailers, 391 of which are company-owned sales centers. <h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Flight_services">Flight services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Flight services" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<div class="floatleft"><a href="/wiki/File:FlightSafety-Logo-Color.svg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="FlightSafety-Logo-Color.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/FlightSafety-Logo-Color.svg/150px-FlightSafety-Logo-Color.svg.png" decoding="async" width="150" height="32" data-file-width="1038" data-file-height="219"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 150px;height: 32px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/FlightSafety-Logo-Color.svg/150px-FlightSafety-Logo-Color.svg.png" data-alt="FlightSafety-Logo-Color.svg" data-width="150" data-height="32" data-srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/FlightSafety-Logo-Color.svg/225px-FlightSafety-Logo-Color.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/FlightSafety-Logo-Color.svg/300px-FlightSafety-Logo-Color.svg.png 2x"> </span></a></div>
<p>In 1996, Berkshire acquired FlightSafety International Inc. (or FSI), founded in 1951 by <a href="/wiki/Albert_Lee_Ueltschi" title="Albert Lee Ueltschi">Albert Lee Ueltschi</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> FSI's corporate headquarters is located at <a href="/wiki/LaGuardia_Airport" title="LaGuardia Airport">LaGuardia Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Flushing,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Flushing, New York">Flushing, New York</a>. It supplies high technology <a href="/wiki/Pilot_training" class="mw-redirect" title="Pilot training">pilot training</a> to aircraft operators in the fields of military, governmental, corporate, and <a href="/wiki/Regional_airline" title="Regional airline">regional</a> or <a href="/wiki/Mainline_(aeronautics)" title="Mainline (aeronautics)">mainline</a> flying. FlightSafety is the world's leading provider of professional aviation training services. According to its website, the company has 1,800 instructors and offers more than 4,000 individual courses for 135 aircraft types, using more than 320 <a href="/wiki/Flight_simulators" class="mw-redirect" title="Flight simulators">flight simulators</a> to serve customers from 167 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p><p>Berkshire acquired <a href="/wiki/NetJets" title="NetJets">NetJets</a> Inc. in 1998.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> NetJets is the world's leading provider of <a href="/wiki/Fractional_ownership" title="Fractional ownership">fractional ownership</a> programs for <a href="/wiki/General_aviation" title="General aviation">general aviation</a> aircraft. In 1986, NetJets created the fractional ownership of aircraft concept and introduced its NetJets program in the United States with one aircraft type. In 2019, the NetJets program operated more than 10 aircraft types with a fleet size of greater than 750.
</p>

<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_non-insurance">Other non-insurance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Other non-insurance" data-section="17" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In 2002, Berkshire acquired Albecca Inc. Albecca is headquartered in Norcross, Georgia, and primarily does business under the Larson-Juhl name. Albecca designs, manufactures and distributes custom framing products, including wood and metal molding, matboard, foamboard, glass, equipment and other framing supplies. Berkshire acquired CTB International Corp. in 2002. CTB, headquartered in Milford, Indiana, is a designer, manufacturer and marketer of systems used in the grain industry and in the production of poultry, hogs, and eggs. Products are produced in the United States and Europe and are sold primarily through a global network of independent dealers and distributors, with peak sales occurring in the second and third quarters.
</p><p>Berkshire acquired <a href="/wiki/McLane_Company" title="McLane Company">McLane Company</a>, Inc. in May 2003 from <a href="/wiki/Walmart" title="Walmart">Walmart</a>, which brought on other subsidiaries such as Professional Datasolutions, Inc. and Salado Sales, among others. McLane provides wholesale distribution and logistics services in all 50 states and internationally in Brazil to customers that include discount retailers, convenience stores, quick-service restaurants, drug stores and movie theatre complexes. <a href="/wiki/Scott_Fetzer" class="mw-redirect" title="Scott Fetzer">Scott Fetzer</a> Companies–The Scott Fetzer Companies are a diversified group of 21 businesses that manufacture and distribute a wide variety of products for residential, industrial and institutional use. The three most significant of these businesses are <a href="/wiki/Kirby_Company" title="Kirby Company">Kirby home cleaning systems</a>, Wayne Water Systems and <a href="/wiki/Campbell_Hausfeld" title="Campbell Hausfeld">Campbell Hausfeld</a> products. Scott Fetzer also manufactures <a href="/wiki/Ginsu" title="Ginsu">Ginsu</a> knives.

Headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Worth,_Texas" title="Fort Worth, Texas">Fort Worth, Texas</a>, TTI, Inc. is the largest distributor specialist of passive, interconnect and electromechanical components. TTI's extensive product line includes; resistors, capacitors, connectors, potentiometers, trimmers, magnetic and circuit protection components, wire and cable, identification products, application tools and electromechanical devices. On December 25, 2007, Berkshire Hathaway acquired <a href="/wiki/Marmon_Group" title="Marmon Group">Marmon Group</a>. <h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Finance_and_financial_products">Finance and financial products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Finance and financial products" data-section="18" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Investments">Investments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Investments" data-section="19" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>As well as owning companies outright, Berkshire maintains a concentrated portfolio of equities and investments which has historically been managed by Warren Buffett. Since 2010, <a href="/wiki/Todd_Combs" title="Todd Combs">Todd Combs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ted_Weschler" title="Ted Weschler">Ted Weschler</a> also work alongside Buffett in managing investments. Buffett has spoken very highly of both in public interviews and in the 2015 letter to shareholders he described hiring them both as "one of my best moves".<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> In the 2016 letter to shareholders, Warren revealed that each of them independently manages greater than $10 billion on behalf of Berkshire.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup></p><p>As of March 2017<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, 65% of Berkshire's equity securities were concentrated in five companies: American Express Company ($12.0 billion), Apple Inc. ($19.2 billion), The Coca-Cola Company ($17.0 billion), <a href="/wiki/International_Business_Machines_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="International Business Machines Corporation">International Business Machines Corporation</a> ("IBM") ($11.2 billion), and Wells Fargo &amp; Company ($27.8 billion).<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> After a selloff of IBM stock in February 2018,<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup> on May 4, 2018, Buffett announced that Berkshire had completely sold its stake in IBM, and purchased more of Apple.<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup></p><p>At the peak of the financial crisis in September 2008, Berkshire invested $5 billion in preferred stock in <a href="/wiki/Goldman_Sachs" title="Goldman Sachs">Goldman Sachs</a> to provide it with a source of funding when capital markets had become constrained. The preferred stock yielded an annual interest rate of 10% earning Berkshire $500 million in interest income per year. Berkshire also received warrants to purchase 43.5 million shares with a strike price of $115 per share, which were exercisable at any time for a five-year term.<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup> Goldman maintained the right to purchase back the preferred stock and in March 2011 exercised this right paying $5.5 billion to Berkshire (the preferred stock could only be purchased back at a 10% premium). The warrants have been exercised and Berkshire holds 3% of the share capital of Goldman Sachs. Profit on the preferred stock was estimated at $1.8 billion<sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup> and exercising the warrants has yielded a profit of more than $2 billion, although Berkshire's continued ownership of shares in Goldman Sachs means the entire profit cannot be quantified.
</p><p>On August 26, 2011, Berkshire Hathaway purchased $5 billion of preferred shares in <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_America" title="Bank of America">Bank of America</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup> The investment has an annual interest cost of 6% earning Berkshire $300 million in annual interest. Alongside the preferred stock investment, Berkshire obtained warrants allowing Berkshire to buy 700 million common shares at $7.14 per share any time before September 2, 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup> Based on the share price in June 2017, this position has yielded a profit of more than $10 billion excluding the annual interest earned from the preferred stock.
</p><p>In 2006 Berkshire Hathaway Inc. acquired <a href="/wiki/Russell_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Russell Corporation">Russell Corporation</a> for $600 million, in fact getting most shares and brands in many sports leagues – including <a href="/wiki/Spalding_(sports_equipment)" class="mw-redirect" title="Spalding (sports equipment)">Spalding NBA official basketballs</a>, <a href="/wiki/BIKE_Athletic_Company" title="BIKE Athletic Company">BIKE Athletic Company</a> protections, AAI (American Athletic) Gymnastics' tables, bars, rings, horses or Dudley softball balls and accessories.
</p><p>In 2008, Berkshire purchased preferred stock in <a href="/wiki/Wrigley_Company" title="Wrigley Company">Wrigley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goldman_Sachs" title="Goldman Sachs">Goldman Sachs</a>, and <a href="/wiki/GE" class="mw-redirect" title="GE">GE</a> totaling $14.5 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup></p><p>On November 3, 2009, Berkshire Hathaway announced that using stock and cash totaling $26 billion, it would acquire the remaining 77.4 percent of the <a href="/wiki/Burlington_Northern_Santa_Fe_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Burlington Northern Santa Fe Corporation">Burlington Northern Santa Fe Corporation</a>, parent of <a href="/wiki/BNSF_Railway" title="BNSF Railway">BNSF Railway</a>, that it did not already own.<sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup> This was the largest acquisition to date in Berkshire's history.<sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup></p><p>On March 14, 2011, Berkshire Hathaway announced that it would acquire the <a href="/wiki/Lubrizol" title="Lubrizol">Lubrizol Corporation</a> for $9 billion in cash, a deal that was described as one of the largest deals ever for Berkshire Hathaway.<sup id="cite_ref-93" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-93">[93]</a></sup></p><p>On March 25, 2011, Berkshire Hathaway made its first foray<sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup> into the Indian insurance sector with its non-direct subsidiary BerkshireInsurance.com.<sup id="cite_ref-95" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-96" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-96">[96]</a></sup></p><p>On February 14, 2013, Berkshire Hathaway Inc and <a href="/wiki/3G_Capital" title="3G Capital">3G Capital</a> announced plans to purchase <a href="/wiki/H._J._Heinz_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="H. J. Heinz Company">H.J. Heinz Co.</a> for $72.50 per share or $28 billion including debt.<sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97">[97]</a></sup> The company became a majority owner of Heinz on June 18, 2015, after exercising a warrant to acquire 46,195,652 shares of common stock for a total price of $461,956.52 increasing its stake to 52.5%.<sup id="cite_ref-98" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-98">[98]</a></sup></p><p>Berkshire owns 1.74 million shares of <a href="/wiki/Gannett" title="Gannett">Gannett</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-99" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99">[99]</a></sup> The company also holds part of newspaper publisher <a href="/wiki/Lee_Enterprises" title="Lee Enterprises">Lee Enterprises</a> after buying some of Lee's debt after its bankruptcy filing.<sup id="cite_ref-100" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-100">[100]</a></sup></p><p>On August 10, 2015, the boards of directors of Berkshire Hathaway Inc. and <a href="/wiki/Precision_Castparts_Corp." title="Precision Castparts Corp.">Precision Castparts Corp.</a> unanimously approved a definitive agreement for Berkshire Hathaway to acquire, for $235 per share in cash, all outstanding PCC shares.<sup id="cite_ref-101" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-101">[101]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Investments_in_Amazon.com_Inc.">Investments in Amazon.com Inc.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Investments in Amazon.com Inc." data-section="20" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>On May 2, 2019, Warren Buffett told CNBC that one of Berkshire's investment managers, either Todd Combs or Ted Weschler, have been purchasing <a href="/wiki/Amazon_(company)" title="Amazon (company)">Amazon</a> shares. "One of the fellows in the office that manage money ... bought some Amazon so it will show up in the 13F," Buffett told CNBC. Buffett continued; "Yeah, I've been a fan, and I've been an idiot for not buying. But I want you to know it's no personality changes taking place."<sup id="cite_ref-102" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-102">[102]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Investment_in_Apple_Inc.">Investment in Apple Inc.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Investment in Apple Inc." data-section="21" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In May 2016, it was revealed in a regulatory filing that Berkshire had acquired a stake in <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple Inc</a>. The initial position was for 9.8 million shares (0.2% of Apple) worth $1 billion. By the end of June 2016, this stake had increased to 15.2 million shares (0.3% of Apple). Berkshire then restarted buying Apple stock again between September to December. By December 31, 2016, Berkshire had built up a stake of 57.4 million shares (1.1% of Apple) with an estimated average acquisition price of $110 per share. Aggressive stock purchases continued and by March 31, 2017, Berkshire had amassed a stake of 129 million shares (2.5% of Apple). In the 2017 annual report, Berkshire Hathaway disclosed its total position by December 31, 2017, to be 166 million shares (3.3% of Apple). As of February 2019, Berkshire owns 5.4% of Apple with 255,300,329 shares.<sup id="cite_ref-reuters_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In media reports, Buffett says that Apple has developed an ecosystem and level of brand loyalty that provides it with a competitive moat, and that consumers appear to have a degree of price insensitivity when it comes to the iPhone. While Buffett has famously avoided tech stocks, he has said that Apple is a consumer products company and that he understands consumer products businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-103" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-103">[103]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Investments_in_US_airlines">Investments in US airlines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Investments in US airlines" data-section="22" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In 2016, Berkshire surprised investors by making large equity investments in the major US <a href="/wiki/Airline" title="Airline">airlines</a>. Buffett had previously described airlines as a "deathtrap for investors". Buffett had made an investment in US Airways in 1989 which, although he sold for a profit, almost lost Berkshire a substantial sum of money.<sup id="cite_ref-104" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-104">[104]</a></sup> As of 2017<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, Berkshire is currently the largest shareholder in <a href="/wiki/United_Airlines" title="United Airlines">United Airlines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Delta_Air_Lines" title="Delta Air Lines">Delta Air Lines</a> and a top 3 shareholder in <a href="/wiki/Southwest_Airlines" title="Southwest Airlines">Southwest Airlines</a> and <a href="/wiki/American_Airlines" title="American Airlines">American Airlines</a>. Buffett himself has described this as a "call on the industry" rather than a choice in an individual company. American Airline's CEO <a href="/wiki/Doug_Parker" title="Doug Parker">Doug Parker</a> is said to have won over Ted Weschler in arguing that the airline industry had consolidated sufficiently and rationalized supply such that longer-term profitability could be achieved in an industry that has <a href="/wiki/Airline#Economic_considerations" title="Airline">historically been loss-making</a> in aggregate.<sup id="cite_ref-105" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-105">[105]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Prior_Investments">Prior Investments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkshire_Hathaway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Prior Investments" data-section="23" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>Berkshire previously held a considerable stake in <a href="/wiki/Tesco" title="Tesco">Tesco Plc</a>, the UK grocery retailer. Berkshire made its first investment in Tesco in 2006 and in 2012 raised this stake to over 5% of the company<sup id="cite_ref-106" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-106">[106]</a></sup> with a cost for the investment of $2.3 billion. Buffett sold around 30% of this stake in 2013 when he "soured somewhat on the company's then-management"<sup id="cite_ref-107" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-107">[107]</a></sup> realizing a profit of $43 million. As Tesco's problems mounted through 2014, Berkshire sold all the remaining shares with Buffett saying to shareholders that the delay in selling shares was costly. Berkshire made an after-tax loss of $444 million on the Tesco investment.<sup id="cite_ref-108" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-108">[108]</a></sup></p>
