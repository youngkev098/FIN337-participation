<p><b>Autodesk, Inc.</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> <a href="/wiki/Software" title="Software">software</a> corporation that makes software services for the architecture, engineering, construction, manufacturing, media, education, and entertainment industries. Autodesk is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/San_Rafael,_California" title="San Rafael, California">San Rafael, California</a>, and features a gallery of its customers' work<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> in its San Francisco building. The company has offices worldwide. Its U.S. locations are  California, <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>,  New Hampshire and Massachusetts. Its Canada offices are located in <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>.

AutoCAD, which is the company's flagship <a href="/wiki/Computer-aided_design" title="Computer-aided design">computer-aided design</a> (CAD) software and Revit software are primarily used by architects, engineers, and structural designers to design, draft, and model buildings and other structures. Autodesk software has been used in many fields, and on projects from the <a href="/wiki/One_World_Trade_Center" title="One World Trade Center">One World Trade Center</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> to <a href="/wiki/Tesla,_Inc." title="Tesla, Inc.">Tesla electric cars</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>Autodesk became best known for <a href="/wiki/AutoCAD" title="AutoCAD">AutoCAD</a>, but now develops a broad range of software for design, engineering, and entertainment—and a line of software for consumers, including <a href="/wiki/Autodesk_SketchBook_Pro" title="Autodesk SketchBook Pro">Sketchbook</a>. The manufacturing industry uses Autodesk's <a href="/wiki/Digital_prototyping" title="Digital prototyping">digital prototyping</a> software—including <a href="/wiki/Autodesk_Inventor" title="Autodesk Inventor">Autodesk Inventor</a>, Fusion 360, and the Autodesk Product Design Suite—to visualize, simulate, and analyze real-world performance using a digital model in the design process.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The company's <a href="/wiki/Revit" class="mw-redirect" title="Revit">Revit</a> line of software for <a href="/wiki/Building_information_modeling" title="Building information modeling">building information modeling</a> is designed to let users explore the planning, construction, and management of a building virtually before it is built.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>Autodesk's <a href="/wiki/Autodesk_Media_and_Entertainment" title="Autodesk Media and Entertainment">Media and Entertainment</a> division creates software for visual effects, color grading, and editing as well as animation, game development, and design visualization.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Autodesk_3ds_Max" title="Autodesk 3ds Max">3ds Max</a> and <a href="/wiki/Autodesk_Maya" title="Autodesk Maya">Maya</a> are both 3D animation software used in film visual effects and game development.
</p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Platforms">Platforms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Autodesk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Platforms" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:AutoCAD_2006_drawing.png" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/3e/AutoCAD_2006_drawing.png/250px-AutoCAD_2006_drawing.png" decoding="async" width="250" height="180" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="372" data-file-height="268"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 250px;height: 180px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/3e/AutoCAD_2006_drawing.png/250px-AutoCAD_2006_drawing.png" data-alt="" data-width="250" data-height="180" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:AutoCAD_2006_drawing.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A screenshot of <a href="/wiki/AutoCAD" title="AutoCAD">AutoCAD</a>, Autodesk's flagship product.</div></div></div>
<p>Platform Solutions and Emerging Business (PSEB) division develops and manages the product foundation for most Autodesk offerings across multiple markets, including Autodesk's flagship product <a href="/wiki/AutoCAD" title="AutoCAD">AutoCAD</a>, AutoCAD LT, AutoCAD for Mac, and AutoCAD mobile app (formerly AutoCAD 360). Autodesk Suites, Subscription and Web Services, which includes Autodesk Cloud, Autodesk Labs, and Global Engineering are also part of PSEB. In what was seen as an unusual step for a maker of high-end business software, Autodesk began offering AutoCAD LT 2012 for Mac through the Apple Mac App Store.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Also part of PSEB is the Autodesk Consumer Product Group, which was created in November 2010 to generate interest in 3-D design and “foster a new wave of designers who hunger for sophisticated software”.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The products from the group include Motion FX, and SketchBook. Users range from children, students and artists to makers and DIYers.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Training_And_Certification">Training And Certification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Autodesk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Training And Certification" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Autodesk offers certificates in two categories: Autodesk Certified User and Advanced Certified Professional.
</p>
<ul><li><b>Autodesk Certified User-</b> Verifies entry-level skills in key Autodesk products. Designed for students and instructors who wish to demonstrate basic proficiency. Curriculum, courseware, and exams offered for independent study or institutional integration.</li>
<li><b>Advanced Certified Professional-</b> Validates more advanced skills, including complex workflow and design challenges. Designed for students seeking a competitive advantage in a specific product area.</li></ul><h3 class="in-block"><span id="Architecture.2C_engineering_and_construction"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture,_engineering_and_construction">Architecture, engineering and construction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Autodesk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Architecture, engineering and construction" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The Architecture, Engineering and Construction (AEC) industry group is headquartered in Boston, Massachusetts, in a LEED Platinum building<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> designed and built using Autodesk software.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Autodesk's architecture, engineering, and construction solutions include <a href="/wiki/AutoCAD" title="AutoCAD">AutoCAD</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Autodesk_Revit" title="Autodesk Revit">Revit</a>, which is their flagship product for relational <a href="/wiki/Building_information_modeling" title="Building information modeling">Building information modeling</a>. The AEC division also develops and manages software for the Construction industry, including BIM 360, <a href="/wiki/Advance_Steel" title="Advance Steel">Advance Steel</a>, and the NavisWorks (acquired 2007) product tools; the Infrastructure industry, including Civil 3D, and InfraWorks; and the MEP industry, including Fabrication CADmep. The Autodesk Services Marketplace offering helps its clients train their team in AEC Industry.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Projects that have used software from the Autodesk AEC division include the NASA Ames building,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> the San Francisco Bay Bridge,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> the Shanghai Tower, and New York's One World Trade Center.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Genetic_engineering">Genetic engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Autodesk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Genetic engineering" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20180327212058/https://lifesciences.autodesk.com/">Autodesk Life Sciences</a> is an extensible toolkit for genetic engineering. It visualises DNA code (Molecule Viewer), and has a tool for writing DNA code (genetic constructor). The tool allows work on molecule-level, rather than nucleobase-level (A, C, G, T) constructs.
</p><p>In 2018, all projects were suspended.<sup id="cite_ref-lifesciences_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lifesciences-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Manufacturing">Manufacturing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Autodesk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Manufacturing" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Autodesk's manufacturing industry group is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland, Oregon</a>. The company's manufacturing software is used in various manufacturing segments, including industrial machinery, electro-mechanical, tool and die, industrial equipment, automotive components, and consumer products. Products include Fusion 360, the Product Design &amp; Manufacturing Collection, <a href="/wiki/Autodesk_Inventor" title="Autodesk Inventor">Autodesk Inventor</a>, <a href="/wiki/NEi_Nastran" title="NEi Nastran">Inventor Nastran</a> (formerly Nastran In-CAD), Inventor CAM (formerly Autodesk HSM and Inventor HSM), Inventor Tolerance Analysis, Alias products, Factory Design Utilities, <a href="/wiki/Autodesk_Vault" title="Autodesk Vault">Autodesk Vault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Autodesk_Simulation" title="Autodesk Simulation">Autodesk CFD</a> (formerly Autodesk Simulation CFD), <a href="/wiki/Moldflow" title="Moldflow">Moldflow</a>, Netfabb, FeatureCAM, <a href="/wiki/PowerMILL" title="PowerMILL">PowerMill</a>, PowerInspect, <a href="/wiki/Delcam#Advanced_Manufacturing_Solutions" title="Delcam">PowerShape</a>, and VRED.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Media_and_entertainment">Media and entertainment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Autodesk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Media and entertainment" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Autodesk_Media_and_Entertainment" title="Autodesk Media and Entertainment">Autodesk Media and Entertainment</a> products are designed for digital media creation, management, and delivery, from film and television visual effects, color grading, and editing to animation, game development, and design visualization. Autodesk's Media and Entertainment Division is based in Montreal, Quebec. It was established in 1999 after Autodesk, Inc. acquired Discreet Logic, Inc. and merged its operations with Kinetix. In January 2006, Autodesk acquired Alias, a developer of 3D graphics technology. In October 2008, Autodesk acquired the Softimage brand from Avid.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The principal product offerings from the <a href="/wiki/Autodesk_Media_and_Entertainment" title="Autodesk Media and Entertainment">Media and Entertainment Division</a> are <a href="/wiki/IFF_(software)" class="mw-redirect" title="IFF (software)">Flame</a>, <a href="/wiki/Smoke_(software)" class="mw-redirect" title="Smoke (software)">Smoke</a>, and the Media &amp; Entertainment Collection, which include <a href="/wiki/Autodesk_Maya" title="Autodesk Maya">Maya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Autodesk_3ds_Max" title="Autodesk 3ds Max">3ds Max</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arnold_(software)" title="Arnold (software)">Arnold</a>, <a href="/wiki/Autodesk_MotionBuilder" title="Autodesk MotionBuilder">MotionBuilder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Autodesk_Mudbox" title="Autodesk Mudbox">Mudbox</a>, and ReCap Pro.
</p><p>Much of <i><a href="/wiki/Avatar_(2009_film)" title="Avatar (2009 film)">Avatar</a>'</i>s visual effects were created with Autodesk media and entertainment software. Autodesk software enabled <i>Avatar</i> director <a href="/wiki/James_Cameron" title="James Cameron">James Cameron</a> to aim a camera at actors wearing motion-capture suits in a studio and see them as characters in the fictional world of Pandora in the film.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Autodesk software also played a role in the visual effects of <i><a href="/wiki/Alice_in_Wonderland_(2010_film)" title="Alice in Wonderland (2010 film)">Alice in Wonderland</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Curious_Case_of_Benjamin_Button_(film)" title="The Curious Case of Benjamin Button (film)">The Curious Case of Benjamin Button</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows_Part_1" class="mw-redirect" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows Part 1</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Inception" title="Inception">Inception</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Iron_Man_2" title="Iron Man 2">Iron Man 2</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/King_Kong_(2005_film)" title="King Kong (2005 film)"><i>King Kong</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/Gladiator_(2000_film)" title="Gladiator (2000 film)"><i>Gladiator</i></a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Titanic_(1997_film)" title="Titanic (1997 film)">Titanic</a></i>, and other films.<sup id="cite_ref-Fast_Company_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fast_Company-20">[20]</a></sup></p>

<p>Some of Autodesk's "retired" products are listed here:
</p>
<ul><li>Lightscape 3.2 Was the world's only <a href="/wiki/Radiosity_(computer_graphics)" title="Radiosity (computer graphics)">radiosity</a> rendering package at the time (1991) developed from work done by Donald Greenberg at the <a href="/wiki/Cornell_University" title="Cornell University">Cornell University</a> Department of Computer Graphics. The problem with this part of Autodesk's history is that it was a time of discovery in computer graphics, and Cornell was one of the birthplaces for the technology. In this sense Lightscape was more than just another product, it was an essential part of the development of rendering technology generally, and part of its evolution. Additionally the software came from a university research department and represented the start of a development cycle that users the world over were watching closely.  Regardless, Autodesk purchased rights to the software and promptly discontinued its sale. A primitive version of the radiosity renderer was incorporated into the companies 3d Studio Max product, while existing Lightscape customers and the product were simply dropped.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li>Volo View was a web-enabled review and markup tool from Autodesk for engineering data, including support for Autodesk's <a href="/wiki/DWG" class="mw-redirect" title="DWG">DWG</a>, <a href="/wiki/DXF" class="mw-redirect" title="DXF">DXF</a>, and DWF formats. Volo View enabled design teams to communicate ideas and review designs without access to AutoCAD software. Autodesk discontinued sales of Volo View on May 1, 2005. The latest version of the software, Volo View 3, worked with the following file formats: AutoCAD 2004, DWG and DXF; Design Web Format (DWF 6); Autodesk Inventor 7 IPT, IAM, and IDW and raster files. The functionality of this product is largely replaced by Autodesk DWF Composer (versions 1 and 2) later replaced by the free <a href="/wiki/Autodesk_Design_Review" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodesk Design Review">Autodesk Design Review</a>. <ul><li>Autodesk Seek - acquired by BIMobject AB.<sup id="cite_ref-102" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-102">[102]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-103" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-103">[103]</a></sup></li>
<li>Autodesk Pixlr - acquired by 123RF.<sup id="cite_ref-104" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-104">[104]</a></sup></li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Autodesk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: History" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Autodesk's first notable product was <a href="/wiki/AutoCAD" title="AutoCAD">AutoCAD</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Computer-aided_design" title="Computer-aided design">computer-aided design</a> application designed to run on the systems known as "microcomputers" at the time, including those running the <a href="/wiki/8-bit" class="mw-redirect" title="8-bit">8-bit</a> <a href="/wiki/CP/M" title="CP/M">CP/M</a> <a href="/wiki/Operating_system" title="Operating system">operating system</a> and two of the new <a href="/wiki/16-bit" class="mw-redirect" title="16-bit">16-bit</a> systems, the <a href="/wiki/Victor_9000" class="mw-redirect" title="Victor 9000">Victor 9000</a> and the <a href="/wiki/IBM_Personal_Computer" title="IBM Personal Computer">IBM Personal Computer</a> (PC). This tool provided made it affordable for smaller design, engineering, and architecture companies to create detailed technical drawings.
</p><p>Autodesk became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> in 1985. John Walker did not enjoy the process of writing the prospectus, relating the process to "lying on the beach or juggling chainsaws".<sup id="cite_ref-105" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-105">[105]</a></sup></p><p>Release 2.1 of AutoCAD, released in 1986, included <a href="/wiki/AutoLISP" title="AutoLISP">AutoLISP</a>, a built-in <a href="/wiki/Lisp_programming_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Lisp programming language">Lisp programming language</a> interpreter initially based on XLISP.<sup id="cite_ref-106" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-106">[106]</a></sup> This opened the door for <a href="/wiki/Third_party_developer" class="mw-redirect" title="Third party developer">third party developers</a> to extend AutoCAD's functionality, to address a wide range of <a href="/wiki/Vertical_market" title="Vertical market">vertical markets</a>, strengthening AutoCAD's market penetration.
</p><p>Subsequent to AutoCAD Release 13, the company stopped supporting the <a href="/wiki/Unix" title="Unix">Unix</a> environment and the Apple Macintosh platform. After AutoCAD Release 14 (R13 was last DOS &amp; Unix release), first shipped in 1997, Autodesk discontinued development under <a href="/wiki/DOS" title="DOS">DOS</a>, and focused exclusively on <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Microsoft Windows</a>.
</p><p>AutoCAD has grown to become the most widely used CAD program for 2D non-specialized applications.<sup id="cite_ref-107" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-107">[107]</a></sup> The native file formats written by AutoCAD, <a href="/wiki/AutoCAD_DXF" title="AutoCAD DXF">DXF</a> and <a href="/wiki/AutoCAD_DWG" class="mw-redirect" title="AutoCAD DWG">DWG</a>, are also widely used for CAD data <a href="/wiki/Interoperability" title="Interoperability">interoperability</a>.
</p><p>In 1989, Autodesk's sales grew to over $100,000,000 after just four operational years.<sup id="cite_ref-108" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-108">[108]</a></sup></p><p>In the 1990s, with the purchase of Softdesk in 1997, Autodesk started to develop specialty versions of <a href="/wiki/AutoCAD" title="AutoCAD">AutoCAD</a>, targeted to broad industry segments, including architecture, <a href="/wiki/Civil_engineering" title="Civil engineering">civil engineering</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Manufacturing" title="Manufacturing">manufacturing</a>. Since the late 1990s, the company has added a number of significant non-AutoCAD-based products, including <a href="/wiki/Revit" class="mw-redirect" title="Revit">Revit</a>, a parametric building modeling application (acquired in 2002, from Massachusetts-based Revit Technologies for $133 million), and <a href="/wiki/Autodesk_Inventor" title="Autodesk Inventor">Inventor</a>, an internally developed parametric mechanical design CAD application.
</p><p>In 2007, Timothy Vernor sued Autodesk (<i><a href="/wiki/Vernor_v._Autodesk,_Inc." title="Vernor v. Autodesk, Inc.">Vernor v. Autodesk, Inc.</a></i>), alleging that he was entitled to resell "used" copies of AutoCAD software on <a href="/wiki/EBay" title="EBay">eBay</a>. He had obtained the software from an Autodesk licensee at an office liquidation sale.<sup id="cite_ref-109" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-109">[109]</a></sup> A federal district judge in <a href="/wiki/Washington_state" class="mw-redirect" title="Washington state">Washington state</a> denied Autodesk's initial motion to dismiss in early 2008. In February and March 2009, both sides filed motions for summary judgment addressing the issue whether the <a href="/wiki/First_Sale_Doctrine" class="mw-redirect" title="First Sale Doctrine">First Sale Doctrine</a> applies to previously licensed software.<sup id="cite_ref-110" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-110">[110]</a></sup> The Court ruled in Vernor's favor, holding that when the transfer of software to the purchaser materially resembled a sale (non-recurring price, right to perpetual possession of copy) it was, in fact, a "sale with restrictions on use"<sup id="cite_ref-WISE_111-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WISE-111">[111]</a></sup> giving rise to a right to resell the copy under the first-sale doctrine. As such, Autodesk could not pursue an action for copyright infringement against Vernor, who sought to resell used versions of its software on eBay. Autodesk appealed the decision to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Court_of_Appeals_for_the_Ninth_Circuit" title="United States Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit">United States Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit</a>, which reversed the lower court ruling, denying Vernor the right to resale Autodesk software due to Autodesk's nontransferable licensing restrictions.<sup id="cite_ref-112" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-112">[112]</a></sup> In October 2011, the U.S. Supreme Court let stand the 9th Circuit Court of Appeals ruling.<sup id="cite_ref-113" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-113">[113]</a></sup></p><p>Autodesk introduced its current logo at the <a href="/wiki/TED_(conference)" title="TED (conference)">TED conference</a> in <a href="/wiki/Long_Beach,_California" title="Long Beach, California">Long Beach</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> on February 26, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-114" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-114">[114]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-115" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-115">[115]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-116" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-116">[116]</a></sup></p><p>Autodesk announced the largest lay off in its history on November 27, 2017 with the lay off of 1,150 jobs.  This is in addition to the almost 1,000 job cuts announced in January 2016.
The number of Autodesk employees has shrunk from approximately 9,200 to 7,200 in less than 2 years.<sup id="cite_ref-117" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-117">[117]</a></sup></p>

<p>Autodesk CFD (formerly Autodesk Simulation CFD) includes modeling and thermal modeling tools for architectural and MEP applications. Common applications for environmental sustainable design include mechanical ventilation, external flow (wind loading), natural ventilation, and occupant comfort. Other energy applications include analysis for building energy, solar load, advanced energy and heating and cooling.<sup id="cite_ref-165" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-165">[165]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-166" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-166">[166]</a></sup> Autodesk introduced C-FACT, an open-source, science-driven approach to setting <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> reduction targets, which calls for greenhouse gas (GHG) reductions to be made in proportion to a company's gross domestic product (GDP). Unlike other carbon accounting methods, Autodesk's C-FACT measures carbon dioxide emissions that are proportional to a company's global GDP contribution.<sup id="cite_ref-167" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-167">[167]</a></sup> Autodesk will derive its own targets using this approach through 2020.
</p><p>In 2006, Autodesk sponsored a <a href="/wiki/Public_Broadcasting_Service" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Broadcasting Service">PBS</a> program named <i>e² Design</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-168" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-168">[168]</a></sup> which focused on green building design around the world, describing the leaders and technologies that drive sustainable design.
