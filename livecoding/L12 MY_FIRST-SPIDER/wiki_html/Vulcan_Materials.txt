<p><b>Vulcan Materials Company</b> (<a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">NYSE</a>: <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.nyse.com/quote/XNYS:VMC">VMC</a>) is an American company based in <a href="/wiki/Birmingham,_Alabama" title="Birmingham, Alabama">Birmingham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a>. It is principally engaged in the production, distribution and sale of construction materials. Vulcan is the largest producer of construction materials, primarily gravel, crushed stone, and sand, and employs approximately 7,000 people at over 300 facilities. Vulcan serves 19 states, the <a href="/wiki/District_of_Columbia" class="mw-redirect" title="District of Columbia">District of Columbia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>. Vulcan's innovative <a href="/w/index.php?title=Crescent_Market_project&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Crescent Market project (page does not exist)">Crescent Market project</a> led to construction of a large quarry and deep water seaport on the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucatán Peninsula">Yucatán Peninsula</a> of Mexico, just south of <a href="/wiki/Cancun" class="mw-redirect" title="Cancun">Cancun</a>.  This quarry supplies Tampa, New Orleans, Houston, and Brownsville, Texas, as well as other Gulf coast seaports, with crushed limestone via large 62,000 ton self discharging ships.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vulcan_Materials_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>On June 7, 2005, Vulcan completed the sale of its chemicals business, known as Vulcan Chemicals, to <a href="/wiki/Occidental_Chemical_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Occidental Chemical Corporation">Occidental Chemical Corporation</a>. The sale of assets included Vulcan's <a href="/wiki/Chloralkali_process" title="Chloralkali process">chloralkali</a> plants in Wichita, Kansas; Geismar, Louisiana; and Port Edwards, Wisconsin, as well as Vulcan Chemicals' joint venture located in Geismar, Louisiana. In 2005, Vulcan acquired 11 aggregates operations and five asphalt plants in Arizona, Georgia, Indiana and Tennessee.
</p><p>On February 19, 2007, Vulcan announced that it would buy stone and cement producer Florida Rock Industries for $4.7 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Vulcan completed the acquisition of Florida Rock on November 16, 2007.
</p><p>In March 2007, Vulcan announced that it had been named to <a href="/wiki/Fortune_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Fortune Magazine">Fortune Magazine</a>'s list of <i>Most Admired Companies</i> for the sixth time.  The company was ranked first in its industry sector, "Building Materials, Glass."  Overall, Vulcan ranked among the top 10 companies in the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_1000" title="Fortune 1000">Fortune 1000</a> for both long-term investment and <a href="/wiki/Social_responsibility" title="Social responsibility">social responsibility</a>.
</p><p>In January 2014, Vulcan Materials announced they would be selling their Florida Rock Industry (Cement and ready mixed concrete) plants and equipment to <a href="/wiki/Argos_S.A." class="mw-redirect" title="Argos S.A.">Cementos Argos</a>, of Colombia. In March 2014, Vulcan was able to fully divest these properties to Cementos Argos.<sup id="cite_ref-FloridaRockSale_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FloridaRockSale-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p><br></p>
