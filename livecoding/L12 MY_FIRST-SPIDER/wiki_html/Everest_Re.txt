</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everest_Re&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>In 1973, Everest Re Group was founded as Prudential Reinsurance, a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Prudential_Financial" title="Prudential Financial">Prudential Financial</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>  In the 1990s, Prudential Financial considered selling or <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spinning off</a> Prudential Reinsurance.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>  On October 6, 1995, Prudential Reinsurance completed an IPO.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>  The chairman and CEO at that time was Joseph V. Taranto.  Prudential Reinsurance was renamed Everest Re in 1996, after <a href="/wiki/Mount_Everest" title="Mount Everest">Mount Everest</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>  In 2017, Everest Re became a <a href="/wiki/List_of_S%26P_500_companies" title="List of S&amp;P 500 companies">S&amp;P 500 component</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Business">Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everest_Re&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Business" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Everest Re provides <a href="/wiki/Reinsurance" title="Reinsurance">reinsurance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Insurance" title="Insurance">insurance</a> to an international set of customers.  Types of insurance provided include <a href="/wiki/Property_insurance" title="Property insurance">property</a>, <a href="/wiki/Workers%27_compensation" title="Workers' compensation">worker's compensation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crop_insurance" title="Crop insurance">crop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Professional_liability_insurance" title="Professional liability insurance">professional liability</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Accidental_death_and_dismemberment_insurance" title="Accidental death and dismemberment insurance">accident insurance</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Annual_Report_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Annual_Report-1">[1]</a></sup>  As of 2016, 70% of premiums were from <a href="/wiki/Reinsurance" title="Reinsurance">reinsurance</a>, with the remainder in <a href="/wiki/Insurance" title="Insurance">insurance</a> (Total premiums were <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$6 billion</span>).<sup id="cite_ref-Annual_Report_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Annual_Report-1">[1]</a></sup>  61% of premiums were from <a href="/wiki/Property_insurance" title="Property insurance">property insurance</a> and reinsurance, with the remainder from <a href="/wiki/Casualty_insurance" title="Casualty insurance">casualty insurance</a> and reinsurance.<sup id="cite_ref-Annual_Report_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Annual_Report-1">[1]</a></sup>  In reinsurance, 52% of premiums were from the United States, 17% were from Europe, 15% were from Latin America, and the rest were from the rest of the world.<sup id="cite_ref-Annual_Report_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Annual_Report-1">[1]</a></sup>  Everest Re sells <a href="/wiki/Catastrophe_bond" title="Catastrophe bond">catastrophe bonds</a> to investors through a variety of subsidiaries, including Mt. Logan Re and Kilimanjaro Re.
</p><p>Because of the business it is in, Everest Re profits are very sensitive to large-scale disasters, usually called catastrophes.  For instance, Everest Re incurred US$1.2 billion in catastrophe losses in the third quarter of 2017 due to hurricanes <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Harvey" title="Hurricane Harvey">Harvey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Irma" title="Hurricane Irma">Irma</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Maria</a>, as well as the <a href="/wiki/2017_Central_Mexico_earthquake" class="mw-redirect" title="2017 Central Mexico earthquake">Central Mexico earthquake</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
