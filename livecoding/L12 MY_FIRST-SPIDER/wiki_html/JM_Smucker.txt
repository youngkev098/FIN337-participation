</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_J.M._Smucker_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Founding">Founding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_J.M._Smucker_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Founding" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The J.M. Smucker Company was founded in 1897 by <a href="/wiki/Jerome_Monroe_Smucker" title="Jerome Monroe Smucker">Jerome Monroe Smucker</a>. The company produces many types of jellies, jams, and other food items. Jerome was born on December 5, 1858 in Orrville, Ohio. Much of his life was spent as a farmer in Orrville. In 1897 Jerome built a cider mill that was located in Orrville. Fruit that was used was from trees that <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Appleseed" title="Johnny Appleseed">Johnny Appleseed</a> had planted in the early nineteenth century.<sup id="cite_ref-company-history_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-company-history-3">[3]</a></sup> Later, he prepared <a href="/wiki/Apple_butter" title="Apple butter">apple butter</a> and sold it from the back of a horse-drawn wagon.<sup id="cite_ref-company-history_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-company-history-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The company was incorporated in 1921.<sup id="cite_ref-timeline_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeline-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent">Recent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_J.M._Smucker_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Recent" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The J. M. Smucker Company has been headquartered in Orrville, Ohio, since its founding, and has been family-run for four generations. Since 1998, the company has appeared on <a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)"><i>Fortune</i> magazine</a>'s annual listing of the 100 Best Companies to Work For in the United States, ranking number one in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2008, Smucker's announced it had bought the food division of <a href="/wiki/Knott%27s_Berry_Farm" title="Knott's Berry Farm">Knott's Berry Farm</a> from <a href="/wiki/ConAgra_Foods" class="mw-redirect" title="ConAgra Foods">ConAgra Foods</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Cedar_Fair" title="Cedar Fair">Cedar Fair</a> continues to own the theme park itself.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On June 4, 2008, Smucker's announced it would purchase the <a href="/wiki/Folgers" title="Folgers">Folgers</a> coffee brand division from <a href="/wiki/Procter_%26_Gamble" title="Procter &amp; Gamble">Procter &amp; Gamble</a> for $3.3 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Completion of the sale was announced on November 6, 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:SmuckerFoodsOfCanada.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/SmuckerFoodsOfCanada.jpg/220px-SmuckerFoodsOfCanada.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="4928" data-file-height="3264"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 146px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/SmuckerFoodsOfCanada.jpg/220px-SmuckerFoodsOfCanada.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="146" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:SmuckerFoodsOfCanada.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Smucker's Canada, Markham, Ontario</div></div></div>
<p>After an antitrust lawsuit filed by the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Trade_Commission" title="Federal Trade Commission">Federal Trade Commission</a> (FTC) in early March 2018, ConAgra Brands Inc. and J. M. Smucker Co. cancelled a deal for Smucker to purchase the <a href="/wiki/Wesson_cooking_oil" title="Wesson cooking oil">Wesson cooking oil</a> brand. The FTC claimed that Smucker would have controlled at least 70 percent of the market for branded canola and vegetable oils.<sup id="cite_ref-smucker_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smucker-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
