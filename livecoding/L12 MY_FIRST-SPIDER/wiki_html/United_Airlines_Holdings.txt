<p>Early in February 2008, <a href="/wiki/UAL_Corporation" title="UAL Corporation">UAL Corporation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Continental_Airlines" title="Continental Airlines">Continental Airlines</a>  began advanced stages of merger negotiations and were expected to announce their decision in the immediate aftermath of a definitive merger agreement between rival <a href="/wiki/Delta_Air_Lines" title="Delta Air Lines">Delta Air Lines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Airlines" title="Northwest Airlines">Northwest Airlines</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The timing of the events was notable because Northwest's <a href="/wiki/Golden_shares" class="mw-redirect" title="Golden shares">golden shares</a> in Continental (that gave Northwest veto authority against any merger involving Continental) could be redeemed, freeing Continental to pursue a marriage with United. On April 27, 2008, Continental broke off merger negotiations with United and stated it was going to stand alone.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Despite ending merger talks, Continental announced that it will join United in the <a href="/wiki/Star_Alliance" title="Star Alliance">Star Alliance</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>United and <a href="/wiki/US_Airways" title="US Airways">US Airways</a> were in advanced merger talks in late April 2008, following the announcement that <a href="/wiki/Continental_Airlines" title="Continental Airlines">Continental</a> had broken off talks with United.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In June 2008, <a href="/wiki/CEOs" class="mw-redirect" title="CEOs">CEOs</a> of both United Airlines and <a href="/wiki/Continental_Airlines" title="Continental Airlines">Continental Airlines</a> signed an alliance pact presaging their eventual merger. The alliance is an agreement to link international networks and share technology and passenger perks. This agreement is basically a "virtual merger" that includes many of the benefits of a merger without the actual costs and restructuring involved. The alliance took effect about a year after <a href="/wiki/Delta_Air_Lines" title="Delta Air Lines">Delta Air Lines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Airlines" title="Northwest Airlines">Northwest Airlines</a> completed their merger, as that released Continental from the <a href="/wiki/SkyTeam" title="SkyTeam">SkyTeam</a> contract and allowed for the required nine-month notice. Additionally, Continental joined <a href="/wiki/Star_Alliance" title="Star Alliance">Star Alliance</a>, as Delta and Northwest merged.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>United Airlines was reported to be in serious merger discussion with US Airways in early April 2010. A <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">New York Times</a></i> report indicated that a deal was close. Union consent was cited as a major hurdle for negotiators to clear.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On April 22, 2010, United announced that it would not pursue a merger with <a href="/wiki/US_Airways" title="US Airways">US Airways</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>The Board of Directors at Continental and United Airlines approved a stock-swap deal that would combine them into the world's largest airline on Sunday, May 2, 2010. The airlines publicly announced the deal the next day.<sup id="cite_ref-unitedcontinentalmerger1_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unitedcontinentalmerger1-22">[22]</a></sup> This would re-unite <a href="/wiki/Walter_Varney" title="Walter Varney">Walter Varney</a>'s airlines, which offspring includes Continental and United.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>

Although the two airlines remained separated until the operational integration was completed, as of this day both airlines are corporately controlled by the same leadership. Both carriers achieved a single operating certificate from the FAA on November 30, 2011 which allowed both airlines to operate under the name "United".<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>

<p>When United Airlines and Continental Airlines announced their merger in May 2010 they introduced their new corporate branding. It featured the words "United Airlines" in the then-current Continental typeface, and Continental's globe-like logo. United updated their branding once again in August 2010, replacing the words "United Airlines" with the single word <b>UNITED</b> and changing the font to United's traditional upper-case sans-serif font.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> United's new CEO Jeff Smisek, who previously served as Continental's chairman, helped design the new font, integrating it with the existing <a href="/wiki/Lippincott_(brand_consultancy)" title="Lippincott (brand consultancy)">Lippincott</a>-designed Continental graphics. He has noted that he has received over 15,000 emails with suggestions for a new livery.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p><p>The first United 747 featuring the new livery was painted in February 2011. Re-painting and branding is said to be "accelerated" beginning mid-2011.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p><p>On March 1, 2011, United unveiled an "interim" marketing campaign replacing the previous "It's Time to Fly" campaign, which included fingerpaint ads and television spots created by Fallon. On the same date, United removed the iconic 38-year-old <a href="/wiki/Saul_Bass" title="Saul Bass">Saul Bass</a>-designed "Tulip" logo from its website and all new advertisements will feature the former Continental globe logo. This new campaign was used until 2012, when United reconditioned a former slogan, "Fly the Friendly Skies".<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
