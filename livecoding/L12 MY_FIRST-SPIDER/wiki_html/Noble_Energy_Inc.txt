</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_operations">Current operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noble_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Current operations" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>As of December 31, 2018, the company had 1,929 million <a href="/wiki/Barrel_of_oil_equivalent" title="Barrel of oil equivalent">barrels of oil equivalent</a> (1.180<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup>10</sup> GJ) of proved reserves, of which 52% was in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, 43% was in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, and 5% was in <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a>. Of the company's total proved reserves as of December 31, 2018, 62% was <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a>, 14% was <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas_liquids" class="mw-redirect" title="Natural gas liquids">natural gas liquids</a>, and 24% was <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">petroleum</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>In the United States, the company's primary holdings are in the <a href="/wiki/Wattenberg_Gas_Field" title="Wattenberg Gas Field">Wattenberg Gas Field</a> / <a href="/wiki/Denver_Basin" title="Denver Basin">Denver Basin</a> in Colorado, the <a href="/wiki/Delaware_Basin" title="Delaware Basin">Delaware Basin</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Ford_Group" title="Eagle Ford Group">Eagle Ford Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>As of December 31, 2018, the company owned approximately 74,000 net developed acres and 111,000 net undeveloped acres between 10 and 90 miles offshore <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> in water depths ranging from 700 feet to 6,500 feet. Noble holds a 39.66% working interest in the <a href="/wiki/Leviathan_gas_field" title="Leviathan gas field">Leviathan gas field</a>, which it discovered in 2010 and a 25% working interest in the <a href="/wiki/Tamar_gas_field" title="Tamar gas field">Tamar gas field</a>. Through Dolphinus Holdings Limited, Noble sells any excess gas to <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> after its natural gas obligations to Israel and Jordan have been met. The company owns a minority interest in and uses <a href="/wiki/East_Mediterranean_Gas_Company" title="East Mediterranean Gas Company">East Mediterranean Gas Company</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Arab_Gas_Pipeline" title="Arab Gas Pipeline">Arab Gas Pipeline</a> to transport the gas from Israel to Egypt. Exports to <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a> are facilitated by an existing low-capacity pipeline near the Dead Sea and a large capacity pipeline under construction in northern Israel and northern Jordan. Adjacent to the Israel acreage, the company owns a license covering approximately 33,000 net undeveloped acres in the <a href="/wiki/Aphrodite_gas_field" title="Aphrodite gas field">Aphrodite gas field</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p>The company also owns interests in oil fields offshore <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gabon" title="Gabon">Gabon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noble_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>In 1932, <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Noble" class="mw-redirect" title="Lloyd Noble">Lloyd Noble</a> founded the <b>Samedan Oil Corporation</b>, predecessor company to Noble Energy, Inc. The company was named after his children Sam, Ed, and Ann.<sup id="cite_ref-success_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-success-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1968, Samedan acquired its first offshore block in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>. By the 1970s, <b>Noble Affiliates Inc.</b> was set up as a holding company to own <b>Samedan Oil Corporation</b> and a drilling company, <b>Noble Drilling Corporation</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-success_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-success-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1972, Noble Affiliates became a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a> via an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>, listing on the <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-success_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-success-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In 1980, Noble Affiliates was first listed on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> with the ticker symbol NBL. In 1985, Noble Affiliates spun off its subsidiary, <a href="/wiki/Noble_Drilling_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Noble Drilling Corporation">Noble Drilling Corporation</a>. In 1986, the company acquired Energy Development Corporation.
</p><p>In June 2000, the company announced that it will move its headquarters from <a href="/wiki/Ardmore,_Oklahoma" title="Ardmore, Oklahoma">Ardmore, Oklahoma</a> to <a href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Houston, Texas">Houston, Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2000, Charles Davidson became president and <a href="/wiki/CEO" class="mw-redirect" title="CEO">CEO</a> of the company.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>By April 2002, the company had diversified into <a href="/wiki/Methanol" title="Methanol">methanol</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electricity" title="Electricity">electricity</a> production, and changed its name to Noble Energy, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2005, the company acquired Patina Oil &amp; Gas, giving the company acreage in the <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountains" title="Rocky Mountains">Rocky Mountains</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2006, the company sold assets in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> for $625 million.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2006, the company announced a significant discovery in the Raton prospect of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2010, the company acquired assets in the DJ Basin of the <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountains" title="Rocky Mountains">Rocky Mountains</a> from <a href="/wiki/PetroCanada" class="mw-redirect" title="PetroCanada">PetroCanada</a> and <a href="/wiki/Suncor_Energy" title="Suncor Energy">Suncor Energy</a> for $494 million.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2010, the company's Deep Blue exploration well on Green Canyon 723 in the deepwater Gulf of Mexico reached a depth of 32,684 feet.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2010, the company announced the discovery of the <a href="/wiki/Leviathan_gas_field" title="Leviathan gas field">Leviathan gas field</a>, offshore <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2011, the company received the first deepwater drilling permit in the Gulf of Mexico issued by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_the_Interior" title="United States Department of the Interior">United States Department of the Interior</a> after the moratorium following the <i><a href="/wiki/Deepwater_Horizon" title="Deepwater Horizon">Deepwater Horizon</a></i> incident.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In 2012, the company moved its headquarters to a building formerly occupied by <a href="/wiki/Hewlett_Packard" class="mw-redirect" title="Hewlett Packard">Hewlett Packard</a> in northwest Houston, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> and signed a lease for 497,000 square feet of office space.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2012, the company sold assets in the <a href="/wiki/Mid-Continent_oil_province" title="Mid-Continent oil province">Mid-Continent oil province</a> for $594 million.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2014, the company announced plans to establish a center to train technicians in the energy industry at <a href="/wiki/Ruppin_Academic_Center" title="Ruppin Academic Center">Ruppin Academic Center</a> in Israel with a donation of <a href="/wiki/New_Israeli_Shekel" class="mw-redirect" title="New Israeli Shekel">NIS</a> 12 million.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2015, the company acquired Rosetta Resources in a stock transaction. The acquisition gave the company acreage in the <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Ford_Group" title="Eagle Ford Group">Eagle Ford Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In September 2015, the company announced that the drilling of a well in the Cheetah exploration prospect offshore <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a> yielded a dry hole. The company recorded a $33 million charge for this unsuccessful venture.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2017, the company acquired Clayton Williams Energy for $2.7 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2017, the company sold Marcellus Midstream to <a href="/wiki/Quantum_Energy_Partners" title="Quantum Energy Partners">Quantum Energy Partners</a> for $765 million.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The company also sold its assets in the <a href="/wiki/Marcellus_Shale" class="mw-redirect" title="Marcellus Shale">Marcellus Shale</a> for $1.12 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>In January 2018, the company sold part of its interest in the <a href="/wiki/Tamar_gas_field" title="Tamar gas field">Tamar gas field</a> for $800 million.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2018, the company sold its assets in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2018, the company acquired assets in the <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Ford_Group" title="Eagle Ford Group">Eagle Ford Group</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Bakken_Formation" title="Bakken Formation">Bakken Formation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noble_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Controversies" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>In 2015, the government of <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> accused the company of operating illegally in the <a href="/wiki/Falkland_Islands" title="Falkland Islands">Falkland Islands</a>. The government of Argentina does not recognize British control of the Falkland Islands.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, the company settled a claim by the <a href="/wiki/Environmental_Protection_Agency" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental Protection Agency">Environmental Protection Agency</a> that its battery tanks were emitting noxious gases. The settlement included the payment of a $4.95 million civil penalty divided as follows: $3.475 million to the United States; and $1.475 million to Colorado.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
