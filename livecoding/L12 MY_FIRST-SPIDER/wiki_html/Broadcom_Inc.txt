</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Broadcom_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company was founded in 1961 as a semiconductor products division of <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-contra_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-contra-3">[3]</a></sup> The division separated from Hewlett-Packard as part of <a href="/wiki/Agilent_Technologies" title="Agilent Technologies">Agilent Technologies</a> in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-register_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-register-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-techtimes_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-techtimes-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-value_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-value-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kohlberg_Kravis_Roberts" title="Kohlberg Kravis Roberts">KKR</a> and <a href="/wiki/Silver_Lake_Partners" title="Silver Lake Partners">Silver Lake Partners</a> acquired the chip division of Agilent Technologies in 2005 for $2.6 billion and formed Avago Technologies.<sup id="cite_ref-register_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-register-5">[5]</a></sup> Avago Technologies agreed to sell its I/O solutions unit to <a href="/wiki/PMC-Sierra" title="PMC-Sierra">PMC-Sierra</a> for $42.5 million in October 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In August 2008, the company filed an initial public offering of $400 million.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In October 2008, Avago Technologies acquired <a href="/wiki/Infineon_Technologies" title="Infineon Technologies">Infineon Technologies'</a> Munich-based bulk acoustic wave business for €21.5 million.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> 
In 2009, Avago Technologies went public on <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a> with the ticker symbol AVGO.<sup id="cite_ref-value_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-value-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-techspot_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-techspot-13">[13]</a></sup> Avago Technologies announced its agreement to acquire CyOptics, an optical chip and component supplier, for $400 million in April 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-india_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-india-15">[15]</a></sup> The acquisition aimed to expand Avago Technologies' fiber optics product portfolio.<sup id="cite_ref-india_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-india-15">[15]</a></sup> In October 2013, Avago Technologies invested $5 million in Amantys, a <a href="/wiki/Power_electronics" title="Power electronics">power electronics</a> technology provider, as part of a strategic investment agreement between the two companies.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Avago Technologies announced its agreement to acquire <a href="/wiki/LSI_Corporation" title="LSI Corporation">LSI Corporation</a> in December 2013 for $6.6 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-register_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-register-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-techtimes_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-techtimes-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-techspot_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-techspot-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nytimes_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-18">[18]</a></sup> The acquisition helped move Avago Technologies away from specialized products and towards a more mainstream industry, which included chips, especially storage for data centers.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>The company sold its <a href="/wiki/SSD_controller" class="mw-redirect" title="SSD controller">SSD controller</a> business to <a href="/wiki/Seagate_Technology" title="Seagate Technology">Seagate Technology</a> in May 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In August 2014, the company was the ninth largest semiconductor company.<sup id="cite_ref-seeking_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-seeking-6">[6]</a></sup> Avago Technologies agreed to sell LSI's Axxia Networking business to <a href="/wiki/Intel" title="Intel">Intel</a> for $650 million.<sup id="cite_ref-value_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-value-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The company also agreed to buy <a href="/wiki/PLX_Technology" title="PLX Technology">PLX Technology</a>, an integrated circuits designer, for $309 million.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In February 2015, it was announced that Avago Technologies Limited had reached an agreement to acquire <a href="/wiki/Emulex" title="Emulex">Emulex</a> Corporation for $8 per share in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>On 28 May 2015, Avago announced that it would buy <a href="/wiki/Broadcom_Corporation" title="Broadcom Corporation">Broadcom Corporation</a><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> for $37 billion ($17 billion cash and $20 billion in shares).<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The combined company, which would be named Broadcom Ltd., would have annual revenue of $15 billion and a market value of $77 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Broadcom Corp. strengthened Avago Technologies' patent position significantly in sectors such as mobile, the data center and the Internet of Things and made the company the ninth largest holder of patents among the top semiconductor vendors, according to an analysis by technology consulting firm <a href="/wiki/LexInnova_Technologies" title="LexInnova Technologies">LexInnova</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> According to the company's web site, the transaction closed on 1 February 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>In 2016, Broadcom proposed merging with <a href="/wiki/Brocade_Communications_Systems" title="Brocade Communications Systems">Brocade Communications Systems</a>. The merger was delayed for review by the <a href="/wiki/Committee_on_Foreign_Investment_in_the_United_States" title="Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States">Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States</a>. In 2017, Broadcom announced it would relocate its legal address from Singapore to Delaware, which would avoid the review.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> This action was linked to the parent company being renamed from Broadcom Ltd. to Broadcom Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-schizophrenic_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-schizophrenic-29">[29]</a></sup> The pre-2016-merger Broadcom, <a href="/wiki/Broadcom_Corporation" title="Broadcom Corporation">Broadcom Corp.</a>, remains as a wholly owned subsidiary of the renamed parent Broadcom Inc.
</p><p>In November 2017, Broadcom proposed to purchase <a href="/wiki/Qualcomm" title="Qualcomm">Qualcomm</a> for US$130 billion, which was rebuffed by Qualcomm's board.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> President <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Trump</a> blocked the revised $117 billion merger by an <a href="/wiki/Executive_order_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="Executive order (United States)">executive order</a> that cited national security concerns.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>On 11 July 2018, news sources reported that Broadcom and <a href="/wiki/CA_Technologies" title="CA Technologies">CA Technologies</a> agreed on terms for $18.9 billion acquisition.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> And on 5 November 2018 Broadcom announced that it had completed the acquisition of CA Technologies.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p><p>On 9 August 2019, news sources reported that Broadcom had decided to acquire the enterprise security business of Symantec Corporation (the consumer software portion of which is now known as <a href="/wiki/NortonLifeLock" title="NortonLifeLock">NortonLifeLock</a>) for $10.7 billion in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> And on 4 November 2019 Broadcom announced that it had completed the acquisition of the business, as well as the Symantec name and brand.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> In 2019, Broadcom was announced the 5th best <a href="/wiki/Stock" title="Stock">stock</a> of the 2010s, with a total return of 1,956%.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p><p>On October 2019, Broadcom was ordered by the European Union to stop allegedly anticompetitive practices.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p><p>On January 7, 2020, <a href="/wiki/Accenture" title="Accenture">Accenture plc</a> had agreed to acquire Symantec's 300-person cybersecurity services division from Broadcom.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2020, Broadcom announced the world's first Wi-Fi 6E client device, the BCM4389.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
