href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In addition to gold, Newmont mines <a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">copper</a>, <a <div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:208px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Boyce_Thompson.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/2/2a/Boyce_Thompson.jpg" decoding="async" width="206" height="288" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="206" data-file-height="288"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 206px;height: 288px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/2/2a/Boyce_Thompson.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="206" data-height="288" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Boyce_Thompson.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>William Boyce Thompson, founder of Newmont Mining</div></div></div>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newmont_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early years" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The Newmont Company was founded in 1916 in New York by <a href="/wiki/Colonel_(United_States)" title="Colonel (United States)">Colonel</a> <a href="/wiki/William_Boyce_Thompson" title="William Boyce Thompson">William Boyce Thompson</a> as a holding company to invest in Worldwide <a href="/wiki/Mineral" title="Mineral">mineral</a>, <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">oil</a>, and related companies. According to company lore, the name "Newmont" is a portmanteau "New York" and "Montana", reflecting where Thompson made his fortune and where he grew up. Newmont made its first major gold investment in 1917, with a founding 25 percent in the <a href="/wiki/Anglo_American_plc" title="Anglo American plc">Anglo American Corporation</a> of South Africa. Four years later, in 1921, the Newmont Company reincorporated as the Newmont Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 1929, Newmont became a mining company with its first gold product in by acquiring <a href="/wiki/California%27s" class="mw-redirect" title="California's">California's</a> <a href="/wiki/Empire_Mine_State_Historic_Park" title="Empire Mine State Historic Park">Empire Star Mine</a>. By 1939, Newmont was operating 12 gold mines in North America.
</p><p>The company acquired interests overseas. For decades around the middle of the 20th century, Newmont had a controlling interest in the <a href="/wiki/Tsumeb" title="Tsumeb">Tsumeb</a> mine in <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a> and in the O'Okiep Copper Company in <a href="/wiki/Namaqualand" title="Namaqualand">Namaqualand</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>.
</p><p>Beginning in 1925, Newmont acquired interests in a <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> <a href="/wiki/Oil_field" class="mw-redirect" title="Oil field">oil field</a>. Eventually, Newmont's oil interests included more than 70 blocks in the Louisiana, <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> area and oil and gas production in the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea_oil" title="North Sea oil">North Sea</a>.
</p><p>Fred Searls became president in 1947, after serving as the company's exploration geologist.  Searls retired in 1954, and Plato Malozemoff took over as president.<sup id="cite_ref-Hecla_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hecla-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>Newmont discovered the world's first submicroscopic or "invisible" gold at <a href="/wiki/Carlin,_Nevada" title="Carlin, Nevada">Carlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a> in the early 1960s and began production on the first open pit gold mine in the world. The "<a href="/wiki/Carlin_Trend" class="mw-redirect" title="Carlin Trend">Carlin Trend</a>" or "Carlin Unconformity" is the largest gold discovery in North America during the 20th century. In 1971 Newmont began using the <a href="/wiki/Heap_leaching" title="Heap leaching">heap leaching</a> technology on sub-mill grade <a href="/wiki/Ores" class="mw-redirect" title="Ores">ores</a> there.
</p><p>In the 1980s, Newmont thwarted five takeover bids – from <a href="/wiki/Consolidated_Gold_Fields" title="Consolidated Gold Fields">Consolidated Gold Fields</a> (<a href="/wiki/ConsGold" class="mw-redirect" title="ConsGold">ConsGold</a>), <a href="/wiki/T._Boone_Pickens" title="T. Boone Pickens">T. Boone Pickens</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Minorco&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Minorco (page does not exist)">Minorco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hanson_plc" class="mw-redirect" title="Hanson plc">Hanson Industries</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sir_James_Goldsmith" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir James Goldsmith">Sir James Goldsmith</a> – who sought to break Newmont apart and sell its assets to increase shareholder value.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_growth">Major growth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newmont_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Major growth" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>After 1987, the company undertook major restructuring. This included the payment of a US$33 per share <a href="/wiki/Dividend" title="Dividend">dividend</a> to all <a href="/wiki/Shareholders" class="mw-redirect" title="Shareholders">shareholders</a> for a total of US$2.2 billion, of which US$1.75 billion was borrowed. To reduce this debt the company undertook a <a href="/wiki/Divestment" title="Divestment">divestment</a> program involving all of its copper, oil, gas, and <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a> interests.
</p><p>As a further step in the restructuring, the company moved its headquarters from New York City to <a href="/wiki/Denver" title="Denver">Denver</a> in 1988. A decade later, Newmont Mining Corporation and Newmont Gold Company combined assets to form a unified worldwide gold company. Shareholders of both companies had identical interests in the reserves, production and earnings of Newmont Gold's operations.
</p><p>Newmont then merged with <a href="/wiki/Santa_Fe_Railroad" class="mw-redirect" title="Santa Fe Railroad">Santa Fe Pacific Corporation</a> (a former <a href="/wiki/Atchison,_Topeka_and_Santa_Fe_Railway" title="Atchison, Topeka and Santa Fe Railway">Atchison, Topeka and Santa Fe Railway</a> subsidiary, sold in preparation for the merger that produced the <a href="/wiki/BNSF_Railway" title="BNSF Railway">BNSF Railway</a><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>) to form North America's largest gold producer.
</p><p>On June 21, 2000, Newmont announced a merger with Battle Mountain Gold. The merger was completed in January 2001.
</p><p>In February 2002, Newmont completed the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Normandy_Mining_Limited" class="mw-redirect" title="Normandy Mining Limited">Normandy Mining Limited</a> and <a href="/wiki/Franco-Nevada" title="Franco-Nevada">Franco-Nevada Mining Corporation</a> Limited. Newmont faced competition in its bid for Normandy from <a href="/wiki/AngloGold" class="mw-redirect" title="AngloGold">AngloGold</a>. By eventually outbidding the South African company, Newmont became the world's largest gold producer, with an annual production in excess of 8 million ounces.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 2007, the company eliminated its 1.5 million ounce legacy hedge book to make Newmont the world's largest unhedged gold producer. The following year, Newmont acquired Miramar Mining Corporation and its Hope Bay deposit in the Canadian Arctic.
</p><p>In late 2008, Newmont moved its headquarters from Denver to the suburb of <a href="/wiki/Greenwood_Village,_Colorado" title="Greenwood Village, Colorado">Greenwood Village, Colorado</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In 2009, Newmont purchased the remaining one-third interest in Boddington Gold Mine from AngloGold Ashanti, bringing its ownership to 100 percent.
</p><p>In April 2011, the company acquired Canada's Fronteer Gold Inc. for Cdn $2.3 billion. This made the company the world's second-largest gold producer.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, Newmont produced 5.65 million ounces of <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a> at all-in sustaining costs of US$924 per ounce.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The company reported adjusted net income of $780 million for the year,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and further reduced net debt down to US$0.8 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In 2019, it acquired Canada's <a href="/wiki/Goldcorp" title="Goldcorp">Goldcorp</a> for $10 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>

<p>Newmont has purchased and sold a number of operations in recent years:
</p>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Super_Pit_gold_mine" title="Super Pit gold mine">Super Pit gold mine</a></b>: Newmont sold its 50 percent stake in Kalgoorlie Consolidated Gold Mines to Northern Star Resources for $800 million in January 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Golden_Grove_Mine" title="Golden Grove Mine">Golden Grove Mine</a></b>: Owned by <a href="/wiki/Normandy_Mining_Limited" class="mw-redirect" title="Normandy Mining Limited">Normandy Mining Limited</a> since 1991, Golden Grove was acquired by Newmont Australia Ltd in February 2002 when Newmont took over Normandy.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Newmont sold the mine to <a href="/wiki/Oxiana_Limited" title="Oxiana Limited">Oxiana Limited</a> in June 2005 for A$265 million.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pajingo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pajingo (page does not exist)">Pajingo</a></b>: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pajingo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pajingo (page does not exist)">Pajingo</a> (100% owned) is an underground mine located approximately 93 miles (150 kilometers) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Townsville,_Queensland" class="mw-redirect" title="Townsville, Queensland">Townsville, Queensland</a> and 45 miles (72 kilometers) south of the local township of <a href="/wiki/Charters_Towers" title="Charters Towers">Charters Towers</a>. Newmont sold the mine in late 2007; it is now owned by <a href="/wiki/Conquest_Mining" title="Conquest Mining">Conquest Mining</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Bronzewing_Gold_Mine" title="Bronzewing Gold Mine">Bronzewing Gold Mine</a></b>: View Resources purchased the mine in July 2004 from Newmont for A$9.0 million, a package that also included the McClure mining operation, 8 km west of Bronzewing.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Wiluna_Gold_Mine" title="Wiluna Gold Mine">Wiluna Gold Mine</a></b>: Also part of the Normandy acquisition, Gowit Limited, later Agincourt Resources, purchased the mine on 10 December 2003 from Newmont for shares and $3.65 million in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Zarafshan" title="Zarafshan">Zarafshan</a></b>: Newmont was part of a joint venture gold project in Uzbekistan, the first major Western investment in the region since the breakup of the Soviet Union. A difficult place to operate, Uzbekistan expropriates the company's assets in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kori_Kollo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kori Kollo (page does not exist)">Kori Kollo</a></b>:  The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kori_Kollo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kori Kollo (page does not exist)">Kori Kollo</a> open pit mine is on a high plain in northwestern <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a> near <a href="/wiki/Oruro,_Bolivia" title="Oruro, Bolivia">Oruro</a>, on government mining concessions issued to a Bolivian corporation, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Empresa_Minera_Inti_Raymi_S.A.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Empresa Minera Inti Raymi S.A. (page does not exist)">Empresa Minera Inti Raymi S.A.</a> (“Inti Raymi”), in which Newmont had an 88% interest. The remaining  12% was owned by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mrs._Beatriz_Rocabado&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mrs. Beatriz Rocabado (page does not exist)">Mrs. Beatriz Rocabado</a>. <a href="/wiki/Inti_Raymi" title="Inti Raymi">Inti Raymi</a> owned and operated the mine. On July 23, 2009, Newmont announced the transfer of its interest in Empresa Minera Inti Raymi S.A., which owned the Kori Kollo gold mine and Kori Chaca gold mine, to Compania Procesadora de Minerales S.A. ("CPM"), a company controlled by Newmont's long-time Bolivian partner Jose Mercado.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Minahasa" class="mw-redirect" title="Minahasa">Minahasa</a></b>:  Newmont owns 80% of <a href="/wiki/Minahasa" class="mw-redirect" title="Minahasa">Minahasa</a> and the remaining 20% interest is a carried interest held by <a href="/w/index.php?title=P.T._Tanjung_Serapung&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="P.T. Tanjung Serapung (page does not exist)">P.T. Tanjung Serapung</a>, an unrelated Indonesian company. Minahasa is located on the island of <a href="/wiki/Sulawesi" title="Sulawesi">Sulawesi</a>, approximately 1,500 miles (2,414 kilometers) northeast of Jakarta. Mining was completed in late 2001 and gold production was completed in 2004.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Golden_Giant_Mine" title="Golden Giant Mine">Golden Giant</a></b>:  Newmont's Canadian operations previously included two underground mines. <a href="/wiki/Golden_Giant_Mine" title="Golden Giant Mine">Golden Giant</a> (100% owned) was located approximately 25 miles (40 kilometers) east of <a href="/wiki/Marathon,_Ontario" title="Marathon, Ontario">Marathon, Ontario</a>, Canada, and had been in production since 1985. Mining operations at Golden Giant were completed in December 2005 with remnant mining and milling production continuing throughout most of 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holloway_mine&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Holloway mine (page does not exist)">Holloway</a></b>:  Holloway was located approximately 35 miles (56 kilometers) east of <a href="/wiki/Matheson,_Ontario" class="mw-redirect" title="Matheson, Ontario">Matheson, Ontario</a>, and about 400 miles (644 kilometers) northeast of Golden Giant. It was in production since 1996. On November 6, 2006, Newmont completed the sale of the Holloway mine to <a href="/w/index.php?title=St._Andrews_Goldfields_Ltd.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="St. Andrews Goldfields Ltd. (page does not exist)">St. Andrews Goldfields Ltd.</a> resulting in a $13 pre-tax gain.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Empire_Mine_State_Historic_Park" title="Empire Mine State Historic Park">Empire</a>-<a href="/wiki/North_Star_Mine_and_Powerhouse" title="North Star Mine and Powerhouse">Star</a></b>: Located in <a href="/wiki/Grass_Valley,_California" title="Grass Valley, California">Grass Valley, California</a>, the mine has been closed since 1957</li>
<li><b>New Zealand</b> and <b>Indonesia:</b> Newmont completed sale of its Waihi assets in October 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Newmont completed its sale of the Batu Hijau mine to PT Amman Mineral Internasional in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newmont_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Controversies" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">

<p>In August 2004, the Indonesian Ministry of Environment filed a civil lawsuit against Newmont,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> claiming <a href="/wiki/Tailings" title="Tailings">tailings</a> from the company's Minahasa Raya mine <a href="/wiki/Water_pollution" title="Water pollution">polluted</a> <a href="/wiki/Buyat_Bay" title="Buyat Bay">Buyat Bay</a>. The company was cleared by an Indonesian court, with the judge ruling the pollution charges could not be proven.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Akyem.2C_Ghana"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Akyem,_Ghana">Akyem, Ghana</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newmont_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Akyem, Ghana" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, a group of NGOs awarded Newmont with the <a href="/wiki/Public_Eye_on_Davos" title="Public Eye on Davos">Public Eye on Davos</a> award for its Akyem project in Ghana. Newmont said the award was issued based on several paragraphs of text which “clearly were intended to distort the facts”.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p><p>In 2010, Newmont was fined $4.9 million by the Ghanaian Environmental Protection Agency for not preventing, reporting and investigating a cyanide spill at its mine in Ghana in an “appropriate and timely manner”.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Newmont said it would collaborate with the agency to resolve any issues.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
