			<div id="bodyContent" class="content"><div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="mw-parser-output"><section class="mf-section-0" id="mf-section-0"><p><b>TechnipFMC plc</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/ɛ/: 'e' in 'dress'">ɛ</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="'n' in 'nigh'">n</span><span title="/iː/: 'ee' in 'fleece'">iː</span><span title="'p' in 'pie'">p</span><span title="/ɛ/: 'e' in 'dress'">ɛ</span><span title="'f' in 'find'">f</span><span title="/ɛ/: 'e' in 'dress'">ɛ</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/iː/: 'ee' in 'fleece'">iː</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling">Tek-<span style="font-size:90%">NEEP</span>-ef-em-see</i></a>) is a global <a href="/wiki/List_of_oil_exploration_and_production_companies" title="List of oil exploration and production companies">oil and gas</a> company that provides complete project life cycle services for the energy industry. It was ranked 23rd among world's Top 225 International Design Firms in the year 2017 by <a href="/wiki/Engineering_News-Record" title="Engineering News-Record">Engineering News-Record</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The company was formed by the merger of <a href="/wiki/FMC_Technologies" title="FMC Technologies">FMC Technologies</a> of the United States and <a href="/wiki/Technip" title="Technip">Technip</a> of France<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> that was announced in 2016 and completed in 2017. TechnipFMC acts in three distinct segments: <a href="/wiki/Subsea" class="mw-redirect" title="Subsea">subsea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Offshore_construction" title="Offshore construction">offshore</a>/onshore, and surface projects. These projects include offshore <a href="/wiki/Oil_and_gas_exploration" class="mw-redirect" title="Oil and gas exploration">oil and gas exploration</a> and extraction platforms, rigs, crude oil <a href="/wiki/Oil_refinery" title="Oil refinery">refinery</a>, <a href="/wiki/Petrochemical" title="Petrochemical">petrochemical</a> plants such as <a href="/wiki/Ethylene" title="Ethylene">Ethylene</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">Hydrogen</a>, SynGas plants, <a href="/wiki/Naptha" class="mw-redirect" title="Naptha">Naptha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benzene" title="Benzene">Benzene</a> etc. <a href="/wiki/Plastics" class="mw-redirect" title="Plastics">plastics</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Rubber" class="mw-redirect" title="Rubber">rubber</a> industry, <a href="/wiki/Fertiliser" class="mw-redirect" title="Fertiliser">fertiliser</a> plant, onshore as well as floating <a href="/wiki/LNG" class="mw-redirect" title="LNG">LNG</a> plants. The company is headquartered in London, and has major operations in Houston and Paris where its predecessor companies were headquartered. It has about 37,000 employees from 126 nationalities and operates in 48 countries. TechnipFMC stock is listed on the <a href="/wiki/NYSE" class="mw-redirect" title="NYSE">NYSE</a> and <a href="/wiki/Euronext" title="Euronext">Euronext</a> Paris exchange, and is a component of the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500_Index" title="S&amp;P 500 Index">S&amp;P 500</a>, the <a href="/wiki/CAC_40" title="CAC 40">CAC 40</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Dow_Jones_Sustainability_Indices" title="Dow Jones Sustainability Indices">Dow Jones Sustainability Index</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>. </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=TechnipFMC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>TechnipFMC was formed through the merger of FMC Technologies Inc. and French oil-services Technip SA. On January 17, 2017, TechnipFMC announced that it is operating as a unified company after completion of the merger, which created a significant new player in an energy industry wracked by a nearly two-year slump in crude prices. The company has three <a href="/wiki/Headquarters" title="Headquarters">headquarters</a> in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, and <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>. The CEO is Doug Pferdehirt and the executive chairman is Thierry Pilenko.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
TechnipFMC also provides consulting and technology business through two its subsidiaries, PT and <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.genesisoilandgas.com/">Genesis</a>.
Through 2016-2017 the TechnipFMC fleet expanded with the addition of four <a href="/wiki/Pipe-laying_ship" title="Pipe-laying ship">pipe-laying vessels</a> (PLSVs) constructed under the DOF-Technip partnership, two foreign (Skandi Açu and Skandi Buzios) and two Brazilian (Skandi Olinda and Skandi Recife).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Another ship who entered operations was the Deep Explorer, one of the most advanced <a href="/wiki/Diving_support_vessel" title="Diving support vessel">diving support vessels</a> (DSV) in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In August 2019, Doug Pferdehirt announces that TechnipFMC will be split into two independent engineering companies. The separation is expected to be completed by the end of the first semester 2020. The former <a href="/wiki/Technip" title="Technip">Technip</a> entity, without the subsea business, will be renamed Technip Energies.
</p><p><br></p>
