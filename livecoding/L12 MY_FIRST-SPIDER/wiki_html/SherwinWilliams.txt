<p><b>Sherwin-Williams Company</b> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> company in the general <a href="/wiki/Building_material" title="Building material">building materials</a> industry. The company, with headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Cleveland" title="Cleveland">Cleveland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>, primarily engages in the manufacture, distribution, and sale of <a href="/wiki/Paint" title="Paint">paints</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coating" title="Coating">coatings</a> and related products to professional, industrial, commercial, and retail customers primarily in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> and Europe. The company is mostly known through its <b>Sherwin-Williams Paints</b> line. Its consumer brands group manufactures products under such trade names as Dutch Boy, Krylon, Dupli-Color, VHT (a division of Dupli-Color), Minwax, Thompson’s WaterSeal, Pratt &amp; Lambert, White Lightning, Purdy and Kool Seal.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherwin-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Founded by <a href="/wiki/Henry_Sherwin" title="Henry Sherwin">Henry Sherwin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Edward_Williams_(businessman)" title="Edward Williams (businessman)">Edward Williams</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> in Cleveland in 1866, the company operates through four segments: The Americas Group (known as <i> 'Sherwin-Williams Stores'</i>), Consumer Brands Group, Latin America Coatings Group, and Performance Coatings Group.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Sherwin-Williams_Paints">Sherwin-Williams Paints</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherwin-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sherwin-Williams Paints" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sherwin-Williams_Paints_in_Gillette,_Wyoming.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Sherwin-Williams_Paints_in_Gillette%2C_Wyoming.jpg/220px-Sherwin-Williams_Paints_in_Gillette%2C_Wyoming.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="5184" data-file-height="3456"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 147px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Sherwin-Williams_Paints_in_Gillette%2C_Wyoming.jpg/220px-Sherwin-Williams_Paints_in_Gillette%2C_Wyoming.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="147" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sherwin-Williams_Paints_in_Gillette,_Wyoming.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A Sherwin-Williams Paints in <a href="/wiki/Gillette,_Wyoming" title="Gillette, Wyoming">Gillette, Wyoming</a></div></div></div>
<p>Americas Group, known as <b>Sherwin-Williams Paints</b>, was the first section of the company to be established, in 1866. These stores market and sell Sherwin-Williams branded architectural paints and coatings, industrial and marine products, and <a href="/wiki/Original_Equipment_Manufacturer" class="mw-redirect" title="Original Equipment Manufacturer">original equipment manufacturer</a> product finishes and similar items. As of January 1, 2011, the Paint Stores Group operated 3,954 individual paint stores. By January 1, 2016, the Paints stores group had expanded to 4,080 stores.
</p><p>The Consumer Brands Group develops, manufactures, and distributes various paints, coatings, and related products, under the brand names of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Duron_Paint&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Duron Paint (page does not exist)">Duron</a>, Frazee, <a href="/wiki/MAB_Paints" title="MAB Paints">MAB</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minwax" title="Minwax">Minwax</a>, Geocel, Kool Seal, Krylon, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mautz_Paint_Co.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mautz Paint Co. (page does not exist)">Mautz</a>, <a href="http://encyc.org/wiki/Purdy" class="extiw" title="encyc:Purdy">Purdy</a>, Bestt Liebco, Thompson's WaterSeal, H&amp;C, Pratt &amp; Lambert, Uniflex, White Lightning, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin-Senour_Paint&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Martin-Senour Paint (page does not exist)">Martin-Senour</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Boy_Paint" title="Dutch Boy Paint">Dutch Boy</a> and anthony angelillo to third party customers in addition to overseeing the operations maintained by The Americas Group.
</p><p>On August 28, 2007, Sherwin Williams purchased Columbia Paint &amp; Coatings.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> On July 6, 2011, Sherwin-Williams acquired Leighs Paints, based in Bolton UK, manufacturers of intumescent and high performance industrial coatings.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In late 2012, Sherwin Williams began the process of purchasing the <a href="/wiki/Comex_Group" title="Comex Group">Comex Group</a>.  Comex was the 4th largest paint manufacturer in North America.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> After Mexican antitrust regulators voted against the deal twice, Sherwin-Williams bought Comex's US and Canadian divisions for $165 million on September 16, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> PPG, US-based paint and coating company, acquired Comex's Mexican division for $2.3 billion.
</p><p>In 2007, the company introduced its first "Idea Center" in <a href="/wiki/Shaker_Heights,_Ohio" title="Shaker Heights, Ohio">Shaker Heights, Ohio</a>, in an effort to provide customers with an <a href="/wiki/Interior_design" title="Interior design">interior design</a> environment as contrasted with its conventional paint stores.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Sherwin-Williams_Automotive_Finishes">Sherwin-Williams Automotive Finishes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherwin-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sherwin-Williams Automotive Finishes" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The <b>Sherwin-Williams Automotive Finishes</b> segment develops, manufactures, and distributes various <a href="/wiki/Motor_vehicle" title="Motor vehicle">motor vehicle</a> finish, refinish, and touch-up products. It also licenses certain technology and trade names worldwide, as well as distributes Sherwin-Williams branded automotive finish and refinish products through its network of automotive branches. The Automotive Finishes segment opened their 200th automotive branch in Calumet Park, IL on September 21, 2007. The International Coatings segment develops, licenses, manufactures, and distributes various paints, coatings, and related products worldwide through 76 company-operated specialty paint stores, as of the above date, and by outside selling functions to other dealers and distributors.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Sherwin-Williams_Spray_Source">Sherwin-Williams Spray Source</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherwin-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sherwin-Williams Spray Source" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The <b>Sherwin-Williams Spray Source</b> service centers offer new equipment, parts and accessories, maintenance and repairs to rental programs. There are over 100 service centers located across the U.S. and Puerto Rico. They have factory-trained technicians that provide maintenance and repairs on paint sprayers, pressure (cleaners) washers, conventional spray guns, HVLP (High Volume Low Pressure) systems, plural-component equipment from manufacturers like EXEL North America, Kremlin Rexson, Sames, Johnstone, Graco, Titan/Speeflo, Spraytech/Wagner, Capspray, ITW Binks, AccuSpray, Mi-T-M, Pressure Pro, and more. They provide repairs like maintenance, emergency and warranty, telephone troubleshooting support, on-site demonstrations, and training. Prior to 2008, the program was called SherWorks.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Sherwin-Williams_Mexico">Sherwin-Williams Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherwin-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sherwin-Williams Mexico" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1929, <b>Sherwin-Williams Mexico</b> started operations. Headquarters is in Mexico City.
</p><p>Sherwin-Williams Mexico has 109 branches and 200 dealers.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="STIR_magazine">STIR magazine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherwin-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: STIR magazine" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><i>STIR</i> magazine is a bi-annual publication delivering color news and resources to residential and commercial interior designers and architects throughout North America. The magazine is created and published by <a href="/wiki/Hanley_Wood,_LLC" class="mw-redirect" title="Hanley Wood, LLC">Hanley Wood, LLC</a>. The first issue was published in 2004.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Gray_Drug">Gray Drug</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherwin-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Gray Drug" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>

</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Valspar">Valspar</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherwin-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Valspar" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In March 2016, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Valspar" title="Valspar">Valspar</a> would be <a href="/wiki/Mergers_and_acquisitions" title="Mergers and acquisitions">acquired</a> for $9.3 billion <a href="/wiki/U.S._dollar" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. dollar">U.S. dollars</a>. The merger was finalized 1 June 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Commitment_to_Cleveland">Commitment to Cleveland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherwin-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Commitment to Cleveland" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In September, 2019, after years of rumor and speculation, the company announced that it was moving its global headquarters - then in downtown Cleveland - to a larger, more expandable, high-technology-friendly site. It would consider remaining in Cleveland, but was evaluating sites in the region and out of state. 
</p><p>After five months, in February 2020, Sherwin-Williams declared it would remain in the city of Cleveland, which would provide hundreds of jobs for the region as well as an estimated $600 million investment in a new one-million-square-feet HQ building that will become a worksite for 3,500 employees. The company is also building a new 500,000 sq. ft. research and development center on the site of a former VA hospital in <a href="/wiki/Brecksville,_OH" class="mw-redirect" title="Brecksville, OH">Brecksville, OH</a>, saying a suburban, less densely populated location would work better with its research and coating development processes. 
<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>The first step in the procedure took place in March 2020 when the company paid $49.5 million for property currently used as parking lots in downtown Cleveland in an apparent cash transaction. The HQ building is expected to open in 2023.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_controversies">Recent controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherwin-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Recent controversies" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Sherwin-Williams, an American company primarily engaged in the manufacture, distribution, and sale of paints, coatings and related products has been subject to several recent and significant controversies. There has been increasing scrutiny from both the public and governmental agencies, such as the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission">SEC</a>. The issues have ranged widely from cancer-causing toxins contaminating new residential properties to financial fraud.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Lead_paint_lawsuit">Lead paint lawsuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherwin-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Lead paint lawsuit" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Lead_paint" title="Lead paint">Lead paint</a></div>
<p>In January 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Santa_Clara_County_Superior_Court" title="Santa Clara County Superior Court">Santa Clara County Superior Court</a> ruled that Sherwin-Williams, <a href="/wiki/NL_Industries" title="NL Industries">NL Industries</a> and <a href="/wiki/ConAgra_Foods" class="mw-redirect" title="ConAgra Foods">ConAgra</a> were <a href="/wiki/Joint_and_several_liability" title="Joint and several liability">jointly and severally</a> liable for $1.15 billion, to be paid into a lead paint abatement fund to be used to remove lead paint from older housing.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-businessweek_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessweek-14">[14]</a></sup> The judge ruled that the paint companies manufactured, marketed, and sold lead paint without disclosing the health risks to the consumers in spite of "actual and constructive knowledge that it was harmful."<sup id="cite_ref-huffington_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huffington-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>  In March 2014 Sherwin-Williams was denied a new trial.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p><p>
As per their 2013 annual report, Sherwin-Williams considers this case without merit and will appeal the decision. The process may take another two years.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The disposition of <i>The People v. ConAgra Grocery Products Company et al.</i> in the California 6th Appellate District Court on November 14, 2017 is that <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886047036">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style></p><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>... the judgment is reversed, and the matter is remanded to the trial court with directions to (1) recalculate the amount of the abatement fund to limit it to the amount necessary to cover the cost of remediating pre-1951 homes, and (2) hold an evidentiary hearing regarding the appointment of a suitable receiver. The Plaintiff shall recover its costs on appeal.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>On February 14, 2019 the California Supreme Court denied the defendants' request for review, sending the case back to the trial court as ordered by the Appellate Court.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Attempted_diversions_and_national_boycotts">Attempted diversions and national boycotts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherwin-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Attempted diversions and national boycotts" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On April 9, 2018, Milwaukee Mayor <a href="/wiki/Tom_Barrett_(Wisconsin_politician)" title="Tom Barrett (Wisconsin politician)">Tom Barrett</a> and representing attorneys uncovered that Sherwin-Williams tried to ”shift the blame to contaminated water in an effort” to avoid tens of millions of dollars in settlements.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> As many as 170 children have been affected by the potentially fatal lead poisoning.
</p><p>On June 28, 2018 <a href="/wiki/Consumer_Watchdog" title="Consumer Watchdog">Consumer Watchdog</a> declared a national boycott of paint from Sherwin-Williams “stores after the paint maker said it was going forward with a deceptive California ballot measure to erase its liability to the public for poisoning families with lead in its paint.”<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Sherwin-Williams has allegedly been evading accountability resulting in severe damage to its company brand.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Charity_marketing_ploy">Charity marketing ploy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherwin-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Charity marketing ploy" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2014, a charity promotion was created indicating that $1 million would be contributed to the Angels Baseball Foundation “any time an Angels batter parked a home run ball in the can. Well, left-fielder Justin Upton did just that last Tuesday, September 19, 2017 in the ninth inning. That’s when it transpired that there was a catch all along: The ball had to enter the can on the fly. Upton’s homer ball bounced at least once before caroming into the can. So, no contribution.”<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The organization has been nationally criticized for the marketing ploy and the ethics of the organization have been put into question.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2017_federal_class_action_lawsuits">2017 federal class action lawsuits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherwin-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2017 federal class action lawsuits" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Superdeck_lawsuit">Superdeck lawsuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherwin-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Superdeck lawsuit" data-section="15" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In late 2017, a lawsuit was filed against Sherwin-Williams, claiming it was responsible for eighty of its major coating products for being defective. Customers have repeatedly claimed that the quality of the products have failed a few months after applying the coating by peeling and chipping away.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Attorneys are now examining all other products for similar faults.
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Lawsuit_for_cancer_cluster_in_residential_homes">Lawsuit for cancer cluster in residential homes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherwin-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Lawsuit for cancer cluster in residential homes" data-section="16" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>On August 22, 2017 a federal “class action lawsuit was filed in the New Jersey Federal Court” against Sherwin-Williams. Residents are expecting the court “to hold Sherwin-Williams accountable for the contamination that is causing residents, including children, to develop cancer and other serious medical conditions.”<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The residents concern is that contamination has now migrated to surrounding residences and businesses within Gibbsboro, causing a cancer cluster within the town. In August, 2018, the lawsuit was tossed by a federal judge due to the claims of the suit being too broad.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2018_financial_fraud">2018 financial fraud</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherwin-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: 2018 financial fraud" data-section="17" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>According to the Wall Street Journal, in June 2018 a credit rating agency analyst with insider information regarding the then-unannounced acquisition of Valspar by Sherwin-Williams, and two acquaintances were arrested and charged “with securities fraud and conspiracy and face additional civil violations in the continuing investigation.”<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The investigation includes information that the men used inside information before the deal was announced. Financial contracts, transactions and firms associated with the organization may face increased scrutiny as a result.  No Sherwin-Williams employees were connected to the crime.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_recognition">Awards and recognition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherwin-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Awards and recognition" data-section="18" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p><i>STIR</i> magazine has won several industry awards, including a 2005 FOLIO award for best design,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> new custom publication, as well as a 2007 Pearl Award from the Custom Publishing Council for the best in custom Publishing category.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p><p>On June 20, 2011, <i><a href="/wiki/Computerworld" title="Computerworld">Computerworld</a></i> named The Sherwin-Williams Company the #58 Best Place to Work in IT.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p><p>In 2015, Sherwin-Williams was recognized as the most used brand as well as the winner for brand familiarity and quality rating in the Paints category by the Builder magazine.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>As of 2018, Sherwin-Williams was ranked 190th on the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> list of the largest United States corporations by revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Competition">Competition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherwin-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Competition" data-section="19" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>Sherwin-Williams' major competitors are <a href="/wiki/PPG_Industries" title="PPG Industries">PPG</a>, <a href="/wiki/RPM_International_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="RPM International Inc.">RPM International Inc.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Axalta_Coating_Systems" title="Axalta Coating Systems">Axalta Coating Systems</a>, <a href="/wiki/BASF" title="BASF">BASF</a> (through <a href="/wiki/Ace_Hardware" title="Ace Hardware">Ace Hardware</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lowe%27s" title="Lowe's">Lowe's</a>), <a href="/wiki/Behr_(paint)" title="Behr (paint)">Behr</a>, (through <a href="/wiki/The_Home_Depot" title="The Home Depot">The Home Depot</a>), Kelly-Moore Paints, and <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Moore_%26_Co." title="Benjamin Moore &amp; Co.">Benjamin Moore</a>. There are also several smaller, regional competitors, though Sherwin-Williams has acquired a few of them (such as MAB and Columbia Paint &amp; Coatings and Parker Paints) in recent years.  Valspar was acquired in June 2017.
</p>
