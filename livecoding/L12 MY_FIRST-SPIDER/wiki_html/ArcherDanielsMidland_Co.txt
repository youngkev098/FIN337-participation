</p>
<p>The <b>Archer Daniels Midland Company</b> (<b>ADM</b>) is an American global <a href="/wiki/Food_processing" title="Food processing">food processing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Commodities_trading" class="mw-redirect" title="Commodities trading">commodities trading</a> corporation, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The company operates more than 270 plants and 420 crop procurement facilities worldwide, where <a href="/wiki/Cereal" title="Cereal">cereal</a> grains and <a href="/wiki/Oilseed" class="mw-redirect" title="Oilseed">oilseeds</a> are processed into products used in <a href="/wiki/Food" title="Food">food</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beverage" class="mw-redirect" title="Beverage">beverage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nutraceutical" title="Nutraceutical">nutraceutical</a>, <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">industrial</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Animal" title="Animal">animal</a> <a href="/wiki/Fodder" title="Fodder">feed</a> markets worldwide.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Products">Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archer_Daniels_Midland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Products" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Products include <a href="/wiki/Vegetable_oil" title="Vegetable oil">oils</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flour" title="Flour">meal</a> from <a href="/wiki/Soybean" title="Soybean">soybeans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cottonseed</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sunflower" class="mw-redirect" title="Sunflower">sunflower</a> seeds, <a href="/wiki/Canola" class="mw-redirect" title="Canola">canola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peanut" title="Peanut">peanuts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flaxseed" class="mw-redirect" title="Flaxseed">flaxseed</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palm_kernel" title="Palm kernel">Palm kernel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Diacylglycerol_oil" title="Diacylglycerol oil">DAG oil</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Cereal_germ" title="Cereal germ">corn germ</a>, corn gluten feed pellets, <a href="/wiki/Syrup" title="Syrup">syrup</a>, <a href="/wiki/Starch" title="Starch">starch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glucose" title="Glucose">glucose</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dextrose" class="mw-redirect" title="Dextrose">dextrose</a>, crystalline dextrose, <a href="/wiki/High_fructose_corn_syrup" class="mw-redirect" title="High fructose corn syrup">high fructose corn syrup</a> <a href="/wiki/Sugar_substitute" title="Sugar substitute">sweeteners</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cocoa_liquor" class="mw-redirect" title="Cocoa liquor">cocoa liquor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cocoa_powder" class="mw-redirect" title="Cocoa powder">cocoa powder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cocoa_butter" title="Cocoa butter">cocoa butter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chocolate" title="Chocolate">chocolate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ethanol" title="Ethanol">ethanol</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wheat" title="Wheat">wheat</a> <a href="/wiki/Flour" title="Flour">flour</a>. End uses are consumption by people, <a href="/wiki/Livestock" title="Livestock">livestock</a>, and additives for <a href="/wiki/Fuel" title="Fuel">fuel</a>.
</p><p>Long known as a food and ingredients company, it has also invested in fuel production. ADM nearly doubled capital spending in its 2007 budget to an estimated $1.12 billion. The increase is planned for bioenergy projects, focusing on <a href="/wiki/Bioethanol" class="mw-redirect" title="Bioethanol">bioethanol</a> and <a href="/wiki/Biodiesel" title="Biodiesel">biodiesel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Oilseeds_processing">Oilseeds processing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archer_Daniels_Midland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Oilseeds processing" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The Oilseeds Processing segment includes global activities related to the origination, merchandising, crushing, and further processing of oilseeds such as soybeans and soft seeds (cottonseed, sunflower seed, canola, and flaxseed) into vegetable oils and protein meals.<sup id="cite_ref-sec.gov_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sec.gov-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Corn_processing">Corn processing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archer_Daniels_Midland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Corn processing" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>ADM's Corn Processing segment is engaged in corn wet milling and dry milling activities, with its asset base primarily located in the central part of the United States.  The Corn Processing segment converts corn into sweeteners and starches, and bioproducts.  Its products include ingredients used in the food and beverage industry including sweeteners, starch, syrup, glucose (dextrose).  Dextrose and starch are used by the Corn Processing segment as feedstocks for its bioproducts operations.<sup id="cite_ref-sec.gov_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sec.gov-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Agricultural_services">Agricultural services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archer_Daniels_Midland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Agricultural services" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>ADM's Agricultural Services segment uses its U.S. grain elevator, global transportation network, and port operations to buy, store, clean, and transport agricultural commodities, such as oilseeds, corn, wheat, milo, oats, rice, and barley, and resells these commodities primarily as food and feed ingredients and as raw materials for the agricultural processing industry.<sup id="cite_ref-sec.gov_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sec.gov-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Investor_services">Investor services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archer_Daniels_Midland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Investor services" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>ADM Investor Services, Inc. is a registered futures commission merchant and a clearing member of all principal commodities exchanges in the U.S.  ADM Investor Services International, Ltd., a member of commodity exchanges and clearing houses in Europe, and ADMIS Hong Kong Limited, offer broker services in Europe and Asia.<sup id="cite_ref-sec.gov_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sec.gov-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Insurance_services">Insurance services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archer_Daniels_Midland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Insurance services" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>

</p><p>ADM's Crop Risk Services is a managing general agent which sells and services crop insurance policies to farmers.<sup id="cite_ref-sec.gov_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sec.gov-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Sherman_Antitrust_Law_Violation">Sherman Antitrust Law Violation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archer_Daniels_Midland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Sherman Antitrust Law Violation" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1920 the US Department of Justice brought suit against the <a href="/wiki/National_Linseed_Oil_Trust" title="National Linseed Oil Trust">National Linseed Oil Trust</a> for violating the Sherman Antitrust Act. Several co-defendants were named, including the  Archer-Daniels Manufacturing Company. The suit alleged all of these companies were acting in <a href="/wiki/Collusion" title="Collusion">collusion</a> to raise prices, citing a spike in linseed oil costs between 1916 and 1918, when the price rose from $.50 per gallon to $1.80.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Price_fixing">Price fixing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archer_Daniels_Midland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Price fixing" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Lysine_price-fixing_conspiracy" title="Lysine price-fixing conspiracy">Lysine price-fixing conspiracy</a></div>
<p>In 1993, the company was the subject of a lysine price-fixing investigation by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Justice" title="United States Department of Justice">U.S. Justice Department</a>. Senior ADM executives were indicted on criminal charges for engaging in <a href="/wiki/Price_fixing" title="Price fixing">price-fixing</a> within the international <a href="/wiki/Lysine" title="Lysine">lysine</a> market. Three of ADM's top officials, including vice chairman Michael Andreas were eventually sentenced to federal prison in 1999. Moreover, in 1997, the company was fined $100 million, the largest antitrust fine in U.S. history at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mark_Whitacre" title="Mark Whitacre">Mark Whitacre</a>, <a href="/wiki/FBI" class="mw-redirect" title="FBI">FBI</a> informant and whistleblower of the lysine price-fixing conspiracy, would also find himself in legal trouble for <a href="/wiki/Embezzling" class="mw-redirect" title="Embezzling">embezzling</a> money from ADM during his time as an informant for the FBI. In addition, according to ADM's 2005 annual report, a settlement was reached under which ADM paid $400 million in 2005 to settle a class action antitrust suit.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Informant_(book)" title="The Informant (book)">The Informant</a></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Nonfiction" title="Nonfiction">nonfiction</a> <a href="/wiki/Thriller_(genre)" title="Thriller (genre)">thriller</a> book <sup id="cite_ref-Webber_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Webber-25">[25]</a></sup> that documents the mid-1990s <a href="/wiki/Lysine_price-fixing_conspiracy" title="Lysine price-fixing conspiracy">lysine price-fixing conspiracy</a> case and the involvement of ADM executive <a href="/wiki/Mark_Whitacre" title="Mark Whitacre">Mark Whitacre</a>. The book was adapted into the 2009 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Informant!" title="The Informant!">The Informant!</a>. </i>This case has also been featured on episode 168 of <a href="/wiki/This_American_Life" title="This American Life">This American Life</a> called The Fix Is In.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Tax_dodging">Tax dodging</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archer_Daniels_Midland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Tax dodging" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>A noteworthy case of <a href="/wiki/Transfer_mispricing" title="Transfer mispricing">transfer mispricing</a> came to light in 2011 in Argentina involving the world's four largest grain traders: ADM, <a href="/wiki/Bunge_Limited" title="Bunge Limited">Bunge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cargill" title="Cargill">Cargill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Louis_Dreyfus_Company" title="Louis Dreyfus Company">LDC</a>. Argentina's revenue and customs service began an investigation into the four companies when prices for agricultural commodities spiked in 2008 and yet very little profit for the four companies had been reported to the office. As a result of the investigation, it was alleged that the companies had submitted false declarations of sales and routed profits through tax havens or through their headquarters. In some cases, they were said to have used phantom firms to buy grain and had inflated costs in Argentina in order to reduce the recorded profits earned in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> According to the country's revenue and customs service, the outstanding taxes amounted to almost US$1 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The companies involved have denied the allegations. To date, the Argentinian tax authorities have not replied to the <a href="/wiki/Public_Eye_(organization)" title="Public Eye (organization)">Swiss NGO Public Eye</a>’s request regarding the current state of the case.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In its 2018 annual report to the US Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC), Bunge mentioned provisions which suggest that the case is still ongoing: “[A]s of December 31, 2018, Bunge's Argentine subsidiary had received income tax assessments relating to 2006 through 2009 of approximately 1,276 million Argentine pesos (approximately $34 million), plus applicable interest on the outstanding amount of approximately 4,246 million Argentine pesos (approximately $113 million<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup>).”<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Corruption">Corruption</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archer_Daniels_Midland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Corruption" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The Swiss <a href="/wiki/Public_Eye_(organization)" title="Public Eye (organization)">NGO Public Eye</a> has recently elaborated on a corruption case involving grain giant ADM.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> An ADM subsidiary pleaded guilty and agreed to pay criminal fines in excess of USD 17 million in 2013 to resolve charges that it had paid bribes, via vendors, to Ukrainian government officials in order to obtain value-added tax refunds.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In a parallel action, the grain trader consented to a judgment that ordered ADM to pay close to US$37 million in "disgorgement and pre-judgement interest", which brought the total amount of penalties to more than US$54 million.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Entanglement with government officials can even go a step further. In November 2017, in the wake of the revelations dubbed the Paradise Papers about dubious offshore schemes, the French TV broadcast Cash Investigation<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> reported on the problematic dealings of LDC in Brazil. In 2010, the Geneva-based trader joined forces with a subsidiary of the world's biggest soy producer <a href="/wiki/Amaggi_Group" title="Amaggi Group">Amaggi</a> to form Amaggi &amp; LD Commodities Ltda. Amaggi is owned by Blairo Maggi, former Minister of Agriculture and a large landowner known as the “king of soy” who was Governor of the state of Mato Grosso when the joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Louis_Dreyfus_Company" title="Louis Dreyfus Company">LDC</a> was established. Amaggi &amp; LD Commodities Ltda opened a trust based in the Cayman Islands the same year. The beneficial owners of the trust were all members of the Maggi family. Blairo Maggi himself has claimed never to have received any money from the trust. But allegations against him should have raised a red flag: Maggi was under investigation by the Brazilian judiciary for corruption and money laundering for his time as Governor of Mato Grosso. The administration under Maggi “is suspected of having enforced a scheme of monthly bribes paid to state lawmakers in exchange for political support”.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Brazilian prosecutors filed charges against Blairo Maggi in May 2018, accusing him of orchestrating a bribery scheme in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Louis_Dreyfus_Company" title="Louis Dreyfus Company">LDC</a> thus knowingly relied on an individual classified as a PEP for its business activities in Brazil.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> When it set up the joint venture in 2010, Blairo Maggi had an important role in government, which he had already mixed with his private-sector activities, creating clear conflicts of interest.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Violation_of_the_Foreign_Corrupt_Practices_Act">Violation of the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archer_Daniels_Midland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Violation of the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On December 20, 2013, the SEC announced that it had charged ADM for failing to prevent illicit payments (<a href="/wiki/Bribery" title="Bribery">bribes</a>) made by its foreign subsidiaries to Ukrainian government officials in violation of the <a href="/wiki/Foreign_Corrupt_Practices_Act" title="Foreign Corrupt Practices Act">FCPA</a>. ADM agreed to pay more than $36 million to settle the SEC's charges.<sup id="cite_ref-SEC_Charges_Archer-Daniels-Midland_Company_with_FCPA_Violations_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEC_Charges_Archer-Daniels-Midland_Company_with_FCPA_Violations-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_record">Environmental record</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archer_Daniels_Midland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Environmental record" data-section="15" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Gran_Chaco#Conservation_issues" title="Gran Chaco">Gran Chaco § Conservation issues</a></div>
<p>The company has been the subject of several major federal lawsuits related to <a href="/wiki/Air_pollution" title="Air pollution">air pollution</a>. In 2001, it agreed to pay a $1.46 million fine for violating federal and Illinois clean-air regulations at its Decatur feed plant and to spend $1.6 million to reduce air pollution there.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> 
In 2003, the company settled federal air pollution complaints related to its efforts to avoid <a href="/wiki/New_Source_Review" title="New Source Review">New Source Review</a> provisions of the <a href="/wiki/Clean_Air_Act_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="Clean Air Act (United States)">Clean Air Act</a> that require pollution control upgrades when a plant is modernized. The company paid $4.5 million in penalties and more than $6 million to support environmental projects. In addition, ADM agreed to eliminate more than 60,000 tons of emissions of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_monoxide" title="Carbon monoxide">carbon monoxide</a>, <a href="/wiki/Particulate_matter" class="mw-redirect" title="Particulate matter">particulate matter</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Organic_volatile_chemicals&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Organic volatile chemicals (page does not exist)">organic volatile chemicals</a> and other pollutants from 42 plants in 17 states at a cost of hundreds of millions of dollars.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p><p>ADM is involved in a joint project with <a href="/wiki/Daimler_AG" title="Daimler AG">Daimler AG</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bayer_CropScience" class="mw-redirect" title="Bayer CropScience">Bayer CropScience</a> to develop <a href="/wiki/Jatropha" title="Jatropha">jatropha</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Biofuel" title="Biofuel">biofuel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p><p>In an attempt to reduce its <a href="/wiki/Carbon_footprint" title="Carbon footprint">carbon footprint</a>, the company has partnered with the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Midwest_Geological_Sequestration_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Midwest Geological Sequestration Association (page does not exist)">Midwest Geological Sequestration Association</a> and other organizations to test the <a href="/wiki/Carbon_sequestration" title="Carbon sequestration">disposal of carbon dioxide emissions underground</a>.  If testing is successful, beginning in late 2010 the company expected to dispose of 1,000 metric tons per day of carbon dioxide emissions currently being released to the atmosphere.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Agricultural_subsidies">Agricultural subsidies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archer_Daniels_Midland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Agricultural subsidies" data-section="16" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The company <a href="/wiki/Lobbying" title="Lobbying">lobbies</a> for <a href="/wiki/Agricultural_subsidy" title="Agricultural subsidy">agricultural subsidies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Price_support" title="Price support">price supports</a> including sugar and ethanol. According to a 1995 report by the <a href="/wiki/Libertarianism" title="Libertarianism">libertarian</a> <a href="/wiki/Think_tank" title="Think tank">think tank</a> <a href="/wiki/Cato_Institute" title="Cato Institute">Cato Institute</a>", ADM has cost the American economy billions of dollars since 1980 and has indirectly cost Americans tens of billions of dollars in higher prices and higher taxes over that same period. At least 43 percent of ADM's annual profits are from products heavily subsidized or protected by the American government. Moreover, every $1 of profits earned by ADM's corn sweetener operation costs consumers $10, and every $1 of profits earned by its bioethanol operation costs taxpayers $30."<sup id="cite_ref-cato_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cato-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
