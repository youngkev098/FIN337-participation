</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=AvalonBay_Communities&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company was formed by the 1998 merger between Avalon Properties Inc. and Bay Apartment Communities Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-merger_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-merger-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The merged company owned 40,506 apartment units.<sup id="cite_ref-merger_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-merger-3">[3]</a></sup> Avalon Properties was formed in 1993 as a spinoff from <a href="/wiki/Trammell_Crow_Company" title="Trammell Crow Company">Trammell Crow Company</a> and was led by Richard Michaux, as Chairman and CEO and Chuck Berman, President.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Bay Apartment Communities was formed in 1994 from the apartment business established by Mike Meyer in 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2001, Bryce Blair was named CEO, and the additional role of Chairman in January 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-ceo_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ceo-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 2003, the company sold $450 million in assets and reinvested the proceeds in its development pipeline.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In 2007, the company was added to the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500_Index" title="S&amp;P 500 Index">S&amp;P 500 Index</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In 2011, Timothy J. Naughton was named CEO, and the additional role of chairman in January 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-ceo_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ceo-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>On February 27, 2013, AvalonBay Communities and <a href="/wiki/Equity_Residential" title="Equity Residential">Equity Residential</a> closed a $9 billion deal to acquire <a href="/wiki/Archstone" title="Archstone">Archstone</a> from <a href="/wiki/Lehman_Brothers" title="Lehman Brothers">Lehman Brothers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, a lawsuit was filed by former tenants of Avalon at Edgewater, an AvalonBay community in <a href="/wiki/Edgewater,_New_Jersey" title="Edgewater, New Jersey">Edgewater, New Jersey</a>, who allege that AvalonBay breached its duty of care by allegedly violating fire standards and safety procedures following a five-alarm fire at the community in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
