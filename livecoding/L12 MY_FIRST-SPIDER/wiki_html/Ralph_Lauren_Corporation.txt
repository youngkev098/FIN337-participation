<p><b>Ralph Lauren Corporation</b> is an American fashion company producing products ranging from the mid-range to the luxury segments. They are known for the clothing, marketing and distribution of products in four categories: apparel, home, accessories, and fragrances. The Company's brands include the mid-range Chaps brand, to the sub-premium Lauren Ralph Lauren brand, to the premium Polo Ralph Lauren, Double RL, Ralph Lauren Childrenswear, Denim &amp; Supply Ralph Lauren, and Club Monaco brands, up to the full luxury Ralph Lauren Purple Label and Ralph Lauren Collection brands. Ralph Lauren Corporation is an American, publicly traded holding company headquartered in New York City, and founded in 1967 by American <a href="/wiki/Fashion_designer" class="mw-redirect" title="Fashion designer">fashion designer</a> <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Lauren" title="Ralph Lauren">Ralph Lauren</a>.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ralph_Lauren_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:90px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Polo_Ralph_Lauren_logo.png" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/9/9a/Polo_Ralph_Lauren_logo.png" decoding="async" width="88" height="184" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="88" data-file-height="184"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 88px;height: 184px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/9/9a/Polo_Ralph_Lauren_logo.png" data-alt="" data-width="88" data-height="184" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Polo_Ralph_Lauren_logo.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Polo Ralph Lauren – the flagship brand of the company.</div></div></div>
<p>The Ralph Lauren Corporation started in 1967 with men's ties. At 28 years-old, Lauren worked for the tie manufacturer, Beau Brummell. He persuaded the company's president to let him start his own line.  Drawing on his interests in sports, Lauren named his first full line of menswear ‘Polo’ in 1968. He worked out of a single "drawer" from a showroom in the <a href="/wiki/Empire_State_Building" title="Empire State Building">Empire State Building</a> and made deliveries to stores himself.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>By 1969, the <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> department store <a href="/wiki/Bloomingdale%27s" title="Bloomingdale's">Bloomingdale's</a> sold Lauren's men line exclusively. It was the first time that Bloomingdale's had given a designer his own in-store shop. In 1971, Ralph Lauren Corporation launched a line of tailored shirts for women, which introduced the Polo player emblem, appearing on the shirt cuff. The first full women's collection was launched the following year.<sup id="cite_ref-Ralph_Lauren_Through_the_Years_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ralph_Lauren_Through_the_Years-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>1972 marked the opening of Ralph Lauren's store on Rodeo Drive in <a href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills,_California" title="Beverly Hills, California">Beverly Hills, California</a>, his first freestanding store.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>. In 1972, Lauren released a short-sleeve cotton shirt in 24 colors. This design, emblazoned with the company's famed logo—that of a polo player, created by tennis pro <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Lacoste" title="René Lacoste">René Lacoste</a>—became the brand’s signature look. In 1977 Ralph Lauren Corporation introduced a signature cotton mesh <a href="/wiki/Polo_shirt" title="Polo shirt">polo shirt</a> in various colours, featuring the polo player logo on the chest.
</p><p>In 1974, Ralph Lauren outfitted the male cast of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Great_Gatsby" title="The Great Gatsby">The Great Gatsby</a></i> in costumes chosen from his Polo line - a 1920s-style series of men's suits and sweaters, except for the pink suit which Lauren designed especially for <a href="/wiki/Robert_Redford" title="Robert Redford">Robert Redford</a>’s <i><a href="/wiki/Jay_Gatsby" title="Jay Gatsby">Jay Gatsby</a></i>. In 1977, <a href="/wiki/Diane_Keaton" title="Diane Keaton">Diane Keaton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Woody_Allen" title="Woody Allen">Woody Allen</a> wore Lauren's clothes in the Oscar-winning film, <i><a href="/wiki/Annie_Hall" title="Annie Hall">Annie Hall</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>In 1978, the first Ralph Lauren fragrances, produced by Warner-Lauren, Ltd were launched at Bloomingdale's. Lauren for women, and Polo the men's cologne. This was the first time that a designer introduced two fragrances – one for men and one for women – simultaneously.<sup id="cite_ref-Ralph_Lauren_Through_the_Years_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ralph_Lauren_Through_the_Years-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>The company entered the European market, and went international, in 1981 with the opening of the first freestanding store in <a href="/wiki/Bond_Street" title="Bond Street">New Bond Street</a> in the <a href="/wiki/West_End_of_London" title="West End of London">West End of London</a>, England.  Lauren opened his first flagship in the Rhinelander mansion, on Madison Avenue and 72nd Street in New York City in 1986.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On June 12, 1997, the company becomes a publicly traded company on the New York Stock Exchange.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>The 98-seat restaurant, RL, opened in March 1999 in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a> adjacent to its largest and world flagship Ralph Lauren store at the corner of Chicago and Michigan Avenues on the <a href="/wiki/Magnificent_Mile" title="Magnificent Mile">Magnificent Mile</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> It was followed by the opening of two additional restaurants – Ralph's at 173 Boulevard Saint Germain Paris store in 2010 and The Polo Bar at Polo's store in New York in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>The company launched its website and online shop in 2000 as polo.com by RL Media (a cooperation between Ralph Lauren and NBC). In 2007, Ralph Lauren Corporation acquired the NBC share of RL Media and the website was relaunched as ralphlauren.com. In September 2015, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Larsson_(businessman)" title="Stefan Larsson (businessman)">Stefan Larsson</a> would replace the company's founder, Ralph Lauren, as CEO in November. Lauren stayed on as executive chairman and chief creative officer.<sup id="cite_ref-NYtimes_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYtimes-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2017 it was announced that Larsson had agreed to leave his position as CEO effective May 1, 2017 due to differences with Lauren.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> On May 17, 2017, Ralph Lauren named <a href="/wiki/Patrice_Louvet" title="Patrice Louvet">Patrice Louvet</a> President and <a href="/wiki/Chief_Executive_Officer" class="mw-redirect" title="Chief Executive Officer">Chief Executive Officer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ft-badkar_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ft-badkar-15">[15]</a></sup> Louvet most recently served as Group President, Global Beauty at <a href="/wiki/Procter_%26_Gamble" title="Procter &amp; Gamble">Procter &amp; Gamble</a> (P&amp;G).<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He took over on July 17, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Brands">Brands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ralph_Lauren_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Brands" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ralph_Lauren_wool_scarf.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Ralph_Lauren_wool_scarf.jpg/220px-Ralph_Lauren_wool_scarf.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Ralph_Lauren_wool_scarf.jpg/220px-Ralph_Lauren_wool_scarf.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ralph_Lauren_wool_scarf.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A woolen Polo Ralph Lauren scarf</div></div></div>
<ul><li><b>Ralph Lauren Women's Collection and Ralph Lauren Purple Label:</b> Ralph Lauren Collection for women, launched in 1971, ranges from handmade evening gowns to sportswear. Launched in 1994, Ralph Lauren Purple Label<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> for men offers suiting, custom tailored made-to-measure suits and sportswear, as well as benchmade footwear and made-to-order dress furnishings, accessories, and luggage.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Ralph Lauren Watches and Fine Jewelry:</b> In 2009 Ralph Lauren, together with luxury group Compagnie Financière Richemont SA, launched a collection of timepieces through the Ralph Lauren Watch &amp; Jewelry Co. In 2010, the Ralph Lauren Watch &amp; Jewelry Co. also introduced collections of jewelry.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Ralph by Ralph Lauren:</b> Launched in 1994, Ralph by Ralph Lauren offers suit separates, sport coats, vests, and topcoats.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Polo Ralph Lauren:</b> Men’s Polo, Ralph Lauren’s first complete line of sportswear and tailored clothing launched in 1967. In 2014, Women’s Polo was launched.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Polo Sport:</b> Polo Sport launched in 1992, a line of activewear for sports and fitness. In 2014, Ralph Lauren debuted the PoloTech Shirt, which featured smart fabric technology that supposedly "captures robust biometrics from the wearer".<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Double RL:</b> Founded in 1993 and named after Ralph Lauren and his wife Ricky's “RRL” ranch in Colorado, RRL offers men a mix of selvage denim, vintage apparel, sportswear and accessories, with roots in workwear and military gear.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Lauren Ralph Lauren:</b> Lauren for Women launched in 1996, offering sportswear, denim, dresses, activewear, and accessories and footwear at a cheaper price point. Lauren for Men offers men's tailored clothing, including suits, sport coats, dress shirts, dress pants, tuxedos, topcoats, and ties at a cheaper price point.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> This brand generally slots above Chaps in price, but below Polo Ralph Lauren.</li>
<li><b>Polo Golf</b> and <b>RLX Golf:</b> Polo Golf launched in 1990 and RLX Golf launched in 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Pink Pony:</b> Established in 2000, a percentage of sales from all Pink Pony products benefit the Pink Pony Fund and other major cancer charities around the world. Pink Pony primarily consists of  women's sportswear and accessories. All Pink Pony items feature a pink Polo Player.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Polo Ralph Lauren Children:</b> Items include polo knit shirts and cashmere cable sweaters.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Denim &amp; Supply Ralph Lauren:</b> The Denim &amp; Supply Ralph Lauren line launched in 2011, inspired by the warehouse and artist communities of <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York and authentic style found in the music festival scene.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Denim &amp; Supply was discontinued in September 2016.</li>
<li><b>Chaps:</b> A mid-range brand featuring casual sportswear, workday, and dresses.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup>  The Chaps brand is available primarily at <a href="/wiki/Kohl%27s" title="Kohl's">Kohl's</a> and <a href="/wiki/Belk" title="Belk">Belk</a> stores, and is priced to compete with <a href="/wiki/PVH_(company)" title="PVH (company)">PVH</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Izod" title="Izod">Izod</a> brand.</li>
<li><b>Club Monaco:</b> Club Monaco designs and markets its own clothing and accessories for men and women.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>American Living:</b> Ralph Lauren launched American Living for men and women in 2008, a mid-range lifestyle brand created exclusively for <a href="/wiki/JCPenney" class="mw-redirect" title="JCPenney">JCPenney</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> This line was comparable to the Chaps line, but was marketed as a more exclusive line, unlike Chaps which is sold at multiple retailers. This line was discontinued in 2012 due to poor sales.  A different American Living line is now sold at <a href="/wiki/Macy%27s" title="Macy's">Macy's</a>; the current American Living line only includes women's clothing.</li>
<li><b>Ralph Lauren Home and Paint:</b> Ralph Lauren Home, the first complete home collection from an American clothing designer, makes its debut in 1983 with home furnishings and accessories. Ralph Lauren Home includes furniture, bed and bath linens, china, crystal, silver, decorative accessories and gifts, as well as lighting, fabric, wall covering, and floor covering. Ralph Lauren launched Paint in 1995, now with over 400 palettes.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Fragrance:</b> In 1978, Ralph Lauren launched his first fragrances: Lauren for women and Polo for men. Originally produced by Warner-Lauren, Ltd, <a href="/wiki/L%27Or%C3%A9al" title="L'Oréal">L’Oréal</a> now produces the Ralph Lauren Fragrances for men and women, including World of Polo (Polo, Polo Blue, Polo Black, Polo Red), Ralph Lauren Romance, Midnight Romance and the Big Pony Collections For Women and For Men.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Ralph Lauren Restaurants:</b> RL Restaurant Chicago opened in 1999, adjacent to its largest Ralph Lauren flagship store in the world on Michigan Avenue along the Magnificent Mile. In 2010, Ralph's was opened in the courtyard and converted stables at 173 Boulevard Saint Germain Paris flagship store. In August 2014, Ralph's Coffee opened on the second floor of the Polo Flagship store in New York City. The Polo Bar, adjacent to the New York City Polo Flagship store, opened in January 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Stores">Stores</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ralph_Lauren_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Stores" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">

<p>The Company ended Fiscal 2016 with 493 directly operated stores: 144 Ralph Lauren stores, 77 Club Monaco stores and 272 Polo factory stores. The Company also operated 583 concession shop locations worldwide at the end of the year.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>

<p>In addition to Company-operated locations, international licensing partners operated 93 Ralph Lauren stores and 42 dedicated shops, as well as 133 <a href="/wiki/Club_Monaco" title="Club Monaco">Club Monaco</a> stores and shops at the end of Fiscal 2016.
</p><p>Ralph Lauren operates its representative flagship stores in New York City on <a href="/wiki/Madison_Avenue" title="Madison Avenue">Madison Avenue</a> – for menswear in the former <a href="/wiki/Rhinelander_Mansion" class="mw-redirect" title="Rhinelander Mansion">Rhinelander Mansion</a>, and for womenswear and home in another structure, across the street, which opened in 2010. The company also manages flagships, for retailing Ralph Lauren collections, in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manhasset,_New_York" title="Manhasset, New York">Manhasset</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greenwich,_Connecticut" title="Greenwich, Connecticut">Greenwich</a> (USA), <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports_sponsorships">Sports sponsorships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ralph_Lauren_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sports sponsorships" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="USTA">USTA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ralph_Lauren_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: USTA" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2005, The United States Tennis Association selects Ralph Lauren Corporation as the official apparel sponsor for the <a href="/wiki/US_Open_(tennis)" title="US Open (tennis)">U.S. Open</a>. As part of the partnership, all on-court ball persons and officials will be dressed in specially-designed Ralph Lauren apparel. This is Polo's first tennis sponsorship.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Wimbledon">Wimbledon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ralph_Lauren_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Wimbledon" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2006, Ralph Lauren Corporation became the official outfitter of  <a href="/wiki/The_Championships,_Wimbledon" title="The Championships, Wimbledon">Wimbledon</a>. Lauren is the first designer in the tennis tournament’s history to be chosen to create uniforms for all on-court officials.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="U.S._Olympic_Team">U.S. Olympic Team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ralph_Lauren_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: U.S. Olympic Team" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Ralph Lauren Corporation is the exclusive Official Parade Outfitter for the U.S. Olympic and Paralympic Teams, with the right to manufacture, distribute, advertise, promote, and sell products in the U.S. which replicate the Parade Outfits and associated leisure wear. The Company has established a partnership with athletes serving as brand ambassadors and as the faces of the advertising, marketing, and public relations campaigns.<sup id="cite_ref-Dodes_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dodes-43">[43]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TorStar_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TorStar-44">[44]</a></sup></p><p>Ralph Lauren Corporation partners with the United States Olympic Committee to become an Official Outfitter of the U.S. Olympic Team, for the 2008 Summer Olympics in Beijing, 2010 Winter Olympics in Vancouver, 2012 Summer Olympics in London, 2014 Winter Olympics in Sochi and 2016 Summer Olympics in Rio.<sup id="cite_ref-Pells_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pells-45">[45]</a></sup></p><p>Ralph Lauren designs the official Opening Ceremony and Closing Ceremony parade outfits for the U.S. teams in addition to an assortment of village-wear apparel and accessories.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p><p>Previously, the Corporation received negative press when it was found to have sourced the clothing it supplied to the 2012 athletes from China,<sup id="cite_ref-NYLauren_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYLauren-47">[47]</a></sup> so it vowed to source everything it produced for the 2014 Olympics from the US. <a href="/wiki/Kraemer_Textiles_Inc." title="Kraemer Textiles Inc.">Kraemer Textiles Inc.</a> spun around 6,000 pounds<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NazUS_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NazUS-49">[49]</a></sup> of <a href="/wiki/Merino" title="Merino">Merino</a> wool yarn from Imperial Stock Ranch in Oregon, which was then sent to Longview Yarns in North Carolina to be dyed. The clothing assembly was completed by Ball of Cotton in California. Ultimately, 40 American vendors were involved with production.<sup id="cite_ref-USAthreads_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USAthreads-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Philanthropy">Philanthropy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ralph_Lauren_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Philanthropy" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<p>In 1989, it co-Founded the Nina Hyde Center for Breast Cancer Research at <a href="/wiki/Georgetown_University_Hospital" class="mw-redirect" title="Georgetown University Hospital">Georgetown University Hospital</a> in <a href="/wiki/Washington_D.C." class="mw-redirect" title="Washington D.C.">Washington D.C.</a> in memory of the late Post fashion correspondent.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p><p>In 1994, Ralph Lauren acted as chairman and creates the name and symbol for Fashion Targets Breast Cancer, a charitable initiative of the CFDA that marshals the goodwill and services of the fashion industry to raise public awareness and funds for breast cancer internationally.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p><p>In 2000, Ralph Lauren Corporation launched its Volunteer Program, which energises employees and creates meaningful connections with the communities in which they work.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p><p>On September 15, 2000, Ralph Lauren Corporation launched the Pink Pony Campaign, a national initiative to reduce disparities in cancer care by raising awareness as well as enhancing prevention, screening, and treatment in poor and underserved communities.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p><p>In 2001, the Polo Ralph Lauren Foundation established the American Heroes Fund following September 11 to allow Polo's 10,000 employees worldwide, as well as their customers, the opportunity to participate in the relief effort.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p><p>In 2003, Ralph Lauren established the Ralph Lauren Center for Cancer Care and Prevention in Harlem. The Center is a collaboration between Ralph Lauren, Memorial Sloan-Kettering, and North General Hospital in Harlem, New York City.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p><p>In 2004, the Polo Fashion School was established, in which company executives work with inner-city youth to offer insights into the fashion business.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p><p>Established in 2006, the Polo Jeans G.I.V.E. (Get Involved Volunteer Exceed) campaign was created to inspire and encourage community service through volunteerism by supporting the efforts of dedicated volunteers and their causes.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p><p>In 2008, the Star-Spangled Banner, the original 1813 flag that inspired Francis Scott Key to write the National Anthem, was preserved by a $10 million contribution to Save America's Treasures from Polo Ralph Lauren in 1998. The flag was then unveiled on Wednesday, November 19, 2008 in a new gallery at the Smithsonian National Museum of American History, in Washington D.C.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2013, Ralph Lauren Corporation announced its commitment to restore the elite <a href="/wiki/%C3%89cole_des_Beaux-Arts" title="École des Beaux-Arts">École des Beaux-Arts</a> in Paris, one of the most influential art schools in France.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p><p>In 2014, Ralph Lauren Corporation partnered with <a href="/wiki/The_Royal_Marsden_NHS_Foundation_Trust" title="The Royal Marsden NHS Foundation Trust">The Royal Marsden NHS Foundation Trust</a>, the largest and most comprehensive cancer center in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, to develop a world-class breast cancer research facility. In 2016, Ralph Lauren Corporation opened the Royal Marsden Ralph Lauren Center for Breast Cancer Research.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2020, Ralph Lauren Corporation donated $10m and started making isolation gowns and medical masks to support the COVID-19 fight.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Levi_Strauss_lawsuit">Levi Strauss lawsuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ralph_Lauren_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Levi Strauss lawsuit" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Levi_Strauss_%26_Co." title="Levi Strauss &amp; Co.">Levi Strauss &amp; Co.</a> filed a lawsuit against <a href="/wiki/Abercrombie_%26_Fitch" title="Abercrombie &amp; Fitch">Abercrombie &amp; Fitch</a> and the RL Corporation in July 2007 for <a href="/wiki/Trademark_infringement" title="Trademark infringement">trademark infringement</a>. It alleged that the separate retailers used Levi's trademarked pocket design of connected arches in the design of some of their respective products.<sup id="cite_ref-levi_63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-levi-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="South_African_Polo_trademark_issues">South African Polo trademark issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ralph_Lauren_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: South African Polo trademark issues" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The Polo brand sold in <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> is not affiliated with the Ralph Lauren <a href="/wiki/Brand" title="Brand">brand</a>. An independent South African company <a href="/wiki/Trademark" title="Trademark">trademarked</a> the Polo name and logo in South Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-Wheeldon_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wheeldon-64">[64]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Power_65-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Power-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Filippa_Hamilton_photo_controversy">Filippa Hamilton photo controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ralph_Lauren_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Filippa Hamilton photo controversy" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, Ralph Lauren apologized for digitally retouching a photograph of model <a href="/wiki/Filippa_Hamilton" title="Filippa Hamilton">Filippa Hamilton</a> to make her look thinner.<sup id="cite_ref-Mail1_66-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mail1-66">[66]</a></sup> Hamilton also claims that she was fired by Ralph Lauren a few days later.<sup id="cite_ref-Huff_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Huff-67">[67]</a></sup> Another model's photo was also digitally retouched<sup id="cite_ref-Mail2_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mail2-68">[68]</a></sup> adding to the row of models.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Accused_of_intellectual_property_violation_by_Cowichan_Tribes">Accused of intellectual property violation by Cowichan Tribes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ralph_Lauren_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Accused of intellectual property violation by Cowichan Tribes" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>After branding the sale of sweaters online as 'Cowichan', CTV News reported that Cowichan Tribes would take "steps to communicate with Ralph Lauren and ensure that our product and name is protected".<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup>A petition through <a href="/wiki/Change.org" title="Change.org">Change.org</a> was set up to encourage Ralph Lauren to take action. This resulted in the product line being taken out of circulation and all mention of the name was removed from the Ralph Lauren website; there have been no further complaints from the Cowichan Tribe since.
</p>
