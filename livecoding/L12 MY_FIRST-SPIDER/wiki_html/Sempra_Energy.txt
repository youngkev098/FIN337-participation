</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sempra_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Sempra Energy was created through the 1998 merger of <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>-based Pacific Enterprises, the parent company of <a href="/wiki/SoCalGas" class="mw-redirect" title="SoCalGas">SoCalGas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Enova_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Enova Corporation">Enova Corporation</a>, the parent company of <a href="/wiki/SDG%26E" class="mw-redirect" title="SDG&amp;E">SDG&amp;E</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In 1999, the company acquired two utilities in South America; Chilquinta Energia in Chile and Luz Del Sur in Peru, which gave Sempra Energy a platform for growth in the rapidly expanding Latin American energy market.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In 2003, Sempra Energy Resources, the former power generation subsidiary of Sempra Energy, completed three state-of-the-art power plant projects in Arizona, California and New Mexico to meet the growing energy needs of the Pacific Southwest.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>Sempra was sued over claims it manipulated natural gas supplies and electricity contracts during the 2001 <a href="/wiki/California_electricity_crisis" title="California electricity crisis">California electricity crisis</a>. In 2006, the company agreed to pay $377 million to settle gas supply claims, and in 2010 another $410 million to settle claims on electricity <a href="/wiki/Price_gouging" title="Price gouging">price gouging</a>, but has never admitted wrongdoing.<sup id="cite_ref-apsettlement_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-apsettlement-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>Sempra Energy Foundation was created by Sempra Energy in 2007 as a 501(c)(3) private foundation. The foundation matches employee's charitable contributions. In 2018, Sempra Energy Foundation matched $1.2 million.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>On March 8, 2018, regulators in <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> approved Sempra Energy's purchase of a majority stake in <a href="/wiki/Oncor" class="mw-redirect" title="Oncor">Oncor</a> for $9.45 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj-billion_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-billion-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>On January 16, 2019, Sempra Energy was added to the <a href="/wiki/Dow_Jones_Utility_Average" title="Dow Jones Utility Average">Dow Jones Utility Average</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Gas_and_Electric" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Gas and Electric">Pacific Gas and Electric</a>.
</p><p>Sempra Energy and <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Aramco" title="Saudi Aramco">Aramco</a> signed a deal to supply LNG for <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a> in May 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2019, Sempra Energy said it would sell its Chilean businesses to China’s State Grid International Development Ltd for $2.23 billion in cash as part of its plan to exit South America and shore up capital.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<ul><li><b>Sempra LNG:</b> Sempra LNG develops, builds and invests in <a href="/wiki/Liquefied_natural_gas" title="Liquefied natural gas">liquefied natural gas</a> facilities and natural gas infrastructure in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>. Cameron LNG, a joint-venture LNG facility under construction in <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>, is expected to be one of the first U.S. liquefaction-export projects to commence operations in 2019. The company is also developing the proposed Port Arthur LNG liquefaction project in Texas and the proposed liquefaction-export facilities at the Energia Costa Azul receipt terminal in <a href="/wiki/Ensenada,_Baja_California" title="Ensenada, Baja California">Ensenada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Southern_California_Gas_Company" title="Southern California Gas Company">Southern California Gas Company</a>:</b> SoCalGas, based in Los Angeles, is the largest natural gas distribution utility in the U.S., providing natural gas service to approximately 21.9 million consumers.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>IEnova:</b> IEnova develops, builds and operates energy infrastructure in Mexico, and is one of the largest private energy companies in the country.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Gas_%26_Electric" title="San Diego Gas &amp; Electric">San Diego Gas &amp; Electric</a>:</b> SDG&amp;E is an electric and natural gas utility that provides energy to approximately 3.7 million consumers in San Diego and southern Orange Counties.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Oncor_Electric_Delivery" title="Oncor Electric Delivery">Oncor Electric Delivery Company LLC</a>:</b> Oncor, based in Dallas, operates the largest distribution and transmission system in the state, providing service to approximately 10 million Texans. Sempra Energy indirectly owns approximately 80 percent of Oncor. In May 2019, Oncor completed the acquisition of InfraREIT, an electricity transmission company in Central, North and West <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
<li>Sempra Energy also operates electric utilities in <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li></ul><h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Former">Former</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sempra_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Former" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>The following subsidiaries have been reformed into Sempra LNG &amp; Midstream and Sempra Renewables:<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<ul><li><b>Sempra U.S. Gas &amp; Power:</b> Sempra U.S. Gas &amp; Power develops clean power solutions<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Use_plain_English#Buzzwords" title="Wikipedia:Use plain English"><span title="The material near this tag may use buzzwords designed solely to impress you or obscure meaning. (September 2019)">buzzword</span></a></i>]</sup> in markets throughout the U.S. with a focus on zero and low-emission fuels. With its affiliates and joint-venture partners, the company owns and operates more than 2,000 megawatts of renewable generating capacity. Sempra U.S. Gas &amp; Power also operates natural gas storage facilities, pipelines and distribution utilities.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li><b>Sempra Generation:</b> Operates or owns interest in <a href="/wiki/Power_station" title="Power station">power stations</a> in five U.S. states and in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, as well as property for potential <a href="/wiki/Solar_power" title="Solar power">solar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wind_power" title="Wind power">wind</a> electric generation.<sup id="cite_ref-200910k13_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-200910k13-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Sempra Pipelines &amp; Storage:</b> Owns natural gas storage facilities in Alabama and Louisiana and interests in two natural gas companies in Argentina, Chilquinta Energía of Chile, and <a href="/wiki/Luz_del_Sur" title="Luz del Sur">Luz del Sur</a> of Peru. It also owns 1,858 miles of distribution pipelines, 216 miles of transmission pipelines, and two compressor stations in Mexico.<sup id="cite_ref-200910k13_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-200910k13-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Sempra LNG:</b> Develops, owns and operates receipt terminals for importing <a href="/wiki/Liquefied_natural_gas" title="Liquefied natural gas">liquefied natural gas</a> to the U.S., including the Energía Costa Azul LNG terminal in <a href="/wiki/Baja_California" title="Baja California">Baja California</a> and the Cameron LNG terminal in <a href="/wiki/Hackberry,_Louisiana" title="Hackberry, Louisiana">Hackberry, Louisiana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-200910k13_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-200910k13-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Sempra Commodities:</b> Sempra's stake in a partnership formed on April 1, 2008 to market and trade natural gas, natural gas liquids, power, petroleum and petroleum products, coal, emissions, ethanol and base metals.<sup id="cite_ref-200910k13_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-200910k13-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Royal_Bank_of_Scotland_Group" title="Royal Bank of Scotland Group">Royal Bank of Scotland Group</a> sold its stake in RBS Sempra Commodities LLC to <a href="/wiki/Noble_Group" title="Noble Group">Noble Americas Gas and Power</a>, as a condition of the <a href="/wiki/HM_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="HM Government">UK Government</a>'s 74 percent stake in the Group on December 1, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-bbco_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbco-17">[17]</a></sup></li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_recognition">Awards and recognition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sempra_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Awards and recognition" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
