For their social media website, see <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a>.</div>

<p><b>Facebook, Inc.</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a> and technology company based in Menlo Park, California. It was founded by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Zuckerberg" title="Mark Zuckerberg">Mark Zuckerberg</a>, along with fellow <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College" title="Harvard College">Harvard College</a> students and roommates <a href="/wiki/Eduardo_Saverin" title="Eduardo Saverin">Eduardo Saverin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andrew_McCollum" title="Andrew McCollum">Andrew McCollum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dustin_Moskovitz" title="Dustin Moskovitz">Dustin Moskovitz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Hughes" title="Chris Hughes">Chris Hughes</a>, originally as TheFacebook.com—today's <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a>, a popular global social networking website. Facebook is one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_public_corporations_by_market_capitalization" title="List of public corporations by market capitalization">world's most valuable companies</a>. It is considered one of the <a href="/wiki/Big_Five_(technology_companies)" class="mw-redirect" title="Big Five (technology companies)">Big Five</a> technology companies along with <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amazon_(company)" title="Amazon (company)">Amazon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Facebook,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Facebook" title="History of Facebook">History of Facebook</a> and <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering_of_Facebook" title="Initial public offering of Facebook">Initial public offering of Facebook</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Facebook_on_Nasdaq.jpeg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Facebook_on_Nasdaq.jpeg/220px-Facebook_on_Nasdaq.jpeg" decoding="async" width="220" height="176" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="592" data-file-height="473"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 176px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Facebook_on_Nasdaq.jpeg/220px-Facebook_on_Nasdaq.jpeg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="176" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Facebook_on_Nasdaq.jpeg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Billboard on the <a href="/wiki/Thomson_Reuters" title="Thomson Reuters">Thomson Reuters</a> building welcomes Facebook to NASDAQ, 2012</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Chart_of_Facebook,_inc._Stock.png" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Chart_of_Facebook%2C_inc._Stock.png/220px-Chart_of_Facebook%2C_inc._Stock.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="150" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1407" data-file-height="957"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 150px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Chart_of_Facebook%2C_inc._Stock.png/220px-Chart_of_Facebook%2C_inc._Stock.png" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="150" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Chart_of_Facebook,_inc._Stock.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Chart of Facebook's stock</div></div></div>
<p>Facebook filed for an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> (IPO) on February 1, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The preliminary prospectus stated that the company was seeking to raise $5 billion. The document announced that the company had 845 million active monthly users and its website featured 2.7 billion daily likes and comments.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> After the IPO, Zuckerberg would retain a 22% ownership share in Facebook and would own 57% of the voting shares.<sup id="cite_ref-fortune_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fortune-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p><a href="/wiki/Underwriter" class="mw-redirect" title="Underwriter">Underwriters</a> valued the shares at $38 each, pricing the company at $104 billion, the largest valuation to date for a newly public company.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On May 16, one day before the IPO, Facebook announced that it would sell 25% more shares than originally planned due to high demand.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The IPO raised $16 billion, making it the third largest in U.S. history (just ahead of <a href="/wiki/AT%26T_Wireless" class="mw-redirect" title="AT&amp;T Wireless">AT&amp;T Wireless</a> and behind only <a href="/wiki/General_Motors" title="General Motors">General Motors</a> and <a href="/wiki/Visa_Inc." title="Visa Inc.">Visa</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-NYT517_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT517-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USN517_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USN517-13">[13]</a></sup> The stock price left the company with a higher <a href="/wiki/Market_capitalization" title="Market capitalization">market capitalization</a> than all but a few U.S. corporations – surpassing heavyweights such as <a href="/wiki/Amazon_(company)" title="Amazon (company)">Amazon</a>, <a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s" title="McDonald's">McDonald's</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">Disney</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kraft_Foods" title="Kraft Foods">Kraft Foods</a> – and made Zuckerberg's stock worth $19 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT517_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT517-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USN517_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USN517-13">[13]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> stated that the offering overcame questions about Facebook's difficulties in attracting advertisers to transform the company into a "must-own stock". <a href="/wiki/James_B._Lee,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="James B. Lee, Jr.">Jimmy Lee</a> of <a href="/wiki/JPMorgan_Chase" title="JPMorgan Chase">JPMorgan Chase</a> described it as "the next great blue-chip".<sup id="cite_ref-NYT517_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT517-12">[12]</a></sup> Writers at <a href="/wiki/TechCrunch" title="TechCrunch">TechCrunch</a>, on the other hand, expressed skepticism, stating, "That's a big multiple to live up to, and [Facebook] will likely need to add bold new revenue streams to justify the mammoth valuation".<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>Trading in the stock, which began on May 18, was delayed that day due to technical problems with the <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" class="mw-redirect" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a> exchange.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The stock struggled to stay above the IPO price for most of the day, forcing underwriters to buy back shares to support the price.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> At closing bell, shares were valued at $38.23,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> only $0.23 above the IPO price and down $3.82 from the opening bell value. The opening was widely described by the financial press as a disappointment.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
The stock nonetheless set a new record for trading volume of an IPO.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On May 25, 2012, the stock ended its first full week of trading at $31.91, a 16.5% decline.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>On May 22, 2012, regulators from <a href="/wiki/Wall_Street" title="Wall Street">Wall Street</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Financial_Industry_Regulatory_Authority" title="Financial Industry Regulatory Authority">Financial Industry Regulatory Authority</a> announced that they had begun to investigate whether banks underwriting Facebook had improperly shared information only with select clients, rather than the general public. <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> Secretary of State <a href="/wiki/William_F._Galvin" title="William F. Galvin">William Galvin</a> subpoenaed <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Stanley" title="Morgan Stanley">Morgan Stanley</a> over the same issue.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The allegations sparked "fury" among some investors and led to the immediate filing of several lawsuits, one of them a <a href="/wiki/Class_action" title="Class action">class action</a> suit claiming more than $2.5 billion in losses due to the IPO.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_L.P." title="Bloomberg L.P.">Bloomberg</a> estimated that <a href="/wiki/Retail_investor" class="mw-redirect" title="Retail investor">retail investors</a> may have lost approximately $630 million on Facebook stock since its debut.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p><p><a href="/wiki/Standard_%26_Poor%27s" title="Standard &amp; Poor's">Standard &amp; Poor's</a> added Facebook, Inc. to its <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a> index on December 21, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>On May 2, 2014, Zuckerberg announced that the company would be changing its internal motto from "Move fast and break things" to "Move fast with stable infrastructure".<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The earlier motto had been described as Zuckerberg's "prime directive to his developers and team" in a 2009 interview in <i><a href="/wiki/Business_Insider" title="Business Insider">Business Insider</a></i>, in which he also said "Unless you are breaking stuff, you are not moving fast enough."<sup id="cite_ref-blodget_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blodget-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>In May 2019, Facebook founded <a href="/wiki/Libra_(cryptocurrency)" class="mw-redirect" title="Libra (cryptocurrency)">Libra Networks</a>, reportedly in order to develop their own <a href="/wiki/Stablecoin" title="Stablecoin">stablecoin</a> <a href="/wiki/Cryptocurrency" title="Cryptocurrency">cryptocurrency</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-yahoo_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo-28">[28]</a></sup> In recent developments it has been reported that Libra is being supported by financial companies like Visa, Mastercard, PayPal and Uber. The consortium of companies is expected to pool in $10 million each to fund the launch of the cryptocurrency coin named Libra.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Mergers_and_acquisitions">Mergers and acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Facebook,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mergers and acquisitions" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For a more comprehensive list, see <a href="/wiki/List_of_mergers_and_acquisitions_by_Facebook" title="List of mergers and acquisitions by Facebook">List of mergers and acquisitions by Facebook</a>.</div>
<p>Throughout its existence, Facebook has acquired multiple companies (often identified as <a href="/wiki/Talent_acquisition" class="mw-redirect" title="Talent acquisition">talent acquisitions</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>One of its first major acquisitions was in April 2012, when Facebook acquired <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> for approximately US$1 billion in cash and stock.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2013, Facebook acquired <a href="/wiki/Onavo" title="Onavo">Onavo</a>, an Israeli <a href="/wiki/Mobile_web_analytics" title="Mobile web analytics">mobile web analytics</a> company.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2014, Facebook announced that it would be buying mobile messaging company <a href="/wiki/WhatsApp" title="WhatsApp">WhatsApp</a> for US$19 billion in cash and stock.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Later that year, Facebook bought <a href="/wiki/Oculus_VR" title="Oculus VR">Oculus VR</a> for $2.3 billion in stock and cash,<sup id="cite_ref-Kotaku_FB_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kotaku_FB-36">[36]</a></sup> which released its first consumer virtual reality headset in 2016.
</p><p>In late July 2019, the company announced it was under <a href="/wiki/Antitrust" class="mw-redirect" title="Antitrust">antitrust</a> investigation by the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Trade_Commission" title="Federal Trade Commission">Federal Trade Commission</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p><p>In late November 2019, Facebook announced the acquisition of game developer Beat Games, responsible for developing one of the year's most popular VR titles, <i>Beat Saber</i>. <sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Lobbying">Lobbying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Facebook,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Lobbying" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In 2019 Facebook spent $16.7m on lobbying and had a team of 71 lobbyists, up from $12.6m and 51 lobbyists in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Management">Management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Facebook,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Management" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Facebook's key management personnel consists of:<sup id="cite_ref-fb_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Mark_Zuckerberg" title="Mark Zuckerberg">Mark Zuckerberg</a> (Chairman and Chief Executive Officer)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sheryl_Sandberg" title="Sheryl Sandberg">Sheryl Sandberg</a> (Chief Operating Officer)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mike_Schroepfer" title="Mike Schroepfer">Mike Schroepfer</a> (Chief Technology Officer)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/David_Wehner" title="David Wehner">David Wehner</a> (Chief Financial Officer)</li></ul><p>As of September 30, 2019<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Facebook,_Inc.&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, Facebook had 43,030 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Board_of_directors">Board of directors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Facebook,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Board of directors" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In April 2019, Facebook nominated <a href="/w/index.php?title=Peggy_Alford&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Peggy Alford (page does not exist)">Peggy Alford</a> to be added as a board member during the May 2019 AGM. If this happens, she will become the first African-American woman to serve in this board, and the second African-American ever to do so.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> As of April 2019, Facebook's <a href="/wiki/Board_of_directors" title="Board of directors">board</a> consists of the following directors;<sup id="cite_ref-fb_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Mark_Zuckerberg" title="Mark Zuckerberg">Mark Zuckerberg</a> (Chairman, Founder and CEO)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sheryl_Sandberg" title="Sheryl Sandberg">Sheryl Sandberg</a> (Executive Director and COO)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Marc_Andreessen" title="Marc Andreessen">Marc Andreessen</a> (Non-Executive Director, Co-Founder and General Partner, <a href="/wiki/Andreessen_Horowitz" title="Andreessen Horowitz">Andreessen Horowitz</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Erskine_Bowles" title="Erskine Bowles">Erskine Bowles</a> (Non-Executive Director, President Emeritus, <a href="/wiki/University_of_North_Carolina" title="University of North Carolina">University of North Carolina</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Chenault" title="Kenneth Chenault">Kenneth Chenault</a> (Non-Executive Director, Chairman and Managing Director, <a href="/wiki/General_Catalyst" title="General Catalyst">General Catalyst</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Susan_Desmond-Hellmann" class="mw-redirect" title="Susan Desmond-Hellmann">Susan Desmond-Hellmann</a> (Non-Executive Director, CEO, <a href="/wiki/Bill_%26_Melinda_Gates_Foundation" title="Bill &amp; Melinda Gates Foundation">Bill &amp; Melinda Gates Foundation</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Reed_Hastings" title="Reed Hastings">Reed Hastings</a> (Non-Executive Director, Chairman, Co-Founder and CEO, <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Peter_Thiel" title="Peter Thiel">Peter Thiel</a> (Non-Executive Director, Co-Founder and Former CEO, <a href="/wiki/PayPal" title="PayPal">PayPal</a>, Founder and President, <a href="/wiki/Clarium_Capital" title="Clarium Capital">Clarium Capital</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Zients" title="Jeffrey Zients">Jeffrey Zients</a> (Non-Executive Director, Former Director, <a href="/wiki/National_Economic_Council_(United_States)" title="National Economic Council (United States)">U.S. National Economic Council</a>)</li></ul><h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Company_governance">Company governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Facebook,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Company governance" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Early Facebook investor and former Zuckerberg mentor <a href="/wiki/Roger_McNamee" title="Roger McNamee">Roger McNamee</a> described Facebook as having "the most centralized decision-making structure I have ever encountered in a large company."<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Schneider" title="Nathan Schneider">Nathan Schneider</a>, a professor of media studies at the University of Colorado Boulder argued for transforming Facebook into a <a href="/wiki/Platform_cooperative" title="Platform cooperative">platform cooperative</a> owned and governed by the users.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p><p>Facebook co-founder Chris Hughes states that CEO Mark Zuckerberg has too much power, that the company is now a monopoly, and that, as a result, it should be split into multiple smaller companies. Hughes called for the breakup of Facebook in an <a href="/wiki/Op-ed" title="Op-ed">op-ed</a> on The New York Times. Hughes says he's concerned that Zuckerberg has surrounded himself with a team that doesn't challenge him and that as a result, it's the U.S. government's job to hold him accountable and curb his "unchecked power." <sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Hughes also said that "Mark's power is unprecedented and un-American."<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Several U.S. politicians agree with Hughes.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> EU Commissioner for Competition Margrethe Vestager has stated that splitting Facebook should only be done as "a remedy of the very last resort", and that splitting Facebook would not solve Facebook's underlying problems.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>

<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Number_of_advertisers">Number of advertisers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Facebook,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Number of advertisers" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In February 2015, Facebook announced that it had reached two million active advertisers with most of the gain coming from small businesses. An active advertiser is an advertiser that has advertised on the Facebook platform in the last 28 days.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> In March 2016, Facebook announced that it reached three million active advertisers with more than 70% from outside the US.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> Prices for advertising follow a variable pricing model based on ad auction bids, potential engagement levels of the advertisement itself. Similar to other online advertising platforms like Google and Twitter, targeting of advertisements is one of the chief merits of advertising vs. traditional mass advertising modes like television and print. Marketing on Facebook is employed through two methods based on the surfing habits, likes and shares, and purchasing data of the audience, namely targeted audiences and "look alike" audiences.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Tax_affairs">Tax affairs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Facebook,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Tax affairs" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Corporation_tax_in_the_Republic_of_Ireland#Multinational_tax_schemes" title="Corporation tax in the Republic of Ireland">Corporation tax in the Republic of Ireland § Multinational tax schemes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Double_Irish_arrangement#Effect_of_Tax_Cuts_and_Jobs_Act" title="Double Irish arrangement">Double Irish arrangement § Effect of Tax Cuts and Jobs Act</a></div>
<p>The US IRS challenged the valuation Facebook used when it transferred IP from the US to Facebook Ireland in 2010 (which Facebook Ireland then revalued higher before charging out), as it was building its <a href="/wiki/Double_Irish" class="mw-redirect" title="Double Irish">double Irish</a> tax structure.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> The case is ongoing and Facebook faces a potential fine of $3–5bn.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p><p>The US <a href="/wiki/Tax_Cuts_and_Jobs_Act_of_2017" title="Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017">Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017</a> changed Facebook's global tax calculations. Facebook Ireland is subject to the US GILTI tax of 10.5% on global intangible profits (i.e. Irish profits). On the basis that Facebook Ireland is paying some tax, the effective minimum US tax for Facebook Ireland will be circa 11%. In contrast, Facebook Inc. would incur a special IP tax rate of 13.125% (the FDII rate) if its Irish business relocated to the US. Tax relief in the US (21% vs. Irish at the GILTI rate) and accelerated capital expensing, would make this effective US rate around 12%.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup></p><p>The insignificance of the US/Irish tax difference was demonstrated when Facebook moved 1.5bn non-EU accounts to the US to limit exposure to <a href="/wiki/GDPR" class="mw-redirect" title="GDPR">GDPR</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-reuters_76-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-76">[76]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Offices">Offices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Facebook,_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Offices" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Users outside of the US and Canada contract with Facebook's Irish subsidiary "Facebook Ireland Limited". This allows Facebook to avoid US taxes for all users in Europe, Asia, Australia, Africa and South America. Facebook is making use of the <a href="/wiki/Double_Irish_arrangement" title="Double Irish arrangement">Double Irish arrangement</a> which allows it to pay just about 2–3% corporation tax on all international revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-bloom2010_78-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloom2010-78">[78]</a></sup> In 2010, Facebook opened its fourth office, in <a href="/wiki/Hyderabad,_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Hyderabad, India">Hyderabad</a><sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> and the first in <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup></p><p>Facebook's <a href="/wiki/Hyderabad,_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Hyderabad, India">Hyderabad</a> center houses online advertising and developer support teams and provide support to users and advertisers.<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup> In India Facebook is registered as 'Facebook India Online Services Pvt Ltd'.<sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup> It also has support centers in <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Austin" class="mw-redirect" title="Austin">Austin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-auto5_87-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto5-87">[87]</a></sup></p><p>Facebook opened its London headquarters in 2017 in Fitzrovia in central London. Facebook opened an office in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge, Massachusetts</a> in 2018. The offices were initially home to Facebook's "Connectivity Lab", a group focused on bringing Internet access those who do not have access to the Internet.<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup></p>
