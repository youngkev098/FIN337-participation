</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Nielsen was founded in 1923 by <a href="/wiki/Arthur_C._Nielsen,_Sr." class="mw-redirect" title="Arthur C. Nielsen, Sr.">Arthur C. Nielsen, Sr.</a>, who invented an approach to measuring competitive sales results that made the concept of "market share" a practical management tool. The company was originally incorporated in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> and later was purchased on May 24, 2006 by a consortium of private equity firms (collectively, the "Sponsors").<sup id="cite_ref-Nielsen_2015AR_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nielsen_2015AR-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Merger_and_listing">Merger and listing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Merger and listing" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In January 2011, Nielsen consummated an initial public offering of common stock and, subsequently, shares started trading on the New York Stock Exchange under the symbol “NLSN”.<sup id="cite_ref-Nielsen_2015AR_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nielsen_2015AR-5">[5]</a></sup> On August 31, 2015, Nielsen N.V., a Dutch public company listed on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>, merged with Nielsen Holdings plc, by way of a cross-border merger under the European Cross-Border Merger Directive, with Nielsen Holdings plc being the surviving company (the "Merger").<sup id="cite_ref-Nielsen_2015AR_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nielsen_2015AR-5">[5]</a></sup> The Merger effectively changed the place of incorporation of Nielsen's publicly traded parent holding company from the Netherlands to England and Wales, with no changes made to the business being conducted by Nielsen prior to the Merger.<sup id="cite_ref-Nielsen_2015AR_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nielsen_2015AR-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Company_information">Company information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Company information" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:NielsenMarkham.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/NielsenMarkham.jpg/220px-NielsenMarkham.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="4801" data-file-height="3179"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 146px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/NielsenMarkham.jpg/220px-NielsenMarkham.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="146" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:NielsenMarkham.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Nielsen office in Canada</div></div></div>
<p>Nielsen is a leading<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> global, independent measurement and data company for <a href="/wiki/Fast-moving_consumer_goods" title="Fast-moving consumer goods">fast-moving consumer goods</a>, <a href="/wiki/Consumer_behavior" class="mw-redirect" title="Consumer behavior">consumer behavior</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Broadcast_media" class="mw-redirect" title="Broadcast media">media</a>. With a presence in more than 100 countries and services covering more than 90% of the globe's GDP and population, Nielsen provides clients with data about what consumers watch (programming, <a href="/wiki/Advertising" title="Advertising">advertising</a>) and what they buy (categories, <a href="/wiki/Brand" title="Brand">brands</a>, products) on a global and local basis and how those choices intersect.<sup id="cite_ref-Nielsen_2015_Annual_Report_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nielsen_2015_Annual_Report-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>The company's operations span developing and emerging markets worldwide, in more than 100 countries. According to SEC records, on February 26, 2015, Nielsen N.V., announced that its board of directors unanimously approved a proposal that resulted in a change in the company's legal domicile, from the Netherlands to the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-Location_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Location-9">[9]</a></sup> Upon approval, the company became incorporated under English law and was registered as a public limited company to be named <b>Nielsen Holdings PLC</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-Nielsen_Holdings_PLC_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nielsen_Holdings_PLC-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>James Attwood Jr. is Nielsen's non-executive Chairman,<sup id="cite_ref-James_Attwood_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-James_Attwood-1">[1]</a></sup> and David Kenny is Nielsen's Chief Executive Officer. James Attwood succeeded Dave Calhoun as Executive Chairman in January 2016. Prior to joining Nielsen as CEO in 2006, Dave Calhoun served as Vice Chairman of The <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a> Company and President and Chief Executive Officer of GE Infrastructure, the largest of six GE business units. He served as Nielsen CEO from to 2006 to 2014, when he was succeeded by Mitch Barns.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Barns joined Nielsen in 1997 and has held various roles in Europe, Asia, and North America leading major parts of the business.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>On December 3, 2018, David Kenny was named CEO of Nielsen, succeeding Mitch Barns.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>While the Nielsen brand is most often associated with television ratings, those TV ratings services comprise approximately one-quarter of the company's business and revenues. After substantial work to simplify the company over the last several years, Nielsen today aligns its business into two reporting segments: Buy (consumer purchasing measurement and analytics) and Watch (media audience measurement and analytics).<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="What_consumers_buy">What consumers buy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: What consumers buy" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Nielsen's Buy segment (approx. 45% of global revenues) primarily helps packaged goods companies and retailers (and Wall Street analysts) understand what consumers are buying in terms of categories, brands and products. The company's aim is to measure all consumer purchases as buying behavior continues to fragment across channels and segments.<sup id="cite_ref-Nielsen_2015_Annual_Report_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nielsen_2015_Annual_Report-8">[8]</a></sup> For example, it is Nielsen's data that measures how much Diet Coke vs. Diet Pepsi is sold in stores, or how much Crest versus Colgate toothpaste is sold. Nielsen accomplishes this by purchasing and analyzing huge amounts of retail data that measures what is being sold in the store, and then combines it with household panel data that captures everything that is brought into the home. These measures of sales performance also fuel a range of forward-looking analytics to help clients improve the precision and efficiency of their advertising spending, maximize the impact of their promotion budgets and optimize their product assortment. They also can provide insights into how changes in product offerings, pricing or <a href="/wiki/Marketing" title="Marketing">marketing</a> would change sales. Major clients include <a href="/wiki/The_Coca-Cola_Company" title="The Coca-Cola Company">The Coca-Cola Company</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nestl%C3%A9" title="Nestlé">Nestlé</a> S.A., <a href="/wiki/The_Procter_%26_Gamble_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="The Procter &amp; Gamble Company">The Procter &amp; Gamble Company</a>, Unilever Group, and <a href="/wiki/Walmart" title="Walmart">Walmart</a>. While the United States is the company's largest market, Nielsen is a global company. In the Buy business, it covers 106 countries representing more than 90% of the world's population.<sup id="cite_ref-Nielsen_2015_Annual_Report_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nielsen_2015_Annual_Report-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="What_consumers_watch">What consumers watch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: What consumers watch" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Nielsen's Watch segment (approx. 55% of global revenues) primarily measures what consumers are watching (and listening to) across most devices: TV, radio, computers, mobile, over-the-top, etc. The company measures <a href="/wiki/Consumption_(economics)" title="Consumption (economics)">consumption</a> of programming and advertising across all distribution points. Nielsen's ratings are used by advertisers and networks to shape the buying and selling of advertising. Major clients include <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a>, <a href="/wiki/NBC_Universal" class="mw-redirect" title="NBC Universal">NBC Universal</a>, <a href="/wiki/News_Corporation_(1980%E2%80%932013)" title="News Corporation (1980–2013)">News Corporation</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">The Walt Disney Company</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-15">[15]</a></sup> Nielsen's Watch business measures media in 47 countries that together represent approximately 80% of global advertising spending. Nielsen's digital audience measurement capability is a rapidly growing part of this footprint.<sup id="cite_ref-Nielsen_2015_Annual_Report_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nielsen_2015_Annual_Report-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Company_history">Company history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Company history" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Arthur_C._Nielsen_and_the_invention_of_.22Market_Share.22"></span><span class="mw-headline" id='Arthur_C._Nielsen_and_the_invention_of_"Market_Share"'>Arthur C. Nielsen and the invention of "Market Share"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title='Edit section: Arthur C. Nielsen and the invention of "Market Share"' data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Arthur_C._Nielsen" class="mw-redirect" title="Arthur C. Nielsen">Arthur C. Nielsen</a> founded the <a href="/wiki/AC_Nielsen_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="AC Nielsen Company">AC Nielsen Company</a> in August 1923<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> with the idea of selling engineering performance surveys. It was the first company to offer <a href="/wiki/Market_research" title="Market research">market research</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Gillespie_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gillespie-18">[18]</a></sup> The company expanded its business in 1932 by creating a retail index that tracked the flow of food and drug purchases. This was the first retail measurement of its kind and for the first time allowed a company to determine its "share" of the market—the origination of the concept of "<a href="/wiki/Market_share" title="Market share">market share</a>"<sup id="cite_ref-Gillespie_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gillespie-18">[18]</a></sup> Arthur C. Nielsen is credited with coining this business term.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio_and_television">Radio and television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Radio and television" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 1936, Arthur C. Nielsen acquired the Audimeter, which measured which <a href="/wiki/Radio" title="Radio">radio</a> stations a radio had been tuned to during the day. After tinkering with the device for a few years, the company created a national radio rating service in 1942.<sup id="cite_ref-Basler_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Basler-19">[19]</a></sup> The company collected information on which stations radios were tuned to in one thousand homes. Then, this survey data was sold to manufacturers who were interested in the popularity of programs and demographic information about listeners for advertising purposes. This was the birth of <a href="/wiki/Audience_measurement" title="Audience measurement">audience measurement</a> that would become the most well-known part of Nielsen's business when applied to television.<sup id="cite_ref-Basler_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Basler-19">[19]</a></sup> Today, these are commonly referred to as "<b><a href="/wiki/Nielsen_ratings" title="Nielsen ratings">Nielsen ratings</a></b>".
</p><p>The company began measuring television audiences in 1950, at a time when the medium was just getting off the ground. Just as with radio, a sampling of homes across the U.S. was used to develop ratings. This information was collected on a device that was attached to a television that recorded what was being watched. In 1953, the company began sending out diaries to a smaller sample of homes ("Nielsen families") within the survey to have them record what they had watched.<sup id="cite_ref-Gillespie_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gillespie-18">[18]</a></sup> This data was put together with information from the devices. This combination of data allowed the company to statistically estimate the number of Americans watching TV and the demographic breakdown of viewers.<sup id="cite_ref-Gillespie_18-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gillespie-18">[18]</a></sup> This became an important tool for advertisers and networks.
</p><p>In the 1980s, the company launched a new measurement device known as the "<a href="/wiki/People_meter" title="People meter">people meter</a>". The device resembles a remote control with buttons for each individual family member and extras for guests. Viewers push a button to signify when they are in the room and push it again when they leave, even if the TV is still on. This form of measurement was intended to provide a more accurate picture of who was watching and when.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2008,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Nielsen released the first in a series of quarterly reports, detailing video and TV usage across the ‘three screens’ – Television, Internet and Mobile devices. The A2/M2 Three Screen Report also includes trends in timeshifted viewing behavior and its relationship to online video viewing, a demographic breakdown of mobile video viewers and DVR penetration.
</p><p>On September 30, 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Nielsen made its Digital Content Ratings available in full syndication for clients.
</p><p>On September 9, 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Nielsen announced that it will retire its paper TV diaries by mid-2017 and provide all electronic measurement in its local television ratings.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Private_equity">Private equity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Private equity" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Nielsen was acquired by the <a href="/wiki/Dun_%26_Bradstreet" title="Dun &amp; Bradstreet">Dun &amp; Bradstreet Company</a> in 1984.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In 1996, D&amp;B divided the company into two separate companies: <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_Media_Research" title="Nielsen Media Research">Nielsen Media Research</a>, which was responsible for TV ratings, and <a href="/wiki/AC_Nielsen" class="mw-redirect" title="AC Nielsen">AC Nielsen</a>, which was responsible for consumer shopping trends and box-office data.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In 1999, Nielsen Media Research was acquired by the Dutch publishing company <a href="/wiki/Verenigde_Nederlandse_Uitgeverijen" title="Verenigde Nederlandse Uitgeverijen">VNU</a> (Verenigde Nederlandse Uitgeverijen).<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> VNU later acquired AC Nielsen and recombined the two businesses in 2001. In between, VNU sold off its newspaper properties to <a href="/wiki/Wegener_(company)" title="Wegener (company)">Wegener</a> and its consumer magazines to <a href="/wiki/Sanoma" title="Sanoma">Sanoma</a>. The company's publishing arm also owned several publications including <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> magazine. VNU combined the Nielsen properties with other research and data collection units including BASES, Claritas, HCI and Spectra. VNU also acquired companies that added to its measurement capabilities.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Becoming_a_public_company">Becoming a public company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Becoming a public company" data-section="11" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Nielsen was a private company from 2006 through 2011. On January 25, 2011 the company listed itself on the New York Stock Exchange and issued an Initial Public Offering (IPO) that raised $1.8 billion in the largest private equity-backed U.S. IPO since 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Mergers.2C_acquisitions.2C_strategic_alliances_and_divestitures"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Mergers,_acquisitions,_strategic_alliances_and_divestitures">Mergers, acquisitions, strategic alliances and divestitures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Mergers, acquisitions, strategic alliances and divestitures" data-section="12" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2004">2004</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2004" data-section="13" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>Nielsen began a joint venture called AGB Nielsen Media Research with <a href="/wiki/WPP_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="WPP Group">WPP Group</a>'s AGB Group, a European competitor which provides similar services.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2006">2006</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2006" data-section="14" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>VNU acquired a majority stake in Buzzmetrics, a company which measures consumer-generated media online. Under the new ownership, Nielsen bought the remaining shares of the company in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> In the same year, Nielsen acquired Telephia, which measures mobile media,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and Bilesim Medya, a Turkish advertising intelligence firm.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> VNU was acquired by a group of six private equity firms: the American <a href="/wiki/Kohlberg_Kravis_Roberts" title="Kohlberg Kravis Roberts">Kohlberg Kravis Roberts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_H._Lee_Partners" title="Thomas H. Lee Partners">Thomas H. Lee Partners</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blackstone_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Blackstone Group">Blackstone Group</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carlyle_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Carlyle Group">Carlyle Group</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hellman_%26_Friedman" title="Hellman &amp; Friedman">Hellman &amp; Friedman</a>, and Dutch equity firm <a href="/wiki/AlpInvest_Partners" title="AlpInvest Partners">AlpInvest Partners</a><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> for £5bn.<sup id="cite_ref-vnubpl_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vnubpl-33">[33]</a></sup> In the same year, the group hired David L. Calhoun, formerly of <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a>, as CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> He renamed VNU as The Nielsen Company in 2007. VNU sold its business publications division for €320m (£210m) to venture capital group <a href="/wiki/3i" title="3i">3i</a>, which then sold the UK division (VNU Business Publications Ltd) to <a href="/wiki/Incisive_Media" title="Incisive Media">Incisive Media</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-vnubpl_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vnubpl-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2007">2007</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: 2007" data-section="15" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In June, Nielsen acquired Telephia, a provider of syndicated consumer research to the telecom and mobile media markets.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2008">2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: 2008" data-section="16" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>Nielsen acquired IAG Research which measures viewer engagement with TV commercials.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The same year, Nielsen made a strategic investment in <a href="/wiki/NeuroFocus" title="NeuroFocus">NeuroFocus</a>, a California firm applying neuroscience brainwave techniques for consumer research. The firm was later fully acquired by Nielsen in 2011<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> In 2009 and 2010, Nielsen sold its business magazines; its well-known entertainment properties went to the new company <a href="/wiki/E5_Global_Media" class="mw-redirect" title="E5 Global Media">e5 Global Media</a>.
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2009">2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: 2009" data-section="17" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>Nielsen acquired The Cambridge Group, a <a href="/wiki/Management_consulting" title="Management consulting">management consulting</a> firm headquartered in Chicago. The firm researches latent and emerging consumer demand.
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2010">2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: 2010" data-section="18" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In June, Nielsen paired with <a href="/wiki/McKinsey_%26_Company" title="McKinsey &amp; Company">McKinsey &amp; Company</a> to create the social media consulting company <a href="/w/index.php?title=NM_Incite&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="NM Incite (page does not exist)">NM Incite</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> NM Incite had operations in 13 global markets, including: US, UK, Germany, Spain, Italy, Australia, New Zealand, China, Japan, India, Brazil, Canada and Korea.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2011">2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: 2011" data-section="19" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In August, Nielsen acquired Marketing Analytics, Inc.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2012">2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: 2012" data-section="20" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>In February, Nielsen launched <a href="/wiki/The_Demand_Institute" title="The Demand Institute">The Demand Institute</a> in collaboration with <a href="/wiki/The_Conference_Board" title="The Conference Board">The Conference Board</a>. The Demand Institute is a non-profit, non-advocacy organization. In July, Nielsen acquired the advertising tech company Vizu. The acquisition was made so that Nielsen can better analyze the effectiveness of online advertisement.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> In November, Nielsen acquired SocialGuide, a provider of social TV measurement, analytics and audience engagement <a href="/wiki/Solution" title="Solution">solutions</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> On December 17, 2012, Nielsen disclosed that it would acquire <a href="/wiki/Arbitron" class="mw-redirect" title="Arbitron">Arbitron</a>, a company primarily involved in <a href="/wiki/Radio" title="Radio">radio</a> audience measurement, for $1.3 billion. The successful acquisition was completed on September 30, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-Nielsen_NV_press_release_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nielsen_NV_press_release-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2013">2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: 2013" data-section="21" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>On June 17, 2013, Nielsen announced that Onex Corp (TSX: OCX) had completed the acquisition of Nielsen Expositions for $950 million in cash consideration. Nielsen Expositions operated tradeshows in the United States. The company was renamed Emerald Expositions Inc. after the transaction.
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2014">2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: 2014" data-section="22" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>On February 3, 2014, Nielsen announced the acquisition of Harris Interactive, Inc. (NASDAQ:HPOL). This allowed Nielsen to take ownership of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Harris_Poll" class="mw-redirect" title="The Harris Poll">The Harris Poll</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Later in February 26, 2014, Nielsen acquired Nexium, a retail in-store execution and sales analytics company.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> On September 18, 2014 Nielsen announced the acquisition of Indicus Analytics Pvt Ltd. On October 8, 2014, Nielsen acquired Affinnova, an international media and marketing research firm. The Affinnova team joins Nielsen's legacy BASES team to form Nielsen's Innovation Practice area.<sup id="cite_ref-nielsenaffinnova_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nielsenaffinnova-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2015">2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: 2015" data-section="23" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>On January 22, 2015, Nielsen acquires Brandbank, specialized in the process of digitally collecting, managing and distributing FMCG product and brand image content for retail syndication across in-store, print promotional and e-commerce platforms.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> On March 4, 2015, Nielsen announced the acquisition of Exelate, a provider of data and technology to facilitate the buying and selling of advertising across programmatic platforms.<sup id="cite_ref-nielsenexelate_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nielsenexelate-48">[48]</a></sup> On May 27, 2015, Nielsen acquired Innerscope Research, which specialized in <a href="/wiki/Consumer_neuroscience" title="Consumer neuroscience">consumer neuroscience</a> using <a href="/wiki/Biometrics" title="Biometrics">biometrics</a>, eye tracking and facial coding. Nielsen renamed its combined offering as Nielsen Consumer Neuroscience, and named Carl Marci as Chief Neuroscientist.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2016">2016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: 2016" data-section="24" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>On March 3, 2016, Nielsen acquired Pointlogic, in marketing decision support systems.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> On March 10, 2016, Nielsen acquired Mumbai-based mobile usage measurer Informate Mobile Intelligence for an undisclosed amount.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> On June 21, 2016, Nielsen acquired Repucom, a sports measurement, evaluation and intelligence company based in <a href="/wiki/Stamford,_Connecticut" title="Stamford, Connecticut">Stamford, Connecticut</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> On December 20, 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> Nielsen announced its agreement with Tribune Media Company to purchase Gracenote, a provider of media and entertainment metadata.
</p>
<h4 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2017">2017</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nielsen_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: 2017" data-section="25" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h4>
<p>On January 5, 2017,<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/The_Carlyle_Group" title="The Carlyle Group">The Carlyle Group</a> acquired Claritas from Nielsen. On January 23, 2017,<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> the Stagwell Group announced that it acquired Nielsen's Harris brand and the Harris Poll through its Stagwell Media LLC. On February 1, 2017,<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> Nielsen completed its acquisition of Gracenote. On February 23, 2017, Nielsen acquired Rhiza, Inc., a Pittsburgh-based media and consumer analytics software firm.
</p>
