<p><b>Fifth Third Bank</b> (<b>5/3 Bank</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Bank" title="Bank">bank</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati" title="Cincinnati">Cincinnati</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>, at <a href="/wiki/Fifth_Third_Center_(Cincinnati)" title="Fifth Third Center (Cincinnati)">Fifth Third Center</a>. It is the principal subsidiary of Fifth Third Bancorp, a <a href="/wiki/Bank_holding_company" title="Bank holding company">bank holding company</a>. One of the largest consumer banks in the <a href="/wiki/Midwestern_United_States" title="Midwestern United States">Midwestern United States</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_Cowley_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_Cowley-2">[2]</a></sup> it operates 1,154 <a href="/wiki/Branch_(banking)" title="Branch (banking)">branches</a> and 2,469 <a href="/wiki/Automated_teller_machine" title="Automated teller machine">automated teller machines</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kentucky" title="Kentucky">Kentucky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">Tennessee</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia" title="West Virginia">West Virginia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>. Fifth Third Bank is incorporated in Ohio.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It was state-chartered until late 2019, when it obtained a national charter.<sup <p>The name "Fifth Third" is derived from the names of the bank's two predecessor companies, <b>Third National Bank</b> and <b>Fifth National Bank</b>, which merged in 1909.
</p><p>The company is ranked 384th on the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It is on the <a href="/wiki/List_of_largest_banks_in_the_United_States" title="List of largest banks in the United States">list of largest banks in the United States</a>.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fifth_Third_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>On June 17, 1858, the <b>Bank of the Ohio Valley</b> opened in Cincinnati. On June 23, 1863, the Third National Bank was organized. On April 29, 1871, the banks merged.
</p><p>On June 1, 1908, Third National Bank and Fifth National Bank merged to become the Fifth-Third National Bank of Cincinnati; the hyphen was later dropped. The merger took place when <a href="/wiki/Prohibition" title="Prohibition">prohibitionist</a> ideas were gaining popularity, and it is legend that "Fifth Third" was better than "Third Fifth", which could have been construed as a reference to three <a href="/wiki/Fifth_(unit)" title="Fifth (unit)">fifths</a> of alcohol.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The name went through several changes until March 24, 1969, when it was changed to Fifth Third Bank.
</p><p>In 1994, the bank acquired <a href="/wiki/Louisville,_Kentucky" title="Louisville, Kentucky">Louisville, Kentucky</a>-based Cumberland Federal Bancorp for $149 million in stock.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>In 1995, the bank acquired 12 branches in <a href="/wiki/Dayton,_Ohio" title="Dayton, Ohio">Dayton, Ohio</a>, from <a href="/wiki/PNC_Financial_Services" title="PNC Financial Services">PNC Financial Services</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The bank also acquired Kentucky Enterprise Bancorp.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In 1998, the company acquired the <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Ohio" title="Columbus, Ohio">Columbus, Ohio</a>-based<sup id="cite_ref-bberg1_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bberg1-9">[9]</a></sup> W. Lyman Case &amp; Company, an originator of commercial real estate loans.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p><p>In 1999, the company acquired Enterprise Federal Bancorp, one of the biggest <a href="/wiki/Savings_and_loan_association" title="Savings and loan association">thrifts</a> in the Cincinnati area with 11 branches, for $96.3 million.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p><p>In March 1999, the company acquired Emerald Financial of <a href="/wiki/Strongsville,_Ohio" title="Strongsville, Ohio">Strongsville, Ohio</a>, which owned 15 branches in <a href="/wiki/Cleveland,_Ohio" class="mw-redirect" title="Cleveland, Ohio">Cleveland, Ohio</a>, for $204 million.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In April 1999, the bank acquired Ashland Bankshares and its subsidiary Bank of Ashland, both based in Kentucky, for $80 million.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>In June 1999, the bank acquired South Florida Bank Holding, which owned four branches.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>In July 1999, the company acquired Peoples Bank Corporation of Indianapolis for $228 million in stock.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2001, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Grand_Rapids,_Michigan" title="Grand Rapids, Michigan">Grand Rapids, Michigan</a>-based Old Kent Bank, which owned over 300 branches in Indiana, Illinois, and Michigan.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p><p>In 2002, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">Tennessee</a>-based Franklin Financial for $240 million.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p><p>In 2004, the company acquired First National Bankshares of <a href="/wiki/Naples,_Florida" title="Naples, Florida">Naples, Florida</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2007, the bank acquired R-G Crown Bank of <a href="/wiki/Casselberry,_Florida" title="Casselberry, Florida">Casselberry, Florida</a>, which owned 30 branches in Florida and three branches in Georgia, for $288 million.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2008, the bank acquired <a href="/wiki/First_Charter_Bank" title="First Charter Bank">First Charter Bank</a> of <a href="/wiki/Charlotte,_North_Carolina" title="Charlotte, North Carolina">Charlotte, North Carolina</a>, with 57 branches in North Carolina and two branches in Atlanta.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>On May 5, 2008, Fifth Third acquired nine branches in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlanta, Georgia">Atlanta, Georgia</a> from <a href="/wiki/First_Horizon_National_Corporation" title="First Horizon National Corporation">First Horizon National Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>On October 31, 2008, the bank acquired the assets of the <a href="/wiki/Bank_failure" title="Bank failure">failed</a> Florida-based <b>Freedom Bank</b> from the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Deposit_Insurance_Corporation" title="Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation">Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2008, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_the_Treasury" title="United States Department of the Treasury">United States Department of the Treasury</a> invested $3.4 billion in the company as part of the <a href="/wiki/Troubled_Asset_Relief_Program" title="Troubled Asset Relief Program">Troubled Asset Relief Program</a> and in February 2011, the company repurchased the investment from the Treasury.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p><p>On March 30, 2009, the company sold 51% of its credit card processing business to <a href="/wiki/Advent_International" title="Advent International">Advent International</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Private_equity_firm" title="Private equity firm">private equity firm</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> In 2011, the <a href="/wiki/Joint_venture" title="Joint venture">joint venture</a> changed its name to Vantiv<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> and in 2018, it merged with to <a href="/wiki/Worldpay_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Worldpay Inc.">Worldpay Inc.</a><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2009, the bank was rumored to be one of the front-runners, alongside <a href="/wiki/Huntington_Bancshares" title="Huntington Bancshares">Huntington Bancshares</a>, to buy the <a href="/wiki/National_City_Corp." title="National City Corp.">National City Corp.</a> branches in the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Erie,_Pennsylvania" title="Erie, Pennsylvania">Erie, Pennsylvania</a>, regions that were being sold by <a href="/wiki/PNC_Financial_Services" title="PNC Financial Services">PNC Financial Services</a> as part of the <a href="/wiki/National_City_acquisition_by_PNC" title="National City acquisition by PNC">National City acquisition by PNC</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> However, PNC sold the branches to <a href="/wiki/First_Niagara_Bank" title="First Niagara Bank">First Niagara Bank</a>.
</p><p>On October 28, 2014, the company announced plans to move its Michigan regional headquarters and 150 employees from <a href="/wiki/Southfield,_Michigan" title="Southfield, Michigan">Southfield, Michigan</a> to 62,000 sq ft (5,800 m<sup>2</sup>) in <a href="/wiki/One_Woodward_Avenue" title="One Woodward Avenue">One Woodward Avenue</a> in <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Detroit" title="Downtown Detroit">downtown Detroit</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>In April 2016, the bank sold 17 branches in the Pittsburgh area to <a href="/wiki/FNB_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="FNB Corporation">FNB Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p><p>In November 2017, the company acquired Louisville-based Epic Insurance Solutions Agency and <a href="/wiki/Human_resources" title="Human resources">human resources</a> firm Integrity HR.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2018, the company acquired Coker Capital Advisors, a <a href="/wiki/Mergers_and_acquisitions" title="Mergers and acquisitions">mergers and acquisitions</a> advisory firm.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2019, the company acquired Chicago-based MB Financial.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p><p><br></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2018_shooting_incident">2018 shooting incident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fifth_Third_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2018 shooting incident" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2018_Cincinnati_shooting" title="2018 Cincinnati shooting">2018 Cincinnati shooting</a></div>
<p>On September 6, 2018, a gunman named Omar Enrique Santa-Perez<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> entered the lobby area of the company headquarters in downtown Cincinnati, shooting and killing 3 people and wounding 2 others before being shot and killed by the Cincinnati Police.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p><p>In February 2020, the company warned customers of a data breach.<sup id="cite_ref-dispatch_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dispatch-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fifth_Third_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Controversies" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_discrimination_settlement">2014 discrimination settlement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fifth_Third_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2014 discrimination settlement" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In August 2014, the company settled with the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Justice" title="United States Department of Justice">United States Department of Justice</a>, resolving allegations that the bank engaged in a pattern of discrimination on the basis of disability and receipt of public assistance, in violation of the <a href="/wiki/Equal_Credit_Opportunity_Act" title="Equal Credit Opportunity Act">Equal Credit Opportunity Act</a>. The company was required to pay $1.5 million to eligible mortgage loan applicants who were asked to provide a letter from their doctor to document the income they received from <a href="/wiki/Social_Security_Disability_Insurance" title="Social Security Disability Insurance">Social Security Disability Insurance</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="2020_CFPB_Fraudulent_Account_Investigation">2020 CFPB Fraudulent Account Investigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fifth_Third_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2020 CFPB Fraudulent Account Investigation" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>On March 9th, 2020, the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau announced a lawsuit against the bank for allegedly opening unauthorized accounts and enrolling consumers in unauthorized products and services.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
