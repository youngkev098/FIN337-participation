</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roper_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>George D. Roper founded the company in the late <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a>, primarily as a manufacturer of <a href="/wiki/Home_appliance" title="Home appliance">home appliances</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pump" title="Pump">pumps</a>, and other industrial products. Roper initiated a corporate <a href="/wiki/Mergers_and_acquisitions" title="Mergers and acquisitions">acquisition</a> program, supported by an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>, in 1992.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In 2001, Brian Jellison, a former executive of <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ingersoll-Rand" class="mw-redirect" title="Ingersoll-Rand">Ingersoll-Rand</a>, joined Roper as <a href="/wiki/Chief_Executive_Officer" class="mw-redirect" title="Chief Executive Officer">Chief Executive Officer</a>. The previous <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a> business strategy has been replaced with an operating company model.  Since 2001 Roper has completed acquisitions accounting for over half its revenues, establishing the company in global growth markets, such as <a href="/wiki/RFID" class="mw-redirect" title="RFID">radio frequency identification</a>(RFID) and <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. Jellison died on November 2, 2018 after a brief illness.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>Roper acquired <a href="/wiki/Sunquest_Information_Systems" title="Sunquest Information Systems">Sunquest Information Systems</a>, a maker of diagnostic and laboratory software for $1.42 billion in cash in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Roper_Pump_Co.">Roper Pump Co.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roper_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Roper Pump Co." data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><b>Roper Pump Co.</b> is part of the founding company from which Roper Industries began.  George D. Roper began the company by founding the Roper legacy by entering into part ownership in the Van Wie Gas Stove Company of Cleveland, Ohio.  The Van Wie plant moved to Rockford Illinois and passed into trusteeship in the early 1890s.  George D. Roper became sole owner of the Van Wie Gas Stove company after the company's debts were paid off on September 1, 1894.  A fire completely destroyed the facilities ten days later.  The factory was rebuilt and named the Eclipse Gas Stove Company, and later expanded to include the Trahern Pump Company in 1906.  The Trahern Pump Company, founded in 1857, would evolve to become Roper Pumps Co., currently located in Commerce, Georgia, United States, North America.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>Roper Pump Co. mainly manufactures downhole progressing cavity pumps used in oil drilling, flow dividers used in power generation, and gear pumps used on tanker rail cars and tanker trucks.  Roper Pump Co. is a US producer of small diameter progressing cavity pumps, flow dividers, and gear pumps.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="TransCore_and_DAT_Solutions">TransCore and DAT Solutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roper_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: TransCore and DAT Solutions" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><b>TransCore</b> is a subsidiary of Roper Industries, based in <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville, Tennessee</a>, that specializes in <a href="/wiki/Intelligent_Transportation_System" class="mw-redirect" title="Intelligent Transportation System">Intelligent Transportation Systems</a>. TransCore was acquired by Roper Industries in 2004.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup><sup id="cite_ref-roperbuystranscore_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-roperbuystranscore-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>Roper has divided TransCore into two units: TransCore; and DAT Solutions. TransCore manages <a href="/wiki/RFID" class="mw-redirect" title="RFID">RFID</a> <a href="/wiki/Transponder" title="Transponder">transponders</a> and readers.  In this family, their original product line reflects their acquisition of Amtech Systems in 2000 (also a supplier of production and automation systems for the manufacture of <a href="/wiki/Solar_cell" title="Solar cell">solar cells</a>).  These transponders come in a plastic case, for either windshield or external mounting.  Their newer line is the eGo windshield sticker transponder system, which is a low-cost, batteryless system designed for one-time attachment to a windshield.  These transponders are found in <a href="/wiki/Electronic_toll_collection" title="Electronic toll collection">electronic toll collection</a>, fleet tracking, payment, <a href="/wiki/Parking" title="Parking">parking</a> and access control applications.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>In addition, TransCore has a substantial services business, designing, building and operating ITS facilities.  This may be a complete electronic toll collection system, with design, build, transponders, equipment, customer service and violation enforcement, or any part of it.  For example, TransCore operates the ETC systems for several <a href="/wiki/E-ZPass" title="E-ZPass">E-ZPass</a> members, even though E-ZPass has an exclusive contract with another manufacturer for transponders.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Good_to_Go_(toll_collection_system)" title="Good to Go (toll collection system)">Good to Go</a> system in <a href="/wiki/Washington_(U.S._state)" class="mw-redirect" title="Washington (U.S. state)">Washington</a> state is an eGo transponder system.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="DAT_Solutions">DAT Solutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roper_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: DAT Solutions" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/DAT_Solutions" title="DAT Solutions">DAT Solutions</a> offers freight load boards products for owner-operators, carriers, brokers, shippers, and <a href="/wiki/Third-party_logistics" title="Third-party logistics">3PLs</a>. The underlying framework is the DAT Network, the first electronic freight posting service, acquired in 2001 from the Jubitz Corporation. DAT Solutions began as <i>Dial-A-Truck</i>, a load board operated at the Jubitz Truck Stop in Portland, Oregon. It evolved to become the original and largest internet load board, a matchmaking service for 100 million freight loads and trucks per year. DAT Solutions offers several load board products including: DAT Power, DAT Express and TruckersEdge.
</p><p>DAT Solutions also offers Tracking &amp; Communications <a href="/wiki/Satellite" title="Satellite">satellite</a> for truck tracking, trailer tracking, and temperature and cargo monitoring systems for commercial and private fleets. They use <a href="/wiki/GPS" class="mw-redirect" title="GPS">GPS</a> and GEOS technology to transmit data from the truck or trailer to the dispatch center.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="The_CBORD_Group.2C_Inc."></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_CBORD_Group,_Inc.">The CBORD Group, Inc.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roper_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: The CBORD Group, Inc." data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Roper acquired The CBORD Group, Inc. on February 21, 2008, followed later in the year by acquisition of its K-12 sister company Horizon Software International, LLC. CBORD was founded in Ithaca, NY in 1975.  CBORD products are used for food services, campus ID privilege control, security, residential life, and on/off-campus commerce for K-12 and higher education, acute and long-term healthcare, corporate facilities, and other campus-based institutions.  The products include CS Gold, CS Access, Odyssey, Foodservice Suite, Webfood, Nutrition Service Suite, Room Service Choice, NetMenu, NetNutrition, C-Store, EventMaster, GET, UGryd, CBORD Patient, OneSource, Horizon School Technology, MyPaymentsPlus, MilleniumPlus, and GeriMenu.  
</p><p>CBORD has offices in: Ithaca, NY; Duluth, GA; Canton, OH; Waco, TX; and Sydney, Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>CBORD made headlines in 2019 with its campus card integration in Apple wallet <sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and its partnership with nonprofit Swipe Out Hunger, to enable college students to donate meals to other students in need via technology <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Compressor_Controls_Corporation_.28CCC.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Compressor_Controls_Corporation_(CCC)">Compressor Controls Corporation (CCC)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roper_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Compressor Controls Corporation (CCC)" data-section="10" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Compressor Controls Corporation (CCC) has specialized in turbomachinery controls for over 35 years. CCC is involved in a broad range of industries including oil, gas, chemical, petrochemical, refineries, LNG, pipelines, steel mills, pharmaceutical, machine-building, and power generation facilities. CCC also constructs new turbomachinery controls and retrofit existing equipment.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
