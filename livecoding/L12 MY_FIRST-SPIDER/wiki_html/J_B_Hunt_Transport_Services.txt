<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For the company's founder, see <a href="/wiki/Johnnie_Bryan_Hunt" title="Johnnie Bryan Hunt">Johnnie Bryan Hunt</a>.</div>
<p><b>J.B. Hunt Transport Services, Inc.</b> (<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.nasdaq.com/market-activity/stocks/jbht">NASDAQ:JBHT</a>) is a transportation and logistics company based in <a href="/wiki/Lowell,_Arkansas" title="Lowell, Arkansas">Lowell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arkansas" title="Arkansas">Arkansas</a>. J.B. Hunt was founded by <a href="/wiki/Johnnie_Bryan_Hunt" title="Johnnie Bryan Hunt">Johnnie Bryan Hunt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johnelle_Hunt" title="Johnelle Hunt">Johnelle Hunt</a> in Arkansas on August 10, 1961. By 1983, J.B. Hunt had grown into the 80th largest trucking firm in the U.S. and earned $623.47 million in revenue. At that time J.B. Hunt was operating 550 tractors, 1,049 trailers, and had roughly 1,050 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4">[4]</a></sup> J.B. Hunt primarily operates large <a href="/wiki/Semi-trailers" class="mw-redirect" title="Semi-trailers">semi-trailer trucks</a> and provides transportation services throughout the continental U.S., Canada and Mexico. The company currently employs over 24,000 and operates more than 12,000 trucks. </section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=J._B._Hunt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company was founded in 1961. By 1983, J.B. Hunt became a publicly held company and had grown into the 80th largest trucking firm in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In 1989, J.B. Hunt Transport began partnering with railroads to offer intermodal service.  The initial railroad partnership was with the former Santa Fe Railway<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/BNSF_Railway" title="BNSF Railway">BNSF Railway</a> Company) and has since grown to other Class I railroads including <a href="/wiki/Norfolk_Southern_Railway" title="Norfolk Southern Railway">Norfolk Southern</a>, <a href="/wiki/CSX_Transportation" title="CSX Transportation">CSX</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canadian_National_Railway" title="Canadian National Railway">CN</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Southern_Railway" title="Kansas City Southern Railway">KCS</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> About two-thirds of the company's revenues and profits come from intermodal.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>The company introduced a dedicated service in 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
