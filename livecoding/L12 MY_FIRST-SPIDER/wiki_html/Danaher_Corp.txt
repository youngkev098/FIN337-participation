class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup></td></tr></tbody></table><p>Danaher is ranked 160th on the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p><p>In 2019, Danaher was ranked by <a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a> as one of the best employers for diversity.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>The company is named after Danaher Creek in <a href="/wiki/Western_Montana" title="Western Montana">Western Montana</a>. It was fishing in this creek where the founders, brothers <a href="/wiki/Steven_M._Rales" title="Steven M. Rales">Steven M. Rales</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mitchell_Rales" title="Mitchell Rales">Mitchell Rales</a>, envisioned a new kind of manufacturing company dedicated to continuous improvement.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Danaher was one of the first companies in North America to adopt "<a href="/wiki/Kaizen" title="Kaizen">Kaizen</a>" principles to manufacturing,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> which is a <a href="/wiki/Lean_manufacturing" title="Lean manufacturing">lean manufacturing</a> Japanese philosophy of continuous improvement, and elimination of waste.
</p>

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danaher_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The company was organized in 1969 as a <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> <a href="/wiki/Real_estate_investment_trust" title="Real estate investment trust">real estate investment trust</a> under its former name DMG, Inc. In 1978, DMG, Inc. was reorganized as a Florida corporation and changed its name to Diversified Mortgage Investors, Inc. Eventually, the company adopted the name Danaher in 1984 and was reincorporated as a Delaware corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-10K_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K-1">[1]</a></sup> In June 1986, Danaher purchased Chicago Pneumatic (CP), which had merged in July 1984 with a sister company, The Jacobs Manufacturing Co. (Jacobs), and which had purchased Matco Tools Corp. (MTC) in April 1981.
</p><p>Danaher in June 1987 sold CP while retaining Jacobs, including its Matco Tools Division. Jacobs' name was changed to Matco Tools Corp. in November 1991, and the other divisions within Jacobs were established as separate operating companies. In January 1993, Danaher formed NMTC, Inc., which acquired a substantial portion of the assets of MTC, including the existing distributorship agreements of MTC.
</p><p>Within two years of Danaher Corp.'s founding in 1984, Danaher Corp. acquired 12 companies as part of a strategy to enter the <a href="/wiki/Manufacturing" title="Manufacturing">manufacturing</a> business. Therefore, in 1986 Danaher added <a href="/wiki/Qualitrol" title="Qualitrol">Qualitrol</a> to its <a href="/wiki/Instrumentation" title="Instrumentation">instrumentation</a> unit.<sup id="cite_ref-About_Qualitrol_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-About_Qualitrol-6">[6]</a></sup> The unit also included <a href="/wiki/Gilbarco_Veeder-Root" title="Gilbarco Veeder-Root">Gilbarco Veeder-Root</a>'s underground fuel storage sensors, Dynapar's motion sensors<sup id="cite_ref-Dynapar_SSI_Encoders_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dynapar_SSI_Encoders-7">[7]</a></sup> and Qualitrol's <a href="/wiki/Pressure_measurement" title="Pressure measurement">pressure</a> and <a href="/wiki/Temperature_measurement" title="Temperature measurement">temperature</a> measurement instruments, used on the electrical transformer industry.<sup id="cite_ref-Danaher_Corporation_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Danaher_Corporation-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>The Danaher Motion group acquired <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kollmorgen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kollmorgen (page does not exist)">Kollmorgen</a>, of <a href="/wiki/Radford,_Virginia" title="Radford, Virginia">Radford, Virginia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="1990.E2.80.932000"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1990–2000">1990–2000</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danaher_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1990–2000" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>1990 Danaher acquires Easco Hand Tools Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
1991 Danaher is selected as exclusive supplier of handtools for Sears.
1994 Danaher acquires the tool company Armstrong- makers of tool brands Armstrong, Allen, and others. Since 1999 Danaher owns 100% of <a href="/wiki/Hach_Company" title="Hach Company">Hach company</a>, broadening the portfolio of chemical, mainly water and wastewater, analytics also with the German company Lange.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="2005.E2.80.932006"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2005–2006">2005–2006</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danaher_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2005–2006" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>UK-based West Instruments provides control and measurement instrumentation and services for the global industrial and process markets. German instrumentation manufacturer PMA was added to the Industrial Controls Group in 2005 and enhances the range of control and measurement instrumentation.
</p><p>In July 2005, Danaher Announces Definitive Agreement To Acquire <a href="/wiki/Leica_Microsystems" title="Leica Microsystems">Leica Microsystems</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Danaher-Corp-Jul-2005-10-Q_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Danaher-Corp-Jul-2005-10-Q-11">[11]</a></sup> The company manufactures a broad range of products for numerous applications requiring microscopic imaging, measurement and analysis. It also offers management systems in the areas of Life Science including biotechnology and medicine, as well as the science of raw materials and industrial quality assurance.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="2007.E2.80.932008"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2007–2008">2007–2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danaher_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2007–2008" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In early 2007 Danaher acquired Australian Pathology Instrument and Engineering company Vision Systems Limited.<sup id="cite_ref-Danaher-Corp-Oct-2006-10-Q_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Danaher-Corp-Oct-2006-10-Q-12">[12]</a></sup> Also in 2007, Danaher made its largest acquisition to date, the purchase of <a href="/wiki/Tektronix,_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Tektronix, Inc.">Tektronix, Inc.</a> for US$2.85 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-Danaher-Corp-Nov-2007-8-K_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Danaher-Corp-Nov-2007-8-K-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="2009.E2.80.932010"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–2010">2009–2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danaher_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2009–2010" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2009 Danaher purchased the Analytical Technologies business unit of Canadian Life Sciences company MDS, Inc. for US$650 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Danaher-Corp-Feb-2010-10-K_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Danaher-Corp-Feb-2010-10-K-15">[15]</a></sup> In a separate, but related transaction, Danaher agreed with <a href="/wiki/Life_Technologies_(Thermo_Fisher_Scientific)" title="Life Technologies (Thermo Fisher Scientific)">Life Technologies Corporation</a> (Nasdaq: LIFE) to acquire the remaining 50% ownership position in AB SCIEX for US$450 million, leaving Danaher as outright owner of AB SCIEX and Molecular Devices. The aggregate purchase price for the combined transactions is $1.1 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="2011.E2.80.932012"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2011–2012">2011–2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danaher_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2011–2012" data-section="6" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In 2011, Danaher acquired <a href="/wiki/Beckman_Coulter" title="Beckman Coulter">Beckman Coulter</a><sup id="cite_ref-Danaher-Corp-Aug-2011-8-K/A_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Danaher-Corp-Aug-2011-8-K/A-17">[17]</a></sup> and sold Accu-sort to <a href="/wiki/Datalogic_S.p.A.#Acquisitions" class="mw-redirect" title="Datalogic S.p.A.">Datalogic</a>.
</p><p>In October 2012, Danaher Corporation and <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Industries" title="Cooper Industries">Cooper Industries</a> agreed to sell their joint venture, <a href="/wiki/Apex_Tool_Group" title="Apex Tool Group">Apex Tool Group</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Bain_Capital" title="Bain Capital">Bain Capital</a> for a fee of around $1.6 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In December 2012 Danaher Corporation acquired Navman Wireless, a provider of on-demand fleet and asset management technology.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="2013.E2.80.932014"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013–2014">2013–2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danaher_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2013–2014" data-section="7" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In September 2014, Danaher Corp announced its intention to buy <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Biocare" title="Nobel Biocare">Nobel Biocare</a> for $2.2 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In October 2014, Danaher Corp announced it would be combining its communications unit with <a href="/wiki/NetScout_Systems" title="NetScout Systems">NetScout Systems</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The action was completed in July 2015<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="2015.E2.80.932016"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–2016">2015–2016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danaher_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2015–2016" data-section="8" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In May 2015, Danaher Corp announced the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Pall_Corporation" title="Pall Corporation">Pall</a> for $13.8 billion,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> which was completed in August 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>In June 2016, Danaher spun off several subsidiaries, including Matco and AMMCO-COATS, to create <a href="/wiki/Fortive" title="Fortive">Fortive</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In September of the same year, the company announced it would acquire <a href="/wiki/Cepheid_Inc" title="Cepheid Inc">Cepheid</a> for $4 billion (including debt).<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2016, Danaher Corporation acquired Phenomenex for $700 million to expand its life sciences space.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="2017.E2.80.93present"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017–present">2017–present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Danaher_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2017–present" data-section="9" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In October 2017, Danaher Corp announced the acquisition of scientific informatics company ID Business Solutions Ltd. (IDBS).<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> It was announced that IDBS would become part of Danaher's life sciences platform.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, Danaher recorded $18.3 billion in revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2018, the business announced it would acquire <a href="/wiki/Integrated_DNA_Technologies" title="Integrated DNA Technologies">Integrated DNA Technologies</a> for an undisclosed price.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2018, Danaher announced its intention to spin off its $2.8 billion Dental segment, into an independent publicly traded company.
<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2019, Danaher announced it has signed an agreement to sell its biomolecular characterization, chromatography hardware and resins, microcarriers and particle validation standards businesses to Sartorius AG for approximately $750 million. The combined revenue of the businesses was approximately $140 million in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2019, Danaher completed the <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">corporate spin-off</a> of Envista, its dental unit.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p><p>On Monday, March 2, 2020, Dr. Stephen Hahn, the F.D.A.’s commissioner, said that the agency had taken actions to allow private labs and companies to begin making their own coronavirus tests in order to greatly expand the U.S. capacity to test for the virus. Dr. Hahn went on to note, "There will be — the estimates we’re getting from industry right now, by the end of this week, close to a million tests will be able to be performed."<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The following morning, a spokeswoman for the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services noted that Dr. Hahn was referring to tests being produced by an outside manufacturer, <a href="/wiki/Integrated_DNA_Technologies" title="Integrated DNA Technologies">Integrated DNA Technologies</a>. IDT is a division of Danaher which was acquired in early 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p><p>In March 2020, Danaher acquired Cytiva, the life science business of <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a>, for $21.4 billion. The unit makes equipment for the research and manufacture of pharmaceuticals.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
