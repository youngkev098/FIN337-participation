<p><b>McCormick &amp; Company</b> is an American food company that manufactures, markets, and distributes spices, seasoning mixes, condiments, and other flavoring products for the industrial, restaurant, institutional, and home markets. A <a href="/wiki/Fortune_1000" title="Fortune 1000">Fortune 1000</a> company, McCormick has approximately 11,700 employees. The company headquarters moved from <a href="/wiki/Sparks,_Maryland" title="Sparks, Maryland">Sparks</a> to <a href="/wiki/Hunt_Valley,_Maryland" title="Hunt Valley, Maryland">Hunt Valley, Maryland</a> in the third quarter of 2018.

</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Brands">Brands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=McCormick_%26_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Brands" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>Its brands include McCormick; <a href="/wiki/Zatarain%27s" title="Zatarain's">Zatarain's</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lawry%27s_and_Adolph%27s" title="Lawry's and Adolph's">Lawry's</a>, <a href="/wiki/Old_Bay_Seasoning" title="Old Bay Seasoning">Old Bay Seasoning</a>, <a href="/wiki/French%27s" title="French's">French's</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frank%27s_RedHot" title="Frank's RedHot">Frank's Red Hot</a>, Mojave Foods, Cattleman's, Giotti, El Guapo, Gourmet Garden, Kitchen Basics, Stubb's, Brand Aromatics, Thai Kitchen and Simply Asia (United States); Ducros, Drogheria &amp; Alimentari, Kamis, Galeo, Margão (Portugal), Silvo, and Vahiné (<a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>); Club House spices and Billy Bee Honey (Canada); <a href="/wiki/Schwartz_(brand)" title="Schwartz (brand)">Schwartz</a> (<a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>); <a href="/wiki/Aeroplane_Jelly" title="Aeroplane Jelly">Aeroplane Jelly</a> and <a href="/wiki/Keen%27s" title="Keen's">Keen's Mustard</a> (McCormick Foods Australia); <a href="/wiki/Kamis" title="Kamis">Kamis</a> (Poland).
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=McCormick_%26_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Willoughby M. McCormick (1864–1932), started the business in Baltimore at age 25 in 1889. From one room and a cellar, the initial products were sold door-to-door and included <a href="/wiki/Root_beer" title="Root beer">root beer</a>, flavoring extracts, <a href="/wiki/Fruit_syrup" title="Fruit syrup">fruit syrups</a> and juices. Seven years later, McCormick bought the F.G. Emmett Spice Company and entered the spice industry.<sup id="cite_ref-early_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-early-7">[7]</a></sup>  In 1903, Willoughby and his brother Roberdeau incorporated the company in Maine;<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> it was reincorporated in Maryland in 1915. Most of the company's assets and records were destroyed in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Baltimore_Fire" title="Great Baltimore Fire">Great Baltimore Fire</a> of February 1904, the third worst conflagration to hit an American city which over two days burned most of the city's central business district, north of the harbor waterfront with most of its then new rising skyscrapers. However, a new five-story building was erected on the same site within 10 months in 1905.<sup id="cite_ref-early_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-early-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>McCormick &amp; Company was investigated by the <a href="/wiki/Food_and_Drug_Administration" title="Food and Drug Administration">United States Bureau of Chemistry</a> in 1916 for adulteration in its <a href="/wiki/Black_pepper" title="Black pepper">black pepper</a> product. The court case resulted in a fine and a requirement that the company label the adulterated product as "ground black pepper containing from 10 percent to 28 percent added pepper shells".<sup id="cite_ref-squad_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-squad-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>Willoughby's nephew <a href="/wiki/Charles_P._McCormick" class="mw-redirect" title="Charles P. McCormick">Charles P. McCormick</a> (1896–1970), began working for the company in the summer of 1912, during his high school years at the all-boys third oldest secondary school in America, <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_City_College" title="Baltimore City College">The Baltimore City College</a> before graduating in 1915, and going on to <a href="/wiki/Johns_Hopkins_University" title="Johns Hopkins University">The Johns Hopkins University</a> in its first years at the new <a href="/wiki/Homewood_Campus_of_Johns_Hopkins_University" title="Homewood Campus of Johns Hopkins University">Homewood campus</a> in north Baltimore, and  was later elected to the company Board of Directors in 1925.<sup id="cite_ref-early_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-early-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mccormick-Hq-1951.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/5/5e/Mccormick-Hq-1951.jpg/220px-Mccormick-Hq-1951.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="162" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="294" data-file-height="216"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 162px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/5/5e/Mccormick-Hq-1951.jpg/220px-Mccormick-Hq-1951.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="162" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mccormick-Hq-1951.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Downtown headquarters in 1951, with the company's iconic red and white spice tins and bottles displayed on the roof</div></div></div>
<p>Willoughby died on November 4, 1932, and Charles was elected President and Chairman of the Board at age 36. The big "Mc" became a trademark for nearly all U.S. products in 1941.
</p><p>Charles P. ("Buzz")  McCormick, Jr. was elected President and Chief Executive Officer in 1987 and re-elected CEO and Chairman of the Board in 1988.
</p><p>The company celebrated its centennial in 1989 with events primarily for employees and those responsible for its success, and arranged for the musical group <a href="/wiki/Up_with_People" title="Up with People">Up with People</a> to give a series of performances across the U.S. for schools, churches, hospitals and similar organizations.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=McCormick_%26_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Acquisitions" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>McCormick acquired <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>-based coffee, spice and extract house <a href="/wiki/A._Schilling_%26_Company" title="A. Schilling &amp; Company">A. Schilling &amp; Company</a> in 1947, enabling McCormick to begin coast-to-coast distribution in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> McCormick continued to use the Schilling name for its Western division until the 1990s, with the last product containers marked Schilling produced in 2002; since then, all of the company's products have been marketed under the McCormick name nationwide.
</p><p>Ben-Hur Products,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> a similar California-based company, was acquired in 1953, and Canada's largest spice firm, Gorman Eckert &amp; Co. Ltd. of <a href="/wiki/London,_Ontario" title="London, Ontario">London, Ontario</a>, was acquired in 1959. Gilroy Foods of <a href="/wiki/Gilroy,_California" title="Gilroy, California">Gilroy, California</a> became a wholly owned subsidiary in 1961. Other acquisitions included Baker Extract Co. in 1962, Cake Mate cake decorating in 1967, Childers Foods (later part of Golden West Foods) in 1968, and Tubed Products, an <a href="/wiki/Easthampton,_Massachusetts" title="Easthampton, Massachusetts">Easthampton, Massachusetts</a> contract food packer and producer of plastic tubes, also in 1968. <a href="/wiki/Charles_P._McCormick" class="mw-redirect" title="Charles P. McCormick">Charles P. McCormick</a> retired in 1969 and was named Chairman Emeritus and died the following year of a heart attack.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>McCormick acquired Golden West Foods, a frozen foods manufacturer and distributor in Gilroy, California, in 1973 and entered that field under the Schilling brand label. The McCormick (east) and Schilling (west) retail units were consolidated to form a Grocery Products Division in 1975 with its headquarters in Baltimore. Additional acquisitions included All Portions in 1975, TV Time Foods of <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a> in 1976, Astro Foods of <a href="/wiki/San_Rafael,_California" title="San Rafael, California">San Rafael, California</a> in 1977, and Han-Dee Pak of <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a> in 1979.
</p><p>In October 1979, Swiss pharmaceutical firm, <a href="/wiki/Sandoz" class="mw-redirect" title="Sandoz">Sandoz, Ltd.</a>, announced its intention of purchasing the company.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> McCormick sued Sandoz in May 1980 and by September Sandoz agreed to relinquish its efforts to purchase McCormick and sold the shares that it acquired in its attempt to purchase the company.
</p><p>Setco, a plastic bottles producer in <a href="/wiki/Culver_City,_California" title="Culver City, California">Culver City, California</a>, and Stange, a specialty flavorings and colorings company of <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, were purchased as subsidiaries in 1981. The company acquired Paterson Jenks, a publicly held <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> corporation, in 1984, and Schwartz, the largest British spice line. Other acquisitions included Armanino Farms, the world's largest grower and processor of chives, from Armanino &amp; Son, Inc., of San Francisco in 1986; and three California companies in 1987: Gentry Foods of Gilroy, Parsley Patch of <a href="/wiki/Windsor,_California" title="Windsor, California">Windsor</a>, and The Herb Farm of <a href="/wiki/Encinitas,_California" title="Encinitas, California">Encinitas</a>.
</p><p>McCormick purchased an interest in the <a href="/wiki/Old_Bay_Seasoning" title="Old Bay Seasoning">Old Bay</a> seasoning brand in 1990, which was regionally famous for its use in preparing and steaming the local seafood delicacy of the <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay" title="Chesapeake Bay">Chesapeake Bay</a> blue crabs and acquired Mojave Foods Corporation of <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> in 1991, and the consumer products business of Golden Dipt Company in 1993. McCormick's 1994 acquisitions included Grupo Pesa of <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, Tuko Oy of <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>, Butto of <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, and Minipack of <a href="/wiki/Southampton" title="Southampton">Southampton</a>, United Kingdom. Chairman Emeritus Charles P. McCormick Jr. was re-elected chairman in 1994. The company sold Golden West Foods in 1995 and Minipack of Southampton in 1996. Also sold in 1996 were Gilroy Foods and Gilroy Energy, as well as Giza National Dehydration of <a href="/wiki/Cairo,_Egypt" class="mw-redirect" title="Cairo, Egypt">Cairo, Egypt</a>. McCormick Canada acquired the French's dry seasoning line in 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p><p>The company acquired Ducros of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> in 2000, later renamed McCormick France. In 2003, McCormick was added to the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">Standard &amp; Poor's 500 Index</a>; acquired UniqSauces of the UK and <a href="/wiki/Zatarain%27s" title="Zatarain's">Zatarain's</a> of <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>; and sold its packaging businesses, Setco and Tubed Products, as well as its Jenks brokerage business assets. The company acquired C.M. van Sillevoldt B.V. of the Netherlands in 2004 and Epicurean International (renamed Simply Asia Foods) in 2006, with its Thai Kitchen and Simply Asia brands. In 2007, the company started a new advertising campaign to encourage people to dispose of older packages of spices, by pointing out that any of their packages that list their address as "Baltimore, MD 21202" are over 15 years old.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In 2008, McCormick acquired Billy Bee Honey Products of Canada, and the <a href="/wiki/Lawry%27s" class="mw-redirect" title="Lawry's">Lawry's</a> brand of seasonings and marinades (it would remain the biggest acquisition in company history for about ten years).<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> To gain <a href="/wiki/Federal_Trade_Commission" title="Federal Trade Commission">FTC</a> approval for the purchase of Lawry's, McCormick agreed to sell its Season-All business to <a href="/wiki/Morton_Salt" title="Morton Salt">Morton Salt</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p><p>In 2011, the company acquired Kitchen Basics, an Ohio-based brand of shelf-stable liquid stock, for $38 million.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> During that year, it also acquired Kamis S.A., a privately held Polish company with leading brands in spices, seasonings, mustards and other flavor products in Poland for $291 million.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> It also bought an 85% stake in Kohinoor Speciality Foods India for $115 million, a joint venture with India-based Kohinoor Foods Ltd to market and sell basmati and ready-to-eat food products in India.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p><p>In mid-2013, the company completed its acquisition of Wuhan Asia-Pacific Condiments Co. Ltd. (WAPC), a seasoning manufacturer in the central region of China with the Daqiao and ChuShiLe bouillon products.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2015, McCormick announced that Lawrence E. Kurzius, head of global operations, would become CEO effective February 2016. Kurzius was a leader at McCormick for 12 years before the announcement and previously held positions at <a href="/wiki/Uncle_Ben%27s_(rice)" class="mw-redirect" title="Uncle Ben's (rice)">Uncle Ben's</a>, Mars Inc., and the <a href="/wiki/Quaker_Oats_Company" title="Quaker Oats Company">Quaker Oats Co</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Baltimore_Bizjournals_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Baltimore_Bizjournals-26">[26]</a></sup></p><p>The company dropped its bid to acquire <a href="/wiki/Premier_Foods" title="Premier Foods">Premier Foods</a> in April 2016 after determining that Premier's asking price wouldn't benefit shareholders.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p><p>Late in 2016, the company acquired Enrico Giotti SpA, a private Italian flavorings company, in a $127 million deal.<sup id="cite_ref-SharrowBaltBusJ_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SharrowBaltBusJ-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p>In 2017, McCormick purchased Reckitt Benckiser's Food Division (“RB Foods”). At over four billion dollars, it topped the Lawry's acquisition a decade earlier, to become the largest acquisition in the company's history.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The addition of French's and Frank's RedHot to McCormick's global portfolio represent the second and third largest brands, respectively, behind the McCormick brand.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Research_.26_Development"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Research_&amp;_Development">Research &amp; Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=McCormick_%26_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Research &amp; Development" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In February 2019, McCormick announced that it worked with <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a> to build an <a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">artificial intelligence</a> (AI) system to analyze decades of data to develop new flavor combinations and seasoning mixes.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup>  The company said it plans to bring its first AI-developed line of seasoning mixes to market in 2019, which will be called "One" for making one-dish meals.
</p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Headquarters">Headquarters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=McCormick_%26_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Headquarters" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:McCormick_headquarters.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/McCormick_headquarters.jpg/220px-McCormick_headquarters.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/McCormick_headquarters.jpg/220px-McCormick_headquarters.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:McCormick_headquarters.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Former headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Sparks,_Maryland" title="Sparks, Maryland">Sparks</a></div></div></div>
<p>In 1970, McCormick moved its manufacturing and corporate offices from Baltimore's Inner Harbor to suburban <a href="/wiki/Sparks,_Maryland" title="Sparks, Maryland">Sparks, Maryland</a>. In 2018, the company's 1,100-employee global headquarters moved from Sparks to nearby <a href="/wiki/Hunt_Valley,_Maryland" title="Hunt Valley, Maryland">Hunt Valley</a>, with a grand opening held on October 2, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p><p>McCormick's consumer segment has brands in approximately 150 countries and territories. The retail range includes spices &amp; herbs, recipe mixes, extracts, condiments, marinades, stocks, broths, bouillons, sauces, toppings, homemade desserts, rice mixes, salad dressings and breadings.
</p><p>McCormick makes flavorings, branded food services products, condiments, coating systems and ingredients for food manufacturers, food service operators and restaurants around the world.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">
