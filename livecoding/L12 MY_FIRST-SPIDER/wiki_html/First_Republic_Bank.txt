</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_history">Corporate history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=First_Republic_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Corporate history" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Changes_in_ownership">Changes in ownership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=First_Republic_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Changes in ownership" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>Started as a small thrift company,<sup id="cite_ref-Android_and_Ensign_in_2016_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Android_and_Ensign_in_2016-2">[2]</a></sup> First Republic was founded in 1985.<sup id="cite_ref-First_Republic_Release_in_2007_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-First_Republic_Release_in_2007-3">[3]</a></sup> Since its founding, it has undergone a series of ownership changes since 2007, beginning with its announced purchase by <a href="/wiki/Merrill_Lynch" class="mw-redirect" title="Merrill Lynch">Merrill Lynch</a> on January 29, 2007 for $1.8 billion to augment their private client business. The company was to be operated as a separate business of Merrill Lynch.<sup id="cite_ref-First_Republic_Release_in_2007_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-First_Republic_Release_in_2007-3">[3]</a></sup>  The Merrill transaction was finalized on September 21, of 2007. <a href="/wiki/Robert_J._McCann" title="Robert J. McCann">Robert J. McCann</a>, president of Merrill's Global Wealth Management group stated their purpose was to accelerate their strategy of "growing our high net worth, private banking business."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Over the same weekend that <a href="/wiki/Lehman_Brothers" title="Lehman Brothers">Lehman Brothers</a> Holdings filed for Chapter 11 <a href="/wiki/Bankruptcy_protection" class="mw-redirect" title="Bankruptcy protection">bankruptcy protection</a>, on September 15, 2008, Merrill Lynch and its subsidiaries were acquired by <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_America" title="Bank of America">Bank of America</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-in_2008_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-in_2008-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>In October 2009, just under 50% of First Republic was purchased by two private equity firms, <a href="/wiki/Colony_Capital" title="Colony Capital">Colony Capital</a> and <a href="/wiki/General_Atlantic" title="General Atlantic">General Atlantic</a>, and a group of other investors that included chairman James Herbert and former COO Katherine August DeWilde. The price was valued at about $1 billion. Both equity firms had prior relationships with First Republic Bank. <a href="/wiki/Thomas_J._Barrack,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Thomas J. Barrack, Jr.">Thomas J. Barrack, Jr.</a>, the head of Colony, had been a board member prior to the Merrill Lynch deal and General Atlantic had been an early investor in the firm putting up about $5 million in 1987.<sup id="cite_ref-in_2009_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-in_2009-6">[6]</a></sup> The deal was consummated on July 1, 2010. The <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>-based  news service reported that an additional $800 million was provided by the new investment consortium to meet new capital requirements established by U.S. regulators.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Gradifi_and_recent_events">Gradifi and recent events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=First_Republic_Bank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Gradifi and recent events" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<p>In the years leading up to 2016, the bank's private wealth management unit acquired advisory teams such as Constellation Wealth Partners.<sup id="cite_ref-Barthel_2016_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Barthel_2016-8">[8]</a></sup>  By April 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-Passy_in_2016_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Passy_in_2016-9">[9]</a></sup> First Republic had opened branches at the <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> and <a href="/wiki/Twitter_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Twitter Inc.">Twitter Inc.</a> headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Silicon_Valley" title="Silicon Valley">Silicon Valley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Perlberg_and_Gopal_in_2016_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Perlberg_and_Gopal_in_2016-10">[10]</a></sup> It also had various high-profile customers.<sup id="cite_ref-Andriotis_and_Ensign_in_2016_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Andriotis_and_Ensign_in_2016-11">[11]</a></sup> <i>Barron's</i> in September 2016 decided to list First Republic Bank at 21 on its list of America's Top 40 Wealth Management Firms.<sup id="cite_ref-Barthel_2016_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Barthel_2016-8">[8]</a></sup> By December 2016, First Republic Bank had $68 billion in assets.<sup id="cite_ref-Kellard_in_2016_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kellard_in_2016-12">[12]</a></sup></p><p>In December 2016, the bank acquired Gradifi, a startup in Boston, for an unknown cash amount.<sup id="cite_ref-Obrien_in_2016_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Obrien_in_2016-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Kellard_in_2016_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kellard_in_2016-12">[12]</a></sup> Then two years old, Gradifi works with companies to help their employees pay off student debt,<sup id="cite_ref-Kellard_in_2016_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kellard_in_2016-12">[12]</a></sup> and First Republic had become a client in August 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-Obrien_in_2016_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Obrien_in_2016-13">[13]</a></sup> Becoming a wholly owned subsidiary of First Republic,<sup id="cite_ref-Obrien_in_2016_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Obrien_in_2016-13">[13]</a></sup> Gradifi continues to operate in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a> under its own name.<sup id="cite_ref-Kellard_in_2016_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kellard_in_2016-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Obrien_in_2016_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Obrien_in_2016-13">[13]</a></sup> Gradifi retained its customer base including <a href="/wiki/PricewaterhouseCoopers" title="PricewaterhouseCoopers">PricewaterhouseCoopers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natixis_Global_Asset_Management" class="mw-redirect" title="Natixis Global Asset Management">Natixis Global Asset Management</a> and <a href="/wiki/Penguin_Random_House" title="Penguin Random House">Penguin Random House</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Obrien_in_2016_13-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Obrien_in_2016-13">[13]</a></sup></p><p>Remaining a large American bank,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> in March 2018, the bank invested in CommonBond, a student loan financier.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> First Republic announced it would expand into <a href="/wiki/Jackson,_Wyoming" title="Jackson, Wyoming">Jackson, Wyoming</a> in May 2018 with a new branch.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Also that month, First Republic signed a deal to double its space at <a href="/wiki/Rockefeller_Center" title="Rockefeller Center">Rockefeller Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In June 2018, <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> ranked First Republic #220 on its Global 2000 list of "growth champions." <i>Forbes</i> also ranked it #268 on America's largest public companies. With 5,350 employees at the time, First Republic had a market cap of $15.8 billion<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and wealth management assets totaling $140.2 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-A_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-A-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
