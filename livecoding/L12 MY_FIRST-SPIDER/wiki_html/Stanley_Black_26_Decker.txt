</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stanley_Black_%26_Decker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<p>The Stanley Works came to existence as a direct result of the 1920 merger of Stanley's Bolt Manufactory, founded by <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Trent_Stanley" title="Frederick Trent Stanley">Frederick Trent Stanley</a> in 1843, and the Stanley Rule and Level Company,<sup id="cite_ref-Patrick's_Blood_and_Gore_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Patrick's_Blood_and_Gore-6">[6]</a></sup> founded by Frederick's cousin, Henry Stanley, in 1857.
</p><p>During World War II, Stanley Works received the <a href="/wiki/Army-Navy_%22E%22_Award" title='Army-Navy "E" Award'>Army-Navy "E" Award</a> for excellence in war production.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Army-Navy_Production_Award_to_Stanley_Works,_presentation_program,_January_20,_1943_-_New_Britain_Industrial_Museum_-_DSC09905.JPG" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Army-Navy_Production_Award_to_Stanley_Works%2C_presentation_program%2C_January_20%2C_1943_-_New_Britain_Industrial_Museum_-_DSC09905.JPG/220px-Army-Navy_Production_Award_to_Stanley_Works%2C_presentation_program%2C_January_20%2C_1943_-_New_Britain_Industrial_Museum_-_DSC09905.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="304" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="3219" data-file-height="4453"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 304px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Army-Navy_Production_Award_to_Stanley_Works%2C_presentation_program%2C_January_20%2C_1943_-_New_Britain_Industrial_Museum_-_DSC09905.JPG/220px-Army-Navy_Production_Award_to_Stanley_Works%2C_presentation_program%2C_January_20%2C_1943_-_New_Britain_Industrial_Museum_-_DSC09905.JPG" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="304" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Army-Navy_Production_Award_to_Stanley_Works,_presentation_program,_January_20,_1943_-_New_Britain_Industrial_Museum_-_DSC09905.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>WWII <a href="/wiki/Army-Navy_%22E%22_Award" title='Army-Navy "E" Award'>Army-Navy Production Award</a> to Stanley Works, presentation program, January 20, 1943</div></div></div>
<p>In May 2002, the company considered moving its corporate headquarters to <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>, but public and governmental outcry forced management to reconsider the move.  By August 2002, the company had decided to maintain its incorporation in the United States.
</p><p>John F. Lundgren was elected as chairman and chief executive officer in 2004, replacing John Trani, a former protégé of <a href="/wiki/Jack_Welch" title="Jack Welch">Jack Welch</a> at <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a>.
</p><p>The Hardware &amp; Home Improvement Group, including the Kwikset, Weiser, Baldwin, National Hardware, Stanley, FANAL, Pfister and EZSET brands, was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Spectrum_Brands" title="Spectrum Brands">Spectrum Brands</a> Holdings, Inc. on  December 17, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p><p>In July 2016, CEO John F. Lundgren stepped down, with President and COO <a href="/wiki/James_M._Loree" title="James M. Loree">James M. Loree</a> taking over as CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stanley_Black_%26_Decker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acquisitions" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<ul><li><b>1937:</b> Stanley Works entered the <a href="/wiki/UK" class="mw-redirect" title="UK">UK</a> market with the acquisition of J.A. Chapman of <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a>, England.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanley_Works_Time_Line,_Resource_Directory_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanley_Works_Time_Line,_Resource_Directory-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1946:</b> Stanley Works acquired <a href="/wiki/North_Brothers_Manufacturing_Company" title="North Brothers Manufacturing Company">North Brothers Manufacturing Company</a> in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia, Pennsylvania</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wkFineTools_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wkFineTools-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1963:</b> Stanley started operations in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> as Stanley-Titan when it bought a 50% share of Titan, a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/BHP_Billiton" class="mw-redirect" title="BHP Billiton">BHP</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1966:</b> Stanley Works acquired Vidmar Cabinets in <a href="/wiki/Allentown,_Pennsylvania" title="Allentown, Pennsylvania">Allentown, Pennsylvania</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1970:</b> Stanley-Titan acquired Turner Tools, based in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>, Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1980:</b> Stanley Works acquired <a href="/wiki/Mac_Tools" title="Mac Tools">Mac Tools</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanley_Works_Time_Line,_Resource_Directory_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanley_Works_Time_Line,_Resource_Directory-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Stanley_brand_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanley_brand-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1984:</b> Stanley Works purchased <a href="/wiki/Proto_(tools)" title="Proto (tools)">Proto</a> from <a href="/wiki/Ingersoll_Rand" title="Ingersoll Rand">Ingersoll Rand</a> and it becomes Stanley Proto.<sup id="cite_ref-Proto_Tools_100th_Anniversary_Timeline_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Proto_Tools_100th_Anniversary_Timeline-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1986:</b> Stanley Works acquired <a href="/wiki/Bostitch" title="Bostitch">Bostitch</a> from <a href="/wiki/Textron" title="Textron">Textron</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanley_Bostitch_History_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanley_Bostitch_History-17">[17]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1990:</b> Stanley Works acquired Goldblatt<sup id="cite_ref-Stanley_Works_Time_Line,_Resource_Directory_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanley_Works_Time_Line,_Resource_Directory-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Stanley_brand_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanley_brand-15">[15]</a></sup> and ZAG Industries.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanley_brand_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanley_brand-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>1990:</b> Acquired Sidchrome Tool Co., head-quartered in Melbourne, Australia. Closed plant in 1996 and started to move all tool manufacturing to <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, whilst sourcing various items from Proto in the USA (marked as Proto on items) due to supply of left-over Australian-made tools being sold out until all manufacturing was fully established in Taiwan.</li>
<li><b>1992:</b> Stanley Works purchased the <a href="/wiki/Chatsworth,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Chatsworth, California">Chatsworth, California</a>-based Monarch Mirror Door Co. Inc., an American manufacturer of sliding and folding mirror-doors.</li>
<li><b>2000:</b> Stanley Works acquired <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160818002110/https://www.stanleysecurity.co.uk/about-us/blick-history">Blick</a> of <a href="/wiki/Swindon,_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Swindon, England">Swindon, England</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Stanley_Works_Time_Line,_Resource_Directory_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanley_Works_Time_Line,_Resource_Directory-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Stanley_brand_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanley_brand-15">[15]</a></sup> a <a href="/wiki/UK" class="mw-redirect" title="UK">UK</a> integrator of security solutions<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Use_plain_English#Buzzwords" title="Wikipedia:Use plain English"><span title="The material near this tag may use buzzwords designed solely to impress you or obscure meaning. (June 2019)">buzzword</span></a></i>]</sup>, communication, and time-management solutions<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Use_plain_English#Buzzwords" title="Wikipedia:Use plain English"><span title="The material near this tag may use buzzwords designed solely to impress you or obscure meaning. (June 2019)">buzzword</span></a></i>]</sup>, and CST Berger.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanley_Works_Time_Line,_Resource_Directory_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanley_Works_Time_Line,_Resource_Directory-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Stanley_brand_15-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanley_brand-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>2002:</b> In October, Stanley Works acquired <a href="/wiki/Best_Lock_Corporation" title="Best Lock Corporation">Best Access Systems</a> of <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis" title="Indianapolis">Indianapolis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>, for $310 million. The acquisition also prompted the creation of a new Access Controls Group for Stanley. Further additions to this new working group included Blick.</li>
<li><b>2004:</b> In January,  Stanley announced plans to acquire Frisco Bay Industries Ltd., a Canadian provider of security integration services, for $45.3 million. In December, the acquisition of ISR Solutions, Inc., headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a>, was announced. ISR Solutions provides the U.S. federal government and commercial customers with access security system services.</li>
<li><b>2005:</b> In January, the acquisition of Security Group, Inc. was announced.  Security Group was composed of two primary operating companies: Sargent &amp; Greenleaf, Inc., a manufacturer of locks; and Safemasters, a North American provider of security installation, maintenance and repair services. An additional acquisition of Precision Hardware was made in 2005.</li>
<li><b>2006:</b> Stanley furthered its corporate assets in the security market by acquiring HSM Electronic Protection Systems after it had been spun off from <a href="/wiki/Honeywell" title="Honeywell">Honeywell</a> in compliance with pre-emptive <a href="/wiki/Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" class="mw-redirect" title="Securities and Exchange Commission">Securities and Exchange Commission</a> antitrust rulings. In the meantime, the company obtained security contracts as the primary contractor to secure three <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> spaceflight centers.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Stanley Works also acquired Facom.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanley_Works_Time_Line,_Resource_Directory_10-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanley_Works_Time_Line,_Resource_Directory-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Stanley_brand_15-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanley_brand-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>2007:</b> Late in the year, Stanley acquired OSI Security of <a href="/wiki/Chula_Vista,_California" title="Chula Vista, California">Chula Vista</a>, California, a provider of battery-operated wireless lock technology and supplies to government, education, and healthcare industries.</li>
<li><b>2008:</b> Stanley acquired Beach Toolbox Industries, headquartered in Smith Falls, Ontario, Canada, then closed the plant.</li>
<li><b>2008:</b> In June, Stanley announced the acquisition of Sonitrol, which provides security systems that use audio listening devices as the primary means of intrusion detection. Stanley also acquired Xmark Corporation, which provides radio frequency identification (RFID) solutions in healthcare environments. As of 2008, many of the Stanley Security Services divisions were being integrated under the HSM brand.</li>
<li><b>2009:</b> On November 2, Stanley announced a merger with <a href="/wiki/Black_%26_Decker" class="mw-redirect" title="Black &amp; Decker">Black &amp; Decker</a> and <a href="/wiki/DeWalt" title="DeWalt">DeWalt</a> tools.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The merger was completed on March 12, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>2010:</b> In July, the company announced the acquisition of CRC-Evans Pipeline International.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/CRC-Evans" title="CRC-Evans">CRC-Evans</a> provides total project support for pipeline construction contractors with automatic welding and other pipeline construction specific equipment and personnel.</li>
<li><b>2011:</b> On September 9, the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Niscayah" title="Niscayah">Niscayah</a> was complete.</li>
<li><b>2012:</b> On January 1, the acquisition of Lista North America from <a href="/wiki/LISTA" title="LISTA">LISTA</a>, headquartered in Holliston, Massachusetts, was completed.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>2012:</b> On June 1, the acquisition of Powers Fasteners, headquartered in Brewster, New York, was completed.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>2012:</b> On June 5, the acquisition of AeroScout, Inc., headquartered in Redwood City, California, was completed.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>2016:</b> Stanley Black &amp; Decker announced in October that it acquired the Irwin, Lenox, and Hilmor tool brands for $1.95 billion from <a href="/wiki/Newell_Brands" title="Newell Brands">Newell Brands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>2017:</b> On January 5, news reports indicated that it would acquire the <a href="/wiki/Craftsman_(tools)" title="Craftsman (tools)">Craftsman</a> brand from KCD, LLC (A <a href="/wiki/Sears_Holdings" title="Sears Holdings">Sears Holdings</a> subsidiary).<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Subsequent reports by <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_L.P." title="Bloomberg L.P.">Bloomberg</a> indicated that the company would pay $525 million initially, an additional $250 million after three years, as well as annual payments on new Craftsman sales for 15 years.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>2018:</b> On September 12, Stanley Black &amp; Decker announced that it has entered into a definitive agreement to acquire a 20 percent stake in <a href="/wiki/MTD_Products" title="MTD Products">MTD Products Inc</a>, a privately held global manufacturer of outdoor power equipment, for $234 million in cash.
