</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Hartford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:The_Hartford_(May,_1875_calendar_postcard)_(14222377016).jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/The_Hartford_%28May%2C_1875_calendar_postcard%29_%2814222377016%29.jpg/220px-The_Hartford_%28May%2C_1875_calendar_postcard%29_%2814222377016%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="150" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="2472" data-file-height="1680"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 150px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/The_Hartford_%28May%2C_1875_calendar_postcard%29_%2814222377016%29.jpg/220px-The_Hartford_%28May%2C_1875_calendar_postcard%29_%2814222377016%29.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="150" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:The_Hartford_(May,_1875_calendar_postcard)_(14222377016).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>1875 postcard calendar for the Hartford Accident Insurance Co.</div></div></div>
<p>The Hartford was founded in 1810 in <a href="/wiki/Hartford" class="mw-redirect" title="Hartford">Hartford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a>. A group of local merchants gathered in a Hartford inn and, with working capital of $15,000, founded the Hartford Fire Insurance Company. The company survived some of the greatest peacetime tragedies in American history. After a <a href="/wiki/Great_Fire_of_New_York" title="Great Fire of New York">huge fire destroyed New York's financial district</a> in 1835, The Hartford's president, Eliphalet Terry, used his personal wealth to cover all of the company's damage claims. Other catastrophic events included the Chicago fire of 1871 and the 1906 San Francisco earthquake and fire.
</p><p>The company logo shows a male deer, which in full maturity was referred to by the <a href="/wiki/Medieval_hunting" title="Medieval hunting">medieval hunting</a> term <i><a href="/wiki/Hart_(deer)" title="Hart (deer)">hart</a></i>.  The etymology of 'Hartford' is the <a href="/wiki/Ford_(crossing)" title="Ford (crossing)">ford</a> where harts cross (deer crossing). The <a href="/wiki/File:HartfordCTseal.png" title="File:HartfordCTseal.png">Seal of the City of Hartford</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> likewise features a mature male deer.
</p>
<h3 class="in-block"><span id="Acquisitions.2C_divestitures_and_related_changes"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions,_divestitures_and_related_changes">Acquisitions, divestitures and related changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Hartford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acquisitions, divestitures and related changes" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h3>
<ul><li>2019: Acquired Navigators, adding specialty products across 22 vertical markets, and offices in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Continental_Europe" title="Continental Europe">Continental Europe</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li>2018, Acquired Y-Risk, a managing general underwriter specializing in economy risks, with managing general agencies across the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li>2014: Sold its Japan annuities business to <a href="/wiki/Orix" title="Orix">Orix Life Insurance Corp.</a><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li>2013: Sold its life insurance business to Prudential, retirement plans to MassMutual, and a broker-dealer to <a href="/wiki/American_International_Group" title="American International Group">AIG</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li>2012: Announced it would focus on property and casualty insurance, group benefits and mutual funds, and would sell its wealth management businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li>2004: Purchased the Group Benefits Division of <a href="/wiki/CNA_Financial" title="CNA Financial">CNA Financial</a>.  The division was based in Chicago, Illinois.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li>2000: Reacquired all the shares of Hartford Life (HLI was delisted from the New York Stock Exchange in 2006).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li>1997: Changed name from ITT-Hartford Group, Inc. to the Hartford Financial Services Group, Inc., and also issued an IPO for its Hartford Life business under the ticker symbol “HLI.”<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li>1995: ITT decided to streamline its operations and release some of its subsidiaries, and The Hartford became an independent entity once again, trading on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> under the symbol "HIG".</li>
<li>1970: The Hartford was acquired by <a href="/wiki/ITT_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="ITT Corporation">ITT Corporation</a> for $1.4 billion,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> at the time the largest corporate takeover in American history. The combined company was renamed ITT-Hartford Group, Inc.</li>
<li>1959: Expanded into the life-insurance business by acquiring The Columbian National Life Insurance Company in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, Massachusetts.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li>1913: Formed the Hartford Accident and Indemnity Company to provide a wide variety of insurance coverage, including accident, automobile-liability, personal-damage, business-interruption and more.</li></ul></section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Hartford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: References" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
