</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(1)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tractor_Supply_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History" data-section="1" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tsc_inside.jpg" class="image"><noscript><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Tsc_inside.jpg/220px-Tsc_inside.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1200"></noscript><span class="lazy-image-placeholder" style="width: 220px;height: 165px;" data-src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Tsc_inside.jpg/220px-Tsc_inside.jpg" data-alt="" data-width="220" data-height="165" data-class="thumbimage"> </span></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Tsc_inside.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Interior of a Tractor Supply Company store in <a href="/wiki/East_Tawas,_Michigan" title="East Tawas, Michigan">East Tawas, Michigan</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Founded as a <a href="/wiki/Mail_order" title="Mail order">mail order</a> <a href="/wiki/Tractor" title="Tractor">tractor</a> parts business in 1938, the first Tractor Supply Co. retail store was founded in 1939 in <a href="/wiki/Minot,_North_Dakota" title="Minot, North Dakota">Minot, North Dakota</a>. From 1998 to 2002, <a href="/wiki/George_Strait" title="George Strait">George Strait</a> was the spokesman for Tractor Supply. Tractor Supply is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Brentwood,_Tennessee" title="Brentwood, Tennessee">Brentwood, Tennessee</a>, a suburb of <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Semiotic_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Semiotic-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In 2004, when the company reported revenues of more than US$1.7 billion, <a href="/wiki/Fortune_magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Fortune magazine"><i>Fortune</i> magazine</a> named Tractor Supply to its list of the 100 fastest growing businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-Semiotic_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Semiotic-4">[4]</a></sup>  As of December 29, 2019, Tractor Supply Company operated 1,844 stores in 49 states.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p><p>On September 4, 2018, the company celebrated its 80th anniversary by ringing the closing bell at the Nasdaq Stock Exchange in New York.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p><p>On October 10, 2013, the company celebrated its 75th anniversary by ringing the opening bell at the Nasdaq stock exchange in New York.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>The company's 1,700th store opened in March 2018.
</p><p>On September 9, 2019, it was announced that the company has 1,800 stores in operation.
</p><p>On March 24, 2020, Tractor Supply started its own animal facts department and is available 24 hours a day.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(2)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Products">Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tractor_Supply_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Products" data-section="2" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-2 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-2">
<p>Tractor Supply lists five major product categories. The <a href="/wiki/Livestock" title="Livestock">livestock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pet" title="Pet">pet</a> products category accounted for 47 percent of the company's sales in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The category with the second highest sales was <a href="/wiki/Household_hardware" title="Household hardware">hardware</a>, tools, truck and towing products, with 21 percent of sales.  Seasonal products such as lawn and garden equipment, gifts and toys were 20 percent of sales, followed by clothing and footwear with 8 percent and agricultural products with 4 percent of sales.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(3)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tractor_Supply_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Marketing" data-section="3" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-3 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-3">
<p>The company has conducted <a href="/wiki/Advertising" title="Advertising">advertising</a> campaigns featuring the <a href="/wiki/Slogan" title="Slogan">slogan</a> "The Stuff You Need Out Here."<sup id="cite_ref-Semiotic_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Semiotic-4">[4]</a></sup> Tractor Supply's mission statement is: "To work hard, have fun and make money by providing legendary service and great products at everyday low prices."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(4)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions_and_subsidiaries">Acquisitions and subsidiaries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tractor_Supply_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Acquisitions and subsidiaries" data-section="4" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-4 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-4">
<p>In September 2016, Tractor Supply Company acquired Petsense, LLC, a leading small-box specialty retailer of pet supplies primarily located in small and mid-size communities.<sup id="cite_ref-Finance.yahoo.com_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Finance.yahoo.com-12">[12]</a></sup> In 2002, TSC was part of a group (also including four liquidation firms) that purchased some of the <a href="/wiki/Lease" title="Lease">leases</a> of the bankrupt <a href="/wiki/George_Edward_Hilt" title="George Edward Hilt">Quality Stores, Inc.</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> a <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>-based company which at one time had operated over 300 stores in 30 states under the names CT Farm &amp; Country; Country General; Quality Farm &amp; Fleet; County Post; Central Farm and Fleet, and FISCO. The company also owns <b>Del's Feed &amp; Farm Supply</b>, a farm retail chain in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Northwest" title="Pacific Northwest">Pacific Northwest</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>, with all but two Del's stores having been converted into Tractor Supply stores.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
</section><h2 class="section-heading" onclick="javascript:mfTempOpenSection(5)"><div class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element indicator mw-ui-icon-small mw-ui-icon-flush-left"></div><span id="TSC_Stores_L.P._.28Canada.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="TSC_Stores_L.P._(Canada)">TSC Stores L.P. (Canada)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tractor_Supply_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: TSC Stores L.P. (Canada)" data-section="5" class="mw-ui-icon mw-ui-icon-element mw-ui-icon-wikimedia-edit-base20 edit-page mw-ui-icon-flush-right">Edit</a></span></h2><section class="mf-section-5 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-5">
<table class="box-Primary_sources plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Primary_sources" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>relies too much on <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">references</a> to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research#Primary,_secondary_and_tertiary_sources" title="Wikipedia:No original research">primary sources</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please improve this section by adding <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research#Primary,_secondary_and_tertiary_sources" title="Wikipedia:No original research">secondary or tertiary sources</a>.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">June 2019</span>)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table><p>Although also operating under the name TSC, this company is not currently affiliated with the U.S. operation of Tractor Supply Company and is Canadian owned and operated.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> TSC Stores L.P. operates stores in <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>. The company retails automotive, animal care, home improvement, and tooling and welding products; clothing collections, footwear, and toys; and farming, fencing, hunting and fishing, outdoor living, pet and birding, and outdoor power equipment. It also serves customers online. The company was founded in 1966 and is based in <a href="/wiki/London,_Ontario" title="London, Ontario">London, Ontario</a>. As of July 18, 2016, TSC Stores L.P. has been operating as a subsidiary of Peavey Industries LP.
</p><p>In November 1966, TSC opened its first Ontario location in London. In 1987, when there were ten Ontario locations, the American parent company sold these locations to the Birch Hill investment company. Prior to July 2016, there were 51 corporate-owned TSC stores in Ontario, mostly in the <a href="/wiki/Southwestern_Ontario" title="Southwestern Ontario">southwest</a>, as well as two stores in <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">Manitoba</a> (<a href="/wiki/Winkler,_Manitoba" title="Winkler, Manitoba">Winkler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brandon,_Manitoba" title="Brandon, Manitoba">Brandon</a>).
</p><p>From 2007 to 2009, TSC Stores was title sponsor of <a href="/wiki/The_Dominion_Tankard" class="mw-redirect" title="The Dominion Tankard">The Dominion Tankard</a> <a href="/wiki/Curling" title="Curling">curling</a> championship in <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ontario" title="Southern Ontario">Southern Ontario</a>.
</p><p>In July 2016, the owner of the <a href="/wiki/Peavey_Mart" title="Peavey Mart">Peavey Mart</a> chain bought a controlling interest in TSC Stores L.P from Birch Hill Equity Partners; full ownership will be achieved during 2017. With TSC's 51 retail stores added in, Peavey Mart will control 87 stores in total, as well as a second distribution centre located in London, Ontario. TSC's two Manitoba stores have been rebranded as Peavey Marts.<sup id="cite_ref-Peavey_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Peavey-17">[17]</a></sup> TSC's Ontario stores will continue to operate under the TSC brand.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
